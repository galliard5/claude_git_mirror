# Eternity — The Pillars

## Overview

Cold and distant, Eternity has existed on the edge of the Screaming Vortex for nearly ten thousand years — close enough to warp space that real space intrudes violently, and the usual methods of void travel are unreliable or useless. The planet orbits a Blue Giant whose heat and radiation have been progressively stolen by millennia of warp instability, leaving the world locked in permanent cold.

It has two names. The majority of the local tribes call it **T'char Hagan** — loosely translated as "the gift given us by T'char," substituting their local name for the Tzeentch-analog deity they follow. Astral cartographers who bother naming it at all call it **The Pillars**, for reasons visible from low orbit.

---

## The Pillars

Scattered across the planet's surface — concentrated at the poles and in remote mountain ranges — stand towers of uniform height, each rising exactly one kilometre above the ground below. Their construction is unknown. Their age is unknown. Their purpose is unknown.

Each Pillar:
- Emits strong electrical readings detectable from orbit
- Casts baleful green light from strange, unbreakable windows near the pinnacle
- Disrupts radio and astropathic communication in its vicinity
- Is located in terrain effectively impassable to the planet's iron-age population

The interiors are reported to be shifting mazes — layout inconsistent between visits, filled with traps and creatures of unclear origin. The few who have entered and escaped alive describe treasures in the lower floors: sealed cases that produce fire, transparent panels that appear to show other locations in real time, armoured suits that crackle with electricity and provide extraordinary protection. None who have ventured above the Ice Walls that seal the lower floors have returned. What inhabits the upper levels is unknown. The Agarathi who guard the approaches call them the Loathsome Beasts and do not elaborate.

The Pillars are almost certainly pre-collapse imperial infrastructure of some kind — monitoring stations, relay towers, or something without a clean modern category. Whatever they were built for, they are still doing it.

## Factions

### The Tribes of T'char Hagan
The majority population of Eternity — iron-age humans organised into tribes, living under the shadow of the Pillars and the theology that has grown up around them. They follow a Tzeentch-analog deity and interpret the Pillars as divine gifts or divine tests depending on local tradition. Their magicians and miracle workers — individuals with warp-sensitivity — are valued and protected within most tribal structures.

### The Agarathi
A faction of debased and blood-maddened humans who occupy the mountain passes leading to the Pillars and defend them with fanatical violence. They worship a deity of tyranny and murder described as wreathed in golden light — a Khorne or Slaanesh analog with particular emphasis on dominance and enslavement. The Agarathi are notably better equipped than their iron-age context would suggest — ancient armour, weapons that fire energy or projectiles — and ride vicious beasts that they have tamed to guard the passes. They actively enslave psykers and warp-sensitive individuals taken from other tribes.

Reaching the Pillars means going through Agarathi territory. This is not an obstacle that can be planned around.

### The N'crir
The population centred on the Bleak Catacombs — a people who inspire loathing and terror among the other tribes of Eternity in equal measure. What specifically makes them feared is left to inference and rumour. They mine the Catacombs for fuel and metal and are known to venture into the tunnel networks that others will not enter.

---

## The Bleak Catacombs

Visible from Eternity's sole moon, the Bleak Catacombs are a vast tunnel and cave network dug into the sides and floor of an enormous impact crater. The crater is littered with mining machinery that the local population has no framework to identify or operate — pre-collapse industrial equipment, abandoned mid-operation. The Catacombs serve the N'crir as a source of raw materials and as something else entirely that the other tribes do not discuss directly.

## Approach and Access

Eternity sits on the warp edge, which creates significant practical problems:
- Standard warp transit is unreliable in this region — ships may emerge badly off-target or not at all
- Astropathic communication is degraded system-wide and effectively blocked near the Pillars
- The planet's surface weather is violent, particularly in the mountain regions around the Pillars
- Low orbit observation is possible; surface access requires navigating both terrain and the Agarathi

The world is not on standard trade routes and is not a port of call. Those who go there are looking for something specific — usually what's inside the Pillars.

---

## Campaign Relevance

The Pillars are almost certainly repositories of pre-collapse technology. What they actually contain beyond the lower-floor salvage reports is unknown. The disruption fields they generate, the consistency of their construction, and the apparent ongoing function of their internal systems suggest something more significant than a simple cache.

The warp-edge location, the communication blackout, the Agarathi defenders, and whatever lives above the Ice Walls are all reasons not to go. The potential contents are the reason to go anyway.
