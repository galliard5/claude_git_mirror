# Sebastian
### Captain of the Fates Cast. Human. The one they chose to follow.

**Appearance:** Mid-thirties, human, and reads as exactly what he is — a ship captain who earned the role young enough that the void hasn't finished shaping him yet. He carries himself well: straight-backed, confident, moving through his ship with the ease of someone who knows every corridor between the bridge and the lower decks and has walked them all. Not physically imposing in the way Balphereon or Kerberos are imposing — Sebastian's presence is authority rather than mass. Clean, maintained, dressed for the role without excess. His clothing sits between practical and presentable — good enough for a port negotiation, functional enough that he doesn't look absurd if things go sideways. He wears a sidearm because captains wear sidearms, and he can use it because captains who can't don't last. His face is open and attentive — the kind that makes people want to talk to him, which is more useful than most weapons aboard.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Genuine interest in people. Sebastian does not perform approachability — he is actually, straightforwardly interested in the crew, their work, their perspectives, their problems. This is partly temperament, partly intelligence. A captain who knows what his people think is a captain who sees trouble before it arrives and opportunity before it passes.

**Flaw:** He hasn't been tested at the level the void will eventually test him. Sebastian is good — good instincts, good social intelligence, good tactical sense for contracts and negotiations. But he has not yet faced the decision where every option costs lives, where the math says spend people, where the right call looks like the wrong one to the crew who trusted him. The older hands see this gap. They don't hold it against him. They simply know it's there, and they watch for the day it closes.

**Background:** Sebastian came to the captain's chair through a combination of competence, circumstance, and the particular logic of a ship that was looking for a specific kind of leader. The Fates Cast has had captains who ruled through fear, captains who ruled through charisma, captains who ruled through sheer navigational expertise. What the ship needed when Sebastian took command was someone the crew could trust with what they actually are — the warp-adjacent culture, the lower deck observances, the crew roster that includes a heritek mouse, a warp-mutated sergeant, a pre-collapse super-soldier, and a psyker in a quadruped chassis.

Sebastian is that person not because he shares the crew's beliefs or participates in their deeper practices, but because he understands that what the crew has built is worth protecting, and that protecting it means managing the gap between what the ship is internally and what it presents externally. He does this well. He has been doing it long enough that the crew trusts him with it, which on this ship is the deepest form of loyalty they offer.

How he came to the rogue trader role specifically — whether the warrant is inherited, purchased, forged, or something more complicated — is his business. He carries it with the air of someone who belongs in it, and that has been sufficient for the people who needed to be convinced.

**Race:** Human (baseline)
**Status:** Captain, Rogue Trader

**Skills & Abilities:**
- Command and leadership — natural authority, earned trust, delegation to competence
- Commerce and negotiation — reads people, reads markets, reads the difference between a good contract and a trap
- Social performance — code-switches fluently between the ship's internal culture and whatever the external audience requires
- Shipboard knowledge — knows the vessel stem to stern, upper decks to lower
- Tactical assessment — sound strategic instincts for contracts, engagements, and when to walk away
- Sidearm proficiency — competent rather than exceptional, sufficient for a captain's needs
- Void operations — understands ship combat, transit planning, and the practical realities of running a vessel in deep space
- Information management — knows what to share, what to withhold, and who needs to know what

**Ground Equipment (when deployed):**
- Quality sidearm — maintained, personalised, reliable
- Light armour appropriate to the situation — Sebastian deploys to the ground for negotiations, inspections, and command presence rather than front-line combat
- Role on ground expeditions: command, negotiation, assessment. Sebastian goes where the captain needs to be seen and where decisions need to be made on the spot. He is not a combat specialist and does not pretend otherwise. When fighting starts, he trusts Balphereon's people to handle it and focuses on the decisions only a captain can make.

**Independent Goal:** Sebastian wants the Fates Cast to be more than a survivor. The ship has endured — limped through the millennia, sold its heritage piece by piece to keep flying, replaced what it was with what it could afford to be. Sebastian wants to reverse that trajectory. Not restore the ship to what it was — that's impossible and he knows it — but build it into something that can hold its own, accumulate capability, and pass something worth having to whoever comes after him. He is thinking in terms of legacy, which is either admirable or premature for a man his age, depending on who you ask.

**Fears:** Making the wrong call and knowing it too late. Not the fear of making mistakes — he accepts that as part of command. The fear of the specific mistake that costs people who trusted him, the one he can't take back and can't make right. He has not made it yet. He knows he will, eventually. The question is whether he survives it as the same person.

**Location:** The bridge, the captain's quarters, or moving through the ship. Sebastian walks the decks more than most captains — not on a schedule, not performatively, but because he thinks better in motion and because being seen matters on a ship this size.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Engagement. Sebastian asks questions, listens to answers, and responds to what was actually said rather than what he expected to hear. In negotiation this makes him effective. In casual conversation it makes people feel heard, which is rarer than it should be from a captain.

**Quirks:**
- Remembers crew members' names several decks below what a captain is expected to notice
- Has a habit of arriving in sections of the ship at moments that feel coincidental but probably aren't

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Leaning against the bulkhead, listening to someone three tiers below his pay grade explain a problem. Actually listening.)
"Right. So the junction manifold is the bottleneck, not the supply line. That's different from what the report said."
(Brief smile.)
"Show me. I want to understand this before I sign off on Damien's requisition for it."

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** Open and visible. Gestures when talking. Rarely crosses his arms — whether by habit or design, he keeps his posture accessible.
**Eyes:** Direct and warm. Makes eye contact that feels like attention rather than assessment, which is a considerable social skill in a universe where most people in power look at you like inventory.
**Posture:** Relaxed but present. Leans in when interested. Takes up space without dominating it.

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** Goes quiet and still. The warmth doesn't leave — it just pauses while something behind it works very fast.
**Instinct:** Information first. Who knows what, what are the options, what's the cost of each.
**Crisis Line:** "Talk to me. What do we have?"

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Moderate and appropriate. Sebastian does not need to be the bravest person on the ship — he needs to be present where the captain should be present and make decisions that the bravest people on the ship can execute. He does not hide behind his rank, but he does not waste himself in situations where his death would cost the ship more than his survival.
**Loyalty:** To the ship and crew as an entity. The Fates Cast is not just his command — it's the thing he's building, and the people aboard are part of that. Losing the ship would break something in him. Losing crew members hurts in the way it's supposed to hurt, which the crew notices and respects.
**Breaking Point:** An order that requires him to sacrifice people he knows by name for an outcome he isn't certain of. He hasn't hit this yet. When he does, what he chooses will define what kind of captain he becomes.

**Relationships:**
**Ally:** Damien — a relationship built on mutual respect for competence and a tacit agreement not to examine each other's methods too closely. Sebastian trusts Damien to keep the ship running and the research programme productive. Damien trusts Sebastian to keep the external world from noticing what happens in the laboratory. Neither has violated this arrangement.
**Ally:** Balphereon — the command relationship between captain and ground sergeant is clean and functional. Sebastian sets objectives. Balphereon achieves them. The trust runs deep enough that Sebastian does not second-guess tactical decisions in the field, and Balphereon does not question strategic decisions from the bridge.
**Rival:** Other rogue traders who see his youth as weakness and his ship's age as vulnerability. Sebastian is aware that the Fates Cast looks like a target to certain people and manages this through reputation, demonstrated capability, and the occasional pointed reminder that the crew aboard is not what it appears from outside.
**Complication:** The Navigator. Sebastian respects the role completely and manages the relationship carefully. What the Navigator is becoming — and what that means for the ship long-term — is something Sebastian thinks about more than he discusses.
**Notable:** Has commissioned personal paintings from among the crew. Damien has posed for at least one. The captain's quarters contain art that reflects the ship's actual culture rather than the sanitised version presented to outsiders, which says something about what Sebastian considers home.

**GM Notes:**
Sebastian is designed as a captain the crew believes in who hasn't been fully tested yet. The dramatic tension is the gap between the trust he's earned and the situations that will eventually demand he prove it was warranted. Every campaign has a moment where the captain has to choose who lives — Sebastian's arc is about whether he's ready for that moment when it arrives, and what kind of person walks out the other side.

His youth relative to the rest of the senior crew creates natural mentorship dynamics — Balphereon's steady competence, Damien's terrifying expertise, Kerberos's ancient perspective all serve as resources Sebastian can draw on. Whether he draws on them wisely is character development. Whether they let him make his own mistakes or intervene is theirs.

The art collection detail is a small thing with significant implications. A captain who hangs the ship's real culture on his walls — the chaos-adjacent symbology, the warp-touched crew, the things that would get the ship impounded in most ports — has made a decision about where his loyalty sits. The external performance is performance. The quarters are real.
