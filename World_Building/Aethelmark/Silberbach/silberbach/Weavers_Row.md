Weaver's Row
============

Commercial street in Silberbach's textile district. Home to Hauss's textile operation and the sphere of influence of Weavers' Guild Master Sorren. A significant node in Corvel's money laundering network.

description: A textile trade street, likely one of the primary commercial strips for fabric, cloth, and related goods in Silberbach. The Weavers' Guild has significant presence and influence here. Multiple textile merchants operating in proximity.

Notable Occupants:
- Hauss: textile merchant, Corvel's complicit receiver for Brenn and Sons payments
- Weavers' Guild: Master Sorren holds authority here; his own name appears on Corvel's client list

Significance: The concentration of a guild master and a complicit merchant on the same commercial street suggests Corvel's network has deep roots in the textile trade specifically — possibly because textile transactions are naturally high-volume and difficult to audit, making them ideal for absorbing laundered funds.

Investigation Notes: Both Hauss and Sorren are named in Oswin's statement. Constable Ferris's investigation will need to move carefully here — guild master involvement means institutional resistance is possible, and Corvel's "higher protection" claim may partially originate from guild connections.
