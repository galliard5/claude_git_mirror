## Background

Mecklau facilitation trade, nine years. Three county court relationships — likely meaning magistrates or clerks who route him appropriate cases or sign off on his documentation without excessive scrutiny. Licensed practitioner on retainer to carry out transformations under his arranged settlements.

He identified Isalia's estate as a referral target after the Kennel Hounds programme in Cendrel drew regional attention to transformation services generally. The estate's imperial archive protection, established consent framework, and thirty-year reputation make it attractive for cases that exceed his practitioners' competence or require protections he cannot provide.

He sent Ossel to test the consent framework. He sent Fenk (using the name Dressel) to test the contract structure. He arranged for three women with southern-court-adjacent situations to visit the estate over six weeks to test whether intake notices problems when they walk in. Then he used Corvin — a man with a weak residual claim — as a door to knock on and a reason to have a direct conversation.

He wants a referral relationship. Cases exceeding his practitioners' competence go north to the estate. Clients needing imperial protection come through the estate's intake. He gets overflow handled properly. The estate gets clients it would not otherwise see.

## What He Wants

A functioning referral pipeline with an established practitioner who has protections he cannot replicate. Access to the estate's archive protection for sensitive cases. A working relationship that survives scrutiny.

He does not want to be in the business of holding people as leverage. Corvin was untidy. He dropped the claim the moment Isalia indicated the conversation was worth having.
