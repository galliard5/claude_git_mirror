The Crescent House
==================

A licensed entertainment house three streets past the dye works, in the part of the Harbor District where the lanterns burn lower and the laughter shifts register. Its sign is a painted wooden fox curled around a crescent moon, tail arranged with just enough artfulness to make travelers pause. It is entirely unremarkable from the outside. That is the point.

## The Surface

The Crescent House operates a legitimate establishment by town standards: feral performers for stage shows, private rooms, drink. It caters to rivermen, travelling merchants, and locals who prefer their business well clear of Merchants' Close. The River Traders' Guild does not inspect it closely. The fee for that inattention is paid promptly and without incident.

Inside: incense and body-musk, velvet furniture going thin at the armrests, a bar stocked with serviceable liquor and one shelf of the genuinely good. The staff are professional and the house is clean. Harren Volke runs a tight operation.

## Harren Volke

The proprietor. A heavyset badger, immaculately dressed, with the unhurried manner of someone who has never needed to raise his voice. Volke has operated the Crescent House for eleven years. He is known in the district as reliable, discreet, and expensive to cross — not because he is violent, but because he knows things about most of the people who could make trouble for him.

He recruits carefully. He looks for people at low points — discharged soldiers, ruined merchants, the recently shamed, anyone standing at a door they can't go back through. He offers them something Isalia's estate does not: transformation without paperwork, without a 30-day consent review, without any record at all. Faster. Cheaper. Clean.

What it is not is safe. The alchemical process Volke uses is competent but operates without the safeguards that licensed practitioners maintain. The consent framework is perfunctory. And the collar that comes with it — crescent sigil, filed nearly smooth — is not a communication tool. It is a mark of ownership, and the person wearing it generally does not fully understand that until some weeks after fitting.

Volke does not consider himself cruel. He considers himself honest about what he is offering. He has simply arranged things so that the honesty arrives slightly too late.

## The Crescent Collar

Volke's collars are modelled closely enough on legitimate manor-issue collars to pass a quick inspection. The crescent sigil is filed down to a near-invisible groove on the inner band. The alchemical conditioning they carry is slow — begins as a mild anxiolytic, shifts over weeks toward compliance erosion. By the time the wearer notices the change in themselves, they typically attribute it to the transformation process rather than the collar.

If an intact Crescent collar were brought to Isalia's estate or examined by a competent alchemist, the modification would be detectable. Volke is aware of this. He has not considered it a significant risk, because the people he collars do not tend to end up at licensed estates.

Until recently.

## The Cellar

Below the main building, past the storage and the staff quarters, a section of the cellar is kept locked. The door is iron, old, set into stonework that predates the building above it by at least a century. A short passage runs beyond it, recently cleared and shored up with new timber. It ends after roughly a hundred feet at a section of original stone — rubble and mortar that Volke had consolidated rather than demolished.

He has not gone further. He knows what is beyond it in the same way he knows most things: through careful listening and the patience to let other people talk. He has never spoken about it to anyone. He keeps the door locked not because he intends to use it, but because an unlocked door is an invitation, and Harren Volke does not issue invitations he doesn't control.

## Active Threads

**The Missing Guard**
Garret Brune — a Valtor garrison regular, three weeks absent from duty. His commander filed paperwork. A colleague places him in the Harbor District on his last known evening. The trail ends at the Crescent House. Volke, if asked, will note that many people pass through his establishment and he cannot be expected to remember all of them.

**Unauthorized Intake**
A client has appeared at Isalia's manor via apparently legitimate referral channels — documentation in order, transformation request standard — but something about them is wrong. Calm in the wrong way. Already partially conditioned. Their collar, on close inspection, has a crescent mark filed smooth. Someone else had them first and then let them go. The question is why.

**The Competitor**
Whispers have reached the estate that someone near the docks offers transformation outside the licensed framework. Cheaper, faster, no waiting period. Isalia's concern is the people subjected to it. Count Valtor's concern, if it reaches him, will be tax revenue and legal jurisdiction. The two concerns are not incompatible, but they will not move at the same pace.

## Connections

- **The Old Temple** — The tunnel beneath the cellar connects (via the sealed lower level) to the pre-town catacombs under the east wing. Neither party is currently aware the other knows about the connection. Volke knows the tunnel exists and where it goes. The clergy know their end is sealed and do not think about what sealed the other end.
- **Isalia's Estate** — Unauthorized collar use, illegal transformation, and possible ongoing client diversion are all grounds for estate involvement, if evidence surfaces.
- **Valtor Keep** — Count Elias Valtor has legal jurisdiction over licensed establishments in Silberbach. The Crescent House holds a legitimate license. Any investigation would require either documented evidence or a pretext Valtor is willing to use.
- **River Traders' Guild** — Volke's informal arrangement with the guild means that any investigation touching the Crescent House will quickly attract guild interest in keeping things quiet.
