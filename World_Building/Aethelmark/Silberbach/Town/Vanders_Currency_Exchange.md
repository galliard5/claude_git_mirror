Vander's Currency Exchange
==========================

Currency exchange business on Merchant's Close, Silberbach. Ground floor operation — Corvel's rooms are above. Corvel uses the back room for client meetings involving significant sums.

description: A currency exchange presenting as a legitimate financial services business. The proprietor is presumably complicit in or at minimum aware of Corvel's use of the premises. The back room serves as a discreet meeting space. Corvel's rooms are accessible from above.

Significance:
Primary location of Corvel's client ledger strongbox — brown leather, brass fittings, unidentified lock. Contains individual ledgers for all clients with transaction records. This is Corvel's primary leverage instrument and the key piece of physical evidence in the investigation.

Address: Merchant's Close, Silberbach. Above the exchange.

Investigation Status:
Constable Ferris has been notified and asked to move on this location before Corvel can relocate the strongbox. Viktor's letter to Ferris described the strongbox and its significance explicitly. Speed is critical — Corvel has had ten days since meeting Oswin, and may have been alerted to heightened scrutiny.

Notes: Voss and Kraemer will need to assess whether Vander's proprietor has independent criminal exposure or was simply a complicit landlord. Either way the premises are central to the investigation.

## Active Conditions
- Active investigation target: Constable Ferris notified, strongbox location known. Corvel may have advance warning. Urgency: immediate — strongbox must be secured before Corvel relocates it.
