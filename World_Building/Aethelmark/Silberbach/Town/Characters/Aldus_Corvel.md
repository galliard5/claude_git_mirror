Aldus Corvel
============

Private financier operating out of Silberbach. Presents as a legitimate financial consultant. Actually operates a money laundering and fraud network, moving funds for clients through complicit textile and trade merchants. Keeps detailed client records as mutual assured destruction.

appearance: Male, approximately fifty. Dresses well. Travels with saddlebags — arrives with them full, leaves with them lighter. Described as pleasant in manner. The pleasantness is the threat.

address: Merchant's Close, Silberbach. Above Vander's Currency Exchange.

Operations:
- Moves embezzled or otherwise problematic funds through complicit merchants who absorb them via inflated invoices
- Known merchant contacts: Hauss (textile, Weaver's Row), Pol (dye import operation)
- Meets clients at Vander's back room or at the village inn south of Silberbach depending on sum involved
- Maintains a locked strongbox at Vander's: brown leather, brass fittings, unidentified lock. Contains individual client ledgers.
- Takes 15% of all funds moved

Known Client List (partial, from Oswin's partial view):
- Oswin Brandt (Steinfeld steward — embezzlement)
- Merchant Hauss (Weaver's Row)
- Merchant Pol (dye imports)
- Guild Master Sorren (Weavers' Guild)
- House Aldenberg (figure seen was larger than all others combined — nature unknown)
- Additional names on list not seen by Oswin

Methods:
- Builds leverage through record-keeping. Each client knows their own file exists. Corvel shows it to them periodically as a reminder.
- States openly that arrangements are "protected at levels that make constabulary involvement inadvisable." Did not elaborate. Did not need to.
- Reached out to Oswin ten days before Viktor's arrival — told him to sit tight. Departed with lighter saddlebags.

Current Status:
- Constable Ferris notified and asked to move on Vander's
- Voss and Kraemer (solicitors) being briefed
- Corvel may have advance warning — he met Oswin ten days before Viktor arrived, suggesting he was alerted when Lukas began pulling ledgers
- The "protection at higher levels" claim is unverified but taken seriously

Threat Level: Significant. The Aldenberg connection and the "higher protection" claim suggest this extends well beyond estate fraud into regional or imperial financial crime.

Trust Level: N/A — active criminal target.

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Unknown in direct confrontation. His methods are leverage and management, not violence. Will not fight if there is another way out.
**Loyalty:** To his own network and continued operation. No personal loyalties.
**Breaking Point:** Loss of the strongbox. Without the records, his leverage disappears and so does his protection.
