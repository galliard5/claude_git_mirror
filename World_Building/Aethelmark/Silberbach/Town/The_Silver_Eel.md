# The Silver Eel

A respectable tavern on the east side of [[Market_Square|Market Square]], walking distance from the docks, the colonnade, and the Old Town gate. Run by Captain Brask Gernholm, a retired river captain who bought the place after his last crossing seventeen years ago and has not been to the docks since.

The Silver Eel is where merchant deals are made over ale, where local gossip circulates at lunch, and where the town crier's announcements are dissected at the bar within an hour of being read. It is the default tavern for travelers of merchant rank who want decent food, fair ale, and conversation that ranges across the river trade rather than around it.

---

## Captain Brask Gernholm

Proprietor. Human, mid-sixties, broad through the shoulders even now, with weathered skin and a beard going from grey to white at the chin. River-country accent, gravelly. Walks with a slight roll he never lost from forty years on barge decks. He keeps the bar himself most nights and lets the kitchen run on its own — he trusts his cook (a stout dwarven woman named Genna whom he has not introduced anyone to in seventeen years and probably never will).

Brask retired from the river after a season that saw him lose two crewmen and nearly his barge. He does not tell the story unless drunk and pressed, and he does not get drunk. What he will say is that the river takes its own and that he stayed lucky longer than most men are entitled to. The Silver Eel was bought with his retirement coin and the careful arithmetic of a man who knew exactly how much he could afford to risk.

He runs the tavern the way he ran his crew — fair, firm, predictable. Disputes that erupt at the tables are settled at the bar with a single sentence and a look. Brask has never thrown a customer out himself. He has, in seventeen years, twice asked Genna to do it. Both times the customer left.

His regulars are mostly former rivermen — men and women who crewed his barges, his fellow captains' barges, the wider river community. Some are old; some are sons and daughters of his old crew taking their parents' usual stools. The conversation runs heavy on river names — places, vessels, people — and a Silberbach-born stranger sitting in earshot will pick up half the regional river lore in an evening.

## Layout

Two storeys plus a cellar.

**Ground floor — common room:**
A large rectangular room, wood-panelled walls, dark beams overhead, a stone hearth on the west wall kept lit through autumn and winter. Twenty tables of various sizes; long benches at the larger tables, chairs at the smaller. The bar runs along the north wall, three barrels deep, with Brask's customary post at the far left where he can see the door.

The walls are covered in river memorabilia — and that is the right word, *covered*, with the density of a place where each object earned its spot. A massive carved wooden eel mounted above the bar, painted silver, given as a retirement gift by Brask's last crew. A weathered ship's wheel on the south wall. Lengths of barge-tackle rope. Three or four old oars. Polished sections of waterwheel. A glass case at the back containing pieces of broken pottery, glassware, and miscellaneous river-found junk that Brask collected over the years and now displays without explanation. A framed map of the Angerap with a dozen pins in it, each pin a place Brask has been; the map is detailed enough that bargemen will sometimes argue with it over their ale.

**Ground floor — back room:**
Off the common room through a door beside the hearth. A private space with one table seating eight, hung with a single wall lantern, no decorations. Bookable for private meetings — merchants finalizing deals, factors negotiating contracts, the occasional sensitive conversation. Brask charges by the hour and does not enter once the door is closed unless summoned. The back room hosts the kind of business that does not get done in the open and does not get done at the Drowned Rat.

**Ground floor — kitchen:**
Genna's domain. Door opens off the bar. Brask does not go in unless he is bringing her something or invited.

**Upper floor — rooms:**
Six rooms for rent, four single, two shared. Plain but clean, beds with proper mattresses (rare in this price range), a chamber pot, a basin, a small window. Travelers of merchant rank stay here. A maid named Ailse turns the rooms each morning.

**Cellar:**
Beer, wine, the cold storage. Lockable. Brask's office sits in a small alcove off the cellar — the household ledgers, the room reservation book, the back room booking schedule, and a strongbox under the floorboards.

## Atmosphere

**Visual:** Warm. Lamplight in the evenings, fire-glow on the stone hearth, the silver eel above the bar catching light in a way that draws every new arrival's eye on first entry. The memorabilia gives the room a layered, slightly cluttered quality that reads more *lived in* than *busy*.

**Scent:** Beer, woodsmoke, roast meat (Genna's specialty rotates by season — venison stew through autumn and winter, river fish in spring and summer, always with bread), pipe tobacco from the regulars who smoke. No food smells reach the upper rooms — the kitchen is well-vented.

**Sound:** A consistent middle volume. Conversation, never quiet, never roaring. The hearth crackling. The occasional barge-crew song late in the evening when one of the older regulars decides it's that kind of night. Genna's kitchen produces audible chopping and pot-clatter that some regulars find comforting.

## Offerings

**Ale:** Three on tap. The house ale is brewed locally by the Brewers' Guild to Brask's specifications — hearty, slightly sweet, the kind of ale that tells you Brask was a barge captain. A darker porter for evenings. A lighter wheat ale for summer.

**Wine:** Two reds, one white, all imported through the river trade. Genna pairs them with the menu without consulting Brask, who has admitted he doesn't really know wine.

**Food:**
- Bread, hot from the bakery next door, always available
- Cheese, hard and soft, from the market
- Genna's daily stew (rotating)
- River fish — fried, baked, or smoked depending on the catch and the season
- Cold platters with sausage, pickles, and bread for travelers in a hurry
- A standing meat-and-bread plate that is the lunch trade's anchor

**Rooms:** Six. The single rooms run a silver per night including breakfast. The shared rooms run half a silver. Long-stay discounts available on request — Brask and Mira from the manor have a standing arrangement for occasional manor guests.

**Back room:** Booking by the hour, three silvers minimum. Discretion is implicit, not advertised. Brask does not gossip about who books the room or what is said there.

## The Regulars (Types)

The Silver Eel's regulars are mostly drawn from Brask's old river community. The GM should name specific individuals as they appear in scenes. Common types:

- **Old riverman, retired** — sixties or older, drinks at the same table every evening, knows every river story Brask has, and an equal number Brask has never heard. Talks if asked, mostly listens. Several of these in rotation.
- **Active barge crew, in port** — younger, twenty to forty, drinks heartier, eats more, leaves earlier (next dawn departure). Different ones each week as the trade rotates.
- **Merchant doing business** — ranges from local Silberbach merchants meeting their factors to traveling merchants stopping for a meal between barge connections. The back room serves these.
- **Guild scribe or clerk** — comes for the bread and cheese at lunch, brings paperwork, sits at a small table by the window. Two or three different regulars at this slot through the week.
- **Manor courier** — Marek among them. Stops in for ale and bread on the return leg of a town errand. Familiar to Brask.
- **Returned-soldier veteran** — maybe two of them in town; one drinks here, one drinks at the Drowned Rat. The Silver Eel's veteran tells stories, the Drowned Rat's does not.
- **Merchant's wife with her own ledger** — runs the family business while her husband travels; meets her counterparties here because the back room is private and the location is respectable.
- **The town crier off duty** — comes for ale after the evening reading. Knows things he doesn't say at the post.

## Daily Rhythm

- **Dawn:** Genna in the kitchen, ale being tapped, rooms being turned over. Brask eats with Genna. Quiet.
- **Mid-morning:** Lunch trade ramps. Bread arrives from next door. First customers — early lunches, merchants on their way to or from morning meetings.
- **Midday:** Peak lunch. Tables full. Genna's stew is the main draw. Brask works the bar himself.
- **Afternoon:** Lull. A few drinkers, a few late lunches. The back room is used most often during this stretch — quieter, fewer ears.
- **Early evening:** Pickup. Regulars beginning to filter in. The fire built up. Genna prepping for the dinner trade.
- **Evening:** Peak. The common room is at full volume. Travelers eating. Locals drinking. The barge-crew songs sometimes start at this hour.
- **Late evening:** The crowd thins to regulars and overnight guests winding down. Brask still at his post. Quiet conversations. The hearth burning lower.
- **Late night:** The Eel closes by the eleventh bell most nights — Brask runs a respectable house. The Drowned Rat's overflow ends up wherever, but not here.

## Rumors & Gossip

The Silver Eel is a node. Its regulars are connected to the river trade, the guilds, the manor, and through Brask's network, to the broader regional commerce. A patient listener at the bar over a single evening will hear:

- Trade conditions on the Angerap (water levels, current bandit reports, dock fees at downstream ports)
- Guild news (recent appointments, disputes between guildmasters, upcoming negotiations)
- Manor rumors (filtered — Brask's regulars know what they hear from the staff who come in for lunch, which is mostly mundane and current)
- Keep news (which petitions were accepted, who has been seen at the Keep recently, occasional darker mutterings about the Count's mood)
- River-folk gossip (who married, who died, whose barge was taken on the lower river)

Brask will not gossip himself. He listens, holds it, and never repeats. Some patrons treat this as confessional and tell him things they would tell no one else. He carries those without apparent burden, and those patrons remain loyal.

## Hooks

- **A back-room booking that goes wrong.** Two parties booked to meet over a deal; one of them does not come; the other makes a scene. Brask wants it handled quietly.
- **A river captain bringing ill news.** A barge taken on the lower river, a name Brask knows. He spends the rest of the night at the bar saying very little. The regulars feel it. A perceptive player notices.
- **Genna's son arrives.** He has not been seen in fifteen years. He is in trouble. Genna asks Brask, who asks the players, because the trouble is the kind he cannot fix without help.
- **A wealthy stranger asks too many questions about the manor.** Comes to Brask first, who deflects without lying. The stranger then approaches the players, who will need to decide what to tell him.
- **The crier off duty has news he hasn't read out.** Something the council has suppressed. He is drinking it down. A player who shares ale with him learns it.
