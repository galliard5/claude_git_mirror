The Drowned Rat
===============

A rougher tavern in the Harbor District, catering to rivermen, dockworkers, and those who prefer discretion.

Description: **Location:** Harbor District, near the docks

**Atmosphere:**
A dimly lit, smoky establishment with low ceilings and a perpetually damp floor. The air smells of river water, cheap tobacco, and spilled ale. The name comes from a stuffed rat in a tiny glass coffin, displayed behind the bar — supposedly a river rat that drowned in a keg of ale.

**Clientele:**
Rivermen, dockworkers, smugglers, and those who prefer not to be noticed. Questions are discouraged; discretion is valued. Fights are common but rarely lethal — the proprietor doesn't like blood on the floor.

**Offerings:**
- Cheap, strong ale
- Simple food (salt fish, hard bread)
- A few rooms for rent — none of them nice
- A cellar that sees more use than the proprietor admits

**Rumors:**
If you need to know something that isn't public knowledge, this is where you come. But be prepared to pay for information — or to trade some of your own.

Location: Harbor District
