The Old Temple
==============

The largest and oldest religious site in Silberbach, located within the Old Town walls.

Overview: The oldest religious site in Silberbach, the Old Temple predates the town walls. Its architecture reflects centuries of additions and renovations, creating a labyrinthine complex of shrines, chapels, and halls dedicated to various deities.

**Nature:**
As a mixed-faith temple, the Old Temple accommodates multiple religions under one roof. Different wings and chambers are dedicated to different deities, and the temple's priests represent various faiths.

**Services:**
- Daily rituals and prayers
- Marriage ceremonies
- Funeral rites
- Religious festivals
- Blessings and consecrations
- Spiritual counsel

## History

The temple's foundations predate the current structure by several centuries. The original site was not a mixed-faith sanctuary — it was built for a single cult whose name the current clergy does not preserve in any public record. The priests call this era "the first foundation" in administrative documents and decline to elaborate. The transition to mixed-faith use occurred gradually over two or three generations, with each new faith absorbing and overwriting the previous one until the original purpose was buried under enough layers to be declared historical rather than dangerous.

The architecture gives it away to anyone who looks carefully. The east wing's proportions are wrong — the ceiling too low, the columns too close together, the stonework a different quarry than everything built after. It was not designed for a congregation. It was designed for something that stood at the center and did not move.

## The Sealed Level

Below the temple's lowest public crypt, behind the foundation altar in the east wing, lies a second level. The current high clergy know it exists. They keep it locked and do not speak of it to junior priests. Two of the younger clergy believe it flooded at some point in the past century. It did not flood.

The catacombs below are dry, intact, and older than anything else in Silberbach. The altar the original cult constructed is still there — bone, silver, and material that does not match any known craft tradition. Temple records note it was inspected once, approximately eighty years ago, by a senior cleric from the regional diocese. His written report was sealed. He did not return to Silberbach.

The altar responds to living flesh. The clergy prefer not to think about what that implies about the last time it was used.

## The Tunnel Connection

A stone passage once ran northeast from the catacombs beneath the old town, connecting the sealed level to the outer district — a procession route, or an escape, depending on who was using it. The temple sealed their end decades ago with mortared stone and no documentation of the work. The other end was sealed more recently, and by someone else: an iron door in the cellar of the building now known as the Crescent House. That door is locked. Harren Volke, the current proprietor, has had the first stretch of passage cleared and shored up on his side. He has not gone further. He does not need to. He simply needs it to remain an option.
