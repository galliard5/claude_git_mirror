Silberbach
==========

A mid-sized river valley town of 5,000-6,000 souls, built on the bend of the Angerap River. Agricultural and textile hub with mining interests.

Overview: Silberbach ("Silver Stream") sits at a sharp bend in the Angerap River, where the water turns from northeast to west. The town began as a fortified settlement within the river's protective curve, its old stone walls now enclosing only the administrative heart — the Count's Keep, the Mayor's Mansion, and the Old Temple. Over centuries, the town spilled beyond those walls like water over a dam, spreading along the riverbank and into the pastoral valley beyond.

Today, Silberbach is a thriving market town of 5,000-6,000 souls, its wealth built on grain, wool, and the silver that gives the town its name. Barges crowd the natural harbor; market stalls fill the New Town squares; and the distant mountains to the north still yield their mineral treasures.

History: **Founding:**
Silberbach began as a strategic river crossing and trading post, its location at the bend of the Angerap offering natural defense. The original walls were built to enclose the inner curve, with the river serving as a moat on two sides.

**Growth:**
As trade along the river flourished, the town outgrew its walls. Rather than expand the fortifications, the settlement spread outward organically — first along the riverbank, then inland toward the fertile plains. The Old Town became the domain of the Count and the wealthy; the New Town became the domain of merchants, craftsmen, and common folk.

**Modern Era:**
The town is now a regional hub for agriculture, textiles, and mining. The old walls stand as a reminder of Silberbach's origins, but the town's true strength lies in its trade connections and the industrious spirit of its people.

Population: 5,000 - 6,000

Region: River Valley

Geography: **Location:** Built on a pronounced bend of the Angerap River, which flows from the northeast before turning sharply west.

**Surroundings:**
- **North:** Distant mountains (half-day's ride) — source of silver, iron, and gemstones
- **East:** Dense forests — timber, hunting, foraging
- **South & West:** Rolling pastoral plains — grain, livestock, wool production

**River:** The Angerap is navigable by shallow-draft barges, connecting Silberbach to larger trade hubs downstream. The bend creates a natural harbor on the inner curve, sheltered from the main current.

Layout: **The Old Town (The Bend)**
The original settlement, now enclosed by ancient stone walls. The river forms a natural moat on two sides. Contains:
- Count's Keep (seat of House Valtor)
- Mayor's Mansion
- Town Hall and council chambers
- Treasury and tax offices
- The main temple complex
- Wealthy merchant residences

**The New Town (Spillover)**
Grown organically beyond the old walls, sprawling along the riverbank and inland. Contains:
- Markets and shops
- Craftsmen's workshops
- Guild halls
- Taverns and inns
- Common housing

**The Harbor District**
Along the inner bend of the river, where the current slows. Warehouses, docks, boat builders, and river traders.

**The Outskirts**
Farms, mills, and estates radiating outward — including Isalia's Manor Kennels & Stables to the southeast.

Economy: **Primary Industries:**
- **Agriculture:** Grain (wheat, rye, barley), vegetables, orchards
- **Textiles:** Wool production, weaving, dyeing, finishing
- **River Trade:** Transport of goods downstream to larger cities

**Secondary Industries:**
- **Mining:** Silver, iron ore, and gemstones from the northern mountains
- **Timber:** Logging from eastern forests
- **Crafts:** Blacksmithing, tanning, brewing, carpentry

**Trade Routes:**
- River barges carry goods west to larger markets
- Caravans travel the valley road connecting to mountain passes
- Weekly market days draw farmers and merchants from surrounding villages

Government: **Secular Authority:**
- **Count Elias Valtor** — Ruler of the River Valley, resides in the Keep
- **Mayor** — Elected by town council, manages civic affairs
- **Town Council** — Wealthy merchants and guild representatives
- **Bailiff** — Enforces laws, collects taxes
- **Constable** — Commands the town watch

**Guild Influence:**
The eleven guilds hold significant political power. Guildmasters often sit on the council, and guild disputes can shape town policy.

**Imperial Connection:**
As part of the Holy Roman Empire equivalent, the town owes taxes and levies to the Emperor. The Count mediates between local interests and imperial demands.

Religion: **Mixed Fantasy Faiths:**
Silberbach is religiously diverse, with multiple temples and shrines coexisting — sometimes peacefully, sometimes with tension.

**Major Temples:**
- **The Old Temple** — Within the Old Town walls, the largest and oldest religious site
- **Shrines** — Scattered throughout the New Town, dedicated to various deities

**Religious Tolerance:**
The town's trade connections have brought diverse faiths. While the Count officially endorses no single religion, he maintains order between competing sects. Religious festivals from different faiths mark the calendar year.

Atmosphere: **By Day:**
The town hums with industry — the clatter of looms from weavers' workshops, the ring of hammers from smithies, the calls of merchants hawking wares in the market squares. Barges unload at the docks; carts rumble through the New Town streets; farmers lead livestock to the weekly markets.

**By Night:**
The Old Town grows quiet behind its walls, but the New Town comes alive with taverns, alehouses, and the glow of hearth-fires. The harbor district never truly sleeps — river trade operates at all hours, and sailors seek entertainment after long voyages.

**Seasonal Rhythms:**
- **Spring:** Planting season, river trade resumes after winter thaw
- **Summer:** Peak trading season, religious festivals, outdoor markets
- **Autumn:** Harvest festivals, wool shearing, preparation for winter
- **Winter:** River trade slows, indoor crafts dominate, social gatherings in taverns and guild halls

Notable Features: **The Silver Bridge**
A stone bridge spanning the Angerap at the narrowest point of the bend, connecting the Old Town to the New Town. Lined with small shops and shrines.

**The Market Square**
The heart of the New Town, where weekly markets draw farmers and merchants from the surrounding countryside. Permanent stalls cluster around a central fountain.

**The Harbor**
A natural harbor formed by the river bend, sheltered from the main current. Docks, warehouses, and the offices of the River Traders' Guild.

**The Old Walls**
Ancient stone fortifications, now more symbolic than defensive. The gates remain, marking the boundary between the Old Town and the New.

**The Mill Race**
A diverted channel of the Angerap powering several grain mills and a fulling mill for textile production.

Transformed in City Service: **Infrastructure Labor:**
The city employs transformed individuals in various infrastructure roles, similar to the guilds and noble houses. Oxen and draft horses for heavy hauling, messenger birds for official communications, and other forms suited to specific tasks. These workers are compensated and treated as municipal employees.

**Punitive Transformation:**
For certain crimes where the penalty involves long-term forced labor, the city courts may sentence convicts to transformation as oxen or donkeys for city labor projects. This is reserved for cases where traditional imprisonment or fines are deemed insufficient — typically repeat offenders or those whose crimes involve shirking honest work. The transformation is temporary in theory (reversion potion available after sentence), but some serve long enough that reversion becomes difficult.

**Town Watch Beasts:**
The town watch maintains a small squad of transformed animals for law enforcement:
- **Hounds and Wolves** — Tracking suspects, searching buildings, patrolling at night
- **Hawks and Other Birds** — Aerial overwatch during large gatherings, tracking fleeing criminals, surveying the town from above

These positions are voluntary, not punitive. Watchmen who choose transformation receive a small bonus in pay, and the city covers their care and maintenance during an initial enlistment term (typically 3-5 years). After the term, they may revert or continue in service.

**Communication Amulets:**
All transformed watch members receive a communication amulet integrated into their collar, allowing them to speak normally despite their animal forms. This is essential for coordinating with human watchmen and reporting findings.

**Military Integration:**
Transformed watch members are fully integrated into the town watch's rank structure. They can earn promotions, take on leadership roles within their specialty, and advance their careers just like any other watchman. A hawk might start as a scout, then be promoted to lead an aerial surveillance unit. A wolf might advance from tracker to senior handler. This integration ensures they have a clear career path and are valued as professionals rather than mere tools.

**Distinctive Collars:**
The transformed guards wear distinctive collars marking them as city property. They're well-treated and considered valuable assets, though their existence makes some citizens uneasy about how easily the watch can track them.
