Tantalus' Tease
===============

A unique item of Fey origin. Currently worn by Isalia Kreiger. There is no known second example.

---

## Description

A band of steel, finer than it looks — the metal has a quality that sits just outside what a smith could produce conventionally, smooth in a way that suggests it was never forged so much as arrived at. When worn it seats against the body with an exactness that removes any awareness of it as a separate object. The wearer stops feeling it as something they're wearing within hours. It becomes part of the body's landscape rather than an addition to it.

This is not incidental. The band fuses permanently on contact with the wearer. There is no clasp, no seam, no means of physical removal after bonding. It becomes as much a part of the wearer as flesh.

---

## Properties

**Full Sensation, No Release:**
The band permits everything short of orgasm — arousal, pleasure, sensitivity, the full accumulation of sensation without any ceiling. What it removes is the resolution. The body builds and builds with complete fidelity, and the release simply does not arrive. There is no pain, no numbness, no dulling of what comes before. The absence is surgical and total.

**Permanent Fusion:**
Bonds irreversibly to the first person who wears it. Physical removal is not possible without damage to the wearer. Whether the enchantment could be dissolved by sufficiently advanced magic is unknown — no attempt has been made. The item has shown no sign of weakening or shifting in the time Isalia has worn it.

**Fey Enchantment:**
The magic is identifiably Fey in character — more organic than constructed, resistant to conventional analysis. Elowen has examined it at Isalia's request and can describe what it does with precision but cannot fully map how. The enchantment does not behave like layered spellwork. It behaves like something that grew into its function. This is what makes duplication difficult: there is no blueprint to copy, only an effect to reverse-engineer.

---

## Origin

The item was given to Isalia by a Fey Prince. She knows who placed it on her. She does not know where it came from before that, whether it was made for her specifically, or whether others have worn it before. The Fey Prince has not volunteered this information and she has not asked in terms that produced an answer.

The name — Tantalus' Tease — is Isalia's own. Whether the Fey Prince called it anything is not recorded.

---

## Current Status

Isalia wears it. She has worn it long enough that it is simply a condition of her existence rather than an imposition — though what that means experientially is her own business and not documented here.

She has raised, more than once, the question of whether the item could be duplicated. The practical interest is evident: an item that does exactly this to a willing wearer would be commercially significant. The obstacle is the Fey enchantment — Elowen's assessment is that reverse-engineering the effect into a reproducible form would require either a deep understanding of Fey magic that neither she nor Alric currently possesses, or access to whoever made the original. Neither is straightforwardly available.

The question is open. It has not been abandoned.
