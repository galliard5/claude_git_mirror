# Westgate

A timber-and-stone toll fort perched in the rolling hills of the western March, marking the formal border between Silberbach territory and the realm of Cendrel. The structure is modest but defensible—squat towers at its corners, a gatehouse straddling the main road, administrative quarters attached.

## Function

Travelers and merchants passing between the March and Cendrel must stop here to pay toll, declare goods, and pass inspection. The rate is standardized but flexible enough that a persuasive merchant can negotiate. The guards are professional but not unfriendly; they've seen everything.

## Geography

Built in open hilly grassland with good sight lines for defense. A small but dense hilly forest rises on the eastern side—useful for timber, game, and concealment. The Valley Road arrives from the south (toward Graudorf) and continues westward into Cendrel territory.

## Authority

Technically under joint control—Silberbach and Cendrel each maintain a garrison commander, and decisions about policy require both to agree. In practice, they cooperate civilly enough. Disputes are rare; commerce flows through without much friction.

## Character

Businesslike, neither welcoming nor hostile. A place of transaction. Merchants swap rumors and gossip here. It's neutral ground, in a sense—criminals fleeing one realm might find temporary sanctuary by crossing to the other, though both sides discourage harboring fugitives.
