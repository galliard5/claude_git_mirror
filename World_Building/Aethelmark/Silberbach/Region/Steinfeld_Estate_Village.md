# Steinfeld Estate Village (Steindorf)

The village known formally as Steindorf at the foot of the [[House_Steinfeld|Steinfeld]] estate's lands. Population approximately 220. One day's ride south of Silberbach via the Pastoral Road branch that breaks off the Valley Road at Kaelen's Ford and continues southeast into Steinfeld territory.

A working estate village. The kind of settlement that exists because the estate exists, that runs on the rhythm of the agricultural year, and whose residents have been bonded to Steinfeld lands for generations on terms that are inherited rather than negotiated. Recent events at the estate (Viktor Steinfeld's pregnancy, the Oswin Brandt embezzlement exposure, the broader investigation reach into local merchant networks) have shaken the village's previously-stable register.

---

## Layout

The village sits on the Pastoral Road branch, with the Steinfeld estate's main house visible on the slight rise to the south. The settlement is small enough to walk end-to-end in fifteen minutes.

**The Village Center:**
- The village green with its central well
- The Steindorf chapel-house — small, traditional, the village's social anchor
- The estate's tithe-barn (where the village's grain and produce contributions to the estate are collected)
- The Stone Boar — the village's only inn, run by a human family
- A small smithy (run by a master smith with a standing contract for estate ironwork)
- The village's three permanent market stalls (vegetables, bread, basic provisions)

**The Lanes:**
- Three short residential lanes branching from the green
- Cottages in stone-and-thatch, modest but generally well-maintained
- The village's two larger working compounds — one for the estate's stables (where Hannah Voss historically operated), one for the wool-and-cheese processing

**The Estate Approach:**
- The road that climbs the gentle rise from the village to the Steinfeld manor house
- Lined with the homes of the senior estate staff (those who do not live in the manor itself)
- The estate's outer gate is a quarter-mile up this road

## Economy and Daily Life

**Bonded farming.** The village's working population is primarily bonded to the Steinfeld estate — meaning they farm estate land under inherited tenure, owe a portion of their produce in tithe, and have customary rights and obligations that have been refined across generations. The arrangement has been functional for the families involved across multiple generations; the slow erosion of the broader bonded-farming system at the regional level has not yet reached crisis point at Steinfeld.

**Estate employment.** Approximately a quarter of the working-age population draws income from direct estate employment — household staff, stablehands, gardeners, the broader manor support apparatus. Wages are paid by Lukas Steinfeld in his current administrative role (during Viktor's pregnancy).

**Pastoral mix.** Sheep, cattle (a small herd), and a notable horse-breeding operation (under Hannah Voss historically). Light grain production. Cheese, mutton, occasional beef sold up to Silberbach via the pastoral road.

**Limited commerce.** The village is not a regional trading center. Most outside goods come up from Silberbach via Mira Voss's regular runs (no relation to Hannah; common name) or via the periodic visits of Silberbach merchants who handle estate accounts. The village has its own internal commerce — the smithy, the small market — but no external commercial reach.

---

## The Stone Boar

The village's only inn. Three rooms, plain food, run by the Renner family — humans, two generations. Doubles as the village's social center; the taproom is the place where village news circulates, where bonded-farmer disputes are settled informally before they reach the estate steward, where travelers stopping for a meal find themselves the center of friendly attention.

The Renner family is currently navigating its own quiet difficulty: their elder son was implicated peripherally in the Oswin Brandt embezzlement investigation (he had handled some of the falsified delivery documentation as a junior estate clerk before transferring to a Silberbach merchant). The investigation has not formally reached him; the family's anxiety is real and growing. They have not asked anyone for help.

---

## Politics and Atmosphere

Steindorf is, like Graudorf, politically inert at the regional level — the village does not have its own opinions on the wider political map and would not be asked. Internal politics are entirely about the estate: Viktor's pregnancy, Lukas's current administration, the slow recovery from Hannah Voss's transfer to Isalia's manor (which the village understands incompletely and discusses in lowered voices), the Oswin Brandt situation (which the village understands more clearly and discusses with quiet anger).

The villagers' identification with the estate is strong but uncomplicated — there is no Cendrelais sympathy, no reformist movement, no investigation-related discomfort *beyond* the Renner family's specific situation. The village treats the estate's troubles as its own.

The Greymarch stallion that sired Viktor's foal is known in the village. Bets are quietly being placed on the foal's outcome; the village is invested in the line continuing.

## In-Between Texture

**The Pastoral Road branch.** Steindorf sits on this branch, which connects to the Valley Road at Kaelen's Ford and continues onward to outlying settlements before terminating in scattered farmsteads. Lightly trafficked; reliable in good weather; muddy in spring.

**Banditry:** Minimal. The pastoral country is too thinly trafficked to attract opportunists, and the estate's own protective shadow extends to the village.

**The forest:** The Waldheim forest's southwestern fringe is approximately a half-day's ride east of Steindorf. The contested forest-pasture borderlands between Waldheim and Steinfeld are along this fringe. Local shepherds know exactly where the contested stretches are and avoid them when Waldheim's rangers are visible.

**Religious presence:** The Steindorf chapel-house serves the village's mixed-faith population with a notably traditional emphasis (more conservative than Silberbach's Old Temple, more comfortable with single-tradition observance). The chaplain is a human man named Father Bernhardt who has held the post for twenty-five years and is the closest thing the village has to a community leader outside the estate authority.

**Climate and seasons:** Mild. Temperate. The pastoral country is comfortable across most of the year; spring rains soften the roads; autumn brings the harvest; winter is cold but not severe.

## Notable Figures

- **The Renner family** — proprietors of the Stone Boar; the elder son's investigation peripheral exposure is the family's quiet crisis
- **Father Bernhardt** — village chaplain; conservative; respected; not flexible
- **Master Smith Halsten** *(no relation to Master Yorik Halsten of the Tanners Guild; common name)* — village smith; competent, friendly, has a standing contract with the estate
- **The Vorster family** — the senior bonded-farming family; would speak for the village if needed
- **Old Pell** *(distinct from Pell the manor groom; common name)* — village wisewoman; herbalist; has delivered most of the village's children for thirty years

## Hooks

- **The Renner son's situation surfaces.** Investigation pressure reaches the village. The family must decide whether to confess preemptively, hire defense, or hope the matter passes.
- **A bonded-farming dispute reaches Lukas's office.** A tenant claims the tithe assessment is unfair this season. The dispute is about more than the tithe; the underlying issue is the estate's reduced administrative capacity during Viktor's pregnancy.
- **A Waldheim ranger appears in the village.** Pursuing a question about an incident at the contested borderlands. The villagers know more than they want to share.
- **A traveling merchant from Silberbach asks the wrong questions.** Possibly someone from the broader Corvel investigation reach, possibly someone with their own agenda. Their interest is in Hannah Voss specifically.
- **The foal arrives.** Viktor's pregnancy concludes; the village's quiet betting pool resolves; the estate's celebration draws the wider noble interest.
