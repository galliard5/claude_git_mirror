# Master Henrick Cael

Human, male, early sixties. Master of the Farmers Guild for nine years. Master farmer in the formal sense — one of the relatively few freehold farmers in the river valley, holding his family's freehold in the southwestern hills. Splits his time between Silberbach (when guild business requires it) and the freehold (which his sons run during his absences).

---

## Appearance

Average height, the stocky build of a farmer who still works his own fields when at home. Iron-grey hair cut short for practicality. Weathered face — the deep tan and crow's feet of fifty years outdoors. Hands that show the work: callused, joint-thickened, scarred at the knuckles, the nails worn short and cleaned only adequately.

Wears the master's robe of wheat-gold wool with brown trim at formal functions and council meetings. Works in plain wool tunics and trousers when at the freehold. The master's pendant of office (the wheat-and-crook crest in bronze) on a leather thong, which Cael chose deliberately when he took the chair — leather was the right material for a farmer's master, in his view.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Practical mediation.**
The Farmers Guild is largely a structure for managing disputes — among bonded farmers, between farmers and noble landlords, between farmers and the market. Cael's tenure has been characterised by competent dispute resolution and steady coordination. He listens completely. He decides clearly. He does not waste time. Both parties to a dispute typically come away believing the resolution was fair, even when they did not get what they wanted.

**Flaw: Conservative on guild structural matters.**
Has resisted suggestions to reform the bonded-farmer voting structure, the apprenticeship framework, the relationship with the noble landlords. The conservative instinct has held the guild's operational stability — Cael's predecessor pushed harder on structural reform and produced internal turmoil that took years to settle. But the backlog of unaddressed issues is real and growing, and Cael's wardens have begun, gently, to suggest that the next Master will inherit problems Cael could have addressed.

---

## Background

Born to the Cael freehold family in the southwestern hills, half a day's ride west of Silberbach. Eldest son. Trained on the freehold from boyhood. Married Ema — daughter of a neighbouring farming family — at twenty-five in a match that was practical and became affectionate. Six children, all grown:

- Halen (eldest son) — runs the freehold alongside Cael, will inherit
- Mira (eldest daughter) — married into a smaller farming family, lives a few hours away
- Bren (middle son) — left the trade, became a journeyman carpenter (in [[Master_Joren_Vesker|Vesker]]'s shop, in fact); the only Cael not in farming
- Aldric (third son) — works the freehold under Halen; will inherit a smaller subdivision when Cael is gone
- Lera (younger daughter) — married a Silberbach merchant family, lives in the town
- Per (youngest son) — twenty-three, still working the freehold, considering whether to stay

Inherited the freehold at thirty-eight at his father's death. Took on operational warden role at fifty (grain warden) for six years. Stood for Master at fifty-three after the previous Master's retirement and won by a narrow margin. Has held the chair for nine years.

Ema is alive and well; lives at the freehold; comes to Silberbach perhaps once a year for the Harvest Fair. Cael is grateful for her every day.

---

## Voice

Slow river-country accent, warm vowels, the cadence of a man who has spent his life talking to other farmers and is patient with explaining things. Drops into specific agricultural register when needed and is comfortable in plain register when appropriate.

**Default Conversational Move: Restate the dispute.**
Cael repeats back what each party has said before he responds. The repetition is partly his teaching habit (he has trained many young farmers) and mostly his mediation method — when each party hears their own position said back to them, they often find it easier to hear the other party's.

**Quirks:**
- Carries a small clay vial of soil from the freehold in a pouch — habit from his father, never explained
- Says "Ema would like that" or "Ema would not like that" to himself when small domestic things come up; it is his anchor
- Eats simply by preference — bread, hard cheese, occasional mutton, ale
- Visits the Farmers' House refectory rather than dining at noble or merchant tables, even when invited

## Relationships

**Ema (his wife)** — Thirty-seven years. The center. Runs the freehold's domestic life and increasingly the freehold's operational life as Cael spends more time in Silberbach. Their working partnership is the foundation of his life.

**The six children** — All settled, all visited as often as he can manage. Closest to Halen (operational successor) and to Bren (the carpenter, the unusual one).

**The three wardens** — Working partnership. Grain warden is competent; pastoral warden is excellent; commercial warden is the youngest of the three and the most ambitious. All push him on structural reform; he holds the line.

**[[Master_Korin_Halberd|Master Halberd]]** — Carpenters Guild Master. Cael is friendly with Halberd. Their children's lives connect (Bren works in Vesker's shop, which is Halberd's guild). They confer at council more easily than most master pairs.

**Master Heinrek Vohlmann (Brewers)** — Working professional respect. The Farmers Guild supplies the Brewers' barley. Cael and Vohlmann have a steady contract relationship and a steady cordial respect.

**Master Edda Reinhart (Tanners)** — Cordial professional respect. Cael voted for her at her election; the bonded-farmer voting bloc was important to her win.

**The bonded farmers** — His base. Cael sees them at the Farmers' House continuously; they bring disputes; they bring requests; they bring the texture of the regional pastoral economy to him daily.

**The noble landlords** — Counterparties. Cael has worked with most of the regional houses at the contract level. He is on cordial terms with Kreiger's stewards, Waldheim's, and Grauwald's; more guarded with Valtor's; coolly professional with Aldenberg's.

**[[Tob]]** — Manor cook. Cael does not know him well personally but has signed the manor feed contracts annually for nine years and respects the precision of Tob's specifications.

---

## Independent Goal

Hand the guild on to a successor who can do the structural work he has not done. He does not yet know who. The wardens are candidates; one of the more politically active master farmers from the western valley is a candidate; he has time to decide.

Beyond the guild, see the freehold continue under Halen and the rest of the family. Eventually retire to the freehold full-time. Eventually walk the south fields with Ema in the morning and not have to be anywhere else.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Average and weathered. Cael has handled hostile noble clients, angry farmers in disputes, the occasional cattle-thief case where authority had to be visible. Not a fighter; competent in the working sense.
**Loyalty:** To Ema. To the freehold. To the children. To the guild's operational stability.
**Breaking Point:** A noble landlord causing real harm to bonded farmers under his protection without legal cover. Cael would force the issue, despite his structural conservatism, because the protection of his bonded members is the actual job.

## Daily Routine (in Silberbach)

- **Pre-dawn:** Up. Reads correspondence from the freehold.
- **Mid-morning:** Office work at the Farmers' House. Disputes heard in the dispute resolution chamber when active.
- **Midday:** Refectory.
- **Afternoon:** Variable. Council meetings. Master-to-master meetings. Contract reviews.
- **Late afternoon:** Reconciliation with the wardens.
- **Evening:** Quiet. Reads.
- **Night:** Sleeps in his quarters at the Farmers' House.

When at the freehold (a third of his time): Up before dawn, in the fields with Halen until midday, midday with Ema, afternoon back in the fields, evening with the family. The most contented stretches of his life are the freehold weeks.
