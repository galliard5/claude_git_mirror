# Farmers Guild

The Farmers Guild of Silberbach. Coordinates the agricultural operations of the river valley — the smallholders, the pastoral families, and the few larger working estates that are not part of noble holdings. Supplies the town's foodstuffs through the market and through standing contracts, manages the seasonal harvest cycles, runs the market stalls' agricultural side, and provides the feed contracts that supply the manor's canine residents, the stables, and the surrogate staff.

The largest guild by membership and the smallest by political weight per member. Most farmers are bonded to land they do not own and operate within obligations to noble landlords; the guild's structure is largely about coordination, market access, and dispute resolution rather than producer power.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A sheaf of wheat crossed with a shepherd's crook over a furrow
**Colors:** Wheat-gold and earth-brown
**Motto:** "By the Season, the Bread"
**Hall:** The Farmers' House — a large but modest building on the south side of the New Town, near Market Square. Houses the guild's central offices, the dispute resolution chamber, the market clerk's coordinating post, and a substantial storeroom for shared seed stock and tools.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Henrick Cael (see below)
**Wardens:** Three — one for grain operations, one for pastoral operations, one for market and commerce
**Membership tiers:**
- *Master farmers* — those who own their own land or hold full freehold tenure; relatively few in number, structurally important, full voting rights
- *Bonded farmers* — those who farm under noble or merchant landlord obligation; the bulk of the membership; voting on operational matters
- *Pastoral families* — sheep, cattle, goat operations; their own subset of membership with internal coordination
- *Apprentices* — children of bonded families typically; the trade is largely transmitted within families

The guild does not have the typical apprentice-journeyman-master progression because farming knowledge is mostly inherited rather than formally taught. The structure adapts: "apprentices" in the Farmers Guild are usually young farmers learning under their own families with guild-recognised standing.

---

## What They Coordinate

**Grain operations:**
- Wheat, barley, rye, oats — the staple grains of the valley
- Seasonal cycles coordinated through the guild's grain warden
- Standing contracts to mills, bakeries, brewers (the Brewers Guild's barley supply)

**Pastoral operations:**
- Sheep (the largest pastoral category — wool plus mutton/lamb), cattle (smaller-scale, mostly dairy with some beef), goats, poultry
- The Grauwald barony is the largest pastoral operation in the region but operates outside the Farmers Guild's authority (noble holding); the guild coordinates with Grauwald rather than commanding it
- Hay production for the region's stables, including the manor's

**Market operations:**
- Coordination with [[Market_Square]]'s clerk Heidrun on stall allocation for agricultural produce
- Supply of the permanent stalls (vegetables, fruit, eggs, cheese, smoked and salted meat)
- Seasonal markets (the Wool Market, the Harvest Fair, the Winter Market) co-managed with the relevant other guilds

**Specialty:**
- Herbal and medicinal plants — small specialty grown by a handful of families, supplied to the manor's apothecary and the Old Temple's clergy
- Honey — a few apiarist families, modest production
- Fish — technically falls under River Traders for trade purposes, but the riverside subsistence fisheries are coordinated through the Farmers Guild

---

## Manor Relationship

The manor is one of the guild's most predictable contracts:

- **Canine resident feed** — bulk grain and meat supply weighted to specific recipes (Tob handles the recipes; the guild handles the bulk); the guild has been running this contract since the manor's expansion seven years ago
- **Stable hay** — seasonal supply, coordinated through Sable's people; the pastoral warden handles this
- **Surrogate staff feed** — higher-quality grain and dairy for the surrogate group; small volume, high standards
- **Seasonal produce** — vegetables and fruit through the market channel rather than direct contract; the manor's kitchen sends day-runners to the market
- **Specialty herbs** — for Alric's apothecary; coordinated with the herb-stall family at Market Square (whose stall is Annalise's, which connects through Drest)

The Farmers Guild's relationship with the manor is steady and largely operational. There is little political weight to it on either side — the contracts are just what they are.

## Master Henrick Cael

Current Master. Human, male, early sixties, master farmer (one of the few freehold farmers in the valley). Family has held its land for five generations — a small but solid freehold operation in the southwestern hills. Master for nine years.

**Appearance:** Average height, the stocky build of a farmer who still works his own fields. Iron-grey hair cut short, weathered face, hands that show fifty years of agricultural labor. Wears the master's robe of wheat-gold wool with brown trim at formal functions; works in plain wool tunics and trousers most other times. The Master's pendant (the wheat-and-crook crest in bronze) on a leather thong.

**Personality:**
- *Core trait:* Practical mediation. The Farmers Guild is largely a structure for managing disputes among bonded farmers, between farmers and landlords, between farmers and the market. Cael's tenure has been characterised by competent dispute resolution and steady coordination. He listens. He decides. He does not waste time.
- *Flaw:* Conservative on guild structural matters. Has resisted suggestions to reform the bonded-farmer voting structure, the apprentice framework, the relationship with the noble landlords. The conservative instinct has held the guild's operational stability but has also accumulated a backlog of structural reforms that someone, eventually, will have to address.

**Background:** Born to the Cael freehold family. Inherited at thirty-eight at his father's death. Married Ema (a farming family neighbor) at twenty-five; six children, most still in the trade. Has been Master for nine years. Splits his time between the Farmers' House in Silberbach and the family freehold a half-day's ride west.

**Voice:** Slow river-country accent, warm vowels, the cadence of a man who has spent his life talking to other farmers and is patient with explaining things. Drops into specific agricultural register when needed.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** Highest membership, lowest political weight per member. Cael's voice at the masters' council carries the structural weight of farming's importance to the regional economy but he rarely pushes hard on inter-guild politics. The Farmers Guild is, by his preference, the steady background of the regional economy rather than a political actor.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral. Cael will not align the guild with any noble faction. The bonded farmers' relationships with their landlords vary widely; the guild cannot take a position without breaking with members on one side or the other.

**Manor relationship:** Steady contract, mutually respected. Treated as a respected client.

**Investigations:** None.

---

## Hooks

- **A bonded-farmer dispute reaches Cael's chamber.** A farmer claims his landlord has demanded labor obligations beyond what is contracted. The landlord is a noble. The dispute is politically delicate.
- **A grain shortage looms.** A poor spring portends a thin harvest. The guild has to decide how to handle the supply contracts that will not be fulfillable.
- **A pastoral family loses its flock to disease.** Guild solidarity reserves are insufficient to fully restore them. Cael has to decide on partial relief.
- **The manor wants a new feed specification.** Tob has refined his recipes again. The guild has to source unusual grains in unusual proportions.
- **The Wool Market falls into a guild dispute.** Weavers, Fullers, and Farmers all have stakes. Cael must coordinate.
