# Fullers and Dyers Guild

The Fullers and Dyers Guild of Silberbach. Operates the dye works on the south bank of the Angerap (downwind of the town for excellent reasons) and the fulling shops in the New Town. Takes raw cloth from the Weavers Guild and finishes it for the regional textile trade through fulling (cleaning and thickening), dyeing (color application), and final finishing.

The dye works produces the persistent industrial smell that travelers arriving by river or by the southern road notice immediately. The smell is the guild's most public characteristic. The guild's members are quietly proud of it.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A fuller's hammer crossed with a dyer's stirring-stick over a colored cloth bolt
**Colors:** Indigo and madder-red (the two most common dyes)
**Motto:** "By Water and Color"
**Hall:** The Dyers' House — a stone building near the dye works, structurally robust because the dye works' chemistry has historically been hard on the surrounding buildings.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Albrecht Halren, dwarven, mid-sixties, master dyer. Has held the chair for seven years.

**Wardens:** Two — one for fulling operations, one for dyeing operations.

**Notable masters:**
- *Master Halren himself* — runs the largest dye operation; specialises in indigo and the high-end blue dyes that command premium prices
- *Master Karsten, fuller* — head of the principal fulling operation in the New Town; supplies finished cloth to merchants for downstream sale
- *Master Yulen, dyer* — specialises in red dyes from madder root; runs the second-largest dye operation; politically more active than Halren and a possible eventual successor

The guild and its workers exist with a particular relationship to the dye works' public health implications. Working in the dye yards is not as immediately dangerous as the tanning yards but the long-term health effects are real — workers experience higher rates of certain skin conditions, occasional respiratory issues, and shorter average working lifespans. The guild has internal conventions for caring for retired workers and has built up a small mutual-aid fund over generations.

---

## What They Do

- *Fulling* — the cleaning and thickening of raw cloth via mechanical processing in the fulling mills along the millrace
- *Dyeing* — color application through the major dye categories (indigo for blues, madder for reds, woad for cheaper blues, weld and other plants for yellows, walnut and oak gall for browns and blacks, the more expensive specialty dyes for premium work)
- *Finishing* — the final treatment of cloth for resale, including napping, calendering, and pressing
- *Specialty work* — premium dyed cloth for noble households and ceremonial use; the manor purchases specific dyed goods for the Garden Wing and for staff garments through this channel

---

## Manor Relationship

Modest but steady. The manor purchases finished dyed cloth through the Weavers Guild commercial channel; the Fullers and Dyers see the manor's purchases as downstream activity rather than direct contracting. Specific manor-favored colors include the deep wine reds and the manor's house-color charcoal greys, both of which Halren's operation supplies.

No direct manor-side counterpart at the guild's commercial level; the relationship runs through the broader textile commerce.

---

## Politics & Position

Mid-tier political weight. The guild is regionally important for textile finishing but does not have political reach beyond its commercial role. Master Halren has been a quiet voice at the masters' council; his colleague Master Yulen has been more vocal and is positioned for the eventual succession.

Officially neutral on regional politics. The dye works' clients include all the major regional houses and the imperial supply chain.

No current investigation involvement.

---

## Hooks

- **A specific dye color falls out of supply.** A ship from a downstream city carrying premium indigo is lost; the guild faces a six-month gap on premium blue work and must decide which contracts get priority.
- **A worker dies from chronic exposure.** Routine, regrettably; but this particular worker had been speaking with [[Constable_Ferris]] about other matters before his death. Coincidence?
- **Master Yulen pushes for the succession.** Halren is still in good health but Yulen is positioning openly. Internal politics.
- **A new dye chemistry promises better blues at lower cost.** A dyer-master has been experimenting. The guild has to decide whether to scale up the technique or suppress it (it threatens existing contracts).
- **The dye works' downwind effect reaches a politically inconvenient nose.** A noble visitor takes offense; the council must respond.
