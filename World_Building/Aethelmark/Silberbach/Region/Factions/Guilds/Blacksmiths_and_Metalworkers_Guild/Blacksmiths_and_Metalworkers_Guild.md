# Blacksmiths and Metalworkers Guild

The Blacksmiths and Metalworkers Guild of Silberbach. Operates the smithies scattered through the New Town, the larger forge complex along the millrace, and the small specialty metalwork shops that handle copper, tin, and brass. Handles the daily metalwork of the town — horseshoes, tools, hardware, ironmongery — plus the more complex work that does not rise to the premium specialty of [[House_Aldenberg|House Aldenberg]]'s mountain forges.

Structurally downstream of Aldenberg. The premium steel for blades, plate, and military equipment comes from the Aldenberg forges; the Silberbach Blacksmiths Guild handles everything else and serves as the local fabricator and finisher when Aldenberg-supplied steel needs working into final form.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A hammer over an anvil with three sparks rising
**Colors:** Iron-black and forge-red
**Motto:** "By Heat and Hammer"
**Hall:** The Smiths' House — a substantial stone building near the millrace forges, with the guild's office space, dispute chamber, and a small testing-forge that masters use for inspection samples.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Hendrik Vorn, human, mid-fifties, master blacksmith. Has held the chair for five years.

**Wardens:** Two — one for ironwork (blacksmithing proper), one for finer metalwork (copper, tin, brass, occasional silver).

**Notable masters:**
- *Master Vorn himself* — runs the principal millrace forge; supplies the keep's daily ironwork and the manor's collar reinforcements (the iron components mounted to the leather foundations Halsten produces)
- *Master Brigid, ironwright* — runs the second-largest forge, specialised in agricultural and household ironwork
- *Master Rikkard, finer metals* — handles copper and brass work, including the Brewers Guild's kettle maintenance and the Old Temple's ceremonial pieces

---

## What They Make

- *Daily ironwork* — horseshoes, tools, hardware, hinges, latches, the metal infrastructure of every building and every working operation
- *Reinforcement and assembly* — taking Aldenberg-supplied premium steel and working it into final form (blades hilted and hilted, plate fitted and adjusted)
- *Manor collar reinforcements* — the iron components that mount to Halsten's leather collar foundations; the manor buys these in lots matched to the leather lots
- *Keep ironwork* — daily standing contract for the Count's facilities; gates, hinges, weapons-rack hardware, the hundred small iron components that a fortified building needs replaced regularly
- *Copper and brass* — kettles for brewers, kitchen ware for households of rank, ceremonial pieces for the temple, the small specialty work that the finer-metals warden coordinates

---

## Manor Relationship

The manor's collar reinforcement contract is the guild's largest single specialty commission and is handled directly by Master Vorn's forge. The contract follows the same lot structure as the Tanners and Leatherworkers' collar foundation contract — when the leather lots are produced, the iron reinforcements are produced in matched lots and the two are delivered to the manor's enchanting workshop together. Coordination between Vorn's forge and Halsten's shop is informal but reliable; they have synchronised their lot schedules over the years.

Beyond the collar work, the manor purchases standard ironwork through the broader guild commercial channel — gate hardware for the kennel expansions, kitchen ironware for Tob's ovens, the everyday metal infrastructure.

---

## Politics & Position

Mid-tier political weight. The guild is essential to daily commerce but is structurally subordinate to Aldenberg on premium work, which limits its political reach. Master Vorn is competent and respected at the masters' council without being dominant.

Officially neutral on regional politics. The guild ships ironwork to every house — Valtor's keep most prominently, Kreiger's estate, Kaelen's hall, the rest. Political alignment would forfeit revenue.

The relationship with Aldenberg is the guild's most politically meaningful external dynamic. Aldenberg's forges and the Silberbach Blacksmiths cooperate operationally and could in principle compete commercially; both sides have, by long convention, treated their work as complementary rather than competitive. The convention has held for generations.

No current investigation involvement.

## Hooks

- **An Aldenberg shipment of premium steel arrives flawed.** Master Vorn's forge cannot complete a noble commission to specification because the upstream material is bad. Diplomatic and commercial implications.
- **A keep ironwork failure.** A gate hinge gives way at an inconvenient moment. The keep's chamberlain wants to know why. Vorn's forge is responsible.
- **A new master applicant from outside the guild's traditional lines.** A capable smith from a smaller settlement seeks master rank in Silberbach. Internal politics about admission.
- **The collar reinforcement contract is renegotiated.** Mira proposes adjusted terms; Vorn's forge has cost pressures; the negotiation is more complicated than usual.
- **A counterfeit silver mark appears on local coinage.** Suspicion falls on the finer-metals masters. Master Rikkard must defend his shops.
