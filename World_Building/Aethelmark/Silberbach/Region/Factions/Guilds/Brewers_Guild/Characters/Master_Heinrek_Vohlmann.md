# Master Heinrek Vohlmann

Dwarven, male, mid-sixties. Master Brewer of the Silberbach Brewers Guild for the past fourteen years. The senior figure in the regional brewing trade. Brews [[Brask_Gernholm|Brask]]'s Silver Eel reserve himself, which is unusual for a Master and which the regulars at the Eel notice without ever being told.

---

## Appearance

Average dwarven height — four feet eight — broad through the shoulders even now, with the heavy forearms of a man who still works the mash paddle when he wants the feel of a batch. Thick grey beard kept close-trimmed for brewhouse hygiene; close-cut grey hair around the bald top of his head. Dark eyes, set deep, miss very little. A network of small scars across the back of his left hand from forty years of wort-burns.

Wears the guild's master-brewer apron over plain brown wool when at the brewhouse — the apron is heavily-worn leather with copper rivets, a working garment. Wears the master's robe of green wool with copper trim at formal guild functions and the Master's chain of office (a copper kettle pendant on a heavy chain) at every guild masters' council meeting. The chain is on a peg by his office door at all other times.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Operational seriousness.**
Vohlmann runs the guild as a working operation. Standards are enforced, contracts are honored, errors are caught early, and political theatre is minimized. The dwarven temperament finds reliability more interesting than charm. He has built the Brewers' reputation over fourteen years on the quality of his enforcement, and the guild has the cleanest books and the most consistent product among the eleven major guilds.

**Flaw: Underweights political signaling.**
Vohlmann handles substantive problems efficiently and does not always recognise when a situation calls for visible posture as well as actual competence. The discrepancy episode at the manor was a case in point — his response was correct (internal investigation, dismissal of the dishonest assistant, restitution to the manor) but his response was also entirely private. A more political Master would have made some public gesture of supporting the manor's revised policy. Vohlmann did not. The two operational wardens, between them, occasionally push him toward visibility on matters where the substance alone is not the whole of what is needed. He defers to them when they are insistent.

---

## Background

Born to a brewing family in the southern dwarven settlements — the kind of dwarven community where every household ran its own small brewhouse and the trade ran in the family for generations. His father was a journeyman in a regional guild; his mother managed the household's brewing operation. Vohlmann learned mash work before he could properly read.

Came to Silberbach at twenty-four to apprentice under Master Olbrek Reinhardt, then the senior master brewer of the central brewhouse. Reinhardt was a hard apprentice-master and a fair one. Vohlmann completed his apprenticeship at thirty-one and married Reinhardt's daughter, Brenna, in the same year. Brenna was a brewer in her own right; the marriage was a working partnership as well as a personal one.

They had one son, Karel, born when Vohlmann was thirty-three. Brenna died of childbed fever after a second pregnancy that did not carry to term, when Vohlmann was forty-eight and Karel was fifteen. He has not remarried. The brewhouse Reinhardt left them — by then taken over fully by Vohlmann after his father-in-law's retirement — was the structure that held him through the grief, and by extension, the structure that produced the kind of Master he has been since.

Took on operational warden role at fifty-two, became Master at fifty-eight after Reinhardt's successor retired. The election was largely a formality; Vohlmann was the consensus candidate.

---

## The Master's Work

Vohlmann's specific responsibilities, in his own working priority:

1. **Quality enforcement.** Standards across the central brewhouse and the dozen independent master operations. Personally inspects the central brewhouse twice a week and visits each independent operation at least quarterly.
2. **Contract review.** Any contract above a volume threshold passes his desk. He reads them all. He has caught more bad contracts than any of his wardens combined.
3. **Apprenticeship and journeyman placement.** Senior member promotions and apprentice bonding require his sign-off. He pays particular attention to which apprentices are placed under which masters; the personality fit matters as much as the technical fit.
4. **Specialty brewing.** Vohlmann personally brews several premium contracts — most notably Brask's reserve at the Silver Eel — because the technical work pleases him and because keeping his hand in the actual brewing is what keeps him competent to enforce standards.
5. **Inter-guild relations.** Coordinates with the other guild masters at the masters' council. He is not the dominant voice but he is a respected one, and his guild has not been on the losing side of any major masters'-council vote in his tenure.

What he does NOT do, by deliberate choice:
- Cultivate noble-house relationships beyond the strictly commercial
- Take public political positions
- Engage in the kind of guild-master rivalries that consume political energy elsewhere

## Voice

Slow, deliberate, dwarven cadence. Doesn't waste sentences. When he says a contract is acceptable, it is. When he says a contract is not acceptable, it is not, and the negotiation either ends or restarts on the issue he has named. Drops into more familiar register with old colleagues (Reinhardt-era brewers, Brask) and stays formal with everyone else.

**Default Conversational Move: Specify the standard.**
Vohlmann anchors conversations in concrete standards — what the contract calls for, what the brewhouse procedure requires, what the guild's own rules say. The specificity is partly habit and partly a deliberate strategy: arguments grounded in standards have a referee; arguments not so grounded escalate.

**Quirks:**
- Touches the Master's chain of office only when transferring it formally (e.g., handing meeting authority to a warden); never during a meeting itself
- Tastes every batch his name is on, no exceptions
- Refers to his late wife as "Brenna" and only Brenna; never "my wife" or "the late Mrs Vohlmann"; the name usage is its own form of memorial
- Eats the same midday meal every day at the brewhouse refectory — bread, hard cheese, a small bowl of mash-pot stew that the refectory cook makes specifically for him

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In his office at the Brewers' House. A junior journeyman has just brought him a contract draft from the commercial warden's desk.)*
*(Reads it twice. Sets it down.)*
"The clause on volume default. Re-write it. The current language allows the buyer to invoke default at twenty percent shortage; the guild's standard is thirty. We do not extend that benefit without compensating concessions on price."
*(Hands the draft back.)*
"Tell Warden Vesper to bring it back when it is fixed."

## Relationships

**Karel Vohlmann (his son)** — Journeyman in the central brewhouse, age thirty-two. Adequate brewer but has not yet shown the temperament for master-track work. Vohlmann is patient with this and quietly worried; he does not know whether his son will eventually grow into the trade or settle as a competent journeyman for life. Either is acceptable. Neither has been said aloud.

**[[Drest]]** — Journeyman handling the manor account. Vohlmann has been quietly grooming Drest for an eventual warden seat for the past two years. Has not told Drest. Drest's handling of the discrepancy episode (delayed flagging, but clean resolution) was instructive — Vohlmann logged the delay without making it public and watched the resolution prove itself out.

**The two wardens (Vesper and Halberd)** — Operational and commercial wardens respectively. Vohlmann's working partners. Vesper is a half-elven master brewer who handles standards and inspections; Halberd is a human master brewer who handles contracts and external relations. The three confer weekly. Vohlmann trusts both of them and listens particularly to Halberd on the political-signaling matters where Vohlmann's own instincts are weak.

**[[Brask_Gernholm|Brask]]** — Personal client and something close to a friend. Fifteen years of brewing Brask's reserve. They share occasional evening meals at the Silver Eel — the Eel's back room when both of them want privacy, or at the bar itself when Vohlmann wants to be visible to his colleagues.

**[[Mira_Toren|Mira]]** — Manor accounts counterpart. Professional respect, formal but warm. Vohlmann handled the discrepancy restitution conversation with her personally and earned a level of trust he has not fully spent yet.

**Master Olbrek Reinhardt's memory** — His father-in-law, dead twenty years. The brewhouse Vohlmann runs is still, in some sense, Reinhardt's. He has not changed the office furniture in twenty years.

**Brenna's memory** — Walking quietly through every working day of his life since forty-eight.

**The other guild masters** — Working professional relationships. Closer to the Carpenters Master and the Tanners Master than to the Weavers (Sorren is on the Corvel client list and Vohlmann is privately uncomfortable with him) or the Merchants (politically more active than Vohlmann finds comfortable).

---

## Independent Goal

Run the guild well until he can hand it on. He does not have a clear successor identified — Drest is a candidate, the operational warden Vesper is a candidate, an outside master brewer from one of the independent operations could be a candidate. Vohlmann is not in a hurry to choose and has at least another decade in him.

Beyond the guild, see Karel become whatever he is going to become. Maintain Brenna's brewing recipes (he has the book; he has not added to it; he reviews it once a year). Brew well.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** High in his proper register. Vohlmann would face down a noble or a competing guild master without flinching on a matter of standards; he does not yield ground that has been fairly earned. In actual physical conflict he is sixty-five and has not fought since his thirties; would be effective for two minutes and then severely outmatched.
**Loyalty:** To the guild. To Karel. To Brenna's memory. To Brask. To Reinhardt's memory. The order is held quietly.
**Breaking Point:** A direct attack on the guild's institutional integrity — corruption that would damage the guild's reputation across multiple generations. He would respond with full institutional weight and personal fury, in that order.

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Cold water. Walks to the brewhouse from his quarters at the Brewers' House.
- **Mid-morning:** Office work — contracts, correspondence, scheduling.
- **Late morning:** Brewhouse inspection, twice weekly; on other mornings, brewing his own contracted reserves (Brask's, primarily).
- **Midday:** Refectory. Same meal as always.
- **Afternoon:** Variable. Inter-guild meetings on council days, quarterly visits to independent operations, in-house apprentice and journeyman reviews.
- **Late afternoon:** End-of-day reconciliation with the wardens.
- **Evening:** Quiet. Sometimes the Silver Eel; usually his own quarters with a meal he prepares himself.
- **Night:** Reads — brewing histories, regional histories, Brenna's recipe book once a year. Sleeps early.
