# Drest

Halfling. Journeyman brewer at the Brewers Guild. Mid-thirties. Has handled the manor's account on the guild's side for the past four years and is being quietly groomed by Master Vohlmann as a candidate for warden eventually, though Drest has not been told this yet.

---

## Appearance

Three feet two inches, average build for a halfling man. Sandy-brown hair worn slightly long for guild fashion — pushed back from his face, frequently kept in place with whatever hand he is not currently using. Open features, a quick smile, sun-flushed cheeks from the brewhouse heat. Brown eyes that catch the light and crinkle when he laughs.

Wears the guild's journeyman tunic — green wool with copper trim at the collar — over plain working clothes. A leather apron when actively in the brewhouse. Strong forearms from twelve years of mash work, with a small puckered burn scar on the inside of his left wrist from a steam accident at twenty-two that he tells the apprentices about as a cautionary tale.

Carries a leather messenger satchel on delivery days with the count slate, the receiving forms, and a small flask of his own preferred small beer — which he does not share with manor staff during business hours and does share with them after.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Cheerful reliability.**
Drest is the kind of man you trust because he has not given you any reason not to, over years. He shows up when he says. He delivers what was contracted. He flags problems early. He is genuinely friendly without being performatively so. People like working with him because the work goes smoothly and the conversation around the work is pleasant.

**Flaw: Conflict-averse to the point of postponement.**
Drest will deliver bad news, but he will sometimes wait too long to do it because he wants to be sure first, and his being-sure process is slower than the situation can absorb. He flagged the barrel discrepancies eventually, but he had been noticing them for two months before he raised them with the guild's commercial warden, and that delay cost the manor real money. He understands this in retrospect. He has not entirely fixed it in himself.

---

## Background

Born to a halfling family in a small farming hamlet two hours south of Silberbach. The family had a small brewing operation on the side — household-scale, supplying themselves and their neighbours — and Drest grew up with the smell of mash in the kitchen. Apprenticed to a master brewer in Silberbach at fifteen, completed his apprenticeship at twenty-two, became a journeyman in the central brewhouse, and has been there ever since.

Met his wife, Annalise, four years ago — she runs a small herb-and-spice stall at Market Square (one of the stalls Heidrun the elder leases out, and yes, the same Annalise who supplies the herb stall referenced in [[Market_Square]]). They married within a year. Two children, both very young: a daughter, three, and a son, just turned one. Drest is besotted with both and talks about them often enough that his guild colleagues have started gently teasing him.

He took over the manor account from a retiring journeyman four years ago, around the same time as his marriage. The combination of the new account and the new wife produced a particularly happy stretch in his life, which is reflected in how he conducts himself professionally — Drest's reputation with the manor was built during the years he was at his most contented, and the goodwill from that period has carried since.

## The Manor Account

Drest's specific working relationship with the manor:

- **Weekly deliveries:** Standard barrels (common ale and small beer), Thursday mornings. Handled with the kennel/stable-side staff for the unloading and with [[Rennik_Tor|Rennik]] (manor quartermaster) for the receiving paperwork. Now also with the new countersign procedure under the revised receiving policy — Drest is the guild-side counter-signer.
- **Monthly check-ins:** Drest meets with [[Mira_Toren|Mira]] once a month for an account review. The meetings are brief, professional, and lately slightly more careful since the discrepancy episode.
- **Special orders:** Festival ales, banquet quantities, the manor's premium reserve orders for guests of rank. Drest places these with Master Vohlmann directly and supervises the production.
- **Tob coordination:** Drest and [[Tob]] have a working relationship that runs alongside the formal account. Tob orders what the kitchen needs; Drest delivers and sometimes brings extra small beer for the surrogate staff (Merra and the others) at no charge — a personal courtesy Vohlmann is aware of and has not stopped.

---

## The Discrepancy Episode

Six months ago Drest began to notice that the manor's barrel counts were running slightly under the contracted amounts on roughly one delivery in three. The shortages were small — one to three barrels in a forty-barrel delivery — and easy to dismiss individually. He noticed because he was the one logging the counts on the guild's side and the pattern was visible to him in a way it would not have been to a less attentive journeyman.

He sat on the observation for two months. His thinking, in his own retrospective account: he wanted to be sure it was not a counting error on his end; he wanted to rule out coincidence; he did not want to accuse a colleague at the central brewhouse of negligence or worse without evidence; and he was, frankly, hoping the pattern would resolve itself and let him not have to raise it.

The pattern did not resolve. Two months in, Drest brought the issue to the commercial warden, who took it to Master Vohlmann, who instituted an internal review that revealed an outbound counting procedure that had been corrupted by a single dishonest assistant in the central brewhouse. The assistant was dismissed; the practice was corrected; restitution was offered to the manor through Mira.

Mira accepted the restitution and used the episode to institute the revised receiving policy. Drest was the natural counter-signer because he was the one who had flagged it. The whole resolution was clean enough that Drest came out of it with an enhanced reputation despite the two months of delay; Master Vohlmann noted the delay and the resolution in a single private comment to Drest, which Drest took to heart.

He has not made the same delay again, on anything else, since.

## Voice

Halfling cadence — slightly faster than human speech, words clipped at the ends, vowels warm. Friendly opening register that drops into more careful working speech when actually transacting business. Says *yes* and *aye* roughly interchangeably. Drops into small-talk easily and exits it just as easily when it is time to work.

**Default Conversational Move: Confirm and elaborate.**
Drest repeats the substance of what was said, then adds the relevant context that supports or qualifies it. The repetition is a working habit (it doubles as a count check on instructions) and the elaboration is friendliness — he gives people more than the minimum because he likes the people he is talking to.

**Quirks:**
- Absentmindedly hums while counting barrels — the same brewing-song his apprentice master taught him fifteen years ago
- Always has small beer in his flask; offers it to manor staff after work concludes, never during
- Keeps a small carved wooden duckling in his satchel pocket — a present from his daughter, replaced when she gives him another one
- When tired, shifts entirely into halfling cadence and his speech becomes harder for human ears to follow

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(At the manor's receiving area, delivery just unloaded, count slate in hand.)*
"Forty-two on the manifest, forty-two on the count, aye. Rennik, that's your line for the countersign — there, yes."
*(Hands over the slate, takes it back, signs his own line.)*
"Tob about? I brought the extra small beer for Merra and the others, if you want me to take it round."

## Relationships

**[[Master Heinrek Vohlmann|Master Vohlmann]]** — Direct guild superior. Drest respects him professionally and is being groomed by him without yet knowing it. Vohlmann does not give compliments. The lack of correction Drest receives is itself the compliment.

**Annalise (his wife)** — The other gravity in his life. Runs the herb-and-spice stall at Market Square. Drest stops at the stall on his way home most evenings. They were both surprised by how much they liked each other when they first met and have continued to be quietly surprised by it.

**[[Mira_Toren|Mira]]** — Manor accounts counterpart. Professional respect. Drest is slightly more careful in her presence since the discrepancy episode, which Mira has registered without comment. The relationship is good.

**[[Tob]]** — Working friend. Tob does not have many warm relationships at the manor; Drest is one of them. The two have spent enough hours over the years on receiving-area paperwork that they have learned each other.

**[[Rennik_Tor|Rennik]]** — Receiving-side counter-party. Cordial professional relationship. Rennik appreciates Drest's count discipline.

**[[Manor_Surrogate_Staff|Merra and the surrogates]]** — Personal courtesy. Drest brings them small beer because he can and because Tob mentioned once that they appreciated it. Has done so for two years.

**The central brewhouse colleagues** — Friendly. Drest is the kind of journeyman the apprentices come to with questions because he answers without making them feel small. The dishonest assistant who caused the discrepancy episode was not someone Drest had been close to.

**Heidrun the elder** — Market Square clerk. Has known her since Annalise leased the herb stall. Heidrun is not friendly with most people. She is, by her standards, friendly with Drest.

## Independent Goal

Make warden eventually. Raise his children well. Keep the manor account clean and the guild relationship steady. None of these are urgent; all of them are durable.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Average. Drest is a brewer, not a fighter. He has had basic guild militia training as required of journeymen but has never used it in earnest.
**Loyalty:** To Annalise and the children. To the guild. To Master Vohlmann. To the manor account in a working sense.
**Breaking Point:** A direct threat to his family. Drest is not someone who fights, but he would protect his wife and children with anything to hand.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Pre-dawn:** Up. Quiet breakfast at home. Walks to the central brewhouse.
- **Dawn:** Brewhouse work. Mash schedules, fermentation checks, barrel counts.
- **Mid-morning:** Account work — delivery scheduling, contract paperwork, customer correspondence.
- **Midday:** Brief meal at the brewhouse refectory or, on Thursdays, a quick stop at the herb stall to see Annalise.
- **Afternoon:** Deliveries when scheduled (Thursdays for the manor); brewhouse work otherwise.
- **Late afternoon:** End-of-day cleanup at the brewhouse, count reconciliation.
- **Evening:** Walk to Market Square to collect Annalise, then home together.
- **Night:** With his family. Reads to his daughter at bedtime. Sleeps.
