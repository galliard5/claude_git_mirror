# Brewers Guild

The Brewers Guild of Silberbach. Produces ale, beer, small beer, and seasonal specialty brews for the town, the river trade, the noble houses, the manor, and the surrounding country. One of the eleven major guilds, mid-tier in political weight, high-tier in daily-life relevance — half of Silberbach drinks Brewers Guild work daily and most of the rest do at least weekly.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A copper kettle on a green field, three hop-vines rising
**Colors:** Green and copper
**Motto:** "By Grain and Patience"
**Hall:** The Brewers' House — a stone-and-timber building on the millrace, three streets north of Market Square. The hall sits adjacent to the guild's central brewhouse and the cooper's yard the guild leases to its bonded cooperage.

---

## Structure

**Master Brewer of the Guild:** Master Heinrek Vohlmann (see below)
**Wardens:** Two — one operational (oversees brewhouse and cooperage standards), one commercial (handles contracts, deliveries, disputes with merchant counterparties)
**Membership tiers:**
- *Master brewers* — independent operators with their own brewhouses, full guild voting privileges
- *Journeymen* — qualified brewers working under master brewers or in the guild's central brewhouse, voting on operational matters only
- *Apprentices* — bonded to a master brewer for typically seven years, no voting

The guild operates a central brewhouse for bulk production (the workhorse ale that fills most of the daily volume) plus regulates approximately a dozen independent master-brewer operations across Silberbach. The independents handle their own production but pay guild dues, follow guild standards, and route through the guild for any contract above a certain volume threshold.

---

## What They Make

**Daily volume:**
- *Common ale* — the workhorse, brewed continuously at the central brewhouse, supplied throughout the town
- *Small beer* — lower alcohol, daily-drinking standard for households and labor
- *Wheat ale* — lighter, summer-favored, somewhat seasonal

**Specialty work:**
- *Porter* — darker, evening-favored
- *Spiced winter ale* — seasonal, premium
- *Brewer's reserve* — small-batch, contracted to specific clients (Brask Gernholm at the Silver Eel has a standing arrangement; Master Vohlmann brews to Brask's specifications himself, which is unusual)
- *Small specialty brews* — wedding ales, festival batches, custom commissions for the noble houses

The guild does not produce wine (House Rothwyn's territory) or distilled spirits (no formal regional industry; what exists is small-scale farmhouse work).

---

## Manor Relationship

The manor is a long-standing client of the guild. Standard barrel deliveries once weekly, plus seasonal premium orders for events and guests. Tob (manor head cook) handles weekly orders directly with the guild's commercial side; Mira (manor head accountant) handles the contracts and the periodic financial review.

[[Drest]] is the regular Brewers Guild contact who handles the manor account on the guild's side — has done so for several years, knows the manor well, has good working relationships with both Tob and Mira.

**Recent development:** Following Mira's flagged discrepancies on barrel deliveries (short-counts that had been accumulating slowly), the manor instituted a revised receiving policy: full count on delivery, countersign required. The Brewers Guild was the first counterparty under the new policy, tested on a Thursday delivery in mid-April. Drest was the manor-side counter-signer on the guild's behalf. The arrangement was explained, accepted, and is now standard for the manor's Brewers deliveries.

Master Vohlmann was informed of the new policy through Drest. His response was characteristic: he asked whether the discrepancies had been the guild's error or someone else's, accepted Drest's account, instituted his own internal review of the central brewhouse's outbound count procedures, and wrote Mira a brief courteous letter acknowledging the policy. He did not push back. The manor reads this as appropriate professional response. The guild reads it as Vohlmann handling a problem before it became one.

---

## Master Heinrek Vohlmann

The current Master Brewer of the Guild. Dwarven, mid-sixties, has held the Master's title for fourteen years. Inherited the position from a guild election after the previous Master's retirement — Vohlmann had been one of the two senior wardens for a decade prior and was the consensus successor.

**Appearance:** Average dwarven height, broad through the shoulders even now, with the heavy forearms of a man who still tastes his own batches by lifting the mash paddle himself when he wants to. Thick grey beard, close-cut grey hair, dark eyes that miss very little. Wears the guild's master-brewer apron over plain wool when at the brewhouse and a master's robe of green wool with copper trim at formal functions. The Master's chain of office (a copper kettle pendant) on his chest at any guild meeting.

**Personality:**
- *Core trait:* Operational seriousness. Vohlmann runs the guild as an operation — meaning standards are enforced, contracts are honored, errors are caught early, and political theatre is minimized. He has the dwarven temperament that finds reliability more interesting than charm and has built a guild reputation on the quality of his enforcement.
- *Flaw:* Underweights political signaling. Vohlmann handles substantive problems efficiently and does not always recognise when a situation calls for visible posture as well as actual competence. The new manor receiving policy is an example — his response was correct but quiet, and a less private guild Master might have made a public gesture of supporting the policy that would have served broader political purposes. Vohlmann does not think in those terms. The two operational wardens, between them, occasionally push him toward visibility.

**Background:** Born to a brewing family in the southern dwarven settlements; came to Silberbach at twenty-four to apprentice under a master brewer who later became his father-in-law. Married at thirty, widowed at forty-eight, has one son currently a journeyman in the central brewhouse. Took over his father-in-law's brewhouse at fifty, became operational warden at fifty-two, became Master at fifty-eight after the predecessor's retirement. Has not remarried.

**Voice:** Slow, deliberate, dwarven cadence. Doesn't waste sentences. When he says a contract is acceptable, it is. When he says a contract is not acceptable, it is not, and the negotiation either ends or restarts on the issue he has named.

**Relationships:**
- [[Drest]] — Trusted journeyman handling the manor account; Vohlmann's read on Drest is positive and stable
- [[Mira_Toren|Mira]] (manor accounts) — Working professional respect, formal but warm
- [[Brask_Gernholm|Brask Gernholm]] (Silver Eel) — Personal client; Vohlmann brews Brask's reserve himself; the two have known each other for fifteen years and are something close to friends
- *His son, Karel* — Journeyman in the central brewhouse, adequate brewer but not yet showing master temperament. Vohlmann is patient.
- *The two wardens* — Working alliance. Vohlmann respects them both and listens to them on the political side where his own instincts are weaker.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** Mid-tier political weight. The Brewers are essential to daily life but do not control critical commerce in the way the River Traders or Weavers do. Master Vohlmann is respected at the guild masters' council but is not one of the dominant voices.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral. Vohlmann has not aligned the guild with the Valtor-Kaelen tension and has resisted internal pressure to do so. The guild ships to both sides; the guild is paid by both sides; the guild does not need to take a public position. This is the standard Brewers Guild policy across multiple Masters' tenures and Vohlmann sees no reason to break it.

**Manor relationship:** Long-standing client; reliable revenue; treated as a respected commercial counterparty without political coloring. Vohlmann does not have a personal opinion on Isalia or the manor's services — they are a customer, the customer pays, the customer is treated correctly.

**Investigations:** No current guild involvement in the Corvel investigation or any other active legal matter. The Brewers' books are clean and the Master would prefer to keep them that way.

## Hooks

- **A bad batch reaches the market.** Discovered in time, but the source is unclear — central brewhouse error, sabotage, or contamination upstream? Vohlmann needs to know before another batch goes out.
- **A senior journeyman wants to break free as an independent master brewer.** The guild's permission is needed; the politics are interesting. Drest is friends with the journeyman in question.
- **A noble house threatens to take its business elsewhere over a price dispute.** Vohlmann has to decide whether to hold or fold. Either choice has costs.
- **A new spiced winter ale recipe.** A young brewer presents something unusual to Master Vohlmann. He likes it. The question is how to launch it without breaking the guild's standardised inventory.
- **An old apprenticeship debt comes due.** A bonded apprentice is approaching the end of his seven years and his master has died. The guild must decide who absorbs the apprentice's remaining time.
