# Miners Guild

The Miners Guild. Operates the labor side of the northern mountain mining operations — the working miners' coordinating body, with members spread across the seven major mines and the smaller subordinate operations that produce silver, iron, and gemstones for the regional and imperial economy.

Structurally distinct from House Aldenberg. The Hold owns the mining concessions on three of the seven major mines and tolls every shipment that passes through the mountain passes; the imperial authority owns concessions on the rest; the guild represents the workers across all of them. The relationship between the guild and Aldenberg is professional and cooperative when working conditions are stable, contentious when they are not.

The most distant of Silberbach's eleven major guilds — most members live and work in the mountain settlements rather than in Silberbach proper. The guild maintains a satellite office at the Smiths' House (sharing space rather than running its own hall) and the Master's chair is held by rotation among master miners from the various mountain operations, with the Master traveling to Silberbach for council meetings rather than residing there.

## Identity

**Crest:** A miner's pick crossed with a lantern over a stylized mountain
**Colors:** Slate-grey and lantern-yellow
**Motto:** "From the Deep, the Light"
**Hall:** Office space within the Smiths' House in Silberbach; the guild's actual operational center is at Aldenberg Hold's lower hall, where the working master miners meet quarterly.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Veska, dwarven, female, late seventies, master miner from the largest of the imperial-concession mines. Has held the chair for the past three years on a rotating term that will pass to a different master at the next general meeting.

**Wardens:** Three — one for working conditions and safety, one for commercial coordination (toll matters, shipment logistics with the River Traders), one for mutual aid (the guild's relief fund for injured miners and surviving families).

**Notable masters:**
- *Master Veska herself* — senior figure across all three of the imperial-concession mines; respected across the guild's full membership for half a century of mining work
- *Master Tarn, dwarven* — runs one of the Aldenberg-concession operations; the guild's most diplomatically careful figure when navigating Hold-versus-Empire politics
- *Master Lurdis, human* — runs a smaller specialty gemstone operation; the guild's bridge to the gemstone polishers and setters at Aldenberg Hold

---

## What They Coordinate

- *Working conditions* — wages, shift patterns, safety standards, the perpetual negotiation with both Aldenberg and the imperial mine-managers
- *Mutual aid* — the relief fund supports injured miners and their families; mining accidents are common enough to make this a core institutional function
- *Pass tolls* — Aldenberg charges every shipment of ore moving down to Silberbach; the guild does not contest the tolls but does monitor them for political escalation
- *Commercial coordination* — with the Blacksmiths Guild on iron, with the Aldenberg forges on premium steel feedstock, with imperial assayers on silver and gemstone shipments
- *Safety standards* — collapse prevention, water pumping coordination, ventilation, the specific safety practices that keep mining accidents at the minimum-possible rather than at the actuarial maximum

---

## Manor Relationship

Distant. The manor has no direct contracts with the Miners Guild. Indirect connections exist through the Blacksmiths and Aldenberg supply chains — the iron in the manor's collar reinforcements ultimately came from these mines — but the relationship runs through several intermediaries.

---

## Politics & Position

Lower-tier political weight in Silberbach proper, despite mining's economic importance. The geographic distance and the structural subordination to both Aldenberg and the imperial concession-holders limit the guild's direct political reach.

The guild's most important political balance is between Aldenberg and the imperial authority. Both sides want the mines productive; both sides want the workers cooperative; neither side wants the working conditions to deteriorate to the point of slowdown or revolt. Master Veska's tenure has been characterised by patient diplomatic balancing of the two upstream powers, and the guild has had no major work stoppages in her three years.

Officially neutral on the Valtor-Kaelen dispute. The guild's members are far enough from the politics of the river valley that they do not, in practice, track them closely.

No current investigation involvement.

---

## Hooks

- **A mine collapse.** The most common kind of crisis. Worker deaths, mutual aid demands, blame allocation, and the specific question of whether safety standards held.
- **A new vein discovered.** A premium silver vein at one of the Aldenberg-concession mines. The Hold wants extraction accelerated; the guild's safety warden wants the standard preparation period; the negotiation is real.
- **An imperial assessor visits.** Spring audit overdue and increasingly delayed (per [[Valtor_Keep]] active conditions); when the assessor finally arrives, the assessor will inspect the mine concessions as well as the keep treasury. The guild has to host.
- **A working-condition strike at one of the mines.** Master Veska must travel from her own mine to the affected operation. While she is gone, other operations may face their own pressures.
- **A gemstone smuggling investigation.** Cut gemstones leaving the region without proper imperial assessment. Master Lurdis is interviewed first.
