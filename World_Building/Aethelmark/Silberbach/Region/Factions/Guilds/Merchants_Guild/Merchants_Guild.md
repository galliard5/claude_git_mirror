# Merchants Guild

The Merchants Guild of Silberbach. The regional trade-coordination body covering merchants who handle commerce across guild and craft boundaries — the importers, exporters, factor's-houses, and trading operations whose business is movement of goods rather than production. Operates the merchant register, sets market standards, resolves disputes between merchants and between merchants and the production guilds, and represents merchant interests at the masters' council and to the noble houses.

The most politically active of Silberbach's eleven major guilds. The Merchants' Master is one of the dominant voices at the council and one of the few guild figures who deals routinely with the imperial authority on commercial-political matters.

The guild's most distinctive operational feature is the transformed-courier messenger network — a specialty service maintained through a long-running arrangement with [[Isalias_Manor]] that sets the Merchants apart from comparable guilds in other regional cities.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A merchant's scales over a coiled scroll
**Colors:** Royal purple and gold
**Motto:** "By Trust and Trade"
**Hall:** The Merchants' House — a large, well-appointed building on Merchants' Close, with the guild's commercial chambers, the merchants' register, the courier dispatch office, and the Master's offices.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Albrecht Kessel, human, late fifties, master merchant. Has held the chair for eight years. Politically active by temperament and by the position's structural requirements; the most visible of the guild masters at noble functions and at imperial business.

**Wardens:** Three — one for trade standards (regulation of merchant practices), one for commerce (contracts and disputes), one for courier operations (the transformed-messenger network specifically).

**Notable masters:**
- *Master Kessel himself* — runs the Kessel trading house, one of the larger merchant operations in Silberbach; deals primarily in imported goods through the river trade
- *Master Erika Vohlann, dwarven* — runs a specialty trading operation focused on Aldenberg's gemstone exports; politically allied with Kessel
- *Master Dieter Reibach, human* — the Courier warden; runs the dispatch office; coordinates directly with [[Isalias_Manor]] on the transformed-courier arrangement

---

## What They Coordinate

- *Market standards and regulation* — across all merchant operations in Silberbach, with enforcement authority delegated by the Count under longstanding charter
- *Inter-guild commercial relations* — the Merchants Guild is the structural intermediary between production guilds (Weavers, Tanners, Brewers, etc.) and the broader regional and imperial markets
- *Dispute resolution* — between merchants, between merchants and production guilds, between merchants and noble households on commercial matters
- *The transformed-courier network* — the guild's signature messenger service (see below)
- *Imperial commerce coordination* — the guild's Master is the standard regional point of contact for imperial trade matters that touch Silberbach
- *The Vance family* and several other merchant houses operate within the guild's regulatory framework — including [[Elara_Kaelen|Baroness Elara Kaelen]]'s family of origin, whose continued merchant operations connect Kreiger-aligned funding networks to standard guild commerce

## The Transformed Courier Network

The guild's distinctive operational specialty. A messenger and transport service staffed by transformed individuals — horses for speed and endurance on road routes, wolves for forest paths, hawks and ravens for long-distance communication and reconnaissance, occasionally other forms for specialty work.

**Recruitment:** Some couriers come to the guild already transformed and seek employment; others come to the guild seeking transformation and the guild sponsors the procedure at [[Isalias_Manor]] in exchange for a term of indentured service (typically five to ten years). The arrangement is voluntary and contractual: the individual chooses their form (subject to the guild's operational needs), serves their term, and then becomes a free agent. Many stay with the guild afterward; some take their skills elsewhere; a small number transition to private work for noble houses or for production guilds (Waldheim's rangers occasionally hire from this pool when the courier-side skills translate to ranger-side work).

**Compensation:** Well-compensated. The guild treats its transformed couriers with respect because their reliability directly impacts guild profits. Indentured-service rates are calibrated to be genuinely fair after factoring in the transformation cost; free-agent rates are competitive with any specialty messenger work in the region.

**Communication amulets:** All guild-sponsored transformed couriers receive a collar-integrated communication amulet that allows them to speak normally despite their forms. The amulets are crafted by [[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]] at the manor's enchanting workshop and are standard issue — no transformed courier works without one. The amulet is keyed to the wearer at fitting and remains active for the duration of guild service plus an additional year if the courier transitions to free-agent status (a courtesy the guild extends to maintain working relationships).

**Livery:** All guild couriers wear distinctive purple-and-gold livery that marks them as official messengers, regardless of their form — typically a sash for human-form couriers, a marked collar for transformed couriers (in addition to the speech-amulet collar). The livery confers protected-messenger status in most regional territories under longstanding commercial convention; bandits and rival factions are less respectful but the convention generally holds.

**Arrangement with Isalia's Manor:** A standing contract for transformation services, refresher consultations (every three years), reversion if requested at the end of service, and the supply of speech-amulet collars. The contract has been in place for over a decade and runs through Master Reibach (the courier warden) on the guild's side and through [[Mira_Toren|Mira]] and [[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]] on the manor's. The arrangement is discreet but not secret — everyone in Silberbach knows the guild employs transformed couriers; the specific contractual terms remain private.

---

## Manor Relationship

The most operationally substantial guild-to-manor contract in the region by total annual value. The transformed-courier arrangement plus the speech-amulet collar supply plus occasional specialty consultations adds up to a steady, valuable, long-term relationship that both sides treat carefully.

Beyond the courier framework, the guild handles standard commercial matters for the manor when imports or exports are involved — the manor purchases certain alchemical components from imperial sources through guild channels; the manor occasionally sells finished enchanted items to distant clients through guild-coordinated shipments. These arrangements run through the guild's commerce warden rather than the courier warden.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** Top-tier political weight, alongside the River Traders. The Merchants' Master is one of the most visible guild figures at the masters' council and one of the most visible at noble functions.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral, with practical leanings that vary by issue. Master Kessel is more politically active than most guild masters and has been navigating the Valtor-Kaelen tension carefully — the Vance family's connections to Baroness Kaelen create a specific exposure, but Kessel has handled it through formal-channels deniability without forcing a public position. The guild does business with all sides.

**Manor relationship:** Long-standing, valued, structurally important.

**Investigations:** None directly. The Merchants Guild is structurally exposed to the Corvel investigation through the broader merchant network (Aldus Corvel was, before exposure, a working merchant in Silberbach; some of his client list overlaps with guild members), but the guild's own institutional books are clean and the investigation has not surfaced guild-level complicity.

## Hooks

- **An indentured courier wants out early.** Five years into a ten-year contract, a transformed courier requests release. The guild has to decide whether to grant, refuse, or renegotiate. The reasons may matter; the precedent definitely does.
- **A speech-amulet failure on a courier in the field.** The amulet stops working mid-route. The courier is unable to speak. The guild needs Elowen to investigate; the manor needs the courier brought back; the question is what failed and why.
- **A noble house wants its own private courier.** A noble approaches the guild seeking exclusive rights to a specific transformed courier. The guild's longstanding policy is that couriers are guild employees, not private retainers; the noble offers enough money to make the question worth asking.
- **An imperial trade-license dispute.** Master Kessel must travel to the imperial capital to defend a guild position. While he is gone, the guild's political weight at the council is reduced and rivals push.
- **A courier brings back political news the guild was not expecting.** A transformed messenger arrives with intelligence about another house's movements — overheard during a routine delivery. Master Kessel has to decide whether to act on it, sit on it, or sell it.
