# River Traders Guild

The River Traders Guild of Silberbach. Operates the Angerap dock infrastructure, regulates river commerce, vets every barge that arrives or departs Silberbach, manages the dockmasters' authority, and coordinates the broader regional river trade with downstream and upstream guild counterparts.

Politically heavyweight. The Angerap is the regional commercial spine; the guild that controls dock access controls a substantial fraction of Silberbach's economic life. The guild's Master is one of the dominant voices at the masters' council and one of the few guild figures who deals directly with the Count on commercial-political matters.

The guild also maintains what is referred to in private as *the harbor accommodation* — the informal arrangement with [[Harren_Volke]] at [[The_Crescent_House|the Crescent House]] under which the guild's enforcement attention is selectively absent from certain harbor-edge activities in exchange for the regular tribute the Crescent House pays. The arrangement has been operational for over a decade. The guild's Master is aware. Whether it should continue is a recurring private question.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A river barge poling-staff crossed with a shipping-hook over a wave-and-scroll
**Colors:** Deep blue and silver
**Motto:** "By the Current, Bound"
**Hall:** The River House — a substantial timber-and-stone building at the head of the docks, with the dockmasters' office on the ground floor, the guild's commercial chambers on the second floor, and the Master's office and meeting rooms on the third.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Rolf Vannholt (see below)
**Wardens:** Three — one for dock operations and dockmasters, one for upstream trade, one for downstream trade
**Membership tiers:**
- *Master river traders* — the senior figures, captains of their own barges or operators of the larger trading houses, full voting privileges
- *Captains* — barge captains operating individual vessels, voting on operational matters
- *Crew* — bonded to specific captains; not voting members but recognised guild members
- *Dockmasters* — guild employees rather than members in the conventional sense; specialised position with its own internal hierarchy

The dockmasters are the guild's daily face — they meet every barge, log every cargo, charge every dock fee, and make the immediate calls about who docks where. They are organised into a dockmasters' rota under [[Senior_Dockmaster_Bero|Senior Dockmaster Bero]], who reports to the dock-operations warden.

---

## What They Coordinate

- *Dock operations* — every barge in and out of Silberbach passes through the guild's authority
- *Dock fees* — graduated by tonnage; the guild's primary revenue stream
- *River trade contracts* — major shipping arrangements, freight contracts, downstream merchant partnerships
- *Inter-guild river coordination* — relationships with the equivalent guilds in the larger downstream cities and upstream settlements
- *Barge-builder oversight* — the small boatyard along the south bank operates under guild regulation; new barges are guild-inspected before launch
- *Customs coordination* — for noble or imperial cargo, the guild works with the Count's customs officer
- *The harbor accommodation* — the informal arrangement (see below)

---

## The Harbor Accommodation

The Crescent House pays regular tribute to the River Traders Guild — typically 50 silver per quarter, delivered through Harren Volke's intermediary — in exchange for the guild's enforcement attention being selectively absent from certain harbor-edge activities. Specifically:

- Late-night barge movements that bypass standard customs procedure
- Cargo manifests that are technically incomplete
- Crew members whose papers do not match the guild's standard expectations

The arrangement does not extend to:
- Major contraband (the guild will report)
- Trafficking in persons (the guild will report and intervene)
- Anything that draws imperial attention (the guild will not protect against)

The arrangement has been operational for over a decade, surviving two changes of guild Master. Master Vannholt knows about it, has not formally endorsed it, has not formally ended it, and has been thinking about whether to do so for the past two years. The dockmasters know about it operationally; the dock-operations warden manages it on Vannholt's behalf. Senior Dockmaster Bero is the guild member who handles the day-to-day execution and is the most visibly compromised by the arrangement, though he has not personally taken bribes — the tribute goes to the guild's quiet account.

The recent disappearance of [[Garret Brune]] (a Valtor garrison regular last seen at the Crescent House) is, among other things, a stress test of the arrangement. If the missing-guard investigation reaches the harbor accommodation, the arrangement may not survive the exposure.

---

## Manor Relationship

Steady commercial relationship. The manor occasionally ships goods through the river trade — finished enchanted items to clients in distant cities, supplies received from downstream — and the manor's couriers (Marek and others) cross the docks regularly. The relationship is professional and uncomplicated.

Vannholt has had no direct dealings with Isalia. The dockmasters know the manor's couriers by sight and pass them through without complication.

---

## Master Rolf Vannholt

Current Master. Human, male, mid-fifties, master river trader. Came up through barge captaincy — captained his own barge for fifteen years before transitioning to shore-based trading operations and eventually to guild leadership. Master for seven years.

**Appearance:** Tall, broad through the shoulders, the durable build of a man who handled barge cargo for two decades. Brown hair gone iron-grey, beard trimmed close. Weather-darkened skin even now. Wears the master's robe of deep-blue wool with silver trim at formal functions; works in plain wool with a captain's coat in the office. The Master's pendant of office (the poling-staff-and-hook in silver) on a heavy chain.

**Personality:**
- *Core trait:* Pragmatic authority. Vannholt grew up with the river trade's particular ethics — practical accommodations, working relationships, the long-term value of letting some things lie. The harbor accommodation is one of these pragmatic positions.
- *Flaw:* Has been deferring the harbor accommodation question for two years. The arrangement may be becoming politically untenable; Vannholt knows this; he has not yet decided what to do.

**Background:** Born to a river-trade family. Apprenticed at fourteen as a barge-hand, captained at twenty-eight, ran his own trading operation from thirty-five, took on warden role at forty-five, became Master at forty-eight. Married, two grown sons (both in the trade, one captaining his own barge, one running a smaller trading operation in a downstream city), one grown daughter (married a merchant from the southern empire, lives a great distance away).

**Voice:** Direct, slightly slow, river-cadence — the kind of speech rhythm developed by spending years on barge decks where shouting carried but quieter speech did not. Drops into specific maritime register easily.

## Senior Dockmaster Bero

The senior dockmaster — runs the daily dockmasters' rota, makes the immediate calls about which barges dock where, executes the harbor accommodation in practice, knows every captain on the river by name. Half-elven, male, late forties.

**Appearance:** Average height, lean build, sun-darkened skin. Long pale hair tied back during the working day. Pale eyes. Wears the dockmaster's distinctive blue coat with silver braid that signals his authority on the wharf.

**Personality:** Watchful, businesslike, accommodating within limits. Has held the senior dockmaster position for nine years and has seen every kind of cargo, every kind of captain, and every kind of trouble. Does not get rattled. Does not get sentimental.

**Bero and the harbor accommodation:** He is the guild member who executes it operationally. He has not personally taken bribes — the tribute flows to the guild's quiet account. He has, however, been the visible face of the arrangement for nine years and is the figure most exposed if the arrangement comes apart.

**Voice:** Quick, clipped, half-elven cadence. Direct to the point of bluntness with strangers; warmer with regular captains he knows.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** Top-tier political weight. The Master sits at the masters' council with substantial influence and direct access to the Count.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral, with quiet practical leanings. Vannholt has not aligned with any noble faction publicly but has been carefully professional in dealings with all of them. The Count knows he can rely on the guild for routine commercial cooperation; the Kreiger network knows the guild will not be a hostile actor.

**Manor relationship:** Steady. Uncomplicated.

**Investigations:** None directly. The harbor accommodation is structurally untouched by the Corvel investigation but vulnerable to the missing-guard investigation if it reaches the docks.

---

## Hooks

- **The missing-guard investigation reaches the docks.** Constable Ferris or a Valtor garrison representative arrives with questions about Garret Brune. Bero is interviewed first; Vannholt is interviewed if Bero's answers are insufficient. The harbor accommodation is the larger question hovering behind the specific case.
- **A barge arrives carrying contraband.** Cargo discovered. Whether the cargo was supposed to pass under the harbor accommodation's deniability is the question; the captain's testimony will determine the answer.
- **Two captains come to blows over a dock berth.** The dockmasters can resolve it; or it escalates to the wardens; or it reaches the Master. Each escalation has political cost.
- **A river-trade dispute with a downstream city's guild.** Vannholt must travel downstream to negotiate. While he is gone, the harbor accommodation is being executed without him; if anything breaks, he is not on hand to manage it.
- **Bero requests a transfer.** Nine years of executing the arrangement have begun to wear. He wants out. Vannholt has to decide whether to grant the transfer (which surfaces the succession question for the senior dockmaster role and risks his replacement learning what is going on) or to refuse (which keeps Bero in place but may break him).
