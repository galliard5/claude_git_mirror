# Senior Dockmaster Bero

Half-elven, male, late forties. Senior Dockmaster of Silberbach's docks under the River Traders Guild. Runs the daily dockmasters' rota, makes the immediate calls about which barges dock where, knows every captain on the river by name, and is the operational executor of the harbor accommodation between the guild and the Crescent House. Has held the senior position for nine years. Is wearing thin.

---

## Appearance

Average height, lean build, sun-darkened skin from twenty years of dock work. Long pale hair tied back during the working day in the practical river-worker's style. Pale grey eyes, sharp-featured half-elven face. Pointed ears partially visible when his hair is up.

Wears the dockmaster's distinctive blue coat with silver braid that signals his authority on the wharf — recognisable at distance, deliberately so. A leather satchel at his hip carries the day's logging tablets, a cargo-hook for emergencies, and a small flask of stronger spirit than dockmasters are supposed to carry on duty (he has not been caught and would not particularly care if he were).

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Watchful businesslike accommodation.**
Bero has held this position for nine years and has seen every kind of cargo, every kind of captain, and every kind of trouble. He does not get rattled. He does not get sentimental. The work is the work; the captains are the captains; some things get reported and some things do not, and the rules for which is which are mostly clear and have been held for years.

**Flaw: The accommodation is wearing him.**
Bero has personally executed the harbor accommodation for nine years. He has not personally taken bribes — the tribute flows to the guild's quiet account, structurally separate from his own pay. But he has been the visible face of the arrangement for nine years, and he has been the one who looks the other way at things he might otherwise feel obligated to report. The cumulative weight is real. He drinks more than he did five years ago. His wife has noticed. He has begun to think about asking for transfer.

---

## Background

Born to a half-elven family in a smaller settlement upstream of Silberbach. Came to the docks at sixteen as a barge-hand. Worked through every position on the wharf — barge-hand, factor's assistant, junior dockmaster, dockmaster — over twenty years. Promoted to senior dockmaster nine years ago at thirty-eight, when his predecessor retired.

Married Halrana — a half-elven woman from the same upriver community as his family — at twenty-six. Two children, both grown: a son working as a barge captain on a downstream river, and a daughter who married a Silberbach merchant family and lives in the New Town. Bero and Halrana share a small house in the New Town near the docks; he walks to work in the dark each morning.

The first six years of the senior position were what he had hoped for — substantial responsibility, real authority, the satisfaction of competent work. The last three years have changed. The harbor accommodation has not changed in those three years; what has changed is Bero's tolerance for executing it.

---

## The Job

**Daily responsibilities:**
- Meet every barge that arrives during his shift
- Assign berths, log cargo, charge dock fees
- Coordinate the dockmasters' rota — typically four dockmasters on duty at any time across the wharf
- Handle disputes that the junior dockmasters cannot resolve
- Report to the dock-operations warden on the day's events

**The harbor accommodation in his daily work:**
- Recognise the captains and crews whose barges fall under the arrangement
- Wave through the technical irregularities (manifests, customs, late-night movements) that the arrangement covers
- Pass any complications up to the dock-operations warden rather than handling himself
- Maintain the appearance of standard enforcement to anyone watching from outside

The arrangement requires constant judgment about what falls under it and what does not. Cargo that would have been waved through eight years ago might be too politically dangerous to wave through now; Bero is the one who decides in real time. The judgment burden is significant and is part of what is wearing him.

---

## Voice

Quick, clipped, half-elven cadence. Direct to the point of bluntness with strangers; warmer with regular captains he has known for years. Uses dockworker's slang easily. Drops into more formal register only when the dock-operations warden or the Master is present.

**Default Conversational Move: Process.**
Bero treats every conversation as something to be handled efficiently — what is being asked, what can be granted, what must be refused, what the next step is. The processing register is his professional default and has begun to bleed into his personal life, which Halrana has noticed.

**Quirks:**
- Always has a small flask in his coat; rarely refills it during work hours but checks it
- Keeps a count of every barge in the harbor at any given time in his head; can tell you the number on demand
- Uses captains' first names with the regular captains and last names with the irregulars
- Tightens his hand around the satchel strap when stressed — the only visible tell

## Relationships

**Halrana (his wife)** — Twenty-one years. Foundation. She has noticed the wear and has not pressed him about it. She has also begun to set out his evening meal earlier than she used to and to keep the lamp lit later, which is her way of letting him know she sees what is happening.

**The two children** — Settled in their own lives. He sees the daughter in the New Town occasionally; the son is captaining downstream and visits perhaps twice a year.

**The dockmasters under his command** — Four to six rotating, plus seasonal additions during the busy stretches. Bero has trained most of them and runs a competent team. None of them know the harbor accommodation's full structure; two of them have figured out enough of it to know not to ask.

**[[Master_Rolf_Vannholt|Master Vannholt]]** — Indirect superior. Bero respects Vannholt and is aware that Vannholt has been deferring the harbor accommodation question. He has not pushed Vannholt on it. He is, increasingly, considering whether to.

**The dock-operations warden** — Direct supervisor. The two of them confer daily; the warden is the one who carries operational decisions up to Vannholt.

**[[Harren_Volke|Harren Volke]]** — Counterparty in the harbor accommodation, on the Crescent House side. Bero has met Volke maybe a dozen times in nine years. The meetings are professional, brief, and have grown shorter as the accommodation has grown more uncomfortable.

**The regular captains who know him** — Real working friendships in some cases. Bero has been on this wharf for thirty years; the older captains are his cohort. He has dinner at the Silver Eel once a month with a small group of them.

---

## Independent Goal

Get out of the senior dockmaster role within the next year or two — either by transfer to a different guild position, or by stepping back to a regular dockmaster role under a successor. He does not yet want to leave the docks entirely; he wants out of being the face of the arrangement. He has not yet asked Vannholt.

Beyond the work, see Halrana through her own slow aging well. Watch the daughter's children grow up.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Tested and weathered. Bero has handled storm emergencies, dockyard fires, brawls between rival barge crews, and one attempted boarding by a hostile captain. He does not freeze. Has a cargo-hook in the satchel and the will to use it.
**Loyalty:** To Halrana. To the guild. To the working captains. To Vannholt within limits. The arrangement does not command loyalty; it commands compliance.
**Breaking Point:** The exposure of the arrangement under conditions that would put him personally on trial. He would not protect Volke at his own cost.

## Daily Routine

- **Pre-dawn:** Up. Halrana's tea.
- **Dawn:** At the wharf. Reviews the previous shift's logs. First barges of the day.
- **Mid-morning:** Continuous dock activity. Bero is visible, present, audible. The wharf is at its busiest.
- **Midday:** Quick meal at a wharfside food stall, often standing.
- **Afternoon:** Continuous dock activity. The afternoon's heaviest commerce.
- **Late afternoon:** Shift handoff to the evening senior dockmaster (Bero does not work the late shift; the second senior dockmaster covers).
- **Evening:** Walks home through the New Town. Halrana. The flask, more than it should be.
- **Night:** Reads occasionally — half-elven histories from his upriver origins. Sleeps short nights.
