# Master Rolf Vannholt

Human, male, mid-fifties. Master of the River Traders Guild for the past seven years. Came up through barge captaincy — fifteen years of his own barge before transitioning to shore-based trading and then to guild leadership. Now sits at the top of one of the most politically weighty guilds in Silberbach and has been deferring a critical strategic decision for two years.

---

## Appearance

Tall, broad through the shoulders, the durable build of a man who handled barge cargo for two decades. Brown hair gone iron-grey, beard trimmed close. Weather-darkened skin from twenty years on river decks; the tan does not entirely fade despite the past decade's office-bound work. Strong hands with the specific calluses of a river-man — rope, poling-staff, cargo hooks.

Wears the master's robe of deep-blue wool with silver trim at formal functions. Works in plain wool with a captain's coat in the office — a personal touch that signals to other river traders that he has not forgotten where he came from. The Master's pendant of office (the poling-staff-and-hook in silver) on a heavy chain.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Pragmatic authority.**
Vannholt grew up with the river trade's particular working ethics — practical accommodations, durable relationships, the long-term value of letting some things lie. He runs the guild on those principles. The harbor accommodation with the Crescent House is the most visible expression: a long-running arrangement that produces small revenue and avoids large complications, by design.

**Flaw: Has been deferring the harbor accommodation question for two years.**
The arrangement may be becoming politically untenable. Vannholt knows this. He has thought about it carefully and at length. He has not made a decision. The deferral is not laziness — it is the genuine difficulty of the choice, where any direction has costs. But the cost of continuing to defer is also real and is now beginning to compound.

---

## Background

Born to a river-trade family at a smaller settlement upstream of Silberbach. Apprenticed at fourteen as a barge-hand. Earned his captaincy at twenty-eight after working through every position on a working barge. Captained his own barge for fifteen years across the Angerap and the broader regional river network. Married Halena at thirty-two (a river-trade family's daughter; the network connection was useful but the marriage was its own thing). Two sons, both in the trade — one captains his own barge, one runs a smaller trading operation in a downstream city. One daughter, who married a merchant from the southern empire and lives a great distance away.

Transitioned to shore-based trading at forty-three, after a near-disaster on the lower river that almost cost him his barge and three crewmen. Built a successful trading house from forty-three to forty-eight. Stood for warden at forty-five and won; took on the dock-operations warden role for three years; was elected Master at forty-eight when his predecessor retired. Has held the chair for seven years.

Halena lives at their house in the New Town. They are quietly happy.

---

## Voice

Direct, slightly slow, river-cadence — the kind of speech rhythm developed by spending years on barge decks where shouting carried but quieter speech did not. Drops into specific maritime register easily and is comfortable in the council's more formal register when needed.

**Default Conversational Move: Take the long view.**
Vannholt frames decisions in their long-term consequences — what the decision produces over five years rather than what it produces this week. The frame is partly his own working temperament and partly a useful counterweight to the dockmasters' immediate-pressure register that surrounds him.

**Quirks:**
- Keeps a pole-staff in the corner of his office; touches it occasionally when thinking
- Says "the river" with specific reverence; the Angerap is, in his framing, a living counterparty
- Smokes a pipe in the evenings — never during the working day
- Has a small carved wooden barge on his desk that his elder son made for him as an apprentice gift twenty years ago

---

## Relationships

**Halena (his wife)** — Foundation. The river-trade family network introduced them; the marriage has been its own working partnership for over twenty years.

**Three children** — All in their own lives, all visited rarely. The elder son captains his own barge and Vannholt sees him most often, at the docks, on professional terms.

**The three wardens** — Working partnership. Dock-operations warden (manages Bero and the dockmasters, executes the harbor accommodation operationally) is the most politically loaded of the three. Vannholt trusts him within limits.

**[[Senior_Dockmaster_Bero|Senior Dockmaster Bero]]** — The face of the guild's daily dock authority and the operational executor of the harbor accommodation. Vannholt respects him; Vannholt is also aware that Bero is wearing thin under nine years of executing an arrangement that has become uncomfortable.

**[[Harren_Volke|Harren Volke]]** — Counterparty in the harbor accommodation. They have met twice. The arrangement is conducted at one operational remove (the dock-operations warden handles the relationship; the tribute flows through Bero's channel; Vannholt has structurally distanced himself from direct contact).

**The other guild masters** — Working professional respect. Vannholt is closer to Master Vohlmann (Brewers) and Master Halberd (Carpenters) than to Sorren (Weavers; Vannholt is privately uncomfortable with the Corvel investigation's shadow) or to Master Kessel (Merchants; politically more active than Vannholt finds comfortable).

**Count Elias Valtor** — Direct working counterparty. Vannholt meets with the Count's chamberlain monthly and with the Count personally several times a year. The relationship is correctly professional. Neither pretends warmth.

**The downstream guild masters** — Network relationships across the river. Vannholt corresponds regularly with the masters at three downstream city guilds and travels downstream for major negotiations once or twice a year.

---

## Independent Goal

Make the harbor accommodation decision. Either commit to it openly (which has political risks) or end it cleanly (which has different political risks but at least closes the question). Continue the guild's stable management until a successor is identifiable.

Beyond the guild, see his sons keep their barges and operations afloat; see Halena well; eventually retire to a small house downstream where he can watch the river rather than work it.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** High and weathered. Vannholt has handled storm-emergencies on the river, hostile boarding attempts twice, and one near-mutiny in his early captaincy. He does not flinch in physical danger; he does not initiate it either.
**Loyalty:** To Halena. To the guild. To the river. To Bero (within limits) for years of service.
**Breaking Point:** A river-trade catastrophe that exposes structural weakness in the guild. He would respond with full institutional weight. Also: a personal betrayal by Bero that revealed deeper compromise than Vannholt currently believes is present. He has been watching for this and has not seen it.

## Daily Routine

- **Pre-dawn:** Up. Reads the previous day's dockmasters' reports.
- **Dawn:** Walks to the River House from his New Town residence.
- **Mid-morning:** Office work, correspondence, contract reviews.
- **Late morning:** Meeting with the dock-operations warden; sometimes with Bero directly.
- **Midday:** Refectory at the River House.
- **Afternoon:** Variable. Council meetings on relevant days. Direct meetings with major captains in port. Occasional trips to the docks to walk the wharf.
- **Late afternoon:** Reconciliation with the wardens.
- **Evening:** Home. Halena. The pipe.
- **Night:** Reads — river histories, some regional politics, occasionally older texts in the trade languages he learned as a young captain.
