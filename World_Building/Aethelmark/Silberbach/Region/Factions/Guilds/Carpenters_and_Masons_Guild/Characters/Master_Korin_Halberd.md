# Master Korin Halberd

Human, male, late fifties. Master of the Carpenters and Masons Guild for the past four years. Master carpenter by trade. Operational warden for eight years prior to his elevation to the chair. The consensus successor when the previous Master died of heart failure four years ago — a deliberate choice by the guild to follow a more politically active leader with a steadier one.

---

## Appearance

Tall, broad-shouldered, the build of a working carpenter who has not yet stopped working entirely. Brown hair gone mostly grey, full beard trimmed practically. Dark brown eyes, weathered face, hands that show forty years of timber work — calluses, an old splinter scar along the right index finger, the slight thickening of long use across both wrists.

Wears the master's robe of stone-grey wool with oak-brown trim at formal functions and plain working clothes most other times. The Master's pendant of office (the adze-and-chisel crest in iron) on a heavy chain at council meetings; on a peg by his office door otherwise.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Steadying presence.**
Halberd is not flashy. He is the master who keeps the guild's working register calm, the contracts honored, the apprentices trained, the masters' council meetings on schedule. He inherited a guild that had been run more politically by the previous Master and was elected explicitly to settle it down. Four years in, the guild is settled. People who needed steadying have been steadied. He has done what he was hired to do.

**Flaw: Avoids confrontation when confrontation is necessary.**
The steadying instinct sometimes shades into not pushing hard enough on issues that need pushing. The masonry-side standards have slipped slightly under his tenure because two masonry masters have been resisting updates and Halberd has not forced the issue. He is aware of this. He has not yet decided what to do about it. His operational wardens have been pointing at the problem for over a year.

---

## Background

Born to a carpenter family in Silberbach — three generations of the trade. Apprenticed at fifteen to a master outside the family (the family wanted him learning under outside discipline). Built his own master operation at thirty-two. Married Lera at thirty-four — daughter of a stonemason family, an arranged match that became a love marriage in the second decade.

Three children: Halen (eldest, settled in the river trade as a barge factor), Bren (middle, married a baker and helps run the bakery), Mirre (youngest, married a smith from Aldenberg territory and lives in the mountains). None in the trade. Halberd does not push.

Lera died six years ago in a fever winter. Halberd has not remarried. Lives alone in the family quarters at the Builders' House with a small staff that handles his domestic life. Reads more than he used to.

Took on operational warden role at fifty. Stood for Master at fifty-six and won by acclamation when the previous Master died. Has held the chair for four years.

---

## Voice

Steady, slightly slow, careful with words. The cadence of a man who has spent his career talking to clients about timber specifications and listening to them describe what they want. Does not waste sentences but is comfortable with longer ones than Vohlmann or Reinhart. Drops into specific technical register when the conversation calls for it.

**Default Conversational Move: Acknowledge the question.**
Halberd starts answers by reframing what was asked back to the asker, then proceeds. The reframing is partly his teaching habit (he has trained many apprentices) and partly a way of giving himself a beat to think.

**Quirks:**
- Carries a small carpenter's pencil behind his ear; uses it for sketching solutions on whatever surface is to hand
- Sketches when thinking — joinery, structural problems, occasionally idle drawings of timber framing
- Says "Lera would have noticed that" occasionally, to himself, when something domestic surfaces
- Reads at the Builders' House refectory in the evenings; the staff have learned to leave him alone

## Relationships

**Lera's memory** — Walking quietly through every working day of his life since fifty-four.

**His three children** — All settled, all visited rarely. He writes more than he visits. The grandchildren scattered across three regions; he sees them less often than he would like.

**The three wardens** — Working partnership. The carpentry-side warden is reliable; the masonry-side warden has been pushing the standards issue and Halberd has been not-quite-acting on it; the commercial warden is competent.

**[[Master_Joren_Vesker|Master Vesker]]** — Senior carpenter master who runs the manor account. Halberd has known him for twenty years. Trusts him completely on the manor work. Considers him a candidate for an eventual warden seat if Vesker wants one.

**The two resistant masonry masters** — The current friction. Halberd does not dislike them. He simply does not yet want the fight that confronting them will produce.

**Master Heinrek Vohlmann (Brewers)** — Working professional respect at the council. Halberd is closer to Vohlmann than to most other masters; the two operational temperaments suit each other.

**Master Edda Reinhart (Tanners)** — Working professional respect. Halberd voted for her at her election. They confer occasionally at council on matters that touch both trades.

**The Count's chamberlain** — Direct counterparty for the keep maintenance contract. Cordial professional relationship.

---

## Independent Goal

Hand the guild on to a successor who is more willing to push than he is. Halberd does not yet know who. He has another five to ten years in office and is not in a hurry.

Beyond the guild, see the grandchildren more. Read more. Eventually go visit Mirre in Aldenberg territory; he has not been to the mountains in fifteen years.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Above average. Halberd has stood in front of unhappy noble clients, building accidents in progress, and apprentice families whose sons died on his sites. He does not flinch. Physically he is sixty and not fighting fit; would be effective for a brief situation only.
**Loyalty:** To Lera's memory. To the guild. To Vesker as ally. To the working masters who keep the guild functioning.
**Breaking Point:** A construction accident that kills an apprentice through standards negligence. Halberd would finally force the masonry standards issue and would be unforgiving in doing so.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Cold water. Walks to the Builders' House office.
- **Mid-morning:** Office work — contracts, correspondence, the council's paperwork.
- **Late morning:** Walks the Builders' House depot, sometimes inspects active sites within the town.
- **Midday:** Refectory. Reads while eating.
- **Afternoon:** Variable. Council meetings on relevant days. Apprentice and journeyman reviews. Master-to-master meetings.
- **Late afternoon:** Reconciliation with the wardens.
- **Evening:** Refectory again. Reads.
- **Night:** Quarters at the Builders' House. Sleeps comfortably.
