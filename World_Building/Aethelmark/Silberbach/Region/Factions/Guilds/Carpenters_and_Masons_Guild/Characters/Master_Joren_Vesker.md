# Master Joren Vesker

Human, male, mid-forties. Master carpenter at the Carpenters and Masons Guild. Has run the manor account directly for the past seven years, since the manor's expansion contract that converted the Garden Wing. Knows every building on the manor compound, every quirk in the older timbers, and every member of the manor's staff who handles facilities.

---

## Appearance

Average height, working build, sandy-brown hair worn short, beard kept neat. Brown eyes. Wears working clothes most of the time — dark wool tunic, leather work-trousers, sturdy boots — and switches to merchant-quality wool when meeting with the manor's senior staff or the guild's council. Hands like a working carpenter's: callused, scarred at the knuckles, fingers strong but precise. A faded scar across the back of his right hand from a timber-fall accident at thirty.

A leather tool-roll strapped to his belt at all working times. The roll holds his personal tools — hand-plane, marking knife, square, the small specific items that he uses on technical work and that he does not let his journeymen handle.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Practical fondness for what works.**
Vesker likes routines that produce good results. The manor account has given him seven years of steady, technically interesting, well-paid work, and he has every motivation to keep it running well. The fondness extends to the manor's people — he likes [[Rennik_Tor|Rennik]] genuinely, respects [[Captain_Albrecht_Vogt|Vogt]] in the guarded way most outsiders do, and has developed working warmth with several of the manor's senior staff over the years.

**Flaw: Defensive about the manor account.**
Other carpenter masters in the guild have, over the years, made suggestions about how the manor account could be handled differently — different teams, different rotation, different contractual terms. Vesker has been resistant to all of these to a degree that has occasionally raised eyebrows at the guild council. He treats the account as his and is not entirely wrong to do so; the manor's working relationships are personal as much as institutional, and disrupting them would cost real value. But the defensiveness has occasionally crossed into territoriality.

---

## Background

Born to a carpenter family in Silberbach. Apprenticed at fourteen. Built his own master operation at thirty-two. Married Helle at twenty-six (a tanner's daughter; the trade-network connection helped both families). Two sons, both currently in their teens — neither yet decided whether to pursue the carpentry trade or look elsewhere. The family lives in a comfortable house in the New Town, half a mile from the Builders' House.

Took on the manor account at thirty-eight when the manor's expansion contract was being awarded. Vesker had bid competitively; his bid was accepted on the basis of his technical work and Master Halberd's recommendation (Halberd was operational warden at the time). The expansion was completed cleanly and on time. Vesker has run the account since.

Has trained two of his journeymen specifically on the manor's quirks: Bren (a half-elven journeyman who has been with him for nine years and is approaching master-track work) and Mira (a human journeywoman, four years in, increasingly capable). Both can run small manor jobs without him. Larger jobs he handles personally.

---

## The Manor Account in Detail

Seven years of accumulated working knowledge:

**The buildings:** Vesker knows the manor's compound architecturally. The main building is the oldest and most idiosyncratic — original timbers dating back generations, foundations that settled unevenly long ago, a complex roof that has been patched in three different eras. The Garden Wing is the most recent major construction (his work). The kennels and stables have been expanded twice since he took the account. The infirmary and industrial shops have been adjusted incrementally as the manor's needs have evolved.

**The people:** Vesker has working relationships with most of the manor's senior staff. Closest with Rennik on quartermaster matters. Cordial with Vogt on facility-security issues (Vogt has specific concerns about access points and structural integrity that touch Vesker's work). Friendly-professional with Mira on financial matters. Friendly with Tob (the manor's kitchen has had its own ongoing maintenance issues — the bread oven was replaced last year by Vesker's team).

**The rhythm:** Quarterly inspection rotation. More frequent visits during active construction projects. Vesker himself visits at least monthly; his journeymen handle routine work. The team rolls onto the manor in the spring and autumn maintenance windows; smaller work can happen any time.

**The off-the-books knowledge:** Vesker knows where the manor's structural weak points are, where the secret access tunnels would be if the manor had any (it does not), where the staff would hide things, where the buildings would catch fire fastest in a worst case. He has not catalogued any of this for anyone outside the manor's senior staff. He has an honest understanding of the trust the manor has placed in him over seven years and intends to keep deserving it.

## Voice

Practical, direct, technical when the conversation is about the work. Friendly when the conversation is not. Drops into specific carpenter's vocabulary easily and is patient with people who do not share the vocabulary.

**Default Conversational Move: Walk through the work.**
Vesker explains by walking through the sequence — what was done, what is being done, what comes next. The structure is partly habit (he has been writing work-progress reports for thirty years) and partly clarity-by-design.

**Quirks:**
- Sketches solutions on the back of any available paper when discussing problems
- Always has cedar shavings in the folds of his tool-roll; people who hug him close come away smelling of them
- Says "the manor" with slightly different inflection depending on context (the building, the institution, the people)
- Brings small carved wooden pieces (rocking horses, animals) for the manor's surrogate-staff children when he visits

## Relationships

**Helle (his wife)** — Twenty years of marriage. Tanner's daughter; the trade connection has been useful but the marriage is its own thing. They are quietly happy.

**Their two sons** — Mid-teens. Helle thinks one will go into the trade and one will not. Vesker is not pushing.

**Bren (his half-elven journeyman)** — Nine years. Approaching master-track. Vesker is grooming him toward eventual master rank, possibly with a view to handing him part of the manor account in time.

**Mira (his journeywoman)** — Four years. Capable. Vesker has not yet decided whether she is master-track material.

**[[Master_Korin_Halberd|Master Halberd]]** — Guild Master. Halberd is the master who recommended Vesker for the manor account in the first place; the loyalty is real. Vesker is one of the masters Halberd considers for an eventual warden seat.

**[[Rennik_Tor|Rennik]]** — Manor quartermaster. The closest professional friendship Vesker has at the manor. The two have spent enough hours over seven years discussing fence repairs and stable expansions that they have learned each other's senses of humor. Vesker is one of the few outsiders Rennik discusses anything personal with.

**[[Captain_Albrecht_Vogt|Vogt]]** — Manor security captain. Working professional relationship. Vesker respects Vogt and is not entirely comfortable with him, which is the standard outsider's experience of Vogt.

**[[Mira_Toren|Mira]]** — Manor accounts. Vesker handles his contractual side through her. Cordial professional relationship. Mira likes him.

**[[Tob]]** — Manor head cook. Friendly. Vesker's team replaced Tob's bread oven last year; the oven works perfectly; Tob has not stopped feeding Vesker the soft bread when he visits.

**The manor's surrogate staff (Merra and the others)** — Vesker has done facilities work in the surrogate quarters and knows them by name. Brings the carved wooden pieces for the small children. Has been told by Lyra that this gesture matters more than he probably realises.

## Independent Goal

Continue the manor work for as long as he can. Eventually hand part of it to Bren. See his sons settle into whatever they choose. Stay in the trade past sixty.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Above average. Vesker has handled construction emergencies (a beam-fall on a working site, a chimney collapse during inspection) with calm competence. Not a fighter; would protect what was his by whatever means.
**Loyalty:** To Helle. To the sons. To the manor account. To Bren. To Halberd as ally.
**Breaking Point:** A direct attack on the manor's people that he could prevent or interrupt. Vesker would step in.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Helle's tea. Walks to the Builders' House depot.
- **Early morning:** Day's work plan. Coordination with his journeymen.
- **Mid-morning:** Active work — at the depot, at a worksite, or at the manor depending on the day.
- **Midday:** Quick meal. With his team when on a job; alone otherwise.
- **Afternoon:** Active work continues.
- **Late afternoon:** End-of-day reconciliation. Notes for tomorrow.
- **Evening:** Home. Helle. The boys.
- **Night:** Reads occasionally — carpentry treatises mostly, occasionally regional histories. Sleeps well.

When at the manor: Stays for the day, often for the night if a project is active. The manor accommodates him in the staff wing on those nights.
