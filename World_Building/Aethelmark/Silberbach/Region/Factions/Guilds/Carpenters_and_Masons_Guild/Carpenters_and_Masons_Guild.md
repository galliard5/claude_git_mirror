# Carpenters and Masons Guild

The Carpenters and Masons Guild of Silberbach. Combined construction guild covering both timber and stone work — an unusual structure compared to other regional towns, where the two crafts often operate as separate guilds. The combination is historical, dating to a guild merger sixty years ago that has not been undone, and the working relationship between the two crafts within the guild is closer than the equivalent in most regional cities.

Builds, repairs, and maintains: the buildings of Silberbach Town, the manor's expansion and ongoing maintenance, the keep's defensive works, the docks and warehouses, the bridges, the mills, and the regional infrastructure that does not fall under specialised craft authority (boats are River Traders' Guild work; metalwork is Blacksmiths').

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A carpenter's adze crossed with a mason's chisel over a stone-and-timber arch
**Colors:** Stone-grey and oak-brown
**Motto:** "From Stone and Timber, Stand"
**Hall:** The Builders' House — a substantial stone building (its own most prominent advertisement) on the east side of the New Town, near the Old Town wall.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Korin Halberd (see below)
**Wardens:** Three — one for carpentry standards, one for masonry standards, one for commerce
**Membership tiers:**
- *Master carpenters and master masons* — independent operators with their own crews, full guild voting privileges
- *Journeymen carpenters and masons* — qualified workers, voting on operational matters
- *Apprentices* — bonded for typically seven years across either craft

The guild also maintains a quartermaster's depot for shared materials (specialty timbers, dressed stone, the larger tools that small shops cannot maintain individually) which is run by the commercial warden. The depot is one of the guild's most useful institutional assets — it lets smaller masters take on jobs they could not otherwise resource.

---

## What They Do

**Daily output:**
- *Town construction and repair* — ongoing maintenance of Silberbach's buildings under the council's contract
- *Private commissions* — noble houses, merchant operations, the manor, smaller private clients
- *Specialty construction* — kennels, stables, mill works, bridge repairs, defensive works
- *Cabinet and finish carpentry* — interior fitting, furniture for clients of rank

**Specialty work:**
- *The keep's defensive maintenance* — a continuous standing contract with House Valtor; the masonry side of this work is some of the most technically demanding the guild handles
- *The manor's kennel and stable maintenance* — ongoing under [[Master Joren Vesker|Master Vesker]], who runs the dedicated team
- *Bridge work on the Silver Bridge and the smaller millrace bridges* — periodic, specialised
- *Religious construction* — the Old Temple's structural maintenance, occasional shrine work

The combined-guild structure produces particular efficiency on projects requiring both crafts simultaneously — kennel construction (timber framing on stone foundation), the manor's expansion work, the keep's repair operations, the bridges. This is one of the strengths Master Halberd cites when fending off the periodic suggestions to split the guild back into separate operations.

---

## Manor Relationship

The manor is a steady, valued client. Ongoing contracts cover:

- **Routine maintenance** — kennels, stables, fencing, gates, the manor's buildings; handled by Vesker's team on a quarterly inspection rotation
- **Construction projects** — the manor's expansion seven years ago (including the Garden Wing conversion) was a major guild contract; smaller subsequent projects (workshop expansions, infirmary improvements) have followed
- **Specialty timber and stone supply** — the depot supplies materials when the manor's enchanter or alchemist need particular components for their own work

[[Master Joren Vesker]] handles the manor account directly on the guild's side. He has run the manor team for seven years (since the expansion contract) and knows the manor's buildings intimately. Visits at least monthly, more often when work is active. Relationship with [[Rennik_Tor|Rennik]] (manor quartermaster) and with [[Captain_Albrecht_Vogt|Vogt]] (security captain, on facilities-related issues) is strong.

---

## Master Korin Halberd

Current Master of the Guild. Human, male, late fifties, master carpenter by trade. Took the master's chair four years ago after the previous Master's death from heart failure. Halberd was the consensus successor — operational warden for eight years prior, well-regarded across both crafts within the guild, no major political enemies.

**Appearance:** Tall, broad-shouldered, the build of a working carpenter who has not yet stopped working entirely. Brown hair gone mostly grey, full beard trimmed practically. Dark brown eyes, weathered face, hands that show forty years of timber work. Wears the master's robe of stone-grey wool with oak-brown trim at formal functions and plain working clothes most other times. The Master's pendant of office (the adze-and-chisel crest in iron) on a heavy chain.

**Personality:**
- *Core trait:* Steadying presence. Halberd is not flashy. He is the master who keeps the guild's working register calm, the contracts honored, the apprentices trained, the masters' council meetings on schedule. The previous Master was more politically active and slightly more dramatic; Halberd is the corrective, by deliberate guild choice.
- *Flaw:* Avoids confrontation when confrontation is necessary. Halberd's steadying instinct sometimes shades into not pushing hard enough on issues that need pushing. The masonry-side standards have slipped slightly under his tenure because two of the masonry masters have been resisting standards updates and Halberd has not forced the issue.

**Background:** Born to a carpenter family in Silberbach. Apprenticed at fifteen to a master not in the family (the family wanted him learning under outside discipline). Built his own master operation at thirty-two. Married at thirty-four; widow now (lost his wife six years ago to a fever winter). Three grown children, all settled in their own lives, none in the trade.

**Voice:** Steady, slightly slow, careful with words. The cadence of a man who has spent his career talking to clients about timber specifications and listening to them describe what they want. Does not waste sentences but is comfortable with longer ones than Vohlmann or Reinhart.

## Master Joren Vesker

Senior carpenter master who runs the manor account directly. Human, male, mid-forties, master carpenter, took on the manor account when he was thirty-eight at the time of the manor's expansion contract. Has run it since.

**Appearance:** Average height, working build, sandy-brown hair, beard kept neat. Brown eyes. Wears working clothes most of the time and switches to merchant-quality wool when meeting with the manor's senior staff or the guild's council. Hands like Halberd's but younger.

**Personality:** Practical, straightforward, fond of the manor work specifically because the maintenance rhythm gives him steady predictable income and the construction projects are interesting. Likes [[Rennik_Tor|Rennik]] (the two have a working rapport that approaches friendship) and respects [[Captain_Albrecht_Vogt|Vogt]] without being entirely comfortable with him.

**Manor working relationship:** Direct, frequent, technically substantive. Knows every building on the manor compound. Has trained two of his journeymen specifically on the manor's quirks — old timbers in the main building that have specific repair requirements, the unusual stone foundation under the kennels, the Garden Wing's conversion specifications.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** High operational weight. Construction touches every other trade. The Carpenters and Masons are essential to nearly every building project in the region and command commensurate political weight at the masters' council.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral. The guild ships construction services to every house — Valtor (the keep), Kreiger (their estate), Kaelen (Hall maintenance), all the others. Halberd's tenure has explicitly not aligned the guild with any political faction.

**Manor relationship:** Steady, valuable, respected. Treated as a professional client without political coloring.

**Investigations:** None. The guild's books are clean. The masonry standards issue is internal and operational, not legal.

## Hooks

- **The masonry standards problem.** Two masonry masters have been resisting Halberd's standards updates. The standards are arguably overdue for refresh. Someone needs to force the issue.
- **A keep maintenance issue surfaces.** A structural problem in one of the older keep towers. The Count expects it fixed quickly. The guild's most experienced mason for that kind of work is one of the resistant masters.
- **The manor wants a major expansion.** A new building to house specific operations. The contract would be substantial. Vesker wants it; another master wants it; Halberd has to decide.
- **An apprentice is killed in a construction accident.** Standards investigation reveals contributing factors that touch the masonry standards issue.
- **The Silver Bridge needs repair.** A periodic structural maintenance issue. The work is highly visible and politically sensitive (the bridge is the symbolic spine of the town). Halberd must select carefully.
