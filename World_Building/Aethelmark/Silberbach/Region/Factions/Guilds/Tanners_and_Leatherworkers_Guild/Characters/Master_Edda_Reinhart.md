# Master Edda Reinhart

Half-elven, female, late fifties. Master of the Tanners and Leatherworkers Guild for the past six years. The first tanner (rather than leatherworker) to hold the master's chair in a generation, elected in a contested vote that turned on whether the guild was over-prioritizing leatherwork concerns at the expense of the tanning yards' working conditions. Built her own three-yard tannery operation over twenty years before standing for Master.

---

## Appearance

Tall, sinewy, the kind of build that comes from forty years of tanning yard labor refined by half-elven longevity. Long pale hair worn in a single thick braid down her back, mostly silver now. Pale grey eyes, sharp-featured face. Pointed ears partially visible when her hair is up, which is most of the time.

Wears practical clothing — a heavy linen tunic over leather work-trousers — even at most guild functions, switching to formal wear only for inter-guild masters' council meetings. Hands callused beyond ordinary measure, with the kind of staining around the nail beds that no amount of washing fully removes. The Master's pendant of office (the curved tanner's knife-and-awl crest in copper) on a leather thong, not a chain — Reinhart's own touch when she took office.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Earned authority.**
Reinhart did not come up through political maneuvering. Forty years of work in the tanning yards, her own three-yard master operation built from one over two decades, and an election win that recognised actual labor experience over political polish. Her authority is grounded in the simple fact that she has done the worst job in the guild for longer than most of her colleagues have been masters.

**Flaw: Holds grudges quietly.**
Reinhart does not perform offense. She remembers slights with permanent precision. The leatherworker masters who opposed her election are still on the guild's council; she has not forgiven any of them, has not retaliated, has not forgotten. Her memory sometimes inflects council decisions in ways that are not strictly substantive — a slightly slower response on a matter the opposing master cares about, a slightly tighter standard applied to that master's contracts. Subtle. Visible only to those who know to look. Effective.

---

## Background

Born to a half-elven family in a smaller settlement up the river — a community of half-elves and humans living together, the settlement that produced [[Dena]] (the manor's infirmary assistant) some thirty years later. Reinhart left the settlement at twenty to apprentice in the Silberbach tanning yards, the only place in the region with a guild structure that would accept her.

The apprenticeship was brutal. Tanning yard work in any era is hard on the body; in the sixteenth-century it is harder still, and the chemical exposure is genuinely dangerous. Apprentice retention in the yards has historically run at less than half over the seven-year bond. Reinhart survived. She has not romanticised this. She has also not forgotten how few of her cohort made it through with her.

Built her own master operation at thirty-five — one yard, then two, then three over the following twenty years. Married Halren, a tanner colleague, at thirty-eight. Had a daughter, Maren, when she was forty-two. Halren died when Reinhart was forty-six in a tanning yard accident — chemicals that should have been stored separately had been stored together by an apprentice who had not been adequately supervised. The supervisor was not Halren. Reinhart did not pursue legal recourse against the supervisor's master because the guild's politics at the time made such recourse impossible. She has not forgotten this either.

Maren is now twenty-three and a master tanner in her mother's largest yard. The relationship between mother and daughter is functional and warm in a quiet register.

Reinhart became operational warden at fifty-one, one of the wardens for tanning standards. Stood for Master at fifty-two and lost; stood again at fifty-three and won.

## Master's Tenure

Six years in office. The guild has held together better than many predicted at her election. Specific structural achievements:

**Tanning yard standards:** Working conditions in the three guild-overseen tanning yards have measurably improved. Apprentice retention is up. Chemical storage rules are now enforced. Two senior masters who were notorious for unsafe practices have been reformed (one by being warned, one by being fined enough times to take the message).

**Leatherworker integration:** The split between the two crafts within the guild is a perennial structural tension. Reinhart has not solved it but has held the balance better than her opponents predicted. She has resisted leatherworker pressure to have a leatherworker warden hold the commercial portfolio (Reinhart appointed a tanner instead, who has done the job competently).

**Manor relationship:** The most valuable client account. Reinhart has handled this carefully — letting [[Master_Yorik_Halsten|Master Halsten]] continue to run the technical relationship with [[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]] without political interference, while ensuring the contract terms are reviewed annually for fairness. Halsten is one of the leatherworker masters who supported her election, which makes the arrangement easier.

**The grudges:** Three of the leatherworker masters who opposed her election are still on the council. Reinhart has not retaliated against any of them in any public way. Her wardens are aware that she pays close attention to their contracts and standards reviews. The wardens manage this carefully. The three masters know.

## Voice

Direct, rougher cadence than most masters, with a slight half-elven length to the vowels that does not soften the directness. Uses fewer words than the Silberbach norm and means each one. Drops into half-elven phrases occasionally — usually when speaking with members of her own background, but sometimes deliberately when she wants a moment of unpolitical warmth.

**Default Conversational Move: Cut to substance.**
Reinhart skips the conversational preliminaries. She names what is being asked, names what is being offered, and asks whether the trade is acceptable. The directness reads as rude to nobles and unfamiliar merchants and as refreshing to working tradesmen. She has not adjusted to either reaction in six years.

**Quirks:**
- Touches the back of her left hand absently when thinking — old chemical scars, gone numb in patches
- Drinks small beer at council meetings while other masters drink wine
- Refers to her late husband as "Halren" without elaboration; never "my husband"
- Wears the master's pendant on leather rather than the traditional chain — her one piece of personalisation in office

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In the council chamber. A leatherworker master who opposed her election has just made a long argument for adjusting collar contract terms in the leatherworkers' favor.)*
*(Beat.)*
"No. The terms are fair. The manor has not asked for renegotiation."
*(Looks at Halsten directly.)*
"Halsten — anything to add?"

## Relationships

**Maren Reinhart (her daughter)** — The other gravity in her life. Master tanner in her mother's largest yard. They confer daily on operations and weekly on the larger picture. The relationship is the closest Reinhart has, by some distance.

**Halren's memory** — Walking quietly through every working day of her life since forty-six. She has not remarried. She has not entertained the question.

**[[Master_Yorik_Halsten|Master Halsten]]** — Senior leatherworker master who runs the manor collar contract. Supported her election. Trusted ally. The two confer privately as needed; Reinhart trusts his judgment on leatherworker-side matters and lets him run them with minimal oversight.

**The two wardens** — Operational and commercial. Both are tanners by trade (Reinhart's appointments). Both are competent. Reinhart's working partnership with them is professional and effective.

**[[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]]** — Manor's lead enchanter, the person on the manor side who handles the collar specifications. Reinhart has met her three times across the six years. Each meeting was substantive and brief. Reinhart respects Elowen's craft and Elowen respects Reinhart's enforcement of leather quality. They are not friends. They are excellent counterparties.

**[[Mira_Toren|Mira]]** — Manor accounts. Cordial professional relationship. Mira handles the formal contract administration; Reinhart signs off annually.

**The three opposing leatherworker masters** — The grudges. Reinhart does not see them socially; sees them at council; speaks to them only in formal capacity.

**Master Heinrek Vohlmann** — Brewers Guild Master. Cordial professional relationship. They meet at council. Reinhart respects Vohlmann's operational seriousness; Vohlmann respects Reinhart's hard-won authority. Neither performs the friendship.

**[[Dena]]** — They have not met. Both are from the same upriver settlement, generations apart. Reinhart became aware of Dena's hire at the manor through guild channels; has not pursued any introduction. The settlement they share would be a complicated topic of conversation between them. Possibly someday.

## Independent Goal

Hand the guild on to a successor who will continue protecting the tanning yard's working conditions. Reinhart does not yet know who this is — possibly her daughter Maren in another fifteen years; possibly one of her current wardens; possibly someone not yet visible. The succession question matters to her more than the guild's politics matter to her, and is one of the few things she is patient about.

Beyond the guild, see Maren build a life that is not purely the family operation. Maren has not yet married. Reinhart does not push.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** High and weathered. Reinhart has faced down apprentices' families during accident inquests, hostile noble clients, and the masters' council vote that elected her. She does not flinch. Physically she has the half-elven longevity advantage and the tanning-yard-survivor's hardiness, and would be more dangerous in a brief fight than her age suggests. Not a fighter; not soft.
**Loyalty:** To Maren. To the tanning yards' working conditions. To Halren's memory. To the guild's institutional integrity. To Halsten as ally. To the manor as honored client.
**Breaking Point:** Another preventable tanning yard death due to negligence by another master. Reinhart's response would not be measured. The kind of response is up to the GM but it would not stay private.

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Tea. Walks to the largest tanning yard from her quarters (she has a small set of rooms at the Tanners' House but lives most weeks at the yard).
- **Mid-morning:** Yard inspection. Talks with foremen. Talks with apprentices.
- **Late morning:** Office work at the Tanners' House — contracts, correspondence, the council's paperwork.
- **Midday:** Quick meal. Often standing.
- **Afternoon:** Variable. Council meetings on relevant days. Inspections at the other two yards on rotation. Master-to-master meetings.
- **Late afternoon:** Reconciliation with the wardens.
- **Evening:** With Maren when their schedules align. Alone otherwise. Eats simply.
- **Night:** Reads — half-elven histories mostly, occasionally tannery treatises. Sleeps short nights.
