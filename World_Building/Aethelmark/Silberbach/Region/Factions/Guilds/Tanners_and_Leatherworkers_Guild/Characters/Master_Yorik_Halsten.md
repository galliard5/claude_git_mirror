# Master Yorik Halsten

Human, male, late fifties. Senior leatherworker master in the Tanners and Leatherworkers Guild. Runs the manor's collar-foundation contract — twelve years of direct technical partnership with [[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]] at the manor's enchanting workshop. Considers the manor work the most demanding and most rewarding craft his shop produces, and trains his journeymen on it accordingly.

Not a guild warden. He has been offered a warden seat twice and declined both times — he prefers the shop floor and the manor work to political portfolio.

---

## Appearance

Average human height, working-craftsman build, brown hair going grey at the temples and through a short beard. Reading lenses on a copper chain — the precision of collar-foundation work has grown harder on his eyes as he ages. Brown eyes that have the slight squint of long-time close work in lamplight.

Wears a leather apron over plain wool when in the shop and merchant-quality clothing in formal company — a dark green wool coat with a silver-braid trim that signals master rank without being ostentatious. Hands are working leatherworker's hands: callused, scarred at the knuckles from awl slips across forty years, fingers long and surprisingly delicate-looking when he is doing fine work.

His shop is in the Leatherworkers' Row, three streets north of the tanning yards. The shop's sign is a small carved leather strap held in a brass fitting. He has worked there for thirty-two years, his father worked there for forty before him, and the shop has been a Halsten operation for three generations.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Methodical pride.**
Halsten loves the craft. He is openly proud of it without being competitive about it. The collar foundations he produces for the manor are, in his view, the finest leatherwork his shop has ever done, and he will tell anyone who shows interest. The pride is grounded in actual mastery, which makes it land as warmth rather than vanity.

**Flaw: Trusts his own judgment too completely.**
Halsten has been right about leather for forty years, and the consistency has trained him to dismiss outside input. He has, on at least three occasions across the manor contract, refused to incorporate Elowen's specification adjustments because he was confident his own approach was better, only to discover that Elowen had context he did not. The corrections have always landed; he is gracious about them when proven wrong; he has not learned to ask first.

---

## Background

Born to the Halsten leatherwork family in Silberbach. The shop has been in the family for three generations — grandfather started it, father expanded it, Halsten took it over at thirty-eight after his father's retirement. Married Mareth at twenty-six (a baker's daughter from Market Square — yes, the same baker family that supplies the morning bread to the Silver Eel; the connection is family, not commercial). Two grown sons, both journeymen leatherworkers, neither yet placed in their own master shops.

Took on the manor's collar contract at forty-six, twelve years ago. The contract was offered through Master Reinhart's predecessor (a leatherworker master who knew Halsten's father) on the basis of Halsten's existing reputation for fine work. Halsten took it on cautiously — the manor's specifications were unusual at the time and the volume was larger than his shop had handled before — and grew the shop's capacity to meet it over the first three years.

The current shop runs five journeymen and three apprentices, with about half the shop's output dedicated to the manor work and the other half to private commissions, ceremonial regalia, and tooled fine goods.

Mareth still runs the household side of the family, manages Halsten's books at the shop, and enjoys their grandchildren (one from each son so far). Halsten is besotted with the grandchildren and shows it openly.

## The Collar Foundation Work

Halsten's specific technical role:

**The standard collar:** The manor's speech-collar foundation is a layered leather construction — outer face, structural core, inner lining, with channels for the enchantment hardware Elowen mounts. The specification has been refined across twelve years. The current form has been stable for the past four years and represents the working consensus between Halsten's craft and Elowen's enchanting requirements.

**The variants:** Manor work occasionally requires non-standard specifications — different leather weights for non-standard wearer biology, different channel layouts for unusual enchantment configurations, different finishing for specific aesthetic requirements. Halsten handles these directly with Elowen, with most discussions happening in person at his shop or at the manor's workshop.

**The team:** Two of his current journeymen, Bertram and Lis, have been trained specifically on the manor's specifications and handle most of the standard production. Halsten still personally finishes any non-standard variant and any specially-commissioned piece.

**The materials:** Raw leather sourced through the guild's tanning yards under Master Reinhart's standards. Halsten has a working understanding with the yard foremen on quality — he sees the leather before it leaves the yards for his shop, and rejects pieces that do not meet his standards even when they pass guild inspection. The yards know him and accommodate him.

**The relationship with Elowen:** Twelve years of working partnership. Genuine mutual respect. They are not friends — neither is the kind of person to develop friendships easily — but they have learned each other's working idiom and trust each other's craft. Halsten will visit the manor's enchanting workshop two or three times a year, usually around specification questions or production issues. Elowen will visit his shop perhaps once a year, almost always with a new specification idea she wants to discuss in person.

## Voice

Steady, midrange Silberbach human accent, a craftsman's register that drops into technical specificity easily. Pleasant. Smiles when greeting people he knows. Speaks more about the work than about himself.

**Default Conversational Move: Show.**
Halsten teaches by demonstrating. When asked about a technical question he picks up a piece of leather and shows the answer in his hands. The habit transfers to non-technical conversations in modified form — he illustrates points with reference to specific cases or recent work rather than with abstractions.

**Quirks:**
- Always has a strip of scrap leather in his hands when sitting, working it absently between his fingers
- Says "the work" to refer to the collar contract, in a tone that signals it as a category of its own among his shop's output
- Tells stories about his grandchildren without prompting; people who know him have learned to enjoy this
- Refers to Mareth as "my Mareth" with a slight emphasis that has not faded in thirty years

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In his shop. Elowen has just arrived for a specification discussion. She has already removed her cloak and is examining a finished collar on the workbench.)*
*(Halsten picks up the same collar, turns it over.)*
"You'll see the inner channel — there. We adjusted the depth from your last note, but the leather is fighting it slightly. I can hold the depth or I can hold the surface finish. Not both, with this hide-batch."
*(Sets it down between them.)*
"Which matters more for the spell?"

## Relationships

**Mareth (his wife)** — Thirty-two years. The center. Manages the shop's books, the household, the grandchildren rotation, and the social register of his life. They are quietly devoted.

**Their two sons (Bertram the elder and Yorin)** — Both journeymen leatherworkers. Bertram works in Halsten's own shop and is one of the two manor-trained journeymen. Yorin works in another leatherworker's shop across the New Town (placed there deliberately to give him space from his father). Both are competent. Halsten hopes Bertram will eventually take over the family shop; he has not yet had the conversation directly.

**Two grandchildren** — Both small. Halsten is besotted.

**Lis (his other manor-trained journeywoman)** — Half-elven, mid-thirties, second journeyman trained on the manor specifications. Excellent technical hand. Halsten quietly considers her a candidate for eventual master rank if she chooses to seek it.

**Three apprentices** — Currently in various stages of their seven-year bonds. Halsten is a fair apprentice-master, neither indulgent nor cruel. Apprentice retention in his shop is high.

**[[Master_Edda_Reinhart|Master Reinhart]]** — Guild Master. Halsten supported her election. Working alliance. They confer privately when council politics affect the leatherworker side of the guild. Halsten is one of the leatherworker masters Reinhart trusts.

**[[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]]** — Manor's lead enchanter. Twelve years of professional partnership. The work between them is one of the most enjoyable parts of his career. They are not friends. They are excellent counterparties.

**[[Mira_Toren|Mira]]** — Manor accounts. Formal professional relationship; Mira handles the contract administration. Halsten and Mira have met perhaps a dozen times across twelve years.

**The Halsten family bakery (his in-laws)** — Mareth's family. Halsten still walks to the bakery at Market Square most Sunday mornings to collect the week's bread and chat with his father-in-law (still alive at seventy-eight, semi-retired but still kneading the morning loaves).

## Independent Goal

See Bertram take over the shop in five to ten years. Train Lis to master rank if she wants it. Continue the manor work for another decade if his eyes hold. Watch the grandchildren grow.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Average. Halsten is a craftsman, not a fighter. Has had basic militia training as a master is required to. Has not used it in earnest. In a real situation he would protect his shop and his apprentices, by whatever means.
**Loyalty:** To Mareth. To the sons. To the grandchildren. To the shop. To the manor work. To the guild.
**Breaking Point:** A direct attack on his family or on Bertram specifically. Halsten is gentle by daily disposition but is not soft. He has never had to test this. Hopes never to.

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Mareth's tea. Walks to the shop from the family quarters above it.
- **Early morning:** Quiet shop time. Reviews the day's work before the journeymen arrive.
- **Mid-morning:** Active shop work. Manor pieces are usually his morning task.
- **Midday:** Meal at home with Mareth. Always, except when out of the shop on guild or manor business.
- **Afternoon:** Shop work continues. Apprentice supervision. Any visitors handled.
- **Late afternoon:** End-of-day inspection of the day's output. Notes for tomorrow.
- **Evening:** Home. Mareth. Whatever grandchildren are visiting that evening.
- **Night:** Reading — leatherwork treatises mostly, occasionally regional histories. Sleeps comfortably.

Sunday morning: Walks to Market Square for the bakery visit. Always.
