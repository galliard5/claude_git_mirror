# Tanners and Leatherworkers Guild

The Tanners and Leatherworkers Guild of Silberbach. Operates the tanning yards along the south bank of the Angerap (downwind of the town for a reason) and the leatherworkers' shops scattered through the New Town. Produces all hide-derived goods for the region: harness, saddles, boots, work garments, restraint hardware, military leather, and the foundational leather components that the manor and other specialty workshops finish with enchantment, tooling, or hardware.

One of the more politically weighty guilds in Silberbach by virtue of operational integration with House Aldenberg's leather demands (Aldenberg's mountain forges need substantial protective leather work) and the manor's extensive collar and restraint commission stream.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A tanner's curved scraping knife crossed with an awl over a stretched hide
**Colors:** Brown and ochre
**Motto:** "From Hide to Hand"
**Hall:** The Tanners' House — a substantial brick-and-timber building on the New Town's south side, near the dye works (the smells overlap regularly), with the principal tannery yard adjacent and the leatherworkers' shops one street north.

---

## Structure

**Master Tanner of the Guild:** Master Edda Reinhart (see below)
**Wardens:** Three — one for tanning standards (raw hide processing), one for leatherwork standards (finished goods), one for commerce (contracts and disputes)
**Membership tiers:**
- *Master tanners and master leatherworkers* — independent operators, full voting privileges
- *Journeymen* — the working backbone of the guild, voting on operational matters
- *Apprentices* — bonded for typically seven years; the trade is harder on the body than brewing or carpentry, and apprentice retention is lower than in those guilds

The guild's structure unusually splits two related crafts under one roof. Tanners produce the cured hides; leatherworkers shape the finished goods. The two crafts have somewhat different temperaments and politics — tanners are accustomed to outdoor labor in unpleasant conditions and command high wages for it; leatherworkers do indoor craft work and command different wages. The guild's structure has historically favored leatherworker leadership in the master's chair, though Master Reinhart's tenure has shifted that balance somewhat.

---

## What They Make

**Daily output:**
- *Cured hides* — raw material output to leatherworkers, harness-makers, bookbinders, and other downstream trades
- *Boots and work garments* — basic clothing for laborers and craftsmen
- *Harness and saddlery* — for stables, carriage operations, and military use
- *Tooled leather goods* — belts, satchels, scabbards, the household ordinary

**Specialty work:**
- *Restraint hardware* — the leather components for binding equipment of various kinds; supplied to the manor in regular bulk and to other clients on commission
- *Speech-collar foundations* — the leather backing on which Elowen's enchantments at the manor are mounted; the manor purchases these in lots of 50 to 100 at a time and finishes them at the manor's own enchanting workshop
- *Heavy protective leather* — the layered work that goes into Aldenberg's forge protective wear and into noble military equipment
- *Decorative tooled work* — high-end commissions for noble households, ceremonial regalia, fine goods

The collar foundations alone are a substantial revenue stream. The manor's commission has run continuously for roughly twelve years and is the largest single ongoing contract on the leatherworkers' books.

---

## Manor Relationship

The manor is the Tanners and Leatherworkers Guild's largest single client by revenue, a fact the guild's commercial warden has been quietly grateful for during the past decade of regional economic shifts. The relationship runs on multiple channels:

- **Collar foundations** — bulk orders, placed seasonally, to a precise specification the manor refined over years with the guild's leatherworkers
- **Restraint hardware** — varied commissions, smaller volumes, more complex specifications
- **Standard manor leather** — boots and work garments for the manor's staff, supplied through the standard guild commercial channel

The collar foundations specifically are produced by a small team of senior leatherworkers under [[Master Yorik Halsten|Master Halsten]], who runs the most established of the leatherwork shops within the guild. Halsten has handled the manor's collar work for the entire twelve-year run of the contract and has trained two of his current journeymen specifically on the manor's specifications. The relationship between Halsten and [[Elowen_Vale|Elowen]] (the manor's lead enchanter) is the operational engine of the collar work — they coordinate directly on any specification adjustments, with the formal contract handled at the guild-and-Mira level.

## Master Edda Reinhart

Current Master of the Guild. Half-elven, female, late fifties, master tanner by trade — the first tanner to hold the Master's chair in a generation, succeeding three consecutive leatherworker masters. Elected six years ago after a contested guild vote that turned on whether the guild was over-prioritizing leatherwork concerns at the expense of the tanning yards' working conditions.

**Appearance:** Tall, sinewy, the kind of build that comes from forty years of tanning yard labor refined by half-elven longevity. Long pale hair worn in a single thick braid down her back, mostly silver now. Pale grey eyes, sharp-featured face. Wears practical clothing — a heavy linen tunic over leather work-trousers — even at most guild functions, switching to formal wear only for inter-guild masters' council meetings. Hands callused beyond ordinary measure, with the kind of staining around the nail beds that no amount of washing fully removes.

**Personality:**
- *Core trait:* Earned authority. Reinhart did not come up through political maneuvering; she came up through forty years of work in the tanning yards, built her own master operation from a single yard to three over twenty years, and won the master's chair in a vote that recognised actual labor experience over political polish. Her authority is grounded in the simple fact that she has done the worst job in the guild for longer than most of her colleagues have been masters.
- *Flaw:* Holds grudges quietly. Reinhart does not perform offense, but she remembers slights with permanent precision. The leatherworker masters who opposed her election are still on the guild's council; she has not forgiven any of them, has not retaliated, and has not forgotten. Her memory sometimes inflects council decisions in ways that are not strictly substantive.

**Background:** Born to a half-elven family in a smaller settlement up the river; came to Silberbach at twenty as an apprentice in the tanning yards. Survived the apprenticeship, which is not guaranteed in that trade. Built her own master operation at thirty-five. Married a tanner colleague at thirty-eight, widowed at forty-six (a tanning-yard accident — chemicals badly stored). Has a daughter from that marriage, now adult and working as a master tanner in her mother's largest yard.

Has been Master for six years. The guild has held together better than many predicted, partly because Reinhart's political flaw is contained by her substantive competence and partly because her wardens have been good ones.

**Voice:** Direct, rougher cadence than most masters, with a slight half-elven length to the vowels that does not soften the directness. Uses fewer words than the Silberbach norm and means each one. Drops into half-elven phrases occasionally with members of her own background.

## Master Yorik Halsten

Senior leatherworker master who runs the manor's collar-foundation contract. Human, male, late fifties, Silberbach-born. Took over his father's leatherwork shop at thirty-eight; took on the manor's collar contract at forty-six (twelve years ago). One of the senior leatherworker masters in the guild and a respected figure in his trade, though not currently a warden.

**Appearance:** Average height, working-craftsman build, brown hair going grey at the temples. Reading lenses on a chain — the precision work of collar foundations is harder on the eyes as he ages. Wears a leather apron over plain wool when in the shop and merchant-quality clothing in formal company.

**Personality:** Methodical, technically focused, friendly without being effusive. Loves the craft and is openly proud of the manor work specifically — he considers the collar foundations the highest expression of his shop's capability and trains his journeymen on them with corresponding care.

**Manor working relationship:** Direct correspondence with Elowen on technical matters; periodic in-person meetings at his shop or at the manor's enchanting workshop when a specification requires it. The two have a warm professional relationship of the kind that develops between two craftspeople whose work depends on each other's quality.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** High operational weight. The guild is one of the most-trafficked in Silberbach because nearly every other trade buys leather. Master Reinhart's political weight at the masters' council is solid — neither dominant nor marginal.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral, with a quiet lean toward Kaelen reformist positions. Reinhart has not made the alignment public, but she has voted with the Kaelen-aligned guilds on several council matters and her wardens know her preferences. The manor's substantial business is part of why she has not been more open about this — Reinhart does not want to make the manor's commission appear politically transactional, which would damage both the manor and the guild.

**Manor relationship:** The most valuable single client. Treated correctly, professionally, and with quiet warmth at the master's level.

**Investigations:** None. The guild's books are clean. The tanning yards are dirty in the literal sense but operationally honest.

## Hooks

- **An Aldenberg protective-wear contract goes wrong.** A shipment of forge protective leather is rejected by Aldenberg as substandard. Reinhart needs to know whether the fault is hers, Halsten's, or someone in between — fast, before the relationship damage accumulates.
- **The manor wants a new specification.** A collar foundation variant for a use case that is unusual even for the manor. Halsten consults Reinhart, Reinhart considers the political implications, and the answer is interesting.
- **An apprentice dies in a tanning yard accident.** It happens. The family grief, the guild liability, and the question of whether the standards held are all pieces Reinhart has to manage in parallel.
- **A leatherworker master accuses Halsten of taking guild secrets to private contracts.** The accusation is politically motivated (one of the masters who opposed Reinhart's election); the substance is more complicated than the accuser realises.
- **A new noble client wants restraint hardware with specifications the manor would consider questionable.** The guild can refuse, accept, or refer. Reinhart has to decide.
