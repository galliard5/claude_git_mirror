# Bakers and Butchers Guild

The Bakers and Butchers Guild of Silberbach. Combined guild covering the two food-production trades — an unusual structure, like the Carpenters and Masons combination, dating to a guild merger sixty-something years ago. The two crafts retain their distinct identities (a baker is not a butcher) while sharing institutional structure, which produces some operational tension and considerable working efficiency on supply matters that touch both (animal fat for pastry work, sausage casings, the working relationship with the Tanners Guild on hides).

Operates: the bakeries scattered through Silberbach (the major bakery on Market Square that supplies the Silver Eel, several smaller neighborhood bakeries, the dedicated bakeries that serve specific noble households), the butcher shops along the small lane behind Market Square known as Butcher's Way, and a small smokehouse on the Angerap's south bank.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A baker's paddle crossed with a butcher's cleaver over a round loaf
**Colors:** Wheat-cream and dark red
**Motto:** "By Loaf and Cleaver"
**Hall:** The Bakers' and Butchers' House — a modest building on a side street off Butcher's Way, smelling at all times of bread and smoke depending on which way the wind is blowing.

---

## Structure

**Master of the Guild:** Master Hanna Vesley, human, late fifties, master baker. The Bakers' and Butchers' Guild has alternated its master's chair between the two trades by informal tradition for the past four masters; Vesley is the current baker-master and her predecessor was a butcher.

**Wardens:** Two — one for bakery operations and standards, one for butchery operations and standards.

**Notable masters:**
- *Master Berten, the elder baker* — head of the Berten family bakery on Market Square; supplies the Silver Eel; father-in-law to [[Master_Yorik_Halsten|Master Yorik Halsten]] of the Tanners and Leatherworkers Guild (Halsten married Berten's daughter Mareth thirty-two years ago). The Berten family connections across the trades are dense; Halsten still visits the bakery on Sunday mornings.
- *Master Garven, butcher* — runs the largest butcher operation on Butcher's Way; supplies most of the noble households and the manor's regular meat contract. The previous Master before Vesley.
- *Master Renik, baker* — runs the bakery that supplies the Old Temple's daily bread (a charity-rate contract that has been honored across three generations of Renik bakers).

---

## What They Make

- *Daily bread* — the foundation of Silberbach's daily diet; multiple bakeries, multiple grades from common loaf to fine white bread for noble tables
- *Meat* — fresh, smoked, salted; supplied through shops and market stalls
- *Sausages* — extensive variety, regional specialties
- *Pastries and pies* — both savory (meat pies for working lunches) and sweet (the Silberbach honey-cake is a guild specialty)
- *Holiday and festival breads* — seasonal specialties tied to the religious calendar
- *Contract supply* — to the noble households, the manor, the keep, the temple, the larger inns

---

## Manor Relationship

Steady, unremarkable. The manor receives daily bread (delivered by one of the Halsten Senior bakery's apprentices, usually before dawn) and weekly meat orders (delivered from Garven's operation through the standard kitchen channel). Tob handles the manor side. The contracts are clean and have been steady for years.

The manor's specialty needs — soft bread for [[Pip|Pip]], specific cuts for the canine residents' premium feed, particular pastries for occasional events — are handled within the standard contract without political weight.

---

## Politics & Position

Mid-tier political weight. The guild is essential but its essential nature does not produce political leverage — everyone needs bread; the bakers' loyalty is purchased the same way and the same price by every faction. Master Vesley is a respected but not dominant voice at the masters' council.

Officially neutral on regional politics. The Halsten Senior bakery supplies the Silver Eel, which puts it in working contact with the broader town network; this has not produced political alignment.

No current investigation involvement.

---

## Hooks

- **A bread shortage during a poor harvest.** The guild has to coordinate with the Farmers and Brewers (the brewers compete for grain) on rationing.
- **A botulism case from a butcher's smokehouse.** Investigation, liability, the question of which master's standards slipped.
- **A festival bread commission turns political.** A noble house wants an unusually elaborate festival bread for a specific event; the politics of the event are sensitive; Vesley has to decide.
- **The Halsten bakery faces a succession question.** Master Halsten Senior is approaching seventy. Who takes over? The connections to the Tanners Guild side of the Halsten family are part of the conversation.
- **A meat-supply price-fixing accusation.** Multiple butcher masters accused of coordinating prices. The guild's own dispute resolution must address it before external authorities do.
