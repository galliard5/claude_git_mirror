# Weavers Guild

The Weavers Guild of Silberbach. One of the larger guilds by membership and one of the most politically weighted by virtue of the textile economy's centrality to regional commerce. Operates the major weaving shops on Weavers' Row, coordinates with the Fullers and Dyers Guild on finished cloth production, and supplies the regional textile trade through standing contracts and the seasonal Wool Market.

Currently shadowed by the Corvel investigation. The guild's Master appears on Aldus Corvel's client list (per Oswin Brandt's signed statement); a Weavers' Row textile merchant appears as one of Corvel's complicit absorbers of laundered funds. Whether the laundering is personal involvement by Sorren or whether it extends through guild infrastructure is the question Constable Ferris's investigation has not yet answered.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A weaver's shuttle crossed with a distaff over a bolt of cloth
**Colors:** Indigo and ivory
**Motto:** "Thread Binds the Loom"
**Hall:** The Weavers' House — a substantial three-storey building at the head of Weavers' Row, with the main weaving shops behind it and the merchant shops along the row itself.

---

## Structure

**Master Weaver of the Guild:** [[Sorren|Master Sorren]] — currently named in active investigation
**Wardens:** Two — one operational, one commercial
**Membership tiers:**
- *Master weavers* — independent operators with their own shops
- *Journeymen* — qualified workers, voting on operational matters
- *Apprentices* — bonded for typically seven years

The guild also coordinates with a network of independent merchants who buy finished cloth from member shops and sell it through Silberbach and the river trade. These merchants are not formally guild members but are tied to the guild through standing contracts; [[Hauss]] is one such merchant.

---

## What They Make

- *Common cloth* — wool and linen for daily wear, household textiles, working garments
- *Fine cloth* — wool of higher count, linen with finer weave, blends for noble household use
- *Specialty work* — the Weavers' Row merchants' showcase product, including some imported-thread weaves and seasonal patterns
- *Tapestry work* — small-scale (the larger ceremonial tapestries are imported); the manor and several noble houses commission from a handful of senior masters
- *Bolt cloth for the dye works* — supplied to the Fullers and Dyers Guild as raw stock for finishing

The Weavers Guild is a major upstream supplier to other regional textile activity. Their cloth feeds the dye works (Fullers and Dyers), the leatherworkers' garment-side production (Tanners and Leatherworkers), the bakers' and butchers' apron supply, and the noble households' standing wardrobes.

---

## Manor Relationship

Steady commercial contract:

- *Standard household linens* — bedsheets, towels, kitchen cloth
- *Staff garments* — tunics and trousers for the manor's working staff (the manor purchases bolt cloth and has its own seamstress make up garments to the manor's specifications)
- *Specialty textiles* — for the Garden Wing (interior fabrics, finer linens), for the infirmary (specific medical-grade linens that have particular cleaning requirements)
- *Surrogate Staff garments* — particular specifications, larger volume than ordinary

The contract has been steady for years. The Master's name on the Corvel list does not directly affect the manor's textile contract — the contract is operationally clean — but it has made Mira watchful. She has mentioned the situation to Isalia, and Isalia has indicated the manor will continue the contract until or unless the investigation surfaces evidence that affects the supply itself.

---

## Politics & Position

**Within the guild structure:** High political weight. Textiles are central commerce; the Weavers' Master sits prominently at the masters' council.

**Position on regional politics:** Officially neutral, but Sorren personally has been more politically active than most masters — visible at noble functions, present in the social register of the town in a way Cael (Farmers) and Vohlmann (Brewers) deliberately are not. Whether this political activity is connected to the Corvel arrangements is part of the investigation's question.

**Manor relationship:** Steady contract. Investigation's shadow is on the Master, not currently on the contract.

**Investigations:** **Active.** Sorren is named on Corvel's client list as seen by Oswin; Hauss is named both on Corvel's client list and as a complicit merchant absorbing laundered funds through inflated invoices. The investigation is ongoing under [[Constable_Ferris]] with [[Voss_and_Kraemer]] handling the legal review.

---

## The Investigation Shadow

The Corvel investigation places two figures connected to the guild under active scrutiny:

[[Sorren|Master Sorren]] — Guild Master. Name on Corvel's list. Nature of involvement unclear; figure amount not recalled by Oswin. The investigation has not yet established whether his involvement is personal financial arrangement or whether it extends through guild infrastructure (which would massively expand the laundering scale and political damage).

[[Hauss]] — Weavers' Row textile merchant. Both named on Corvel's list and identified as one of Corvel's complicit merchants — absorbs laundered funds through inflated invoices on textile transactions. His complicity is more fully established than Sorren's; he is presumably a more imminent investigation target.

The guild as an institution has no current legal exposure — the books at the Weavers' House are clean and the operational framework appears not to be the laundering channel. But the political damage from both Sorren's name on the list and Hauss's confirmed complicity is real, growing, and felt across the council. The two operational wardens are being quietly approached by senior journeymen and master weavers about whether the guild can continue business with these clouds over the leadership.

## Hooks

- **The investigation reaches the guild.** Constable Ferris brings questions to the Weavers' House. Sorren must answer. The guild's wardens watch.
- **A senior master moves to depose Sorren.** Internal political maneuvering takes shape. The grounds are ostensibly the investigation; the actual motivation is the long-running rivalry between Sorren and one specific master.
- **Hauss flees.** Or is arrested. Either way, the scandal reaches the row and the guild has to manage its visible consequences.
- **The manor contract is renegotiated.** Mira, on Isalia's instruction, requests a closer review of the contract's terms. The implication is plain. Sorren must accept, deflect, or escalate.
- **A new Master is needed.** If Sorren is forced out (by investigation or by internal politics), the succession question becomes urgent. Multiple candidates with conflicting agendas.
