Briar
=====

Canine (transformation complete), Female. A military deserter sentenced to lupine transformation and indefinite servitude under House Steinfeld's kennel master, per Count Valtor's judicial decree. Former name unknown — chose the name Briar at the moment of acceptance.

appearance: Pre-transformation — sun-weathered, groom's hands (calloused in the specific way of someone who handles animals daily, not a fighter's calluses). Bound with enchanted rope that glowed cobalt under stress. Smelled of prison straw and old fear. Chin lifted anyway.

Post-transformation — female, Blackwater line (*Canis lupus familiaris*). Black pelt with crimson throat markings — House Steinfeld's colors, specified by Count Valtor's addendum. Kreiger sigil brand, small, on the flank, applied by Dr. Korr post-transformation. Fully cognizant. No speaking collar assigned at time of transfer — treated as canine under Steinfeld's authority unless a collar is granted.

personality: **Core Trait: Stubborn conviction.**
Briar stole a horse and hid her in a valley with clean water and grass rather than sell her or use her. That is not a thief's logic. It is someone who acted on what they believed was right and accepted the consequences rather than recant.

**Flaw: Tends toward claims that cannot be proven.**
Her accusations against the Steinfeld breeding program were specific and detailed — pressure points, healing salves timed to inspections, labor accelerants. Isalia's evidence didn't support it. Whether she was wrong, deceived, or telling a truth the records were designed not to show is unresolved.

background: Stole an imperial courier's mare — prize stock, six generations of Steinfeld breeding lineage. Did not sell her. Hid her in a valley south of Steinfeld's pastures, with grass and clean water, away from what Briar described as systematic mistreatment of the breeding mares.

Isalia investigated the claim against her own inspection records, Alric's notes, and Timothy's testimony. The evidence did not support Briar's account. Briar maintained the mistreatment was concealed before inspections using the estate's own alchemy shed. The claim was noted but not confirmed.

Count Valtor's sentence: lupine transformation, heightened aggression suppressants permanent, servitude under House Steinfeld's kennel master. The aggression suppressants required either gelding or female form — Lyra cited historical data showing female judicial transformations average 18% lower mortality rates and better working conditions. Briar chose female form. She chose the name Briar at the same moment.

The transformation was administered by Alric using a Blackwater line specimen. Dr. Korr applied the Kreiger brand and the Steinfeld pelt markings post-procedure.
