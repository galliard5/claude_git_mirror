House Aldenberg
===============

Barony. Controls the mountain passes leading to the northern mines. Known for producing the finest steel in the region. Formally neutral in the Valtor-Kaelen dispute despite an old marriage tie to House Valtor that has effectively decayed into historical footnote.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A silver anvil on a black mountain
**Colors:** Black and silver
**Motto:** "By the Hammer, Ours"
**Seat:** Aldenberg Hold — a stone-and-iron fortress carved into the mountainside above the largest of the northern mines, half a day's ride from Silberbach.

---

## Holdings

**Primary Seat:** Aldenberg Hold, in the northern mountains
**Territory:** The mountain passes leading from the river valley up to the silver, iron, and gemstone mining regions. The Hold sits at the strategic chokepoint — anyone moving ore down to Silberbach passes through Aldenberg toll-stations, and anyone moving finished steel back up does the same.
**Vassals:** Two minor knightly families holding small holdings in the passes; one bonded master forge family who pay rent in kind (one in twelve blades produced)
**Income:** Mountain pass tolls, mining concessions on three of the seven northern mines (the rest belong directly to Valtor or to the Empire), the Aldenberg forges' steel production, and a smaller revenue stream from gemstone polishing and setting work done in the Hold itself

---

## Standing & Influence

Aldenberg's regional weight comes from one fact: their steel is the best for two weeks' ride in any direction. The blades, plate, and weapons they produce are sought across the Empire and beyond, and the forge masters who work for House Aldenberg are the children and grandchildren of forge masters who worked for House Aldenberg. The technique is institutional, the quality is generational, and it cannot be replicated quickly elsewhere.

This makes Aldenberg structurally important to every regional power that wants to be armed. House Valtor relies on them for weapons and armor — the garrison's blades and the Count's personal guard armor are Aldenberg-forged, and the relationship is ongoing and contractual. House Kaelen has bought from them, less frequently, at full price. House Steinfeld has commissioned cavalry equipment. The Empire has placed bulk orders for imperial garrisons across three administrative regions.

The Baron and Baroness understand the position they occupy and have made a deliberate strategic choice: they are useful to everyone, and they pick no side. Neutrality is the policy, and the policy is enforced internally with the same care the family applies to forge quality.

## The Valtor Marriage Tie

A historical note worth recording because it confuses outsiders. Approximately forty years ago, Lady Mira Aldenberg (the present Baron's great-aunt) married Lord Hadrick Valtor, a cousin of the then-ruling Count and not in the direct line of succession. The marriage was politically arranged at a moment when Aldenberg and Valtor were both consolidating power in the river valley.

Hadrick died approximately twenty years later, of a wasting illness, without producing issue. Lady Mira outlived him by a decade and returned to the Aldenberg Hold for her final years. She is buried in the family crypt at the Hold.

The marriage tie therefore exists as a matter of formal record. It does not exist as a present political force. The current Count Valtor and Baron Aldenberg do not refer to one another as kin in any practical sense, and neither family treats the relationship as creating obligation. When Valtor.md notes Aldenberg as a marriage ally, that is technically correct and politically meaningless. The Hold is genuinely neutral.

This is not a secret. It is simply old enough that nobody who matters in either house remembers it as anything but history.

## Key Members

- **Baron Edmund Aldenberg** — Current ruler. Mid-fifties. A serious, careful man who took the Hold from his father twenty years ago and has run it without scandal since. Spent his youth at the forges; can still strike a billet. Believes Aldenberg's neutrality is the family's central strategic asset and has refused several tempting offers to break it.
- **Baroness Helga Aldenberg** — Edmund's wife, dwarven, from a forge-master family in the northern mountains rather than another noble house. The marriage was unusual when made — a baron marrying down rather than across — and entirely successful in the working sense. Helga manages the forge contracts and the Hold's commercial operations; Edmund handles politics and tolls. They confer on everything.
- **Young Baron Stefan Aldenberg** — Heir, late twenties. Currently apprenticing under a senior forge master at the Hold. The expectation is that he will, like his father, know the work before he runs it. He has not yet shown his father's political caution; the family is watching.
- **Lady Margreth Aldenberg** — Stefan's younger sister, mid-twenties. Unmarried. Increasingly involved in the family's commercial diplomacy — has made the trip down to Silberbach four times in the past year, which is more often than her father visits. The forge masters like her. So do several of the merchant families.

## Politics & Secrets

**Alliances:**
- None formally declared. Aldenberg policy is to maintain working relations with all river valley powers without binding to any.
- Strong informal ties to the **forge master guild** in the northern mountains — these are bonded craftsmen, not nobility, but their loyalty to the Hold is generational and institutional.
- Cordial relations with **House Meerhold** through long-distance trade — Aldenberg buys salt for forge work; Meerhold buys finished steel for coastal defense.

**Rivalries:**
- None active. The neutrality policy means Aldenberg deliberately avoids making enemies among the houses they trade with. Internal rivalries among the forge masters and mining concessions are real but stay inside the Hold.

**Position on the Valtor-Kaelen dispute:**
Officially neutral. Both Edmund and Helga are privately uncomfortable with what Anthony Valtor's testimony exposed, but they have not made any public move. Their reading of the situation is that the Empire will ultimately move on Valtor, that Valtor will resist, that the resistance will fail, and that Aldenberg's position is best preserved by not having pre-committed to either side. Their support is the political prize neither house can quite secure — and the family intends to keep it that way until it must be spent.

**Relationship to Manor:**
Cordial. Aldenberg provides occasional steel work to Isalia's manor — collar reinforcements, custom restraint hardware, the iron components of certain enchanted items that Elowen later finishes. The arrangement is small in volume and significant in quality; the manor is a respected client. Lady Margreth has handled the recent commissions personally on her trips down.

## Current Situation

The family is currently navigating the most uncomfortable political moment of Edmund's tenure. Aldus Corvel's client list, exposed during the Steinfeld investigation, includes House Aldenberg — meaning that someone in the Hold has been laundering funds through Corvel's network. Edmund does not yet know which member of the household is responsible, and he is conducting an internal investigation of remarkable discretion.

Three possibilities are live: a senior steward acting without authorization, a trusted forge master moving payments outside formal channels, or a member of the family itself. Edmund has not ruled out any of them. He has shared the situation with Helga and with no one else.

The intelligence reached the Hold via Hannah Voss's transfer to Isalia's manor, which then routed indirectly through Duke Romaine's network back to Edmund. Aldenberg owes the Kreiger family a quiet debt for the warning, and Edmund knows he owes it.

Stefan, the heir, has not been told. The family's political caution holds even within itself.

The pressure on Aldenberg is therefore *internal* — a slow-motion crisis being managed in private. The neutrality policy holds in public. The relationship with Valtor remains contractual. The forges still produce. But the Hold is watching itself.

## Atmosphere & Place

The Hold is built into the mountainside above the largest of the family's mines, with the main living quarters cut directly into the rock. The principal entrance is a heavy iron gate set into a stone façade carved with the family anvil-and-mountain. Inside, the architecture is functional rather than ornamental — broad halls suited to large retinues passing through, side chambers for forge masters and trade delegations, a great hall for formal court that doubles as a guild meeting space when the forge masters convene.

The forges themselves are below — three primary workshops cut deeper into the mountain, each specialised. The heat is constant; the noise is constant; the family quarters are positioned far enough above that the work is audible only as a low rhythmic thrum, like a heartbeat the Hold sleeps to.

Visitors arriving from the valley road travel up a switchback track that climbs for two hours from the lowland. The view from the main gate at the top is across the entire river valley toward Silberbach. The Hold is hard to surprise.
