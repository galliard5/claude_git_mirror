House Waldheim
==============

Barony. Forest territory known for timber and hunting. Supplies the region's bowyers and shipbuilders.

Details: **Crest:** A brown bear rampant on a green pine tree
**Colors:** Green and brown
**Importance:** Controls the eastern forests and timber trade. Supplies bowyers, shipbuilders, and carpenters throughout the region. Their rangers are legendary trackers. Currently in a dispute with House Steinfeld over hunting rights.

**Transformed Rangers:**
Some of House Waldheim's rangers are transformed individuals — wolves, bears, and other forest creatures wearing house heraldry on specially crafted collars. These rangers patrol the woods in forms better suited to the terrain, their heightened senses making them unparalleled trackers. The arrangement is mutually beneficial: the house gains exceptional scouts, and the transformed retain positions of respect and purpose.

**Communication Amulets:**
All transformed rangers receive a communication amulet integrated into their collar, allowing them to speak normally despite their animal forms. This is essential for coordinating patrols and reporting findings. The amulets are crafted by the house enchanters and are standard issue for all transformed in service.

**Military Integration:**
Transformed rangers are fully integrated into House Waldheim's military structure. They hold rank, can earn promotions, and are treated as valued soldiers rather than mere animals. A wolf might serve as a scout under a human sergeant, then earn promotion to lead a tracking unit. This integration gives them a clear career path and stake in the house's success.

**The Collar System:**
[[Isalias_Manor_Kennels_and_Stables]] works closely with both House Waldheim and House Steinfeld to ensure that transformed individuals who wander the forest are not mistaken for game. A specific collar design — green leather with silver bells — marks any transformed person as "not prey." Hunters from both houses are trained to recognize these collars and pass the word to any visiting sportsmen. The system has prevented accidents for years, though it requires constant vigilance during hunting season.
