House Waldheim
==============

Barony. Controls the eastern forests and the timber trade that runs through them. Supplies the bowyers, shipbuilders, and carpenters of the wider region. Their rangers — many of them transformed — are legendary across the river valley. Currently engaged in a low-grade dispute with House Steinfeld over hunting rights along the contested forest-pasture borderlands.

---

## Identity

**Crest:** A brown bear rampant on a green pine tree
**Colors:** Green and brown
**Motto:** "The Forest Knows Its Own"
**Seat:** Waldheim Lodge — a sprawling timber-and-stone hunting lodge at the eastern edge of the forest, two days' ride east of Silberbach by road, longer by forest paths.

---

## Holdings

**Primary Seat:** Waldheim Lodge in the eastern forest
**Territory:** Approximately forty square miles of mixed eastern forest — old-growth oak and pine, scattered clearings used for game management, three logging settlements, and a network of ranger paths that connects every part of the territory to the Lodge within a day's travel.
**Vassals:** Three lodgekeeper families holding subordinate stations in the deeper forest; one logging-settlement reeve who reports directly to the family; the Sergovy line, a senior cadet branch holding their own smaller estate within Waldheim territory.
**Income:** Timber sales (the largest income, supplied to bowyers, shipbuilders, and carpenters across three administrative regions), game and hunt fees (visitors pay for the right to hunt under ranger supervision), tanned hides and antler/bone work, and a smaller revenue stream from medicinal herbs and forest products gathered under licence.

---

## Standing & Influence

Waldheim's regional weight is grounded in the forest itself. The eastern forest is the only sustained source of high-quality timber within several days' reach of Silberbach, and the family's management of it is professional, generational, and deeply institutional. Bowyers across the empire know Waldheim yew. Shipbuilders downriver know Waldheim oak. The family's relationship with the timber market is the closest thing the river valley has to a textile or salt monopoly.

Their rangers compound this influence. The Waldheim rangers are not merely foresters — they are a trained military auxiliary, fully integrated into the house's command structure, with the transformed members serving alongside human and half-elven members under shared rank. They patrol the forest, escort logging operations, conduct visiting hunts, and provide reconnaissance for any military operation the family is contracted into. The ranger force is small (roughly forty active members) but the unit's reputation greatly exceeds its size.

The family's standing is therefore practical rather than political. They are listened to in matters concerning the forest. They are largely left alone in matters concerning everything else.

## Key Members

- **Baron Rolund Waldheim** — Current ruler. Sixties. A weathered, practical man who came up through the ranger corps before inheriting the Hold from his father. Still walks the forest paths, still recognises every senior ranger, still hunts. Quietly progressive on the question of transformed rangers' status — under his rule, the integration of transformed members into the regular command structure was completed. Married late, has only two children, both adult.
- **Baroness Helewise Waldheim** — Rolund's wife. Late fifties. From a smaller forester family, married into Waldheim for the practical merger of two timber operations. Manages the Lodge's domestic and commercial life; runs the timber accounts and the seasonal contracts personally.
- **Lord Geralt Waldheim** — Heir, mid-thirties. Active ranger captain. Currently leads the most elite of the ranger units (the Greenwatch), which patrols the deepest forest and the contested Steinfeld borderlands. Has not married. Engaged to be the next Baron when his father retires, which Rolund has indicated will be in approximately two years if his health holds.
- **Lady Aurel Waldheim** — Geralt's younger sister, late twenties. Unmarried. Manages the family's communications with external clients (bowyer guilds, shipbuilder commissions, distant noble houses placing timber orders). Travels more than her brother and is in Silberbach at least once a season.
- **Sir Vorek Sergovy** — Head of the Sergovy cadet branch. Distant cousin to Rolund, holds his own smaller estate within Waldheim territory at the Sergovy holding (well to the south of the Lodge, near the contested Steinfeld border). Senior figure in the Greenwatch. Has been positioned by Rolund as a possible regent if Geralt is incapacitated. *(See Sergovy Waldheim scenario, pending.)*

## The Rangers

The Waldheim ranger corps is the house's signature institution. Roughly forty active members, organised into four units of varying speciality.

**Composition:** Approximately one-third human, one-third half-elven (descended from the older families of the deep forest), and one-third transformed. The transformed rangers include several wolves, two bears, three transformed deer (used as scouts; their forms allow them to range in territories the predator-form rangers cannot enter without disturbing the game), and a handful of birds (a hawk and two ravens) used for aerial reconnaissance and rapid messaging.

**Command structure:** Captain Geralt at the top, four unit commanders below him (two human, one half-elven, one transformed wolf — Captain Rhys, who has held the position for eight years and is the senior tracker in the corps). Promotion runs on demonstrated competence. The transformed rangers earn rank, draw pay, and are addressed by their rank-titles like any other member.

**Communication amulets:** All transformed rangers wear collar-integrated communication amulets, allowing them to speak normally despite their forms. The amulets are crafted by the Lodge's enchanter (a half-elven woman named Edda Greenfeather who has held the post for forty years and trained two successors). The standard issue is non-removable in the field — the amulet is keyed to the wearer at fitting and remains active for the rest of their service.

**Distinctive collars:** Green leather with silver bells, marking the wearer as *not prey*. The collar design is the result of the multi-house safety protocol coordinated with [[Isalias_Manor]] and [[House_Steinfeld]] — see The Collar System below.

**Ranger duties:** Forest patrol, anti-poaching, escort of logging operations, escort of hunting parties, reconnaissance, message-running between the Lodge and outlying settlements, anti-banditry, occasional military deployment when the family is contracted into wider operations.

## The Collar System

A multi-house safety protocol coordinated between House Waldheim, House Steinfeld, and Isalia's Manor. The system addresses a real problem: transformed individuals in animal forms could otherwise be mistaken for game during hunting season, leading to fatal accidents.

**The protocol:**
- All transformed rangers in Waldheim service wear the green-leather-and-silver-bells collar
- All transformed individuals contracted out from Isalia's Manor for forest work wear the same collar (the manor maintains a small stock for these arrangements)
- Steinfeld's hunters and Waldheim's hunters are trained to recognise the collar at distance and pass the word to any visiting sportsmen
- Visiting hunters who arrive without familiarity with the system are briefed at the Lodge before any hunt begins
- Violations carry serious consequences: a hunter who shoots a collared individual faces both criminal investigation by the Count's authority and a civil action from whichever house the individual belongs to

The system has prevented accidents for years. It requires constant vigilance during hunting season. The most recent close call was three years ago, when a visiting noble from a distant house took a shot at a transformed scout and was prevented from completing it only because a senior ranger physically pushed his bow down. The visitor was diplomatically banned from Waldheim hunting.

## Politics & Secrets

**Alliances:**
- **House Kreiger** — informal alignment. Duke Romaine's progressive views on transformed individuals match Waldheim's institutional practice; the two families correspond cordially.
- **Isalia's Manor** — operational alliance through the collar system and through occasional contractor arrangements when the manor needs forest specialists.
- **Bowyer and shipbuilder guilds across the region** — long-standing commercial relationships that function as quiet political support; these guilds advocate for Waldheim's interests in their respective regions.

**Rivalries:**
- **House Steinfeld** — active dispute over hunting rights along the contested forest-pasture borderlands. The dispute is genuine but managed; both houses recognise the cost of letting it escalate. Geralt and the Steinfeld baron's senior huntsman have a working relationship and resolve most incidents directly. The collar system is one of the points where the two houses cooperate productively, which is part of what keeps the larger dispute from fracturing into open hostility.

**Secret Agenda:**
None per se. Waldheim is a relatively transparent house — its interests are visible (forest, timber, rangers, autonomy) and its practices are public. What it does keep private is the *internal* politics of the ranger corps, particularly the recent integration of transformed members at command rank. The decision to promote Captain Rhys was contested within the corps at the time, and the resolution required Rolund to spend some political capital both internally and with peer houses who took offence at being expected to address a wolf as Captain. The family does not advertise that this was hard. It simply insists, in every public-facing interaction, that the result is the policy.

**Position on the Valtor-Kaelen dispute:**
Quietly aligned with Kaelen's reform direction without being formally allied. Rolund will not publicly advocate for transformed rights legislation, but he will not vote against it either, and his rangers' visibility is itself an argument in Kaelen's favour. The position is deniable and intentional.

**Relationship to Manor:**
Operational and respectful. The collar system is the most concrete tie. The manor occasionally contracts ranger consultations when working with new transformation candidates whose proposed forms involve forest-living considerations. The manor has recommended Waldheim service to several transformed individuals seeking employment after their adjustment phase, and Waldheim has hired three of those recommendations over the past five years (one is now a senior tracker).

## Atmosphere & Place

Waldheim Lodge is a long, low timber-and-stone building set into a forest clearing, with the family quarters at one end, the ranger barracks at the other, and a great hall in between that doubles as the meeting place for visiting hunts and contracted clients. The Lodge smells of woodsmoke, leather, beeswax (from the polishing), and the resinous register of the surrounding pine. The walls of the great hall are hung with antlers, hunting trophies, and a small collection of portraits — informal, mostly of past rangers rather than past barons.

The grounds are working forest. There is no formal garden. The clearing around the Lodge is kept clear by deliberate management — selective felling, controlled burns, the kind of woodcraft that produces a place that looks natural and is in fact precisely shaped. The boundary of the clearing is patrolled. The forest beyond it is marked.

Visitors approach via the eastern road, which becomes a forest track after the second day's ride from Silberbach. The Lodge has no gate and no walls. The defensive measure is the rangers themselves and the territory's reputation. A traveller who reaches the Lodge unannounced has been observed for at least the last hour of their approach.
