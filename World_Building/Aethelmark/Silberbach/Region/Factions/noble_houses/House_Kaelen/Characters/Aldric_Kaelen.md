# Aldric Kaelen the Younger

Human, male, twenty-two years old. Heir to House Kaelen. Currently studying law at the imperial capital's senior academy. Named after Emperor Aldric in deliberate homage by his parents — a gesture of political alignment that has aged well alongside the boy who carries the name. Distinguished from the Emperor in conversation by the suffix *the Younger*, by his title (he is styled *Lord Aldric* in formal writing), or simply by context.

---

## Appearance

Average height for his age, lean — has not yet filled out the way his father did in his thirties. Dark brown hair cut short for practical reasons (capital fashion is fussier than he has time for). Grey-blue eyes that he inherited from his mother. Wears the plain dark academic robes of the imperial law school by day and Kaelen colours only at the formal occasions the academy requires. Carries an ink-stained leather satchel of correspondence and case notes. The satchel is heavier than his sword (he wears one because it is required of his rank, not because he uses it).

Looks younger than his age, which he has not yet learned to use to advantage. Stands straight when he remembers to.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Inherited seriousness without inherited weight.**
Aldric is, recognisably, his father's son — the same patient register, the same legal habit of clarifying questions before answering them. What he has not yet acquired is his father's accumulated weight of compromise and lost causes. He is at the stage of his life where the ideals are still untouched by the costs of pursuing them. He believes the reform legislation will pass because he believes it must, and he does not yet know how that conviction will erode under twenty years of slow politics.

This is not innocence. He understands the politics intellectually. He has not yet *experienced* them. The difference will eventually matter.

**Flaw: Treats older voices as if they owe him explanations.**
Aldric grew up at Kaelen Hall hearing the reformist case made by his parents and the visiting allies. He absorbed the framework before he developed any other intellectual framework, and the result is that he assumes anyone who disagrees has either failed to think it through or is acting in bad faith. He is wrong about this and does not yet know it. His professors at the academy are correcting him, slowly, by exposing him to genuine well-reasoned conservative legal theory. The corrections are taking. Whether they will take in time is not yet clear.

---

## Background

Born and raised at Kaelen Hall. Tutored at home in his early years, then sent to a regional preparatory academy at fifteen, then to the imperial law school at nineteen. He is in his third year of a five-year course of study and is consistently in the top tier of his cohort.

The choice to send him so far afield was deliberate. His parents wanted him exposed to legal training that did not exist anywhere closer to the river valley, and they wanted him at sufficient distance from House Valtor that any political pressure on the family would not reach him directly. The arrangement has worked. The Count has not moved against the boy because the boy is, in the imperial capital, under the indirect protection of Emperor Aldric's court — which does not announce itself but which is real.

Aldric's specific course of study is the legal personhood of transformed individuals. His thesis, due in his fifth year, will argue from existing case law that the current legal framework is internally contradictory and proposes a unified theory under which transformed individuals retain personhood with specific carve-outs for procedural matters. The framework, if accepted, becomes the basis for reform legislation.

His advisor at the academy is Professor Reinhart Vesper, an elderly half-elven legal scholar who knew Romaine Kreiger in their younger days and who took Aldric on partly out of intellectual interest and partly out of a quiet favour-network the Kaelen family was not aware of when they enrolled him. Vesper has not told Aldric about the favour-network and does not intend to.

---

## At the Academy

Aldric's life at the academy is structured around scholarship and a small circle of like-minded peers. Three classmates regularly work with him on the personhood theory: a half-elven woman named Kresta from the eastern provinces, a human man named Beren whose family is minor nobility from the southern empire, and a tiefling man named Sariv whose presence at the academy at all is a quiet political achievement. The four of them have been working together for two years and produce some of the most-cited student scholarship in the academy's reformist circles.

Aldric is ambivalently aware that his social life is narrow. He has noticed that his peers go out drinking and to musical performances and to other ordinary academic-young-man pursuits more often than he does. He has tried to participate. The participation is awkward enough that he has stopped pushing himself and reconciled to being the studious one. His mother, when she noticed in her last letter, told him not to worry about it. His father did not mention it. Aldric reads his father's silence as approval and is probably correct.

He goes home to Kaelen Hall during academy breaks — three weeks twice a year. The visits are short and his parents try to make sure he is not pressed into political service during them. The arrangement is a kindness. Aldric understands and accepts it.

## Voice

Slightly hesitant when speaking aloud — not from lack of confidence but from the legal habit of not speaking until he has fully formulated. Writes with much more fluency than he speaks. Has a specifically academic register that does not yet adjust well to informal company.

**Default Conversational Move: Cite.**
Aldric supports his positions with case references and existing scholarship. This is what he is being trained to do; it is also what he was raised by his father to do. He has not yet noticed that he sometimes does it when no citation is needed.

**Quirks:**
- Carries his satchel everywhere; it is his anchor
- Quotes case law almost reflexively, often with full citation; classmates correct him gently
- Stutters when caught flat-footed in conversation; recovers quickly
- Writes long letters to both parents weekly; the letter to his father is intellectual, the letter to his mother is personal

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In a corner of the academy library, late evening, surrounded by case books. A classmate has just asked him whether he has any plans for the upcoming feast.)*
*(Pause. Considers the question genuinely.)*
"I had not made any. I should perhaps make some."
*(Returns to his writing.)*
"Beren is going. I will accompany him."

## Relationships

**[[Torvin_Kaelen|His father]]** — The relationship that has shaped him most. Aldric writes weekly. Torvin keeps every letter. The correspondence is intellectual, both sides developing legal theory together; Aldric has surpassed his father in academic rigour and has not yet realised it.

**[[Elara_Kaelen|His mother]]** — The relationship he relies on most. Elara writes shorter letters but more honest ones; she tells him things his father will not (the family finances, the Count's mood, the political weather of Silberbach). He trusts her completely and does not always realise how much.

**Professor Reinhart Vesper** — His academic advisor. Mentor, in the proper sense. Aldric does not yet know that Vesper knew Romaine; the mentorship is real on its own terms and the additional context is held back as protection.

**Kresta, Beren, Sariv** — Three classmates and intellectual collaborators. The closest things to friends he has. He is fond of all three and does not yet have the vocabulary to say so.

**Emperor Aldric** — His namesake; not someone he has met. Aldric is aware that being at the academy puts him geographically close to the imperial court without putting him politically close. He has not sought introduction. His mother has indicated this is correct.

**Anthony Valtor** — Has not met him. Has read transcripts of the testimony. Is preparing to write a major paper on the case as part of his thesis work. Is privately fascinated by the situation and personally outraged on Anthony's behalf, in a young man's way.

**House Valtor in general** — Aldric's view of the Count is shaped by his parents' framing and by his own legal analysis. He thinks the Count is a poor steward, a competent enemy, and not (despite the embezzlement) a stupid man. He has not met him.

## Independent Goal

Complete his thesis. Become the lawyer who drafts the reform legislation. See his parents live to see it pass. See himself become the kind of man his father is, without losing what is currently his (the unspoiled belief that the work is worth the cost).

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Untested. Aldric has had proper noble military training and is competent with a sword in the academic-trained sense. He has not been in a real fight. His response to threat is unknown.
**Loyalty:** To his parents. To the work. To his classmates in that order, with the order being something he has not yet had to test.
**Breaking Point:** Untested. Probably involves a direct attack on his parents or a betrayal by Vesper. Neither has happened.

---

## Daily Routine (at the academy)

- **Dawn:** Up. Reading at his small desk in his rooms.
- **Morning:** Classes.
- **Midday:** Quick meal at the academy refectory.
- **Afternoon:** Library. Research. Writing. Sometimes meets with Vesper or with the working group of three.
- **Evening:** Meal in his rooms or at the refectory. Reading aloud for his own retention (a habit his mother taught him).
- **Late evening:** Letters home. Sleep.

The routine is monastic. He knows it. He has decided he can be more interesting after the thesis is done.
