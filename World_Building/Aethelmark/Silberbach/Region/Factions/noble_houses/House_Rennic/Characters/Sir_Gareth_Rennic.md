# Sir Gareth Rennic

Human, male, mid-sixties. Knight of House Rennic. Military commander of the Eastern Marches. Younger brother to Lord Edric. Has held the marches' military command for thirty-one years and intends to die in the saddle before he hands it on.

---

## Appearance

Shorter and broader than his brother; built like a man who has spent his life in armour and out of it for the same reasons. Iron-grey hair cropped short for the helmet line. Thick beard going white at the chin. Dark eyes, sharp. A diagonal scar across the bridge of his nose from a campaign thirty years ago. Wears Rennic colours in the field with a worn leather surcoat; wears them at the Hall with a cleaner one but does not change anything else. Carries a sword that is older than two of his junior knights and that has been re-pommelled three times. The blade is the original.

Walks with the rolling gait of a long-time horseman. Sits a horse better than any man in the marches and is content to be the second-best swordsman, because he has a third-best swordsman whose loyalty is unshakable and whose duty it is to be in front of him.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Practical bluntness.**
Gareth says what he thinks. Says it plainly. Says it once. People who want him to repeat it can ask, and he will, but the second time will be slightly shorter and considerably less polite. The directness is not rudeness — it is a working soldier's instinct that ambiguity gets people killed and clarity is therefore a moral obligation.

**Flaw: Has limited patience for diplomacy and shows it.**
Gareth's brother handles the diplomatic register. Gareth does not. When circumstances force him into diplomatic conversations he is stiff, abrupt, and visibly relieved when they end. He has been told, by Edric and by others, that this is a problem; he has agreed; he has not changed. The brothers' arrangement (Edric does the diplomacy, Gareth does the military) was settled decades ago specifically because of this and has held ever since.

---

## Background

Born to House Rennic at Rennic Hall as the third child. Eldest brother died at twenty-three; Edric became heir and went to the imperial capital for diplomatic training; Gareth went to the marches' military school at fourteen and never left the marches except for two campaigns elsewhere in his thirties.

Took military command of the marches at thirty-three when his father's old commander retired. Has held it since. Married a marcher knight's daughter forty years ago — Lady Brenna, of a minor knightly family with deep marches roots. The marriage has been quietly happy. Two grown sons (one a captain in the marches' standing force, one a knight in service to House Kreiger), one daughter married to a southern marcher lord.

The marches under his command have been securely held for three decades. Border incursions are rare and dealt with quickly. Bandit activity is limited to the deep forest fringes and is managed by the rangers. Gareth's military reputation is solid rather than spectacular: he has not lost a major engagement, has not initiated one unnecessarily, and has built a force that knows its work.

---

## The Brotherly Partnership

The arrangement between Edric and Gareth is the operational engine of House Rennic's regional position. Edric handles diplomacy, imperial relations, and the political register. Gareth handles military, borderlands operations, and practical security. They confer monthly, share information continuously, and trust each other absolutely.

Disagreements between them are real but contained. Gareth thinks Edric occasionally over-thinks situations that need direct action. Edric thinks Gareth occasionally under-thinks situations that need finesse. Both are sometimes right. The brothers have learned to defer to whichever one's judgment is more relevant to the specific matter, and the deferring goes both directions.

Practical example: when border raiders crossed the eastern boundary three winters ago, Gareth handled the military response (caught them, killed those who fought, returned the rest to imperial authority) while Edric handled the diplomatic aftermath with the eastern empire. Both pieces of work were necessary. Either piece without the other would have produced a worse outcome.

## Voice

Direct. Lower-class plain speech with the marcher accent — the deep-marches register, slower than the river-country vowels and harder on the consonants. Uses few words. Curses regularly and without emphasis (it is just how he talks).

**Default Conversational Move: Specify the action.**
Gareth turns talk into instruction. *What needs doing. Who does it. When.* If a conversation is not producing actionable items at his pace, he ends it and goes do the work himself.

**Quirks:**
- Eats fast and finishes before everyone else; resumes conversation while the rest are still eating
- Removes the scabbard belt before sitting at table; never the sword itself
- Says *brother* in private about Edric and *Lord Rennic* in public
- Sleeps four hours a night and considers more excessive

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In the marches' command tent, looking at a map. A junior knight has just suggested a complex multi-pronged response.)*
*(Long look at the map. Then at the knight.)*
"No."
*(Points at one location.)*
"Here. Tomorrow before dawn. Two columns. You take the north, Bren takes the south. We meet them coming or we meet them not coming. Either way it ends tomorrow."

## Relationships

**[[Lord_Edric_Rennic|Edric]]** — Brother and political partner. The bond is durable and rarely sentimental in expression. They love each other in the gruff register of brothers who grew up together and have outlived the rest of their generation in the family.

**Lady Brenna Rennic, his wife** — His marriage. Forty years steady. She runs the household at the Hall during his absences (which are frequent) and the Hall does not falter. He is gentler with her than with anyone else; she is the one person who sees him fully off-duty.

**Their three children** — Two sons, one daughter. He sees the sons regularly because their work overlaps with his (the captain in the marches' force, the Kreiger-service knight). The daughter he sees yearly when she travels north with her husband to visit. He is closer to her than the sons in some ways — she is the one who calls him on his bluntness.

**[[Lady_Seria_Rennic|Seria, his niece]]** — Fond pride. She has chosen a path he could not have walked himself; he respects her for it. He has been kept apprised of her current situation at the manor and has offered to ride down to the manor himself if she needs him. She has declined politely. He understood and approves.

**Lord Tomas R. Rennic, his nephew (the heir)** — At court. Gareth has not seen him in two years. Will train him in the marches' military when he returns home, if he is willing.

**The marches' standing force** — Forty-five regular soldiers under his direct command, plus sixty-five auxiliaries on a graduated reserve system. He knows every regular by name. Most of the auxiliaries he knows by name as well. The force has a low casualty rate by border-defense standards and a high re-enlistment rate.

**The marcher knights** — Twelve knights of various subordinate families who hold their lands under Rennic obligation and ride with him when called. Mostly older men now; he is bringing on a generation of younger knights through his sons' contemporaries.

**Count Elias Valtor** — Cordial-formal in person; privately not much regarded. Gareth thinks the Count has run his county into political trouble through his own choices and is not inclined to extend much sympathy. Has said so to Edric in private and nowhere else.

**Baron Rolund Waldheim** — Working acquaintance. Gareth respects the rangers and the Waldheim borderlands operation; he has cooperated with Geralt's units on three occasions when raiders crossed both territories. Mutual professional respect.

## Independent Goal

Hold the marches secure for the rest of his service life. Train his eventual successor. Die in the saddle if the saddle is willing.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** High and tested. Gareth has been in over forty serious engagements in his career and has not run from any of them. He has been wounded six times; the bridge-of-nose scar is the most visible. He fights from the front when the situation demands it and from the centre of the formation when discipline demands that instead.
**Loyalty:** To Edric. To his wife. To his children. To his force. To the marches. The order is firm and has never been tested in conflict.
**Breaking Point:** Untested by anything yet survived. Probably involves a direct attack on his wife, his brother, or his force in a betrayal that he could not have anticipated. Likely response: catastrophic violence with no diplomatic cover.

---

## Daily Routine (when at Rennic Hall)

- **Pre-dawn:** Up. Cold water. Inspection of the Hall's guard.
- **Dawn:** Morning drill with whatever subordinates are in residence.
- **Mid-morning:** Office work — reports from the field, dispatches, training schedules.
- **Midday:** Quick meal. With his wife when she is available.
- **Afternoon:** Riding the grounds, visiting the forces' encampments within reach, reviewing the marches' standing.
- **Evening:** Meal at the Hall. With Brenna; with Edric if he is in residence.
- **Late evening:** Reads — military histories mostly, occasionally his daughter's letters more than once. Sleeps four hours.

When in the field, the routine compresses. He sleeps less, eats faster, and is happier.
