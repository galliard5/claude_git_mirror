# Lord Tomas R. Rennic

Human, male, sixteen years old. Heir to House Rennic. Currently in his second year as a page at the Emperor's court. The *R.* in his styled name distinguishes him from his distant cousin Tomas K. Rennic; the family uses the middle initial because both Tomases were named after a shared great-grandfather and the older generation insisted on continuing the name.

---

## Appearance

Tall for sixteen but not yet grown into himself — the Rennic family bones are spare, and he is at the gangly stage. Dark brown hair worn in the court fashion (cut short, oiled, swept back). Pale grey eyes inherited from his father. Clean-shaven; he can almost grow a beard but the result is patchy enough that the court barber discourages him.

Wears the Rennic colours (gold and forest green) in the court's specific page register — formal but limited, since pages are not permitted full noble dress. Carries a small ceremonial knife on a court-issued belt. Has not yet been entrusted with a sword.

Looks slightly nervous in court settings (because he is) and slightly more relaxed in the page school (because he has friends there).

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Watchful absorption.**
Tomas is paying attention. He has been paying attention since he arrived at court at fourteen, and the watching has become his most defining habit. He observes the politics of the court the way his father observes the politics of the marches, and he is being trained — by his father's letters, by his court tutors, by the Emperor's own preferences — to take what he observes and turn it into eventual action.

**Flaw: Has not yet learned to test his observations.**
The watching is real, but Tomas is sixteen and has not yet had the experience of being wrong about something important. His readings of the court's politics are often acute and occasionally deeply mistaken; he has not yet developed the habit of checking his work against people who would push back. His tutors at the page school are working on this. His father's letters do not push back as hard as they should because his father wants him to develop confidence first.

---

## Background

Born at Rennic Hall in the Eastern Marches. Raised primarily by his mother (Lady Calantha) for the first four years, then more by his father after his mother's death twelve years ago. Tutored at home by a series of marches' scholars and visiting imperial-trained scholars; the education was deliberately wide, with a heavy weight on languages, history, and political theory.

Sent to the Emperor's court as a page at fourteen — the standard age, the standard placement for a marcher heir of his rank, but conducted with deliberate care because the Rennic family's diplomatic role makes any imperial placement politically meaningful. He has been at court for two years. Two more years of page service remain before he is recalled home for the next stage of training.

His sister Seria is fifteen years older than he is and feels more like an aunt than a sibling; he respects her professionally and is fond of her personally, in roughly that order. His uncle Sir Gareth he sees yearly when home; he is wary of his uncle's bluntness and impressed by his uncle's competence.

---

## At Court

The Emperor's court accommodates approximately thirty pages at any given time — sons of noble houses across the empire, ages twelve to eighteen. The page school provides a structured education in court protocol, languages, history, and diplomatic theory. Pages also serve practical functions: carrying messages, attending court functions in formal capacity, occasionally serving at the Emperor's table on ceremonial occasions.

Tomas's specific situation has features unique to him:

- **He is being watched.** The imperial intelligencers track all pages, but they track Tomas more attentively because his father is the empire's quiet intermediary in the river valley. Tomas knows this, was told by his father before he came, and has built it into how he conducts himself.
- **He is being prepared.** The Emperor has had two private audiences with him — both brief, both ceremonial in form, both genuinely substantive in content. The Emperor knows who Tomas is and what he is being trained for. The court is aware of this.
- **He is friends with two pages whose families would not have predicted it.** A young man from a southern noble house his father has tense relations with, and a half-elven page from the eastern empire. The friendships are real. They are also exactly the sort of cross-house alliances that diplomatic-track pages are encouraged to form. Tomas is aware of the structural intent and continues to enjoy the friendships anyway.

---

## What He's Studying

The page school's curriculum, plus a tailored supplement his father arranged for through correspondence:

- **Imperial law** — required for all pages; Tomas is in the second tier of his cohort, solid but not exceptional
- **Diplomatic theory** — his father's specific area; the page school's instructors know the family's interest and adjust accordingly. Tomas excels here.
- **Languages** — fluent in Common, conversational in the southern empire's primary trade dialect, just beginning the eastern empire's formal court language
- **History** — particularly regional history of the river valley, an unusual emphasis for a page school. His father wants him to understand the politics he will eventually be navigating.
- **Sword training** — adequate. He is not a natural fighter and his uncle has not yet had the opportunity to assess him properly.

His tutors include Master Verrin, an elderly scholar who once served Tomas's grandfather; Lady Marvelle, the page school's head of diplomatic studies; and a half-elven swordsmaster named Caelin who has been working on his form patiently for two years.

## Voice

Articulate for sixteen. Speaks in the slightly formal register the page school instills. Stutters very slightly when caught off-guard; recovers quickly. Has not yet developed the long-pause-before-answering habit his father has — Tomas answers too quickly because he is sixteen and wants to demonstrate that he was paying attention.

**Default Conversational Move: Demonstrate.**
Tomas shows what he has noticed. He answers a question by displaying the observation that informs his answer, which is structurally how the page school is teaching him to think. The habit is appropriate for the page school and will need to evolve when he becomes a working diplomat.

**Quirks:**
- Touches the hilt of his ceremonial knife when nervous; does not realise he does this
- Writes neatly because his earliest tutor required it; the habit has stuck
- Says *Father* with deliberate weight in correspondence; says *Lord Rennic* in court
- Has begun keeping a private journal of court observations — his father suggested it; he has not yet shown anyone the contents

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In the page school's library, after class. A fellow page has just asked him whether he has spoken to the Emperor recently.)*
*(Slight pause. Sets down his book.)*
"Twice, in the past four months. Both formal."
*(Returns to the book.)*
"He asked about the marches both times. Different questions, in roughly the same way."

## Relationships

**[[Lord_Edric_Rennic|His father]]** — The most important relationship of his life. They correspond weekly. Edric's letters are dense with diplomatic instruction, family news, and gentle correction. Tomas has kept every one and rereads the older ones. He does not know that his father has done the same with his.

**[[Lady_Seria_Rennic|Seria]]** — Distant elder sister. Aunt-like in feel because of the age gap. He admires her. He does not yet know about the Valerius Corvinus situation in detail; his father has not written about it.

**[[Sir_Gareth_Rennic|Uncle Gareth]]** — Wary fondness. Gareth's bluntness is intimidating to a sixteen-year-old. Tomas understands intellectually that the uncle is the practical engine of the marches and respects the work; he is not yet at ease in his uncle's presence.

**Tomas K. Rennic** — Distant cousin, seminary-bound. They have met once in their lives, when Tomas R. was nine. Both of them are aware of the name confusion and resigned to it.

**The Emperor** — Sovereign. Tomas has had two audiences. He thinks of the Emperor with appropriate awe and is aware that his father served the Emperor in a very different capacity decades ago. He has not asked his father about this directly; he is waiting for his father to bring it up.

**Master Verrin, his tutor** — Mentor. The old scholar is fond of Tomas in a way that exceeds the professional. Tomas is not entirely sure why and has stopped wondering; the affection is good and he accepts it.

**Lord Verren of the southern empire (his friend)** — Page-school friend. Genuine companionship. Both of them are aware their houses have political tensions; both of them are friends anyway.

**Caelin, his swordsmaster** — Working relationship. Tomas would like to be a better swordsman than he currently is. Caelin is patient and not optimistic.

## Independent Goal

Complete his page service. Return home for the next phase of his training. Eventually take up the diplomatic role his father holds. Eventually inherit the title and not embarrass the family in either capacity. He is sixteen; the goals are appropriate to his age.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Untested. Tomas has not been in any situation requiring it.
**Loyalty:** To his father. To Seria. To Verrin. To his friends at the page school. The order is intact and untested.
**Breaking Point:** Untested. Probably involves a direct attack on his father; unclear what his response would be.

## Daily Routine (at the Emperor's court page school)

- **Dawn:** Up. Page-school morning prayers and inspection.
- **Morning:** Classes — law, history, languages, diplomatic theory in rotation.
- **Midday:** Page-school refectory.
- **Afternoon:** Sword training, riding, occasional ceremonial duties at court.
- **Late afternoon:** Study time. Letters home.
- **Evening:** Page-school evening meal.
- **Late evening:** Reading. The journal he keeps. Sleep.
