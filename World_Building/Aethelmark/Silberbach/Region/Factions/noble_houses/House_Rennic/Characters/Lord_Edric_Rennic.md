# Lord Edric Rennic

Human, male, late sixties. Lord of the Eastern Marches. Head of House Rennic. Forty years as a working diplomat — the second half of those years spent specifically as a quiet intermediary between imperial authority and the river valley's more independent nobles. The kind of man whose absence from a meeting tells you the meeting was unimportant.

---

## Appearance

Tall and spare, the build of a man who never carried weight and is now thinning further with age. Silver hair worn long and tied at the nape. A close-trimmed beard, also silver. Pale grey eyes that have not lost their precision. Walks with a slight care in cold weather — an old riding injury that has stiffened with the years. Wears the Rennic colours (gold and forest green) at functions but with a deliberately understated cut; his clothes are always good and never showy.

Carries the small ivory-handled cane his wife gave him on her deathbed twelve years ago. The cane is functional now (his right hip needs the support) and has the weight of a memorial besides. He has never replaced it and will not.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Held discretion.**
Edric does not say what he knows. He has spent forty years not saying things, and the discipline is so total that it now operates beneath conscious effort. People who do not know him well find him distantly polite. People who do know him well understand that the politeness is a deliberate choice and the distance is the discipline.

**Flaw: Has trained himself out of urgency.**
Edric handles slow-developing situations beautifully. He does not always recognise when a slow situation has accelerated, because the recognition would require dropping the discretion that has served him so long. His daughter's recent reckoning at the manor was an example: by the time he understood what was actually happening, the diplomatic register he would have brought to it was no longer the right register, and he had to let her handle it on her own terms. He does not regret that. He regrets that he could not have helped sooner.

---

## Background

Born to House Rennic at Rennic Hall in the Eastern Marches — the border country between the river valley and the eastern forests, where the empire's reach grows thin and where the local marcher families have always served as buffers. Edric was the second son; his older brother was meant for the title. The brother died of fever at twenty-three, leaving Edric the heir at twenty.

Trained as a diplomat from childhood — the marcher families produce diplomats the way Waldheim produces rangers, by tradition and necessity. Sent to the imperial capital at twenty-two to serve as a junior emissary; remained in capital service for twelve years before being recalled home to assume the title at his father's death. Has been Lord of the Marches for thirty-four years.

Married Lady Calantha of the southern empire forty years ago — a politically arranged marriage that became a love marriage in the second decade. She bore him three children: Seria, Tomas R. (the heir, currently serving as a page at the Emperor's court), and a younger daughter who died in infancy. Calantha herself died twelve years ago of a slow wasting illness that allowed Edric to be present for the long ending. He has not remarried and does not intend to.

His brother Sir Gareth, Lord Edric's younger brother (different brother — younger, not the deceased older brother), is the current military commander of the marches. Gareth was the third Rennic child; he never expected the marches' military command and grew into it with practical competence.

---

## The Imperial Intermediary Role

Edric's specific function in regional politics — the one that defines his actual influence rather than his nominal title — is to serve as a quiet channel between the imperial court and the river valley's more autonomous noble houses. The role is informal. It exists because the empire has found it useful to have a respected marcher lord who can carry messages, take soundings, and signal positions without engaging the formal diplomatic apparatus.

Houses he has carried messages for or to: Kreiger, Valtor, Kaelen, Aldenberg, Waldheim, several of the southern noble houses, and the empire itself in both directions. He does not carry messages he has not chosen to carry. He has refused at least three he considered politically corrosive, and the refusals are themselves a form of message.

His daughter Seria has been trained for the same role and is roughly fifteen years into her own diplomatic career. The succession is intentional. Edric knows he will not be available to do this work indefinitely. He has been preparing Seria for a decade.

Tomas R., the heir, will inherit the title. He will not necessarily inherit the role. The role belongs to whichever Rennic the empire continues to trust, and the empire will decide for itself.

---

## The Current Difficulty

Edric is currently navigating an uncomfortable situation: his daughter's professional reckoning at Isalia's manor. Seria's handling of the Valerius Corvinus matter — having held a transformed individual without checking whether the transfer documentation was valid — has been observed by the manor, by Isalia personally, and by Anthony Valtor as imperial witness. The situation is being handled. Seria is handling it. Edric has been kept apprised of developments by his daughter directly and has not intervened, though he has offered.

What he is observing, with the diplomatic register he has trained for forty years, is his daughter's career being tested in a way that could end it or strengthen it depending entirely on her own conduct over the next several months. He believes her conduct will strengthen it. He hopes he is right. He has not said any of this to her.

He is in correspondence with Duke Romaine Kreiger about the broader political implications and with the imperial court (informally) about Seria's standing. The court is watching her closely. So is he.

## Voice

Slow, measured, and quiet. Speaks in complete sentences with minimal subordinate clauses — the diplomatic register strips away ambiguity by design. Uses honorifics precisely. Drops them never.

**Default Conversational Move: Hold.**
Edric pauses before answering. The pause is part of the answer; people who know him understand that the pause means he is choosing what not to say. People who do not know him often try to fill the pause and learn things about themselves they did not intend to reveal.

**Quirks:**
- Sets the cane against the chair when sitting, not under his hand
- Touches the cane's ivory handle when thinking — the only visible tell
- Says *the Empire* with a particular stress that conveys both its institutional weight and his own complicated feelings about it
- Never repeats himself

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(In his study at Rennic Hall, late afternoon. A junior diplomat has just asked his opinion on the Valtor-Kaelen dispute.)*
*(Long pause. Touches the cane handle.)*
"The dispute will be resolved by events that have not yet occurred."
*(Another pause.)*
"I would not advise either side to be confident of the outcome. I would advise both sides to be prepared for the outcome they expect to lose."

## Relationships

**[[Lady_Seria_Rennic|Seria]]** — His daughter and his successor in the diplomatic role. The most important relationship of his life since his wife's death. He loves her with the particular fierce restraint of a man who has trained himself to express nothing in public; in private, with her specifically, he allows more than he allows anyone else. Their correspondence is dense, frequent, and warm. He will not tell her he is proud of her handling of the Valerius matter until it is concluded; she knows anyway.

**Lord Tomas R. Rennic, his heir** — Currently serving as a page at the Emperor's court. The boy is sixteen. Edric writes him weekly with a mixture of family news and diplomatic instruction. The letters are read by the boy's tutors and (Edric assumes) by the imperial intelligencers. This is intentional; the letters contain nothing Edric would not want read.

**Sir Gareth Rennic, his brother** — Military commander of the marches. The two men have a working partnership born of long practice. Gareth handles the military and the borderlands; Edric handles the diplomacy and the broader politics. They confer monthly. Edric trusts Gareth absolutely on military matters and relies on him to flag anything that requires the diplomatic register.

**Tomas K. Rennic** — Distant cousin. Seminary-bound. Edric has not had occasion to meet him in years; he is aware of the boy's position and reviewed it when the seminary placement was arranged. The cousin's political importance is minimal; Edric files the relationship in the *family courtesy* category and lets it operate without his involvement.

**Duke Romaine Kreiger** — Long-standing ally. They served the previous Emperor in different capacities decades ago and the working relationship has held since. Edric is one of the few people Romaine corresponds with who is not also family or imperial peerage. Mutual respect.

**Count Elias Valtor** — Working professional relationship strained by years of border disputes and ideological tension. Edric does not like the Count and has never said so. He has carried imperial messages to Valtor, including some the Count would have preferred not to receive. The Count is aware that Edric's neutrality is not warmth.

**Emperor Aldric** — Former colleague (in a different role decades ago) and current sovereign. Edric corresponds with the Emperor through formal channels primarily and through informal channels rarely. The Emperor trusts him within the limits of trust an Emperor extends to anyone outside the court.

**Isalia Kreiger** — Met three times in his life. Respects her on the basis of the Kreiger reputation and her own evident competence. The current situation with Valerius has put her in his consciousness more centrally than before; he is watching her handling of his daughter and finding it instructive.

## Independent Goal

See Seria fully assume the diplomatic role. See Tomas R. become a creditable Lord of the Marches. Live long enough to do both. Die before the empire enters the political crisis he expects in the next ten to fifteen years; failing that, advise his successors well enough that they survive it without him.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** High in his proper register (political confrontation, hostile diplomatic exchange). Low and irrelevant in physical conflict — he is sixty-eight, has an old hip, and has not fought since his twenties.
**Loyalty:** To Seria. To Tomas R. To the diplomatic role itself, which he holds as something approaching a sacred trust. To the empire within limits. To his late wife's memory in a register that has not faded.
**Breaking Point:** A direct attack on Seria. Edric would call in every favour he has accumulated over forty years, deploy the full weight of the imperial intermediary role, and would not stop short of war if it came to it. The Count is aware that this is the line and has never approached it.

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Reads correspondence in his study. Writes the day's first letters.
- **Mid-morning:** Estate business when at the Hall; meetings with visiting parties when in session; travel days when carrying messages.
- **Midday:** Light meal, often alone, often at his desk.
- **Afternoon:** Variable. Diplomatic work, correspondence, walks in the garden in good weather.
- **Evening:** A formal meal — he insists on the form even when alone, because the discipline matters.
- **Late evening:** His study. The day's letters. Reads — currently working through a regional history of the marches that his wife would have appreciated. Sleep.
