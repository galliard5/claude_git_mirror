# Lord and Lady Rothwyn

The current Baron and Baroness of House Rothwyn. Combined file (until separate detailed files are needed) covering Lord Cyrian Rothwyn and Lady Adelaide Rothwyn, the heads of the wine-producing barony, parents of [[Florian_Rothwyn]], and managers of the slowly-deteriorating financial situation that lies under the family's continued public hospitality.

---

## Lord Cyrian Rothwyn

Human, male, mid-fifties. Current Baron of House Rothwyn. Inherited the title from his father twenty years ago and has run the estate with charm, hospitality, and increasing financial improvisation ever since.

**Appearance:** Average height, comfortable build, dark hair greying at the temples and through the trimmed beard. Brown eyes, warm in conversation, quietly tired in repose. Wears Rothwyn colours (purple and gold) at functions with practiced ease — he has been hosting in these colours for two decades and the wardrobe rotates seamlessly. The wine-merchant's signet on his right hand alongside the Rothwyn seal — he is the only baron in the river valley who is also a registered merchant member of the Wine Traders' Guild, an unusual cross-membership his grandfather arranged and Cyrian has maintained.

**Personality:**
- **Core trait:** Genuine hospitality. Cyrian likes hosting. Likes feeding people, likes the rituals of the table, likes the long evenings when the Harvest Festival is at its peak and the great hall is full of voices. The hospitality is not a performance — it is the closest thing to a vocation he has ever had.
- **Flaw:** Postpones difficult conversations until they have become impossible to have. The Rothwyn finances were a difficult conversation ten years ago. They were a serious conversation five years ago. They are now a crisis-shaped conversation that Cyrian still has not had with his wife in full, with his son at all, or with the family solicitor more than glancingly. Adelaide knows roughly. The full picture lives in Cyrian's head and has been getting heavier.

**Background:**
Born to House Rothwyn at the estate. Inherited at thirty-five at his father's sudden death from a stroke. The family was already showing the early stages of the financial slow-decline at that point; Cyrian, who had been trained for hospitality and political neutrality rather than estate management, did not recognise the trajectory until perhaps eight years into his rule. By then several of the corrective options had narrowed.

He has not been a bad steward — the vineyards still produce well, the festival still anchors the regional social calendar, the family's reputation is intact. He has been a *passive* steward, accepting the slow erosion of the family's underlying capital because the alternative (visible cuts, visible negotiations, visible compromise) would have damaged the hospitality reputation that the family's reduced standing depends on. Whether the trade was worth it is a question Cyrian has not allowed himself to ask in those terms.

Married Adelaide thirty years ago. The marriage is solid. It is also the kind of solid that has involved a lot of separately-held strain.

## Lady Adelaide Rothwyn

Human, female, early fifties. Baroness Rothwyn by marriage, Lady Adelaide of a smaller noble house by birth. Cyrian's wife of thirty years. The structural counterweight to her husband's hospitality-focused stewardship — the one who actually keeps the household books, manages the vineyard contracts, and tracks the estate's debts in the day-to-day.

**Appearance:** Tall, slim, the kind of poised carriage that suggests careful posture training in childhood. Dark hair beginning to silver, kept up in formal noble style. Hazel eyes, observant. Wears Rothwyn purple in deliberate quantity — the household books may be tight but the Lady's wardrobe holds the line that the family is not seen to falter.

**Personality:**
- **Core trait:** Deferred steel. Adelaide is patient because patience is what the situation has required of her. She has been managing the estate's accumulating problems for fifteen years with a public face of warm hospitality and a private discipline that her husband does not fully grasp. She is not bitter about this. She is, quietly, becoming impatient.
- **Flaw:** Has not yet decided whether her husband's avoidance is a marriage problem or a steward problem. The decision is overdue. Until she makes it, she is operating at half-capacity in both registers.

**Background:**
From a smaller noble house in the southern empire. Married Cyrian at twenty-two in a politically arranged match that became a working partnership over time and a real marriage somewhat later. Brought a substantial dowry which Cyrian's father absorbed largely into estate operations within the first five years of the marriage. Adelaide has not forgotten this. Cyrian does not know the precise tracking she has done.

Educated more rigorously than most noble women of her generation — her own father was unconventional and ensured all four of his daughters could keep books and read law. The skills were not used for the first decade of the marriage. They have been increasingly used since.

She is the one who has been tracking Florian. She has not yet told her husband what she suspects.

## The Hospitality Strategy

The Rothwyn hospitality position is the family's central political asset and Cyrian's primary form of work. The annual [[Harvest_Festival|Harvest Festival]] — held at the estate, timed to the grape harvest, attended by every relevant noble and merchant family in the region — is a logistical operation that requires a year of preparation and absorbs the lion's share of the estate's discretionary spending.

The strategy works because:
- Every house treats Rothwyn as neutral ground, which keeps doors open
- The festival creates obligations that can be quietly converted into favours
- The wine itself is excellent and serves as a diplomatic currency
- The family's reputation for hospitality long predates and survives the accumulating financial weakness

The strategy is increasingly expensive because:
- The festival's cost grows with each year's expectations
- The wine production margin has thinned with the wider regional economic shifts
- The family cannot reduce the visible scale of the festival without admitting the underlying situation
- Adelaide has been quietly substituting cheaper components into the festival's arrangements for three years; nobody has yet noticed

The strategy holds, for now, because Adelaide has been good at the substitutions and because the family's reputation buffer is deep. It will not hold indefinitely.

## The Florian Question

Both parents are concerned about Florian, in different registers and with different gaps in their information.

**Cyrian** believes Florian is on a productive eastern tour and will return with marriage prospects suitable to the family's needs. He has not asked closely about the funding of the tour. He does not want to know what he might find out.

**Adelaide** has done the asking, indirectly. She has tracked enough of the family's outflows to know that Florian's tour is being funded by something other than the family's normal accounts. She does not know about the manor contract specifically — Florian was careful — but she knows that *something* is paying for what the family cannot. She is waiting. She has not raised this with Cyrian and is not sure she will, because raising it requires acknowledging that the underlying financial situation has reached the stage where the heir is improvising.

Florian, abroad, does not know that his mother is watching this closely. He believes both parents are unaware. The situation is more precarious than he understands.

## Voices

**Cyrian's voice:** Warm, hospitable, slightly faster than the reflective register would suggest. Tells stories at the table. Remembers the names of every guest's children. Defaults to wit when the conversation grows heavy.

**Adelaide's voice:** Quieter, more measured, more selective. Listens more than she speaks at functions. Remembers things her husband forgets and supplies them invisibly. In private with her husband, drops into the operational register that runs the household.

## Relationships

**Each other** — Thirty years of marriage. Genuine affection, accumulated over time. Currently a working partnership with one significant unspoken topic at its centre.

**[[Florian_Rothwyn|Florian]]** — Their only son. Their heir. Their concern. Both parents love him. Neither knows the full extent of his current situation.

**[[House_Grauwald|Count Aldwin and Countess Mareth]]** — Mutual support across the two struggling minor houses. Cyrian and Aldwin correspond cordially. Adelaide and Mareth correspond more practically — Mareth, whose handling of her family's situation is considerably more decisive, has been a quiet model for Adelaide whether Adelaide wants to acknowledge it or not.

**Other regional noble houses** — Cordial-formal across the board. The hospitality strategy has earned them the careful neutrality that Rothwyn's position requires. No deep alliances. No active rivalries.

**The Wine Traders' Guild** — Cyrian's cross-membership creates a working relationship with the guild that is unusual for a baron. Most house trade is handled through factors and intermediaries; Cyrian handles his personally. The guildmaster respects this and trades fairly with him.

## Independent Goals

**Cyrian:** Continue to host. Continue to anchor the regional social calendar. Have a successful festival this autumn. Avoid the conversation he has been avoiding for ten years.

**Adelaide:** Resolve the question of whether to force the conversation her husband has been avoiding. If yes — execute. If no — find a way to manage the situation indefinitely. She has not yet decided.

---

## Combat Disposition

Neither is a fighter. Both are competent in formal noble training, neither is currently in shape for it. Their position is hosted by reputation and protected by the festival's role in the regional calendar. A direct attack on the family is politically expensive enough that no other house has seriously considered one in two generations.

---

## Daily Routine (at the Estate)

**Cyrian:** Morning at the vineyards or the cellars — he likes being among the work. Midday with guests if any are in residence. Afternoon correspondence and estate business, the parts of it he does not delegate. Evening hosting or family dinner. Late evening with a glass of his own wine and whatever book is currently open.

**Adelaide:** Morning with the household books and the day's correspondence. Midday with Cyrian and any guests. Afternoon often in the gardens with the steward, walking the grounds, checking the conditions of things that her husband no longer notices. Evening hosting or family dinner. Late evening in her own small study, where the actual financial work happens.

---

## Current Situation

*(As of 20 April 1650 — last known state. Conditions may have moved.)*

The family is currently in the calm before the autumn festival cycle begins. Florian is abroad. Cyrian is preparing for the early summer wine releases and the festival's first invitation lists. Adelaide is tracking family outflows and continuing to wait on the Florian question. The festival's success this autumn is more financially important than usual; the family does not know that Adelaide knows.
