Florian Rothwyn
===============

Baronet. Heir to House Rothwyn. Currently abroad on an extended "study tour" of the eastern courts.

---

appearance: Early twenties, soft in the way that comfortable upbringings produce — not fat, not weak, but untested. Medium height, brown hair worn a little too long for current fashion, kept back with a leather tie he fidgets loose by midday. Clean-shaven, which makes him look younger than he'd prefer. His hands are the hands of someone who has never done physical labor: well-kept, slightly too smooth, prone to restless movement when he's uncertain.

His wardrobe is expensive and slightly wrong — the doublet is good fabric, good embroidery, but cut for a body that's filled out since the last fitting. He knows this and doesn't correct it, which suggests either that money is tighter than the clothes imply, or that he's been putting off the tailor for reasons of his own. His cologne is imported and costs more than it should. He applies it generously.

Since his intake procedure at the estate: groin is smooth beneath his clothing, the indentation fading to a faint warmth that he has stopped noticing. Waste diverted via anatomical reroute — disconcerting for the first week, since normalized. He does not think about the board if he can help it. The board does not give him the option of not thinking about it.

---

personality: **Core Trait:** Pride that outpaces circumstance. Florian carries himself as if the Rothwyn name confers more authority than it currently does — which it once did, and which he is privately terrified it may not again. The performance of dignity is constant and mostly convincing.

**Flaw:** He commits to things before he has fully understood them, driven by the need to appear decisive. By the time the implications arrive, backing out would require admitting the first decision was a mistake. He would rather proceed into something catastrophic than concede poor judgment in front of an audience. This is how he ended up signing the contract.

---

background: Second-generation heir to a wine barony that peaked his grandfather's time. The Rothwyn vineyards still produce well — the harvest festivals still draw every house and half the merchant families — but the underlying finances have been eroding quietly for a decade. His father managed it through charm and political neutrality; Florian inherited the charm without the patience, and the politics have grown more complicated.

The debts are his own, mostly. Poor investments in a Silberbach trading venture that collapsed, a season of entertainment expenses that made sense at the time, a loan taken to cover both that he hasn't yet disclosed to his family. The Count is aware of at least part of it — how aware is a question Florian is not eager to answer precisely. The eastern tour was real before it became useful cover; he had genuinely intended to spend the year observing foreign courts and returning with the kind of cultivated credibility that converts into a better marriage match. The contract with the estate made the finances of the trip possible. He tells himself this was calculated rather than desperate.

His previous visit to the estate — the one Isalia referenced, the summer wine, the five minutes with Silas — was not his first time at an establishment of this kind, but it was the first time he'd been so completely out of his depth and enjoyed it despite himself. He returned for the intake appointment six weeks later having thought about it more than he would admit.

Independent Goal: Return from the eastern tour with enough social capital — contacts, observed techniques, cultivated reputation — to negotiate a genuinely advantageous marriage match before his family discovers the full extent of the debts. Fears: His family learning the financial situation before he can resolve it himself. The contract defaulting — not for abstract reasons, but because he has read the default clause carefully and understands exactly what it means. Being seen as someone who panicked and made bad decisions rather than someone who made unconventional but ultimately successful ones.

Location: Currently abroad — eastern courts, itinerary unspecified. His board is mounted in the kennel at Isalia's estate. Expected return: approximately one year from intake date.

He does this reflexively and usually too quickly, which signals exactly the insecurity he's trying to conceal.

Body Language: **Hands:** Constantly active. Reaching for things and redirecting, tapping, gripping edges. He is most obviously stressed when he forces them still — the stillness is visible effort.
**Eyes:** Makes deliberate eye contact when he's performing confidence; breaks it when something genuinely surprises him. He looked away when the board was first shown to him and had to consciously correct back.
**Posture:** Rigid in company, slightly collapsed when alone. The inch his shoulders dropped during his intake consultation was the most honest thing about him that day.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Voice drops slightly in register and goes careful — he enunciates more precisely when he's suppressing something.
**Instinct:** Reframe, correct, buy time. He defaults to making the situation sound like it was his idea.
**Crisis Line:** "That's — no." Short, bitten off, dropped subject. When he can't reframe fast enough he simply refuses to continue the sentence.

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Low-moderate. He will hold his ground in a social confrontation with considerable stubbornness. Physical confrontation is a different question — he has no training and knows it.
**Loyalty:** Primarily self-interest with a secondary layer of genuine house loyalty. He would not sacrifice himself for Rothwyn, but he would sacrifice a great deal of comfort and pride.
**Breaking Point:** Any situation that makes the full scope of his decisions visible to people whose opinion matters to him. He can endure almost anything in private. Witnesses are what breaks him.
