House Rothwyn
=============

Barony. Wealthy agricultural estate known for vineyards and wine production. Hosts the annual Harvest Festival.

## Standing & Influence

The Rothwyn name carries genuine regional weight, though more through hospitality and tradition than political muscle. The family has cultivated neutrality as a deliberate strategy — by hosting the annual Harvest Festival, they position themselves as a meeting ground rather than a faction, which keeps doors open across houses that might otherwise exclude them.

Their wines are prized throughout the region and serve as both income and diplomatic currency. A case of Rothwyn vintage is a known gesture of goodwill among the nobility. The label still commands respect even as the underlying finances have quietly eroded over the past decade — the surface is maintained, the reputation intact, but the margin is thinner than outsiders know.

---

## The Harvest Festival

Held annually at the estate, timed to the grape harvest. The region's premier social event — nobles, merchant families, and guild representatives all attend, often conducting business they couldn't politely arrange elsewhere. The Rothwyns provide the venue, the wine, and the fiction of neutrality. In exchange they receive access: to information, to relationships, to the kind of goodwill that can be quietly converted to favors.

The festival is the family's most important political asset and the reason they are tolerated by larger houses who would otherwise overlook them.
