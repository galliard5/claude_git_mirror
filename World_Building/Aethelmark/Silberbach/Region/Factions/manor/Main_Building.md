Main Building
=============

The public face of the manor — reception, store, and customer intake.

Description: **Ground Floor:**

*Reception Hall:*
The public face of the manor — clean stone floors, warm wood paneling, comfortable seating for waiting clients. A large desk dominates the entrance where visitors are greeted and directed. Tasteful decorations hint at the manor's purposes without being crude.

*The Store (The Apothecary):*
The manor's retail and consultation space. See [[The_Apothecary]] for full detail — layout, inventory, staff, and operational notes.

*Customer Intake:*
Three private consultation rooms where clients discuss their needs with staff. Contracts are signed here, medical histories taken, and transformation requests reviewed. Soundproofed for discretion.

Isalia's private consultation office adjoins this area. The desk is heavy oak scarred from decades of claws and quills. An ever-warm teapot sits on the desk — a minor enchantment, always ready, jasmine tea the default. Apple brandy at the sideboard for clients who need something stronger. The framed imperial charter hangs above the desk, Duke Romaine's wax seal still vibrant crimson against yellowed parchment.

*Guest Dining Room:*
A small but elegant dining area for visiting clients and dignitaries. Seats up to eight at a polished oak table. Meals are prepared in the main kitchen and served by staff. Used for business dinners, private negotiations, or simply hosting travelers who need a place to stay.

*Administrative Offices:*
- **Mira's Office** — Ledgers, account books, and a locked strongbox
- **Elias's Office** — Scheduling boards, staff rosters, supply orders
- **Kira's Desk** — Message logs, courier schedules, correspondence

**Second Floor:**

*Isalia's Quarters:*
Private chambers accessible via two routes — the main stairs from reception (for formal occasions) and the back stairs from the staff wing (for daily use). Includes a sitting room, private study, and sleeping chamber. A balcony overlooks the pastures.

The office used for private client consultations is warded — a claw drawn along the doorframe activates threads of blue light through the woodgrain, sealing the room against eavesdropping. The wards are old and well-set; they engage without ceremony and without announcement to the client.

*Guest Rooms:*
Two modest but comfortable chambers for clients in transit or those recovering from procedures. Each contains a bed, washbasin, small desk, and wardrobe. Not luxurious, but clean and private. Staff check in regularly on guests' needs.
