Lira, the Sable Doe
===================

Elf → Fallow Deer Breeder, Female. Sacred sensuality. Her offspring shows signs of latent magic.

appearance: A graceful fallow doe with a coat the color of autumn leaves — dappled tan and white, soft and sleek. Her eyes are large and dark, holding a gentleness that seems almost sacred. Her movements are liquid and deliberate, each step placed with the care of a dancer. A speech-collar rests against her throat, its metal warmed by her fur. There's something ethereal about her, a beauty that transcends her animal form.

personality: **Core Trait: Sacred sensuality.**
Lira views pleasure and connection as divine expression.

**Flaw: Lingering spiritual uncertainty.**
The loss of her temple left her questioning her faith's place in the world. Her devotion is now centered on her child and the peace she has found at the manor.

background: Once Lirael Moonshadow, a priestess of a fertility goddess in a distant elven kingdom. Her order taught that sensuality was sacred — a gift to be shared, a communion with the divine. She served for centuries, bringing comfort and joy to those who sought her temple's blessings.

Then came the reforms. A new high priest decided that such "earthly" practices were beneath their goddess. The temple's sacred workers were dismissed, their service declared heretical. Lirael found herself cast out after three hundred years of devotion, her purpose stripped away.

She wandered for decades before finding Isalia's manor. The doe form appealed to her — gentle, graceful, associated with the hunt and the forest. She chose it to continue her sacred work in a new form, serving as a breeder for those who sought the manor's services.

Her offspring, now two years old, has begun to show signs of latent magic — a blessing from her goddess, perhaps, or something new entirely. She watches the child with quiet wonder and no small amount of concern.
