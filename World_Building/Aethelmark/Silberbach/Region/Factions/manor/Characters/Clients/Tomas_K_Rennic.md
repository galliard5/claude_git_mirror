Tomas K. Rennic
===============

Human (partially transformed — partial rabbit), Male. A young man who arrived at the manor as a walk-in client seeking permanent alteration before beginning his studies at Saint Verena's Seminary. Minor cousin of House Rennic — not the heir.

appearance: Pre-transformation — young, slightly built, with scuffed boots, flushed easily, and a habit of hunching his shoulders inward. Fresh scratches on his knuckles at time of arrival. Carried a travel pack and the crumpled acceptance letter from Saint Verena's Seminary in his pocket.

Post-transformation — his body remains roughly human in shape and proportion, bipedal and capable of speech. White fur with brown streaks covers him entirely. His jaw has realigned around elongated incisors; whiskers extend from his muzzle. His ears are long, upright, and swivel independently toward sound before he can stop them. His rear feet have become digitigrade paws with delicate dark claws — he can no longer wear boots. His tailbone has lengthened into a small puff of white fur at his lower back.

His clothing was altered by Lyra before discharge: seams let out around the thighs, sleeves tapered for narrower wrists. He carries a smooth river stone in his pocket — given to him by Lyra during recovery. The seminary letter remains with him, its wax seal broken.

personality: **Core Trait: Shame-driven self-denial.**
Tomas believes that if he removes desire from his body, he will be free of sin. He conflates the physical with the spiritual — treating the vessel as the problem rather than the meaning he has assigned to it.

**Flaw: Displacement.**
He cannot name what he is actually running from. When pressed, he retreats into religious language — purity, sin, cleanness — using doctrine as a barrier against self-examination. Alric named it directly: *what you're paying for isn't purity. It's distance. From your own skin.*

background: Tomas spent four years in rigorous preparation for seminary — dawn prayers, late-night recitations, the full devotional discipline of Saint Verena's tradition. His father wept when the acceptance letter arrived.

He stole a pamphlet about transformation services from his uncle's study. The decision to visit the manor was not impulsive — it was the result of a long private calculation that he kept entirely to himself.

The precipitating event is not stated directly but is implied: the rector of his home parish made unwanted physical contact during scripture lessons, lingering on Tomas's wrist in a way that he responded to with shame rather than anger. His solution was not to name the rector's behavior but to attempt to scour the response from himself. *If I don't, I'll never stop wanting. And wanting is sin.*

He arrived at the manor a few weeks before his seminary enrollment date. Saint Verena's is three weeks' ride northeast.

Independent Goal: Reach Saint Verena's and take his vows. He believes the procedures will make that life possible — that he has solved the problem his body posed.

Fears: That wanting will return anyway. That his body will continue to tell truths his theology cannot contain. The ending of his story suggests this is already happening.

Location: Last seen traveling the road northeast toward Saint Verena's Seminary. Three weeks' ride from the manor.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move: Stall.**
Tomas does not speak fluently about himself. He blurts things out in clumsy rushes, then winces at his own voice. He gestures at his body when words fail him. He uses vague approximations — *a rabbit, on two legs, walking, talking like a man, but... rabbit* — rather than direct statements.

**Quirks:**

- Reaches for absent objects — his missing pack, the letter in his pocket — when anxious.
- Post-transformation: his ears swivel toward sounds before he can stop them; he freezes when he realizes he's done it.
- Caught himself gnawing absently at the infirmary sheet during recovery. Recoiled with shame before realizing no one had seen.

Body Language: **Hands:** Clumsy and restless. Fumbles with straps, presses fingers against his thighs, digs claws into surfaces when overwhelmed. Post-transformation: claws click against hard surfaces involuntarily.
**Eyes:** Avoids direct contact when speaking about himself. Fixes on objects — the workbench, the vial, the ceiling beams — rather than people.
**Posture:** Hunched shoulders, slightly inward. Does not take up space easily.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Throat goes dry; he swallows visibly and repeatedly.
**Instinct:** Retreat into religious framing. Names the feeling as sin rather than examining what it is.
**Crisis Line:** *"I know."* — said quietly, when someone describes consequences he has already accepted.

**Breaking Point:** Any direct physical threat. He has no frame for it.

**Lyra Vex:** She gave him a river stone and said the one thing that landed: *"They teach you guilt before they teach you grace."* He has not yet understood what she meant.

## Notes

The story ends before Tomas reaches the seminary. His final moment — mirroring a wild rabbit on the road, then watching it flee — is deliberately unresolved. His body is already responding to instincts his theology has no category for.

Whether he arrives at Saint Verena's, what happens when he does, and whether the procedures gave him what he sought are all open. He is not at peace. He may not know that yet.
