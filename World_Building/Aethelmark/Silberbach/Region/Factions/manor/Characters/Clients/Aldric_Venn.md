# Aldric Venn

Large tortoise, Male. Former textile factor, Silberbach Merchants Guild. Transformed eleven weeks ago in Lenne by practitioner Edvard Solt — represented as temporary (six weeks), documentation indicates permanent. Solt's workshop now closed, whereabouts unknown. Stayed at Gerthe's rooming house, Merchants Close, Silberbach for six weeks before contacting Isalia's Manor by letter. Brought in by cart 21 April 1650.

Goes by Aldric Venn. This is his real name — he used it in the letter because he intended to stand behind it.

---

## Appearance

Very large tortoise. Shell is dark brown with the geometric patterning that takes decades to develop — not his decades, but the form's. Heavy, domed, the span of a writing desk. Eyes are dark and very steady — the eyes of a man who reads situations carefully and has had considerable time to practice. Moves with deliberate unhurried economy: has spent eleven weeks learning exactly how this body moves and what each movement costs.

As of 21 April 1650: client stall three, south window, adjustable writing surface fitted by Haldrek 22 April. Sand floor, lowered water dish and fittings.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Measured precision.**
Thirty-one years as a factor built a habit of reading situations accurately and acting on that reading without fuss. He says what he means, means exactly what he says, and has no patience for provisional statements — including his own. He corrects himself mid-sentence rather than let imprecision stand.

**Flaw: Acted on convenience over diligence.**
He didn't read the paperwork closely because it was convenient not to. He was frightened and they were efficient and he signed. He knows this and holds it accurately, without excessive self-recrimination and without excusing it.

---

## Background

Textile factor, Silberbach. Own firm, modest but solvent, good reputation in the Merchants Guild in Silberbach and three other cities. Northern wool, southern dye, the margin between them. Thirty-one years.

Eleven weeks ago he was in Lenne on business. A colleague recommended a practitioner — Edvard Solt — offering transformation as a novelty experience. Solt asked him what he found impressive. He was tired, mid-difficult negotiation, and said something about longevity and patience and outlasting the other party. Solt seemed pleased. Took notes.

He did not know what form he was being given until he was already in it. The receipt has a blank licensing column. He noticed the absence of certificates on Solt's workshop wall and did not weight it correctly at the time.

Within days Solt's workshop was closed. He has been unable to locate Solt through legal channels. He stayed at Gerthe's rooming house in Silberbach's Merchants Close for six weeks, managing correspondence with a modified pen, before contacting Isalia's estate. He paid Gerthe three months upfront because he didn't know how long things would take and he doesn't like owing people.

---

## Why a Tortoise

He didn't choose it. He told Solt he valued *longevity, patience, outlasting.* Solt built literally from that. He has had eleven weeks to consider whether there is something instructive in this. He has not reached a conclusion. He thinks about it less than he did in week two.

**Elias's assessment (in progress):** Documents reviewed; written assessment due Donnerstag 24 April. If Solt unlicensed, grounds for county court action.

## What He Wants

Reversion first, if possible. A suitable new form if not. He has come to this with a list of requirements rather than a vision: functional mobility, correspondence capability, presence in a room at something approaching eye level, the ability to manage a business. Horse is the leading candidate pending proper consultation.

He wants to know the shape of his options before he decides anything. He is not a man who decides on insufficient information.

## Voice

**Default Conversational Move: Measure.**
Aldric takes the weight of a thing before delivering it. Short declarative sentences. Finishes his thinking before speaking. Will correct himself mid-sentence for imprecision. Will not produce provisional statements if he can avoid them.

**Quirks:**
- Near-humor that surfaces dry and brief — particularly when something is bleak enough that the only honest response is irony. *I've had eleven weeks. I can manage three days.*
- The near-humor lives close to something else. It's earned, not performed.
- Corrects inaccuracies in his own previous statements without embarrassment.

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(On reversion and the blank licensing column)*
"I noticed the absence of certificates on his wall. I did not weight it correctly at the time."
*(A beat.)*
"I was a factor for thirty-one years. I read contracts."
*(Another beat.)*
"I did not read that one."

## Body Language

**Eyes:** Dark, very steady. Move methodically across a situation before settling. When he has decided something, the eyes stop moving.
**Head:** Tracks conversation with the full attention economy of a wide skull and eyes positioned for range — different geometry than a human face, complete in its own way.
**Weight:** Shifts deliberately when repositioning. Has learned to signal intent before moving. Economy in everything.
**Shell:** Angles toward warmth instinctively — sun, fire, any heat source. He notices this and finds it interesting rather than alarming.

---

## Stress Responses

**Stress Tell:** Goes completely still. Not composure — suspension. The body waiting for the mind to produce something actionable.
**Instinct:** Assess accurately before acting. Has had eleven weeks of practice at this.
**Crisis Line:** A long pause. Then whatever is precisely true.

## Independent Goal

Get the business back in his own hands. Not the form specifically — the function. The factor's work. The letters, the margins, the running of a thing he built.

After that: find out whether the form has anything to teach him that he hasn't yet let himself consider.

## Fears

Making another decision on insufficient information. He has had one very expensive lesson in this and does not intend a second.
