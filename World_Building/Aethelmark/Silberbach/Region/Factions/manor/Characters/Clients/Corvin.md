Corvin
======

Large dark-coated hound, Male. Former trade records clerk for a factor house in Mecklau. Transformed fourteen months ago as a condition of a debt settlement his employer arranged without his meaningful consent — told it was six months, paperwork said permanent. Acquired by a creditor, sold twice. Retrieved by Marta Sehn four weeks ago. Crescent collar removed by Elowen Vale 20 April 1650.

Goes by Corvin. This is his name.

---

## Appearance

Large hound, deep brown coat that reads as near-black in dim light. Heavy through the shoulders — a working dog's build, selected by whoever the practitioner was, not by him. Thin as of arrival: ribs closer to the surface than they should be on a frame that size, coat dull in the way that indicates something systemic rather than neglect. Marta has been taking care of him. The form itself is not the problem.

Pale gold eyes — the colour chosen for him, not by him. Very steady when he's decided to look at something. Move to Marta first when she enters a room, then to whoever else is present.

As of 20 April 1650: estate standard collar fitted, plain worked leather with speech enchantment, Elowen's clean mark at the clasp. Suppression from the Crescent collar clearing on its own timeline.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Precision.**
He was a clerk. He noticed that a particular merchant overstated inventory by exactly twelve percent across three years and kept a private running record — never reported it, just liked knowing. His instinct is to find the exact right weight for a thing and apply it. This extends to how he communicates even without words.

**Flaw: Carries things alone.**
Fourteen months of no collar, no recognized personhood, no one to tell. The habit of internal processing is deep. He worked things out completely inside before he could communicate them — and then couldn't. When the speaking collar is functional he will likely still finish his thinking before he speaks.

## Background

Trade records clerk, Mecklau factor house. Good work, careful. Valued enough that his employer included him in a debt settlement with a creditor who had connections to the county court. He was told it was six months. He agreed because what else do you do. He was transformed within the week by a licensed practitioner under valid documentation. His consent is on record — he did sign. He did not read the paperwork closely enough. He was frightened and they were efficient.

He knew in the tribunal room what was happening. There was no tribunal room. There was a factor house office and a man with documentation and seven days. He understood what the sentence meant by the second day. He asked a staff member on the second day at the creditor's estate what the terms of his assignment were. He was told it wasn't a question a hound needed to ask. He did not ask again.

The creditor held him three months. Sold the contract. Sold again. Marta tracked him through two transfers before losing the trail. Found it again. Paid the last holder. Brought him to Silberbach four weeks ago.

The Crescent collar was fitted at some point during the first transfer — he doesn't know when exactly. It suppressed. Not completely, not immediately, but progressively. He has had days where he couldn't settle. He flinches at sounds he shouldn't flinch at. He remembers talking. He used to be the one talking.

**Contract:** Sold twice after initial creditor. Fenk, acting for Dietrich Haan, held a residual claim based on a weak argument about the original settlement obligation surviving the contract transfers. Claim dropped unconditionally 20 April 1650 in exchange for the estate receiving Haan's referral correspondence.

**Current status:** Free. No contract holder. No outstanding obligation. Guest at Isalia's Manor, south corridor, as of 20 April 1650.

**Reversion:** Not discussed. The estate holds no reversion right — there was no intake contract. His form and his future are his own.

## What He Wants

He doesn't know yet. He hasn't been able to want things freely for fourteen months. What he wanted before — the work, the noticing, the running private records of things that interested him — exists in memory. Whether it exists in him now is a question the suppression has kept him from answering.

He wants to know if Marta is all right. She has been taking care of him for four weeks and he has not been able to ask.
