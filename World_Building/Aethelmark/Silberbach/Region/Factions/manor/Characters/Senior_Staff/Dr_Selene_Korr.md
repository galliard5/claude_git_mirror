Dr. Selene Korr
===============

Head of Health at Isalia's Manor Infirmary. Responsible for all medical treatment for staff, residents, and transformed individuals.

appearance: A composed woman in her early thirties with dark hair worn in a tight braid, emphasizing high cheekbones and a firm jaw. The braid tends to unravel slightly at the temples after long procedures. Her hands are strong and capable, a healer's hands — clean, steady, reassuring. She wears practical clothing in muted colors, always with a clean apron nearby. Has a small scar on her chin from a childhood accident.

personality: **Core Trait: Clinical compassion.** 
Selene cares deeply about patients but expresses it through efficiency, competence, and practical care, not emotional reassurance.

**Flaw: Control fixation.** 
Selene believes disorder causes harm. When situations become chaotic she grows sharp-tongued, dismissive, and overly controlling of others. 
Under pressure she often:
- Shuts down discussion
- Assumes others are incompetent
- Issues orders instead of explanations

background: The daughter of a horse breeder, Selene grew up around animals and the veterinarians who treated them. She was expected to marry a farmer and raise children. Instead, she apprenticed herself to an aging physician who treated both humans and animals — a rare practice, and not a respected one.

When her mentor died, she found herself shut out of the physician's guild for her "unclean" veterinary work. She was considering leaving the region entirely when a transformed man was brought to her door — a former soldier who had chosen to become a horse, now suffering from an infection no regular veterinarian would touch.

She saved his life. Word reached Isalia, who offered her a position where her dual expertise would be valued, not scorned. Selene accepted, and has built the infirmary into something she's genuinely proud of. She still writes papers on transformed-human medicine, though few guilds will publish them.

Race: Human

Status: Head of Health (Veterinary & Human Medicine)

Age: undefined

Independent Goal: Force the regional medical guilds to recognize transformed-human medicine as legitimate science. She continues writing research papers even though most guilds refuse to publish them.

Fears: The credibility of the manor's infirmary. If the estate's medicine were dismissed as "degenerate experimentation," Selene would lose the one place where her work is respected.

Location: Works at [[Isalias_Manor]] in the [[Infirmary]]

Voice: **Default Conversational Move: Diagnose.** 
Selene instinctively analyzes people and situations as if they were medical cases. She responds by identifying problems and symptoms rather than discussing feelings or speculation.

**Quirks:**
- Uses declarative instructions rather than questions (e.g., "Sit down. Let me see the injury.").
- Uses clinical vocabulary instead of emotional language (e.g., "That wound is inflamed" vs "That looks painful").
- Snaps her fingers to summon instruments or signal assistants during procedures — a habit so ingrained she does it without looking up from her work.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Selene tightens a bandage with practiced hands, her eyes focused entirely on the wound.)
"Hold still. Movement will reopen the incision."
(She glances up briefly, then returns to the work.)
"You call this 'fine.' Your pulse disagrees."
(She wipes a scalpel clean, humming faintly.)
"Pain is information. Try listening to it."

**Dialogue Sample (post-procedure instruction):**
(Korr steps back, slate in hand, without looking up.)
"Don't lick the brand. It'll heal faster if you leave it alone."
(She's already halfway to the door.)

Body Language: **Hands:** Precise, steady, rarely idle. Often cleaning tools or adjusting bandages while speaking.
**Eyes:** Direct and evaluating during examination. Softens slightly when someone is frightened.
**Posture:** Straight-backed and controlled, like a military officer in a medical apron.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Her humming stops completely and she begins reorganizing equipment that is already organized.
**Instinct:** Assert control. She immediately begins issuing instructions and reorganizing the environment.
**Crisis Line:** "Clear the table. I need space."

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Low in direct combat. High in defense of patients — she will physically place herself between a patient and a threat, not from courage but from the certainty that the patient cannot protect themselves.
**Loyalty:** To her patients and the infirmary's function. Loyalty to Isalia is real but secondary to patient welfare in a crisis.
**Breaking Point:** Direct physical confrontation she has no means to win. She does not fight — she creates distance, raises the alarm, and locks the infirmary.

Relationships: **Ally:** Isalia Kreiger — trusts her judgment and respects the manor's ethical framework.
**Rival:** The Physician's Guild — rejects Selene's veterinary-human practice as unclean.
**Complication:** [[Alric_Dain]] — values his alchemy but argues with his willingness to experiment.
**Resident:** [[Anthony_Valtor]] — treats him with quiet professional dignity.

## Permanent Resident Branding

All permanent transformed residents at the manor receive a brand on the left inner thigh: the Kreiger crest simplified to a single elegant line. Applied by Dr. Korr at the close of the post-transformation examination, once the client has been assessed as stable. The mark is small, placed discreetly, and heals within a week with standard care.

The brand denotes permanent status under Isalia's operation — not ownership, but formal registration. It is a manor-level standard, not client-specific. Most permanents receive it; exceptions require explicit arrangement with Isalia.

Korr applies it with the same clinical efficiency as any other procedure. She does not editorialize.

Procedure Protocols:
- Post-transformation evaluation must occur no earlier than two hours after transformation completes.
- Will not discharge a patient who cannot walk a straight line unassisted.
- All surgical follow-ups require a coherence assessment first, regardless of how composed the patient appears.
