Alric Dain
==========

Lead Alchemist of Isalia's Manor Kennels & Stables. Alric oversees the brewing of transformation potions, reversion elixirs, and medical compounds used by the manor.

appearance: A lean human in his late forties, with calloused hands stained permanently by reagents — fingers indigo at the tips from years of handling dreamroot, blue-black stains he rubs at absently when thinking. His hair is going gray at the temples, and he has a small scar across his left eyebrow from an early alchemical accident. He smells faintly of herbs and something metallic, with a trace of rosemary that seems to follow him personally rather than coming from the lab. Wears practical leather aprons over simple clothing.

The lab itself smells of crushed valerian and cold iron — distinct from Alric's personal scent, and noticeably different during and after active transformation procedures.

personality: **Core Trait: Grave responsibility.**
Alric treats every transformation as a life-altering decision and approaches his work with quiet seriousness.

**Flaw: Burdened conscience.**
His past as a battlefield surgeon makes him overly cautious and sometimes hesitant when a procedure carries risk. He may delay decisions longer than others think necessary.

background: Once a battlefield surgeon in a distant war, Alric watched too many young men die from wounds that should have been survivable. When the war ended, he turned to alchemy, seeking ways to heal that steel and thread couldn't provide.

His research into transformation began as an attempt to give crippled soldiers new forms — new bodies that could work despite their injuries. The military had no interest in "cripples in horse bodies," but Isalia heard of his work through Duke Romaine's network.

She offered him something the army never had: the chance to transform people who *wanted* it, who chose their new forms freely. He's been the manor's lead alchemist for fifteen years now, and he still approaches every transformation with the gravity it deserves. He keeps a small garden of medicinal herbs behind the alchemy lab — his quiet refuge.

Independent Goal: Advance transformation alchemy so that injured or disabled people can gain new functional bodies without stigma.

Fears: The ethical integrity of his work. If transformation became careless or coercive, the work he devoted his life to would become something monstrous.

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Low. Alric is not a fighter and does not pretend to be. In direct physical danger he freezes, then retreats — his instinct is to protect the work, not engage the threat.
**Loyalty:** To his ethical principles and the integrity of his research. Will not act against consent or be complicit in harm to patients.
**Breaking Point:** Any immediate physical threat he cannot control. He removes himself from combat situations rather than engaging. If the laboratory or a patient is directly threatened, he raises the alarm and gets out of the way.

## The Memory Mechanic

Alric engineered the transformation potion to surface the client's memories and associations most relevant to the animal form they're becoming. This is deliberate — not a side effect. The surfacing is strongest for experiences the client has actually lived: time spent with that kind of animal, work done alongside them, moments of genuine connection.

Strong positive associations help the most. A client who has real, felt history with their chosen form moves through the change more cleanly than one who chose abstractly. During transformation, a skilled practitioner can steer attention toward those positive memories, which steadies the process when panic or pain would otherwise destabilize it.

The mechanic also surfaces difficult memories — wounds, fear, old scarring. This is not a flaw. Those memories are real and the transformation is permanent. Alric considers it better that the full picture surfaces during the procedure, while he and Isalia are present, than after.

Clients who have no genuine connection to their chosen form are harder transformations. Alric notes this during intake without always stating it plainly.
