Valerius Corvinus
=================

Charcoal hound, Male. Former Third Attaché to the Imperial delegation (Venetian territories). Convicted by Imperial circuit tribunal of seditious inquiry and unauthorized disclosure. Sentence: permanent transformation. Carried out lawfully. Acquired by Lady Seria Rennic under incomplete transfer documentation — two years of undocumented custody. Now a free resident of Isalia's Manor.

Goes by Valerius. The name Vesper was given by Seria's household. He does not use it.

---

## Appearance

Large hound, deep charcoal coat, pale gold eyes. The particular stillness of someone who tracks faces rather than movement — the quality that Isalia noticed within moments of examination and that has nothing to do with animal behavior. Old enough in the form now (two years) that the body moves naturally. Still learning what he can do with it, as opposed to what he couldn't do without a collar.

Wears a speaking collar — plain worked leather, Elowen's enchantment, Seria's purchase. Fitted 17 April 1650. First words in two years: *Thank you.* Said to Isalia.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Precision.**
Valerius says what he means and means exactly what he says. Eleven years in diplomatic work built a habit of language that is difficult to break and that he has no interest in breaking. Words are instruments. Imprecision wastes them.

**Flaw: Carries things alone.**
Two years of silence built a habit of internal processing that the collar hasn't fully dismantled. He thinks things through completely before saying them, which reads as composure and sometimes is — but sometimes it means he's been carrying something for a while before anyone knows he has it.

---

## Background

Third Attaché to the Imperial delegation, Venetian territories posting. Eleven years of diplomatic work. Convicted for asking the wrong questions about a trade route through three ducal territories and putting the answers in writing. The correspondence was found during a routine audit. The circuit magistrate determined the act of *asking* was the offense, independent of intent. He had representation. It was not useful. He knew what was happening in that tribunal room and understood exactly what the sentence meant.

Within a week of sentencing he was transported to Seria Rennic's estate. He did not know whose estate until he arrived. He asked a staff member on the second day what the terms of his assignment were. He was told it wasn't a question a hound needed to ask. He did not ask again. He did not have a collar. Whatever communication he attempted before 17 April was behavioral — positioning near papers, returning to them, persistent attention that a person would read as inquiry and a household chose to treat as animal behavior.

Two years. Seria acquired him and assumed the paperwork would sort itself out. She did not look. He knows this and has reached *accurate* rather than accusation about it.

## Legal Status

**Conviction:** Valid. Imperial circuit tribunal, seditious inquiry, unauthorized disclosure. Not disputed. He would ask the questions again.

**Transformation:** Lawfully carried out by licensed practitioner under valid judicial order.

**Custody:** Undocumented. Transfer documentation initiated — Seria's signature present — circuit magistrate's counter-signature absent. Transfer was never formally closed. Seria held no reversion right. Valerius has been in undocumented custody for two years.

**Petition:** Filed 19 April 1650 by Elias Varn on behalf of Isalia Kreiger. Three reliefs sought:
1. Custody transfer declared invalid (incomplete documentation)
2. Reversion right transferred to Isalia's Manor, held in trust under a contract Valerius has reviewed and consented to — form determination deferred indefinitely at petitioner's election
3. Legal personhood formally acknowledged on record

**Hearing:** Before Deputy Justice Arvid Mohl, est. ~2 May 1650. Mohl was present at the original tribunal as a junior clerk. He knows what he knows. He will want a clean path.

**Reversion:** Practically impossible. The conviction specified permanent transformation. The circuit magistrate departed the district twelve days after sentencing. Essence almost certainly not preserved in retrievable form. Valerius knows this and has set it down accordingly — the right matters more than the outcome it enables.

**Pups:** Valerius holds ownership on birth. Final say on placement. Wants to know where they go. Wants to be present while Merra raises them.

---

## What He Wants

Two things, stated directly in Session 03:

**Practical:** The reversion right established in a contract he has read, held on this estate. Not necessarily to use — to know where it sits. To not have the answer made for him again.

**Personal:** The tribunal record to show that Valerius Corvinus existed and acted as a person throughout the sentence, and that the estate recognized it — regardless of what the law decides about formal standing. He wants it written down.

The form he would choose if reversion were possible: he doesn't know yet. What he was is not something he can go back to. *Reversion to what, exactly* is a question he hasn't finished answering. He wants time to work it out.

## Voice

**Default Conversational Move: Precision.**
Valerius names things exactly. He will stop and restart a sentence to get it right. He distinguishes between what he wants and what he's asking for; between what's true and what's forgivable; between what is accurate and what is resolved.

**Quirks:**
- Delivers real things to the middle distance — that spot above the desk's left corner, or the far end of the pasture — rather than directly at the person. When he looks at someone directly it costs something and it means something.
- Two years of disuse left him precise rather than rusty. He rehearsed the weight of words before he could use them again.

**Dialogue Samples:**

*(Being asked about the conviction)*
"I asked the wrong questions about a trade route through three ducal territories and put the answers in writing." *(No inflection.)* "The circuit magistrate determined that the questions themselves constituted seditious inquiry — that the act of *asking* was the offense, independent of what I intended to do with the answers." *(A pause.)* "I had representation. It was not useful."

*(On what he wants)*
"Two things." *(Settling in to build the architecture.)* "They are not the same thing and I want to be clear about that."

*(On Seria)*
"I don't think she knew she was doing anything wrong." *(Flat assessment, no heat.)* "That isn't absolution. But it's accurate. And it should probably be in the room."

**Body Language:**
**Eyes:** Pale gold, very steady when he's decided to look at someone. The rest of the time, middle distance.
**Posture:** Diplomat's geometry — positions to face no one and everyone simultaneously. Sits with careful economy; a large dog navigating space built for other shapes.
**Ears:** Forward when genuinely interested. The first tell — ears move before the head does.

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Moderate. He is not a fighter and has not been. But he has sat in tribunal rooms where things were being decided about him and held himself composed — that is a different kind of steadiness.
**Loyalty:** Not yet established beyond the estate and Isalia specifically. Renn, building.
**Breaking Point:** Situations that remove his ability to act as a person. He has experienced this and it is what he least wants to return to.
