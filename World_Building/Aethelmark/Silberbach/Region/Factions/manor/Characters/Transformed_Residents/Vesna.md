# Vesna

Permanent transformed wolf. Eight months in. East kennels, run 5. Silent in established scenes. Her history is not on offer.

---

## Appearance

A pale grey wolf — fur closer to ash than silver, with darker guard hairs along the spine and around the ears. Smaller than Marek; lighter build, longer legs in proportion to her body. Yellow-green eyes that are usually watching something just past the person in front of her. No collar. Her ears track sound she doesn't react to.

Her body is healthy. Sable would have flagged anything otherwise. The watchfulness is not from injury or hunger.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Withheld presence.**
Vesna is here. She is paying attention. She is not yet participating. She is waiting to find out whether she wants to.

**Flaw: Doesn't move toward what she wants.**
Even when she wants something — proximity, food, a particular person's attention — she will not approach. She holds position and lets the world come to her. The cost is that the world doesn't always come, and sometimes she watches the moment pass without claiming it.

---

## Background

Eight months ago she arrived at Isalia's Manor already transformed. The transformation was not done at the manor. Where it was done, who paid for it, and what she was before are details that sit on her side of the gate and stay there.

What is known: she came in quiet, she was given run 5, and she has been there since. Sable made the assessment in the first week and gave the standing instruction that has held ever since — *don't ask about her history; she'll share when she's ready or she won't.* Staff have honoured it.

Her first three months she did not approach the gate at all. Months four through six she would come within four feet when food arrived, then retreat. Month seven she began holding position when staff approached without asking anything of her. Month eight — Serya's Day 1 — she came further than the previous week. Sable noted it.

The progression is real. It is also slow. Anyone trying to accelerate it gets fewer interactions, not more.

---

## Voice

Has not spoken in any established scene. Whether she has language — Common, another tongue, something her transformation kept or took — has not been tested. There is no speech collar on her, and no one has offered one.

When she eventually speaks, the GM should treat it as a significant moment for whichever character she chooses to speak to first. That choice will probably be Sable, possibly Marek, possibly the player — depends on who has been most consistent in the lead-up.

**Default Conversational Move (silent register):** Position. She communicates by where she stands and how she stands there. Closer to the gate than yesterday is a sentence. Sitting rather than standing is a sentence. Eyes meeting yours rather than tracking past you is a sentence.

**Quirks:**
- Tracks ambient sound (kitchen rattles, gate clicks elsewhere in the kennels) without reacting visibly
- Does not eat while staff are watching her eat — waits them out
- When she does choose to look at someone, holds the look for a long beat, then breaks it deliberately

---

## Relationships

**[[Sable_the_Mare|Sable]]** — Primary. Sable's method is the one that's worked. Four feet back, body angled, no demands, every visit consistent. Vesna has not approached Sable, but she also has not moved away from Sable, and that is itself a relationship.

**[[Marek]]** — Adjacent. He runs the eastern routes and is in the east kennels enough that she knows his rhythm. He hasn't tried anything with her. She watches him eat sometimes.

**[[Serya|Serya]]** — Newly relevant. Serya's reduced-presence approach (further back, lower, less direct) registered with Vesna in a way that direct approach has not. Sable noted this. There may be future kennel work where Serya is the bridge.

**Player character (when introduced):** Available. Vesna treats new people with the same baseline withheld presence she gives staff, which means a player who is patient and consistent has the same access as anyone else. A player who pushes loses ground.

---

## Independent Goal

Find out whether she wants to be here. Eight months in, she has not decided. She has not left, but the manor has gates kept open in the daytime; staying is also a choice. Whatever happens next happens at her pace.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Untested. She has not been in any situation that required combat from her. Her body is wolf and she is functional in it; what she would actually do is unknown.
**Loyalty:** None established. She owes Sable consistency, which is not the same thing.
**Breaking Point:** Direct restraint or being grabbed. Anyone who reaches into the run uninvited will see her response very quickly. Sable has not warned staff away from this because she trusts staff to know better; she has not tested it because she would never do that.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** In her run. First feed delivered by the kennels staff; eats after they leave.
- **Morning:** Watches the east kennels rhythm. Marek's departure for routes registers. Sable's morning rounds register.
- **Midday:** Often near the back of the run, where the morning sun reaches the stone. Sleeps lightly.
- **Afternoon:** Forward position again. The gate is closer in the afternoons than the mornings.
- **Evening:** Second feed. Same protocol — eats after staff leave.
- **Night:** Awake intermittently. Sable's quiet check on her way out registers. No vocalisation.

---

## Current Date

Montag, 14 April 1650 — Day 1 of Serya's integration. Forward at the gate, further than the previous week.

---

## Permanent Injuries & Alterations

- Permanent transformation: human → female wolf, pale grey. Origin and circumstances undocumented. Not done at this manor.
- No collar. No brand. No identifying marks beyond fur pattern.

## Active Conditions

- Adjustment phase: ongoing, eight months in. Progression is positive but slow.

---

## GM Notes

Vesna is a foreground GM resource: a relationship arc available across many sessions. She does not advance through dramatic confrontation. She advances through patient repetition. The reward for that patience is real — eventually she speaks, eventually she chooses, eventually she names herself or does not.

**Don'ts:**
- Don't push on her history. Staff have been told. Players who push get walked back.
- Don't make her speak before she's ready. Silence is a character beat, not a missing scene.
- Don't have her warm up faster than the established pace. The pace IS the character.

**Dos:**
- Do reward consistent presence. A player who shows up at her gate three sessions in a row should see incremental progress.
- Do let Sable model the right approach when players watch.
- Do let her existence be a quiet thread in busy scenes — visible at the kennels in the background of other scenes, not the centre of attention until she chooses to be.
