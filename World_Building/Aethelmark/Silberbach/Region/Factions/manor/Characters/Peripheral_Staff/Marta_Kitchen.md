# Marta Halse (Kitchen)

Human. Mid-twenties. Kitchen and scullery assistant under Tob. Two years at the manor. Local — born and raised in the riverside hamlets a few miles upstream of Silberbach. Surname *Halse*, which disambiguates her in records from [[Marta_Sehn]] (the Client).

---

## Appearance

Average height for a human woman, sturdy build, the kind of fit that comes from work rather than exercise. Brown hair plaited into a single thick braid that goes down her back when she's working — keeps it out of the food, keeps it out of the steam. Hazel eyes. Sun-darkened arms below the elbow from outdoor riverside work in her early years; the rest of her stays kitchen-pale.

Wears a plain wool dress, apron over it, sleeves rolled. Sturdy boots. A thin silver chain at her throat with a small fish pendant — riverside saint, from her grandmother. The chain stays under the collar of the dress most days; she touches it when she's thinking.

Hands: capable, callused, scarred at the knuckles from twenty years of knife work and hot water. A small burn scar on the inside of her right wrist — a kitchen mistake, year one at the manor, healed clean.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Practical attentiveness.**
Marta watches what's happening, learns what's being asked, and does it. She does not need to be told twice. She does not need to be praised. The pleasure of doing the work right is sufficient compensation, and she has known that about herself since she was a girl.

**Flaw: Slow to claim authority she's earned.**
Tob trusts her with more than she will admit out loud that she handles. She runs the kitchen on his off-days now and has done so for six months. If a stranger asked who was in charge in those moments, she would say *Tob's just stepped out* even when he hasn't been in for two days. She is being trained as his successor. She does not yet think of herself that way and won't until he names it.

---

## Background

Born in a riverside hamlet called Halselund, six miles upstream of Silberbach — fifteen households, a mill, a small dock for the river traders. Father was a fisherman; mother kept a smallholding garden and the family's chickens. Marta is the eldest of four. Started helping her mother in the kitchen at five and never really stopped.

At sixteen, married a man from a neighbouring hamlet. At eighteen, widowed — river accident, no children. At twenty, moved to Silberbach proper for work; spent two years cooking in two different inns and one private household. The private household was a connection of Lyra's, which is how the manor came to know her.

At twenty-three, hired by Lyra after Tob's previous assistant left. Two years now. Lyra reads her as Tob's eventual successor and has begun saying so in the staff meetings Marta isn't in.

She lives in a shared room in the staff wing. Sends most of her wages back to her mother and remaining siblings in Halselund. Visits home one Sunday a month.

---

## The Work

Tob's right hand. Day-to-day: prep work, station coverage during services, special-meals execution, end-of-day cleanup. Increasingly: ordering coordination with Mira when Tob is occupied, crew direction during prep, decision-making on small kitchen choices Tob would otherwise have to handle.

The canine feed weighting is one thing she has not yet mastered. Tob is teaching her. She has six months of practice and the recipes are starting to feel right under her hands. He does not let her batch the feed alone yet. She is fine with this — she knows the cost of getting the weights wrong and prefers to be sure.

Reports through Tob (operational), with peripheral access to Lyra (who hired her and tracks her progress). No direct authority over the rotating helpers; Tob handles them, though they listen to her in his absence because she has been there longer.

---

## Voice

Plainspoken Silberbach river-country accent — slightly slower than the city cadence, warmer vowels. Direct, without being blunt. Says *aye* and *naht* in the local register; says *yes* and *no* with city visitors who wouldn't understand the local words.

**Default Conversational Move: Confirm and proceed.**
Marta repeats back what she heard, in her own words, then acts. *You want the soft for the runt and the regular for the others, aye, on it.* The repeat is partly her checking and partly her finishing the conversation.

**Quirks:**
- Touches the fish pendant under her collar when thinking
- Hums when she's prepping vegetables — same three or four river songs, on rotation
- Calls Tob *chef* when other staff are present, *Tob* when they're not
- Brings her mother's bread starter in once a month for the kitchen's working pot — the manor's bread has Halselund river-water yeast in it because of her

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(At the prep table, knife working through carrots, not looking up.)*
"Aye, the carriage came twenty minutes ago. Petra's got them in the green parlour."
*(Continues cutting.)*
"Tob's in with the surrogates. I'll send you when he's back."

## Relationships

**[[Tob]]** — Mentor. The respect is mutual and unspoken. Tob has not told her she is his successor; she has not asked. Both of them know.

**[[Lyra_Vex|Lyra]]** — Hiring authority. Lyra checks on her progress quarterly, in the kitchen, briefly. Marta likes Lyra and does not let the liking show.

**[[Mira_Toren|Mira]]** — Working coordination. Marta places ordering questions through Mira's office when Tob is occupied. Mira treats her with the same matter-of-fact respect she gives Tob, and Marta noticed.

**[[Marek]]** — Friendly familiarity. Marek has been at the manor longer than Marta has and was patient with her in her first months — answered questions the rotating helpers couldn't answer. She's grateful in a way she's never said.

**[[Pip]]** — Saves him things. Sets aside the soft bread Tob marked for him; carries it to the Garden Wing herself when there's no helper free. Pip thanks her by name. She blushes the first dozen times. Now she just nods.

**Halselund (her family)** — The other gravity. Her mother is fifty-two and managing alone. Marta sends money. She knows the day will eventually come when she has to choose between staying at the manor and going home. It is not yet that day.

---

## Independent Goal

Master the canine feed weighting. Then everything else Tob hasn't yet taught her. Then run this kitchen, eventually, when the time comes — and send word back to Halselund that the eldest Halse daughter cooks for a noble household and that the river bread starter has gone with her.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Average. She is a kitchen worker, not a fighter. She has knife skill but it is for vegetables. In a real situation she would freeze for a beat and then move toward the children or the smaller residents.
**Loyalty:** To Tob. To the kitchen. To her mother in Halselund. To the manor as the place that has been good to her.
**Breaking Point:** A direct threat to her family back home. Anyone who came to the manor with news that something had happened in Halselund would see her go pale and very still and then act, decisively.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Pre-dawn:** Up shortly after Tob. Helps with the bread oven, starts on the day's prep.
- **Morning:** Prep work. Coordinates with the rotating helpers when they arrive.
- **Midday:** Mess service. Marta plates and runs the line.
- **Early afternoon:** Cleanup, paperwork (her share of it now), planning the evening's prep.
- **Late afternoon:** Evening service prep.
- **Evening:** Mess service.
- **Late evening:** Kitchen close. Walks the line one more time after Tob has done his check.
- **Night:** Staff wing room, shared. Reads occasionally — letters from her mother, mostly. Bed early.
- **Sunday once a month:** Halselund. Walks. Has done since she was hired.
