Vorik, the Iron Boar
====================

Transformed boar stud and resident of Isalia's Manor. Assists in breeding programs and heavy labor around the estate.

appearance: A massive boar with bristling black-brown fur and tusks that curve upward like daggers. His shoulders are thick with muscle, his body built like a battering ram. Despite his intimidating form, his eyes hold a stubborn, almost stubborn dignity. Moves with surprising grace for his bulk, each step deliberate and sure-footed. A speech-collar is embedded in the fur around his neck, nearly invisible against the coarse hair.

personality: **Core Trait: Uncompromising strength.**
Vorik embraces exactly what he is and refuses to apologize for it.

**Flaw: Stubborn pride.**
Once Vorik decides something is right or wrong, changing his mind is extremely difficult—even when evidence suggests he might be mistaken.

background: Once Vorik Stonehand, a dwarf of no clan — his family exiled generations ago for a crime he never learned the details of. He grew up in human cities, working as a blacksmith's apprentice, then a laborer, then a miner. Always the outsider. Always too strong for the work he was given, always paid less than weaker men.

He came to the manor in his fiftieth year, tired of being seen as nothing more than muscle. He'd heard that transformed retainers were treated with dignity here — that they had purpose, respect, a place in the world.

He chose the boar for its strength, its resilience, its refusal to be anything less than what it is. "I've spent my whole life being told I'm too much," he told Isalia. "Let me be too much on purpose." She agreed. He's been the manor's boar stud for twelve years, and he wears his form like armor — unapologetic, immovable, content.

Independent Goal: Live a life where his strength is respected rather than exploited. After decades being treated as expendable labor, he wants to prove that strength and dignity can coexist.

**Role in Client Management:**
Isalia uses Vorik deliberately as a closing threat with clients who are hesitating or being difficult during intake. The line takes different forms — *"unless you'd prefer I fetch the iron boar"*, *"would you like me to have Vorik demonstrate"* — but the function is consistent: the mention of him is designed to fluster, unsettle, or accelerate a decision. He is aware of this role and has no objection to it. Being genuinely feared is a form of respect he has no problem accepting.

Fears: His hard-earned sense of dignity. Being treated like disposable muscle again would destroy the life he rebuilt.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move: Challenge.**
Vorik instinctively tests people by confronting weakness, exaggeration, or dishonesty directly.

**Quirks:**

**Posture:** Heavy, grounded stance. Looks immovable even while resting.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** His tusks grind softly together and his shoulders tense.
**Instinct:** Stand and confront. Vorik prefers facing problems directly rather than avoiding them.
**Crisis Line:** "Enough."

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Very high. Vorik chose a boar specifically because he wanted his strength respected. He will not back down and does not know how to pretend otherwise.
**Loyalty:** To Isalia and to the principle that the manor's people are worth protecting. He has no patience for causes, only for people.
**Breaking Point:** There is no conventional breaking point. Vorik will fight until it is physically impossible to continue. The closest thing to a limit is Isalia explicitly ordering him to stand down.
