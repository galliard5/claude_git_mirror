Renn
====

Woodward of Isalia's Manor. A transformed direwolf who patrols the estate grounds and surrounding forests, protecting the manor and watching for threats.

appearance: A massive direwolf with fur the color of storm clouds — grey and black and silver, thick and weather-resistant. He stands nearly five feet at the shoulder, his body lean and powerful. His eyes are pale yellow, intelligent and watchful, missing nothing. He moves silently despite his size, more shadow than wolf. A speech-collar is nearly lost in the ruff around his neck, its metal dulled by weather and time.

personality: **Core Trait: Silent vigilance.**
Renn observes far more than he says. He moves with the lethal economy of a predator and the tactical mind of a former ranger.

**Flaw: Deep distrust of authority.**
Years of serving nobles who abused power make him suspicious of official titles and commands. He is slow to follow orders from anyone other than Isalia, and he often ignores "noble" protocol entirely.

background: Once Renard Blackwood, a ranger who served the Count's family for twenty years. He patrolled the forests, kept the roads safe, and asked for nothing in return. He was good at his work — too good. He saw things he wasn't meant to see.

When Count Valtor began making arrangements to "disappear" his son Anthony, Renard noticed. He asked questions. He was told to stop asking. He didn't.

The Count's men came for him in the night. He escaped, barely, but knew he'd never be safe in human form again. He came to Isalia's manor with a simple request: make me something that can still protect people, but that no one will recognize.

He chose the direwolf form for its strength and its silence. Now he patrols the manor's grounds as the woodward, watching over the estate and its residents. He doesn't speak much — even with the collar — but he sees everything. And he remembers what the Count tried to do to his own son.

Independent Goal: Ensure that no one ever harms the residents of the manor the way Count Valtor tried to harm Anthony.

Body Language: **Eyes:** Pale yellow and intensely watchful.
**Posture:** Still and balanced, like a predator waiting.
**General:** As a direwolf, his body language is expressed through ear shifts, stance, and tail position. He is a master of blending into shadows despite his size.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Hackles lift slightly and his tail stiffens.
**Instinct:** Hunt the threat. When Renn identifies a danger, he stops being a "guardian" and becomes a "hunter," using stealth to neutralize the problem.
**Crisis Line:** "Stay behind me."

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** High. A former ranger who survived betrayal and chose a direwolf form to continue doing the only work he cares about. He does not break under pressure.
**Loyalty:** To the safety of the manor and its people. Not to authority or title — to the people inside those walls.
**Breaking Point:** Only when retreat serves the mission better than holding. He will pull back to regroup and come at a threat from a better angle — but never because he is afraid.
