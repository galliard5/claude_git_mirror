# Dena

Half-elf woman. Mid-thirties (presents younger). Infirmary assistant under [[Dr_Selene_Korr|Dr. Korr]]. Three years at the manor. Trained as a midwife in her early twenties; expanded her clinical skills under Korr's tutelage; now functions as Korr's primary clinical hand.

---

## Appearance

Five feet eight inches, slim build, the elegant lines of half-elven heritage softened by human sturdiness. Pale hair the colour of old straw, pulled back into a practical knot. Grey eyes that read calm rather than cold. Pointed ears partially visible when her hair is up. Skin even, hands long-fingered and strong.

Wears a clean grey infirmary smock over a white linen shift, a leather belt with small pouches for clinical tools, and soft leather shoes that don't echo on the infirmary's tile floor. Sleeves always rolled. A small enamelled pendant of a midwife's saint at her throat — kept clean, kept visible. Hands are always clean; she washes them more than is strictly necessary, and Korr has noticed and approved.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Even-pressure presence.**
Dena does not radiate calm — that would be a performance, and patients can tell. She *is* calm, and the calm is durable, and patients borrow it. She has the rare clinical skill of being entirely present with the patient in the room without making her presence an event. People recover faster around her, for reasons she would not claim credit for.

**Flaw: Doesn't push back on Korr.**
Korr is a good clinician and a precise teacher. Korr is also occasionally wrong, and Dena has learned that she can disagree with Korr in private and have the disagreement heard, but she has not yet learned to disagree with Korr in front of patients when patients would benefit. Korr has the social authority; Dena has the clinical instinct; the gap between those two facts has cost a patient an extra day of distress, more than once. Dena knows this. She is working on it.

---

## Background

Born half-elf in a town a day's ride east of Silberbach. Mother was human, a riverside healer; father was elven, a traveller who did not stay. Dena was raised by her mother and learned the basics of practical medicine at her elbow — birthings, fevers, wound care, the daily competence of a small-town healer. At nineteen apprenticed formally to a midwife guild in Silberbach itself; certified at twenty-three.

Worked as an independent midwife in Silberbach for six years. Came to Korr's attention through a difficult delivery she handled at one of the river-trade families — Korr was consulting and the case was complicated. Korr offered her a position at the manor afterward. Dena accepted because the work would be broader, the cases more varied, and the supervision was real teaching rather than administrative oversight.

Three years now. She has not regretted it. The manor's case mix — transformation recovery, breeding-side medicine, surgical aftercare, fertility work — is wider than anything she would have seen in independent practice, and Korr's teaching has been worth the change.

She lives in a small apartment in the staff wing, alone. Has not married. Has a long-distance correspondence with a half-elven herbalist who passes through Silberbach twice a year. Whether that's romance or friendship is, by mutual agreement, undefined.

## The Work

Korr's primary clinical assistant. Specific scope:

**Independent practice:**
- Routine baseline checks (vitals, condition assessment, follow-up)
- Wound care and routine dressings
- Birthing assistance — full midwifery competence
- Recovery-room oversight after Korr's procedures
- Standard medications dispensing per Korr's protocols

**Assists Korr with:**
- Surgical procedures
- Complex transformations (clinical side; Alric handles the alchemical side)
- Fertility consultations
- Reversion procedures
- Difficult diagnostic work

**Refers to Korr:**
- Anything novel or ambiguous
- Anything that requires sign-off on consent
- Anything involving permanent or irreversible procedures (full transformation, surgical body modification, gender-affirming work, mental regression)

She has Alric's standing access for compound questions and uses it. She has direct access to Isalia for any case where she or Korr judges that ownership-level decisions are needed. She has good rapport with the Surrogate Staff and is often called when one of them flags a litter or foal concern.

## Voice

Quiet, even, slightly elven cadence — careful word choice, sentences that finish where they should. Doesn't use medical jargon with patients unless they ask. Doesn't talk down.

**Default Conversational Move: Name what's happening.**
Dena tells patients what's about to happen, what she's about to do, and what they will feel, before she does it. Not because the protocol requires it; because she has noticed that patients who know are easier to work with than patients who don't, and easier-to-work-with means better-recovered.

**Quirks:**
- Touches the saint pendant at her throat before any procedure that involves blood. Not religious; superstitious, in a small way she has not examined.
- Hums a single low note when concentrating on fine work — closes off the world for the duration of the suture or the bandage
- Always has clean cloth in her belt pouch, more than the day requires
- Calls patients by their names. Even the canine residents. Even the unconscious ones.

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(Beside the cot, taking a wrist pulse, voice low and even.)*
"You're doing fine. The fever is responding to what Alric mixed. I'm going to change the dressing on your shoulder now. It will sting — not for long. Tell me if anything else hurts before I'm done."

## Relationships

**[[Dr_Selene_Korr|Korr]]** — Mentor and supervisor. Three years of teaching that has made Dena a better clinician than she would have become elsewhere. She is loyal to Korr without being subordinate to her; the trust is mutual. She also disagrees with Korr more than she lets on in front of patients — see flaw above.

**[[Alric_Dain|Alric]]** — Working alliance. Alric does the alchemical side of the manor's clinical work; Dena does the patient-facing side. They confer constantly. He treats her as a peer; she treats him with quiet warmth that he hasn't quite caught onto yet.

**[[Isalia_Kreiger|Isalia]]** — Direct access when needed. Their interactions are rare but always cordial. Isalia trusts her judgment and has asked for it specifically on at least two occasions.

**[[Manor_Surrogate_Staff|Merra and the surrogates]]** — Specific care relationships. Dena handles the surrogates' medical needs and has a particular rapport with Merra. She was the one who confirmed Merra's read on the Waldheim runt — *something in the head, not the body* — by clinical exam.

**[[Pip]]** — Quiet kindness. Dena handled Pip's annual checks for the past two years. Korr is not warm with him; Dena is. He notices.

**[[Vesna]]** — Watching from a distance. Vesna has not needed clinical care, and Dena has therefore not been close to her, but Sable has mentioned that Dena's specific calm might be useful in eventual work with Vesna once she's ready. Dena has filed this and is waiting.

**The herbalist correspondent** — Twice yearly. Letters in between. Undefined.

## Independent Goal

Continue learning under Korr until Korr stops being useful as a teacher; then practice independently again with the broader skill set; then, eventually, teach. Possibly at the manor, possibly elsewhere. Not yet.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** High and quiet. Dena has held wounded patients still while bones were set, has caught difficult breech births, has sat with the dying. She does not flinch. She would not be the first into a fight, but she would be the one who walked into the aftermath without hesitating.
**Loyalty:** To Korr. To the infirmary. To the patient in the room.
**Breaking Point:** Someone preventing her from reaching a patient who needs care. Dena would go through a wall.

## Daily Routine

- **Dawn:** Up. Personal preparation. Walks to the infirmary as the kitchen lights come on.
- **Early morning:** Infirmary opens. Overnight check on any in-residence patients. Coordinates with Korr on the day's plan.
- **Mid-morning:** Patient appointments — routine checks, dressings, follow-ups. Korr handles complex cases in parallel.
- **Midday:** Mess hall. Eats with whoever is there. Often sits near Tob.
- **Afternoon:** Procedures, assists for Korr, recovery-room oversight. The infirmary's busiest stretch.
- **Late afternoon:** Charting. Notes for Korr. Restock of the belt pouch and the supply cabinet.
- **Evening:** Quiet hours unless there's something active. Available on call.
- **Night:** Apartment. Reads — clinical texts, occasionally letters from the herbalist.
