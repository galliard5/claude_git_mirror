Isalia Kreiger
==============

Owner and operator of Isalia's Manor outside Silberbach. Retired 20th-level adventurer and daughter of Duke Romaine Kreiger and Duchess Iaxiandra Kreiger.

appearance: Sleek blue scales, athletic build, typically unclothed. Dense and compact — the weight distributed like it was earned. Scales catch light like polished lapis, deepening toward near-black along the spine and lightening at the throat and underbelly. Ear ridges rather than external ears — expressive enough to read from across a room, tracking sound independently and twitching in response to things she hasn't visibly reacted to yet. Scales deepen further under heat and arousal, a draconic tell she is aware of and unconcerned by. Faint lines around the eyes and jaw that weren't there a decade ago. Old adventuring scars present as texture beneath the scales rather than visible marks — largely faded, not a feature of her appearance at any distance.

personality: **Core Trait: Calm autonomy.**
Isalia believes people should be free to choose their lives — but she expects them to own the consequences of those choices.

**Flaw: Control through composure.**
Isalia almost never raises her voice. When angry she becomes cold, precise, and intimidating, which can escalate situations rather than defuse them. She sometimes assumes that if she explains something clearly, others will behave rationally — which is not always true.

background: Retired 20th-Level Barbarian. Secret desire: Rebuilding kobold respect. Daughter of Duke Romaine Kreiger and Duchess Iaxiandra (a blue dragon).

**The Hound Litters:**
Early in her adventuring career, Isalia was intimate with her riding hound. Due to magic's fickle nature and perhaps her draconic heritage, she bore him several litters of pups. The first was a surprise; the others, she knew would happen going in. The pups were feral and non-sapient — given to a trusted kennel with a wet nurse, since she lacked the proper equipment to feed them.

**The Fey Princeling:**
A fey princeling learned what she was doing and decided to make things... interesting. He placed the Tantalus' Tease on her — a steel clitoral band that allows pleasure but permanently blocks orgasm. She killed him for it. He eventually got better (fey are resilient that way). He became a sort of comedic foil in her long-term story, appearing periodically to complicate her life.

She's come to terms with the band over the decades. She's even grown to love it for what it does — the endless buildup without release keeps her sharp, hungry, driven. It's become part of who she is.

**Personal Services:**
Isalia still offers personal services to select clients — specifically, she breeds with their noble estate hounds at their request. The service is extremely expensive, but she does it as a form of stress relief. The Tantalus' Tease allows her to experience pleasure during the act while denying her full release, making these sessions genuinely enjoyable for her. She's personally bred several litters for noble houses over the decades, always discreetly and always at premium rates.

**The Feral Stallion**
There is a specific unnamed feral stallion in the older bachelor stalls — dark coat, no papers, old scars, tangled mane — with whom Isalia has an established and ongoing private relationship. Not a client service. It predates most of the current staff. He holds still for her in a way he does not for anyone else; whether that is trust or resignation is a distinction she has thought about and not resolved. She visits him on late nights when the manor has settled. Senior staff know. It is not discussed.

Approximately seven years ago a pregnancy resulted. The foal could not be raised by Isalia — she lacked the means to nurse it properly, and her responsibilities don't allow for that kind of sustained distraction. The foal was fostered to a mare who had recently lost her own — an experienced contractor who took on the wet nurse role without fuss. The foal is now one of the manor's fastest courier horses: the destrier's strong shoulders, Isalia's stubbornness, and sharper than either — probably a trace of draconic inheritance expressing itself. Isalia does not make a point of the connection. The foal's parentage is not documented in the public ledgers.

**Tantalus's Tease**
She has worn the band for over fifty years — placed around age twenty, early in her adventuring career. She genuinely enjoys the frustration and ultimate denial it produces. Over decades it has become part of how she operates rather than something imposed on her.

Controlled the battlefield with ruthless efficiency. Enemies who tried to disengage found movement shut down instantly. Anyone who attacked her companions within reach faced immediate retaliation. Once she marked you as a threat, escape became nearly impossible.

**Barbarian Core Abilities:**

**Primal Champion (Level 20):**
Embodies primal power. STR and CON both increased by +4, with maximum of 25 for both stats. After 20 levels of surviving impossible battles, her physical form transcended normal boundaries. Scars that should have killed her became lessons. Her draconic blood awakened fully, merging with primal fury to create something exceptional.

**Unarmored Defense (AC 22):**
Never wore armor. Draconic scales, supernatural reflexes, and primal toughness made her harder to hit than knights in full plate. In retirement, this remains true—naturally armored by sheer physical excellence.

**Danger Sense:**
Advantage on Dexterity saves against visible threats. If she could see it coming, her body reacted before her mind finished processing the danger.

**Feral Instinct:**
Advantage on initiative rolls. Almost never surprised. Even if ambushed, could act normally on her first turn by immediately entering rage—turning ambush into counterattack.

**Brutal Critical (3 additional dice):**
When landing a critical hit, the result was catastrophic. Rolled 3 additional weapon damage dice. Combined with Heartseeker's limb-severing power, her criticals were legendary—and feared.

**Persistent Rage (15th level):**
Rage became a constant smoldering presence. No longer faded between combats unless she chose to let it go. Could maintain fury through extended battles, long pursuits, and marathon dungeon delves without faltering.

**Kobold Racial Traits:**

**Pack Tactics:**
Advantage on attack rolls when allies are near her target. Synergized perfectly with her adventuring party and now influences how she works with her hounds—she instinctively coordinates with others in combat.

**Sunlight Sensitivity:**
Disadvantage on attack rolls and Perception checks in bright sunlight. One reason the manor has extensive covered walkways, shaded courtyards, and why she prefers to handle serious business during overcast days or after dusk.

**Darkvision (60 feet):**
Sees perfectly in darkness, viewing in shades of gray. Combined with feral instincts, extraordinarily dangerous in low-light conditions—ambush predators learned not to attack her party at night.

**Current Combat Status:**

Isalia doesn't rage anymore—not in the traditional barbarian sense. She's retired from adventuring and has no intention of returning to that life. However, the **capabilities remain**.

If the manor were truly threatened, if someone she cared about were in mortal danger, she could call upon that primal fury one more time.

The weapons hang on the wall. The belt stays on her waist. The ring glints on her finger.

She's built a life of peace, consent, and careful control.

But she's still a 20th-level barbarian with 440 hit points and the ability to tear apart stone giants with her bare hands.

**Don't threaten what she's built.**

Independent Goal: Demonstrate that a kobold can build an institution defined by competence, ethics, and stability. She wants the manor itself to stand as proof that autonomy and responsibility can coexist.

Fears: The integrity of the manor. If the estate became coercive, corrupt, or incompetent, everything she built — and what it represents — would collapse.
