# Reinheim

The riverside settlement on the Angerap further upstream of Halselund. Population approximately 480. Two days' travel upstream from Silberbach via the Valley Road or by river barge. Reinheim sits at the junction where a smaller tributary stream joins the Angerap, providing both river access and a secondary water source that has supported the settlement's particular economy across many generations.

Reinheim is unusual in the March because of its demographic composition — approximately forty percent of the population is half-elven, descended from elven traders who settled along the upper Angerap centuries ago and intermarried with the human population. The half-elven cultural register pervades the settlement: longer family memories, a slightly different religious tradition, distinctive crafts that have not spread to the rest of the March, and a working multi-ethnic stability that Silberbach's residents would recognise from Waldheim Lodge but extended into a larger and longer-established community.

Reinheim is the home settlement of [[Master_Edda_Reinhart]] (Tanners Guild), [[Dena]] (the manor's infirmary assistant), and Halrana (Senior Dockmaster Bero's wife). The settlement does not advertise itself; visitors discover its character only by spending time there.

---

## Layout

The settlement spreads along both banks of the Angerap at the tributary junction, connected by a wooden bridge that has been replaced multiple times across generations. The settlement is larger than Halselund but smaller than Kaelen's Ford — the size of a substantial village rather than a town.

**The North Bank (the older settlement):**
- The principal cluster of houses, including the oldest stone dwellings (some dating to the original elven settlement, with subsequent generations of human-and-half-elven additions)
- The chapel-house — an unusual building with both a traditional Aethelmark chapel structure and a separate elven-tradition shrine attached at the rear; the two religious traditions operate side-by-side under a shared chaplaincy
- The market plaza, smaller than the Ford's, but better-stocked than Halselund's; supports a permanent fish stall, two produce stalls, a craft stall, and the river-trade booth where smaller upriver merchants sell to local buyers
- The dock, larger than Halselund's, accommodating both the local fishing boats and the more substantial passenger and trade barges
- The senior families' compounds — the Reinhart family compound (now reduced to extended cousins; Master Reinhart's direct family lives in Silberbach), the Voll family compound (Halrana's birth family), several others

**The South Bank (the working districts):**
- Across the bridge, the more recently-developed working districts
- The fulling shop (the settlement maintains its own small fulling operation, an unusual specialty connection to the Silberbach Fullers' Guild)
- The smithy (for hometown ironwork)
- The tannery — small, the operation Master Reinhart's family ran before she left for Silberbach; now operated by extended family
- The smaller residential streets housing the bonded farming families and the working-class population

**The Tributary Stream:**
- Joins the Angerap from the north
- Powers the small grain mill at the upstream end of the settlement
- Provides freshwater independent of the Angerap, important for the tanning operation and for clean drinking water

## Economy and Daily Life

**Mixed working economy.** Reinheim is unusual in the March because its economy is broader than the typical small settlement's monoculture. Fishing (substantial, like Halselund), farming (broader than Halselund — more arable land, more bonded holdings), small craft production (the fulling shop, the smithy, the tannery, plus a handful of leatherworkers and weavers serving the local market), and modest river trade (the dock supports more substantial barge traffic than Halselund's).

**The tanning operation.** Small but present. Operates under the Reinhart family's continued ownership (extended family, since Master Reinhart's direct line lives in Silberbach now) and supplies the local market plus a small surplus that goes to Silberbach via the regular barge connections. Master Reinhart's annual visit home is one of the settlement's quietly anticipated events.

**The fulling operation.** A specialty connection: the Reinheim fulling shop processes wool cloth from local production and from goods that travel up the river for finishing. The operation is small but produces a quality that Silberbach's master fullers respect.

**The chapel and the elven tradition.** Religious life is one of the settlement's distinctive features. The shared chapel-and-shrine arrangement is a working ecumenical structure that has held for generations; the elven-tradition observances are conducted in a register that newcomers find subtly strange (more emphasis on inherited ancestor-relationships, less on imperial-period saints) but that integrates with the broader Aethelmark religious accommodation.

**The Inn (the Bridge House).** Reinheim's principal inn, located at the northern end of the bridge. Five rooms (three single, two shared), substantial food including some half-elven specialties that have not spread elsewhere in the March. Run by the Voll family — Halrana's birth family, three generations in the trade. The Voll family is one of the settlement's notable nodes; they correspond regularly with Halrana in Silberbach, and Senior Dockmaster Bero has visited them several times across the years.

## The Bridge House

Halrana's birth family's inn. Friendly, well-run, with the characteristic Reinheim half-elven cultural texture in its food, its taproom register, and its visitor accommodation. The family is proud of Halrana and of her settled life in Silberbach; they ask after her often. They have, more than once, raised the possibility of her returning home eventually, when Bero's career allows it. The conversation has not been resolved.

## Politics and Atmosphere

Reinheim has its own distinct cultural identity that the settlement understates. The half-elven population's longer family memories produce a sense of continuity that newcomers find subtly steadying; the half-elven-and-human stability has held across generations of imperial change without much external attention. The settlement's residents do not perform their distinctness; visitors who pay attention notice it.

Politically, Reinheim is inert at the regional level. The settlement's residents do not have factional opinions on Valtor versus Kaelen worth recording. The bonded landlords (who include several small noble holdings; none of the major March houses) collect tithe and maintain general oversight without pressing on the local political life.

The settlement's economic ties to Silberbach are stronger than its political ones. The fulling shop's connection to the Silberbach Fullers' Guild, the river trade route, the small goods that move both directions, plus the prominent former residents in Silberbach (Reinhart, Dena, Halrana) — these bind the settlement's economy to the seat-city. The political distance, by contrast, is real and continuing.

## The Reinhart-Dena-Halrana Lineage

Three present-day Silberbach figures with Reinheim origins, each from different generations:

**[[Master_Edda_Reinhart]]** — left Reinheim approximately fifty years ago to apprentice in Silberbach's tanning yards. The Reinhart family compound on the North Bank still belongs to extended family; the Master's annual visit home is in midsummer. Her daughter Maren has visited several times. The family's continued tannery operation maintains the working continuity.

**[[Dena]]** — left Reinheim approximately twenty years ago to apprentice as a midwife in Silberbach. Her mother (a riverside healer) trained her in the practical skills that became the foundation of her clinical work under Korr at the manor. Her mother is still alive and lives at the Reinhart compound's western edge. Dena's annual visits home are in early spring; she and her mother spend the days talking quietly about practical medicine.

**Halrana** *(Bero's wife)* — left Reinheim approximately twenty-five years ago when she married Bero. The Voll family at the Bridge House remains in regular contact; Halrana visits home approximately twice a year, more often if Bero can manage the time. Her sister still works the inn alongside Halrana's parents.

The three women are aware of each other's existence in a vague way — Reinhart knows Dena exists at the manor, Dena and Halrana would recognise each other's names — but they have not developed a network with each other. The shared Reinheim origin is a point of quiet recognition rather than active alliance.

## In-Between Texture

**The Valley Road through Reinheim.** Two days from Silberbach. The road is in adequate repair through Reinheim's stretch and continues upstream past the settlement toward the more thinly-inhabited upper-river country. Less heavily trafficked than Kaelen's Ford's stretch but more so than the Pastoral Road or the Coastal Road.

**The river.** Reinheim's most important commercial connection. Daily barge traffic to and from Silberbach; weekly substantial commercial movement; passenger barges that connect smaller upriver settlements through Reinheim as a hub.

**Banditry:** Low. The settlement is too well-established to attract opportunists; the half-elven longer-memory community would notice strangers immediately; the river-and-road combination provides multiple escape routes for any incident.

**Climate and seasons:** River-valley conditions, mild. The tributary stream's flow is more reliable than the Angerap's; in drought years Reinheim retains operational capacity that downstream settlements lose temporarily.

**Religious presence:** The unusual chapel-and-shrine arrangement is the settlement's most distinctive feature. Outsiders sometimes find it strange initially; longer visitors come to appreciate the working ecumenicism.

**Cultural register:** Half-elven cultural pieces are visible to attentive visitors — a particular bread that uses elven flour-blending techniques, a craft style in the local pottery, the older naming conventions some families preserve, the slightly different rhythm of the chapel's seasonal observances. These do not announce themselves; they accumulate.

- **The Voll family** — proprietors of the Bridge House; Halrana's birth family
- **The Reinhart compound's senior cousin** — runs the local tannery; Master Reinhart's distant relative; semi-formal authority within the broader family
- **Dena's mother** — a riverside healer in retirement; lives at the Reinhart compound's edge
- **Father Anvel** — the chapel-house's principal chaplain; half-elven; thirty years in the post; manages the dual chapel-and-shrine ecumenicism
- **The Voll family's senior generation** — Halrana's parents, both alive; manage the Bridge House's working operations

## Hooks

- **A traveler arrives bearing news of an upriver community.** The thinner upper Angerap settlements occasionally send word of trouble downstream. Reinheim is the first substantial settlement to receive it. The hamlet must decide what to forward to Silberbach.
- **The Reinhart family's tannery has a problem.** A working dispute, a chemical issue, a question of succession. Master Reinhart in Silberbach is summoned home for the first time in years.
- **Dena's mother is failing.** The annual spring visit becomes urgent. Dena must decide how long to leave the manor and the working she has there.
- **A Cendrelais visitor seeks out the Reinheim half-elven community.** Possibly a distant relative tracing family connections; possibly something more political. The settlement closes around the visitor cautiously.
- **A barge arrives with cargo that should not be there.** The Reinheim dock is one of the natural points along the river where unusual cargo could be transferred between boats. The local watch (such as it is) notices.
