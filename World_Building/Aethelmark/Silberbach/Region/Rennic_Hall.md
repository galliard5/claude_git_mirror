# Rennic Hall

The border seat of [[House_Rennic]] in the Eastern Marches. Population approximately 600. Three to four days' ride east of Silberbach via the Valley Road eastward. The easternmost substantial settlement in the March of Silberbach and the principal Aethelmark settlement on the long frontier with the Kingdom of the East.

The Kingdom of the East is a separate realm — sovereign, with its own crown, court, and laws — bordering Aethelmark along its entire eastern frontier. Rennic Hall and its surrounding territory function as Aethelmark's working interface with this neighbor: trade, diplomacy, occasional disputes, and the slow continuous business of two empires coexisting at their shared edge. The Easterners are not enemies; they are a separate civilisation with their own history, language register, religious traditions, and political logic, and the Marches' purpose is to manage the relationship rather than fight it.

Lord Edric Rennic rules from Rennic Hall when not traveling on the diplomatic intermediary work that defines his career. Sir Gareth Rennic commands the Marches' military when not in the field; his command tent is at the Hall during quieter seasons.

---

## Layout

The Hall and its associated settlement sit on a low rise in the gently-rolling country east of the imperial heartland. The river network here is different — the Angerap is far to the west; the local rivers are smaller, less navigable, oriented more north-south than east-west. Rennic Hall is overland-traveled rather than river-traveled, which gives the town a different commercial register from anywhere else in the March.

**Rennic Hall (the Hall proper):**
- A substantial stone-and-timber manor house at the rise's crest
- The Hall's outer courtyard, walled but not heavily fortified — the building was built for representational rather than defensive purposes
- The Hall's chapel — older than the manor, dating to the original Rennic settlement of the Marches
- The Hall's stables and the small parade ground where Sir Gareth's command sometimes drills
- The Hall's gardens, modest but well-kept; the late Lady Calantha's particular work, maintained since her death

**The Town:**
- The principal road runs east-west through the center, becoming the Eastern Road as it continues toward the Kingdom of the East
- The market plaza, smaller than the Ford's, oriented around the town crier's post
- The town's two inns — the Marches Inn (better, on the principal road) and the East-Gate Tavern (rougher, near the eastern edge of town)
- The town's chapel-house, distinct from the Hall's chapel — serves the population at large with the mixed-faith framework characteristic of the Empire
- The garrison's town presence — a small barracks, a parade square, the standing patrols
- The merchant quarter — a single street of factor's-houses handling the cross-border trade
- The customs post at the eastern edge of town, where the principal trade with the Kingdom of the East is processed

**The Eastern Approach:**
- The Eastern Road continues east from the town for approximately two days' travel before reaching the formal border crossing
- The road passes through several smaller villages within Rennic-affiliated territory, plus a network of bonded farming hamlets that produce the grain and pastoral goods feeding both Aethelmark and (via licensed export) the Kingdom of the East

## Economy and Daily Life

**Cross-border trade.** The town's distinctive economic engine. Goods moving between Aethelmark and the Kingdom of the East pass through Rennic Hall in both directions. Aethelmark exports primarily worked goods (textiles from Silberbach's Weavers Guild, fine steel from Aldenberg downstream of Rennic but still flowing through the Marches' customs framework, finished leather goods, occasional luxury items) and imports primarily raw materials (the Eastern Kingdom's substantial grain surplus, distinctive eastern wools, horses of a specific breed not found in the Empire, certain timber species, salt from eastern coastal sources that competes with Meerhold's).

**The customs operation.** The eastern customs post is one of the most active customs operations in the Empire. The customs master (a senior imperial functionary appointed from the capital, not a Rennic family member) handles the formal documentation; the practical operation involves a substantial staff of inspectors, factors, and translators (Eastern Common is mostly mutually intelligible with Aethelmark Common, but specific commercial vocabulary diverges enough that translators are useful).

**The garrison.** Sir Gareth's standing force of forty-five regulars and the auxiliaries form a substantial economic presence. The garrison spends, the garrison's families live in the town, the garrison's contracts feed local suppliers.

**Diplomatic traffic.** Rennic Hall hosts visiting diplomatic parties from the Kingdom of the East regularly — sometimes for substantive negotiations, sometimes for ceremonial visits, sometimes for the long-running working relationships between specific officials. The Marches Inn's better rooms are routinely held for such visitors.

**Local agriculture.** Modest. The bonded farming hamlets in Rennic-affiliated territory produce enough for local consumption and a small surplus for the cross-border trade. The land is gentler than the western reaches but the agricultural intensity is lower because so much of the population is engaged in the trade and military economy rather than the agricultural one.

## The Marches Inn

The town's better inn. Eight rooms (four single, four shared), substantial food, a respectable wine cellar that includes Eastern Kingdom imports alongside Aethelmark vintages. Run by a human-and-half-elven family (the Vell family — three generations in the trade) whose Eastern-language fluency is part of the inn's working asset.

The Inn's clientele is exclusively middle-merchant-rank and above. Visiting diplomats stay here when not invited to the Hall directly. The Inn's back parlour is bookable for private meetings; some of the working diplomatic conversations of the Marches happen there, with the Vell family's discretion as institutional.

---

## The East-Gate Tavern

The town's other inn, on the eastern side of town near the customs post. Six rooms, plain food, run by the Hertle family. Serves the customs staff, transient traders, eastern travelers who want familiar faces, and the broader working population of the cross-border economy. Eastern Common is more frequently spoken here than at the Marches Inn; some of the Hertle family's regulars are Easterners with long-standing trade routes through Rennic.

## The Kingdom of the East — A Brief Note for Local Context

The Eastern Kingdom (full name in Aethelmark formal usage: the Kingdom of the Eastern Plains; in working speech: simply *the East*) is a substantial sovereign realm. Important features for travelers and for understanding Rennic Hall's daily life:

- **Sovereign monarchy.** The Eastern Kingdom is ruled by its own king (currently the reigning monarch, who has held the throne for over two decades). Imperial authority does not extend across the border; imperial law does not apply.
- **Cultural distinctness.** The Easterners speak a closely-related but distinct register of Common, follow a partly-overlapping but distinctly-organised faith framework (their pantheon includes deities not honored in Aethelmark and emphasises different aspects of shared deities), maintain different noble structures (their nobility organises through *starostas* — a regional governorship register — rather than imperial counts and barons), and have their own coinage (the *zloty* and lesser denominations).
- **Long peace.** Aethelmark and the Eastern Kingdom have not been at war for over a hundred years. The peace was formalised in a treaty that has been renewed twice and is generally considered durable. Working diplomatic relations are professional and continuous.
- **Trade flow.** The cross-border trade is substantial and well-established. Both realms benefit; both realms protect the framework that allows it.
- **The intermediary tradition.** Lord Edric Rennic's quiet imperial-intermediary role exists in part because the Eastern Kingdom expects Aethelmark to maintain such a function, and the Kingdom maintains its own equivalent counterpart on its side. Rennic and his Eastern counterpart have known each other for over twenty years and correspond regularly on matters that benefit both realms.

This context is generally not foregrounded in everyday town life — it is simply the working frame within which Rennic Hall exists. But it explains why the customs post is large, why diplomatic traffic is regular, and why the town's population includes more multilingual residents than anywhere else in the March.

## Politics and Atmosphere

Rennic Hall has its own distinct cultural register — quieter than Silberbach, more formal than the Ford, more cosmopolitan than Graudorf. The diplomatic traditions of the family shape the town. Visitors report a sense of careful courtesy that pervades local interactions; townspeople are pleasant but reserved with strangers in ways that take longer to warm than the river-country norm.

The town is politically aligned with House Rennic's quiet diplomatic neutrality. There is no strong Valtor sympathy or Kaelen sympathy at the town level; Lord Edric's intermediary role requires his territory to be politically transparent, and the town reflects this. Local conflicts are resolved by Sir Gareth's authority on military matters and by Edric's steward on civil matters; the Marquess's authority is felt through the imperial customs framework but rarely through direct administration.

The town's residents are aware of Lady Seria Rennic's situation at Isalia's manor (she is the family's most visible figure outside the Hall in recent years) but discuss it carefully. The family's reputation is the town's principal export, and the town protects it.

## In-Between Texture

**The Eastern Road.** The principal route west to Silberbach (three to four days), east to the Kingdom border (two days from Rennic Hall to the formal crossing). The road is well-maintained on the Aethelmark side; quality drops slightly on the eastern side until the Easterners' own road maintenance picks up.

**Banditry:** Lower than the western roads. The Marches' standing garrison patrols the principal route; the eastern frontier territory is, paradoxically, safer than some interior stretches because the diplomatic stakes of incidents make both realms invested in suppressing banditry.

**Climate and seasons:** Continental — colder winters than the river valley, hotter summers. Spring and autumn are the trade peaks; winter restricts movement; summer brings the diplomatic visits.

**Religious presence:** The town's mixed-faith chapel-house serves the population with characteristic Aethelmark tolerance. A small Eastern shrine at the customs post — informal, not officially sanctioned but tolerated — accommodates Eastern travelers wanting to perform their own observances before entering or leaving Empire territory. The accommodation is one of the small frictionless gestures that has helped keep the long peace.

**The Eastern Kingdom presence.** Approximately ten to fifteen percent of Rennic Hall's population is of Eastern origin — long-resident merchant families, customs staff with cross-border ties, occasional intermarriages from previous generations. The Eastern presence is normalised; the town is the most ethnically Eastern-influenced settlement in the March by a substantial margin.

## Notable Figures

- **The Vell family** — proprietors of the Marches Inn; multilingual; institutional discretion
- **The Hertle family** — proprietors of the East-Gate Tavern; less polished, more local
- **The customs master** — appointed from the imperial capital; rotates approximately every six years; the current one is in his fourth year
- **The garrison's senior captain** *(under Sir Gareth)* — runs the standing force's daily operations; lives in the town
- **The Hall steward** — handles civil administration during Edric's diplomatic absences

## Hooks

- **A diplomatic incident.** An Eastern traveler is detained at the customs post under questionable circumstances. Edric is away. The decision falls to lower authority.
- **A trade dispute with political implications.** A merchant claims customs corruption; the customs master claims the merchant is the corrupt party; the truth is more complicated.
- **An Eastern visitor with unusual interest in Lady Seria's situation.** Possibly diplomatic legitimate, possibly something else.
- **A border raid (rare).** A rare incursion from a non-state actor (bandit group operating in the borderland forests). Sir Gareth must respond without escalating into a diplomatic incident.
- **A scholar's request.** A scholar from the imperial capital arrives seeking access to the Hall's archives — Rennic family records of cross-border diplomacy. Edric must decide what to share.
