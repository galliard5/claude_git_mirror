# Kaelen's Ford

The town that grew up around the river crossing. Population approximately 1,200, making it the second-largest settlement in the March of Silberbach. Half a day's ride south of Silberbach along the Valley Road. Seat of [[House_Kaelen]], whose Kaelen Hall sits on the higher ground north of the town proper.

The town exists because the Angerap narrows here enough to be forded at low water and bridged at higher. Every traveler moving overland between the March's northern half and its southern half passes through Kaelen's Ford. The toll point at the bridge is the second-most-trafficked tax-collection station in the March.

---

## Layout

The town occupies both banks of the Angerap, connected by the Kaelen Bridge — a stone bridge approximately a hundred and twenty feet across at its longest span, built two generations ago to replace a wooden predecessor. The bridge is the town's spine; the principal businesses cluster at both ends of it.

**The North Bank (the Hall side):**
- Kaelen Hall on the higher ground, half a mile back from the river
- The Marquess's toll house at the bridge's north entrance (operated by Kaelen authority but reporting to Silberbach)
- The Bridge Inn — the larger of the town's two inns, where merchants traveling overland typically stay
- A small cluster of merchant warehouses
- The Ford's chapel-house (mixed-faith, smaller cousin of the Old Temple)
- The northern residential streets — modest but well-maintained, populated mostly by tradesmen and Hall-affiliated staff

**The South Bank (the Crossing side):**
- The toll house's southern annex
- The Ford's market plaza (smaller than Silberbach's Market Square but functional)
- The Ford's smithy, run by a master smith who handles both bridge maintenance and travelers' urgent ironwork
- The Crossing Inn — the smaller of the two inns, more affordable, more local
- The horse-yard where travelers stable their mounts and where Kaelen's horse-breeding operation maintains a sales paddock
- The southern residential streets — older, denser, with the families who have worked the crossing for generations

**The Bridge itself** is lined with small shops at its widest section — a handful of leatherworkers, a notary's stall, two food vendors, a tiny shrine to the river spirits. The shops were not part of the original construction; they accumulated. Periodic Kaelen attempts to clear them have failed against the rents the town reliably collects.

---

## Economy and Daily Life

**The toll.** Every overland traveler crossing the bridge pays a graduated fee — a copper for a single person on foot, several for a mounted rider, more for carts and pack animals, substantial sums for cargo movement. The toll is collected by a staff of four under a senior toll-master who reports to Baron Torvin's steward. Toll revenues are split: a share to House Kaelen, a share to the marquess (the standard imperial assessment), a share to the bridge maintenance fund.

**Horse breeding.** Kaelen Hall maintains a working horse-breeding operation, primarily of riding stock and light draft. The Ford's southern paddock is the public-facing piece of this; sales happen here regularly. The breeding stock proper is at the Hall.

**Travelers' services.** Two inns, the smithy, the horse-yard, a small but reliable saddler, the notary, several food vendors. The town's economy is structurally dependent on through-traffic; an unusually quiet week at the bridge produces a noticeable slowdown across the businesses.

**The market plaza.** Smaller than Silberbach's. Operates daily with a handful of permanent stalls (vegetables, bread, cheese, smoked meat) and weekly market days that bring farmers from the surrounding countryside. The market is friendly and thinly profitable for most stallholders.

**Fishing.** The Angerap's bend at the Ford produces a stable population of river fish; a small fishing community works the shoreline north and south of the bridge. The catch supplies the inns and the market.

---

## The Bridge Inn

The larger of the town's two inns. Run by a human family named Marrek — a husband-and-wife operation now in its third generation, with two grown children helping. The Inn keeps eight rooms (six single, two shared), serves substantial food, maintains a respectable wine cellar, and stables roughly fifteen horses on a reliable basis.

Merchants traveling between Silberbach and the southern reaches of the March use the Bridge Inn by default. The Inn is also a quiet hub for political conversations — Baron Torvin himself occasionally takes meals there when he wants to be visible to his subjects without staging a formal court appearance. Elara has met visiting merchant allies at the Inn on multiple occasions; the back parlour is bookable for private meetings.

---

## The Crossing Inn

The smaller, more affordable inn on the south bank. Run by a halfling man named Per Hoffer who inherited it from his father and has been running it competently for fifteen years. Six rooms, all shared. Plain food, decent ale, and the kind of clientele that does not stay at the Bridge Inn — bonded farmers in town for market day, traveling craftsmen on tight budgets, occasional Kaelen-Hall staff on personal business.

The Crossing's regulars know each other; outsiders are tolerated rather than welcomed. A useful place for a player character to listen and not be noticed.

---

## Politics and Atmosphere

The Ford's residents identify strongly with House Kaelen — Baron Torvin is well-regarded, the toll arrangement is perceived as fair, the family's reformist sympathies are quietly supported in the town's working-class register. The Hall's protective shadow is felt; the town is more comfortable than its size would suggest, partly because Torvin actually administers it competently.

Visitors from Silberbach who arrive expecting a smaller version of the seat-city find instead a different texture — slower, more pastoral, more closely-knit. The Marquess has not visited Kaelen's Ford in living memory; the political distance between Valtor and Kaelen is felt at the local level as much as at the noble one. Townsfolk who have business with imperial authority handle it through Torvin's steward rather than through any direct channel to the marquess's keep.

## In-Between Texture

**Roads:** The Valley Road north to Silberbach is the town's principal route; runs along the river's east bank. The same road continues south past the Ford toward the Pastoral Road junction (which leads onward to Grauwald) and toward the Westgate route. Both directions are well-traveled.

**Banditry:** Minimal in the immediate vicinity — the Hall's protective presence and the bridge's collected toll-staff visibility discourage opportunists. More distant stretches of the Valley Road see occasional incidents but the town itself is among the safest of the March's settlements.

**The river:** Barge traffic does not stop at the Ford as a primary destination — the river is wider and slower here than at Silberbach but lacks proper harbor facilities. Boats can tie up in emergencies; regular traffic does not. Goods moving between Silberbach and Cendrel cross the bridge as overland cargo when they need to, or remain on river barges that pass under the bridge without halting.

**Religious life:** The chapel-house on the north bank serves the town's mixed-faith population. Smaller wayside shrines along the river and at the road junctions mark the spaces where travelers traditionally pause for blessing. The river spirits' shrine on the bridge itself is the most-visited.

## Hooks

- **A toll dispute escalates.** A merchant refuses to pay; the toll-master holds the cargo; the merchant's family connections create political pressure. Torvin must decide whether to enforce or accommodate.
- **A horse goes missing from the breeding paddock.** Kaelen's Ford's reputation for security is at stake. The investigation reveals more than the missing horse.
- **A Cendrelais traveler with uncertain papers.** Crossing south-to-north on the Valley Road. The toll-master is uncertain whether to pass them; the town's local watch is uncertain whether to detain them. Torvin's steward must decide.
- **An incident on the bridge.** A confrontation between travelers escalates physically. The crowd of merchants, locals, and toll-staff who witness it have very different stories about who started it.
- **A meeting at the Bridge Inn's back parlour.** Reformist allies of Torvin's have gathered. A spy is present. Whose?
