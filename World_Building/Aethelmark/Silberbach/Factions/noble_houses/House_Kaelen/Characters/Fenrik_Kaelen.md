Fenrik Kaelen
=============

Baron. Cousin to Baron Torvin Kaelen of the Ford. Werewolf.

---

appearance: Immediately identifiable as not quite human even in repose. Wolfish silhouette — the shoulders are wrong for a man in a cloak, broader and slightly hunched in the way of something that carries its weight differently. Silver-furred where the morning light catches him, which is immediately. Amber eyes that register detail too quickly.

His muzzle is the most readable thing about him — the emotions he controls in his posture surface there. A sliver of white fang when he lies. Nostrils flaring when the scent shifts. The ruff rising along his shoulders under stress before he can stop it.

Well-dressed in the way of a baron who takes the trappings seriously. The wolfish quality sits under the good wool and the polished belt, not over it.

---

personality: **Core Trait:** Protective instinct expressed as control. Fenrik manages things — situations, information, rooms. The visit to Isalia's manor was an exercise in this: he came with funds, with threat intelligence, and with the manner of someone accustomed to arriving and resolving.

**Flaw:** Underestimates people who don't signal threat the way he expects. Isalia's stillness read to him as approachability — a mistake he recalibrated from, but slowly.

---

background: Cousin to Torvin Kaelen of the Ford, holding his own barony at a location not yet documented. The two branches of the family share progressive politics and the Kaelen name but are separate houses for practical purposes.

Mira von Aldenberg is his sister — the familial connection between House Kaelen and House Aldenberg is not fully established in the record. Whether this is a marriage alliance, a complicated family history, or something else is undocumented.

When Mira disappeared and Aldenberg's guard began inquiring, Fenrik came to Isalia's estate personally rather than sending a steward. He arrived at dawn, unannounced, having already assessed the situation enough to know it was delicate. He brought the dowry funds as a practical measure — not a bribe, he would insist — and threat intelligence about Valtor's writs.

He left without the outcome he came for, which was explicit confirmation of his sister's location. He left with the outcome Isalia offered, which was Isalia's word and her reasons. His ruff settled on the way out. That is probably close enough to trust.
