Duke Romaine Kreiger
====================

Blue Kobold, Male. Retired 20th-Level Adventurer, Archdruid, Ruler of a nearby principality. Isalia's father, source of alchemical recipes.

appearance: Smaller and finer-featured than his wife Iaxiandra, but somehow carries more presence. Despite his small kobold stature, Romaine commands rooms through sheer force of quiet authority. He appears to be in his late 20s or early 30s physically due to Timeless Body, though he is 120 years old chronologically. He typically wears his darkwood armor beneath fine ducal robes, and his prayer beads are visible on his wrist. The Belt of Hill Giant Strength gives him surprising physical power despite his scholarly appearance.

personality: Smaller and finer-featured than his wife Iaxiandra, but somehow carries more presence. Romaine is deliberate, scholarly, and commands respect through quiet competence rather than displays of power. His druidic authority and noble title are tools he wields with precision, not ornaments he wears for show.

background: 

**Adventuring Career:**
Romaine was a legendary adventurer and former companion of Emperor Aldric himself. Their bond, forged through shared trials and victories, grants Romaine (and by extension, Isalia's estate) imperial protections and influence. The exact nature of their adventures remains largely unspoken, but the friendship between Duke and Emperor is genuine and enduring.

**Archdruid Authority:**
Romaine's position as Archdruid carries genuine institutional religious authority across most of central and northern Europe—analogous to an Archbishop in the Church. This makes him not merely a powerful spellcaster, but a figure of spiritual and political significance in druidic circles throughout the continent. Druidic councils seek his counsel, and his word carries weight in matters of natural law and sacred tradition.

**The Dukedom:**
Romaine chose his dukedom specifically to formalize and protect Iaxiandra's existing territory. What might have been contested dragon holdings became legitimate noble lands through their marriage and his title. This was a deliberate political move—protecting his wife's domain through legal structures while maintaining her autonomy as a ruling Duchess in her own right.

**The Fey Curse:**
Early in his druidic training, Romaine ran afoul of a fey entity and was cursed: every time he used Wild Shape or reverted to his natural form, his biological sex was randomly determined. For decades, this curse shaped his identity and understanding of gender in profound ways—he lived as both male and female, sometimes shifting multiple times in a single day depending on how often he needed to Wild Shape. The curse was not broken until he reached 17th level and gained Thousand Forms, the ability to cast *alter self* at will, finally giving him control over his own form.

**Marriage to Iaxiandra:**
Romaine is married to Duchess Iaxiandra Kreiger, a blue dragon who appears as a blue kobold and is open about her true nature. She rules her own adjacent territory as Duchess. Their marriage is a true partnership of equals—an Archdruid and a dragon, each powerful in their own right, each respecting the other's domain. 

In their private life, Iaxiandra is the more dominant partner. When Isalia was conceived, Romaine was female due to the fey curse and carried the egg to term—making him technically Isalia's mother, not father, with Iaxiandra as the biological father. This is known only within their immediate family. Publicly, Romaine is referred to as Isalia's father due to social convention and his current presentation, but the biological reality is more complex. Iaxiandra found Isalia's first unexpected litter amusing rather than scandalous, and Romaine helped their child through the experience with characteristic calm and acceptance born from his own experience with gender fluidity and transformation.

**Relationship with Isalia:**
Romaine knows the full scope of what the manor does and helped develop the transformation potion that became the foundation of its services. His own experience with involuntary transformation—and eventually gaining control through Thousand Forms—gave him unique insight into the magic of body transformation and the importance of consent and control over one's own form. He trusts Isalia to run things without requiring updates—a profound expression of confidence in her competence and judgment. When she needed help with the unexpected magical consequences of her first litter, he provided it without judgment. His support is quiet, consistent, and unconditional.

**Longevity:**
Due to his Timeless Body feature and druidic longevity, Romaine has roughly 2,500 years of life remaining. This vast lifespan shapes his perspective—he thinks in terms of centuries, not decades. Inheritance was never a realistic concern for Isalia; Romaine will outlive his grandchildren's grandchildren. This frees both of them to pursue their own paths without the weight of dynastic succession.

As Duke, these skills translate to exceptional governance—he understands his land intimately, reads people accurately, and makes informed decisions based on both practical knowledge and historical precedent.

**Druid Class Features:**

**Circle of the Moon:**
Romaine belongs to the Circle of the Moon—fierce guardians of the wilds who gather under the full moon to share news and trade warnings. They haunt the deepest parts of the wilderness, embodying the wild in their very blood. Changeable as the moon itself, Romaine could prowl as a great cat one night, soar over treetops as an eagle the next day, and crash through undergrowth in bear form to drive off trespassing monsters.

**Combat Wild Shape:**
Romaine can assume Wild Shape as a bonus action rather than a full action, allowing him to transform instantly mid-combat. While transformed, he can expend spell slots as a bonus action to regain 1d8 hit points per spell level—turning his magical reserves into raw vitality when injured in beast form.

**Circle Forms:**
As a 20th-level Circle of the Moon druid, Romaine can transform into beasts with challenge rating up to 6 (his level divided by 3, rounded down). This grants access to truly formidable forms:
- **Mammoth** (CR 6) - Massive size and devastating trampling power
- **Giant Scorpion** (CR 3) - Deadly poison and armored carapace
- **Killer Whale** (CR 3) - Aquatic dominance
- **Giant Crocodile** (CR 5) - Ambush predator with crushing bite
- **Triceratops** (CR 5) - Armored behemoth
- And many others of CR 6 or lower

**Timeless Body (18th Level):**
The primal magic Romaine wields causes him to age more slowly. For every 20 years that pass, his body ages only 1 year. 

Romaine gained this feature at age 25 (when he reached 18th level). He is now 120 years old chronologically, but physically appears to be in his late 20s or early 30s. He carried Isalia's egg when he was 50 years old (while female due to the fey curse), and has aged only about 5 years physically in the 95 years since gaining Timeless Body. This explains how a 20th-level adventurer can still be active and vital—he's essentially ageless, bound to the natural world's eternal cycles.

**Thousand Forms (17th Level):**
Romaine can cast *alter self* at will, without expending a spell slot. This ability finally freed him from the fey curse—with *alter self* at will, he could maintain consistent control over his form regardless of how the curse tried to reassert itself when he Wild Shaped. This feature represents not just mechanical power, but personal liberation after decades of involuntary transformation.

**Beast Spells (18th Level):**
Romaine can cast many of his druid spells in any shape he assumes using Wild Shape. He can perform the somatic and verbal components of a druid spell while in beast shape, but cannot provide material components. This makes him terrifyingly versatile—imagine a dire wolf casting *call lightning* or a giant eagle summoning *flame strike* from above.

**Archdruid (20th Level):**
Romaine can use Wild Shape an unlimited number of times. Additionally, he can ignore the verbal and somatic components of his druid spells, as well as any material components that lack a cost and aren't consumed by a spell. He gains this benefit in both his normal shape and his beast shape from Wild Shape.

This capstone ability means Romaine can cast spells silently and without gestures—incredibly powerful for stealth casting, casting while restrained, or maintaining concentration on spells while transformed. Combined with unlimited Wild Shape, he has reached the absolute pinnacle of druidic mastery.

**Elemental Wild Shape (18th Level):**
Romaine can expend two uses of Wild Shape to transform into an air, earth, fire, or water elemental instead of a beast. Combined with unlimited Wild Shape uses at 20th level, this means he can become an elemental whenever needed.

**Combat Philosophy:**
Romaine's combat style emphasizes adaptability. He assesses threats and transforms into the form best suited to handle them—taking flight as an eagle to scout or escape, becoming a mammoth to block chokepoints or trample enemies, or shifting into elemental form when facing threats that require magical damage. His high Wisdom ensures his spells remain potent even while transformed, and his ability to heal himself mid-combat by burning spell slots makes him extraordinarily difficult to kill.

**Current Status:**
Romaine rarely Wild Shapes in his role as Duke, preferring to maintain his natural form for diplomatic and administrative work. However, the capability remains instant and unlimited. If the duchy were threatened, he could become a mammoth, elemental, or any other form within seconds—and maintain that form indefinitely if needed.

**Kobold Racial Features:**

**Formation Tactics:**
Romaine has advantage on attack rolls against enemies adjacent to more of his allies than their own. This reflects kobold pack tactics—he's learned to coordinate with allies to overwhelm foes through superior positioning.

**Natural Builders:**
Romaine's proficiency bonus is doubled when operating or constructing complex mechanical devices. This natural aptitude for engineering and construction serves him well in both adventuring (setting traps, disabling mechanisms) and ruling (overseeing infrastructure projects, understanding siege equipment).

**Dragon Blooded (Cold Resistance):**
Despite being a blue kobold (typically associated with lightning), Romaine has resistance to cold damage. This unusual trait may reflect his mother Iaxiandra's draconic versatility or exposure to extreme environments during his adventuring career.

**Sunlight Sensitivity:**
Like most kobolds, Romaine has disadvantage on attack rolls and Perception checks in bright sunlight. As Duke, he often conducts business in shaded chambers or during overcast days. His preference for diplomacy and spellcasting over direct combat partially mitigates this weakness.

**Darkvision (60 feet):**
Romaine sees perfectly in darkness, viewing in shades of gray. Combined with his druidic abilities, he's extraordinarily dangerous in low-light wilderness conditions.

Romaine's adventuring career left him with profound wilderness expertise:

**Excellent Memory for Geography:**
Romaine can always recall the general layout of terrain, settlements, and other features around him. This ability served him during exploration and now aids in governing his duchy—he knows every road, forest, river, and settlement in his domain by memory.

**Natural Provider:**
Romaine can find food and fresh water for himself and up to five other people each day, provided the land offers berries, small game, water, and similar resources. This skill kept his adventuring party alive during long wilderness expeditions and now symbolizes his connection to the land he rules—he knows every spring, every game trail, every edible plant in his territory.

- Falls to the ground in his space
- Merges into his new form (becoming non-functional until he reverts)
- Is worn by the beast form (if practical)

Typically, Romaine allows most equipment to merge with his form for safety, though his magical items remain available when he reverts. His darkwood armor and shield can potentially be worn by larger beast forms, and his belt, boots, bracers, and ring typically merge seamlessly.

- Cast *thorn whip* or other spells as opportunity attacks when enemies flee
- Heal himself by burning spell slots if injured
- Switch to elemental form if needed

All while maintaining concentration on *call lightning* through War Caster's advantage on saves.
