Viktor Steinfeld
================

Minor heir of House Steinfeld. Permanently transformed into a mare via contract with Isalia's Manor. Confirmed pregnant via artificial insemination. Now managing the Steinfeld estate.

appearance: A silver-grey mare, Steinfeld's heraldic colors. Well-built, refined. Shod for road travel. Carries saddlebags on the road; works from a converted stall-office at the estate.

personality: Pragmatic and deliberate. Makes decisions methodically and expects them to be acted on cleanly. Warm with people who earn it — direct without being cold. Owns his choices completely, including the transformation.

background: Viktor had planned the transformation for several years before acting on it. His father's declining health and eventual death became the catalyst — inheriting the estate forced the timeline. He arranged the contract with Isalia's Manor, underwent permanent transformation, and conceived a foal naturally while there — bred by a Meerhold stallion at the estate. Confirmed conception before departing.

Isalia confirmed to Mira von Aldenberg during her intake that Viktor chose his form and position deliberately — wanted to understand what the estate's contractors went through, so he did it himself. Isalia described him as not one for half-measures. This information was the deciding factor in Mira signing her own contract.

The sire of the foal is the larger of the estate's Greymarch pair — a dark bay stallion being sold to House Meerhold. For provenance documentation, a sibling relationship will be established via letter from Isalia's estate.

Viktor returned to the estate on foot with Stablemaster Hannah Voss, conducting tenant farm visits along the road. On arrival, discovered his steward Oswin Brandt had embezzled over fifty gold across three years through a network of fictitious contractors connected to a financier named Aldus Corvel. Viktor has moved quickly — confessions obtained, constable and independent solicitors notified, and Corvel being investigated. The situation has implications extending to House Aldenberg.
