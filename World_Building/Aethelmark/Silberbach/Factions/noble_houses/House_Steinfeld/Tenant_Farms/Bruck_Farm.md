Bruck Farm
==========

Tenant farm on the Steinfeld estate. Approximately one hour south of the main estate on the road toward Silberbach. Stone buildings, well-maintained mortar, flagstone yard. The land his father held before him.

description: Stone walls with recently repointed mortar. Flagstone yard, not mud. A woodpile stacked with the precision of a man who takes satisfaction in small things done correctly. A barn, a kitchen garden, north and near pastures with a sheep flock in good condition — thick winter wool, no obvious stragglers.

An old grey-muzzled dog named Hund spends most of his time in a patch of sun or in front of the hearth.

The farmhouse interior: low-ceilinged, warm. Wide farm door. A table with two chairs and a bench. Shelves with crockery and a few books, spines worn. A coat on a peg by the door, repaired at the shoulder more than once.

Land and Stock:
- Sheep primary — good quality winter flock
- Some grain, kitchen garden
- North pasture: chronic drainage problem, soil compresses, water sits and runs back toward the fence instead of away. Three years deferred. Druss now authorized for repair.

Tenancy:
- Holder: Edric Bruck, sixty-three
- History: forty years, father before him
- Lease payment: on time, always early, never asked for reduction including two bad harvest years

Pending Work:
- North pasture drainage: Druss to assess and complete. Edric Bruck personally recommends Druss based on neighbor's culvert work (held through two wet seasons). Bruck told Viktor: "Tell him Bruck sent you. He'll know the land."

Notes: Bruck is the unofficial voice of the tenant farmers. His read on who and what is worth trusting carries weight with the other tenants. Viktor's visit — walking the pasture, committing to the drainage repair, sitting for midday meal — landed well.

## Active Conditions
- North pasture drainage: Druss authorised, repair pending. Three years deferred. Urgency: short-term — wet season will worsen it.
