# Serya

Born Sergovy Waldheim. Seventh-in-line scion of House Waldheim. Permanently transformed into a female wolf at Isalia's Manor. Registered as an independent client under the name Serya — records sealed, not filed as House business. Goes by Serya. The prior name and identity are known to Isalia and Lyra only.

---

## Appearance

**Pre-transformation:** Wiry, lean, and capable — the kind of build that comes from outdoor work. Unremarkable noble features. Late teens to early twenties.

**Post-transformation:** A tawny-coated female wolf, well-built through the shoulders. Old hound-bite scars visible along the inner thighs — raised tissue that stood out stark against new fur from the moment the change completed. Golden eyes. Still learning the weight of her own claws. Moves with growing confidence as the body settles.

Wears a plain speaking collar, Kreiger-issue. Branded on the left inner thigh — the Kreiger crest simplified to a single elegant line, placed by Dr. Korr on transformation day.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Deliberate.**
Serya left rather than argued, chose a permanent transformation over confrontation, and said *"I won't be coming back as this"* before she'd read a single page of the consent forms. She commits fully once the decision is made.

**Flaw: Avoids confrontation until it's unavoidable.**
Not cowardice — calculated risk management. She will not fight someone she cannot beat. She will not run from something she thinks she can handle. The gap between those two positions is where she sometimes misjudges.

---

## Background

Grew up on the margins of House Waldheim's household. Close enough to court to understand its rhythms, distant enough from succession to be largely ignored. Spent time in the kennels rather than the hall — the handlers accepted her when she was useful, which she was.

Lord Albrecht Waldheim decided Sergovy should enter the priesthood. Sergovy told him he was traveling to Brennmark to reflect. He then went entirely the wrong direction.

Arrived at Isalia's Manor as a job applicant with a thin cover story that dissolved on contact with Isalia's first question. Negotiated intake as an independent client rather than a kennel hand — full female wolf transformation, contract sealed. Transformation completed the following dawn. The prior name and the Waldheim connection are known to Isalia and Lyra and held under client privilege.

The scars on her inner thighs predate the transformation. Bite patterns, practiced — a history that Alric noted clinically and Isalia read clearly.
