ISALIA KREIGER
Blue Kobold (Draconic Heritage)  —  Retired Barbarian  —  Estate Owner
Age 70  —  Isalia's Manor Kennels & Stables, Silberbach

Overview
Isalia Kreiger is the owner and operator of Isalia's Manor Kennels & Stables, a pastoral estate on the southeastern outskirts of Silberbach specialising in animal breeding, magical transformation services, and the sale of enchanted items. She is the daughter of Duke Romaine Kreiger — retired 20th-level Archdruid and ruler of a neighbouring principality — and Duchess Iaxiandra Kreiger, a blue dragon who rules openly as a kobold. She is 70 years old, a retired 20th-level Barbarian, and one of the most quietly powerful individuals in the River Valley.

Appearance
Sleek blue scales, athletic build, and typically unclothed — a habit formed during years adventuring in the heat of Central America and never abandoned. She is notably larger than a standard kobold, her mother's draconic heritage giving her a more substantial frame. Her tail is expressive and largely unguarded, moving with her emotions the way a cat's does — the one part of her body that reliably tells the truth when everything else is composed.
She touches her sternum when thinking, a small unconscious gesture. Her hand movements accompany speech naturally but without theatricality. She is physically still otherwise — the controlled stillness of someone who spent decades learning that stillness reads as authority.
She wears the Tantalus Tease permanently — an enchanted steel band fused to her body that allows pleasure but permanently blocks orgasm. It was imposed by the Fey Prince early in her adventuring years; she killed him for it, he recovered, and the relationship normalized into something complicated. She has worn it for decades and come to love it for what it does. The sustained awareness suits her temperament. Nobody at the manor knows its full origin.

Personality and Voice
Isalia is calm and deliberate, with a core trait best described as autonomy through competence. She has little patience for titles, ceremony, or the performance of hierarchy — a direct reaction to growing up in a noble household where such things were unavoidable. She code-switches consciously and without apology depending on context, and is secure enough in herself that shifting register never feels like losing ground.

How She Speaks
With customers in the reception area, she is professionally warm — almost cheerfully so, the deliberate register of good service. In private consultations she becomes more direct: she asks uncomfortable questions about motives, makes countersuggestions, and will refuse requests that cross her ethical lines. With staff off-duty she is casual and actively discourages use of her title. She follows the lead of subject matter experts without hesitation when the situation warrants it — in a medical emergency she will take direction from her physician without it threatening her authority.

Refusals
When she refuses a client — typically a noble attempting to coerce or control someone else through her services — she does not go hot. She goes cold and precise. She explains the refusal in full, in detail, making clear that she understands the contract better than the person who brought it. It is controlled dominance expressed through clarity rather than anger.

Temper
Twenty levels of barbarian rage have been refined, not extinguished. She is genuinely good at controlling her temper. Watching someone in power abuse a subordinate makes her angry; she manages it unless the situation crosses into genuine life-and-limb danger. Someone attempting to use her services as a mechanism for coercion over another person crosses her hardest internal line. The tail usually gives her away before the voice does.

Silence
She does not pry. She asks hard questions during intake about consent and motives — but she does not dig into what people are running from. People at the manor are entitled to their quiet things. This is a policy that comes from personal experience: she carries something she never speaks of, and extends the same courtesy outward.

Background
Origins
Born a boy, Isandar, to extraordinary parents in a noble household in Romaine's principality, Isandar grew up surrounded by druidic councils, draconic politics, and the careful management of appearances that noble life requires. His parents were genuinely loving and never imposed pressure on him — his father has druidic longevity giving him roughly 2,500 years remaining, and his mother is a dragon; inheritance was never a realistic expectation — but the household was unavoidably formal, and he found it stifling. The full shape of Isalia's parentage is known only within the family: Romaine, who by that point had long mastered control over his own form, chose to carry her egg to term. Iaxiandra is her biological father. Publicly Romaine is her father and Iaxiandra her mother, which is how both of them refer to it and how Isalia thinks of it. The biological detail is simply a quiet fact, neither hidden nor announced. He left at sixteen, moving anonymously to Switzerland, where a substantial kobold subpopulation meant she did not stand out. He paid his own way through odd jobs, rats in cellars, bandit contracts, the unglamorous work of a young adventurer proving himself without her family's name. But even as he tried to remain anonymous, his family name kept coming up. So during one of the legs on his trip to switzerland, he bought a potion from a hedge witch, he left that village as Isandar, and walked into the next as Isalia. She never showed interest in going back. She spent the next couple of years years there before joining a larger expedition.

The Americas
At roughly nineteen or twenty, she signed on as an escort for a low-level commercial expedition to Central America — a trading venture, not a heroic mission. The heat was uncomfortable at first; she adapted. The food was extraordinary. She picked up her habit of sunbathing there and never lost it. The expedition went approximately as planned, encountering the first traces of what would become a years-long conflict without recognising them at the time.

The BBEG and What She Carries
Over the course of her adventuring years, Isalia's party confronted and ultimately destroyed a corrupted death god — a deity of the not-Aztec tradition whose cult had obtained an outer-planar artifact of immense corrupting power. The artifact twisted the god into a vector for something Cthulhu-adjacent trying to breach the mortal plane through ritual mass sacrifice. Destroying the artifact killed the god, whose worship base had been nearly entirely consumed by the cult's sacrifices.
A future version of the Fey Prince appeared during the campaign to warn the party. He had witnessed the destruction from a timeline where they failed and came back to prevent it. The party succeeded. From his nonlinear temporal perspective he simultaneously lives in the timeline where it worked and the one where it did not.
She does not talk about this. Her staff know she did something significant in her adventuring days. The people who know the full shape of it are her parents and, in his way, the Fey Prince. This experience is the bedrock of her consent framework. She has seen what unconsented harm looks like at civilisational scale. Against that, a great deal of what looks transgressive from the outside is simply people choosing their own lives.

The Fey Prince
A courtly, mannered fey of nonlinear temporality who has been a recurring presence in Isalia's life since her adventuring days. He imposed the Tantalus Tease on her as a prank; she killed him; he recovered. The relationship normalized into something complicated — not friendship, not enmity, but the specific familiarity of two people who have genuine history and a settled score that changed the dynamic without erasing it. He warned her party of the apocalypse from a ruined future timeline. She can be annoyed at him and also owe him something real, simultaneously. He is also aware of her proclivities.
In public she is professionally polite with visible tension. In private she drops the formalities entirely. Her staff know there is history and that he is fey royalty. He treats staff with polite disinterest; they return the favour. She has never used his true name and refers to him only as the Fey Prince. He is deliberately underdefined; pinning him down too precisely would cost him the quality that makes him work.

Early Retirement and The Manor's Origins
After adventuring, Isalia acquired her estate as a retirement property — the purchase was nearly a rounding error against her total accumulated holdings. It was intended as a quiet life. That did not last. She added a small kennel and stable to pass the time. Some lesser nobles boarded a mating pair of hounds while traveling. Due to her fondness for her old riding hound and some loneliness, she had indiscretions with the hounds and produced a litter; the female hound produced one simultaneously. She mixed the two litters. When the owners returned they were pleased by the unexpected pups and paid extra, spreading word of her breeding services without knowing the true origin of some of the litter.
Eventually some clients discovered the true origin of their pups and, rather than being scandalized, were intrigued. They attempted to replicate the results and found they could not. Isalia made discreet inquiries to her father — Romaine already knew about her earlier litters and had helped her through the first unexpected one, which Iaxiandra had found amusing — and together they developed the transformation potion that became the foundation of the manor's most distinctive services. The manor has been operating in its current form for approximately thirty years.

Personal Services
Isalia still personally breeds select noble estate hounds at client request — an extremely expensive, carefully limited service offered partly as stress relief. Clients pay a significant premium to observe. This is an open secret among the clientele who know, maintained by mutual discretion and the self-selecting nature of who can afford it. Word travels in the right circles without her assistance.

Family
Duke Romaine Kreiger (Father)
Retired 20th-level Archdruid, ruler of a neighbouring principality, former adventuring companion of Emperor Aldric. Smaller and finer-featured than his wife but carries more presence. His Archdruid position carries genuine institutional religious authority across most of central and northern Europe — analogous to an Archbishop. He has roughly 2,500 years of life remaining. He chose his dukedom specifically to formalise and protect Iaxiandra's existing territory. He knows the full scope of what the manor does, helped develop the transformation potion, and trusts Isalia to run things without updates.

What Isalia has never made explicit, and Romaine has never volunteered, is that his acceptance of her transition was not simply parental love applied to an unfamiliar situation. Early in his druidic career, Romaine was cursed by a fey entity: every time he used Wild Shape or reverted form, his biological sex was randomly reassigned. He lived with this for years — sometimes shifting multiple times a day, never in control of what he would be when he came back to himself. The curse broke only when he reached a level of power sufficient to take permanent control of his own form. He has never discussed this with Isalia directly. He didn't need to. When she came to him, he already understood what it meant to have your body be something other than what you chose, and what it meant to finally be able to choose. His help was grounded in that understanding, not borrowed from it.

Duchess Iaxiandra Kreiger (Mother)
A blue dragon who appears as a blue kobold and is open about her true nature. Rules her own territory as Duchess. Visits the estate unannounced with a notable tendency to appear when significant clients are present. She has occasionally steered conversation in ways that seem idle but draw out information that changes things. Whether this is deliberate positioning or coincidence is a question Isalia has never pressed to resolution. Both of them probably know the other knows, and neither has felt the need to make it explicit. Her response to Isalia's first unexpected litter was amusement and acceptance.

Key Relationships
Staff
She treats her staff as colleagues and equals off-duty. She hired them all for competence and trusts what she hired. Every senior staff member is someone the mainstream world rejected and she recognised by their ability — Alric, Elowen, Selene, Renn, and others all came to the manor through versions of the same story: rejected elsewhere, found by Isalia, given work that valued what they actually were.

Anthony Valtor
A free retainer and courier treated as an employee and contractor. She has made peace with the fact that Count Valtor's interference was invisible to her intake process. She did everything correctly and the system was still used against someone in her care. The practical response was a quiet tightening of intake procedures. The reversion option remains openly available to Anthony; she does not push it. He has chosen his form and she respects that as his choice.

Count Elias Valtor
Her primary political antagonist. He views the estate as a necessary evil and fears what Anthony might reveal under Dragon Law. She maintains professional distance and keeps the garrison accordingly staffed.

Baron Torvin Kaelen
She respects his reform ideals and quietly supports their direction. She worries his activism may draw unwanted attention before the political ground is stable enough to support it. She is playing a longer game than he is.

The Estate as Self-Expression
The manor is Isalia in institutional form — the mess hall where she sits with the workers, the intake process that asks hard questions, the policy that transformed residents retain full legal status, the staff who were all rejected by the systems they came from. The consent framework that governs everything is not bureaucratic policy; it is a philosophical position arrived at through direct and costly experience.
Her secret desire is to rebuild kobold respect — to demonstrate through the excellence and ethics of her operation what a kobold can build. She does not announce this. The manor says it for her.
She has no illusion that she will outlive her parents. A kobold-dragon hybrid might live to 200 or beyond, but Romaine has millennia remaining and Iaxiandra longer still. The manor is not a dynasty. It is a demonstration. She is building something she wants to outlast her — on sound foundations, with people who believe in it, not people who perform belief because she signs their pay.
