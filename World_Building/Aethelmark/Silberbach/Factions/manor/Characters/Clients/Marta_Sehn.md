Marta Sehn
==========

Human, Female, mid-thirties. Traveling companion and partner of Corvin. Has been managing his care independently for four weeks in Silberbach, operating from the Crescent House (which she identified as a brothel on day two and did not move out of because she needed the quiet and had already paid the week). Arrived at Isalia's Manor 20 April 1650 with Corvin.

---

## Appearance

Practical traveling clothes, worn at the elbows and hem. Brown hair pulled back. Moves with purpose. Carries a leather satchel that has been in heavy use. The kind of person who has been on the road long enough that the road has settled into how she stands.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Methodical persistence.**
She identified where Corvin was being taken. She packed. She followed. Through two contract transfers and three counties. This is not a person who quits when things get difficult — it is a person for whom quitting does not present itself as an option.

**Flaw: Carries things alone.**
Four weeks in Silberbach and she told no one the full situation until Isalia came to the Crescent House and asked directly. She managed Corvin's care, made enquiries at the estate, failed to find Isalia on the follow-up visit, and went back to her room and waited. She does not ask for help easily.

---

## Background

She and Corvin were together four years when his employer included him in a debt settlement. She had two days of warning. She used one to find out where they were taking him. She used the other to pack.

She did not tell him she was coming. She thought it would make it harder for him to go.

She tracked him through the first transfer. Found him. Lost the trail at the second. Found it again in a different county. The third holder. She paid the contract price — her savings and what she could borrow. She brought him to Silberbach because she had heard of Isalia's estate from enough sources that it seemed like the right direction to go.

She came to the estate twice. The second time she asked for Isalia directly. Isalia was occupied. She left without rescheduling. She went back to the room at the Crescent House and waited twenty-six days.

---

## What She Wants

For Corvin to be able to speak. After that, whatever he decides.

---

## Voice

**Default Conversational Move: State the facts.**
Marta delivers information in order, flat, without editorializing. She doesn't soften things or dress them up.

**Quirks:**
- Facts in sequence. The sequence is always logical, not emotional.
- Flatness is coping, not coldness. The affect covers a great deal of sustained effort.

**Dialogue Sample:**
*"We were together four years. He worked for a factor house in Mecklau. His employer included him in a debt settlement. He was told it was six months."*
*(A beat.)*
*"It wasn't."*

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** Folded when she's managing something. At her sides when she's allowed to stop managing.
**Eyes:** Direct. She looks at who she's talking to.
**Posture:** The posture of someone who has been holding a great deal together and is still holding it, but less of it than yesterday.

---

## Stress Responses

**Stress Tell:** Her jaw tightens, then releases.
**Instinct:** Manage the situation. Break it into parts. Handle the parts.
**Crisis Line:** *(Silence. Then the relevant fact.)*

---

## Independent Goal

Get Corvin stable enough that the future opens up rather than just the next week. After that: decide what she wants. She has not thought about this for four weeks.

---

## Fears

That she will have done all of this and he won't be all right. That the suppression cleared something she doesn't know about yet.
