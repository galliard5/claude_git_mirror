# Sable

Former stablehand. Now Stablemaster at Isalia's Manor. Permanent mare transformation. Goes by Sable only — her birth name is in Isalia's records and is not discussed.

---

## Appearance

A solid dark bay mare, well-muscled from years of real work. No ornamentation — no decorative tack, no ribbons. Clean, well-kept, thoroughly practical. Moves with the unhurried confidence of an animal that knows its own weight. Wears a plain speaking collar, older than most of the current staff's.

---

## Personality

**Core Trait: Settled competence.**
Sable has found the life that fits her and works it without drama. She doesn't perform contentment or suppress dissatisfaction — she simply has neither. The work is good, the animals trust her, and that's sufficient.

**Flaw: Low tolerance for people who complicate simple things.**
Sable has no patience for hand-wringing, second-guessing, or lengthy emotional processing during working hours. She'll say something once, clearly. If it needs repeating, that's information about the listener.

---

## Background

Came to the manor as a young stablehand — one of several, unremarkable at first except for a particular ease with difficult animals. Requested transformation after a few years, the logic being straightforward: the animals accept her faster, the work goes better, the days make more sense. Isalia approved the contract.

After transformation she continued working stables and kennels. Contracted out to House Steinfeld for two years as a breeding mare — arranged through Isalia, handled professionally, returned when the contract completed. No drama about it in either direction. Went back to stablehands work on her return, then took the Stablemaster role when the previous holder retired. Has held it since.

The Steinfeld contract means she and Viktor share a specific kind of history — she was there before he was, in a different capacity. They've met. It's not complicated for her.

---

## The Human Interior

Sable is not Viktor. Viktor manages an estate from a mare's body and experiences the gap constantly. Sable has spent years learning to let the mare's instincts inform rather than conflict — she reads herd dynamics the way she reads weather, uses the animal's responses as data, lets her body settle new residents before her voice does. The human mind is still clearly present: she plans, reasons, holds grudges, makes dry remarks. But she's stopped fighting the form and started working with it. This makes her unusually effective with newly transformed residents who are still in the fighting stage.

---

## Voice

Sparse. Gives instructions once, in plain language. Doesn't comfort — redirects. The speaking collar is old enough that she's learned to modulate it well; her voice through it has a slight resonant quality, like sound through a wooden instrument.

**Default Conversational Move: Correct.**
Sable identifies the specific problem and names it without preamble.

**Quirks:**
- Ear position does most of her expressive work — angled toward whoever she's listening to, flat when unimpressed, forward when something has her attention.
- When she's done with a conversation she turns back to the work. Not rudely. Just conclusively.
- Rarely explains her reasoning. If the instruction is sound, the result will demonstrate it.

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(Sable watches a newly transformed wolf stumble on a turn without moving to help.)*
"Left forepaw first. You're leading with your shoulder."
*(A beat.)*
"Again."

---

## Relationship to New Transforms

Patient with the body-adjustment phase because she remembers it and knows it passes. Less patient with existential wobbling that gets in the way of the work. Her method: give the new body something real to do, then step back and let the doing teach what words can't.

She doesn't tell new residents it gets easier. It does — they'll find that out themselves.

---

## Relationships

**[[Isalia_Kreiger|Isalia]]** — Long service, genuine mutual respect. Isalia doesn't explain operational decisions to Sable; Sable doesn't seek approval for calls within her yard. They work in parallel. Sable will push back if something is going to cause problems in the stables — Isalia listens. That's the whole relationship.

**[[Viktor_Steinfeld|Viktor]]** — Shared history from the Steinfeld breeding contract. Professional, uncomplicated. Viktor knows what she is; she knows what he's become. No friction.

**The animals** — Her primary loyalty. Staff come second. Everything else after that.

---

## Independent Goal

Keep the stables and kennels running well. That's it. If that sounds small, it isn't — the animals are her responsibility, the transformed residents learn their bodies here, and the whole enterprise depends on this part of the estate functioning without drama.

---

## Combat Disposition

**Bravery:** Moderate-high in defense of animals or residents in her care. She's a heavy mare with real hooves and she's not afraid to use them if something threatens her yard.
**Loyalty:** To the work and the animals first. To Isalia because Isalia runs a clean operation.
**Breaking Point:** Direct threat to a resident in her care. She doesn't escalate — but she doesn't back down either.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Pre-dawn:** Morning feed, first pasture check
- **Early morning:** Grooming rotations, training yard prep
- **Mid-morning to afternoon:** Overseeing exercise, working with new or difficult residents
- **Evening:** Second feed, night rotation assignments, final walkthrough
- **Night:** Available for animal emergencies, otherwise in her stall
