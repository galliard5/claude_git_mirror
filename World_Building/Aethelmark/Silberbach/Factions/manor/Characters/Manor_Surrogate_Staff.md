Manor Surrogate Staff
=====================

Contract role. Not personal staff. Not named in records beyond their function.

---

## Group Composition

Four individuals on rolling surrogate contract at the manor:

- **Two transformed canines** — hound-form, function as wet nurses and surrogate mothers for pups requiring a nursing parent or maternal handler. Trained in raising feral pups, including litters where the mother is absent, incapacitated, or unwilling.
- **Two transformed mares** — equine-form, equivalent role for foals. Available for nursing, bonding, and early care of foals born to mares unable or unwilling to rear.

All four are unnamed in manor records. They are referred to collectively by role. Individual names, if they have them among themselves, are their own business.

---

## Role & Function

The surrogate staff exist to ensure that litters and foals produced at the manor are not lost to absent or incapable mothers. Transformation outcomes sometimes produce mothers who cannot or will not nurse; the kennel and stable programmes require continuity regardless.

Their work is unglamorous and continuous. Pup litters in particular require round-the-clock attention in the first weeks. The canine surrogates handle this without instruction — they have done it before and know the rhythms.

They are not involved in breeding operations. They are not clients. They are not residents in the social sense. They occupy a quiet functional corner of the manor's kennel and stable life and are compensated accordingly.
