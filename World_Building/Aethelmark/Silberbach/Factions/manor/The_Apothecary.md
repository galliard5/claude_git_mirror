The Apothecary
==============

*Commonly called "the store" by staff. No sign above the door — clients who need one haven't been properly referred.*

The public-facing shop of the manor, opening off the Reception Hall through a heavy oak door fitted with a brass handle worn smooth from use. This is where clients move from curiosity to contract — where the ledger opens, the price lists come out, and the decision gets made.

---

## Description

The room is long and narrow, lit by south-facing windows and two iron chandelier frames hung with good beeswax candles. The floor is flagstone, clean and slightly uneven with age. The smell is particular: dried herbs from the ceiling rack, faint residual magic from Elowen's items in the cases, beeswax, and something underneath all of it that is hard to name — the faint mineral sweetness of alchemical compounds that have been brewed in the building next door for years and slowly seeped into the plaster.

Glass-fronted display cases line both walls, their shelves lit by small enchanted stones that give off a steady warm glow. Locks on all of them. The cases are not crammed — items are spaced with room to breathe, each one labeled in Mira's meticulous hand with a card noting name and general purpose. Prices are not on the cards. Prices are in the binder on the front counter, available to any client who asks.

A long oak counter bisects the room roughly at its midpoint. Behind it: shelving, a locked cabinet for items not on public display, a stool for whoever is staffing the counter, and a door through to the administrative offices.

At the room's far end, near the door to the intake corridor, a consultation alcove is separated from the main floor by a low railing and two upholstered chairs. A sample [[Portal_Panties|portal panel]] is mounted on a short display post here — it sits at roughly hip height, its spiral carvings dark and inert without a bonded client, but the craftsmanship is visible. Clients examine it. Some touch it. Staff do not comment unless asked.

## Inventory

Items in the cases are organized loosely by category, though the arrangement shifts when new stock arrives or an item sells.

**Transformation Services** *(consultation required — not sold off-shelf)*
Price list available at the counter. Three variants available: genital-only, partial, and full transformation. No inherent species restriction — any species Alric has studied can be produced. Current convenience stock covers wolfhound, destrier, doe, boar, and vixen in both sexes where applicable; anything outside that is commissioned directly with Alric. The reversion elixir is included in all standard contracts unless permanence is specified and signed for.

**Reversion Elixirs** *(available off-shelf, tiered pricing)*
Stock kept behind the counter, not on display. Alric brews these on a standard rotation. Three grades: standard (covers all estate-produced transformations), partial (targeted system reversion — specialized, requires consultation), and emergency (accelerated dissolution, higher cost, higher physical toll). A client does not need a prior estate transformation to purchase — but Selene will ask questions.

**Enchanted Accessories**
Speech-enchantment collars in several weights and materials — leather, worked steel, engraved brass. Each has an on/off toggle that the *staff* control, not the wearer. Sample collars are in the left-hand case. Restraint hardware (padded cuffs, leads, adjustment rings) shares the same case. Branded sigil kits — for marking, not for burning — are kept in a small cedar box on the counter shelf and shown by request.

**Portal Devices**
The [[Portal_Panties|Portal Panties]] system is the primary offering here. The sample board in the consultation alcove is available to examine. Fittings are not done in the store — clients are moved to the intake rooms once they're ready. Membranes are kept in a locked case near the counter, labeled only by size notation.

**Medical Compounds** *(from Alric's lab)*
Wound salves, recovery compounds, fever reducers, and a small selection of fertility aids. Also stocked: the estate's standard pain management preparations — used during and after procedures. These sit in a small dedicated case near the window, plainly labeled, with a card referencing Dr. Korr for anything requiring clinical guidance.

**Specialty Items** *(Elowen's work and select imports)*
The back half of the right-hand case holds Elowen's finished pieces — items that have come out of commission or been produced as speculative stock. Each one is unique. Elowen prices her own work. The imports sit beside them: items sourced through Isalia's guild contacts in Silberbach and beyond, typically once or twice a year when she makes the trip.

## Staff

**Primary Counter:** [[Kira_Lann]] / [[Liora]]
Kira and Liora cover the reception and store counter interchangeably depending on the shift. Both know the inventory and the binder. Kira talks faster; Liora reads clients more quickly. The result is the same — clients are directed efficiently, inquiries are logged, and the counter runs without gaps.

**Major Consultations:** [[Isalia_Kreiger]]
When a client's inquiry is significant enough — large contract, complex commission, first-time hesitation that needs a steady hand — Kira pulls Isalia. Isalia will take the counter herself, or lead the client directly to an intake room. She does not spend time on the floor for walk-ins who haven't come referred.

**Technical Questions:** [[Alric_Dain]] or [[Elowen_Vale]] by appointment
Neither comes to the floor willingly. If a client needs to speak to the alchemist about a custom transformation compound or to the enchanter about a bespoke item, Kira logs the request and a time is arranged. The craftspeople come to the consultation alcove; they do not receive clients in their workshops.

**Medical Guidance:** [[Dr_Selene_Korr]] by referral
Selene does not staff the store. If a client's questions touch on anatomy, procedure safety, or post-transformation care, Kira flags it and Selene is consulted. She may come to the store for a brief assessment before directing the client to the infirmary.

## Atmosphere

Calm and professional. The store does not announce its purposes from the street and does not need to perform once clients are inside. Staff who work here are accustomed to clients who arrive curious, uncomfortable, aroused, embarrassed, or some layered combination of all four. None of that is commented on.

Clients are given time to look. No one shadows them through the cases or hurries them to the counter. When they're ready, they come to Kira, and the conversation starts from wherever they are.

The consultation alcove at the back exists because some clients need to sit down before they can ask the question they came to ask. The chairs are good ones. Tea is available if the session runs long.

---

## Notes for Staff

- Price binder is updated by Mira at the start of each month. Do not quote prices from memory.
- The locked cabinet behind the counter holds items pending client pickup and anything Elowen has flagged as not yet ready for display. Do not show these without her clearance.
- The sample portal board in the alcove is inert but functional — it will respond to body heat. Clients sometimes discover this accidentally. Handle without comment.
- Intake paperwork starts at the counter before the client moves to the intake rooms. Do not skip this step for walk-ins regardless of their title.
