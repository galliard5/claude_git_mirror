Merchants' Guild
================

Oversees trade and market regulations.

Details: **Role:** Oversees trade and market regulations throughout the region. Maintains standards, resolves disputes between merchants, and represents merchant interests to noble houses.

**Transformed Couriers:**
The Merchants' Guild quietly sponsors transformed individuals as couriers, messengers, and transport specialists. Horses for speed and endurance, wolves for forest routes, birds for long-distance communication — the guild values efficiency above all. These transformed couriers are well-compensated and treated with respect, as their reliability directly impacts guild profits.

**Sponsored Transformations:**
Some couriers came to the guild seeking transformation themselves. The guild will sponsor the transformation procedure at [[Isalias_Manor_Kennels_and_Stables]] in exchange for a term of indentured service — typically five to ten years. The arrangement is voluntary and contractual: the individual chooses their form, serves their term, and then becomes a free agent. Many stay with the guild afterward; others take their skills elsewhere. The guild sees it as an investment in reliable, loyal employees.

**Communication Amulets:**
All guild-sponsored transformed receive a communication amulet integrated into their collar. This allows them to speak normally despite their animal forms, essential for their work as messengers and couriers. The amulet is standard issue — no transformed courier works without one.

**Arrangement with Isalia's Manor:**
The guild maintains a standing contract with [[Isalias_Manor_Kennels_and_Stables]] for courier recruitment and training. Transformed individuals who show aptitude for the work are offered positions with the guild's messenger network. The arrangement is discreet but not secret — everyone knows the guild employs transformed couriers, but the specific terms remain private.

**Notable Practice:**
Guild couriers wear distinctive livery that marks them as official messengers, regardless of their form. This protection extends to them in most territories, though bandits and rival factions are less respectful of guild authority.
