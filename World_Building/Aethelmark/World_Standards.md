# World Standards — Aethelmark

**Purpose:** Single reference for all recurring world standards. When a session uses a day name, month, time of day, or unit of measure, it comes from here. Cross-reference currency to `Aethelmark.md`.

---

## Calendar

### Year Structure

The Aethelmark calendar follows the standard imperial year: twelve months, approximately 365 days, organized into four seasons. The new year begins on 1. Januar.

### Months

| # | Name | English Equivalent | Season |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Januar | January | Winter |
| 2 | Februar | February | Winter |
| 3 | März | March | Late Winter / Early Spring |
| 4 | April | April | Spring |
| 5 | Mai | May | Spring |
| 6 | Juni | June | Early Summer |
| 7 | Juli | July | Summer |
| 8 | August | August | Late Summer |
| 9 | September | September | Early Autumn |
| 10 | Oktober | October | Autumn |
| 11 | November | November | Late Autumn |
| 12 | Dezember | December | Winter |

**In-world usage:** People say *"the fourth of April"* or *"mid-Juli"* in everyday speech. *"Early März"* and *"late Oktober"* are common season markers. Written dates use the format: **DD. Month YYYY** — e.g. *10. April 1650*.

### Seasons & Their Rhythms

**Winter (Dezember–Februar):** River trade slows or halts. Guild halls become social centers. Heavy indoor craft work. Estate supply volume drops slightly — fewer clients travel.

**Spring (März–Mai):** River trade resumes. Planting season. Roads recover from mud season by late April. Client travel picks up. Isalia's busiest intake period begins.

**Summer (Juni–August):** Peak trading season. All roads and river routes active. Religious festivals. Outdoor markets. Estate at full operational capacity.

**Autumn (September–November):** Harvest season. Wool shearing. Farmers Guild busiest. Supply prices shift as stores are built for winter. Guild assembly season — annual accounts settled, contracts renewed.

## Days of the Week

Seven days, beginning Monday. The seven-day week is standard across the empire.

| Name | English Equivalent | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Montag | Monday | Working day. Week begins. |
| Dienstag | Tuesday | Working day. |
| Mittwoch | Wednesday | Mid-week. Major delivery day for the estate. |
| Donnerstag | Thursday | Working day. Second ale delivery day. |
| Freitag | Friday | Market Day in Silberbach. Deliveries cluster around this day. |
| Samstag | Saturday | Half-day for many guild workers. Light operations. |
| Sonntag | Sunday | Rest day. Temple observance. Minimal commerce. Skeleton staff at estate. |

**Estate rhythm note:** The estate does not close on Sonntag — animals require daily care regardless — but non-essential administrative and craft work pauses. Clients do not typically schedule intake on Sonntag. Deliveries do not run.

## Timekeeping

### Primary System — Bells

The primary method of marking time for most of the population is the church bell. The **Old Temple** in Silberbach's Old Town rings canonical hours throughout the day. These bells carry clearly to the estate on calm mornings — the estate is only about 1.5 miles from the Old Town gates.

The estate also has its own **yard bell**, rung by staff to mark meals and shift changes. The yard bell follows the canonical hour schedule approximately, adjusted for practical estate rhythms.

### Canonical Hours (Approximate Clock Equivalents)

| Bell Name | Approximate Time | Estate Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Matutin** (Matins) | ~3am | Deep night. Night watch change. |
| **Laudes** (Lauds) | ~5–6am | Dawn. Sable begins stable rounds. Kitchen fires lit. |
| **Prim** (Prime) | ~6am | Sunrise. Start of the working day. |
| **Terz** (Terce) | ~9am | Mid-morning. Deliveries expected. Alric in lab. |
| **Sext** (Sext) | Noon | Midday. Lunch bell. Mess hall fills. |
| **Non** (None) | ~3pm | Mid-afternoon. Peak activity. Apothecary busy. |
| **Vesper** (Vespers) | ~6pm | Sunset/evening. End of working day. Dinner bell. |
| **Komplet** (Compline) | ~9pm | Night. Estate settles. Night watch begins. |

**In everyday speech:** People say *"just past Terz"* or *"before Vesper"* rather than quoting hours. *"Around Sext"* means approximately noon. *"After Komplet"* means late at night. Precise hour references (*"at the second hour past noon"*) are used by administrators and officials, not in casual conversation.

### The Silberbach Clock Tower

The town square in Silberbach's New Town has a mechanical clock tower, installed within the last generation. It strikes the hour from Prime to Komplet. Its bells are audible throughout the New Town and, in favorable wind conditions, as far as the estate.

The clock tower is considered a mark of civic modernity — Count Valtor had it commissioned. Guild merchants use it for scheduling. It does not replace the canonical hour system; it supplements it. Many residents still think in bell terms rather than numbered hours.

**Practical note:** The clock tower strikes hours only — it does not subdivide the hour. *"Half past Sext"* is still a colloquial expression; the clock just confirms when Sext struck.

## Common Time Expressions

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *A sennight* | Seven days (one week) |
| *A fortnight* | Fourteen days (two weeks) |
| *A quarter-day* | One of the four major calendar markers (März, Juni, September, Dezember openings) — when rents, contracts, and accounts are typically settled |
| *By nightfall* | Before Vesper, approximately |
| *At first light* | Around Laudes, dawn |
| *Before the market* | Before Freitag midday, when Silberbach market is busiest |
| *After the bells* | After the hour strikes — implies promptness |
| *Three days hence* | In three days' time |
| *Toward the end of the month* | Last week of the month, when accounts are reviewed |

## Weights and Measures

### Distance

| Unit | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Inch** | Fine measurements — collar fittings, hardware dimensions, Elowen's enchanting tolerances |
| **Foot** | Room dimensions, short distances, building measurements |
| **Yard** (3 feet) | Field plots, building layouts, training yard descriptions |
| **Mile** (1,760 yards) | Travel distance. A day's walk on good road is roughly 15–20 miles. |
| **Acre** | Land area — farms, pastures, estate grounds |

**In speech:** *"A stone's throw"* (very close), *"a short walk"* (5–10 minutes on foot), *"half a day's ride"* (roughly 20–25 miles by horse at a steady pace). The estate is approximately **1 mile** from Silberbach's Market Square (20 minutes on foot, 10 by horse) and roughly **1.5 miles** from the Old Town gates. Clock tower bells are audible from the estate on calm mornings.

### Weight

| Unit | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Ounce** | Small quantities — spices, medicines, alchemical compounds, precious materials |
| **Pound** (16 oz) | General goods, food, bulk ingredients |
| **Hundredweight** (100 lbs) | Bulk supply loads — grain sacks, hardware orders |
| **Ton** (2,000 lbs) | Heavy bulk deliveries — hay, timber, stone |

### Volume

| Unit | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Pint** | Small liquid measures — medicines, tinctures, precise alchemical quantities |
| **Quart** (2 pints) | Larger portions — kitchen use, oils |
| **Gallon** (4 quarts) | Stable use, bulk liquids, daily kitchen volume |
| **Barrel** (~30–40 gallons) | Ale deliveries, wine casks, bulk storage |

**Estate note:** Ale deliveries are counted in barrels. Alric's ingredient orders use ounces for precision compounds and pounds for bulk herbs. Hay and grain deliveries are counted in hundredweights (sacks) and tons (cartloads).

## Currency Reference

See `Aethelmark.md` for the full currency section. Summary:

**100 Pence (copper) = 10 Dollars (silver) = 1 Crown (gold) = 1/10 Throne (platinum)**

For bulk transfer or large transactions, treasury bars may be used — marked, weighed, and valued at 1,000 times the equivalent coin. A gold treasury bar equals 1,000 Crowns.

Coins: Copper Pence, Silver Dollar, Gold Crown, Platinum Throne.
