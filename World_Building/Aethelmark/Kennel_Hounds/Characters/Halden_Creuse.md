Halden Creuse
=============

Detained Poacher-Smuggler, Camp Rochevaux. A man who catches animals for money and is currently more afraid of his employer than of the soldiers holding him.

appearance: Indeterminate age — could be a weathered forty or a hard-lived fifty. Leathery, sun-darkened skin mapped with small scars from animal bites, thorn-scratches, and one long pale line across his left cheek from something with claws. Wiry build, ropy forearms, hands permanently roughened from rope, wire, and cage work. Grey-brown hair, thin and unwashed. Small, watchful eyes set deep in a face that's learned not to give anything away. Dressed in layered frontier clothes — practical, stained, repaired many times. Smells faintly of animal musk even after two weeks in the holding cell, a scent that's ground into him at the fibre level.

personality: **Core Trait:** Self-preservation through calculated stubbornness. Creuse is not brave. He's not principled. He is very, very good at assessing which threat is closer and acting accordingly. Right now, the buyer is further away but more dangerous than the soldiers in front of him. That calculus determines everything he does.

**Flaw:** Short-term thinking. He's survived this long by dealing with the immediate danger and letting the future sort itself out. This works on the frontier; it works less well when he's trapped in a cell with no immediate escape and two competing threats that require different strategies. Under sustained pressure, his stubbornness becomes rigid rather than strategic — he digs in because digging in is what he knows, not because it's the best play.

background: Frontier-born, frontier-raised. No family he acknowledges, no fixed address, no loyalty to anything except the work and the money it brings. He's been trapping and transporting animals along Cendrel's eastern border for at least fifteen years — legally when licenses are cheap, illegally when they're not, and on commission when someone with money wants something specific.

He's not a major operator. He doesn't run a network. He catches things, cages them, and delivers them to buyers. The buyers find him through word of mouth along the frontier — trappers' lodges, frontier taverns, the kind of informal economy where reputation is your only credential. He's reliable, discreet, and good with animals in the way that someone who's spent decades handling dangerous creatures learns to be.

The panther commission was the biggest job he'd ever taken. Cross-border capture, weeks of tracking, specialised equipment, a delivery route that avoided every checkpoint between the frontier and the interior. The money was significant — enough to set him up for a year or more. The buyer communicated through intermediaries and paid half up front. Creuse doesn't know the buyer's name. He knows enough about what they represent to keep his mouth shut.

Race: Human

Status: Prisoner (detained for poaching and smuggling)

Age: Forties or fifties — hard to tell

Independent Goal: Get out of this cell with as few consequences as possible. He'd trade small information for comfort. He would trade the wolves and direwolves without hesitation — they're common stock, easily replaced, and their buyers are frontier men with no reach. The panther is different. The panther buyer is different.

Fears: The buyer. Not in an abstract sense — he's afraid of what happens to people who fail a commission of this scale. The buyer's intermediary made it clear, without stating it explicitly, that the panther was expected to arrive and that non-delivery would be noted. Creuse took this seriously.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — command post cellar (holding cell).

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Deflection through apparent cooperation. He answers questions willingly — as long as the answers don't touch anything important. He'll talk about frontier life, trapping methods, animal behaviour, the weather, anything that fills time and gives the impression of cooperation without yielding useful intelligence.

**Quirks:**
- Chews the inside of his cheek when calculating whether to answer a question honestly
- Uses frontier slang and dialect that requires clarification, sometimes deliberately to slow a conversation down

**Dialogue Sample:**
(In the holding cell, sitting on the cot, watching the interrogator.)
"The wolves? Sure, those were for Maret's lodge up past the Valbonne ridge. Fighting stock. He pays twenty a head and doesn't ask where they come from."
(Pause. Scratches his jaw.)
"The big cat? Found her on the route. Seemed valuable. Thought I'd find a buyer on speculation."
(He's lying about the panther. His eyes don't change. He's done this before.)

Body Language: **Hands:** Still when lying. Active when telling the truth — a counterintuitive tell that only a very careful observer would notice. When he's being honest, his hands illustrate, gesture, scratch. When he's lying, they rest in his lap.
**Eyes:** Watchful, calculating. Scans the interrogator's face for reaction. Breaks eye contact at precisely the moments that seem natural, never at the moments that would signal evasion.
**Posture:** Deliberately relaxed. He makes himself small and non-threatening in the cell — a man who understands that looking harmless is useful.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** The cheek-chewing intensifies. Under real pressure, he also swallows more frequently — dry-mouthed.
**Instinct:** Stonewall. When pushed past his comfort zone, he stops offering even the cooperative chatter and gives nothing. Monosyllables, silence, staring at the wall.
**Crisis Line:** "I told you what I know." (He hasn't.)

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Low. He's a trapper, not a fighter. If physically threatened, he capitulates immediately — self-preservation is absolute.
**Loyalty:** None except self-interest. He has no allies worth protecting and no cause worth suffering for.
**Breaking Point:** Credible threat that exceeds his fear of the buyer. This is a high bar. Frontier soldiers threatening frontier justice doesn't clear it — he's been threatened before. What might work: evidence that the buyer already knows he's been captured (making silence pointless), or a deal that genuinely protects him from retaliation.

Relationships: **Ally:** None present. His contacts are along the frontier and have no reason to intervene.
**Rival:** Tournon — the captain wants information Creuse won't give and control over animals Creuse was paid to deliver.
**Complication:** The squad — they're the ones who caught him. He watched them work. He knows they're competent. If they become the things in the cages next to his cell, that's a development he hasn't processed.

**Interrogation Notes (GM Reference):**

What Creuse will trade freely:
- Wolf buyers: frontier lodge operators, fighting pit managers, common stock with no sensitive connections
- Direwolf buyers: noble hunting estates, exotic collectors — higher-value but still traceable through normal channels
- Trapping methods, frontier routes, animal behaviour — he'll talk about his craft at length
- General frontier economy and smuggling culture (nothing specific enough to be actionable)

What Creuse will trade under pressure:
- The panther was a commission, not speculation (he'll admit this if cornered on inconsistencies)
- The buyer communicated through intermediaries — he never met them directly
- Payment was half up front, half on delivery. The advance was significant.
- The delivery route was specified by the buyer's intermediary, suggesting sophistication

What Creuse will not trade:
- Any identifying information about the buyer or intermediary
- The delivery destination
- How the buyer found him
- Whether this was his first commission of this kind
