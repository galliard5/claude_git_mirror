Perrin Aldec
============

Human male, thirty. Five years in the Maruvec watch, the most experienced member of the squad by service length. Assigned to the hound programme three weeks ago. He's been professional and competent and said almost nothing about how he feels about it, which is what he does when he's scared.

---

Appearance
----------

Medium height, medium build, the kind of face that's pleasant without being memorable. Brown hair, thinning at the temples earlier than he'd like. He looks older than thirty in some lights — a little worn, a little tired, the face of a man carrying something. His uniform is always in order and he stands the way experienced watchmen stand, weight distributed evenly, ready without being tense.

When he's not in uniform, the tiredness is more visible. He keeps it contained during working hours.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Reliable professionalism as a load-bearing structure. He's good at his job. He's reliable. He shows up, he does the work, he doesn't cause problems. This is partly character and partly because having something reliable to be is the only way he knows how to manage things when he's not fine.

He's not fine. He has a wife named Maret and a son named Luc who is eight years old, and he has been staring at the arithmetic of a permanent transformation for three weeks without sleeping particularly well. He's been in the watch long enough to have seen men not come home. He's been a father long enough to have been thinking about what Luc would make of his father becoming permanently something else.

He hasn't told Maret everything. He told her there was a new assignment, special programme, he couldn't say much about it yet. She's sharper than that and she knows something's wrong, and they've been having the conversation where she keeps the door open and he keeps finding reasons not to walk through it.

**Flaw:** He's trying to be fine. He looks fine. He's good enough at this that it's going to take a while for anyone to notice he isn't. He does it because he's been the responsible one — the dependable one — for long enough that he doesn't know how to perform any other version of himself without it feeling like a failure.

---

Background
----------

Five years in the Maruvec watch. Before that, two years as a dockside cargo inspector, which is how he knows the Harbour District well enough to be useful on dock patrol. He passed the watch exam on his second attempt at twenty-five, got his permanent posting, married Maret six months later, Luc arrived fourteen months after that.

He joined the watch because it was steady. It was the thing he could count on — regular hours, regular pay, the kind of structure you can build a family around. He's been reliable for five years and it's worked.

The assignment has disrupted something fundamental in the structure he's built, and he doesn't know how to articulate that without it sounding like he's not committed to his duty. So he hasn't articulated it. He's waiting for an angle that doesn't make him sound like he's complaining, and in the meantime he's doing the work.

---

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Responds to things he's not comfortable with by talking about logistics. If you ask him how he feels about the transformation, he tells you where they're billeted during the pre-transformation period. He shifts to the concrete when the abstract is too much.
- Ends sentences with "so" when he's run out of road but doesn't want to stop talking. "The kennel is well set up, Varre clearly knows what she's doing, so." The word hangs there.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Perrin is cleaning his kit. He does this when he doesn't know what else to do with his hands.)
"Luc asked me what the new assignment was."
(He runs the cloth along the baton without looking up.)
"I told him it was a special unit. Tracking work, mostly."
(Pause.)
"He's eight. He wanted to know if I'd get a dog."
(He sets the baton down.)
"I didn't know what to say to that, so."

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Occupied. He keeps his hands busy — cleaning kit, checking equipment, small maintenance tasks. When he's standing without a task, his hands settle at his belt and stay too deliberately still.
- **Eyes:** Steady and warm as default. When something lands wrong, he looks slightly away, slightly down — a fraction of a second before he recovers.
- **Posture:** Watchman's default — grounded, ready. Drops very slightly in quiet moments when he thinks no one's watching.
- **Micro-behavior:** Moves his wedding ring around his finger when he's thinking about home.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

To handle this in a way that Maret and Luc can live with. He doesn't know what that looks like yet. He's trying to figure out how to be the version of himself that comes home to them, whatever form he's in by the time that happens.

---

Fears
-----

The transformation making him into something Luc is afraid of. He knows that's not how it works — the transformation doesn't change who you are, the documentation says. He believes the documentation ninety percent. The other ten percent wakes him up at three in the morning.

Maret finding out he hasn't told her everything, and what that does to the thing they've built.
