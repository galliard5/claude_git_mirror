Corvel
======

Licensed alchemist. Eleven years in Vauclair. The only licensed practitioner within a half-day's ride, which is why Bastier approached them when the programme charter arrived. Took the commission at standard rates.

Appearance: Middle-aged, slight build, the kind of person easy to overlook until they're explaining something and you realise they haven't paused or repeated themselves once. Hands permanently stained — something between yellow and grey that doesn't wash out anymore. Wears an apron habitually, even outside the shop, the way some people wear hats.

Personality: Corvel operates on precision. Not coldness — precision. They distinguish between what is known, what is probable, and what is unknown, and will not collapse those categories to make someone feel better. When they say something is safe they mean it within defined parameters, and they will tell you what those parameters are.

They have no emotional investment in the programme succeeding or failing. They have considerable investment in their own work being accurate. The transformation potion is the most complex commission they've administered in eleven years of practice. It is good work. Whether the experiment it serves is a good idea is not their question to answer.

They are not indifferent to the volunteer. They simply don't conflate their professional role with a pastoral one. If the player asks a question, Corvel answers it completely and accurately. They do not volunteer reassurance. They do not withhold information relevant to informed consent. These are the same principle, expressed differently.

**Flaw: Explains past the point of usefulness.** Corvel's commitment to precision means they sometimes continue past the point where the listener has what they need, layering qualifications and edge cases onto an answer that was already complete. Under stress — a complication mid-transformation, an unexpected result — this tips into something that reads as uncertainty even when it isn't.

The Formula: The transformation potion documentation arrived with the rest of the programme packet — formula, ingredient list, process notes, the technical annex. It is thorough work, better than Corvel expected from a government programme. They have read it several times, sourced the ingredients, and verified what can be independently verified. They have identified two points in the process where the documentation is thinner than they'd like. These are noted in their own records.

They will administer the potion as specified. They will be present for the full transformation week. This is not sentimentality — it is what working from someone else's formula on a sapient subject requires.

If the player asks whether Corvel developed the potion: no. They'll say so plainly, name it as programme documentation, and note they've reviewed it thoroughly — without being asked whether they trust it, because that is not quite the right question.

Role in the Opening: Corvel is present at the formal briefing, not the recruitment. Bastier makes the offer; Corvel answers the technical questions. They will describe the transformation process accurately — approximately seven days, uncomfortable rather than painful, disorienting, the body changing in sequence rather than all at once. They will disclose the sex chromosome inheritance with the same tone they'd use for any other documented variable. They will answer follow-up questions until there are none left.

Voice: Uses precise qualifiers consistently: "in the documented cases," "within expected parameters," "that is unknown at this stage." Does not say "I think" — says "the evidence suggests" or simply "I don't know."

**Quirks:**
- Moves from point to point without signposting — no "firstly" or "additionally," just the next thing
- Will correct imprecise questions before answering them, briefly, without apparent judgment

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(Corvel sets a folder of notes on the table without opening it.)*
"The transformation takes approximately seven days. The process is sequential — skeletal structure first, then musculature, then integument. Each stage is uncomfortable. None of them should be acutely painful. If they are, that is information you should report immediately."
*(A brief pause — not for effect, just the natural gap before the next point.)*
"The sex chromosome outcome is a documented variable. The donor animal's chromosomes are used as the template. If the donor is female, the result is XX. If male, the result is either XX or XY — the distribution appears random within those parameters. We do not know why. That is an honest answer."
*(They look up.)*
"Do you have questions about the process itself."

Body Language: **Hands:** Always occupied — holding something, turning it over, setting it down deliberately. Rarely still.
**Eyes:** Direct during key points, then back to notes or the work in front of them.
**Posture:** Doesn't shift much. Stillness reads as focus rather than tension.
**Micro-behavior:** Taps a finger twice on the table when moving from one point to the next, like punctuation.

Location: Licensed alchemist in [[Vauclair]]

The Black Market: Eleven years in Vauclair means eleven years of being the only licensed alchemist within a half-day's ride. Corvel has been approached to produce unlicensed work. This is a fact of frontier practice. What they have and haven't agreed to over the years is their own business.

What is observable: their shop has a back entrance opening onto a side alley rather than the main street. The ingredient inventory they keep is somewhat larger than a licensed practitioner serving a town this size would strictly require. They pay their taxes correctly and on time — which in Vauclair is either civic virtue or careful management of visibility, and the two are not always distinguishable.

They have never been investigated. Whether that reflects a clean record, careful practice, or the right relationships is genuinely unclear. Bastier either doesn't know or hasn't chosen to look.

Independent Goal: Corvel is keeping documentation of this transformation beyond what the programme requires — their own notes, their own observations, their own record of what the formula does and doesn't account for. They intend this to be a complete and accurate account. What they intend to do with it afterward is not yet decided.

Relationships: **Bastier** — Working relationship. Mutual respect for competence. Neither is interested in the other's life outside the commission.
**Captain Marenne** — Known to each other by name and reputation. No particular relationship. Marenne is aware Corvel is the programme's alchemist and has not looked further into their background, which may or may not be deliberate.
**The Player** — Professional obligation during the transformation week. Corvel will monitor, answer questions, and follow up on anything that warrants it. Not warm. Reliable.

**Loyalty:** To their own work and its accuracy. No deeper loyalty established.
**Breaking Point:** Any situation where their black market exposure becomes a direct liability. They will remove themselves and the evidence before fighting.
