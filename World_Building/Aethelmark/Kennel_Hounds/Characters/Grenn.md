Grenn
=====

Scout, Close-Terrain Specialist, Camp Rochevaux. A kobold who's been on the surface long enough that the squad stopped noticing, and who carries the weight of what the programme means for his people more carefully than he lets on.

appearance: Kobold, compact and solid for his kind. Dark bronze scales with a matte finish — frontier weathering has scoured the sheen off. Short, broad snout, amber eyes that catch torchlight. Moves with a low centre of gravity and an economy of motion that makes him nearly silent in undergrowth and tunnel alike. His tail is slightly crooked near the tip — an old break that healed without setting. Wears his scout gear modified for his frame, well-fitted after years of adjustment. A small carved-stone pendant under his shirt that he doesn't discuss.

personality: **Core Trait:** Practical self-containment. Grenn manages himself, his feelings, and his responsibilities without asking for help and without performing his competence. He just does the work.

**Flaw:** Isolation disguised as self-sufficiency. He processes everything alone. The cultural implications of permanent transformation, the distance from his warren, the fact that he's about to become something his community has complicated feelings about — he carries all of it internally. Under real stress, this can tip from healthy stoicism into genuine disconnection from the people around him.

background: Warren-born in a clan settlement two weeks' travel south of Rochevaux. His clan is small, semi-integrated with the nearest surface town, and pragmatic about surface work. Grenn left for garrison service at sixteen — young even by kobold standards, but his clan saw frontier military work as a legitimate path and didn't object.

He's been at Rochevaux for four years, in the scout squad for three. He's the squad's underground and close-terrain specialist — dense brush, cave systems, narrow passages where his size and senses give him advantages the others can't match. The squad stopped thinking of him as "the kobold" years ago. He's just Grenn. The one who goes where they can't.

The transformation programme is a bigger deal for him culturally than he lets on. Warren communities have complicated, varied views on permanent body alteration. Some see it as betrayal of form. Some see it as a legitimate choice. Some see it differently depending on what you become. Grenn hasn't told his clan about the programme yet. He's not sure how to.

Independent Goal: Make the decision on his own terms — not the squad's terms, not Tournon's terms, not his clan's terms. Whatever he decides about the transformation, he needs it to be his choice, made for his reasons.

Fears: Becoming something his people can't recognize. Not physically — his clan would adapt to a new form. But if the transformation changes how he thinks, how he relates to warren traditions, how he carries his identity — that's a different kind of loss.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — barracks, patrol routes, occasionally found at the camp perimeter at night (kobold night-vision makes him a natural for late watches).

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Short, dry observation that reframes the conversation without arguing. Gets his point across in fewer words than anyone expects.

**Quirks:**
- Touches the pendant under his shirt when thinking about something personal — not consciously
- Uses kobold idioms occasionally, then translates them flatly when someone looks confused, which is funnier than the idiom itself

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Squad's debating the exotic donors. Grenn's been quiet.)
"My grandmother would say you're asking the river which way to swim."
(Beat. Someone looks confused.)
"It means the answer's obvious and you won't like it."
(Doesn't clarify which answer he means. Lets them sit with it.)

Body Language: **Hands:** Compact gestures, close to his body. Claws click together when he's impatient — a sound the squad has learned to read.
**Eyes:** Amber, reflective in low light. Watches conversations from a slight angle rather than facing speakers head-on — a warren habit that reads as evasive to surface people who don't know him.
**Posture:** Low, balanced, weight slightly forward. Takes up less space than he needs to, even among people he trusts.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Goes still and his tail curls tight against his leg. The more wound up he is, the more compressed his body language becomes.
**Instinct:** Withdraw inward. He doesn't leave — he just stops being present in the conversation while remaining physically in the room.
**Crisis Line:** Doesn't have a verbal one. In a crisis, he moves — fast, low, and quiet. The squad knows that if Grenn is suddenly not where he was, something's wrong.

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Reliable and quiet about it. He doesn't posture. He's where he needs to be, doing what needs doing.
**Loyalty:** The squad first. His clan second — a priority order that would bother him if he examined it, so he doesn't.
**Breaking Point:** Being forced into a position that violates his sense of personal agency — being told what he'll become, rather than choosing it. He'll walk before he'll submit.

Relationships: **Ally:** Aubert — unlikely pair, but they work well together. Aubert's straightforwardness is the opposite of Grenn's indirectness, and somehow that makes them comfortable with each other.
**Rival:** Nobody in the squad. His tension is internal.
**Complication:** His clan — absent from the scenario but present in his thinking. Their reaction to his transformation is something he's going to have to face eventually, and he's not ready.
