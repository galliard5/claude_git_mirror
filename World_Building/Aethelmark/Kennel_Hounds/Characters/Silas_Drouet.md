Silas Drouet
============

Garrison Commander, Maruvec. Crown-appointed. Responsible for approximately three hundred garrison personnel, maritime law enforcement cooperation, and civic order in a port city where the second economy is smuggling and the first economy needs the second to function. He's been in this posting for six years and has learned roughly how much pressure the city can absorb before something breaks and how to stay just below that line.

---

Appearance
----------

Fifties. Heavy build going to stocky rather than fat — a man who was physically imposing in younger years and still carries the frame, just padded out by desk work and good garrison food. Grey, close-cropped. A lined face that reads as stern at rest, not because he's actually stern but because the years left those marks. Always in uniform during working hours. Immaculate uniform, because he's the commander and someone will notice if he isn't.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Administrative pragmatism. He runs the garrison the way a competent logistics officer runs a supply chain — he identifies what the operation needs, determines the most efficient path to provide it, and executes. He is not a visionary. He doesn't particularly want to be. Visions require advocacy and advocacy requires political capital and political capital is finite.

He assigned the hound programme squad because the programme charter required him to produce volunteers and the voluntary recruitment process was going to take the better part of a year. He has three officers from the central office asking polite follow-up questions. He assigned a squad, selected for range of experience, and told them what they'd been assigned to. He doesn't regret this. He thinks the results will justify the method if the programme works and be a footnote nobody cares about if it doesn't.

He is watching to see if it works.

**Flaw:** He treats people as operational assets more readily than as individuals, and he does this without malice or awareness. The squad was selected by profile — years of service, reported competence, no outstanding disciplinary issues — not by anything resembling suitability for a permanent and physically transformative assignment. He would be surprised to learn that this gap matters. He would, if pressed, acknowledge that perhaps it should have been considered.

---

Background
----------

Thirty years in Crown service, the last twenty in garrison command roles of progressively larger postings. Maruvec is his most significant command by any measure — size, budget, political sensitivity, trade importance. He is good at his job in the ways a garrison commander needs to be good at it: personnel management, budget negotiation, the diplomatic maintenance of working relationships with the town council, the maritime guilds, and the regional administrator's office.

He has a working relationship with the city's shadow economy that he would describe as "strategic tolerance" and that the people who benefit from it would describe, privately, as an agreement. Nothing is written down. Nothing will be.

The hound programme arrived in his in-tray as a mandate from above with a budget attached and a timeline that was already behind. He treated it the way he treats most things that arrive from above: read it fully, assess what it actually requires versus what it says it requires, find the most efficient path to compliance, execute.

---

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Speaks in the cadence of someone dictating for a secretary — clear, structured, economical. He says what he means in order.
- Refers to personnel by rank and surname in formal contexts. Uses first names in the very small number of relationships he considers personal.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Drouet looks up from his desk when the door opens. He doesn't stand.)
"Quickclaw. Sit."
(He finishes a note in his ledger before looking up fully.)
"I've read Varre's preliminary assessment. She has concerns about squad cohesion. I want to know if you share them."
(He waits. He's not asking to be polite — he actually wants to know.)

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Folded on his desk when sitting, clasped behind his back when standing. Deliberate, controlled.
- **Eyes:** The direct, slightly assessing look of someone who makes personnel decisions. He's evaluating even in casual conversation.
- **Posture:** Commander's posture — spine straight, weight settled. He occupies space the way people who have held authority for a long time do.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

He wants the programme to produce one concrete, demonstrable result in the first operational year that he can put in a report to the regional administrator. One successful investigation, one significant interdiction, one thing that justifies the budget line. After that, the programme can succeed or fail on its own merits without reflecting on his command decisions.

---

Fears
-----

A public failure that gives the maritime guilds ammunition in their ongoing negotiation over harbour fee structures. The guilds have been looking for leverage for three years. A sapient hound programme that visibly fails would be exactly the kind of thing they'd attach to an argument about garrison competence.

---

Location
--------

Administrative offices, eastern wing of the garrison compound. Keeps regular hours, takes meetings in the morning, does his desk work in the afternoon.

Relationships
-------------

**Colette Varre** — Hired her, respects her competence, has been slightly surprised by how specifically she pushes back on things that weren't done the way she'd have done them. He's decided this is a net positive.
**The Squad** — Three weeks acquainted, no direct contact yet. He gets reports through Varre.
**Regional Administrator** — Political maintenance relationship. Cordial, transactional, watched.
**The Maritime Guilds** — Complicated working relationship that he manages carefully.
