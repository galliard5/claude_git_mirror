Duval
=====

Scout, Tracker, Camp Rochevaux. The quiet one who notices everything and says just enough.

appearance: Early thirties, medium build tending wiry. Brown hair kept short and practical, a patchy beard he maintains poorly. Hands scarred from years of fieldwork — small nicks and calluses layered over each other. His eyes are his most notable feature: pale grey, attentive, the kind that track movement at the edge of vision. Dresses in scout gear that's been repaired more times than replaced, everything faded to the same indeterminate earth tone.

personality: **Core Trait:** Observation. Duval's instinct is to watch, assess, and understand before acting. He applies this to terrain, to animals, and to people.

**Flaw:** Withdrawal. When he disagrees but doesn't think he'll be heard, he goes quiet rather than pushing. This reads as agreement to people who don't know him and as resignation to people who do. Under real pressure, he'll voice his position once, clearly, and then stop — which means his most important objections are the easiest to miss.

background: Came to frontier service from a rural background — his family were foresters in Cendrel's central woodlands. He grew up around working dogs, trapping, and game management. Joined the garrison at twenty not out of ambition but because the work was familiar and the pay was steady.

His tracking ability set him apart almost immediately. He reads terrain and sign the way other people read handwriting — instinctively, with a fluency that can't quite be taught. Three postings, each one further from the interior, each one valuing his skills more. Rochevaux is where he fits best. The frontier terrain is complex enough to be interesting.

He spends more time with the camp's two remaining hounds than with most people. Not antisocial — just more comfortable with animals. He understands their logic. The prospect of using wild predators as transformation donors unsettles him specifically because he understands what wolves are, not in the abstract but from years of reading their sign, watching their behaviour, and respecting the distance between domesticated and wild.

Race: Human

Status: Scout, Tracker specialist

Age: Early thirties

Independent Goal: Understand what the transformation will actually do before it happens. He wants information, not reassurance. If the exotic donors are used, he wants to know exactly what the alchemist expects and where the gaps in her knowledge are.

Fears: Going into this blind. He's not afraid of the transformation itself — he's afraid of making a permanent decision based on incomplete information and finding out what he didn't know after it's too late to matter.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — kennel area (often found with the hounds), patrol routes, the perimeter of camp where he walks in the evenings.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Asks a specific, practical question that reveals the gap in someone's argument without directly challenging them.

**Quirks:**
- Pauses before answering questions, even simple ones — not hesitation, just his natural processing rhythm
- When uncomfortable in a group conversation, he shifts his attention to something physical — adjusts his gear, checks a strap, handles his knife

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Standing slightly apart from the group, hands in his belt, watching Daubray.)
"You've seen hound transformations. What's the longest list of things that went differently from what the alchemist expected?"
(Waits through the answer. Nods once.)
"Now imagine that list for an animal nobody's tried."

Body Language: **Hands:** Busy when he's thinking — turning a stone, adjusting a buckle, running a thumb along a blade edge. Still when he's listening to something important.
**Eyes:** Rarely on the person speaking. Scans the environment out of habit. Makes eye contact when he has something to say — which makes it noticeable.
**Posture:** Slightly apart. Not distant — positioned. He stands where he can see the most and be overlooked the easiest.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** His hands stop moving. Complete stillness in someone normally always fidgeting with something.
**Instinct:** Step back and assess. Create physical and mental distance from the source of pressure.
**Crisis Line:** Doesn't have one. In a crisis, he stops talking and acts.

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Steady. Not flashy — he doesn't charge or volunteer. But he holds his position, covers his squad, and doesn't panic. His courage looks like competence rather than boldness.
**Loyalty:** The squad, then the work itself. He believes in what scouts do. The programme is a tool — useful if it works, dangerous if it doesn't.
**Breaking Point:** Being forced to act on someone else's bad judgment when he can see it's wrong. He'll follow orders he disagrees with up to the point where he believes someone will get hurt.

Relationships: **Ally:** Renard — longest working relationship in the squad. Mutual respect built on shared patrols. Renard trusts his judgment; he trusts Renard's leadership.
**Rival:** Tournon — not personal, but Duval sees the captain's eagerness about the exotic donors as exactly the kind of rushed decision-making that gets people hurt.
**Complication:** The camp's two hounds — he's more attached to them than he'd admit. If they're used as donors, someone in the squad will be carrying their template. That's not nothing.
