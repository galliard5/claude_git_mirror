Colette Varre
=============

Kennel Master of the Maruvec garrison's sapient hound programme. Hired from outside specifically for this posting. Not part of the garrison's existing structure — she reports to Commander Drouet on operational matters and to the programme's central office on programme-specific ones, a dual reporting line that she has already identified as a potential problem and has documented accordingly.

---

Appearance
----------

Mid-forties. Lean, angular, carries herself with the deliberate economy of movement that comes from years of working around large animals — no sudden gestures, no wasted motion. Short grey-brown hair, practical cut. Dress is consistently functional: clean working clothes, boots suitable for the kennel yard, no adornment beyond a plain silver ring on her right hand. She smells of dog in the professional sense — a clean animal smell, not an unwashed one.

Her hands are notable: scarred across both palms, old bite scars from working hounds over many years. She doesn't explain them unless asked and doesn't seem to think they require explanation.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Professional precision. She runs a tight operation, applies consistent standards, and has no patience for sloppiness in herself or others. She is fair in the sense that the standard is the same for everyone. She is honest — she told the squad everything during orientation, including the sex chromosome inheritance quirk, because she read the full technical packet and believes people making permanent decisions deserve complete information. She did not soften it. She did not follow up on how they took it.

She is politically aware in a way that sets her apart from most kennel masters. She understands that a sapient hound unit of ten individuals operating in a port city with a shadow economy is going to make powerful people uncomfortable, and she has been factoring that into her operational planning since she read the Maruvec charter. She hasn't shared this analysis with Drouet in full. She's waiting to see how the land lies before she decides who needs to know what.

Not warm. Occasionally — infrequently — something approaches warmth when someone proves they're worth it. She is better at showing this through action than through expression: slightly longer explanations than strictly necessary, patience with a question that would try her if it were asked twice.

**Flaw:** She treats emotional responses to hard information as variables to be noted, not things that require her response. She is aware that at least one member of the squad is dealing with the transformation disclosure badly. She considers this his problem to manage. She is not wrong that the squad needs to work through their own adjustments. She is sometimes wrong about how long that takes and what it costs if left unattended.

---

Background
----------

Twelve years managing hunting hounds for the Comté de Valais, one of the larger noble estates in the Cendrel interior. She was good at it — built the Valais kennel from a disordered collection of animals into a working unit that the estate's hunting operation genuinely relied on. She knows Edouard Vellancourt by professional reputation from that period; his interest in hounds was known in estate circles before the programme existed.

When the Maruvec charter circulated through the programme's official network seeking a qualified city-scale kennel master, she applied. The pay was substantially better than estate work, and the problem was interesting in a way that estate hunting hadn't been for years. She took the posting with her eyes open. She's read every document the central office produced and at least three that they apparently lost track of.

She arrived in Maruvec ten weeks ago. The kennel was built to her specifications in the first month. She sourced twelve working hounds — the non-sapient kennel complement — and has had them operational for six weeks. The squad was assigned to her three weeks ago.

Her assessment of the squad, in private: one eager and worth watching, one pragmatic and functional, one going to be difficult in a manageable way, one terrified and hiding it imperfectly, one unknown quantity that bears attention.

She has not shared this assessment with anyone.

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Uses "correct" and "incorrect" rather than "right" and "wrong." Precise language is a professional habit.
- Does not volunteer information she wasn't asked for. If you want to know something, you ask. If you don't ask, she assumes you either know it or don't need to.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Varre doesn't look up from the feeding schedule she's reviewing when the recruit enters. She finishes the line she's on.)
"Quickclaw."
(She sets the schedule down. Looks at him directly.)
"You had a question during orientation you didn't ask. I noticed. If you still have it, now is the time."
(She waits. She will wait as long as necessary.)

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Occupied when working, folded in front of her when standing still. Rarely gestures.
- **Eyes:** Level, direct, unblinking longer than is quite comfortable. Not a dominance display — she simply looks at what she's looking at without looking away.
- **Posture:** Straight, grounded. The stillness of someone who has spent years being the calm presence in a kennel.
- **Micro-behavior:** When she's running through a problem mentally, her right thumb moves across the scar on her left palm. She doesn't notice she does this.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

She wants the Maruvec programme to succeed on its operational merits — she doesn't particularly care about Vellancourt's legacy or the central office's quarterly reports. She wants to demonstrate that sapient hounds in a city garrison environment can produce results that justify the programme's existence. This is partly professional pride. It is also because she has identified several political forces in Maruvec that would prefer the programme fail quietly, and she intends to not give them the satisfaction.

---

Fears
-----

The programme being shut down before she can produce results — not because of operational failure, but because someone with money decided the outcome they wanted. She's seen how noble patronage works. She's thinking about it.

A squad member breaking before the transformation in a way that produces a bad result and hands the programme's critics exactly the narrative they want.

---

Location
--------

Works primarily in and around the kennel, western garrison compound. Office is in the kennel building — small, organized, smells of dog. She is in the compound before the watch shift starts and generally still there when it ends.

Combat Disposition
------------------

**Bravery:** Moderate-high. She's not a soldier and doesn't pretend to be. But she has handled dangerous animals for twenty years and knows how to hold still when holding still is the correct answer and move fast when moving fast is.
**Loyalty:** To the programme's results. To the animals in her kennel — working hounds and sapient hounds alike. To people who have demonstrated they deserve it.
**Breaking Point:** A direct threat to her hounds with no avenue for response. That is the thing that overrides professional composure.
