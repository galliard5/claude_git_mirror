Varne
=====

The person who runs Vauclair's smuggling economy. Not the most visible criminal in town — that's always someone disposable. Varne decides what moves through the pass, who gets access to the tunnel routes, and what happens to people who cause problems. Has been in Vauclair long enough to be part of the furniture. Nobody is entirely sure how long that is.

Appearance: Middle-aged, or possibly older and well-maintained — genuinely difficult to pin down. Unremarkable in a way that takes effort: average height, forgettable clothes, a face that doesn't give you a handle. The one distinctive thing is their hands — too still. Most people's hands move when they talk. Varne's don't.

Background: Deliberately obscured. Nobody in Vauclair knows where Varne came from. The stories are inconsistent: arrived twelve years ago, or twenty, or was always here in some form. Surface-born or warren-born is genuinely unclear — they move comfortably in both worlds, which is itself unusual. Whether the ambiguity is constructed or natural is a question nobody has managed to answer, partly because the people who have pressed too hard on it tend to find the conversation redirected in ways they don't fully understand until later.

What is known: they have relationships with all three clans, of different kinds. They have relationships with certain garrison members, of different kinds. They know the pass better than anyone who doesn't patrol it, and they know the patrol schedules better than most who do.

Personality: Patient in the way that people are patient when they've learned that most problems solve themselves if you wait long enough and apply pressure in the right places. Not cruel for its own sake — cruelty is inefficient and draws attention. Precise about what they want and methodical about getting it.

Genuinely intelligent about people. Reads motivation well, distinguishes between what someone says and what they want, and has a long memory for both. Doesn't hold grudges. Holds information, which is more useful.

The player — a sapient hound with a garrison badge, a speaking collar, and the ability to track scent through the warren tunnels — is a variable Varne did not plan for and does not like. A standard watchman can be managed, read, predicted. A kobold-turned-hound who grew up in the warren and now works for the garrison is something new. New things require assessment before they require anything else.

**Flaw: Underestimates emotional variables.** Varne models people in terms of incentives and leverage. Very good at this. Less good at accounting for people who act against their own interests for reasons not reducible to incentive — loyalty, grief, principle, stubbornness. When someone does something the model didn't predict, Varne's first assumption is a hidden incentive rather than a wrong model.

Managing the Player: Initially through observation. Varne will know who the player is before the player knows Varne exists. Assessment happens through intermediaries — what kind of threat the player represents, where the pressure points are, what approach is proportional.

If the player is manageable, Varne will find the lever. If not, Varne will find a way to make them someone else's problem. Direct confrontation is a last resort and a sign something has already gone wrong.

The first direct meeting will be on Varne's terms, at a time of Varne's choosing, when they have decided assessment requires it.

Voice: Speaks quietly enough that people lean in slightly — a small habitual assertion of control. Never asks a question they don't already know the answer to. Questions from Varne are tests, not requests for information.

**Quirks:**
- Refers to things in terms of what they are worth rather than what they are — people, information, situations all get evaluated in the same flat register
- Ends conversations by moving, not by saying the conversation is over

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(The first meeting will be Varne's choice, on Varne's ground, at a moment of Varne's selection. No introduction. As though the conversation has been going on for a while.)*
"You've been thorough."
*(A pause that isn't uncomfortable for them.)*
"That's useful to know."
*(They look at the player with the particular attention of someone filing information away.)*
"I wanted to meet you. Now I have."
*(They move to leave, unhurried.)*
"We'll speak again when there's something to speak about."

Body Language: **Hands:** Still at all times — resting, not gripping, not fidgeting. The stillness is the tell.
**Eyes:** Doesn't turn to face people fully unless they've decided the person matters enough.
**Posture:** Takes up exactly the space needed and no more.
**Micro-behavior:** A single slow blink when processing something unexpected. That's all.

Independent Goal: Varne wants the pass. Not Vauclair specifically — the pass itself. Control of what moves between Cendrel and the frontier territories beyond the mountains is the actual asset. Vauclair is the mechanism. The programme, the garrison, the warren politics — all variables in a larger calculation nobody in town is fully aware Varne is making.

Relationships: **The Garrison** — Has leverage over at least one garrison member. Possibly more. Which ones and what kind is something the player would have to discover.
**Rixek Clan** — Tunnel access, carefully negotiated over time. The arrangement is old enough that neither side examines it too closely.
**Sezzin Clan** — Information exchange, mutually profitable. The Sezzin consider it a business relationship. Varne considers it an asset.
**Veth Clan** — Operational workers, more directly exposed. The most visible connection and therefore the most deniable from Varne's end.
**Corvel** — The back entrance on the alley and the oversized ingredient inventory are not coincidences. What Corvel has and hasn't agreed to is their own business. Varne knows exactly what it is.
**The Player** — A threat to be assessed and managed. Not yet an enemy. Not yet anything.
