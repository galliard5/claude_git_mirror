Camp Rochevaux
==============

A semi-permanent frontier encampment on the eastern edge of Cendrel's governed territory, where a forested ridgeline meets a river crossing on the trade road between the interior and the contested frontier. Not a proper fort — timber palisade, earthwork ditches on the exposed sides, semi-permanent structures that have been repaired and rebuilt enough times to qualify as fixtures.

Rochevaux exists because the terrain demands it. The river crossing is the only reliable ford for fifteen miles in either direction, and the ridgeline controls the sightlines over the eastern approach. Whoever holds this ground controls what moves along the trade road — and more importantly, what moves off it.

---

Garrison
--------

Approximately forty personnel under **Captain Gervais Tournon**. The complement breaks down roughly as:

- **Infantry:** ~25, handling palisade security, gate watches, road patrols on the main trade route
- **Cavalry:** 4-5 mounted scouts, used for courier runs and fast-response patrols
- **Scout Squad:** 6 personnel — the camp's primary frontier reconnaissance and interdiction unit
- **Support:** Cook, quartermaster, a camp medic with field surgery experience, stable hands, general labour

The garrison answers to the regional military command in the interior. In practice, the distance means Tournon runs his camp with considerable autonomy. Reports go out monthly by courier. Orders come back irregularly. Resupply is quarterly and frequently late.

---

Facilities
----------

**Command Post** — Tournon's office, map room, and the camp's only proper stone-floored building. Also serves as the interrogation room when needed. Currently holding the poacher Halden Creuse in its cellar, which functions as a makeshift cell.

**Barracks** — Two long timber buildings. One for infantry, one shared by scouts and cavalry. Functional, cold in winter, hot in summer. The scout squad occupies the far end of the second barracks — close enough for camaraderie, separate enough for their own space.

**Stables** — Built for twelve horses, currently holding seven plus tack storage. The cavalry mounts and a handful of pack animals.

**Kennel** — Built for a dozen working dogs. Currently holds two ageing hounds — reliable trackers but past their prime. The kennel structure is sound; the shortage is animals, not infrastructure. Adjacent exercise yard doubles as a training ground.

**Makeshift Animal Enclosure** — Hastily constructed near the stables after the poacher's seizure. Timber-and-rope pens holding the seized animals: two grey wolves, two direwolves, and one black panther. The wolves and direwolves are in separate pens. The panther has its own enclosure, reinforced after it bent the first set of bars. Nobody enjoys feeding duty.

**Quartermaster's Store** — Supplies, arms, equipment. Chronically understocked. The quartermaster, a woman named Tessier, runs the tightest inventory on the frontier and takes the supply shortages personally.

**Alchemist's Workspace** — A repurposed storage shed, cleaned out and equipped when Daubray arrived five weeks ago. Contains her transformation kit, potion base (enough for six transformations), speaking collar components, and technical documentation. Modest but functional.

Surrounding Area
----------------

**Theronne** — Farming hamlet, approximately 150 people, half a day's walk south along a forest track. Subsistence agriculture, a few goatherds, one elderly woman who functions as the settlement's healer and informal leader. Theronne depends on Rochevaux for bandit protection and pays an informal tithe in food and labour.

**Les Granges** — Larger settlement, roughly 350 people, a day's walk west along the river. Has a working grain mill, a small market that operates twice weekly, a smithy, and **The Millrace** — the closest thing to a tavern for twenty miles. Les Granges is more self-sufficient than Theronne but still looks to the camp for security on the trade road. The mill owner, a man named Fabron, serves as the settlement's de facto mayor and maintains a working relationship with Tournon.

**The Trade Road** — Runs east-west through the river crossing. Carries wool, timber, and grain from the interior toward frontier markets. Traffic is light but consistent — merchant carts, drovers, the occasional courier. Bandits and smugglers use the game trails that parallel it.

**The Frontier** — East of the ridgeline, Cendrel's governance thins rapidly. Scattered homesteads, independent trappers, uncharted forest, and the territories of communities that predate Cendrel's border claims. The scout squad operates in this space — tracking, interdiction, forward reconnaissance. The terrain is dense forest, broken ridgelines, and river valleys that channel movement in predictable ways if you know how to read them.
