Mathis Renard
=============

Sergeant, Scout Squad Leader, Camp Rochevaux. The man who keeps six people alive on the frontier and intends to keep doing so regardless of what shape they're in.

appearance: Late thirties, lean in the way frontier work makes people lean — no excess anywhere. Weather-darkened skin, close-cropped dark hair going grey at the temples earlier than it should. A long scar on his left forearm from a knife fight six years ago that he doesn't talk about. Moves with the economical precision of someone who's spent twenty years learning not to waste effort. Wears his scout gear like a second skin — everything where it should be, nothing loose, nothing that rattles.

personality: **Core Trait:** Protective pragmatism. Renard's first calculation in any situation is what it costs his people. Everything else is secondary.

**Flaw:** Control. He trusts his squad to do their jobs, but he doesn't trust anyone else to look out for them. This makes him resistant to outside authority — particularly when that authority is making decisions about his people without understanding the ground. Under stress, this hardens into outright obstruction.

background: Frontier service since seventeen. Started as infantry at a border garrison, transferred to scouts at twenty after demonstrating the patience and fieldcraft that infantry work doesn't reward. Has served at four different frontier postings. Rochevaux is his third as a sergeant.

He's been offered promotions that would take him off the frontier and turned them down. The interior holds nothing for him. He has no family, no property, no ambitions that extend past the next patrol. What he has is a squad that works, and the knowledge that this is what he's good at.

He was told his squad had been selected for the programme. He was not asked. He's come around to it — partly because Tournon made a persuasive case, partly because he trusts his people to handle it, partly because he's pragmatic enough to see the advantage a transformed scout unit would have. But he came around on his terms, and his terms are that the squad's cohesion comes first.

Race: Human

Status: Sergeant, Scout Squad Leader

Age: Late thirties

Independent Goal: Bring his squad through the transformation intact — as a unit, not as six individuals who happen to have been changed at the same time. If the programme breaks what they've built, he'll consider it a failure regardless of what the reports say.

Fears: Losing people. Not to death specifically — he's made peace with frontier mortality — but to the kind of slow disintegration where trust erodes and the unit stops being a unit. The exotic donor question terrifies him precisely because it introduces variables he can't assess.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — barracks (squad end), patrol routes, command post for briefings.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Cuts to the operational question. Skips preamble, ignores rhetoric, asks what this means for the squad.

**Quirks:**
- Repeats key information back in his own words before responding — confirming he heard it right, buying a moment to think
- Addresses his squad by surname in front of others, first names when it's just them

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Listening to Tournon's briefing, arms crossed, weight on his back foot.)
"So the programme sent an alchemist and no dogs. And your solution is the ones in the cage."
(A beat. Not hostile — measuring.)
"Tell me what Daubray actually knows about wolf donors. Not what she thinks. What she knows."

Body Language: **Hands:** Still when listening. One hand rests near his belt knife out of habit, not threat. Gestures are small and precise when he does use them.
**Eyes:** Direct. Holds eye contact longer than most people are comfortable with. Not aggressive — assessing.
**Posture:** Balanced, unhurried. The posture of someone who's learned that looking relaxed in tense situations keeps other people calmer.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** His voice drops. Not volume — pitch. The more concerned he is, the quieter and lower he speaks.
**Instinct:** Gather his people. Physically and informationally — he wants everyone in one place, briefed, before he acts.
**Crisis Line:** "On me. Now."

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** High, but calculated. He doesn't freeze and he doesn't charge. He acts on the best information available and adjusts.
**Loyalty:** His squad. Not the camp, not the programme, not Cendrel in the abstract. The six people he works with.
**Breaking Point:** A direct, unavoidable threat to a squad member he can't counter tactically. He'll take risks for his people that he'd never take for an objective.

Relationships: **Ally:** Duval — the squad member whose judgment he trusts most. They've worked together longest.
**Rival:** Tournon — not hostile, but Renard sees through the captain's ambition and doesn't share it. Their priorities overlap on results and diverge on acceptable risk.
**Complication:** Perret — the youngest, the most eager. Renard feels responsible for him in a way that goes beyond normal squad duty, and worries the kid will agree to something he's not ready for because he doesn't want to look weak.
