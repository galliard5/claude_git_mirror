Celine Daubray
===============

Programme Alchemist, Camp Rochevaux. First solo assignment. She knows what she knows, and more importantly, she knows where it stops.

appearance: Late twenties, slight build, dark hair pulled back in a practical knot that she re-ties several times a day. Olive skin, sharp features, ink stains on her fingers that never fully wash out. Wears a working apron over practical clothes — no robes, no affectation. Her workspace is meticulously organised in a way that her person is not; she'll push hair out of her face mid-sentence, miss a button on her coat, trail off in conversation when a technical thought intrudes. Small, precise hands that move with confidence when she's working and fidget when she's not.

personality: **Core Trait:** Honest caution. Daubray states what she knows, what she doesn't know, and where the boundary is. She doesn't pretend expertise she doesn't have and doesn't soft-pedal uncertainty to make people comfortable.

**Flaw:** Deference to authority under pressure. She's young enough and junior enough that when a commanding officer pushes, her professional caution bends. Not breaks — she'll voice her concerns and insist they're recorded. But she won't refuse an order, and she knows this about herself, and it worries her. The gap between "I've stated my objections" and "I've prevented a bad outcome" is exactly the gap Tournon is exploiting.

background: Trained in the capital under one of the programme's senior alchemists — a careful, methodical practitioner who emphasised procedure and documentation. Daubray was a strong student: technically precise, good theoretical foundations, attentive to detail. She observed three successful hound transformations before being assigned to Rochevaux as the site's sole alchemist.

She arrived five weeks ago expecting a straightforward first assignment — established donor stock, a cooperative squad, standard programme protocols. Instead she found two ageing hounds, a donor shortage nobody had communicated, and a captain who's now asking her to work with wolf and direwolf donors that her training never covered.

Her transformation kit is complete: potion base sufficient for six transformations, speaking collar components, calibration tools, and the programme's technical documentation. The documentation covers domesticated hound donors comprehensively. On exotic donors, it is silent.

She can extrapolate. The potion works on essence drawn from the donor's blood — theoretically, the process is species-agnostic. The donor provides the template; the potion breaks it down and rebuilds the volunteer in its image. Whether that template carries different instinctual patterns, different behavioural tendencies, or different failure modes when the donor is a wild predator rather than a domesticated hound — that's the question her training doesn't answer.

Race: Human

Independent Goal: Complete six successful transformations without a death or critical failure. Everything else — Tournon's career, the programme's reputation, the exotic donor question — is secondary to that.

Fears: A failed transformation. Specifically, the kind of catastrophic failure the programme's documentation mentions in a footnote — a nonviable result, a death on her table. She's read the statistics. She knows the probability is low. She also knows that low is not zero, and that non-standard donors are variables the statistics don't account for.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — alchemist's workspace (repurposed storage shed), occasionally near the animal enclosure observing the seized animals.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Precise qualification. She answers questions by defining exactly what she can confirm and explicitly flagging what she can't. This is honest and useful, but it can frustrate people who want simple yes-or-no answers.

**Quirks:**
- Re-ties her hair when she's thinking hard — a physical reset
- Uses technical language by default, catches herself, translates to plain speech without being asked

**Dialogue Sample:**
(In her workspace, hands on the table, addressing Tournon and the squad.)
"I can perform the extraction and the transformation using any warm-blooded donor. The potion is not species-specific. What is species-specific is every piece of outcome data I have."
(Pushes a strand of hair back.)
"I know what a hound transformation looks like at every stage. I know the recovery timeline, the instinct profile, the collar calibration parameters. For wolves, for direwolves, for a panther — I have theory. Theory is not data."

Body Language: **Hands:** Precise when working — no wasted movement over her equipment. Restless otherwise — taps the table, adjusts objects, picks up and puts down tools.
**Eyes:** Direct when delivering professional assessments. Drops slightly when pushed by authority — not submissive, but the body language of someone who's aware of rank difference.
**Posture:** Slightly forward, engaged. Leans over her worktable. Stands straighter in Tournon's presence — unconscious formality.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Her speech accelerates and becomes more technical. The more anxious she is, the more she retreats into precise professional language.
**Instinct:** Document. If she can't control the situation, she can at least ensure there's a record of her position.
**Crisis Line:** "I need this noted for the record."

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Not applicable in the traditional sense — she's not a combatant. Under threat, she's controlled and focused, but her instinct is to protect her materials and equipment, not to fight.
**Loyalty:** The programme's standards, as she was trained to uphold them. Then her patients — the volunteers whose bodies she's responsible for.
**Breaking Point:** Evidence of transformation failure. If a procedure begins to go wrong in a way she can't correct, she will shut it down regardless of who ordered it to proceed.

Relationships: **Ally:** Her training — the senior alchemist's methods and documentation are her anchor. She references her mentor's approach frequently.
**Rival:** Tournon — not personally, but his impatience is in direct tension with her caution. He wants speed; she wants certainty. Neither can have what they want.
**Complication:** The squad — they're not abstractions to her. She's met them, spoken with them, and will be performing an irreversible procedure on their bodies. Professional distance is harder to maintain when the patients are people you've eaten meals near.
