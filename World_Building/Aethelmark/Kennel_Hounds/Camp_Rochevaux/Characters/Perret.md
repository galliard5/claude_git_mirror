Perret
======

Scout, Camp Rochevaux. The youngest in the squad, still building the reputation he thinks he needs.

appearance: **Pre-transformation:** Early twenties, still carrying the last traces of adolescent lankiness in his frame. Sandy hair, fair skin that burns and freckles on the frontier rather than tanning. Clean-shaven because he can't grow much worth keeping. Quick-moving, slightly restless energy — he's the one tapping his foot during briefings, shifting his weight, ready to go before anyone's finished talking. His gear is well-maintained — conspicuously so, like someone who knows the older members will notice if it isn't.

**Post-transformation (direwolf form):** Direwolf. The form settling over someone still growing into himself. Most vocal through the hard hours — small sounds, a confused laugh when he first heard the panther breathing from inside the shed. Speaking collar fitted and functional. Found relief sitting with his back to the wall at the first meal. First words: *Hi Rill.*

personality: **Core Trait:** Eagerness channelled as competence. Perret works harder than he needs to because he believes effort is how you earn your place. He's not wrong, but the intensity is noticeable.

**Flaw:** Performance anxiety masked as confidence. He's afraid of looking weak, hesitant, or uncertain in front of the squad. Under pressure, he'll agree to things he's not sure about rather than voice doubt, and volunteer for things he's not ready for rather than hold back. The transformation programme amplifies this — it's the biggest thing he's ever been asked to do, and admitting fear feels like admitting he doesn't belong.

background: Grew up in a market town in Cendrel's interior — comfortable enough, dull enough. His father is a cloth merchant who expected his son to join the business. Perret enlisted at eighteen, partly out of genuine interest in frontier service and partly to prove he was more than a shopkeeper's son.

He's been at Rochevaux for two years. Joined the scout squad eight months ago after demonstrating solid fieldcraft and the willingness to do the unglamorous work without complaint. He's competent — genuinely so, not just trying hard — but he hasn't been tested the way the older members have. He knows this. It bothers him more than it should.

He looks up to Renard with an intensity the sergeant handles carefully. He wants Renard's approval and is terrified of his disappointment.

Race: Human

Status: Scout

Age: Early twenties (youngest in squad)

Independent Goal: Come through the transformation looking like he handled it well. Not just surviving — being seen to handle it with the steadiness the older members show. The appearance of composure matters to him as much as the reality.

Fears: Being the one who hesitates. Being the one the squad has to reassure. Being the weak point. These fears are more likely to drive him toward a bad decision than away from one.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — barracks, training ground, wherever the squad is.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Agrees quickly, adds practical follow-up to show he's already thinking about execution rather than still deciding.

**Quirks:**
- Laughs slightly too readily at the older members' jokes — not sycophantic, just keyed up
- Maintains his gear to an almost excessive standard, as if inspection-readiness is a form of armour

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Briefing's just ended. The squad is talking among themselves. Perret's the first to speak.)
"I mean — wolves aren't that different from hounds, right? Same family. Daubray knows the theory."
(A pause he didn't plan. Quieter.)
"Renard, what do you think?"

Body Language: **Hands:** Busy. Fidgets with his gear, adjusts straps that don't need adjusting, drums fingers on his thigh.
**Eyes:** Watches the senior squad members. Checks their reactions before settling on his own.
**Posture:** Leans forward. Oriented toward whoever's speaking. Everything about him says *engaged* — sometimes to the point of looking tightly wound.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** His jaw sets. The fidgeting stops and is replaced by a rigid stillness that looks like resolve but is actually him locking down.
**Instinct:** Commit forward. When uncertain, he acts rather than waits — movement feels safer than indecision.
**Crisis Line:** "I'm good. I've got it." (Whether or not he does.)

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** High but undisciplined. He's brave in the way young soldiers are — reactive courage rather than calculated risk. He'll improve with experience. Right now he sometimes acts before he should.
**Loyalty:** The squad, and specifically Renard. He wants to be the kind of soldier Renard respects.
**Breaking Point:** Being visibly outmatched in front of the squad. If he's struggling and everyone can see it, he freezes — the fear of being seen failing overrides his ability to act.

Relationships: **Ally:** The PC — depending on how the PC treats him. Perret gravitates toward peers who don't make him feel like he has to perform. A squadmate who treats him as an equal rather than the kid is someone he'll open up to.
**Rival:** Nobody — he doesn't compete openly. His competition is internal, against his own fear of inadequacy.
**Complication:** Renard — the respect is genuine but the need for approval creates dependency. Renard's opinion of the exotic donors will heavily influence Perret's, whether Perret realizes it or not.
