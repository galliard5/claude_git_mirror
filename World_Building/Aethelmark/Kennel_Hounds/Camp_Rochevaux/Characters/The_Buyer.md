The Buyer
=========

Facility Owner, Eastern Frontier. The man who commissioned a panther from across the border and built something quiet in the woods to use it. Currently face-down in his own front yard.

appearance: City-dressed — too well-dressed for the frontier. City leather, clean linen, dye that doesn't come from this region. The kind of grooming that says interior money and interior habits transplanted to an isolated property. Faint chemical smell — same edge as the alchemist's shed. The relief on his face when the cart arrived was genuine; so was the composure that replaced it. Mid-build, not physically imposing. His authority comes from resources and connections, not presence.

personality: **Core Trait:** Controlled acquisition. The Buyer identifies what he wants, commissions it precisely, and builds the infrastructure to use it. He operates through intermediaries, planning, and money rather than direct action.

**Flaw:** Composure dependency. When his control of the situation was broken — Rill's headbutt, the squad in his yard, his facility taken — he cracked on one critical detail (the contractor's inside programme knowledge) before shutting down. His calculating nature requires the illusion of control to function.

background: Unknown in detail. City origin clear from dress, accent, and grooming. Chemical smell on his person suggests he works with or alongside the alchemist rather than simply employing one. The facility is purpose-built for animal handling and transformation work — repurposed from an older structure, isolated deliberately, concealed from the road.

He commissioned Halden Creuse to capture a black panther from south or east of Cendrel's border. The operation required planning, advance payment, a specified delivery route. The intermediary who contacted Creuse made clear that non-delivery would be noted. This is not a hobbyist — this is infrastructure.

The critical revelation: the contractor who engaged the Buyer specified a panther and a speaking collar. This implies inside programme knowledge. But the panther was never reported as a potential donor — the decision to use seized animals as donors was internal to Camp Rochevaux, made days after the seizure report was filed. Either the contractor knew about the panther before Rochevaux did, or there's a second site with exotic donors that also hasn't been reported.
