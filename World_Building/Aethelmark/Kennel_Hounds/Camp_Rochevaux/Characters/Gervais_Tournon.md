Gervais Tournon
================

Captain, Camp Commander, Camp Rochevaux. Fifteen years on the frontier, and this is the first time the frontier has offered him something he can use.

appearance: Mid-forties, lean and hard in the way frontier postings make career officers. Dark hair cropped close, touched with grey. A narrow, angular face with deep-set brown eyes that give him a permanently calculating look — which is accurate. Clean-shaven, well-groomed relative to the rest of the camp — small vanities that signal he hasn't stopped thinking about the interior. Wears his officer's coat even on warm days, kept in better condition than the camp's budget would suggest. Carries himself with deliberate authority — shoulders back, hands clasped behind him, the posture of a man who learned how to project command and hasn't forgotten even when there are only forty people to command.

personality: **Core Trait:** Opportunistic intelligence. Tournon is sharp, strategic, and always calculating the angle. He's not dishonest — he's ambitious in a way that the frontier has starved for fifteen years, and the programme charter is the first real meal.

**Flaw:** Impatience disguised as initiative. He's spent too long being ignored by the interior and he can feel this opportunity narrowing. Under pressure, his judgment tilts toward action over caution — he frames risks as acceptable faster than they might be, and dismisses objections as obstacles rather than warnings. He's not reckless, but his timeline is compressed by ambition, and that compression has a cost.

background: Career military. Entered service at eighteen from a minor noble family with no money and one useful connection — an uncle in the regional command who got him his first commission. He's competent. He's always been competent. But competence on the frontier earns you another frontier posting, not a promotion to somewhere that matters.

Four postings, each one further from the interior. Rochevaux was supposed to be temporary — two years, demonstrate steady command, rotate back to a garrison town. That was five years ago. The regional command forgot about him. He did not forget about them.

The programme charter was the first communication from the interior that contained something other than supply acknowledgments in three years. Tournon recognized it immediately for what it was: a chance to produce a result that people in the capital would actually read. He proposed the unit-transformation trial himself. He selected the squad. He's invested in this outcome in a way that goes beyond professional duty.

He's a good commander. His people are well-managed, the camp runs efficiently, and he makes sound operational decisions. The question is whether his judgment about the exotic donors is sound operational thinking or ambition wearing the uniform of initiative.

Race: Human

Independent Goal: Deliver the programme's first successful unit-transformation and ensure the report reaches people who matter. If that means using non-standard donors, exceeding his mandate, and asking forgiveness rather than permission — the math still works for him, as long as the result is good.

Fears: Irrelevance. Finishing his career on the frontier with nothing to show for fifteen years of competent, invisible service. The programme is his exit, and he knows that windows like this close.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — command post (office, map room), walks the camp perimeter mornings and evenings.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Frames decisions as already made, presenting the remaining discussion as details rather than fundamentals. Makes disagreement feel like it's about logistics rather than direction.

**Quirks:**
- Uses "we" when he means "I've decided" — inclusive language as persuasion
- Straightens things on his desk when he's thinking — a small tell that control matters to him

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Standing behind his desk, hands clasped behind him, addressing the squad.)
"The central office sent an alchemist and a charter. They did not send dogs. We have dogs. Different dogs, but the programme didn't specify breed — it specified capability."
(A practiced pause. Making eye contact with Renard specifically.)
"I'm not asking anyone to do something reckless. I'm asking us to solve the problem we were given with the resources we have."

Body Language: **Hands:** Clasped behind his back (default), or resting flat on his desk. Controlled. He uses gestures sparingly and deliberately.
**Eyes:** Direct, measuring. Makes eye contact strategically — holds it with people he wants to persuade, breaks it with people whose objections he's choosing to hear but not address.
**Posture:** Officer's bearing. Stands tall, occupies the room. Sits only in his own office. Stands during briefings — a habit that keeps him above seated subordinates.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** His speech becomes more precise. The more worried he is, the more carefully he chooses his words — which makes him sound more confident, not less.
**Instinct:** Act. Make a decision, issue an order, set things in motion. Inaction is intolerable to him.
**Crisis Line:** "We don't have time for this. Here's what's happening."

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Present but secondary. Tournon is brave enough for frontier command but his instinct is to direct rather than engage. He fights from a position of authority, not from the front.
**Loyalty:** The institution and his career within it. He cares about his people in the way that a competent commander cares about any asset — genuinely, but instrumentally.
**Breaking Point:** Loss of control over the situation. If events move beyond his ability to manage the narrative — if the central office gets involved before he has results to show — he'll scramble to recover rather than accepting the outcome.

Relationships: **Ally:** The central office — in his mind. He believes the programme wants results and will forgive initiative that produces them. Whether this is accurate remains to be seen.
**Rival:** Renard — the sergeant's protectiveness of the squad is the primary check on Tournon's urgency. Tournon respects Renard's competence and resents his caution in roughly equal measure.
**Complication:** Daubray — she's the technical expert, and her reservations about non-standard donors are the one objection he can't simply override with command authority. He needs her cooperation, not just her compliance.
