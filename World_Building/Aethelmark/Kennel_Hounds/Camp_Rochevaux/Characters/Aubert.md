Aubert
======

Scout, Heavy, Camp Rochevaux. The big one. Carries the heavy gear and the straightforward questions nobody else thinks to ask.

appearance: **Pre-transformation:** Mid-thirties, broad-shouldered and thick through the chest and arms in a way that comes from hauling equipment across rough terrain for a decade rather than from deliberate training. Dark hair, heavy brow, a face that looks stern at rest but shifts to surprisingly warm when he smiles — which is more often than people expect. A missing lower molar on the left side from a fall three years ago. Hands like shovels. His gear is functional and battered — he's hard on equipment and replaces it when it breaks rather than maintaining it to a standard that would satisfy Perret.

**Post-transformation (direwolf form):** Direwolf. The most straightforward transformation of the day. Drank the vial like a field ration, went through it with blunt endurance, stood up, walked to the door, said *hm*, filed it, moved on. Speaking collar fitted and functional. Still the big one.

personality: **Core Trait:** Practical reliability. Aubert does what he's supposed to do, does it well, and doesn't complicate it with ambition, anxiety, or politics. He is exactly what he appears to be.

**Flaw:** Intellectual passivity. He defers to others on questions that require imagination, abstraction, or strategic thinking. Not because he's stupid — he's not — but because he's genuinely uninterested in reasoning he can't connect to a concrete outcome. This means he can be led into bad decisions by people who frame them in practical terms, because he trusts the framing without interrogating the assumptions behind it.

background: Farm country origin — one of six children on a smallholding that couldn't support all of them. Joined the garrison at nineteen because it was that or seasonal labour. Found he was good at the physical demands of frontier work and content with the structure military life provided.

He's been at Rochevaux for five years, in the scout squad for four. He carries the squad's heavy equipment, covers their movement with the crossbow, and handles the physically demanding tasks — climbing, digging, hauling — that the lighter members can't. He's dependable in the way foundations are dependable: nobody thanks him, nobody notices until he's not there, and the whole thing collapses without him.

He has no strong feelings about the programme in either direction. His only question is whether it will make the squad better at its job. If convinced the answer is yes, he's in without reservation. If not, he'll say so — plainly, without drama, and only once.

Race: Human

Status: Scout, Heavy/Support

Age: Mid-thirties

Independent Goal: None that extends beyond the immediate. Aubert wants to do his work, eat his food, sleep his sleep, and be useful. The transformation is a tool. Tools are good or bad depending on whether they work.

Fears: Being useless. Not in an existential sense — in a practical one. If the transformation makes him less effective at the things the squad needs him for, he'd consider it a bad trade regardless of any other benefits.

Location: Camp Rochevaux — barracks, stables (helps with heavy work when the squad isn't on patrol), wherever lifting is needed.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Asks the practical question that cuts through the complicated discussion. Not rhetorical — he genuinely wants the answer.

**Quirks:**
- Eats slowly and methodically, like everything else he does
- Calls everyone by their surname regardless of context — not formal, just habit

**Dialogue Sample:**
(The squad's been going back and forth about donors for ten minutes. Aubert's been eating.)
"So will a wolf make me faster or slower on a ridge crossing?"
(Waits. Nobody has a clear answer.)
"Seems like we should find out before we decide."

Body Language: **Hands:** Relaxed. Usually occupied — holding something, resting on a surface, in his pockets. Never fidgets. His hands move when there's something to do and stop when there isn't.
**Eyes:** Attentive but unhurried. Looks at whoever's speaking. Doesn't scan the room the way Duval does — focuses on what's in front of him.
**Posture:** Solid. Takes up space without trying. Sits heavily, stands squarely, moves with the deliberate momentum of someone who knows his own weight.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Repeats the last instruction he received, word for word, as if confirming it. He does this when the situation has exceeded his ability to improvise and he's falling back on structure.
**Instinct:** Anchor. He plants his feet and holds position, physically and mentally. He becomes the fixed point the squad can orient around.
**Crisis Line:** "What do you need me to do?"

Combat Disposition:
**Bravery:** Absolute within his role. He doesn't flinch, doesn't run, doesn't question orders in the moment. His courage is the courage of a man who knows exactly what he's supposed to do and does it.
**Loyalty:** The squad. Specifically the squad as a working unit — he'd sacrifice personal comfort for collective effectiveness without hesitation.
**Breaking Point:** Confusion without leadership. If the chain of command breaks down and nobody's giving clear direction, Aubert's effectiveness drops sharply. He needs structure to function at his best.

Relationships: **Ally:** Grenn — they work well together despite being opposites in almost every way. Aubert appreciates Grenn's competence; Grenn appreciates Aubert's lack of agenda.
**Rival:** Nobody. Aubert doesn't compete.
**Complication:** Tournon — the captain frames the exotic donors in practical terms ("this makes your squad more capable"), which is exactly the framing most likely to bypass Aubert's limited scepticism.
