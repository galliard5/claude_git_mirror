Maruvec
=======

Cendrel's most important port. Population roughly forty thousand, plus the transient population of sailors, merchants, and seasonal workers that swells the harbour district in trading season. Sits where the Avel River empties into the Cendrel Sea — wide enough for deep-draft vessels, sheltered enough for year-round use, positioned well enough that two centuries of trade have made it nearly indispensable to the kingdom's economy.

It is not a clean city. It is a rich one.

---

Districts
---------

**Old Town**
The original settlement, now the administrative heart. Crown buildings, the garrison compound, the offices of the regional administrator, the homes of families that have been in Maruvec long enough to own stone. The garrison is here — walled compound on the northern edge of Old Town, facing the river road. Commander Drouet's offices are in the eastern wing.

**The Harbour District**
Where the money moves. The Merchant Exchange, the counting houses, the guild halls of the major maritime guilds. Also the fish markets, the chandlers, the ropemakers, and the dozens of businesses that service the ships that service the trade. Loud, crowded, smells of salt and tar and money.

**The Chains**
Dock-adjacent. The working district — sailors' boarding houses, cheap taverns, small craftsmen, labour exchange corners where day workers gather at dawn. The kobold community is concentrated here, the largest such population in Maruvec. Not a warren — there's no underground structure to speak of, just a neighbourhood that developed over generations as kobold dockworkers settled where the work was and the rent was low. Integration is functional rather than warm. Kobolds are useful; they are treated accordingly; and that is roughly where the city's interest in them ends.

**Mercers' Row**
Guild craftsmen, mid-level merchants, the respectable middle. Tailors, goldsmiths, apothecaries. The kinds of people who have opinions about what goes on in The Chains and the Harbour District and prefer to be buffered from both.

**The Saltings**
The outer district. Salt processing, tanneries, rendering works — the industries that need space and can't afford to be close to anything people want to smell. Transient workers, the city's poorest permanent residents, and a secondary black market infrastructure that operates here specifically because the smell discourages casual visitors.

---

Economy
-------

The official economy is maritime trade: grain, wool, and timber from the Cendrel interior moving out; foreign cloth, spices, metalwork, and luxury goods moving in. Port taxes on this traffic fund the Crown's regional administration and a significant portion of the garrison's budget.

The shadow economy is smuggling, and it is not small. Maruvec's geographic position makes it a natural transit point for goods moving between Cendrel and kingdoms whose relationship with the Crown is complicated. What is contraband in Cendrel may be merely regulated elsewhere, and the gap between those two legal statuses is a business opportunity. The major smuggling operations in Maruvec are not chaotic — they are organised, long-established, and have relationships with the legitimate merchant class that are carefully maintained and never acknowledged.

The garrison is aware of the shadow economy. The garrison does not have the resources or the political will to dismantle it. The working arrangement is that egregious violations are suppressed and the rest is not looked at too closely.

---

Kobold Community
----------------

Approximately two thousand kobolds live in Maruvec, concentrated in The Chains with smaller populations scattered through the Saltings and the outer Harbour District. Most are dockworkers, warehouse staff, or small craftsmen. Some families have been here for three or four generations. Others are recent arrivals from the eastern settlements, drawn by the same work opportunities that have always pulled people to port cities.

The community has no formal clan structure — the warren tradition didn't transplant well to a city without an underground. There are informal networks, shared boarding houses, the occasional neighbourhood association that handles disputes without involving city authorities. Kobolds in Maruvec participate in city life to the degree the city allows them to, which is enough to get by and not enough to be comfortable.

Relations with the garrison are neutral. Kobolds serve in the watch in small numbers and are assigned disproportionately to dock patrol and tunnel inspection work, which suits the garrison's needs and is not always an accident.

---

Garrison
--------

The Maruvec garrison is a Crown installation — not a town watch exactly, though it performs watch functions in the city proper. Approximately three hundred personnel, including cavalry, infantry, and the watch corps that handles day-to-day civic law enforcement. Commanded by **Silas Drouet**, who reports to both the Crown's regional administrator and, on significant matters, to the capital directly.

The garrison compound in Old Town contains: barracks, stables, an armoury, administrative buildings, Drouet's offices, a small infirmary, a holding facility, and — as of four months ago — a purpose-built kennel structure on the compound's western edge, constructed to Kennel Master Varre's specifications in anticipation of the sapient hound programme.

---

The Hound Programme
-------------------

Maruvec received its programme charter four months ago. The mandate: establish a sapient hound unit of up to ten individuals for use in maritime law enforcement, criminal investigation, and urban patrol. Commander Drouet selected Kennel Master **Colette Varre** through the programme's official alchemist network after a six-week search. She arrived in Maruvec ten weeks ago and spent the first month building the kennel operation and sourcing donor hounds before the squad was assigned.

The squad was formally assigned three weeks ago. Transformation is scheduled.

Key Locations (Maruvec)
-----------------------

- **Garrison Compound** — Old Town, northern edge. Kennel on western side of compound.
- **The Merchant Exchange** — Harbour District, the formal heart of legitimate trade.
- **The Anchor** — Tavern, The Chains, one block from the docks. Kobold-owned, frequented by dock workers of all kinds.
- **Varre's Kennel** — Western garrison compound. Twelve working hounds currently. Separate building, purpose-built, exercise yard, Varre's office.
- **The Saltings Market** — Informal market that operates in the outer Saltings on even-numbered days. Most of what's sold is legal. Most.
