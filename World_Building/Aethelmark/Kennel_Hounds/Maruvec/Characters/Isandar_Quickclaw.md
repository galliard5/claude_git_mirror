Isandar Quickclaw
=================

Kobold male, nineteen years old. Two months and change into his enlistment with the Maruvec garrison watch. Assigned three weeks ago to the sapient hound programme squad.

---

Appearance
----------

Lean, slightly under average kobold height — the dock district diet of his childhood didn't always stretch, and it shows in his build. Scales are a muted olive-bronze, darker along the spine and fading to lighter at the jaw and underbelly. Eyes pale amber. Moves quickly but deliberately, the kind of unhurried watchfulness that comes from growing up in a dense neighbourhood where paying attention has a practical value.

His uniform is kept tidy. This matters to him — not for show, but because it's the one thing in his daily routine he fully controls. His equipment is similarly maintained: clean, checked regularly, stored correctly.

Hands have the slight roughness of someone who has done physical work since adolescence and doesn't think about it.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Pragmatic curiosity. He wants to understand how things work — not just that they do, but the mechanism underneath. Patient with complexity. Patient with tedious tasks if he believes they lead somewhere. Less patient with people who make things complicated without cause.

He's not warm by default and not cold either. He observes before he commits. He's been in the watch long enough to have formed opinions about his squadmates and not long enough to have expressed most of them.

**Flaw:** Thoroughness that tips into hesitation. He prefers a complete picture before he moves. In situations that don't allow for a complete picture — which is most of them — he sometimes waits a beat too long while his mind runs through what he doesn't know yet. He's aware of this. He hasn't fully corrected for it.

---

Background
----------

His parents are immigrants — Kaz and Yara Quickclaw, originally from a settlement in Cendrel's eastern interior. They came to Maruvec seven years ago following work and have stayed because the cost of moving again exceeds what moving would gain them. Kaz does carpentry labour through a day-work exchange in The Chains; Yara takes in mending and laundry for households in Mercers' Row. They are not poor by the standards of The Chains. They are not secure by any standard Isandar finds comfortable.

He grew up in The Chains. Parish school when his parents could spare him from odd jobs. Developed an early habit of reading whatever he could borrow or buy cheaply — he spent two years working through the practical sections of a water-damaged copy of a garrison logistics manual he found at a market stall, because it was the most substantial text he could afford and it seemed like it might be useful. It was.

He enlisted for the steady income and the structure. A uniform means a known wage, known duties, and a framework for advancement that doesn't depend on who you happen to know. He's been competent in basic patrol — reliable, thorough, slightly slower to commit than some of his peers — but patrol work doesn't reward thoroughness the way he'd like. He's been waiting for something that does.

The hound assignment interests him. He's read what he could find about the programme. He has questions he hasn't asked yet.

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Asks a clarifying question before answering complex ones. "What do you mean by X?" is his default response to anything that could be interpreted multiple ways — he wants the actual question before he gives the actual answer.
- Prefers precise language. Corrects imprecision in himself, and very occasionally, carefully, in others. Doesn't do this to be difficult. Does it because sloppy language produces sloppy understanding.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Isandar listens to the end of what's being said before he responds. Full attention.)
"I want to make sure I understand the sequence before I agree to it."
(Pause — not hesitation, calculation.)
"Walk me through what happens after the transformation. From the beginning."

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Still when thinking, deliberate when working. Doesn't gesture much. When he's uncertain, his left hand finds the cuff of his right sleeve — a small, contained movement.
- **Eyes:** Observant. Tends to track the room before settling on the person he's talking to. Not suspicious — cataloguing.
- **Posture:** Upright, slightly forward-leaning when paying attention. Pulls back slightly when he doesn't like something he's just been told.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

To establish enough of a career that his parents' situation stabilises. Not ambition for its own sake — practical security, for people who have never had enough of it. He hasn't told either of his parents this is what he's working toward. He doesn't think he needs to.

---

Fears
-----

The incomplete-information fear has been confronted directly. He asked Cault about the lost case, received the full account, and decided it didn't change anything. The transformation happened. The permanent decision was made. What remains: being the thing that makes his parents' situation worse — now carrying the additional weight of having become something his mother is waiting to see come back.

The new fear, not yet articulated: that the recovery period ends and the operational reality doesn't match the curiosity that carried him through.
