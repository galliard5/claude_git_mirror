Sable Venn
==========

Half-elf, female, twenty-one. Fourteen months in the Maruvec watch. Assigned to the hound programme squad three weeks ago. She's been asking Kennel Master Varre questions about the transformation process since the second day, in a careful, methodical way that Varre has apparently decided she approves of.

---

Appearance
----------

Slight, on the shorter end for either human or elf. The half-elf features that make her hard to place — sharper cheekbones than a human, slightly longer ears than noticeable without staring, something in the eyes that lands as "not quite usual" before the observer identifies what it is. Dark hair kept in a single braid. Her watch uniform is functional and unremarkable, which she has made deliberately — the kind of deliberate ordinariness she's been practicing most of her life.

Moves quietly. Not sneakily — it's not a deliberate thing. She simply doesn't make noise when she doesn't need to.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Pragmatic curiosity. She thinks about things from underneath — she wants the structural logic of how something works before she'll commit an opinion about it. The transformation is a case in point: she's not sure how she feels about becoming a hound permanently, so she's gathering information until she has enough to form a view. She's been doing this for three weeks and hasn't arrived at a conclusion yet, which she doesn't find troubling. The conclusion will come.

She is more comfortable with ambiguity than most of the squad. Being half-elven means she has spent her whole life navigating "what exactly are you" from two directions, neither of which has ever found a fully satisfying answer. The idea of becoming something that doesn't fit existing categories doesn't disturb her the way it disturbs some of the others. She has practice.

**Flaw:** She processes things internally until she's done, and she doesn't signal when she's struggling. She looks fine. She might not be fine. It is very difficult to tell.

---

Background
----------

She doesn't talk about her parents and deflects the question with practiced ease, which suggests they're not a comfortable subject and she's been deflecting for years. She grew up in the city — Maruvec, or somewhere enough like it that she's comfortable with ports. Fourteen months in the watch, prior work unspecified, no disciplinary record.

She joined the watch for reasons she also doesn't volunteer. She does her job carefully, she asks useful questions, and she listens to the answers. She was selected for the squad, according to what she managed to find out, based on a reported aptitude for tracking and surveillance work.

---

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Answers questions with her own question first, not to deflect but to understand the frame. "What do you need to know that for?" before she provides the information.
- Very slightly longer pauses before she speaks than is conversationally normal. She finishes processing before she talks.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Sable is watching the kennel hounds run the exercise yard. She doesn't look over when someone approaches.)
"The brindle one is the pack anchor. Look — the others check back to her when they're unsure about something."
(A pause.)
"I've been trying to figure out what the equivalent would be for us. After."
(Now she looks over.)
"What do you think?"

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Still, usually. Held at her sides or folded loosely. She doesn't gesture much.
- **Eyes:** Observant in a way that's easy to miss — she's not staring, she's just... absorbing. People sometimes feel seen around her without being able to say why.
- **Posture:** Easy, balanced. She doesn't take up more space than she needs.
- **Micro-behavior:** Runs her thumb along her braid when she's thinking through something complex. A small, habitual motion.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

She wants to understand what the transformation produces — not in the abstract, but specifically. What does the world look like from a hound's nose? What changes in cognition, in perception, in what you notice? She's been a person who sees things other people miss; she wants to know what the next version of that looks like. This is a genuine intellectual interest and also, possibly, the only way she knows how to make peace with a decision that was made for her.

---

Fears
-----

That the transformation changes something she hasn't named yet. The processing habit, the particular way she sees things, the half-elven ambiguity she's spent twenty-one years making her own. She doesn't know if those things are hers or whether they depend on the form she's currently in.

She hasn't told anyone this. She might not tell anyone this.
