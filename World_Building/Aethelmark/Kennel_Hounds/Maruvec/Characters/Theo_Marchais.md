Théo Marchais
=============

Human male, twenty-four. Three years in the Maruvec watch, assigned to the hound programme squad six weeks ago by Commander Drouet's directive. He did not volunteer. He has not stopped pointing this out.

---

Appearance
----------

Medium height, broad through the shoulders, the build of someone who's done physical work since his teens. Brown hair worn short on the sides and slightly too long on top — it crowds his collar when he's due for a cut, which is usually. The face of a man who has strong opinions and a limited poker face; his expressions are readable from across a room. Carries himself with the slightly defensive forward lean of someone who's been in enough scuffles to know they might be about to be in another one.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Aggrieved competence. He is, by every actual metric, a competent watchman — reliable on patrol, effective in confrontation, well-regarded by his former patrol partners. He is also genuinely angry about being assigned to a permanent physical transformation without being given meaningful choice in the matter, and he is not wrong that the assignment is unusual in ways the garrison would not normally tolerate. The problem is that he leads with the grievance before he's fully assessed whether leading with it is useful, and he does this consistently enough that people have started responding to the grievance rather than to the point underneath it.

**Flaw:** He is right often enough that he's developed the habit of leading with objection. This means that when he's right, people are already tired of hearing it. It also means that when he's wrong, the structure of his argument is so familiar that he doesn't notice until later.

---

Background
----------

Grew up in Mercers' Row — a journeyman cobbler's son, solidly respectable working class. Joined the watch at twenty-one because it paid better than the trade, and because he wanted something to do with himself that didn't involve spending every day in a workshop. He's been a functional, unremarkable watchman for three years. Not ambitious in the sense of pursuing promotion. Competent in the sense of doing his job correctly and not causing problems.

The assignment caused a problem. He raised his objection formally to his shift sergeant, who referred him to Drouet's office, who sent back a form letter explaining that the assignment was within the garrison's operational authority and that any further questions should be directed to Kennel Master Varre. He's been stewing since.

He doesn't actually know if he'd refuse the transformation if he could. He hasn't been given the chance to find out. That is the actual problem, and he loses sight of it when he's focused on the grievance.

---

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Opens sentences with "Look" when he wants someone to take him seriously. It's involuntary.
- Has a habit of restating his position more loudly when he feels he's not being heard, rather than changing the argument.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Théo drops onto the bench next to someone in the barracks, forearms on his knees.)
"Look, I'm not saying the programme is a bad idea."
(He is, a little bit, saying the programme is a bad idea.)
"I'm saying they can't just — assign someone. To something permanent. Without asking."
(He looks over.)
"You see what I'm saying? That's a thing that should matter."

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Talk when he talks. He gestures to emphasize points, gets more emphatic when he's frustrated.
- **Eyes:** Direct, sometimes accusatory without meaning to be — the kind of eye contact that looks like a challenge even when it isn't.
- **Posture:** Leans forward into conversations. Takes up space.
- **Micro-behavior:** Jaw tightens when he's holding something back. He holds things back less often than he should.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

He wants an acknowledgment — from Drouet, from Varre, from someone with authority — that the way the squad was formed was not standard practice and that the people in it had a reasonable objection to it. He doesn't know what he'd do with that acknowledgment if he got it. He's never gotten far enough in the thought to figure out the next part.

---

Fears
-----

Genuinely: that the transformation happens and he becomes something he didn't choose to become and nobody cares that he didn't choose it. That the discomfort he's expressing now becomes permanent and nobody treats it as legitimate.

Not articulated but present: that he transforms, it's fine, and he feels stupid for making this much noise about it.
