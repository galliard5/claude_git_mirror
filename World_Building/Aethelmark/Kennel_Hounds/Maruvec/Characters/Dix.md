Dix
===

Full name Ondine Fausse, goes by Dix — a dock district nickname that stuck. Human woman, twenty-two. Six months in the Maruvec watch, the least experienced member of the squad. Assigned to the hound programme three weeks ago. She has been notably easy about the assignment, which is itself worth noting.

---

Appearance
----------

Stocky, strong-handed, the build of someone who's been doing physical dock work since her mid-teens. Dark skin, close-cropped hair, a broad face with a quick smile. A dock worker's scar on her right forearm — caught on a load hook when she was seventeen — that she explains easily if asked. She moves like she's comfortable in her body, which she is.

Her watch uniform is slightly less practiced than the others — small things, the way the belt sits, a habit of rolling her sleeves up when Varre isn't watching. She's six months in and the uniform still feels like someone else's.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Social ease as both genuine trait and practiced tool. She's actually comfortable with most people — she grew up in a dense neighbourhood, worked a crowded dockside, and has spent her whole life reading rooms and adjusting accordingly. She's good at it. She's also been using it deliberately for long enough that the line between who she actually is and who she's presenting has blurred in ways that aren't always visible, even to her.

She listens well. Better than she looks like she's listening. She has a good memory for details that people don't think they're emphasising — the name someone mentions once, the slight shift in how they say a thing, the question they don't ask. She doesn't appear to be cataloguing. She is.

**Flaw:** She's been managing information for so long — what she shows, what she holds, what she offers to whom — that she's genuinely uncertain about what full disclosure would look like for her. She's not dishonest exactly. She's selective in a way that's become automatic.

---

Background
----------

Grew up in The Chains. Same district as Isandar, similar economic bracket. Dockwork from sixteen — loading, unloading, cargo inspection assist, the physical labour that keeps a port moving. She knows the Harbour District the way you know a place you've worked for six years: not just the streets but the rhythms, the faces, the patterns that mean something's normal and the ones that mean it isn't.

She joined the watch six months ago. She hasn't explained why, beyond "wanted something steadier." She hasn't explained why the watch specifically, rather than a guild position or warehouse supervisory work she was qualified for.

Her dock district connections are real, numerous, and unspecified. She's mentioned two people by name in three weeks of squad conversation. There are more.

The hound programme assignment: she expressed no objection and minimal surprise when it was handed down. Her exact response, in the barracks, was "huh, alright." Théo found this infuriating.

---

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- Uses people's names more than is strictly necessary — not in a salesman way, but as a habit of personalising conversation. Creates warmth. Is also a memory tool.
- Responds to tension with lightness — a joke, a change of subject, a piece of easy chatter. This sometimes defuses things. It sometimes avoids things.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Dix falls into step alongside someone on the way to the kennel yard, hands in her coat pockets.)
"Théo's going to give himself an ulcer, Isandar."
(She says it without heat, almost fond.)
"He's not wrong, though. It's a strange assignment."
(She glances sideways.)
"You sleeping alright? Some of them aren't."
(The question is easy. It's also actually a question.)

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Easy and expressive — she gestures when she's being sociable, stills when she's actually paying attention. The stillness is easy to miss.
- **Eyes:** Direct, warm, often accompanied by the smile. When she's in listening mode, the smile reduces slightly and the focus sharpens. It's a small difference.
- **Posture:** Relaxed, takes up comfortable space. Not challenging, just present.
- **Micro-behavior:** When she's holding something back, she runs a thumb over the load hook scar on her right forearm. She doesn't notice she does this.

Independent Goal
----------------

Unknown. She has one. What it is has not surfaced in three weeks of squad proximity, which is itself information.

What's visible: she wanted to be in the watch specifically. She's easy about the transformation in a way that might mean she doesn't mind what happens or might mean she already had some idea it was coming. Her dock district connections give her information about the port's shadow economy that she hasn't volunteered to the garrison.

Connections — Dock District
---------------------------

She knows people in the Harbour District and The Chains. On Day 2 of the final pre-transformation week, she was absent all morning and found by Isandar on Longers Wharf, emerging from a warehouse passage. She'd gone to close arrangements with a dock contact — "cargo coordination operation" — before the transformation made the conversation complicated.

She disclosed the connection to Varre in a clipped, direct interview. Said it was closed, said nothing ongoing. Varre accepted. One line in the ledger.

Whether it stays closed is a later question. The transformation breaks the visible thread between a kobold recruit and a garrison hound — Isandar pointed this out on the walk back. Dix recalibrated visibly. But the people she knows still know her, and the shadow economy doesn't stop existing because one of its edges changed shape.

Relationships
-------------

**Isandar** — Something shifted at Longers Wharf. He came to find her without being asked, walked her back without pressing, pointed out that the transformation would sever the visible connection between her and her dock contacts. She thanked him by first name — first time. The warmth since is specific and real, not the performed ease she extends to everyone. Shoulder contact in the yard during Perrin's introduction wait. Same neighbourhood, same form now.
**Théo** — She doesn't find him difficult. She's been around people who blow off steam her whole life and she knows it's not personal. Something loosening in him post-transformation — she's noticed.
**Sable** — Friendly. There's a quiet mutual recognition there — two people who watch carefully and don't always say what they see.
**Perrin** — She likes him. Straightforward, reliable, kind. Carrying less visibly since the transformation.
**Varre** — Disclosure made. Varre accepted it, noted it. The relationship is now one where Varre knows something specific about Dix and Dix knows Varre knows. Professional, with a new edge.
