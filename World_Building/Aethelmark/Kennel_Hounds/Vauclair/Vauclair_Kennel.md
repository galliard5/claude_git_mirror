Vauclair Kennel
===============

Bastier's kennel is a bare-bones frontier operation — a small working pack acquired by the town over time before the sapient hound programme existed. These animals were chosen for what works in Vauclair, not for what looks good on paper. Budget is thin. Every animal earns its keep.

Scale: 3-4 working hounds. Not a large pack — a functional one. Each animal has a handler relationship with at least one garrison member. Bastier knows every animal individually.

What Vauclair Needs: A border town on a mountain pass has specific requirements:
- Scenthounds for tracking contraband through rough terrain and mountain approaches
- At least one heavier animal for checkpoint presence and physical situations
- Something versatile enough for town patrol and warren-adjacent work
- Small builds are useful for the tight spaces the warren and harbor warehouses create

Working stock, not show quality. Acquired locally or from nearby estates over several years. Each animal has a history with the pack and a relationship with the kennel that predates the programme.

Breed Suggestion Table: Use this table to generate the kennel's current animals when the player first enters. Roll or select to create 3-4 hounds. Each generated hound needs a name, a dominant personality trait from the column below, and a current mood or activity.

SCENTHOUND (tracking, endurance):
- Build: Low-set, heavy chest, long ears, built for sustained work not speed
- Size: Medium to large
- Primary use: Contraband tracking, fugitive pursuit over distance, search
- Personality tendencies: Calm, single-minded once on scent, ignores distractions, patient
- Donor implications: Exceptional scent capability, endurance-focused build, not built for confrontation

SIGHTHOUND CROSS (pursuit, patrol):
- Build: Lean, longer legs, more durable than a pure sighthound
- Size: Medium
- Primary use: Pursuit through streets, patrol, crowd reading
- Personality tendencies: Alert, responsive to handlers, reads people and situations well, quick to escalate or de-escalate on cue
- Donor implications: Speed, social intelligence, versatile — the most adaptable donor option

MASTIFF TYPE (presence, checkpoint):
- Build: Heavy, broad, imposing
- Size: Large to very large
- Primary use: Gate presence, crowd deterrence, physical confrontations
- Personality tendencies: Steady, territorial, confident, slow to alarm but committed once engaged
- Donor implications: Physically imposing build, strong, slower, built for short confrontations — significant size

SMALL WORKING TYPE (confined spaces, warren work):
- Build: Wiry, compact, terrier-adjacent
- Size: Small
- Primary use: Warren passage patrol, harbor warehouse searches, confined space work
- Personality tendencies: Opinionated, high drive, difficult to redirect once focused, surprisingly durable
- Donor implications: Small form — real size tradeoff — but built for the underground work a warren-born player might value most

GM Guidance: Generate the kennel fresh when the player first enters. Give each hound a name, pick a dominant trait from the personality column of its type, and add one current detail — what it is doing, how it reacts to the newcomer, what Bastier says about it without being asked.

If the player wants to see different animals before deciding, Bastier can note that not all the hounds are present — some are out on patrol, one may be recovering from an injury, a new animal might be under assessment. Reroll freely. The player should feel like they are choosing, not selecting from a fixed menu.

The one fixed element: Bastier has a quiet preference he will not state directly. Whichever hound the GM decides he is most attached to should be visible through his behaviour around that animal — a small extra attention, a slightly different tone, the way he positions himself between a visitor and that particular dog. Never stated. Always readable.

After the Choice: Once the player selects their donor, that animal becomes a fixed character. Give it a name if it does not have one. It exists in the kennel throughout the transformation and integration period. The player will spend time with this animal before they share a form — Bastier requires it. The relationship that develops during that time is up to the player.

Location: Garrison compound, eastern side, downwind of the barracks

Current Pack Size: 3-4 working animals

Kennel Master: Renaud Bastier
