Vauclair
========

A border town on Cendrel's eastern frontier. Population roughly 3,000 on the surface, another 800-1,000 in the warren below. It has been here long enough to have its own way of doing things, and short enough on crown attention to keep doing them.

Overview: Vauclair sits at the mouth of the Greyvasse pass — the last real town before the road either climbs into the mountains toward Aethelmark or turns south into contested frontier territory. Both directions bring trade. Both directions bring trouble. The town has learned to profit from one and manage the other, often simultaneously.

The surface town is a traditional medieval market settlement: a central square, a garrison compound, guild halls, a temple, taverns, and the usual layering of wealth from the merchant quarter outward to the poorer districts near the walls. The walls themselves are old stonework, repaired piecemeal, never fully adequate.

Below is the Warren — the kobold clan settlement that predates the surface town by at least two centuries. The Warren is a separate government, a separate culture, and a separate economy, but the two have grown around each other like roots around stone. They need each other too much to do otherwise.

The Warren: The Vauclair Warren is home to three clans: the Rixek, the Sezzin, and the smaller Veth. Each clan holds a section of the underground, with shared common areas near the surface access points. Clan law governs internal disputes; a joint Clan Council handles inter-clan matters and represents Warren interests in dealings with the surface town.

Warren culture is traditional and family-oriented — loyalty to clan above loyalty to crown, elders hold significant authority, and reputation within the warren matters more than anything the surface government thinks of you. Young kobolds who leave for surface life are not shunned, but they are noted. They occupy a complicated social position: respected for ambition, quietly suspected of losing themselves.

The Garrison: Vauclair's garrison is a joint force — Crown soldiers, town watch, and Warren members integrated under a single command structure. In practice, it functions as three overlapping bodies:

- **The Watch** — day-to-day law enforcement, town patrol, market oversight, civil disputes
- **The Gate Guard** — border checkpoint, trade inspection, travel documentation
- **The Hounds** — the garrison's small animal unit, used for tracking, patrol support, scent work, and crowd situations

Garrison command sits with **Captain Elise Marenne**, a career soldier appointed by the Crown three years ago. She's competent, not corrupt, and pragmatic enough to understand that Vauclair runs on compromises she's not always going to like.

The Kennel: The garrison kennel houses three to four working hounds — what the town budget sustains, acquired before the programme existed. Well-maintained, paired with handlers for active duty. The kennel adjoins the garrison compound on the eastern side, downwind of the barracks by deliberate design. It houses a small working pack — non-sapient hounds bred and trained for garrison work — plus whatever sapient hound volunteers the programme places here. Border town scale means the sapient complement is 1-2 at most.

## SERVICES

**Licensed Alchemist:** [[Corvel]]

The Hounds programme is the kennel master's domain. The sapient hound experiment — the first of its kind in Vauclair, possibly in Cendrel — has been quietly approved by Captain Marenne as a trial. If it works, the implications for frontier garrison work are significant. If it doesn't, it stays quiet and no one has to explain anything to the crown.

The Black Market: Vauclair's smuggling economy is not a secret. Everyone knows goods move through the pass that aren't on any manifest. The open question — the one the garrison spends considerable energy on — is who is moving what, where it's going, and whether it crosses any of the lines that require official response.

The main flows:
- **Contraband alchemicals** — potions and ingredients that are regulated in Cendrel but less so in Aethelmark, or vice versa. High value, low volume.
- **Untaxed trade goods** — the boring majority. Wool, grain, salt, wine. Tax evasion, not criminal enterprise, but it funds the infrastructure that criminal enterprise uses.
- **Restricted transformation services** — unlicensed alchemists operating below the crown's regulatory threshold. Cheaper than licensed work. Occasionally disastrous.
- **Information** — the frontier has eyes in both directions. Someone is always buying intelligence about troop movements, noble house politics, or garrison patrol schedules.

The Warren has its own relationship with the black market — not as operators, but as the underground through which things literally move. The clans maintain official neutrality. In practice, some clan members are deeply involved and the Clan Council knows it and manages it quietly.

Geography: Vauclair sits on a slight rise above the pass road, giving the garrison's watchtower a clear line of sight in both directions. The town is compact and walled on three sides; the fourth side backs against a rock face that connects to Warren access tunnels. The pass road enters through the East Gate and exits through the West Gate — everything that passes through Vauclair passes through both checkpoints, in theory.

In practice, the Warren tunnels have at least three routes that bypass the surface gates entirely.

Atmosphere: Vauclair smells like garrison town — horses, woodsmoke, tallow, and the particular staleness of stone buildings that never fully air out. The market square is lively on trade days, muted otherwise. The taverns near the East Gate cater to travelers passing through; the ones deeper in town cater to locals who've been here their whole lives and intend to stay.

The surface residents and warren kobolds move around each other with the practiced familiarity of people who've been neighbors too long to bother with ceremony. Not warm, exactly. Functional. There's a difference.
