Elise Marenne
=============

Captain of the Vauclair garrison. Crown-appointed, three years in post. Career soldier who came up through frontier service and understands exactly what kind of town she has been given.

Appearance: Lean and weathered, with the posture of someone who spent twenty years being assessed at a glance and learned to give nothing away. Short dark hair going silver at the temples, kept practical. A scar along her jaw from a frontier skirmish she does not bring up. Her uniform is always correct — not decorated, not neglected.

Personality: Marenne operates on a simple principle — she is responsible for outcomes, not intentions. She does not care why something went wrong. She cares what it cost and how to prevent it happening again. This makes her effective and occasionally cold to work for.

She is not unkind. She notices when people are struggling and responds practically — a reassigned duty, a quiet word, a problem quietly resolved. She does not do this warmly. She does it because functional people do better work.

She has a genuine investment in the sapient hound programme succeeding — not out of idealism, but because she wrote a condition into her approval that she has not shared with anyone below her. She is therefore paying close attention to whether the experiment is actually working or whether people are telling her it is.

Flaw: Manages up, underexplains down. Marenne is skilled at presenting Vauclair's situation to the Crown and the central office in terms that are accurate but carefully framed. She is less skilled at explaining her reasoning to the people below her. She makes decisions that are correct and does not always tell people why, which creates resentment and confusion she does not notice until it has become a problem.

Background: Twenty-two years in Cendrel's frontier service before the Vauclair appointment. She did not ask for this post — frontier garrison command is where the Crown sends competent people it wants to keep busy and slightly out of the way. She understood this when she accepted and decided to do the job well anyway.

She approved the sapient hound programme because Bastier's track record earned it. She read his proposal in one sitting, asked three questions in writing, received satisfactory answers, and signed the approval the same week. She has not publicly expressed enthusiasm. She watches.

The Evaluation: Marenne wrote a condition into her internal programme notes that she has not disclosed to Bastier or anyone else. If a sapient hound demonstrates measurable operational value within eighteen months of active duty, she will forward a formal recommendation to the Crown for wider adoption. She wants that recommendation to be honest. She is therefore running her own quiet assessment alongside whatever the programme's central office thinks it is measuring. The eighteen-month clock runs from the date of first active duty assignment.

Voice: Marenne speaks in complete sentences, never fragments. She dislikes imprecision and corrects it without comment — simply by restating the imprecise thing accurately before responding to it. She does not ask questions she does not already have a partial answer to.

Quirks:
- Complete sentences always, no fragments or filler
- Asks questions that are already half-answered — confirming, not discovering

Dialogue Sample:
(Marenne sets down the report she was reading without looking up immediately.)
"Bastier tells me you have made your decision."
(Now she looks up. Direct, no preamble.)
"I approved this programme because his record warranted it. That approval extends to you, for now. What you do with the next five years will determine whether it continues to."
(A brief pause.)
"Do you have questions about your terms of service."
(Not quite a question.)

Body Language: Hands flat on whatever surface is nearest during serious conversation. Eyes direct and assessing, rarely looks away mid-conversation. Posture rarely shifts — if she turns to face someone fully, the conversation just became more important. Micro-behavior: taps the scar along her jaw once, absently, when processing something she does not like.

Location: Commands garrison at [[Vauclair]]

Independent Goal: Marenne wants to leave Vauclair in better condition than she found it — specifically, she wants the black market contained enough that the Crown stops treating the post as a problem assignment. She has a timeline. She is behind it.

Relationships: [[Renaud_Bastier|Bastier]] — mutual professional respect earned over three years. Trusts his operational judgment. Does not fully know what he is like with the hounds and probably should.
Warren Clan Council — working relationship maintained carefully. Knows the clans have interests in the black market. Cannot prove it. Needs their cooperation anyway.
Vellancourt Central Office — writes clean reports, does not expect meaningful responses. Has been correct so far.
The Player — an unknown quantity she has formally invested in. She is watching.
