Cendrel
=======

A mid-sized kingdom with a Burgundian character — old noble houses, a crown that never quite controls its borders, and a frontier edge that breeds hard people and loose rules.

Overview: Cendrel sits to the west of Aethelmark, separated by the Greyvasse mountain range and a shared trade road that both kingdoms depend on. Where Aethelmark has the structure and density of the Holy Roman Empire, Cendrel is leaner — a kingdom that has always had more land than it could properly govern, and more border than it could properly defend.

The interior is wealthy enough: river valleys, vineyards, wool towns, and market cities where guild politics run as deep as they do in Aethelmark. But the eastern and southern borders — where Cendrel butts against the Wilds and contested frontier territories — are a different world. Towns out here are half-garrison, half-smuggling hub, policed as much by necessity as by law.

Government: Cendrel is a hereditary monarchy, currently ruled by King Aldouart III — a calculating man who understands that controlling the frontier requires tolerance for things he'd rather not officially know about. Below the crown, powerful noble houses manage their territories with considerable autonomy. The relationship between the crown and regional lords is tense, cooperative, and transactional.

Local governance in border towns follows a dual structure: a Crown-appointed Garrison Commander handles military matters, while an elected Town Council handles civic affairs. In practice, the two overlap constantly and neither is fully in charge.

The Frontier: Cendrel's eastern and southern borders are officially claimed but practically ungoverned beyond garrisoned towns. The Wilds beyond are home to independent clans, monster territories, refugee settlements, and a thriving black market economy that funnels contraband in both directions.

The border towns exist to regulate what passes through — and to tax it. What they cannot tax, they sometimes ignore. What they cannot ignore, they suppress. The line between those categories shifts constantly.

Kobold Integration: Kobolds are a recognized minority in Cendrel, concentrated in warrens that predate most surface settlements. The relationship between surface towns and kobold warrens varies by region. In the interior, kobolds are tolerated as craftsmen and laborers. On the frontier, the relationship is older and more complex — warrens and surface towns have co-evolved out of mutual necessity, and kobold clan law is informally recognized alongside Crown law in many border settlements.

Kobolds serve in town garrisons on the frontier as a matter of course. Their underground familiarity makes them invaluable for tunnel patrol, warren-side intelligence, and tracking contraband that moves through underground routes.

Magic and Transformation: Cendrel shares the same general magical framework as Aethelmark — transformation is legal, regulated, and practiced. The regulatory structure is slightly looser on the frontier, where an alchemist's license matters less than their results. Transformation potions are available through licensed practitioners in larger towns; experimental work happens quietly in smaller ones.

Sapient transformed individuals retain full legal status. Speaking collars are recognized as personal property. Transformed individuals in garrison service are paid on the same scale as their non-transformed equivalents — a matter Cendrel codified earlier than Aethelmark, driven by frontier practicality rather than principle.

Relationship with Aethelmark: Trade partners, broadly neutral. The shared mountain road carries wool, wine, silver, and grain in both directions. Diplomatic relations are cordial and economically motivated. Neither kingdom wants a war; both kingdoms quietly compete for influence over the frontier territories between them.

Smugglers and black market operators frequently exploit the gap between the two kingdoms' legal codes — what's contraband in one is merely regulated in the other, and the frontier between them is a grey zone that benefits everyone who knows how to use it.

Government: Hereditary Monarchy

Current Ruler: King Aldouart III

Time Period: 16th Century equivalent

Neighbor: Aethelmark (trade partner, neutral)

Key Themes: Frontier pragmatism over ideology, the gap between official law and working reality, communities that built their own rules before the crown arrived to enforce its own
