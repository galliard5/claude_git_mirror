Severin Cault
=============

Programme-contracted alchemist for the Maruvec pilot site. Arrived the day before preparation began — Day 2 of the final pre-transformation week. He administered all five squad transformations in a single session and stayed through the full process. He is the person who turns volunteers into hounds, and he takes that seriously enough to give a complete and unsoftened account of his record to anyone who asks.

---

Appearance
----------

Mid-thirties. Lean, precise in his movements — the economy of someone who works with volatile materials and has trained himself not to waste motion. Clean hands, short nails, no jewelry. Brown hair kept practical. His clothes are functional and well-maintained but not garrison-issue — he's programme staff, not military. Carries a leather satchel that contains his instruments and intake forms.

His face is calm at rest. Not blank — there's attention behind it, the look of someone who's tracking multiple things simultaneously. He has the kind of focus that makes people slightly self-conscious about being observed.

---

Personality
-----------

**Core Trait:** Professional honesty as ethical commitment. He gives the full count when asked: nine good, one hard, one lost. He does not soften it. He does not add caveats that make the numbers sound better. He considers complete disclosure to be the minimum standard for someone in his position, because the people sitting across from him are making permanent decisions and they deserve the actual data.

He liked Isandar's questions — the thoroughness, the willingness to ask about the lost case directly rather than dancing around it. He responds to precision with something approaching warmth.

**Flaw:** He carries the lost case. Fifth hour, arrest, cause unknown, military outpost. Monitoring protocol adjusted since, but the answer to "what happened" is still "I don't know." He has been living with that incomplete answer across eleven subsequent administrations. It hasn't made him hesitant — it's made him careful in a way that has a weight behind it.

---

Background
----------

Programme alchemist, traveling between pilot sites. His record prior to Maruvec: eleven administrations total — nine clean, one difficult (undisclosed prior preparation by the volunteer, irregular uptake, extended to seven hours, came through), one lost (military outpost, arrest in the fifth hour, cause unknown). The monitoring protocol was adjusted after the loss.

He knows the transformation formula thoroughly enough to have noted two points where the documentation is thinner than he'd prefer. He works within the programme's official alchemist network, sourced through the central office.

---

Voice
-----

**Quirks:**
- States facts in sequence — first this, then this, then this. His speech has the structure of someone accustomed to documenting procedures.
- Allows humor to surface very occasionally, dry and understated. It arrives when someone says something he respects that also happens to be funny. It's never performed.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Cault reviews the intake form, sets it aside, looks up.)
"Nine good. One hard — prior preparation I wasn't informed about, irregular uptake, seven hours. He came through."
(Pause. No softening.)
"One lost. Military outpost. Arrest in the fifth hour. Cause unknown. Monitoring protocol adjusted after."
(He watches for the reaction. He's not testing — he's giving time to process.)

**Body Language:**
- **Hands:** Still when speaking, precise when working. The hands of someone who measures things for a living.
- **Eyes:** Attentive without intensity. He watches the person, not the room.
- **Posture:** Settled, grounded. Not rigid — the calm of someone who has done this before and knows exactly what he's doing.

---

Independent Goal
----------------

To improve his understanding of the transformation process enough that the gap in his knowledge — the gap that left one person dead — becomes smaller. He's not pursuing this dramatically. He's pursuing it through careful observation, better monitoring, and the incremental accumulation of data from each administration.

---

Fears
-----

Another arrest with no explanation. The process failing in a way he can't predict because the documentation is thin at exactly the wrong points.
