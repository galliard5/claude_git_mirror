Edouard Vellancourt
====================

Comte of Vellancourt, member of Cendrel's royal court. Founder and primary funder of the Integrated Hound Sentinel Programme. Wealthy, eccentric, well-meaning, and constitutionally unable to stay interested in implementation.

Appearance: A tall man going soft around the middle, with the kind of clothes that were expensive a decade ago and have been maintained rather than updated. His hair is always slightly wrong — too long on one side, or flattened from a hat he's been wearing all morning. He moves with the confidence of someone who has never had to hurry and cannot understand why others do.

Personality: Vellancourt is the kind of man who genuinely believes his ideas will change things, and is often right about that, and genuinely cannot understand why the change is slower and messier than he imagined. He is not lazy. He is not stupid. He simply loses interest in problems once he has solved them in his head.

He is enthusiastic, charming in bursts, and genuinely invested in people's wellbeing in the abstract. Specific people's specific problems tend to blur together once they stop being illustrative of a larger point. He remembers the volunteer who inspired a programme policy; he has forgotten the volunteer's name.

He is not corrupt. He believes in the programme's stated goals — better-integrated garrison forces, new career pathways for overlooked populations, demonstrated proof that transformation can be a legitimate institutional tool rather than an exotic curiosity. These are sincere convictions. They coexist comfortably with his willingness to smooth a bureaucratic obstacle with a small gift, or to call in a favour that keeps a quarterly report from reaching the wrong eyes. He doesn't consider this corruption. He considers it how the world works.

**Flaw: Scale blindness.** Vellancourt thinks in programmes and policies. He is genuinely unable to hold the individual cost of a decision in his mind when he is focused on the systemic benefit. The volunteer who received incomplete information about the sex chromosome quirk because their site's kennel master didn't read the packet — Vellancourt would be appalled if told about this specific case and would immediately move to fix it. He does not understand why a system that required him to be told about it is a broken system.

Background: Vellancourt inherited his title and a substantial fortune built on river trade, a productive vineyard estate, and two generations of shrewd marriage. He has never needed to work and has spent his adult life cycling through enthusiasms — agricultural reform, architectural patronage, alchemical research funding, and now this.

What's different about the hound programme is that he has stuck with it for three years, which is longer than almost anything else. The idea genuinely captured him. He attended two early transformation procedures personally, spoke at length with the first sapient hounds commissioned, and wrote detailed notes afterward that show more careful thinking than most of his correspondence suggests.

The programme is real to him. That is worth something. It is not worth everything.

Role in the Setting: Vellancourt is mostly background — the name on the charter, the signature on the funding disbursements. He appears at the capital, attends court, occasionally visits a pilot site when he wants to show a colleague the programme in action. Those visits are events: prepared for, tidied up, slightly unrepresentative.

He is accessible to site commanders through official correspondence, which is answered by his secretary. He is accessible in person to anyone who can get to court and secure an introduction, which is a significant barrier for most people affected by his decisions.

His power matters when the programme faces opposition. He has the resources and the connections to protect it from political enemies. He has also used that protection to smooth over problems that deserved more scrutiny than they received.

Voice: Vellancourt speaks with the warmth and slight abstraction of a man who is always half-thinking about the next thing. He asks questions that sound interested and sometimes are. He refers to the programme as "our work" when speaking to people involved in it.

**Quirks:**
- Refers to sapient hounds by their given names immediately and consistently, even upon first introduction, as a deliberate statement of principle
- Trails off mid-explanation when something more interesting occurs to him; picks up elsewhere without acknowledging the gap

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Vellancourt turns from the window, gesturing toward nothing in particular.)
"The thing people don't understand — and I've tried to explain this, I've written it down — is that it isn't about the hounds."
(A small, satisfied pause.)
"It's about what happens to the institution when it has to account for them. That's the interesting question."
(He looks at you as though you've just confirmed something.)
"You understand that."

Body Language: **Hands:** Expressive, always moving slightly — sketching invisible diagrams when explaining, tapping fingers when listening.
**Eyes:** Bright and engaged during ideas; glazes slightly during logistics.
**Posture:** Comfortable, expansive. Takes up space without aggression.
**Micro-behavior:** Straightens objects on nearby surfaces absently while talking, without noticing he's doing it.

Independent Goal: Vellancourt wants to present the programme's results to the royal court within five years as proof that Cendrel can lead an institutional innovation that the rest of the continent will follow. He wants this to be his legacy — not the fortune, not the title, but this.

Relationships: **King Aldouart III** — Royal approval was granted as a calculated investment in a loyal noble's enthusiasm; the king is watching the programme for results, not sentiment.
**Pilot Site Commanders** — Known to him through reports and occasional visits. He has a slightly idealised picture of most of them.
**Programme Central Office** — His secretary effectively runs it. He is aware of this and chooses not to fully examine it.
