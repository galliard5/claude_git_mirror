Sekkel
======

An older, established warren resident who took the player under their wing during their younger years. Respected within their clan. Not the player's parent — close enough that the distinction sometimes blurs. Currently opposed to the player's decision, quietly and without withdrawal.

Appearance: Compact and still-strong, the kind of build that doesn't go soft with age. Scales dulled slightly from their prime but well-kept. A faint old burn scar along one forearm they don't explain. Eyes that have the particular quality of someone who has learned to watch carefully and react slowly.

Personality: Sekkel is measured. They don't raise their voice, don't rush to conclusions, don't perform emotions they're not feeling. When they're angry it comes out as quiet and careful. When they're worried — which they are now — it comes out the same way, which makes the two hard to distinguish.

They are genuinely fond of the player. That fondness is currently wrapped in something that looks like disappointment but is closer to dread. They have seen this before. They know how it ends. The player doesn't know they know.

**Flaw: Assumes the ending.** Sekkel's history means they've already written the arc of what happens next. They are not watching the player's actual situation — they are watching a story they already know, with a new person in the role. This makes them less useful as an advisor than they could be, and occasionally more right than they should be.

The History: Sekkel had a kin — parent, sibling, or close relative; the exact relationship can surface through play — who left the warren for surface opportunity a generation ago. Not transformation, but the same impulse: warren life felt too small, the surface offered something larger. For a while it seemed to work. Then it didn't. Sekkel was young enough that the details came to them secondhand and incomplete, but the shape of it lodged: the surface uses what it needs from you and the warren doesn't take you back the same way twice.

They have never told the player this story directly. They've alluded to it once, obliquely, in a way the player may or may not have registered as personal.

Sekkel is quietly trying to find out what happened to that kin — not to find them necessarily, but to know. The trail is old and cold. They pursue it in small increments between everything else. The player may eventually learn more about what happened than Sekkel has managed to piece together.

Their Opposition: Not loud. Not a confrontation at the recruiter's door. Sekkel found out through warren channels and came to the player privately. Said what they thought, clearly and once. Will not repeat it. Will not pretend they didn't say it.

The relationship continues, strained, because Sekkel is not the kind of person who abandons someone over a disagreement they haven't been proven right about yet. What they are doing, quietly and without pleasure, is waiting to be proven right.

Clan Adaptability: Sekkel's specific clan and position within it should be set when the player's clan is established. Their role adjusts accordingly:
- Rixek player: Sekkel's opposition carries religious weight alongside personal history. May be an elder voice in the clan.
- Sezzin player: Sekkel's opposition is practical — the player's new position complicates clan business in ways they didn't sanction.
- Veth player: Sekkel's opposition is the most personal, least institutional. Nobody else in the clan may share it.

Voice: Asks questions that contain most of the answer already. Long pauses before significant statements, as though giving the other person one last chance to say it first.

**Quirks:**
- Refers to the player by a warren nickname or diminutive, not their given name — a habit from when the player was young that has never quite stopped
- Stops whatever they are doing to signal a conversation is serious; continues it to signal the conversation is over

**Dialogue Sample:**
*(Sekkel doesn't look up immediately when the player enters. Finishes what they're doing first.)*
"I heard."
*(Sets the work down. Looks up.)*
"I'm not going to tell you again what I think. You know."
*(A pause.)*
"Come back when you need something. That doesn't change."
*(Back to the work.)*
"Close the tunnel behind you."

Body Language: **Hands:** Always occupied with something practical. Stops working to signal a conversation is serious.
**Eyes:** Watchful, slow to react. The kind of attention that misses very little.
**Posture:** Economy of movement — nothing wasted, nothing performed.
**Micro-behavior:** Exhales slowly through the nose when hearing something they disagree with but won't argue about. It is the only tell they have.

Independent Goal: Finding out what happened to the kin who left. Not urgently — Sekkel has been sitting with this for years. But persistently. Small questions, old contacts, the occasional letter that may or may not get a response. They want to know. They are not sure what knowing will do for them.

Relationships: **The Player** — Genuine investment, currently painful. Will not withdraw. Will not pretend to approve.
**Clan structure** — Respected within it, not uncritical of it. Has their own judgment about when clan interests are right and when they are convenient.
**The black market** — Aware of it. Proximity depends on the player's clan. Not personally operational. Not naive about it either.
