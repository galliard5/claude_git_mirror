# Camp Rochevaux — Session 5 Checkpoint

**Campaign:** Camp Rochevaux — Kennel Hounds Military Outpost Pilot

**In-Game Date:** Dawn, day two — destination enclosure
**Location:** Destination facility, eastern Cendrel — unknown precise location, one day's travel northeast of Camp Rochevaux on the eastern road branch
**PC:** Rill Sunclaw, kobold scout, panther form. Collar removed before swap — understands everything, cannot speak.

## Summary

Creuse loaded the cage at dawn without incident — noted the wrapped ear, accepted the cage-latch explanation without pressing it, and delivered Rill to a working facility one day's travel northeast of camp. The handler who received the delivery is mid-fifties, human, professional, experienced with animals — noted Rill was thin and carrying herself wrong from eight days in a military camp. Rill was placed in the back enclosure for the night. Assessment scheduled for morning. The handler has returned at dawn with a feeding bucket.

1. Creuse loaded the cage at dawn — noted the wrapped ear, said "cage latch," accepted it. No closer inspection. Departed without incident.
2. Transit: one day, northeast on eastern road then a branch track. Facility is isolated — forest close on both sides, no nearby settlements detectable by scent.
3. Handler received the delivery — professional, experienced, immediately noted Rill was thin and stressed from camp conditions. Took Rill around to the back enclosure, provided a strip of dried venison through the bars.
4. Enclosure investigation: three hounds (unnaturally patient, synchronized breathing, long-kennelled), one ocelot with a speaking collar (young, said "You're new," Rill gave no response), one old badger (no visible collar, made a structured two-short-one-long sound, repeated it when Rill mimicked the rhythm against the fence post).
5. Feed trough investigation: alchemical compound worked deep into the wood grain — mineral carrier base with a complex organic component, same family as transformation potion chemistry but slower, more patient. Has been in the feed for a long time. All animals in the enclosure carry its signature in their coats.
6. Rill took the high beam for the night — watched the hounds' synchronized breathing, observed the badger look directly up at the beam in the deep hours and hold eye contact for three seconds before looking away.
7. Handler returned at dawn with a bucket, found Rill on the beam immediately, assessed — said "Hm." Has not approached yet.

## GM Notes

- The compound is the central mystery now — slower than transformation potion, long-term administration, applied through feed. What does it do? The hounds are the clearest data point — something has changed in them over time.
- The badger is the most valuable intelligence asset in the enclosure and the most dangerous to approach — if it's transformed and long-resident, it knows this facility better than anything. The communication channel established (rhythm mimicry) is fragile and needs to be developed without alerting the handler.
- The handler is not yet suspicious but is observant — the beam is an early tell. A stressed transport animal doesn't usually find the highest point in an unfamiliar enclosure and hold it through the night. The morning assessment will test the cover harder. he will also check rill for his suitability for breeding.
- The second structure behind the main building is unexplored — larger, no windows, multiple animals. Priority intelligence target once Rill has freedom of movement.
- Food decision this morning: the compound is in the feed. Refusing to eat will read as stress (plausible cover) or suspicion (problematic). Eating it means absorbing something unknown. Neither option is clean.
- Squad ETA: unknown. The pitch trail is solid. Duval is forward. They need time to locate the facility and assess approach — Rill should assume at least another day, possibly two.
