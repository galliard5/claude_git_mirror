# Rochevaux Campaign

**Campaign:** Kennel Hounds — Military Outpost Pilot
**Setting:** [[Camp_Rochevaux]], eastern Cendrel frontier
**Estimated Duration:** Variable (open campaign)
**Difficulty:** Medium-High

---

## Overview

A six-person frontier scout squad at Camp Rochevaux has been selected for the Kennel Hounds programme's first unit-transformation trial. The hypothesis: an already-bonded squad will perform better after transformation than individually recruited volunteers. The PC is rank-and-file — one of the six, trusted by the others, living inside the group's dynamics rather than steering them.

The programme is stalled. HQ's records listed a full kennel complement at the camp. There are two ageing hounds. The alchemist has arrived with materials for six transformations and no viable donors. A requisition for replacement hounds was sent weeks ago. The central office acknowledged receipt. Nothing has arrived.

Two weeks ago, the squad intercepted a poacher-smuggler transporting caged exotic animals — grey wolves, direwolves, and a black panther. The animals are in camp custody. Their potential as transformation donors is obvious. Their use is completely outside programme parameters.

**Hook:** The PC's squad captured the poacher. They know these animals. Now those animals might become them.

**Core Conflict:** The squad is ready. The donors are wrong. The commander wants results. The alchemist has reservations. The programme charter doesn't cover this. Someone has to make a call, and six people live with it permanently.

**Resolution:** Multiple paths — wait for proper donors (months), use the exotic animals (unknown consequences), negotiate with HQ for emergency authorization, or some combination. The transformation itself is not the end; it's the beginning. What the squad becomes and how they function afterward is the real story.

## Setup & Background

### The Programme's Hypothesis

The Kennel Hounds programme has, to date, recruited individual volunteers at each pilot site. Vauclair took its first volunteer from the local warren. Maruvec assembled a squad of strangers. The results have been promising but limited by the integration period — newly transformed individuals need time to find their footing, build trust with colleagues, and develop working relationships from scratch.

Camp Rochevaux was selected to test a different approach: take a squad that already works together — that already trusts each other, knows each other's habits, covers each other's weaknesses — and transform them as a unit. If the hypothesis holds, the result should be a squad that skips the integration period entirely and operates at full effectiveness almost immediately.

Captain Tournon proposed the trial after receiving the programme charter. The central office approved it — the first unit-transformation in the programme's history. Tournon selected his best squad. Sergeant Renard's scouts.

### The Donor Shortage

The programme charter specified that the camp's existing kennel would provide donor animals. HQ's records, based on inventory reports that hadn't been updated in over a year, listed eight working hounds. The actual count: two. Dogs die on the frontier. Replacements weren't requisitioned because the camp's previous commander didn't bother with paperwork that didn't affect operations, and nobody at the central office verified the numbers before issuing the charter.

Tournon sent a requisition for replacement donors immediately upon discovering the discrepancy. The central office acknowledged receipt six weeks ago. Proper breeding-stock hounds, sourced through the programme's alchemist network and transported to the frontier, could take months.

### The Poacher

Halden Creuse was intercepted by the scout squad two weeks ago, moving caged animals along a game trail parallel to the trade road. He's a frontier trapper and middleman — catches animals to order, delivers to buyers. He's been doing this for years, sometimes legally, mostly not. He's currently in the camp's holding cell, weathered and uncooperative.

**Seized animals:**

| Animal | Count | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Grey wolves | 2 | Wild-caught, healthy, aggressive. Common frontier stock. |
| Direwolves | 2 | Larger, rarer, genuinely dangerous. High-value animals. |
| Black panther | 1 | Large female, exotic. Does not range naturally in Cendrel. A commissioned capture. |

Five animals, plus the camp's two existing hounds. Seven potential donors for six squad members.

**Critical note on donor mechanics:** The transformation requires only a blood sample from the donor animal. The donor is not consumed or harmed by the process. All seized animals would remain alive and in camp custody after any transformations. This means:
- The animals remain as evidence of Creuse's poaching
- The panther buyer's "property" is still physically present
- Multiple squad members could theoretically use the same donor (producing "siblings")
- The question of what to do with the animals persists after transformation

### The Buyer

Someone commissioned Creuse to capture a black panther and transport it into Cendrel's interior. Panthers don't range here — this animal was caught south or east of the frontier, in territories Cendrel doesn't govern. The operation required planning, resources, and a route avoiding Crown checkpoints.

Creuse won't identify the buyer. He's more afraid of them than of Tournon. The implications range from mundane (a noble with an exotic menagerie) to concerning (someone with transformation expertise commissioning non-standard donor stock for private use). The panther remains in camp custody regardless of whether it's used as a donor, which means the buyer thread stays live.

## Key NPCs

**Captain Gervais Tournon** — Camp Commander
- *Motivation:* Deliver the programme's first successful unit-transformation. This is his ticket out of frontier obscurity.
- *Relationship to Plot:* The decision-maker. He has the practical authority to authorize non-standard donors but not the legal cover. He's pushing for it.
- *Tactics:* Persuasion, framing the opportunity, applying subtle pressure. He won't order the squad to transform against their will — the programme requires volunteers — but he'll make his preference clear. Frames objections as obstacles to overcome rather than warnings to heed.

**Celine Daubray** — Programme Alchemist
- *Motivation:* Complete her first solo assignment successfully without anyone dying.
- *Relationship to Plot:* The technical gatekeeper. She has the skills to perform the transformations. She has reservations about non-standard donors.
- *Tactics:* Professional caution. She'll state her concerns clearly and wants them on record. She can extrapolate from theory — essence is essence, the potion works on the donor's blood regardless of species — but she's honest about the limits of her knowledge. She's never worked with predator donors. Nobody in the programme has. She'll do it if the decision is made, but she won't pretend it's routine.

**Sergeant Mathis Renard** — Scout Squad Leader
- *Motivation:* Protect his squad. Execute the mission. In that order.
- *Relationship to Plot:* The squad's voice. His opinion carries enormous weight with the five scouts under him.
- *Tactics:* Pragmatic leadership. He was told his squad was selected; he wasn't asked. He's come around to the programme — he trusts his people to handle it and sees the tactical advantage. But he'll push back hard against anything that threatens squad cohesion, and the prospect of half his squad becoming wolves while the other half becomes hounds raises exactly that concern.

**Duval** — Scout, Tracker
- *Motivation:* Do the job right. Understand what he's getting into.
- *Relationship to Plot:* The squad member most likely to have informed opinions about using wild predators as donors. He's worked around wolves. He respects what they are.
- *Tactics:* Quiet, considered pushback. Won't grandstand, but will voice concerns directly to Renard and to anyone who asks.

**Perret** — Scout, Youngest
- *Motivation:* Prove himself. Not show fear.
- *Relationship to Plot:* The squad member most vulnerable to pressure — eager to go along, anxious underneath.
- *Tactics:* Follows the group's energy. Will volunteer first if the mood is positive. Will suppress doubts if voicing them would make him look weak.

**Grenn** — Scout, Kobold
- *Motivation:* Serve the squad. Navigate the cultural weight of permanent transformation.
- *Relationship to Plot:* The squad member for whom transformation carries the most cultural freight. Warren communities have complicated views on permanent body alteration.
- *Tactics:* Dry, practical exterior. Won't discuss his feelings about it unless directly pressed by someone he trusts. His warren background means he may have knowledge about transformation that the others don't.

**Aubert** — Scout, Heavy
- *Motivation:* Will this make us better at our jobs?
- *Relationship to Plot:* The practical voice. Doesn't care about programme politics or career advancement. Cares about whether the squad can still do its work.
- *Tactics:* Direct questions, simple assessments. If convinced it works, he's in without reservation. If not, he'll say so plainly.

**Halden Creuse** — Detained Poacher-Smuggler
- *Motivation:* Survive. Don't anger the buyer.
- *Relationship to Plot:* Source of the exotic animals. Source of information about the buyer. Currently providing neither.
- *Tactics:* Stonewalling. He'll trade small information for comfort (better food, blankets, the possibility of reduced charges) but won't give up the buyer without significant leverage or a credible threat that outweighs what the buyer represents.

## Key Locations

**[[Camp_Rochevaux]]** — Frontier Encampment
- *Key Details:* Timber palisade, ~40 personnel. The entire scenario centres here.
- *Key Areas:* Command post (Tournon's office, Creuse's cell), barracks (squad quarters), kennel (two hounds), animal enclosure (seized animals), alchemist's workspace (Daubray's shed).

**Theronne** — Farming Hamlet
- *Key Details:* 150 people, half-day south. Depends on the camp for protection.
- *Mechanics:* Source of local information, supplies, and civilian perspective on the programme.

**Les Granges** — Mill Settlement
- *Key Details:* 350 people, day's walk west. Mill, market, The Millrace tavern.
- *Mechanics:* Larger settlement, more opinions, better supplies. Fabron (mill owner) is Tournon's civilian counterpart.

**The Frontier** — Eastern Wilderness
- *Key Details:* Dense forest, broken ridgelines, river valleys. The squad's operating territory.
- *Mechanics:* Where the squad works. Post-transformation patrols here will be the first real test.

## Main Plot Arc

### Act 1: The Situation

The scenario opens with the programme stalled. The alchemist is here, the squad is selected, and there are no proper donors. The exotic animals are in camp. Tournon hasn't yet formally proposed using them — but the idea is in the air. Everyone can see the animals. Everyone can do the arithmetic.

**Key Events:**
- Tournon calls a briefing with the squad and Daubray to lay out the situation — donors aren't coming anytime soon, these animals are here, he wants opinions
- Daubray presents her assessment: theoretically possible, practically untested, she wants her reservations on record
- The squad reacts — individually and as a group. This is where the PC's voice matters: the squad talks among themselves after the briefing

**Player Choices & Branches:**
- **If the PC advocates for using the exotic animals:** Adds momentum. Renard will weigh this alongside others' opinions.
- **If the PC advocates for waiting:** Adds caution. Tournon will note it. The waiting game has its own consequences — morale, readiness, the central office's patience.
- **If the PC focuses on the investigation (who's the buyer?):** Opens the secondary thread. Tournon may not want the investigation to complicate his timeline.

**Key Information to Reveal:**
- The donor process requires only a blood sample — the animals survive
- Daubray's training covered domesticated hounds exclusively; everything else is extrapolation
- The programme charter specifies hound donors; using anything else exceeds the camp's mandate
- Creuse still won't talk about the buyer

### Act 2: Decisions and Consequences

The squad and command must decide: wait, use the animals, or attempt a middle path. This act covers the decision-making process, any investigation into Creuse and the buyer, and the transformation itself if it proceeds.

**Key Events:**
- Donor allocation discussions — if exotic animals are approved, who gets what? Six people, seven potential donors (2 hounds, 2 wolves, 2 direwolves, 1 panther). The combinations matter.
- Creuse interrogation — the PC may participate or observe. Creuse has information but a price.
- Tournon's decision — he'll push for the exotic donors if resistance isn't strong enough to stop him. He'll frame it as initiative, not recklessness.
- The transformation sequence — if it proceeds, run each squad member's transformation with attention to the donor type. Hound transformations follow known parameters. Wolf, direwolf, and panther transformations are uncharted.

**Potential Opposition:**
- Daubray's professional caution — she'll comply but won't pretend she's confident
- Renard's protectiveness — he needs to believe it won't break his squad
- The unknown buyer — a background threat that may surface

**Donor Allocation Dynamics:**
If exotic donors are used, allocation becomes a charged decision. Considerations:
- The two existing hounds are the "safe" option — who gets them?
- Wolves are wild but at least canid — closer to the programme's design
- Direwolves are larger, more dangerous, and rarer — the instincts may be stronger
- The panther is the furthest outlier — feline, solitary predator, completely outside any framework
- Does the squad choose? Does Renard assign? Does Tournon decide? Does Daubray have input based on compatibility?
- The sex outcome rules apply: female donors always produce female results; male donors produce either. The panther is female. The wolves and direwolves — determine sex per the canon rules at the table.

**Escalation Mechanics:**
- The longer the squad waits, the more Tournon's patience frays and the more likely he is to make the call unilaterally
- Creuse's buyer may send someone to investigate what happened to their shipment
- Morale in the squad degrades with uncertainty — they were told to prepare for transformation, and now they're in limbo

### Act 3: Aftermath

The transformation is not the climax — it's the transition point. What the squad becomes and how they function as a transformed unit is the real story. Act 3 covers the immediate aftermath: adjustment, first patrols, emerging consequences.

**Possible Threads:**

**Adjustment Period**
- Hound-donor transformees have the programme's existing framework to draw on
- Wolf and direwolf transformees are navigating instincts the programme hasn't documented — pack behaviour, prey drive, territorial responses that are stronger and wilder than domesticated hound instincts
- A panther transformee (if applicable) is something entirely new — solitary predator instincts in a squad that relies on teamwork. How does that reconcile?
- Squad cohesion under pressure: do the different donor types create friction? Does the squad's pre-existing bond hold?

**First Operations**
- Tournon will push for early deployment to generate results for his reports
- The frontier doesn't care about adjustment periods — bandits, smugglers, and whatever else is out there will test the squad on the terrain's schedule, not theirs
- Performance differences between donor types will become apparent in the field

**The Buyer**
- The panther is still in camp. The buyer's investment is sitting in a cage.
- If someone comes looking, they'll find a frontier camp with an unexpectedly capable scout unit and a trail that leads back to their commissioned animal
- This thread can escalate from inquiry to confrontation depending on who the buyer is and how much they're willing to risk

## Complications & Twists

**Instinct Bleed:** Wolf and direwolf donors may produce stronger predatory instincts than hound donors. The programme's speaking collar helps manage some of this, but the collar was calibrated for domesticated hound behaviour. Adjustments may be needed. Symptoms: heightened prey response, territorial behaviour, difficulty with impulse control in high-stress situations. These are manageable but noticeable — and different squad members may experience different intensities.

**The Panther Problem:** If someone transforms using the panther as donor, they inherit feline instincts — solitary hunting preference, ambush predator behaviour, a different relationship with the squad dynamic. This isn't unmanageable, but it's a genuine challenge for a unit that relies on coordinated movement and mutual trust. The panther transformee may be the squad's most effective individual operator and its worst team player simultaneously.

**Creuse's Network:** Creuse isn't a solo operator. He has contacts along the frontier who helped him capture and transport these animals. If he's held long enough, someone in his network will notice. They may attempt a rescue, attempt to recover the animals, or simply cut ties and disappear — depending on who they are and what the buyer's instructions were.

**HQ Catches Up:** Eventually, the central office will learn what happened at Rochevaux. Their reaction depends on results. If the unit-transformation is a success, Tournon's initiative may be retroactively approved. If there are complications, he exceeded his mandate with no authorization and used seized evidence as programme resources. The political consequences could end his career — or make it.

**Settlement Reactions:** Theronne and Les Granges depend on the scout squad. When the squad returns from transformation as wolves, direwolves, and possibly a panther instead of hounds, the settlements' reaction matters. Fear, suspicion, or outright hostility from the people they protect would undermine the programme's purpose.

## Plot Hooks & Seeds

- **The Buyer's Identity:** Who commissioned the panther? Noble collector, private transformation practitioner, or something else? This thread can expand into its own investigation arc.
- **Programme Precedent:** If Rochevaux succeeds with exotic donors, does that change the programme? Other sites might request non-standard options. The central office might panic or adapt.
- **Frontier Expansion:** A successful transformed scout unit could justify expanding Rochevaux from an encampment to a proper outpost. Tournon's ambition and the squad's results drive this.
- **Warren Connections:** Grenn's transformation may draw attention from kobold communities — warren-born transformed into a predator form raises questions his people will want answers to.
- **Pack Dynamics:** If multiple squad members share a donor species (two wolves, two direwolves), do shared instincts create sub-groups within the squad? Does the unit fracture along donor lines?

## Rules & Tracking

Same lightweight framework as Vauclair and Maruvec campaigns.

**STATUS:**
- Health: Healthy / Injured (minor) / Injured (significant) / Incapacitated
- Hunger: Fed / Hungry / Famished
- Fatigue: Rested / Tired / Exhausted
- Morale: Stable / Shaken / Broken

**INVENTORY:** Simple list. Items have condition (Good / Worn / Damaged).

**REPUTATION (tracked separately):**
- Camp — how the garrison command sees you
- Squad — how your squadmates see you (starts high — established trust)
- Settlements — how Theronne and Les Granges see you
- Frontier — how the underworld and independent operators see you

Each starts at established levels (Squad: Trusted; Camp: Warm; Settlements: Warm; Frontier: Neutral).

**ADVANCEMENT:** No formal leveling. Skills develop through demonstrated use. Post-transformation abilities emerge through play.

**TRANSFORMATION TRACKING:**
After transformation, track:
- Donor type (hound / wolf / direwolf / panther)
- Instinct management (Controlled / Strained / Slipping)
- Physical adjustment (Adapted / Adjusting / Struggling)
- Collar calibration (Calibrated / Needs Adjustment / Uncalibrated)

## Character Creation

Before play begins, establish:

1. **RACE & BACKGROUND** — The PC's race, history, and how they ended up in the scout squad. They are an established member — not new, not senior, part of the unit's working rhythm.

2. **SQUAD RELATIONSHIPS** — How does the PC relate to Renard, Duval, Perret, Grenn, and Aubert? Who do they eat with? Who do they argue with? Who do they trust most?

3. **STANCE ON THE PROGRAMME** — Did the PC volunteer eagerly, accept pragmatically, or agree reluctantly? This shapes their approach to the donor question.

4. **DONOR PREFERENCE** — When the time comes, what does the PC want? One of the two hounds (safe, tested)? A wolf (wild but canid)? A direwolf (powerful, dangerous)? The panther (completely unknown)? Or do they defer to assignment?

## Tone

Gritty and grounded. The frontier is harsh, the stakes are real, and the programme's institutional optimism meets practical reality at ground level. The PC is not special — they're a competent scout going through something permanent alongside people they trust, in conditions nobody planned for, with resources nobody approved.

The social dynamics within the squad are the heart of the scenario. How six people who trust each other navigate a decision that changes all of them permanently, using donors that nobody vetted, under a commander who wants results more than caution — that's the story.
