Kennel Hounds — Maruvec Campaign
=================================

**Setting:** Maruvec, Cendrel — primary western port city
**Programme Type:** Major City Pilot (up to 10 sapient hounds)
**Player Character:** Isandar Quickclaw, kobold male, 19, 2-3 months in the watch
**Session Format:** Rules-light, narrative-driven. Adult content enabled.

---

Premise
-------

Commander Drouet received the programme charter four months ago. He read it, calculated that voluntary recruitment would take six to eight months he didn't have, and assigned a squad. Five watchmen — one kobold, three humans, one half-elf — were told they'd been selected for the sapient hound programme and given a week to process the information before orientation began.

Kennel Master Colette Varre ran orientation. She disclosed everything: what the programme entails, what the transformation produces, what is known and unknown about the long-term outcome, and the sex chromosome inheritance quirk documented in the alchemist's technical annex. She was precise and complete and did not soften any of it. She then moved on to operational planning.

The squad has been in the pre-transformation period for three weeks. They've been working around the kennel — learning the working hounds, observing operations, spending time with Varre. The transformation is coming. The donor hound selection hasn't been finalised. Isandar is the only one who's genuinely eager. The others are managing.

Player Character Notes
----------------------

**Isandar Quickclaw** — Kobold, 19, two months in the watch before the assignment. Grew up in The Chains, dock district. Parents are immigrants doing odd jobs. Enlisted for the steady income and structure. Pragmatic, thorough, genuinely curious about the programme. Has read what he could find about it. Has questions.

He's the only squad member who wanted this — or as close to wanted as anyone gets when they were assigned rather than asked. This is a point of minor friction with at least one squadmate who finds enthusiasm for a coerced assignment strange.

**Sable Venn** — Half-elf, 21, 14 months in the watch. Pragmatic, curious about the transformation in an almost academic way. Asking Varre careful questions. Has noticed something about Perrin.

Key NPCs
--------

**Colette Varre** — Kennel Master, hired for Maruvec specifically. Precise, politically aware, not warm. She's assessed the squad at three weeks and has opinions she hasn't voiced. Runs a tight kennel. Her operational hound complement is twelve working (non-sapient) animals.

**Silas Drouet** — Garrison Commander. Assigned the squad, watching for results. Practical, not cruel, not deeply invested in the programme's ideals — invested in one demonstrable result in the first operational year.

**The Alchemist** — Programme-contracted. Hasn't appeared yet. Will need to be developed when the transformation sequence begins.

**Dock District Contact** — Dix's undisclosed connection. To be developed as the campaign progresses.

**Maritime Guild Interest** — The guilds have stakes in what sapient hounds can find in a port city. Someone in the guild structure is paying attention. To be developed.

---

Tone
----

Port city gritty. The law is a tool people use, not a neutral force. The shadow economy is the port's second income and the garrison has made its peace with that. Sapient hounds with genuine investigative capability are going to be interesting to people who have things they'd rather not be found. Adult content enabled. Wounds linger. Permanent decisions are permanent.

The squad was assigned, not asked. That's a real grievance and the campaign honours it. The question of what it means to transform because you were told to — versus because you chose to — is present from the start and runs under everything.

Act Structure
=============

ACT 1 — ORIENTATION AND SELECTION
----------------------------------

The campaign opens in the pre-transformation period. The squad has been at this for three weeks. They know each other the way people know each other when they've been put in a room together — functional familiarity, the beginning of opinions. The transformation hasn't happened. The donor hound hasn't been chosen.

**The Donor Hound Selection**
This is the first major player decision and it is permanent. Varre has twelve working hounds in the kennel. Each has a personality, a working history, a place in the pack hierarchy. Isandar must choose one as his template.

This choice shapes some post-transformation traits — not cognition, not identity, but physical tendency (size, build, coat, the particular way a specific animal approaches problems). The player should spend real time in the kennel before deciding. Varre will answer questions about each animal if asked. She won't advise — she considers it his decision to make.

Each hound should be developed as a distinct individual. The player is essentially choosing what physical chapter of themselves they'll inhabit.

**Pre-Transformation Threads:**
- Squad dynamics. Who is Isandar to each of them at this point? What's forming?
- Théo's grievance — is it aimed at the programme, at the garrison, at Varre, at Drouet? Does Isandar engage with it?
- Perrin's quiet fear — is it visible yet? Does anyone notice?
- Dix's ease — does anything make it slip?
- The city, outside the garrison: Isandar's family in The Chains, the dock district, what Maruvec looks like from a recruiter's posting.

**Session Opening Point:**
The campaign begins with Isandar in the kennel yard, morning, watching the working hounds run. It has been three weeks. He knows the animals well enough to have preferences forming. The rest of the squad is somewhere nearby — Théo pointedly elsewhere, Sable asking questions somewhere, Perrin doing something useful, Dix in easy conversation with a kennel handler.

Varre will come to him at some point this morning. She's going to tell him the transformation is scheduled for the end of the week. The donor decision needs to be made.

---

ACT 2 — TRANSFORMATION AND INTEGRATION
----------------------------------------

The transformation sequence. The physical experience of becoming a hound. Recovery. Adjustment.

**The Transformation Itself**
The alchemist administers the potion. The process takes several hours. The experience is not clean — documented accounts (what's available of them) describe it as disorienting, physically intense, and nothing like unconsciousness. The player should experience this fully.

**Recovery Period**
A week, roughly. Learning to move in the new form. The speaking collar — how it works, how it feels, how it changes what communication is. Food, water, rest — the practical realities of a body that is different.

**The Sex Chromosome Quirk**
Varre disclosed this at orientation. The alchemist documented it. It may or may not be relevant to Isandar specifically — the outcome depends on the donor hound's genetics, which aren't fully predictable from inspection. The squad should learn their outcomes during or shortly after the recovery period.

**Pack Dynamics**
The working kennel hounds have a pack structure. Sapient or not, the transformation produces animals that the pack responds to as animals. Integration is not optional — the working hounds need to establish where the new sapient hounds fit. This is social, physical, and occasionally tense. Varre manages it deliberately but cannot fully control it.

**Squad Integration**
Five people in new forms, adjusting simultaneously, with their prior personalities and prior relationships and prior grievances all intact. How does Théo's anger translate? How does Perrin's fear manifest in an animal form that can't easily hide stress responses? How does Dix's social ease work when she's a hound? What does Sable discover about her own question — does it look different from where she is now?

**Operational Prep**
Before the squad goes out, Varre runs them through a structured preparation period. Commands, protocols, how they interface with the non-sapient kennel hounds, how they interface with the human officers they'll work alongside. Practical and unglamorous.

---

ACT 3 — THE JOB
----------------

The squad enters operational rotation. Maruvec is a port city with a shadow economy. Sapient hounds in a garrison context are going to find things.

**Early Duties**
Dock patrol, cargo inspection support, following up on contraband tips. Learning what the city smells like. Learning what's worth flagging and what the garrison has decided to tolerate. Learning the difference — which is significant and not always explicitly communicated.

**The Maritime Guild Thread**
The guilds have stakes in what sapient hounds can do. A guild investigator has been asking quiet questions about the programme since orientation week. This escalates based on what the hounds find in their early operational period.

**Dix's Connection**
The dock district relationships she hasn't disclosed start to produce information — or start to become relevant to things the squad encounters. The question of what she's been doing in the watch, and why she was easy about the assignment, starts to have a shape.

**Perrin's Reckoning**
Something operational puts Perrin in a position where the fear he's been carrying can no longer be fully contained. Maret gets involved — directly or by information reaching her through a channel he didn't anticipate.

**The Political Layer**
Drouet's "strategic tolerance" with the shadow economy is not static. Something the hounds find or don't find starts to put pressure on the arrangement. This doesn't require an immediate climax — it's background pressure that builds.

Transformation System
---------------------

Track the following for Isandar post-transformation:

**STATUS:**
- Health: Healthy / Injured (minor) / Injured (significant) / Incapacitated
- Hunger: Fed / Hungry / Famished
- Fatigue: Rested / Tired / Exhausted
- Morale: Stable / Shaken / Broken

**SPEAKING COLLAR:** Functional / Damaged / Lost
(Loss or damage significantly limits operational communication. Collar is personal property — garrison-issued initially, but it's Isandar's.)

**INVENTORY:** Simple list. Items have condition (Good / Worn / Damaged).
(Inventory changes significantly post-transformation. What a hound carries is different from what a watchman carries.)

**REPUTATION:**
- Garrison — Neutral (start). Range: Hostile / Cold / Neutral / Warm / Trusted
- Street (Chains) — Neutral-Warm (start). This may shift post-transformation.
- Dock District — Neutral (start)
- Maritime Guilds — Neutral (start, changes with operational results)
