# Camp Rochevaux — Session 3 Checkpoint

**In-Game Date:** Day after Session 2 — squad transformation day. All five complete by late afternoon.
**PC:** Rill Sunclaw, kobold scout, panther form. Collar fitted, voice working. Transformation complete since previous session.

## Where We Are

Rill is at the mess hall with the full squad — all six transformed, trying to figure out how to sit at their usual table for the first time in their new bodies. benches and chairs doent work so well anymore. Evening. The cook has something on the fire. Tomorrow Tournon will want a report. Tonight is the first night of figuring out what they've become.

## What Happened

- Rill woke before first light, stretched into the new form (longer spine, tail, jaw), headed out. Found Renard at the well — composed, settled, waiting for the morning to begin.
- Rill hunted two rabbits in the forest: ate one in the field (whole, fur included — tasted good), brought one back for the panther.
- Fed the panther at the enclosure. Brief greeting exchange — low chest sound, ear flick in response. She settled.
- Found Duval at the wolf enclosure; brief exchange. Went to mess, found Grenn. Brief exchange — Grenn confirmed ready in the way that isn't the same as feeling ready.
- Rill spent the day in Daubray's shed sitting with each squad member through their transformation.
- **Duval** — grey wolf, first. Rill guided through pressure and sensory stages. Talked him through: pressure moves, hearing comes in layers, it's not wrong. Duval identified Grenn's footfall at the enclosure by ear alone afterward. Recovery clean.
- **Renard** — grey wolf, second. Contained, deliberate, no narration. One hand-tighten mid-process, that was all. Came out broader — wolf proportions suited to leading from front. Immediately noted night vision and scent range have operational implications.
- **Grenn** — direwolf, midday. Hit a resistance wall mid-process — holding back against the instinct wave. Rill told him to stop resisting, receive it instead. He let go. Came out large, low, ancient-looking. Afterward: said he understood the warren word for knowing where your people are without looking differently now.
- **Aubert** — direwolf, afternoon. Most straightforward of the day. Drank the vial like a field ration, went through it with blunt endurance, stood up, walked to the door, said hm, filed it, moved on.
- **Perret** — direwolf, late afternoon. Spent most of the day at the enclosure and stables waiting to be ready. Most vocal through the hard hours — small sounds, a confused laugh when he first heard the panther breathing from inside the shed. Came out the form settling over someone still growing into himself. First words: Hi Rill. Genuine and slightly overwhelmed.
- Daubray sat when it was done — six transformations, all stable, all collared. Looked at the empty vials. Said: Well. That's that, then. Visibly tired and relieved.
- Rill gathered the squad to the mess. First full assembly as transformed unit.
- Key exchanges: Aubert and Grenn confirmed the direwolf room-feels-smaller experience. Perret found relief sitting with his back to the wall — Duval told him not to be embarrassed, everyone turned. Renard set group protocol: something's wrong, you say it when it's happening. Rill confirmed beds will be bad, instincts arrive before you do, much still unknown. Renard: eat, then we sleep badly and figure out the rest tomorrow.

## GM Notes — Feral Forms (Priority Thread for Next Sessions)

All six squad members now have permanent animal bodies. This is largely unexplored territory in the campaign. Key questions to surface through play:

they are quadrupedal, and dont have hands.. standard equipment cant be used anymore

**Equipment and weapons:** How do they hold a bow, a sword, a knife? What gear still works, what needs adaptation or replacement? Does Rill draw a bow with paws at all, or is the panther's natural capability the replacement?

**Tasks:** Writing reports, operating latches and doors, cooking, handling small objects — what requires conscious workaround vs. what the form handles?

**Sleep:** Beds are wrong for all of them. Circling instinct (Rill confirmed), different heat retention and body shape, different sleep postures. May need ground bedding or modifications.

**Food:** Raw or barely-cooked registers better. Does the mess accommodate? Do they begin hunting for themselves, supplementing rations, both?

**Patrol and operations in civilian areas:** Night vision, scent tracking, speed — but how does a squad of large predators move through Theronne and Les Granges without causing panic? First civilian contact is a significant pending beat.

**Direwolf pack sub-dynamic:** Grenn, Aubert, Perret share donor species. Shared instincts may produce sub-grouping within the squad. Watch for friction or unexpected cohesion along donor lines.

**Panther isolation instinct:** Rill is the only feline in a squad built on coordinated movement. Solitary predator instincts vs. squad function — best individual operator, potentially hardest to keep in formation under instinct pressure.

**Speaking collars:** All fitted and working. But non-verbal communication — posture, ear position, scent, proximity — has shifted for all of them. The squad communicates differently now whether they notice it or not.
