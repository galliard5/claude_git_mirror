Nobles Commission — Session 01
================================

A Noble's Commission

SESSION OVERVIEW
----------------
Lady Seria Rennic, diplomat of House Rennic and a returning client, arrived at Isalia's Manor with her hunting hound, Vesper, requesting another breeding session. What began as a routine commission became something else entirely when Isalia identified Vesper as a transformed human rather than a natural animal — and asked for his consent before proceeding. By the end of the day, Vesper had a speaking collar, a name, and a kennel with a door he could close himself.

---

THE CONSULTATION
----------------
Seria's arrival was handled with the practiced composure of a returning client who knew the manor's protocols. She asked specifically for Isalia. In the consultation room, Vesper was immediately notable: deep charcoal coat, pale gold eyes, and a stillness that no natural hound produces. He tracked faces, not movement. His breathing didn't change when Isalia approached. He was stationed at Seria's feet, not cowering.

Isalia's examination confirmed what her instincts said: proportions subtly wrong for natural breeding, no brands or registration scars anywhere, and the quality of attention in those pale gold eyes was recognition, not animal wariness. She named it plainly — *transformed, or shapeshifter* — and Seria deflected without denying it.

When Isalia asked for his consent directly, Seria's composure fractured. She had not asked him. In two years, she had not asked him.

Vesper nodded. Once. Deliberately.

---

THE SPEAKING COLLAR
-------------------
Isalia commissioned a speaking collar from Elowen before the session proceeded. Seria paid for it without argument — the first thing she paid for that had nothing to do with her own purposes.

Vesper received the collar mid-afternoon, after the first breeding sessions. He was silent for a long moment after it was fitted. Then:

*"Thank you."*

His first words in two years. Directed at Isalia.

What followed was a quiet confrontation. Seria sat across from a man she had owned for two years and heard, for the first time, that he had not consented to anything she had arranged for him. She had acquired him through legal channels. She had not asked his name. She had not asked what he wanted. She had simply proceeded.

Vesper did not shout. He was precise. Seria received it without defending herself.

---

THE BREEDING SESSIONS
---------------------
Multiple sessions occurred throughout the day, all consensual. The encounters were intimate rather than clinical — Vesper had been held in sustained tension for two years, and what surfaced was as much grief as anything else. Isalia kept her bearing throughout and made no performance of the significance she understood it to carry.

Conception is likely. Isalia took a fertility tonic from Alric that evening — one dose, with a second set aside for morning — to ensure it took and to ease the day's accumulated soreness.

---

LEGAL REVIEW
------------
Isalia's staff conducted an initial review of Vesper's documentation during the day's sessions. The finding was significant: no proper penal contract was located. If confirmed, Valerius has full legal standing as a person rather than property.

Implications:
- The breeding contract is void retroactively — lack of consent invalidates it
- The pups, when born, belong to Valerius
- Seria's fee was reduced to cover Valerius's initial stay at the manor
- Seria is required to return with full documentation of the original sentence

Seria accepted all of this. She left with guilt that showed clearly despite her diplomat's composure, and with a stated intention to return with records.

---

COMMUNITY INTRODUCTIONS
------------------------
Before the day ended, Valerius was introduced to three manor residents:

**Anthony** — Transformed pony, Imperial Witness. Valerius recognized what Anthony carried without needing it explained. Two people who have lived inside Imperial legal machinery, in entirely different ways.

**Renn** — Transformed direwolf, ranger, the manor's night patrol. Offered Valerius patrol work. They will begin routes together at dawn.

**Merra** — Transformed hound, kept on manor contract as milkmaid and surrogate mother for pups requiring a wet nurse or maternal handler. One of a small contract group: Merra and one other transformed canine hold these roles alongside a pair of transformed mares in equivalent positions. All are trained in raising feral pups. Merra's particular warmth was evident in how she received him.

---

END OF DAY
----------
Isalia completed estate business: a visit to Alric for the fertility tonic, routine checks with night staff, sign-offs on supplies and schedules. The manor was dark before she reached her quarters.

The Tantalus' Tease hummed its constant background presence — easier to ignore when thoroughly tired.

Valerius slept in his own kennel for the first time. The door was one he could close.

OPEN THREADS
------------
1. **Seria's Documentation** — She must return with the full record of Valerius's sentence. Whether a proper penal contract exists determines his legal standing entirely.
2. **Valerius's Legal Status** — If the sentence was improperly filed, he has full rights as a person and two years of unlawful detention to account for.
3. **The Pups** — Valerius retains claim. He may raise them, place them, or make other arrangements. That decision belongs to him.
4. **Valerius's Integration** — Patrol work with Renn begins immediately. His long-term role at the manor is undefined beyond that.
5. **Seria's Character** — She was confronted with her failure clearly and did not argue it. Whether that guilt produces change, or fades back into the habits of a diplomat, is an open question.
