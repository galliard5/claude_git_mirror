Viktor Steinfeld — Session 02 (Prep)
====================================

Day 3 and Beyond


RECAP — SESSION 01
------------------
Viktor Steinfeld — silver-grey mare, confirmed pregnant by the Greymarch dark bay, sired at Isalia's Manor — returned to the Steinfeld estate on foot with Stablemaster Hannah Voss over two days. Stopped at the Bruck farm en route; Edric opened the gate, flagged Kern's roof and Markel's well unprompted, gave every indication of cautious alignment. Tomis Bruck watched throughout.

Arrived to find his cousin Lukas had been holding the estate steady — and had pulled the ledgers on Head Steward Oswin Brandt. Three years of embezzlement, fifty-one gold confirmed across three fictitious contractors (Brenn and Sons, Hartwell Works, falsified Brennmark completion), laundered through textile merchants Hauss and Pol via a financier named Aldus Corvel operating out of Merchant's Close above Vander's Currency Exchange in Silberbach.

Viktor walked Oswin through the morning tour, let him build his story, then asked about the work orders by date. Oswin folded. Signed a ten-page statement — full network, methods, client list. The client list included Hauss, Pol, Weavers' Guild Master Sorren, and House Aldenberg — Aldenberg's figure larger than all others combined. Corvel had told Oswin his arrangements were "protected at levels that made constabulary involvement inadvisable."

Viktor moved on four fronts that afternoon:
- **Legal:** Pell rode for Silberbach with a sealed letter summoning independent solicitors Voss and Kraemer.
- **Constable:** Second letter to Constable Ferris, directing him to move on Vander's before Corvel could relocate his strongbox.
- **Kreiger:** Hannah departed for Isalia's Manor carrying legitimate business (Viktor's foal provenance letter) and a verbal message for Duke Romaine via Isalia about the Aldenberg connection. Aldenberg supplies Valtor's weapons; Valtor is the most powerful house in the valley. Ducal territory.
- **Oswin:** Confined to estate grounds. Cooperating fully. Viktor told him there may be a third option beyond prison and freedom — privately, he's thinking of Isalia's manor as the option that removes Oswin from Corvel's reach entirely.

Aldren — the existing Steinfeld solicitor — remains on retainer and unaware. His negligence in not flagging the fictitious contractors over three years of annual accounts puts his position under review. A routine meeting is scheduled for Day 3 morning to extract his institutional knowledge before any formal action.

Estate side: Mrs. Aldenmarch had the converted stall ready before Viktor arrived. Pell — seventeen, sharp, discreet — proved his worth on the courier ride. Lukas is steady, numbers-sharp, fully trusted. Tenant repairs authorized (Druss to Bruck and Markel, Kellerman to east pasture, stablehands to Kern).

ALDREN MEETING — PREP NOTES
---------------------------
**Aldren's posture:** Older man, comfortable in the role, has known the Steinfeld family for decades. Will assume Viktor's return is a courtesy meeting — orientation to the estate's legal standing, settling-in formalities, perhaps a discussion of whether anything new is needed given the transformation.

**What Viktor wants to extract:**
- Standing contracts with Silberbach merchants (Hauss, Pol — names Aldren may casually drop)
- Aldenberg-Steinfeld history: any prior legal interaction, any standing arrangements
- Brennmark contract terms — Aldren's understanding of completion criteria
- Aldren's relationship to Vander's Currency Exchange (does he have his own accounts there?)
- General gossip Aldren may volunteer about Silberbach noble-house dynamics

**What Viktor must conceal:**
- That Voss & Kraemer have been summoned
- That Oswin has confessed
- That Aldren himself is under review
- That Corvel is named, targeted, or known

**Aldren's tells if he gets suspicious:**
- Asks why Pell is absent (Pell normally present for estate meetings)
- Asks why Hannah is absent (same)
- Asks pointed questions about specific contractor names from the ledgers
- Volunteers explanations for the fictitious contractors before being asked

**Branch — Aldren is clean (probably negligent only):**
He answers freely, doesn't notice anything off, leaves on schedule. Viktor has the institutional knowledge and a clean read on Aldren's exposure. V&K can move on him cleanly when they arrive.

**Branch — Aldren is complicit:**
He pivots when work orders by date come up. Tries to leave early. Asks about Pell. This changes the calculus — Aldren cannot be allowed to ride back to Silberbach unsupervised today. Viktor may need to invent a reason to keep him on the estate until V&K arrive. Difficult — Aldren will resist obvious detention.

**Branch — Aldren knows something is happening but isn't part of it:**
He's heard rumors in Silberbach. Asks about the constable's letter or about Pell's overnight ride. Doesn't know specifics but knows enough to be uncomfortable. Viktor can either close him out (he leaves confused, V&K can still take him) or bring him partially in (risk).

Default play: assume **clean / negligent** unless Viktor presses in ways that change the read.


PELL'S RETURN — BRANCHED OUTCOMES
---------------------------------
Three readiness states for what happened in Silberbach. Pick at the table based on what serves the story — or roll if you want it random.

**Branch A — Ferris struck in time.**
Constable went that night with two deputies. Forced the room above Vander's at dawn. Strongbox seized. Corvel was at the inn, not at the exchange — taken without struggle. Both held in Silberbach gaol pending investigation. Strongbox contents not yet opened (awaiting V&K or magistrate). Pell brings written confirmation from Ferris.
- *Consequence:* Aldenberg has hours, not days. The Corvel network is decapitated. V&K's job is now decryption and prosecution rather than recovery.
- *Pacing note:* Closes the central thread cleanly. Best if you want Session 02-03 to pivot toward Aldenberg politics rather than fugitive logistics.

**Branch B — Ferris struck late.**
Constable arrived to find the room above Vander's already rifled. Strongbox is there but the key client ledgers are absent. Corvel is missing. Vander is held for questioning but pleads ignorance of the morning's events. Ferris's letter is apologetic and detailed — he moved as fast as he could; someone was faster. Pell may have seen riders going north on the road outside Silberbach the morning he arrived.
- *Consequence:* Corvel is on the road. Whoever tipped him is the live question. Aldren remains a candidate. So does someone at Vander's. So does a Steinfeld estate hand — least likely but not impossible.
- *Pacing note:* Best for sustained tension. Forces Viktor to ask who else is compromised.

**Branch C — Ferris didn't strike.**
Letter from Ferris explaining caution: Vander's Currency Exchange holds its license under House Aldenberg's bond. Ferris doesn't have constabulary authority to enter without a ducal warrant or a sealed magistrate's order. Asks Viktor to provide higher authorization or wait for Voss & Kraemer to handle the warrant process through proper channels. Ferris is not compromised — he is correct on procedure.
- *Consequence:* Slowed timeline. Strongbox still intact but the network has time to coordinate. Ferris is now a reliable ally but a procedurally constrained one. V&K's first job becomes warrant procurement, not interrogation.
- *Pacing note:* The most legally interesting branch. Forces Viktor through proper channels and gives V&K a real role. Recommended if you want this campaign to lean into institutional process.

**GM recommendation:** B or C are the more interesting branches. A wraps the central thread too cleanly for Session 02. C is the cleanest match to the worldbuilding (Aldenberg's reach justifying procedural caution).


VOSS & KRAEMER ARRIVAL — PREP NOTES
-----------------------------------
**Firm reputation:** Independent solicitors based in Silberbach. Senior partners Voss (mid-50s, methodical, ex-circuit magistrate) and Kraemer (40s, sharper-edged, specializes in noble-house exposure work). Take cases on merit; will not accept retainer from Aldenberg or anyone connected. Discretion absolute.

**What they want first:**
1. Oswin's statement and the ledger evidence
2. The Brennmark contract file
3. Aldren's file — every document Aldren has touched in three years
4. The constable's response from Pell
5. A formal interview with Viktor about the scope of what he wants pursued

**Their working assumption:** Viktor wants the criminal exposure (Corvel, network, Aldenberg) AND the institutional cleanup (Aldren, Oswin's fate, contractor reform). They'll structure their work accordingly unless told otherwise.

**Branch A — Both senior partners arrive Day 3 evening.**
Fast and confident. Want to interview Oswin tonight, review files in the morning, meet Aldren by appointment the following afternoon — no warning to Aldren. Move within 48 hours.

**Branch B — Senior partner plus junior, arrive Day 4 morning.**
Voss is delayed in Silberbach on another matter (possibly already aware of the Aldenberg involvement and walking carefully). Kraemer arrives with a competent junior. Kraemer wants to lay groundwork — review files, interview Oswin — and have Voss available before confronting Aldren. Buys two extra days. Aldren has a window to act if he's not clean.

**Branch C — Arrival plus prior knowledge.**
Whichever partners arrive bring an unexpected detail: in the past week, their firm received a discreet inquiry from an unnamed Aldenberg agent making preliminary questions about Viktor's transformation status and the Steinfeld succession. They turned it away. They don't know whether it's coincidence with the current matter or related — but Viktor needs to know they're being watched at the firm level.
- *Combines with A or B.* Use C as a flavor reveal during the first interview, not as a standalone branch.

**GM recommendation:** B is the strongest narrative fit — gives Aldren a window to maneuver, gives Viktor real choices about timing. Combine with C for added pressure.


HANNAH'S RETURN — PREP NOTES
----------------------------
**Expected arrival:** Day 4 evening or Day 5 morning. Hannah is steady, will ride straight through if the news warrants.

**What she carries (guaranteed):**
- Isalia's provenance letter for the Greymarch foal — clean, sealed, in proper form for Meerhold's inspection.
- A short note from Isalia to Viktor — personal, brief, asking after his health and the pregnancy.

**Branch A — Duke Romaine moves.**
Hannah delivered the verbal message to Isalia, who arranged a meeting with the Duke within hours. Romaine received the Aldenberg information with composure, asked careful questions, dispatched an inquiry through his own channels before Hannah left. He asks Viktor to send any further intelligence to Kreiger Manor directly — not through normal couriers. Asks for discretion until he can move the pieces. Isalia also raised Oswin's transformation possibility, unprompted: she'd take him. Conditions are: full disclosure, signed contract, Steinfeld covers transport, post-confession pre-trial timing is ideal for converting his testimony into protected residency.

**Branch B — Romaine cautious; Isalia carries.**
Romaine listened, asked little, said less. Isalia met with Hannah privately afterward — said the Duke is weighing his options. Aldenberg arms Valtor; Valtor is the regional power. Romaine will not act without legal grounding. Isalia's read: consolidate evidence, wait for V&K to provide that grounding, then return to the Duke with a prosecutable file. Until then, do nothing visible. Isalia raises Oswin's third option herself, same conditions as Branch A.

**Branch C — Manor side complicates.**
Hannah brings the provenance letter, but the Aldenberg conversation got bumped. Romaine was absent when Hannah arrived; she had to leave word for him. Meanwhile at the manor: Judge Kinsky has gone into labor, OR Sergovy Waldheim has just arrived at the gates and is being processed, OR someone known to House Steinfeld appeared at the manor as a client. Hannah brings news of whichever complication is most useful. Aldenberg matter is still pending Romaine's return.
- *Combines with B.* If you want the duke's response delayed by manor chaos, use B + C.

**GM recommendation:** B + Isalia's Oswin offer is the strongest single beat. It forces Viktor's hand on the Oswin question and sets up an Isalia-Viktor side conversation by letter or visit.

DECISION POINTS FOR VIKTOR
--------------------------
1. **Aldren — what to reveal, when.** Today's meeting is extraction-only. After V&K arrive, Viktor decides whether Aldren is confronted, dismissed, or prosecuted.
2. **Oswin — third option timing.** Isalia's offer (if Branch A or B on Hannah) lands Day 4-5. Voss & Kraemer may want Oswin's testimony preserved before he transforms. Order matters.
3. **Aldenberg aggression level.** How fast does Viktor want this to move? Pushing fast risks Aldenberg moving first. Holding back risks Aldenberg consolidating. Pell's branch and Hannah's branch will shape this.
4. **Information control.** Who at the estate knows what? Lukas knows everything. Hannah knows everything. Pell knows the courier mission only. Mrs. Aldenmarch knows nothing operational. Tenants know nothing. Maintaining this gets harder as V&K work the case.
5. **Storm's arrival.** When Mira arrives, Viktor will have to host an Aldenberg-name resident on the estate during an active Aldenberg investigation. Decision deferred unless Hannah brings news of imminent arrival.


LURKING COMPLICATIONS
---------------------
- **Corvel may have fled.** Branch B on Pell's return makes this active.
- **Aldren may not be salvageable.** If he reads the room during the morning meeting, his next ride to Silberbach is dangerous.
- **News spreads.** Viktor's transformation and pregnancy will reach Silberbach society within days, regardless of the investigation. Anything that draws attention to the estate accelerates this.
- **Aldenberg's response time.** Even if Ferris struck in time (Branch A on Pell), Aldenberg will hear within 24-48 hours. Their move shapes Sessions 03+.
- **Judge Kinsky's birth.** Mid-April to early May. If she goes into labor during Session 02, Isalia leaves the manor immediately and Hannah may carry that news instead of the Duke's.
- **Storm / Mira's arrival.** Not scheduled but possible. If she arrives during the active Aldenberg investigation, the manor side becomes a problem before Viktor is ready.
