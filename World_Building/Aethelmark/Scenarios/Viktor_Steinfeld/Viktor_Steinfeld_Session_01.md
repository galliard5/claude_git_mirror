Viktor Steinfeld — Session 01
==============================

The Return Home

SESSION OVERVIEW
----------------
Viktor Steinfeld departed Isalia's Manor the morning after his pregnancy was confirmed — a silver-grey mare in Steinfeld's heraldic colors, foal sired by the larger of the estate's Greymarch pair. He made the journey home on foot with Stablemaster Hannah Voss, who had ridden up to escort him back. The trip took the better part of two days and included a first meeting with tenant farmer Edric Bruck. Viktor arrived at the estate to find his cousin Lukas had been holding everything carefully — and had discovered something that couldn't wait.

THE ROAD HOME
-------------
The journey south was productive. Hannah briefed Viktor on the full state of the estate: the Greymarch pair ready for Meerhold's inspection in three weeks, the east pasture needing re-leveling, fourteen items of deferred maintenance in the steward's queue. Viktor made several decisions on the road — Kellerman's crew for the east pasture, Druss for tenant farm drainage and well repairs, stablehands dispatched to Widow Kern's roof, a monthly tenant runner established.

Viktor also placed himself on the estate's breeding contractor schedule at his own request, natural breeding going forward once recovered from the pregnancy. Hannah accepted this without ceremony and filed it accordingly.

Morning sickness arrived on schedule. Hannah had biscuits ready. She had already spoken to the farrier.

BRUCK FARM
----------
Viktor stopped at the Bruck farm on the road south. Edric Bruck had said he'd believe the news when he saw it — and then opened his gate.

The visit covered the north pasture drainage problem (three years deferred, now authorized for Druss), the Kern widow's roof, and Markel's well casing. Bruck flagged both tenant problems unprompted, including the practical note that a woman should go with the stablehands to Kern's farm. He offered midday meal and Viktor accepted. By the time they left, Bruck had given every indication of alignment — cautious, earned, and worth having.

Tomis Bruck was present throughout, quiet and attentive. Worth noting.

ARRIVAL AND THE LEDGERS
-----------------------
Lukas met Viktor in the yard. The study was locked, the ledgers stacked neatly, Aldren's letter on top. Over a working dinner — Hannah, Lukas, Viktor, and a newly appointed groom and runner named Pell — the full picture emerged.

Three years of embezzlement by Head Steward Oswin Brandt. Fifty-one gold confirmed across three fictitious contractors: Brenn and Sons, Hartwell Works, and a falsified completion record for the genuine Brennmark crew. Funds laundered through textile merchants Hauss and Pol via a financier named Aldus Corvel, operating out of Merchant's Close in Silberbach above Vander's Currency Exchange.

Pell contributed a detail no one else had: Corvel met Oswin at the village inn monthly, arriving with full saddlebags and leaving with lighter ones. The last meeting was ten days before Viktor's arrival — the same day Lukas first pulled the ledgers. Oswin had seen Lukas with the records and sent for Corvel immediately.

Pell rode for Silberbach that night with a letter for independent solicitors Voss and Kraemer, sealed under Lukas's authority.

OSWIN'S CONFESSION
------------------
The following morning Viktor walked the estate with Oswin on the normal tour. He let Oswin build his story — the warmth, the prepared estate report, the Brennmark delays. Then he asked about the work orders by date.

Oswin ran out of road.

In the steward's office, with Lukas and Hannah present, he gave a full confession. Fifty-one gold. Three years. Corvel's identity, address, methods. The client list he had glimpsed: merchants Hauss and Pol, Weavers' Guild Master Sorren, and — most significantly — House Aldenberg. The figure beside Aldenberg's name was larger than all others combined.

Corvel had told Oswin at their last meeting that his arrangements were protected "at levels that made constabulary involvement inadvisable."

Oswin signed a ten-page written statement covering everything.

THE RESPONSE
------------
Viktor moved on four fronts simultaneously:

LEGAL: Voss and Kraemer (independent solicitors) summoned by Pell's night ride. Expected within days.

CONSTABLE: A second letter to Constable Ferris in Silberbach, directing him to move on Vander's Currency Exchange before Corvel could relocate his strongbox. The strongbox — brown leather, brass fittings — contains individual client ledgers on everyone in the network.

KREIGER: Hannah departed for Isalia's Manor the same afternoon carrying legitimate business (provenance letter for Viktor's foal, breeding schedule matters) and a verbal message for Isalia about the Aldenberg connection and its potential implications for regional stability. Given that Aldenberg supplies Valtor's weapons and Valtor is the most powerful house in the valley, Viktor and Lukas agreed this was ducal territory — the kind of information that needed to reach Duke Romaine through a trusted channel rather than in writing.

OSWIN: Confined to the estate grounds. Viktor told him there may be a third option beyond prison and freedom, depending on how the Corvel situation resolved. Oswin is cooperating fully. Viktor noted privately that Isalia's manor could provide that third option — a transformation that removes Oswin from Corvel's reach entirely.

The existing solicitor Aldren remains on retainer and unaware of the investigation. His negligence in not flagging the fictitious contractors — despite reviewing the annual accounts where they appeared — puts his position under review. Voss and Kraemer will assess his exposure independently. Viktor scheduled a routine meeting with Aldren for the following morning to extract his institutional knowledge before any formal action.

HOUSEHOLD
---------
Mrs. Aldenmarch had prepared for Viktor's arrival before he got there — door measurements taken, stable stall fitted out, a recipe sourced for oats with apple. She received Viktor's working arrangements without drama and without excess sentiment. The conversation in the kitchen that first evening settled something between them that didn't need naming.

Viktor is sleeping in the converted stable stall pending construction of a proper attached office and stable. The second floor remains unmodified. Ground floor is being repurposed as formal reception space.
