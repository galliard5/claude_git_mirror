# Bandit Recipe — Scenario Prompt

**Setting:** Kennel Hounds / Cendrel
**Type:** Campaign prompt — to be fleshed out before play
**Tone:** Dark — programme's worst-case scenario realized

---

## Core Concept

A courier carrying programme materials — transformation recipe, supporting notes, sample compounds, and an initial supply of speaking collars — is ambushed and captured by bandits on a remote road. Taken back to their camp.

During the capture, most of the documentation is destroyed — burned, scattered, water-damaged. What survives intact is the recipe itself.

The bandit camp has an alchemist. Not programme-trained, but competent enough to follow a recipe. They brew a batch. They need a test subject.

The courier is right there.

**The test:** Either a female courier with a female animal, or a male courier with a male animal — sex of courier and donor match. The transformation works exactly as expected. The bandits now have proof of concept and confidence in the recipe.

**What they don't know:** The destroyed notes contained the sex-chromosome mechanic — that the donor animal's chromosomes determine the outcome, not the volunteer's. Their single successful test happened to match, reinforcing the obvious assumption that what goes in is what comes out. They have no reason to suspect otherwise.

---

## The Courier's Situation

Transformed. No collar. No voice. No way to identify themselves as human to anyone who encounters them. From the bandits' perspective, the test worked — the courier is proof, not a person. They're in a cage or a pen somewhere in the camp, and they stay there.

Nobody is coming to look for them quickly. Courier routes through bandit territory go cold regularly. The programme may not realize materials are missing for days or weeks. By then, the bandits have had time to act.

---

## The Bandits' Operation

The courier was carrying an initial supply of speaking collars alongside the recipe and documentation. The bandits now have both the transformation recipe and functional collars ready to use.

The boss is smart. They don't force anyone. They offer:
- A speaking collar (from the captured supply — finite, and the bandits can't make more)
- Payment
- Volunteer basis only

This builds loyalty rather than resentment. Volunteers get a predator's body with a human mind — faster, stronger, harder to catch. Patrols expecting human bandits are not prepared for something with tactical intelligence and animal capabilities.

The boss sees this as a force multiplier. They're right.

The collar supply is finite. When they run out, new volunteers go without a voice — which changes the calculus of volunteering significantly. Unless someone finds a source for more collars, or figures out how to commission them without revealing why they need them.

---

## The Sex Mechanic Time Bomb

The transformation uses the donor animal's chromosomes as the template. The mechanic works like this:
- A **female donor** produces a **female result. Always.** No exceptions. The donor only contributes XX.
- A **male donor** produces **either male or female.** The distribution appears random — XY contribution resolves unpredictably.

The volunteer's own sex before transformation has no bearing on the outcome. A male bandit using a female wolf will always become female. A male bandit using a male wolf might still become female.

The bandits know none of this. Their single test matched expectations — same sex in, same sex out — and reinforced the obvious assumption. They have no reason to suspect otherwise.

The first mismatch will be confusing. But the pattern they'll eventually notice — if they pay attention — won't make sense either:
- Female donors always produce females. They might figure that one out if they track it.
- Male donors are a coin flip. That looks random. The alchemist can't control it, can't predict it, can't explain it.

There is **no combination that guarantees a male outcome.** Male donors are the best odds, but never certain. For a bandit outfit that skews heavily male, this is a serious recruitment problem. Every volunteer is gambling, and the stakes are permanent.

The bandits can't fix it. No programme support, no research notes, no understanding of the underlying biology. Trial and error is their only diagnostic tool, and the sample sizes in a single camp are too small to establish reliable patterns.

---

## Black Market Spread

The recipe doesn't have to stay with one bandit camp. If the boss is entrepreneurial — or desperate, or dead — the recipe finds its way to the black market.

Once it's loose, it spreads. A copy sold to a fence. A disgruntled bandit trading it for safe passage. An ambitious black market operator recognizing the value of what they're holding.

**The natural brake: the sex problem.**
Most bandit groups, mercenary outfits, and criminal operations skew heavily male. The sex-chromosome mechanic makes this a recruitment crisis:
- Female donor animals guarantee a female result. Every time, no exceptions.
- Male donor animals are the only route that *might* produce a male result — but it's a coin flip at best.
- There is no safe combination. No guaranteed male outcome exists.

The first camp to discover this learns the hard way. The story spreads faster than the recipe. Criminal networks trade in reputation, and "the transformation potion that might make you female — and nobody can tell you the odds" is exactly the kind of story that grows in the telling. A coin flip becomes "more likely than not" in a tavern retelling. An unlucky outcome becomes a cautionary tale.

**This doesn't stop the spread entirely.** Some buyers won't care — someone with mixed-sex volunteers, someone desperate enough to accept the risk, someone who sees female forms as useful for infiltration or other purposes. The recipe retains value as a tool. But the pool of *willing male volunteers* shrinks dramatically once the stories start circulating. Most men won't gamble their sex on a potion nobody fully understands, with odds nobody can promise.

**The irony:** the programme's own undocumented mechanic — never intended as a security feature — becomes the most effective brake on unauthorized proliferation. Institutional response (investigators, crackdowns, programme security) operates on a timescale of weeks or months. Rumor operates overnight.

Every copy of the recipe multiplies the problem, but the problem multiplies slower than it otherwise would:
- Criminal groups gain access to transformation, but male recruitment is self-limiting
- The collar bottleneck remains — collars are harder to source than the potion
- Black market artisans may attempt to reverse-engineer or counterfeit collars, with unpredictable results
- The programme's security model is compromised, but not shattered — controlled access still offers predictability that the black market can't match

---

## Intelligence Signal

If word reaches the programme — reports of bandits using transformation with inconsistent sex outcomes — it tells the programme exactly what happened. Someone is working from the recipe alone without the supporting documentation. That narrows the source to the missing courier shipment immediately.

The inconsistent results are a fingerprint that points directly at stolen, incomplete knowledge.

---

## Open Variables (Establish Before Play)

- Courier identity — who were they? Does anyone miss them personally, not just professionally?
- Bandit camp location and size
- The boss's personality and ambition level — local menace or aspiring warlord?
- The camp alchemist's skill level and moral flexibility
- How many speaking collars were in the shipment (this sets the ceiling for voiced volunteers)
- Which programme site the courier was traveling between
- How long before the programme notices the materials are missing
- Whether the courier can find any way to communicate their situation

---

## Plot Hooks

- The programme dispatches investigators when the courier doesn't arrive
- A transformed bandit is captured during a raid — and they're clearly not programme-affiliated
- Reports of "wild" animals with unusual tactical behavior reach a garrison
- The courier manages to escape or signal somehow
- The sex-mismatch incidents create internal conflict in the bandit camp
- A black market connection (Varne? someone connected to Varne?) becomes aware the recipe is in criminal hands
- The recipe surfaces on the black market — who's selling, and how many copies are already out?
- A black market operator acquires a copy and recognizes what it is
- Programme leadership must decide whether to go public about the breach or try to contain it quietly
