# Wilderness Garrison Scouts — Scenario Prompt

**Setting:** Kennel Hounds / Cendrel
**Type:** Campaign prompt — to be fleshed out before play
**Tone:** Military pragmatism vs. programme oversight

---

## Core Concept

A wilderness garrison — remote, undermanned, practical — receives programme allocation for its scout unit. Some scouts volunteer; others may be voluntold. The transformation process begins normally with standard garrison hounds as donors.

The complication: the garrison has recently confiscated animals from a poacher or illegal trapper. Wolves, possibly a direwolf, maybe a big cat. These animals are sitting in pens within sight of the kennel.

Someone — a scout leader, the garrison commander, or the alchemist — looks at a wolf and thinks: *that's a better scout than any hound.*

They're not wrong. A wolf-form scout in deep wilderness has advantages a hound doesn't — speed, endurance, predator instincts suited to the terrain. From a purely operational standpoint, the logic is sound.

---

## Key Tensions

**Official vs. unofficial.** Did the commander authorize exotic donors as a field adaptation? Or did someone do it quietly, hoping the next programme inspection doesn't look too closely?

**Programme integrity.** The programme was built on controlled parameters — approved donor species, standardized protocols, documented outcomes. Exotic donors are untested. Behavioral bleed from a wolf or big cat may differ significantly from a domesticated hound. Nobody knows because nobody's done it under controlled conditions.

**Visibility.** A scout returns from patrol and they're clearly not a hound. That's hard to hide from visiting supply convoys, inspecting officers, or programme representatives. The first time someone sees a wolf where a hound should be, questions start.

**The scouts themselves.** How do volunteers feel about becoming a wolf instead of a hound? Is it a badge of honor — elite status? Or did someone get a predator form they didn't ask for?

**Behavioral differences.** A big cat's instincts are solitary, ambush-oriented, territorial. A wolf's are pack-driven but with a predator's aggression. These are fundamentally different from a hound's domesticated behavioral baseline. Does transformation carry those instincts? How much?

---

## Open Variables (Establish Before Play)

- Garrison location and terrain (mountain pass, deep forest, border marshland?)
- Commander's personality and relationship with programme oversight
- Site alchemist — by the book or flexible?
- How many scouts transformed, and how many got exotic donors?
- Whether the poacher/trapper is still in custody and has opinions
- Which programme site has oversight authority — Vauclair? Maruvec? Somewhere new?

---

## Plot Hooks

- A programme inspector arrives for a routine check
- A scout in wolf form has a behavioral incident on patrol
- The confiscated animals' original owner wants them back — or wants revenge
- Word reaches the programme's patron (Comte Vellancourt) that unauthorized species are being used
- A scout who received an exotic form wants to stay that way permanently
