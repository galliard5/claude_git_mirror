# Estate Daily Life — GM Toolkit

**Purpose:** This document is not a scripted scenario. It is the operating framework for running any ordinary day or two at the manor — the kind of session where nothing in particular happens and everything does. Its primary function is to give the GM a structured way to introduce faction contacts, fill out missing guild NPCs, and develop the estate's commercial relationships through natural play rather than exposition.

When a delivery arrives, a contact gets a face. When an invoice is queried, a guild becomes a place with a person attached to it. Over several sessions of this, the faction directories fill up organically, and the world becomes navigable at the character level rather than the institutional level.

---

## Two-Stage Workflow

This toolkit is intentionally split across two sessions to manage token overhead.

**Stage 1 — Prep Chat (token-heavy, runs once per session)**
Load this file, the relevant guild stubs, and any character files specific to the planned day. Use this context to generate a Day Brief — a compressed, session-ready document. Commit the Day Brief to file. Close the chat.

**Stage 2 — Play Chat (lean, runs the actual session)**
Load the Day Brief + 3–5 specific character files only. The full toolkit stays on disk. A play chat running on a Day Brief and a handful of character files should stay lean throughout the session.

**Day Brief format:** See `Core_Rules/Templates/Day_Brief_Template.md`. The prep chat generates one Day Brief per session and commits it to `Scenarios/Isalias_Estate/Day_Briefs/` before the play chat opens.

## The Estate's Week — Supply Cadence

The manor runs on scheduled supply relationships. Most deliveries arrive on predictable days; most contacts are regulars the estate staff know by name. The cadence below reflects the standing arrangements Rennik and Mira have negotiated over the years. Disruptions — a day late, a different face at the gate, a short count — are noticeable and worth a reaction from staff.

**Market Day in Silberbach:** Every Freitag (Friday). Most guild contacts who run their own carts attend the market. Deliveries to the estate typically cluster on Mittwoch (Wednesday) and Freitag to bracket the market, with Montag (Monday) deliveries clearing weekend production. Exact days are flexible — establish in play and note here.

## Economic Framework

*Value anchors for commerce. Prices emerge through play — the framework ensures consistency across sessions.*

### Currency

Silberbach and the surrounding region use imperial coinage common across Central Europe. In everyday conversation people refer to coins by their metal — "five crown" rather than "five Gold Crowns," "ten pence" rather than "ten Copper Pence." Both forms are understood.

| Coin | Common Name | Formal Name |
|---|---|---|
| Copper | Pence | Copper Pence |
| Silver | Dollar | Silver Dollar |
| Gold | Crown | Gold Crown |
| Platinum | Throne | Platinum Throne |

**Exchange Rate: 100 Pence = 10 Dollars = 1 Crown = 1/10 Throne**

Mental arithmetic runs clean at these rates. Three Dollars and forty Pence is 340 Pence. A Crown is 1,000 Pence.

**Electrum coins** circulate occasionally — older mints and distant kingdoms. Still accepted in most markets, but value is negotiated at point of exchange rather than fixed. Treated as curiosities rather than functional currency in day-to-day trade.

**Platinum Thrones** are rare in common circulation. Noble dealings, imperial transactions, major property transfers. When someone produces one at a market stall, people notice.

### Estate Income (Approximate Tiers)

The estate's income is high-value and low-volume. A single full transformation contract covers weeks of supply expenses. The day-to-day economics are comfortable, not lavish — which is why Tob complains about bread costs even though the estate can obviously afford them.

| Service / Product | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Medical compounds (standard) | Low Dollars | Wound salves, fever reducers — accessible pricing |
| Medical compounds (specialty) | Dollars–low Crowns | Fertility aids, custom preparations |
| Enchanted collar (standard speech) | Crowns | Elowen's base work |
| Enchanted collar (custom / materials upgrade) | Multi-Crown | Her pricing, not the estate's |
| Reversion elixir (standard) | Crowns | Covers estate-produced transformations |
| Reversion elixir (partial / targeted) | Multi-Crown | Requires consultation with Alric |
| Reversion elixir (emergency / accelerated) | Multi-Crown + | Higher toll, higher cost, Selene's involvement |
| Portal device (fitting + panel) | Multi-Crown | Custom to client — Elowen negotiates directly |
| Transformation (genital-only) | Crowns | Baseline service tier |
| Transformation (partial) | Multi-Crown | Complexity-dependent |
| Transformation (full) | Multi-Crown to significant | Species, complexity, permanence — major variables |
| Elowen's specialty items (display case) | Her pricing | She sets it. No one argues. |
| Breeding services | Dollars–Crowns | Per arrangement; term contracts differ from single sessions |

**Cash flow note:** A few large clients per season underwrite steady operational costs. A dry season creates real pressure. A single major commission from a merchant house buys months of comfortable operation. Commerce scenes have actual stakes — a new client inquiry is revenue, and Isalia reads it that way.

**Established anchor:** Oswin Brandt embezzled 51 Crowns from House Steinfeld over three years — roughly 17 Crowns per year. At 100:10:1, that's 170 Dollars annually. A senior steward earning 30–40 Crowns/year means he skimmed roughly half a year's wages without triggering immediate alarm. Consistent with a slow, careful operation.

## Estate Commerce — What's Available

*What a visitor can reasonably expect to browse, inquire about, or purchase. All prices are in the binder at the Apothecary counter. None are quoted here.*

### Off-Shelf (Browse & Purchase)

- **Medical compounds:** Wound salves, fever reducers, general recovery preparations, pain management compounds. Labeled by use. Card references Dr. Korr for anything clinical.
- **Enchanted collars:** Speech-enchantment variants in leather, worked steel, and engraved brass. Staff-controlled toggle. Sample display in left case.
- **Restraint hardware:** Padded cuffs, leads, adjustment rings. Same case as collars.
- **Reversion elixirs:** Standard grade behind the counter, not on display. Presented on request. Selene may ask questions before a sale proceeds.
- **Branded sigil kits:** Cedar box on the counter shelf. Shown by request.
- **Fertility aids:** In the dedicated medical case. Plainly labeled.

### Consultation Required

- **Transformation services:** Any variant. Price list in the binder. Intake paperwork before anything else.
- **Portal devices:** Sample board in the alcove. Fittings in the intake rooms. Elowen schedules the technical session.
- **Custom alchemical work:** Outside Alric's convenience stock requires a scheduled consultation.
- **Custom enchanting:** Elowen's commissions. Her timeline, her pricing. Contact through Kira.
- **Partial / emergency reversion:** Requires Selene's involvement regardless of stated preference.

### Specialty Display (Elowen's Finished Work + Imports)

Back half of the right-hand case. Unique pieces — no two identical. Contents shift; what's in the case is an in-play discovery. Imports arrive once or twice yearly via Isalia's guild contacts.

*The case rewards attention. A client asks about a specific item. It's been sitting six months with no interest. Or it sold last week and Elowen is muttering about restocking.*

## Visitor Categories

**Client Inquiries (Walk-in or Referred)**
Interface: Kira/Liora at the Apothecary counter. Escalates to Isalia for significant clients. Most referred clients arrive knowing roughly what they want. Walk-ins may need time in the consultation alcove before they can state a purpose. Neither is rushed.
Frequency: Variable by season. Slower in deep winter; active late spring through autumn.

**Noble House Correspondence**
Interface: Kira receives, Lyra sorts and prioritizes, Isalia responds personally to anything above routine. Mostly letters. Occasional in-person representative for significant matters. Unfamiliar house representatives are handled carefully until their purpose is clear.
Frequency: Weekly minimum for active relationships.

**Guild Business (Non-Delivery)**
Interface: Lyra or Isalia depending on the matter. Representatives visit for contract review, dispute resolution, or relationship maintenance. Not adversarial by default — but rarely purely social.
Frequency: Quarterly at most. Unscheduled visits mean something specific prompted it.

**Travelling Merchants (Unofficial)**
Interface: Rennik at the gate or Kira depending on what they're selling. Not discouraged, but the gate is the first filter.
Frequency: Sporadic. More common in summer.

**Officials (Town, County, Imperial)**
Interface: Isalia personally, after Lyra manages initial handling. Documentation is meticulous for a reason. Isalia treats official visits as routine even when they aren't.
Frequency: Rare but established. Staff have a protocol.

## Daily Event Slots — The Procedural Engine

Each day has five time blocks. Most have a *default activity* and a *variable slot*. Not every slot needs a variable event — ordinary days are ordinary.

### Dawn (5–7am)
**Default:** Sable runs morning stable feed and grooming. Vorik does grounds patrol. Tob and Marta begin kitchen prep. Bakers & Butchers delivery arrives in this window if today is a delivery day.

**Variable Slot:** *Who else is up?* A character with insomnia, an early habit, or something on their mind. Or the bread delivery has a new face at the gate.

---

### Morning (8am–noon)
**Default:** Alric in the lab. Elowen in the workshop. Rennik checking inventory. Elias managing administrative boards. Weekly deliveries arrive mid-morning if due. Selene doing rounds.

**Variable Slot:** *A delivery or visitor.* Primary introduction window for guild contacts. A new face with a cart, a letter requiring attention, a regular contact with something to say beyond handing off a manifest.

---

### Midday (noon–2pm)
**Default:** Lunch service in the mess hall. Most staff present. Isalia often reviewing ledgers here. Administrative offices may be briefly empty.

**Variable Slot:** *A conversation.* The mess hall at lunch is where things get said. An overheard comment starts a question. A new resident navigating where to sit and what that means.

---

### Afternoon (2–6pm)
**Default:** Heavy activity — training, craftwork, client appointments, Sable overseeing stable exercise, Vorik on maintenance. Selene may be in procedure. Apothecary sees its most consistent client traffic.

**Variable Slot:** *A client or a complication.* A walk-in at the Apothecary. A delivery dispute needing Rennik's attention. A commission question requiring Elowen on the floor. Something at the stables Sable needs hands for.

---

### Evening (6–9pm)
**Default:** Dinner service. Isalia reviews ledgers after the meal. Kael may be entertaining. Silas doing evening rounds. The estate's social life lives here.

**Variable Slot:** *Atmosphere and character.* Evening events are the least operational and most personal. A long conversation with a resident. Kira passing on something she heard in town. A letter that arrived that afternoon sitting in someone's pocket.

## NPC Discovery Protocol

When a guild contact appears for the first time — a new face with a cart, a name in a letter, a voice at the gate — follow this sequence before introducing them to the scene.

**Step 1 — Verify the relationship**
Which guild? What do they supply? Check the Supply Cadence table. Confirm the delivery day is plausible.

**Step 2 — Establish the contact**
Determine: name, approximate age and build, one immediately visible trait (worn hands, a particular way of standing, a slight accent).

**Step 3 — Set their relationship with the estate**
- *Familiar:* Been doing this delivery for years. Knows the staff and where to go.
- *Newer:* Six months to a year. Competent, still learning the estate's preferences.
- *Covering:* Not the usual person. Knows the manifest, not much else.

**Step 4 — Set their awareness of the estate's nature**
- *Knows and doesn't care:* Pays on time, doesn't make trouble. What happens inside is their business.
- *Connected:* Personal relationship with someone at the estate — a relative who transformed here, a former colleague, a professional acquaintance.
- *Knows and is curious:* Does the job professionally but pays attention.
- *Doesn't know the specifics:* Half-formed ideas, mostly wrong.

**Step 5 — Note one standing thread (optional)**
Something open: hoping to ask about something, an invoice discrepancy they've been meaning to raise, someone asking them questions about the estate that's sitting uncomfortably.

**Step 6 — File after play**
Extract to: `...Guilds/[Guild_Name]/Characters/[First_Last.md]`
Use the Character Sheet Template. Start thin — name, role, brief description, one note — and fill out over subsequent appearances.

## Hook Seed Table

Drop one or two into an appropriate time slot. None are dramatic on their own. Any can grow. Most should stay small.

### Slice-of-Life

**1. Short count**
The morning bread delivery is two loaves short of the manifest. Tob noticed. He has opinions. The contact either made an error, had a difficult morning, or is covering for something at the bakery.

**2. New face**
The regular Brewers Guild contact isn't here. Someone else, covering. They know where things go, mostly. They ask one question they shouldn't need to ask.

**3. Barrel quality**
Today's ale tastes off. Not spoiled, but thin — like it went a little further than it should have. Tob and Rennik disagree on how to handle it. Someone needs to talk to the Brewers Guild.

**4. Invoice discrepancy**
Mira's weekly account review turns up a twelve-dollar difference between what the estate paid last month and what the manifest says was delivered. Probably arithmetic. Probably.

**5. Delivery timing**
The Farmers Guild cart arrived an hour early. Rennik wasn't there to receive it. The carter left the goods in the yard — two sacks of grain in the wrong spot, now in the way of afternoon stable work.

**6. Misdelivery**
A package arrived with the River Traders' monthly shipment addressed to the estate — but Alric doesn't recognize it and it isn't on his order list.

**7. A question at the gate**
A delivery contact, finishing their usual round, pauses before leaving. They've been working up to a question for a few trips. It might be about the estate's services for themselves. It might be about someone else.

**8. A request from Tob**
The cook wants something specific — an ingredient, a spice, a piece of equipment — that the estate doesn't stock and falls outside normal supply relationships. He's asked Rennik. Rennik said no. He's going to ask someone else.

**9. Recognition**
A carter or delivery worker pauses when they see one of the transformed residents. Not alarm — recognition. They knew them before.

**10. Early seasonal pressure**
Word from the Farmers Guild contact: this year's grain harvest looks lean. They'll honor existing contracts, but prices are adjusting next quarter. Rennik takes it to Mira. Mira takes it to Isalia.

**11. Something left behind**
A delivery left something in the yard not on the manifest and not addressed to anyone — a small item, personal-looking, probably dropped by accident. Nobody claims it when the contact returns.

**12. Staff needs a favor**
A staff member quietly asks a regular delivery contact to carry something to town — a letter, a small package, a message. Nothing alarming. But it doesn't go through the estate's usual courier system.

### Faction Intrigue Level

**13. A question that doesn't fit**
A guild contact — not someone who's shown unusual curiosity before — asks specific questions about the estate's operations. How many residents? Do clients stay long? Is the staff turnover high? Each question would be unremarkable alone.

**14. A new arrangement offered**
A Merchants Guild representative arrives with an informal proposal: a discount on certain supplies in exchange for the estate providing a reference for a specific noble house client. Polite and reasonable. Lyra brings it to Isalia. Isalia asks which noble house.

**15. Guild pressure, secondhand**
The Tanners Guild contact mentions in passing that the Merchants Guild has been making noise at the last assembly about estate-adjacent pricing — specifically, the estate's medical compounds undercutting what licensed apothecaries in town can charge.

**16. A letter of introduction**
An unfamiliar courier delivers a letter with a noble house seal. It introduces a representative arriving in three days for a preliminary discussion about a significant commission. The commission is described in deliberately vague terms. Isalia reads it twice.

**17. An overheard conversation**
Two regular delivery contacts are in the yard at the same time — unusual — and appear to know each other. Their conversation stops when someone from the estate approaches. One makes a small joke of it. The other doesn't quite.

## Extraction Notes

After any session using this framework:

**New NPCs generated:** File to their guild's Characters directory using the Character Sheet Template. Even a thin file is better than nothing — it makes the next appearance consistent.

**Supply relationships developed:** If a contact's baseline awareness was established in play, note it in their character file and add a one-line entry to the Supply Cadence table.

**Prices established:** If a specific price was quoted or agreed in play, note it. The binder exists; it has numbers. Make sure those numbers are somewhere in the files.

**Threads opened:** Any hook seed that developed into something larger should be flagged in the Scenario Extraction process.

**What to update here:** If the weekly cadence shifts, update the Supply Cadence table. This document is a living reference.
