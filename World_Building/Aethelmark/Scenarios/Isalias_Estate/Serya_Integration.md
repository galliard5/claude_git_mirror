# Serya Integration

**Campaign:** Isalia's Manor — Serya Arc
**Setting:** [[Isalias_Manor_Kennels_and_Stables]]
**PC:** Serya (formerly Sergovy Waldheim) — permanent female wolf, speaking collar, Kreiger brand
**Estimated Duration:** 2–4 sessions
**Tone:** Slice-of-life. Character-driven. No combat, no fail states. The tension is internal and interpersonal.

---

## Overview

Serya's transformation is complete. The hard part — the decision, the potion, the morning in Alric's lab — is behind her. What's ahead is the quieter, more sustained work of learning to live inside a wolf's body without losing the person she is.

This scenario covers roughly two weeks at the manor. It has no villain and no external threat. The pressure comes from three sources: the body itself (which does not cooperate on command), the manor's quiet expectation that she will eventually define her role there, and the slow-ticking background clock of Lord Albrecht Waldheim — who still believes his son is in Brennmark, and who will eventually stop believing it.

The scenario closes when Serya sits down with Isalia and makes the work decision. Everything else is the road to that conversation.

---

## Setup & Background

Serya came to the manor running from a father who wanted a priest and a social role that fit her as badly as an ill-made boot. She asked for a female wolf. She signed every consent form without reading it. She said *"I won't be coming back as this"* before the ink was dry.

That resolve was real. It is also the easiest part. What follows is the slower reckoning: instinct that moves before thought does, a body that has its own opinions about proximity and hierarchy and smell, and a manor full of people who have all been through some version of this and will not pretend otherwise.

Three things are waiting for her when she wakes up in the east kennels on Day 1:

**Sable** — who will put her to work immediately and explain nothing she doesn't ask about.

**Dr. Korr** — who has two formal evaluations scheduled, will be watching informally in between, and has opinions about the fertility question that she hasn't shared yet.

**Isalia** — who is patient, genuinely busy, and will not push Serya toward the work conversation before she's ready. But the contract terms are open, and open contracts don't stay that way indefinitely.

And somewhere in the background, a letter is traveling. The seminary in Brennmark noted that Lord Albrecht Waldheim's son missed his expected arrival date. That letter is either already at Albrecht's estate or it will be soon. Serya doesn't know this. The player does. The clock is running.

## Key NPCs

**[[Sable_the_Mare|Sable]]** — Stablemaster, Phase 1 supervisor
- *Role:* Primary contact for the first three days. Runs Serya through body adjustment training in the east pasture and training yard.
- *Approach:* Gives instructions once, clearly. Doesn't comfort — redirects. Patient with the physical adjustment phase because she's been through it. Less patient with existential wobbling during working hours.
- *What she offers:* The most practical knowledge Serya will receive. Sable has inhabited her form long enough that she can tell the difference between a body resisting and a person resisting, and she addresses each differently.
- *What she won't give:* Reassurance. If Serya needs to hear it gets easier, she'll find that out herself.

**[[Dr_Selene_Korr|Dr. Korr]]** — Head of Health, evaluations
- *Role:* Formal post-transformation evaluations at Day 3 and Day 10. Informal observation in between. Holds the fertility question.
- *Approach:* Clinical, efficient, direct. Delivers findings without softening them. Won't editorialize about what Serya should do with the information — that's Isalia's territory.
- *Fertility question:* Alric withheld suppressants at Isalia's direction. Korr's Day 10 evaluation will include a viability assessment. She will present the findings to Serya plainly and ask what she wants to do with them. This may be the most uncomfortable conversation in the scenario.
- *What she watches for:* Signs of psychological distress in the adjustment period, physical complications from the transformation, anything that affects the evaluation timeline.

**[[Kael_the_Amber_Manticore|Kael]]** — Discreet observer
- *Role:* Assigned by Isalia to observe from the eastern paddock. Assessing brothel/breeding suitability, but primarily watching how Serya carries herself — whether she performs for an audience or doesn't notice one.
- *Approach:* Conspicuously sunning himself in places where he can observe the training yard without it being obvious. Will not introduce himself as an observer. If Serya notices him and asks, he'll charm his way around a direct answer.
- *What he's actually assessing:* Whether she has the quality that makes someone interesting to be with — not performance, but presence. He noted her scars on transformation day through the paddock fence and found her interesting. He hasn't decided what that means yet.
- *Potential interaction:* If Serya approaches him directly, he'll engage. He's incapable of not engaging with someone paying attention to him. This could be useful or distracting depending on Serya.

**[[Isalia_Kreiger|Isalia]]** — Proprietor, contract holder
- *Role:* Present throughout but not intrusive. Available for genuine consultation. Will initiate the work conversation when Serya signals readiness, not before.
- *Approach:* She read Serya clearly at intake — not the performing type, drawn to pack dynamics, interested in being chosen rather than displaying. She is not going to push her toward brothel work. She is also not going to steer her away from it. The decision belongs to Serya.
- *The contract conversation:* Three real options on the table — brothel work (Kael's report will inform this), breeding (fertility assessment required, Korr's findings matter), courier/guide work in the eastern forests. Each has different contractual terms, different daily rhythms, different exposure to the parts of the manor Serya has only glimpsed. Isalia will lay them out honestly, including what each actually involves day-to-day.
- *What she won't do:* Choose for Serya. She's seen too many residents take roles they thought they wanted and find the reality different. She asks harder questions in this conversation than she did at intake.

## Key Locations

**East Kennels** — [[Kennels_and_Stables]]
- Serya's assigned quarters during integration. Individual run with outdoor access.
- Other canine residents are present — some transformed, some not. The social dynamics here are Serya's first real experience of being inside a pack structure rather than observing one from the outside. It doesn't feel the way she expected.
- *What matters:* Who else is in the east kennels, what their relationships are, whether Serya is treated as a newcomer, a curiosity, or a threat by the resident pack.

**East Pasture & Training Yard** — [[Kennels_and_Stables]]
- Where Sable runs the adjustment training. Open ground, various obstacles, enough space to actually move at wolf speed.
- *What matters:* The gap between what Serya's body can do and what she can consciously control. Running is easy. Stopping on command is harder. Pack response instincts — the urge to follow movement, to respond to certain sounds — surface here whether she wants them to or not.

**The Infirmary** — [[Infirmary]]
- Korr's territory. Both formal evaluations happen here.
- *What matters:* The Day 10 evaluation is where the fertility question becomes explicit. Serya has had ten days to form an opinion; she may not have. The infirmary is a place where the body is discussed as a system, which is simultaneously useful and strange when the body being discussed is yours.

**Isalia's Office** — [[Main_Building]]
- Where the work conversation happens. The same room where Serya sat across the desk from Isalia as a nervous human and admitted she wanted a female wolf.
- *What matters:* The conversation is different now. The asking is over. The question is what comes next.

## Scenario Arc

### Phase 1 — The Body (Days 1–3)

Serya wakes up in the east kennels. The transformation is settled — no residual pain, no obvious complications — but the body is still foreign. Every movement is a negotiation.

Sable appears before Serya has finished figuring out how to get upright without stumbling. She doesn't comment on the stumbling. She says: *"East pasture. Now."*

The training is physical and immediate: gaits, stops, turns, basic directional control. Sable's method is to give the instruction, demonstrate if necessary, and then wait. When Serya fails — and she will — Sable names the specific problem and asks her to try again. When she succeeds, Sable moves to the next thing. There is no praise. There is no reassurance. There is the work.

By Day 3 Serya can move at a controlled trot without losing the front-left coordination that was giving her trouble. She cannot yet run at full speed and stop on command reliably. Sable notes this without concern — it'll come.

Korr's Day 3 evaluation covers basic physical health: reflexes, gait assessment, coat condition, weight, general adjustment indicators. She presents findings in a brief written summary she hands to Serya and keeps a copy for the file. She asks Serya two questions: *"Any discomfort you haven't reported?"* and *"How are you sleeping?"* She accepts the answers at face value and moves on.

**What surfaces in this phase:**
- Instinct responding before thought does — Serya will discover specific triggers that produce wolf responses she didn't choose to have
- The scars on her inner thighs, now visible as pale lines through the fur — other residents may or may not notice, may or may not ask
- Kael watching from the paddock fence, apparently doing nothing in particular

---

### Phase 2 — The Manor (Days 4–14)

Sable continues to supervise but the training shifts — less structured, more integrated. Serya starts accompanying kennel rounds, learning the rhythms of east kennel management: feeding schedules, exercise rotations, which residents need separation and why.

This is where the manor reveals itself as a social environment rather than just a physical one. There are other transformed residents. They have histories and relationships and opinions. Serya is new, she's a wolf, and she has visible bite scarring — none of which goes unnoticed.

The question she's been deferring — what she's going to *do* here — starts pressing in from multiple directions. Kael approaches her in the courtyard near Day 6. He doesn't mention the observation assignment. He talks to her the way he talks to everyone, which is to say he pays her a kind of focused attention that is noticeably different from being ignored. What she does with that attention tells her something about herself.

Korr's Day 10 evaluation is the pivot point of this phase. Full assessment: physical health, gait progress, psychological adjustment indicators, and the fertility question. Korr will present the viability findings plainly — Serya is or isn't suited for breeding based on the assessment, and if she is, the suppressant question needs an answer. This is not Korr pushing her toward anything. It is Korr presenting data that requires a decision.

Serya's answer to the fertility question — even a provisional one, even *"I don't know yet"* — is the signal that she's ready to talk to Isalia.

**What surfaces in this phase:**
- The east kennels' existing social structure and where Serya fits in it
- What being inside pack dynamics actually feels like versus what she expected
- Kael's assessment, conducted entirely through what looks like casual interest
- The fertility question, which requires her to have an opinion about a body she's still getting used to

---

### Phase 3 — The Work Decision

Serya requests a meeting with Isalia, or Isalia finds a natural moment — walking the herb garden, the mess hall after evening meal, wherever the timing feels right rather than formal.

The conversation covers three options in detail:

**Brothel work** — Client-facing, scheduled, supervised initially by Kael. Better suited for someone who wants specific encounters rather than ongoing relationships. Pays well. Requires temperament assessment to be positive — Kael's report matters here. Serya said at intake that she wanted to be chosen, not to perform. Those aren't necessarily the same thing, but they're close enough that Isalia will ask her to think about the distinction.

**Breeding** — Contingent on Korr's fertility findings. More structured — scheduled, contractual, evaluated for compatibility and temperament. Longer-term relationship with the programme rather than individual clients. Serya's reaction at intake when breeding came up (the warmth she couldn't quite suppress) has not escaped Isalia's notice. She won't say this directly. She'll ask Serya what appealed to her about it.

**Courier/guide work** — Eastern forest routes. The work Count Valtor's men have been making harder for transformed runners, which is worth noting plainly. Pays well seasonally. Physically demanding and solitary for stretches. Suits someone who wants the running and the outdoors more than the pack. Isalia's honest assessment: given what she's seen of Serya so far, this may not be the right fit — but it's on the table.

Isalia does not recommend. She asks questions. When the conversation ends, Serya will have told Isalia what she wants to try, and Isalia will draft the contract terms accordingly.

This is where the scenario closes.

## Background Thread — The Father Clock

Lord Albrecht Waldheim received a letter from the Brennmark seminary noting that his son missed his expected arrival date. He has not yet acted on it — his first assumption is probably that Sergovy is delayed on the road, or decided not to go through with the seminary and is too cowardly to say so directly.

This thread does not surface during the integration scenario. It exists in the background. The player knows. Serya does not.

**Approximate timeline:**
- Transformation day: seminary letter either already in transit or arriving at Albrecht's within days
- Albrecht's investigation begins: approximately 2–3 weeks post-letter, assuming he doesn't act immediately
- First contact at the manor (via letter, messenger, or agent): weeks to months depending on how aggressively he pursues it

The integration scenario ends before this thread surfaces. It is the opening pressure of the next scenario in Serya's arc.

## Session Notes

This scenario runs well as 2 sessions: one covering Phase 1 and the early part of Phase 2 (through approximately Day 7), one covering the rest of Phase 2 and the work conversation. A third session is appropriate if the east kennels social dynamics produce interesting material worth dwelling in.

**What to emphasize:**
- The gap between Serya's intentions and her body's responses — this is where the interesting character work lives
- Sable as a model of someone who solved the problem Serya is currently in the middle of, without ever saying so explicitly
- The fertility question as the moment the scenario stops being about physical adjustment and starts being about what Serya actually wants her life to look like

**What not to force:**
- The work decision doesn't need to be dramatic. It can be quiet and certain. Not every significant moment needs weight applied from outside.
- Kael doesn't need to become a significant relationship. He can be a brief, clarifying encounter and nothing more.

**Foreshadowing for later scenarios:**
- Any mention of Count Valtor's interference with courier routes lands differently once the Voss thread is active
- Serya's sealed records are referenced in passing by Lyra or Alric — not dramatically, just as a fact of how the manor operates. This matters later when Albrecht's people start asking questions.
