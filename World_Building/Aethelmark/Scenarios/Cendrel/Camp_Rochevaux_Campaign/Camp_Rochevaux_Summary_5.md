# Camp Rochevaux — Session 5 Checkpoint

**Campaign:** Camp Rochevaux — Kennel Hounds Military Outpost Pilot
**Session:** 5
**In-Game Date:** Transformation day +1 — early afternoon
**Location:** Buyer's facility — back of property, cage on the ground behind the main building
**PC:** Rill Sunclaw, kobold scout, panther form. Collar removed, passed to Grenn before departure. Voice currently unavailable.

---

## Summary

Rill rode out in the cage at dawn, collar off and passed to Grenn in the deep hours before Creuse woke. Creuse loaded the cart without incident — a moment at the bars where he studied the bandaged ear, Rill gave him a low warning growl when his hand came too close, and that was enough. The road east took most of the morning. Rill spent it calibrating: pacing the cage floor to find balance on the moving cart, then working through the nose and ears methodically — isolating the injured ear, measuring the angular shift from the notch, adjusting until the compensation was consistent. Picked up Duval in the treeline twice by sound, confirmed the pitch smell on the axle. The destination is a deliberately isolated facility — repurposed building, old animal-work smell underneath newer habitation, one main structure and a work shed, a paddock, kenneled dogs. The buyer is city-dressed, too well-dressed for the frontier, and was visibly relieved at the cart's arrival. He smells faintly of the same chemical edge as the shed — he works with the alchemist or is one. The alchemist is lean, middle-aged, professional, and wants a blood draw before anything else proceeds. also mentioned breeding the panther for later projects. Duval has worked his way around to the treeline behind the property and has eyes on the back. Rill is in the cage, collar off, holding the resting animal posture.

## GM Notes

**The blood draw** is the immediate pressure point. The cage may be opened for access — this is the first real test of whether Rill can hold the animal register under close physical handling. The alchemist is professional and careful; he won't be reckless. But a blood draw requires proximity, likely restraint or at minimum stillness.

**What the facility is:** The evidence points clearly — isolated location, repurposed animal-work building, active alchemical process, buyer with chemical knowledge, *blood draw as first step.* This is a private transformation operation. The buyer commissioned a panther for donor use and possible breeding, not display. The question is: for whom?

**Communication gap:** Collar is with Grenn in the follow team. Rill cannot speak. Duval has eyes on the back of the property but no channel to Rill. The squad's next move depends on what they observe from the treeline — they are following, not directing.

**The buyer's two words** — *this afternoon* — heard through the wall. The blood draw is this afternoon. Whatever follows the blood draw may also be this afternoon.

**Tone going forward:** The facility is calm, professional, and completely committed to its purpose. Nobody here is nervous. That is the most informative thing about it.
