## Species Overview

The G'lith are a reptilian spacefaring species, roughly humanoid in posture (portrait shows a teal-scaled biped with a long tail, wearing tactical gear with a rifle). They have been interstellar for only a few centuries, expanding aggressively.

**Key biological trait:** Sequential hermaphrodites — G'lith are born female and shift to male as they age. This is a gradual biological transition, not a choice.

**Homeworld:** A double-planet system — twin worlds **Lakar** and **Hesk** orbit each other. Early G'lith exploration brought them across the gap between worlds, and this first interplanetary voyage shaped their culture's relationship with technology and expansion.

**Religion:** The vast majority worship a **deity of technology and advancement** — engineering problems are treated as acts of devotion. Augmentations (cybernetics, genetic modification) are religiously normalized; most G'lith carry basic cybernetics as a cultural baseline.

## The G'lith Technocracy

**Capital colony:** **Akarr** — a devastated alien colony world with dangerous ruins, categorized as high-security "hazard zones." The ruins predate G'lith arrival and are still not fully understood.

**Government structure:** Digital direct democracy — citizens vote on issues via compads and connected devices. In practice this gives civilian opinion enormous weight, though the Technocratic elite has learned to work around it (cash can get charges dropped entirely).

**Administrator:** An older G'lith (male, per sequential biology) held in power through wide popular support. Tension exists within the Technocratic elite over specifics of control.

**Internal tensions:**
- Elite impunity vs. civilian democratic ideals
- Growing argument that a True AI could run government more efficiently than the current system
- Approaching threat of a **Dark Elven Matriarchal Fleet** — generating popular pressure for war

**Cultural values:** Honesty (tied to digital democracy transparency), but combined with a belief that technology can fix any problem — producing fecklessness in many civilians. Outsiders are generally welcomed but routinely ignored.
