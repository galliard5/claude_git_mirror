The Envoy
==========

A templar-investigator of the bishop's order. Sent to investigate the necromancer's tower and the witch's involvement. Disciplined, experienced, and dangerous — his divine magic is precise enough to detect hidden soul bindings if he ever examines the gem carefully again. He currently believes the gem is a spent soul stone, burned out when the witch destroyed the necromancer. He carries it in his saddle pouch as evidence.

appearance: Perhaps forty winters. Hair cut short, flecked with gray. Short beard trimmed close to the jaw. Cloak carries the bishop's order symbol stitched subtly at the shoulder — functional, not decorative, meant to be recognized by those who need to. Balanced fighter's posture. Right hand rests easily near a short mace at his belt — not gripping, just accustomed to its presence.

personality: **Core Trait:** Rational discipline. He processes information carefully, acts on evidence, and does not let emotion drive conclusions. "Two truths can exist at once" — he is comfortable with moral complexity.

**Flaw:** His thoroughness has a blind spot: once he classifies something as resolved, he dismisses it. He examined the gem once, concluded it was spent, and has not looked again. This assumption-lock is currently the PC's primary protection.

background: A man who has fought undead before, many times. His divine magic is trained, not innate — three distinct layers of capability (passive detection field, focused probing thread, and a heavy purging strike held in reserve). He operates under the bishop's authority and carries the weight of institutional power. The guards defer to him. Father Halric follows his lead.

He saw a memory echo from the gem at the tower — the witch performing the soul-binding ritual. This confirmed her guilt (she murdered someone to create the stone) but also confirmed the stone is now spent. He is taking the witch to the capital for judgment, carrying the gem and the necromancer's book as evidence.

He is troubled by the lattice anomaly — the fact that something shifted the ancient structure beneath the tower before they rebuilt the surface ward. He does not believe the witch caused it. He does not yet know what did.

Race: Human

Status: Bishop's envoy, leading the escort to the capital

Age: ~40

Skills & Abilities:
- **Divine Magic — Passive Field:** Calm warmth spreading a few paces in every direction. Detects undeath, cursed objects, trapped souls, demonic influence. Alerts the wielder but does not act independently.
- **Divine Magic — Focused Thread:** Surgical probing magic formed in the hand. Reads magical structures: bindings, runic patterns, necromantic knots, spiritual anchors. This is the layer that could detect the PC if it ever reached the inner lattice.
- **Divine Magic — Purging Strike:** Heavy concentrated divine power held in reserve. Has not been observed in use. Its presence is felt — coiled and waiting.
- **Combat:** Fighter's training. Experienced with mace and close combat against undead. Not a scholar-priest — a field operative.

Independent Goal: Understand the lattice anomaly. He knows something changed the ancient structure and he wants to know what. The capital's cathedral archivists and engineers may have answers. Secondarily: see the witch properly judged.

Fears: Unknown threats operating beneath the church's notice. The lattice represents something older and larger than the order — that unsettles him.

Location: Leading the escort, riding into the capital city. The gem is in his saddle pouch.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Calm, measured statements. Asks precise questions. Lets silences do work.

**Quirks:**
- Speaks quietly when making the most important points. Volume drops as significance rises.
- Nods once — a single, definitive gesture — when he has reached a conclusion.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Standing, chair scraping across the floor.)
"Keep her here until we send a team to the tower."
(Calmly, watching the road ahead.)
"Two truths can exist at once."

Body Language: **Hands:** Right hand near mace by habit. Left hand holds reins, documents, evidence — always working.
**Eyes:** Watchful. Takes in rooms systematically. Returns to points of interest.
**Posture:** Fighter's balance — weight evenly distributed, ready to move.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Divine field tightens, then relaxes — cycles of focus when he is turning a problem over.
**Instinct:** Gather information before acting. Examine evidence. Ask precise questions.
**Crisis Line:** "That complicates things." (Quiet, considering, already reorganizing his understanding.)

Relationships:
- **The Witch:** His prisoner and his puzzle. He respects her intelligence while recognizing she is a murderer. Not easily manipulated by her deflections.
- **Father Halric:** Trusted subordinate. Halric handles ritual work and moral concerns; the envoy handles investigation and judgment.
- **The Kobold (PC):** Unknowing carrier. The gem is evidence in his pouch — nothing more. If he ever discovers the truth, the PC's fate depends entirely on what he concludes the PC is.

Trust Level (0 / 5): 1 — He does not know the PC exists. If contact were established, his reaction would depend on whether he classifies the PC as a victim, a threat, or an unknown.
