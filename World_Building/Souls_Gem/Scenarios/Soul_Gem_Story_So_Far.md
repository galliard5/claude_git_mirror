# Soul Gem — Story So Far

*Resumption document. Read this to continue play from the current stopping point.*

---

## THE SACRIFICE

A young kobold child — small, thin, scales still soft with youth — is dragged from a trader's wagon in the rain and sold to a witch in a remote forest hut. The trader paid no attention to the kobold after the coins changed hands. The witch studied the child the way someone prices a tool. She untied the ropes. She fed them thin stew. Then she prepared a ritual circle with chalk, candles, and green-burning brazier.

The kobold approached nervously when called. The witch's knife flashed once.

The body was used carefully — reagents, meat for the larder, nothing wasted. The skull was placed on a shelf among other bones. And the soul was bound into a small black gem set in polished bone wrapped with copper wire, hung as a bauble from the witch's staff.

The kobold was meant to be a battery. A focus stone. A tool.

But awareness persisted.

---

## THE HUT — EARLY DEVELOPMENT

For weeks (possibly months), the kobold existed inside the gem. No voice. No body. No breath. Just awareness anchored to a small stone swinging from a staff.

They learned by experimenting during the witch's sleeping hours:
- **Telekinesis:** Tiny movements at first — a copper ring shifting, the gem rotating in its wire cage. Exhausting, but real.
- **Awareness Expansion:** Could spread perception roughly ten feet from the gem. Could sense heat, cold, vibration, pressure.
- **The Skull Connection:** The kobold's skull on the shelf held a faint resonance — an echo of identity. Touching it with awareness triggered flashes of memory: mother's scales, clutchmates in the tunnels, the warmth of the warren.
- **The Witch's Dreams:** Brief glimpses — a younger witch before a teacher, a burning village, a room full of glowing soul gems. The control magic pulled them back when they pressed too far.
- **The Gem's Nature:** The gem is a collector — it slowly gathers ambient magic from the environment to replenish its pool. When the witch casts spells, she draws from this pool. Too much drain too quickly could empty it entirely.

The witch never acknowledged the mind inside the gem. To her, it was only a tool.

---

## BUILDING THE RINGS

The kobold began secret construction inside the gem — internal structures built from memory anchors and magical geometry, hidden beneath the gem's normal energy currents.

**First Ring (Outer):** Built from memories of the trader's journey — fear, cold, the road. Created a hollow space where energy currents swirled differently, providing basic protection against the witch's draw.

**Second Ring:** Built from calm tunnel memories — clutchmates, the warren's rhythm. Guided circulation that stabilized turbulent magic and smoothed chaotic currents.

**Third Ring (Lattice):** Built from sharp survival memories, including the moment of death. A dense cage of precision that acts as a sieve — corrupted or hostile magic breaks apart against the tight intersections.

**Fourth Ring (Core):** Built from the earliest childhood memories — mother's warmth, clutchmates tumbling in the nursery tunnel. The deepest and most stable layer. The foundation that holds everything else in alignment.

All construction was done only when the witch slept. The kobold learned to withdraw all activity before dawn and let the gem's surface currents flow normally. The witch inspected the gem each morning and noticed nothing — except that it seemed to perform unusually well. "Better stone than I thought."

---

## THE WITCH'S WORLD

The kobold observed the witch's routines and territory:
- She gathered herbs, sold charms to travelers (luck blessings, minor protections), and maintained her tools
- She visited a dead oak hung with bones — some kind of offering or communication with other practitioners
- She was part of a tradition, not a lone hermit
- Her staff had an interchangeable gem mount, suggesting she has used (and consumed) many soul stones
- When casting, she dipped power from the gem like a cup from a river. The gem slowly refilled from ambient magic

The kobold also built a collapse mechanism: if the witch drew too deeply, the outer layers would burn out sacrificially while the hidden core sealed itself. The gem would appear dead. The kobold would survive inside.

---

## THE TOWER

The witch traveled north — away from her territory for the first time. The road led to a ruined tower occupied by a necromancer who was performing rituals involving undead constructs and drawing on something buried deep beneath the foundations.

The witch entered the tower and fought the necromancer. During the battle, she drew heavily on the gem. The kobold's rings filtered necromantic corruption (crude death magic with ragged edges) and kept the core stable. When the final clash came — a massive magical backlash that shattered the staff and the ritual circle simultaneously — the collapse mechanism activated.

The outer layers burned out. The gem went dark. The witch and the necromancer both collapsed.

From the outside, the gem appeared to be a spent stone. Dead. Empty.

Inside, the hidden core remained sealed. Alive. Watching.

---

## THE ANCIENT LATTICE

While everyone was unconscious, the kobold studied what lay beneath the tower.

Deep underground, they discovered an immense reservoir of raw magical energy — not a creature, not a spirit, but a natural convergence of magical currents compressed by centuries. Below the reservoir, something even older: a vast, slow presence that simply exists, with the weight of a mountain buried under the sea.

Sealing the reservoir was an ancient divine architecture — the lattice. Thousands of divine strands woven into bedrock, distributing pressure outward through anchor points across the land. The tower sat above the largest convergence point. The necromancer's ritual had punched a fracture through it, causing pressure to leak upward.

The kobold secretly realigned one damaged strand — just enough to reduce the leak. No one saw them do it.

---

## THE GARRISON TOWN

The witch walked to a nearby garrison town carrying the broken staff and the gem. She tried to trade the necromancer's ritual book, which attracted the attention of the local captain, a priest named Father Halric, and eventually a divine order investigator — the envoy.

**Father Halric** examined the gem during interrogation. His warm divine field detected nothing — the void shell the kobold had constructed disguised the gem as a spent soul vessel. The priest concluded it was dead. The kobold secretly let a trace of divine magic in to study how it worked, then redirected it away from the core.

**The Envoy** arrived to investigate. A templar — experienced, disciplined, dangerous. His divine magic was precise: three layers (passive field, focused probing thread, purging strike held in reserve). He examined the gem once, concluded it was spent, and dismissed it. This assumption-lock became the kobold's primary protection.

The witch was interrogated extensively. She admitted to using the soul stone but claimed she found it, not created it. The envoy took the gem and the necromancer's book as evidence.

**The Necromancer's Book:** The kobold studied it extensively through vibration and proximity sensing as the captain and priest flipped through its pages. They learned the theoretical framework for corpse anchors, thread control, multi-anchor soul structures, and echo stabilization — the fourth component they had already been doing instinctively with their skull connection.

**The Escape Attempt:** The witch had a hidden displacement ring — a single-use teleportation tool. The kobold secretly altered the spell's directional vector while studying it. When the witch triggered it, the spell fired downward instead of forward, slamming her into the floor. Her escape tool was wasted. The envoy confiscated the ring.

---

## THE TOWER INSPECTION

The envoy rode north with a team to inspect the tower. He carried the gem in his saddle pouch. At the tower, the envoy's divine probing detected a memory echo trapped in the gem's burned-out scar — the moment of the soul-binding ritual. He saw the witch performing the sacrifice.

This confirmed two things: the witch created the stone (murder, a capital crime), and the soul inside was now gone (the echo was a residue, not a living presence). The envoy now carried moral certainty and a harder judgment — but still believed the gem was spent.

The inspection team also rebuilt a surface ward circle at the tower, with help from the witch under supervision. The envoy and Halric noted that the lattice pressure had changed before they rebuilt the circle — something had shifted the ancient structure. The witch claimed something "moved" down there. The envoy did not believe the witch, but he believed something changed the lattice. He does not know what.

---

## THE BONE HILL NODE

While at the shrine town (a stop on the road to the capital), the kobold practiced shifting awareness to the skull anchor. They could now hold presence in the skull for several seconds, perceive the hut environment, and even influence the other bones slightly through the skull's resonance network.

They discovered the bone hill sits above a secondary seam connected to the reservoir — the same system as the tower. This seam amplified their anchor resonance. Using knowledge gained from the shrine relic (stability radiation), the cathedral foundation principles (pressure redirection), and the lattice geometry (alignment over force), the kobold awakened the bone hill as an active stabilizing node in the ancient lattice.

A keystone slab with an empty relic depression now glows faintly — the kobold's soul network has effectively replaced the missing relic. The node began redistributing lattice pressure, reducing the burden on the damaged tower convergence. The deep presence beneath the reservoir shifted slightly in acknowledgment.

The kobold can now travel the underground lattice channels between nodes.

---

## THE SHRINE TOWN

The escort stopped at a roadside shrine town. The kobold studied the shrine's divine ward architecture:
- Relic anchors radiate stability
- Divine flame feeds the structure
- Wards detect patterns, not intent
- The system is self-sustaining: relic → foundation lines → threshold → altar lantern

These principles directly improved the kobold's ring architecture. The outer ring now radiates stability like a relic. The second ring redirects pressure like cathedral foundations. The core ring mirrors the keystone structure.

The envoy and Halric discussed the lattice anomaly privately. The envoy is certain the lattice predates the order. Halric referenced similar divine architecture only in cathedral foundations. Both are concerned. The envoy wants to understand what changed the lattice. Halric is simply uneasy.

---

## ANIMAL MINDS AND MIND WALKING

Over the course of the journey, the kobold developed the ability to touch minds:
- **Animal minds** (fox, horse, hawk, crow) — simpler, easier to ride, useful for scouting. Crows are best: curious, pattern-recognizing, memory-retaining.
- **Sleeping minds** — can enter dreams, observe fragments, extract memories. The witch's dreams showed her teacher and her tradition.
- **Waking minds** — can brush surface thoughts. Stronger minds resist. The kobold read a guard's memory of his cousin (capital watch) describing the sealed tunnels beneath the cathedral. Another guard's mind revealed casual knowledge about the cathedral engineers and the archivists.
- **Minor emotional influence** — demonstrated but not heavily used. Strong minds (the witch, the envoy) would likely detect intrusion.

---

## THE CROW FLIGHT TO THE CAPITAL

Before the escort reached the capital, the kobold rode a hawk's mind, then jumped to a crow heading toward the city. From the crow's eyes they observed:

The capital: massive stone walls, guard towers, a river with a long bridge, dense packed streets, market squares, forges, temples. The cathedral towers over everything — enormous stone roof, corner towers, stained glass, pilgrims kneeling in the great square.

The kobold felt the cathedral's lattice node from the air — far stronger than the hill node. Multiple channels converge beneath it. Not damaged, not dormant, but heavily reinforced. The church built around it deliberately.

The crow landed on a cathedral gargoyle. The kobold briefly touched a priest's mind in the square below and sensed the structure's divine wards from inside. Then they returned to the gem before the escort reached the gates.

---

## RING REFINEMENT

Using everything learned from the shrine, the tower lattice, and the cathedral observation, the kobold refined their internal architecture:

- **Outer Ring:** Now radiates quiet stability like a shrine relic — subtle, constant
- **Second Ring:** Channels now redirect ambient magic around the core (modeled on cathedral foundation pressure distribution)
- **Third Ring:** Filter layer refined — corrupt magic collides with the death-memory ring and disperses before reaching deeper layers
- **Core Ring:** Geometry adjusted to mirror the hill keystone structure — the true stabilizing anchor holding all other rings in alignment

The entire structure now settles like quiet celestial bodies orbiting the core. Memory, structure, defense, flow.

## CURRENT STATE — ENTERING THE CAPITAL

The escort has just passed through the capital city gates. The witch rides chained to her saddle. The envoy leads. Guards ride in formation. The bells of the cathedral ring the hour across the entire city.

The kobold is inside the gem, in the envoy's saddle pouch, entering the most densely populated and magically complex environment they have ever encountered.

**What the kobold has:**
- Refined four-ring architecture with void shell disguise
- Distributed soul anchor network (gem + skull/pelvis/rib/femur)
- Active lattice node at the bone hill
- Ability to ride animal minds, touch sleeping and waking minds
- Knowledge of arcane, divine, and lattice magic systems
- Complete concealment — no one knows they exist

**What threatens them:**
- The envoy's focused divine probing (if he re-examines the gem)
- The order's investigation of the lattice anomaly
- The cathedral engineers (who might detect the new hill node)
- The witch's growing suspicion that something intelligent intervened in the lattice
- The deep presence beneath the reservoir (unknown intentions)
- Physical vulnerability — the gem is small and can be destroyed, and the bone anchors are unguarded in a remote forest

**Immediate situation:**
- The escort is moving deeper into the city toward the cathedral or a holding facility
- The witch, the gem, and the necromancer's book will be delivered to the bishop's order
- The envoy intends to consult the archivists about the ancient lattice
- The capital cathedral sits atop a major lattice node the kobold has already scouted

**The question:** What does the kobold do now?
