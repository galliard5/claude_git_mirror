## Atmosphere & Mood

A small settlement along the road between the garrison town and the capital. Quiet, rural. The shrine is the town's most notable structure — a stone building with an altar, a lantern that burns with divine flame, and a reliquary beneath the foundation containing a relic.

---

## Key Features

**The Shrine:** A consecrated building with layered divine ward architecture. The PC studied it extensively from inside the gem:
- **Altar Lantern:** Burns with steady divine flame, feeding resonance downward into the reliquary.
- **Reliquary:** Beneath the altar stone, contains a relic that anchors the ward system. The relic radiates stability — the same principle the PC later applied to their outer ring.
- **Ward Lines:** Thin divine channels spread through the foundation like veins through a leaf, connecting relic → foundation lines → doorway threshold → altar lantern in a self-sustaining cycle.
- **Threshold Wards:** The doorway detects patterns (not intent) — anything crossing it that matches corruption signatures triggers a response. The PC's void shell passed undetected.

The shrine's ward architecture taught the PC key principles: relic anchors radiate stability, divine flame feeds the structure, wards detect patterns not intent. These lessons were applied to refine the PC's ring architecture.

**The Captain's Hall / Holding Room:** Where the witch was held overnight in the garrison town (separate from the shrine town — this may be a conflation of two stops). The witch attempted escape here using a hidden displacement ring, which the PC had sabotaged by subtly altering the spell's directional vector. The spell fired downward instead of forward, slamming the witch into the floor and wasting her escape tool.

---

## Current Situation

The escort has departed. The shrine resumes its quiet function. The wards settled back to normal after the group left. No lasting impact from the PC's presence.
