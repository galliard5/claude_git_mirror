## Atmosphere & Mood

A crumbling stone tower in a clearing, damaged by the magical battle between the witch and the necromancer. Shattered bones of undead constructs litter the approach. Inside: cracked stone floors, narrow windows casting thin bars of light, dust and the aftertaste of burnt magic. The upper floors still carry faint necromantic residue. The air feels heavy with old power.

---

## Key Features

**The Ruin:** The tower itself is partially collapsed from the battle. The witch's staff broke here. The necromancer's ritual circle on the upper floor is shattered. The envoy's inspection team has visited and rebuilt a surface ward circle, but it is only a temporary brace — not true repair.

**The Ancient Reservoir:** Far beneath the tower foundations lies a massive source of raw magical energy — not a creature, not a spirit, but a knot in the world's fabric where magical currents have gathered naturally over centuries. Immense, unfocused, wild, ancient. The necromancer barely scratched its surface. The reservoir presses upward like a deep ocean against the seabed.

**The Lattice Seal:** An ancient divine architecture built into the stone deep underground. Thousands of divine strands woven into rock, distributing pressure from the reservoir outward through anchor points across the region. The tower sits above the largest convergence point — dozens of lattice strands meet here like spokes of a wheel. This is the heart of the regional stabilizing network.

**The Fracture:** The necromancer's ritual punched a narrow wound through the lattice. Strands are torn and misaligned, causing pressure to leak upward. The PC secretly realigned one strand, reducing the leak. The surface ward rebuilt by the envoy, Halric, and the witch (under supervision) braces the top of the wound but does not repair the deeper damage. The fracture will worsen over time if not properly repaired.

**The Deep Presence:** Beneath the reservoir, something older and slower exists. Not magic in the conventional sense — simply a presence with the weight of a mountain buried under the sea. It does not react to the PC but has shifted slightly in acknowledgment when the lattice changed. Its nature is unknown.

---

## Current Situation

The tower is ruined and the necromancer is dead. The envoy's inspection team has visited, rebuilt a surface ward, and departed. The lattice fracture remains — partially stabilized by the PC's awakened hill node carrying some of the pressure burden, but not repaired. The reservoir remains dormant. The deep presence remains sleeping.

No one currently occupies the tower.
