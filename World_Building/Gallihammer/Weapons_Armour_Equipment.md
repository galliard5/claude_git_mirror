# Gallihammer — Weapons, Armour, and Equipment

## On Thirty Thousand Years of Violence

Humanity has been fighting on Earth for as long as there has been anything worth fighting over, which is to say for longer than anyone alive can reliably trace. The collapse did not end this. It intensified it, stripped away the civilisational infrastructure that had made mass violence somewhat more structured, and left the survivors with whatever weapons they could find, maintain, or build — in an environment already saturated with the most advanced killing tools that a stellar empire had ever produced.

Thirty thousand years later, the planet is not peaceful. Nor is the void.

What this means practically is that humanity has become very good at maintaining weapons it does not fully understand, building weapons from partial knowledge of principles it cannot replicate from scratch, and improvising lethal capability from whatever is available. A community that has survived three hundred generations in a bioweapon-haunted wasteland has had three hundred generations to figure out what works and what kills you before it kills the thing you pointed it at. The learning curve was steep. The graduates are dangerous.

The result is a weapon ecosystem that spans an extraordinary range — from sharpened iron carried by people who have never seen electricity, to working imperial arms that predate every living civilisation by thousands of years, maintained through ritual and luck and occasional genuine understanding. Both ends of this spectrum can kill you. The imperial end can kill considerably more of you, and in more ways.

## Technology Tiers

Understanding what a weapon *is* in this setting requires understanding where it sits on a spectrum of technological sophistication that correlates only loosely with power. The tiers are a framework, not a rigid classification — real weapons blur across lines and defy easy placement.

**Primitive tier:** Weapons produced from locally available materials with locally available knowledge. Knives, spears, bows, black-powder firearms, axes, clubs. The design principles are understood. Repair is local. These weapons can absolutely kill you, and at the ground level of Dead Terra, they remain common. Don't underestimate them.

**Craft tier:** Weapons produced by skilled artisans with some preserved knowledge of more advanced manufacturing. Reliable repeating firearms. Basic energy weapons assembled from scavenged components. Forged blades of genuine quality. A craft-tier weapon is the product of someone who understands what they're making even if they can't explain *why* it works at the level of physics. This is the most common tier for anyone operating at the level of a small community or trade network.

**Industrial tier:** Weapons produced by manufacturing operations with significant resource access and some intact technical knowledge. Standard infantry weapons of reliable quality, consistent ammunition, standardised components. Most Mechanicus-manufactured arms sit at the upper end of this tier. A faction that can field an entire army with industrial-tier weapons has achieved something the majority of post-collapse powers cannot.

**Relic tier:** Weapons that were produced using imperial-era manufacturing knowledge and materials, kept functional through decades or centuries of careful maintenance — often ritualistic in character, with the maintenance procedures preserved as religious observance. The practitioners performing the rituals may not understand what they're doing at the level of engineering; what matters is that the rituals are correct, because they were correct once, and keeping them correct keeps the weapon working. Relic-tier weapons are noticeably superior to industrial-tier equivalents in ways that practitioners of industrial manufacture cannot fully explain or replicate.

**Imperial relic tier:** Weapons produced by the empire itself, still in working order, maintained through millennia of continuous effort or somehow preserved in a state that kept them viable. The gap between this tier and everything below it is not a step up — it is a different category of violence. A working imperial plasma rifle is to a craft-tier lasgun as a craft-tier lasgun is to a pointy stick. Communities, ships, and factions that possess imperial-relic weapons guard them accordingly. These are political assets as much as military ones.

**Incomprehensible tier:** The weapons the empire used on itself during the collapse. Most of them are not in anyone's hands because most of them cannot be held, aimed, or transported by any individual or organisation currently alive. Dead zones across Earth are the memorials of weapons that are either still operating or have done things that the planet has not recovered from. Some of them have no names anymore. Some of them have too many.

## Personal Ranged Weapons

### Solid Projectile Arms

The most universally distributed weapons across the setting — not because they are the best available, but because the principles behind them are understood, the materials are found everywhere, and the manufacturing requirement is achievable at craft tier without exotic components.

**Handguns** of varying configurations are carried across every faction at every power level. A warlord's household guard and a refugee fleeing a bioweapon incursion carry the same basic category of weapon, though the specific examples may be wildly different in quality. The principle is universal. The variance in execution is enormous. A craft-tier handgun from a skilled artisan community will outperform a poorly-maintained industrial-tier piece from a collapsed manufacturing operation, and both may be carried by people who would tell you, if asked, that they have a *gun*.

**Longarms** — rifles, repeating carbines, heavy infantry weapons — are the defining instrument of ground combat at most technology tiers. At craft and industrial tier they operate on fundamentally similar principles: a propellant charge drives a projectile down a barrel. The ammunition is the variable that determines most of the practical difference. Industrial-tier production of consistent, high-velocity, reliably-detonating rounds from a standardised manufacturing run produces weapons that outperform even well-made craft-tier examples simply through the consistency of their inputs.

**The important thing about solid-projectile arms** — the thing that separates this setting's relationship with them from any pre-collapse reference point — is scale of access. Communities that have been surviving for thirty thousand years in weapon-saturated environments have had thirty thousand years to copy, manufacture, teach, and proliferate the principles. Solid-projectile arms are not exotic. They are not controlled. They are not rare. A group of five survivors in a deep ruin has guns. This is not remarkable. What is remarkable is when someone *doesn't*.

**Autorepeaters** — magazine-fed automatic or semi-automatic longarms capable of sustained fire — sit at the upper end of craft tier and the lower end of industrial. They are the standard infantry weapon of any faction that has achieved sufficient manufacturing capacity. Their variants are too numerous to catalogue: every region that produces them produces them slightly differently, and thirty thousand years of production means that the accumulated variants constitute a taxonomy unto themselves. Mechanicus-produced autorepeaters are standardised, reliable, and deliberately designed for maintainability by people who understand what maintenance means.

**Shotguns and spread-fire arms** are disproportionately common in certain contexts: the cramped interior corridors of ships and ruins, bioweapon-infested territory where the threat may not wait for a precise shot, and any environment where the user's threat assessment has concluded that hitting *something* in the general direction matters more than precision. A shotgun is not an optimal weapon. It is a weapon that works under suboptimal conditions, and post-collapse Earth is defined by suboptimal conditions.

**Rotary and high-rate weapons** — multiple-barrel or mechanically accelerated variants capable of volumes of fire that single-barrel weapons cannot match — are crew-served at lower technology tiers and individually operated at upper industrial and relic tiers. The mechanical complexity required to sustain high firing rates is itself a technology-tier marker. A faction that can field and maintain high-rate individual weapons across its infantry force has achieved something.

**Heavy solid-projectile weapons** — autocannons, heavy machineguns, large-calibre crew-served pieces — occupy the territory between infantry weapons and vehicle-mounted armament. They require two to four operators to position, supply, and maintain. They can reliably engage light vehicle armour. They are common enough at industrial tier that any well-equipped military force will include them, and experienced enough at relic tier that specific pieces may have names, histories, and maintenance traditions spanning generations.

### Energy Weapons

The fundamental tension with energy weapons is that their principles are understood at a surface level — they convert stored energy into a directed lethal output — and not understood at all at the level that would allow their principles to be independently engineered. Modern energy weapon production is reconstruction work, not original development: someone has access to a working example, or a sufficiently complete schematic, and produces something that replicates its function without necessarily understanding why the function works.

This matters because it means energy weapons exist on a peculiar tier boundary. A community with access to a working example and the manufacturing capacity to replicate it can produce industrial-tier energy weapons. A community without access to that example cannot produce them at all, regardless of manufacturing capacity. The knowledge bottleneck is the limiting factor, not the material one — and knowledge, in this setting, is the scarcest resource.

**Las weapons** — directed-energy weapons that deliver precise burns at combat ranges — are the most common energy weapon category. At baseline function, a las weapon fires a pulse of coherent energy sufficient to burn through clothing, light armour, and organic tissue. At their best, they are accurate, quiet by the standards of solid-projectile arms, and as reliable as their power cell supply. At their worst — which is most of them, most of the time — they are a tradeoff: less stopping power than a solid-projectile weapon of comparable manufacture, more precision, and a power cell that eventually runs dry.

The power cell is the critical variable. Industrial-tier power cells hold enough charge for sustained use. Craft-tier cells vary wildly. Improvised cells have a habit of delivering their charge at moments other than the intended one. Communities that produce las weapons reliably need a stable manufacturing pipeline for cells as much as for the weapons themselves, and the cell pipeline is usually harder to maintain.

**Overcharging** — running a las weapon at higher power than its rated output — is a practice as universal as it is inadvisable. A standard lasgun pushed to maximum charge can reliably punch through armour that its standard output cannot penetrate. The tradeoffs are significant: dramatically reduced shots per cell, heat buildup in the weapon that can damage internal components, and a chance of cell failure that increases sharply with the degree of overcharge. Soldiers have done the calculation and continue to do it. The practice is deprecated in every Mechanicus technical manual, which everyone knows and nobody has ever cared about.

**Plasma weapons** — weapons that generate and project a bolt of electromagnetically contained superheated matter — sit at the boundary of relic and imperial-relic tiers in almost every practical encounter. The engineering required to reliably generate a coherent plasma bolt is at the edge of what modern manufacturing can achieve, which means that plasma weapons encountered in the field are overwhelmingly either Mechanicus-produced relics or surviving imperial arms. The exception is rough-fabricated improvised plasma weapons produced by desperate or ambitious artisans — and these have a reputation that is entirely deserved. An improvised plasma weapon that functions as intended is approximately twice as powerful as any solid-projectile weapon of equivalent size. An improvised plasma weapon that doesn't function as intended has a containment failure that is, in the immediate vicinity, more dangerous than most of what it was intended to fight.

Mechanicus-produced plasma arms are substantially safer than improvised equivalents. They are not safe. The containment systems that prevent a plasma bolt from becoming an uncontrolled release of superheated material are precise, demanding, and subject to failure under sustained use or combat damage in ways that solid-projectile weapons simply are not. A soldier carrying a plasma weapon understands they are carrying something that can kill everything in front of them and also, under specific circumstances, everything directly beside and behind them. They carry it anyway. This is a rational choice. That fact says something about the threat environment they expect to face.

**Melta weapons** — short-range, directed-heat weapons that deliver an intense thermal burst sufficient to cause rapid material breakdown — are encountered primarily as anti-armour tools. Their practical range is measured in metres rather than hundreds of metres. Within that range, they are among the most reliable tools for defeating heavy armour, fortified positions, and anything that relies on density and mass for protection. The thermal effect is difficult to stop through conventional means because it attacks the material properties of the target rather than relying on kinetic transfer. The limitation is unambiguous: you need to be close. In an anti-armour role this is acceptable. In an infantry exchange it is limiting. Most melta weapons are carried specifically for the moments when something very heavy needs to stop being functional, not as general-purpose arms.

**Flamers** — weapons that project and ignite an accelerant at combat range — are not technically energy weapons in the sense that las or plasma weapons are, but they occupy a similar niche in the force structure: specialised, high-effect, terrifying. They are also, unlike plasma and melta, achievable at craft tier with locally available materials, which means they are more widespread than their effect would suggest. A community with a flamer and the supply of accelerant to feed it has a weapon that works against bioweapon fauna, armoured infantry, entrenched positions, and anything else that is susceptible to being on fire.

Flamers are not effective against everything, and their operators are aware of this. The psychological effect on human opponents is significant. The effect on bioweapon organisms that have evolved past sensitivity to thermal damage is negligible and sometimes counterproductive — certain organisms respond to fire as a territory marker rather than a deterrent. Knowing which threat profile you're facing before you commit to a flamer deployment is a survival skill that experienced operators develop quickly.

### Imperial-Tier Ranged Weapons

The empire's personal weapons were not extensions of modern-tier thinking in better materials. They were products of a different understanding of physics — what energy transfer can be made to mean when the engineering is precise enough, when the materials science is genuinely advanced, when the people building the weapon know what they're doing at every level of the process.

**Bolt weapons** — arms that fire self-propelled explosive rounds in the mass-reactive category — are among the most recoverable of imperial personal weapons because the ammunition is the technological component that matters most, and ammunition degrades. An intact imperial bolt weapon is a precision launcher. Without viable ammunition, it is a heavy piece of history. With it, it is a weapon that delivers a small explosion at the point of penetration inside whatever it hits — defeating armour through kinetic penetration and then detonating inside the resistant structure. The combined effect of penetration and internal detonation makes bolt weapons reliably lethal against armoured targets at ranges that solid-projectile weapons can match for precision but not for effect.

The ammunition question limits bolt weapons in practice. Facilities capable of producing mass-reactive explosive rounds are rare, and the rounds consume themselves with use. A force equipped entirely with imperial bolt weapons and no manufacturing pipeline for ammunition eventually becomes a force equipped with very expensive clubs. The factions that maintain viable bolt weapons in active service also maintain, at great difficulty, the manufacturing capability to feed them.

**Phasic weapons** — a category that appears in fragmented imperial technical records under various designations, all of which describe the same basic class of effect — operate on principles that most modern practitioners cannot reconstruct. Where a las weapon burns and a bolt weapon penetrates, a phasic weapon disrupts. The precise physics involved in the disruption is contested even among Mechanicus arcanum specialists, but the observable effect is consistent: targets struck by phasic discharge undergo rapid, catastrophic failure of biological tissue specifically, with variable effects on non-biological material. Accounts from the collapse wars — preserved in fragmentary form — describe phasic weapons that passed through barriers and structures to deliver their effect to the organic occupants without damaging the barriers themselves.

Whether any phasic weapons remain functional is not confirmed. That they existed is documented. The Mechanicus has recovered what appear to be phasic weapon components from sufficiently intact imperial sites and been unable to determine the operational principles from the components alone. If there are working examples somewhere, the people who have them are not announcing it.

### Arcane Weapons

Arcane ability can be weaponised. This is not theoretical — it is demonstrated regularly in the setting, by individuals with sufficient capability and the will to direct it violently. The question of how to categorise arcane violence in weapon terms is complicated by the fact that arcane capability is not a weapon: it is an ability, and like all abilities it can be turned toward violence or away from it.

What *does* fit the category of arcane weapon is devices — physical objects — that have been constructed or modified to channel, contain, or project arcane effect. These range from the crude to the sophisticated.

At the crude end: objects that have been saturated with arcane investment to the point where they carry a charge that can be delivered by contact. The investment is imprecise and the effect is variable. A crude arcane weapon might disrupt biological function, or project heat, or do something entirely unexpected depending on what the investment drew from. Crude arcane weapons are unstable by nature and have a habit of degrading, misfiring, or producing effects their creators did not anticipate.

At the sophisticated end: engineered warp-adjacent devices, usually Mechanicus arcanum products, that use contained binding principles to channel specific warp-adjacent effects through a directed medium. These are rare, poorly understood even by their creators in many cases, and treated with the respect owed to anything that is either a precision instrument or a catastrophic failure waiting for the right conditions. The working principle is similar to the arcanum war machines described in the Mechanicus entry — containment and direction of something that does not naturally want to be contained — scaled to a handheld form. The scaling does not make the fundamental risk smaller.

## Personal Melee Weapons

Melee weapons require a note that is not required for ranged weapons: they never stopped being relevant. The environments of Dead Terra — ruins, tunnels, the insides of structures, encounters with bioweapon fauna that moves faster than a finger can close — frequently collapse the range at which ranged weapons matter. In the void, boarding actions happen in corridors where a rifle is less useful than the person behind it is dangerous. Powered melee capability is not a primitive holdover. It is a specific capability for a specific set of conditions that arise regularly.

**Bladed weapons** of every configuration exist at every tier. A sharp edge made from viable material is achievable at primitive tier and remains valid input to violence at every tier above it. The variation is in material quality — the difference between a blade that holds an edge and one that dulls on first use is a manufacturing question — and in size and configuration suited to the user's body and purpose. There is no universal correct blade. There is the blade that works for the role you intend to use it in.

**Chain-blades** — powered weapons that drive a continuous loop of cutting links along a blade edge — are craft and industrial tier melee weapons that operate on principles simple enough to be locally maintained. The powered cutting surface dramatically increases the weapon's effective damage output against armoured targets and biological tissue alike. The mechanical complexity that drives the chain loop requires maintenance and is susceptible to jamming under certain conditions (viscous organic matter, debris, mechanical damage to the drive mechanism), which experienced users learn to manage and inexperienced users learn about when it matters most. A chain-blade that jams mid-strike is a club. Knowing how to clear a jam without looking at your hands is a skill worth having.

**Power weapons** — weapons incorporating an energy field that disrupts the molecular cohesion of materials on contact — are at the upper end of industrial tier and into relic tier. The field generation requires a power source, field emitters in the weapon's construction, and a control system to maintain the field at the parameters required for the disruption effect. When all of this works, a power weapon will cut through materials that resist solid blades, including armour plating, with a reliability that the physics of the situation would not otherwise support. The mechanism is that the molecular disruption field deals with the resistance before the blade encounters it.

Power weapons at relic tier are frequently ceremonial as well as functional — they are old enough and rare enough that they carry history, and the people who carry them are usually people in positions where carrying a weapon that expensive and unusual sends a message as well as performing a function. This is not always the case. Sometimes someone carrying a power weapon is simply someone who has one and needs it.

**Impact weapons** — hammers, mauls, shock-batons — are underrated by people who have not watched something survive being shot and then not survive being struck very hard. Impact transfers energy in a way that cuts do not, and against targets where density and resilience are defences, a sufficiently heavy impact can be more effective than an edge. Enhanced impact weapons — those incorporating mass-amplification technology, vibration systems, or internal power to deliver a more damaging strike than the wielder's muscles alone could produce — exist at relic and industrial tiers and are particularly common among individuals who need to remain effective against armoured or biologicially hardened targets in close quarters.

**Biological weapons** — melee armament produced or modified by biomantic work — exist in several configurations. The most straightforward are purpose-built organisms (or portions of organisms) intended as weapons: bone or chitinous structures of unusual hardness, venom-delivery mechanisms, biological cutting surfaces that grow in specific configurations. The more sophisticated are modifications made to an individual's own body by a biomancer — integrated claws, hardened striking surfaces, delivery systems for chemical or biological agents. A warform bioservitor's natural weapons are this category's fullest expression at scale. At the individual level, they represent a form of melee capability that cannot be removed by disarming the subject.

## Grenades and Explosives

Explosives occupy a specific tier in this setting that deserves separate treatment: they are simultaneously among the most achievable weapons at primitive tier (black powder, improvised devices, basic incendiaries) and among the most terrifying at imperial tier, and everything between is accessible to someone.

**Fragmentation munitions** — devices that convert chemical energy into a lethal spray of material at range — are produced at craft and industrial tier with reasonable reliability. Their effectiveness is real and limited: they require proximity, they are not targeted in any precise sense, and their effect against armoured targets is strongly range-dependent. What they reliably achieve is area denial and high casualties against unarmoured personnel. They remain the most common thrown explosive at every tier because the manufacturing requirement is achievable and the effectiveness in the intended use case is consistent.

**Incendiary devices** — designed to project and ignite accelerant rather than fragment — are the thrown counterpart to the flamer and share its threat profile: effective against biologicals and structures, less effective against armour, with the additional utility that a burning area remains dangerous after the device is spent.

**Haywire munitions** — designed to disrupt electronic systems through electromagnetic pulse — are a craft and industrial tier product that has found specific utility in certain tactical contexts. Against heavily augmented cyberservitors and mechanised targets, haywire effect can disable systems in ways that physical damage would not achieve without destroying the target entirely. Against biological targets without augmetics, haywire effect achieves nothing. The tactical usefulness depends entirely on what you're fighting. Knowing what you're about to fight well enough to pack the right munitions is the less glamorous half of effective equipment selection.

**Anti-armour devices** — melta charges, shaped penetrators, focused-explosive demolition tools — are the portable equivalents of anti-armour weapons at larger scale. A melta charge placed on a vehicle or fortified position delivers the same thermal assault as a melta weapon at point-blank range, without requiring someone to stand at point-blank range while aiming. The placement requirement is its own tactical challenge. This category's upper end is Mechanicus and relic-tier precision devices capable of defeating armour that melta weapons at personal scale cannot.

**Nuclear devices at tactical scale** are real, available, and in this setting, not exotic. The empire's collapse involved orbital strikes, engineered plagues, and weapons that nobody fully understood. Thirty thousand years later, the know-how to produce devices that induce nuclear reactions has survived in enough independent knowledge lineages — some well-understood, some ritualistic, some dangerously improvised — that tactical nuclear ordnance is a category that serious military planners in this setting do not dismiss as impossible. Factions with intact Mechanicus support can produce nuclear devices with the controlled-yield properties required for tactical use rather than straightforward city-levelling. Factions without that support have produced cruder equivalents with correspondingly cruder yield control, which is a polite way of saying that the safe range is considerably more conservative.

The relevant cultural point is this: a species that used weapons like this on itself, in its home system, has not forgotten that it used weapons like this on itself. The knowledge is part of the substrate of human violence the way that the knowledge of fire is part of cooking. It would not occur to most communities to be surprised that the option exists. The question is always whether the tactical situation justifies the cost and the aftermath — and sometimes, the answer communities have arrived at is yes.

## Personal Armour and Protection

Protection in the setting is subject to the same technology-tier logic as weapons: the principles of stopping things from killing you are understood at every level, but the execution varies enormously, and the upper tiers produce protection that the lower tiers cannot approach.

### Clothing and Baseline Protection

Below formal armour is the accumulated practical wisdom of communities that have learned, over generations, which materials in their environment offer some protection against which threats. This is not nothing. Heavy canvas impregnated with chemically treated resins. Work clothing incorporating salvaged fibre-composite layers. Head and neck protection improvised from whatever holds up under impact. Communities in bioweapon-heavy environments have developed clothing conventions that provide meaningful resistance to the penetration methods of local fauna while remaining wearable for extended periods — which conventional armour often does not manage.

### Flak and Light Protection

Standard infantry protection at craft and industrial tier: rigid or semi-rigid plates at critical points over softer backing that absorbs distributed impact. Flak protection of quality construction will reliably stop or degrade solid-projectile rounds that are not armour-piercing. It will reduce the severity of fragmentation wounds. It will not stop las burns and does minimal work against plasma or melta effects. It is common because the manufacturing requirement is achievable and the protection it offers against the most commonly encountered threats is genuine.

What distinguishes good flak from poor flak is the backing and distribution. Poorly designed flak protection stops the round but delivers the kinetic energy into the wearer as a blunt impact that can be as incapacitating as penetration at close range. Well-designed backing distributes the transferred energy. Most military-grade Mechanicus production achieves the latter. Most locally-produced equivalents achieve whatever the local craftsperson has worked out through iteration, which is variable.

### Carapace and Hardplate

The intermediate tier — significant rigid protection at most body regions, with joints and articulation remaining as the compromise. Carapace-grade armour is the standard equipment of professional military forces with access to industrial or relic-tier manufacturing: solid front, back, and limb coverage in interlocking plates that maintain protection without entirely sacrificing movement. Against standard-tier weaponry, carapace armour shifts the encounter significantly in the armoured party's favour. Against armour-defeating weapons — heavy solid-projectile anti-armour rounds, plasma, melta, high-powered las — carapace provides meaningful time rather than reliable protection.

The practical limiting factor of carapace armour is sustained use. Full carapace is heavy. Wearing it for hours of active movement is exhausting in ways that light flak is not. Military forces that equip all infantry with carapace need to account for the fatigue factor and ensure their personnel are conditioned accordingly. The forces that figure this out are more effective than the ones that don't, which seems obvious and is consistently learned through experience.

### Void-Sealed and Environment-Hardened Armour

A category worth treating separately because the requirements are different: armour designed for hostile environments — void, irradiated zones, chemically toxic atmospheres — must seal against the environment while also providing physical protection. These are conflicting requirements in terms of construction. Sealing against vacuum or toxic atmosphere requires material integrity at every joint; physical protection benefits from articulation. Reconciling these requirements is an engineering problem that has been solved differently at every tier.

At craft tier: sealed suits with external armour layers added, usually reducing mobility significantly and often uncomfortable to wear for extended periods. At industrial tier: integrated void armour where the seal and the protection are designed together rather than added to each other, producing equipment that functions adequately in both roles without excelling in either. At relic tier: sealed armour of Mechanicus production where the materials and construction approach what the empire considered standard void-equipment, offering genuine protection and genuine environmental sealing in a package that is wearable for extended duty.

At imperial relic tier: powered void armour — suits that incorporate mechanical assistance to carry their own weight, internal atmosphere recycling, sealed sensor systems that replace or supplement biological senses, and structural integrity that can absorb weapons fire that would defeat everything below it. The number of working examples in existence is not large. The people who have them do not lend them out.

### Powered Combat Armour

At relic and imperial-relic tier, armour incorporates power systems that allow the suit to carry its own weight and amplify the wearer's movement rather than restrict it. A fully sealed powered combat suit removes most of the physical limitation problem: the wearer is not carrying the suit's weight because the suit is carrying itself. The wearer is not fighting against the restriction of plate because the suit is moving with their body. What is gained is protection levels that would be immobilising in unpowered equivalent, combined with sustained operational capability that unpowered heavy armour cannot provide.

The catch is maintenance. A powered suit is a complex system. Seals degrade. Power systems require charge and eventually component replacement. The mechanical assistance systems have failure modes that unpowered armour does not — an unpowered suit that takes damage stops being good protection; a powered suit that takes specific damage may actively work against its wearer. The Mechanicus treats powered armour maintenance as a speciality requiring a dedicated chirurgen-equivalent for the suit rather than the soldier. Communities that have such suits but lack the technical support to maintain them tend to lose the suits to attrition and find themselves unable to explain exactly what went wrong.

### Bio-Armour and Biological Protection

Biomancers working in a warform context produce protection through biological means rather than manufactured ones. Bio-armour — subdermal chitin, thickened hide, integrated bone plating, skin chemistry that resists specific chemical threats — has the fundamental advantage of being self-repairing given time and adequate nutrition. A crack in a biological armour layer heals if the organism is healthy. A crack in a carapace plate does not.

Bio-armour at the moderate end is comparable in protective value to industrial-grade flak with some of the properties of carapace at critical points. At the heavy end — purpose-built warform biology — it approaches and in specific configurations exceeds the protective value of non-powered carapace armour, while being integrated into a body that can run, climb, fit through spaces that an equivalent armoured soldier cannot, and does not require external maintenance.

The limitation is that bio-armour takes time to modify and time to repair. A conventional suit of armour that has been damaged can be patched in the field. A warform with damaged armour biology needs time, adequate food, and ideally a biomancer to address significant damage properly. The logistics of biological armour in a military context are different from conventional armour logistics, and forces that don't account for those differences discover what they missed at inconvenient moments.

## Void Equipment

The void presents threats that ground-based equipment does not address and for which ground-based equipment provides no protection. A soldier equipped for a planetary surface can still die in the first minute of a void exposure. Equipment intended for void operations is a speciality category with its own requirements.

### Void Suits

Every function that a void suit must perform is non-negotiable: atmospheric seal, thermal regulation, pressure maintenance. Fail any one of them and the environment resolves the situation within minutes. Good void suits accomplish all three reliably, for extended periods, with fail-safe redundancy. Poor void suits accomplish all three unreliably, for shorter periods, with the kind of failure modes that have produced cautionary traditions in every void-faring community.

At Mechanicus production quality, a void suit is a sealed personal environment with power assist, integrated communication, medical monitoring, and the kind of seal integrity testing that allows the operator to trust the equipment. At locally-produced quality, a void suit is the best the producing community could manage with what they had, and the margin between that and failure is proportional to the care that went into its construction.

Void suits are not armour. They provide minimal physical protection while providing the environmental protection without which physical protection is irrelevant. The combination of void suit and armour is a separate design problem — see sealed armour above — and every solution to it involves compromises.

**Void suit weapons** — arms specifically designed not to breach hull or armour integrity while remaining lethal to biological targets — are a specialised category produced for boarding action contexts. The physics of creating something that can kill a person without creating a hull breach is solvable, and it has been solved in a number of ways. Solutions include: directed energy weapons calibrated to biological penetration depths, solid-projectile arms loaded with frangible or low-velocity rounds that lose integrity on contact with hull material rather than penetrating it, and melee options (which never risked hull integrity to begin with). Each solution involves a tradeoff. Which tradeoff the force accepts depends on what they expect to encounter and how much they trust their operators' fire discipline.

## General Field Equipment

### Optical Enhancement

Seeing better than baseline human vision allows is a meaningful tactical advantage, and the demand for it has produced optical enhancement devices at every tier. Basic magnification optics — scopes, binoculars — are achievable at craft tier and ubiquitous at industrial. Low-light enhancement, thermal imaging, electromagnetic spectrum perception — these enter at industrial-relic tier and improve significantly at relic. Imperial optical systems integrated into helmets and suit visors extend perceptual capability into ranges and spectra that require augmetic modification to approach without the equipment.

The practical question operators frequently ask about any optical enhancement system is: what happens when it fails? An operator who has become reliant on thermal imaging to navigate a dark environment and whose thermal imaging fails at the wrong moment is worse off than one who developed their own low-light operational capability and brought the thermal imaging as a supplement. The equipment augments competence. It does not substitute for it. This is the position that experienced operators take. It is also consistently ignored by inexperienced operators until something happens.

### Medical Equipment

Field medicine in the setting ranges from reasonably sophisticated at Mechanicus-adjacent tiers to aggressively pragmatic at primitive tier. The specific toolset that travels with any individual or unit reflects their expected operational environment and what they have access to.

**Stimulants and stabilisers** — chemical compounds that interrupt shock, sustain consciousness under trauma, and suppress pain sufficiently for function — are produced at every tier from locally foraged materials upward. What separates tiers is reliability and side-effect profile. Industrial and Mechanicus-grade stimulants deliver predictable effect at predictable doses with manageable aftereffects. Locally produced equivalents work until they don't, and the ones that don't are sometimes worse than no intervention.

**Wound closure** — everything from pressure bandages to chemical sealants to bioengineered healing accelerants — similarly spans tiers. A Mechanicus medicae-produced wound sealant that promotes tissue adhesion and reduces infection risk is categorically superior to battlefield improvisation, but battlefield improvisation is what most people have. The minimum viable kit is something that stops the bleeding long enough for the person to get somewhere with better resources or to die in an orderly fashion.

**Rad-treatment and toxin management** — mandatory kit in much of Dead Terra's operational environment — are among the categories most influenced by biomancer knowledge versus Mechanicus knowledge. Biomancer-produced compounds addressing specific bioweapon toxins may not be reproducible at industrial tier because the mechanism is partly arcane. This creates situations where a community with biomancer access has better rad-treatment than a community with Mechanicus support, which would be counterintuitive if both sources weren't approaching the problem from genuinely different directions.

### Communication Equipment

Communication equipment is limited in Dead Terra by the same physics that limit it everywhere: the planet's electromagnetic environment has been significantly disrupted by thirty thousand years of accumulated weapons effects, atmospheric alteration, and residual active systems still doing whatever they were built to do. Short-range communication is reliable at industrial tier. Medium-range is less reliable. Long-range across contested or disrupted electromagnetic environments requires either physical relay networks, specific technology to navigate the interference, or acceptance that your communications will be intermittent.

In the void, communication is a different problem. Local communications within a ship or station are straightforward. Ship-to-ship within a system depends on the equipment quality and range. Beyond system scale, see the standalone Communication entry.

### Power Cells and Energy Supply

The unglamorous infrastructure of any energy-dependent equipment loadout. Las weapons need charged cells. Powered armour needs running power. Haywire devices need energy to convert. Optical enhancement systems drain their sources at rates that vary by equipment quality.

The supply question for energy-dependent equipment is the question that determines whether a force can sustain its capability over time or will revert to its non-energy-dependent options as cells deplete. Forces that manage this well treat energy logistics with the same attention as ammunition logistics. Forces that manage this poorly discover the importance of having backup options when everything runs dry on the third day.

Mechanicus-produced power cells are consistent, reliable, and designed for field recharging from multiple source types where possible. Locally produced cells vary. Improvised cells should be treated with appropriate caution. Imperial-era cells, where they survive functional, hold charge at densities that modern manufacturing cannot replicate — a functional imperial power cell is a valuable object even for a faction that has no imperial weapons to use it in, because someone else probably does, and trade is always an option.

## Faction Profiles

### Mechanicus Forces

Mechanicus military forces are better-equipped than most equivalents at the same size, as a general rule. The institution's access to manufacturing knowledge and its religious commitment to maintaining equipment correctly produces arms and armour of consistent industrial-to-relic-tier quality. Skitarii carry Mechanicus-produced weapons that are standardised for maintenance by their own operators; the logistics of maintaining that standardisation is something the institution has spent considerable effort achieving.

The exception to the superiority assumption is mass. The Mechanicus does not have unlimited manufacturing capacity. A very large secular army may simply outproduce what the Mechanicus can field, even if each Mechanicus weapon and armour piece is superior to its equivalent. Numbers matter. This is something the Mechanicus knows and factors into its military doctrine — which generally prefers quality-multiplier approaches and reserves mass engagement for situations where mass cannot be avoided.

### Warlord and Faction Forces

The most common military organisation on Dead Terra: locally-raised forces, equipped with locally-produced weapons of variable quality, reflecting the specific capabilities of the warlord's territory and manufacturing access. A warlord who controls a region with surviving Mechanicus installations may field forces equipped to industrial or relic tier. A warlord with no such access fields forces equipped to craft tier, and supplements with scavenged weapons where they can be found and maintained.

The key characteristic of warlord forces is variability. A single unit may contain a mix of primitive, craft, and occasional relic-tier equipment depending on who recovered what and how long ago. This creates maintenance challenges — you cannot standardise a maintenance procedure across a unit with five different weapon types — and tactical uncertainty — what your unit can actually do depends on which pieces of equipment are currently functional.

### Void-Faring Crews

Ships' armories reflect the operational requirements of the crew. Boarding action capability, which requires compact, high-effect weapons in confined spaces, is prioritised. Long-range engagement, which is the ship's own weapons' domain, is not. A rogue trader's crew may carry an eclectic mix of personal arms reflecting the varied backgrounds of crew members rather than any standardisation policy. A Mechanicus fleet's ship-board security forces will be standardised to the institution's specifications. Either approach has implications for what happens when things go wrong aboard.

Void crews broadly develop competence with sealed suit operation alongside their weapons proficiency, because the two are inseparable in void operational contexts. A ship's-crew fighter who cannot fight effectively in a void suit is poorly prepared for the most likely environment in which they'll need to fight. This is also consistently learned through experience rather than in advance, despite every experienced void crew member's attempts to explain it.

## The Maintenance Question

The setting's defining relationship with weapons is maintenance. Not design. Not production. Maintenance.

The empire's weapons still exist, in various states, because the people who inherited them kept them functional. The knowledge required to keep a piece of relic-tier or imperial-relic-tier equipment functional has, in most cases, taken the form of ritual — specific procedures, performed in specific sequence, using specific materials, with specific spoken or symbolic accompaniments. The practitioners performing the rituals have not always understood what they were doing at the engineering level. They have understood that not doing it results in the weapon failing, and they have understood that doing it results in the weapon functioning. The practical result is that weapons which should have degraded into uselessness over thousands of years have not, because the maintenance procedures that kept them functional were preserved as religious observance when they could not be preserved as technical knowledge.

This is more robust than it sounds. A ritual that is exactly correct produces exactly the same effect as a procedure performed from first-principles understanding. The weapon does not know the difference. The Mechanicus, which approaches this from the understanding end rather than the ritual end, finds the phenomenon both useful and frustrating — useful because communities preserving weapon maintenance as religious practice have kept equipment functional that would otherwise be irreplaceable, and frustrating because the same communities are often resistant to any change to the ritual even when the Mechanicus can demonstrate that a modification would improve the outcome.

The weapons that have not survived are the ones whose maintenance rituals were lost — through community collapse, through the death of knowledge lineages, through the specific misfortune of the people who knew the ritual dying before they passed it on. These weapons corrode in ruins. They are what's left when the maintenance stops. They are also the things that archaeological teams go looking for, because occasionally a sufficiently intact weapon can be reconstructed or restored from a non-functional state — and a Mechanicus team with genuine technical understanding is in a better position to do this than any ritual-only practitioner.

The meta-point about maintenance is this: in a setting where thirty thousand years of accumulated violence have produced weapons at every tier, from primitive to incomprehensible, the limiting factor on what a community can field is not the weapons themselves. Weapons are everywhere. Ruins are full of them. The limiting factor is whether anyone knows how to keep them working, and whether anyone has managed to ensure that knowledge outlives the person who currently holds it.

Every community that is currently functional has solved this problem, at least partially, for the weapons they depend on. The solutions are as varied as the communities. What the solutions share is that they work — until they don't, and then there's a gap that may be impossible to close.
