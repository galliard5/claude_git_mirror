# Imperial World Herds — Background Notes

*Status: seed notes. Not yet developed lore. To be expanded later.*

## The Observation

On dozens of old Imperial worlds, expeditions consistently document large herds of cattle-like grazing animals. The same general body plan turns up everywhere — clearly an engineered organism that escaped into the wild at some point and has undergone localised evolution across the thirty-thousand-plus years since the Fall. Local drift varies (size, colouring, climate adaptation, scale density), but the underlying template is recognisably the same animal wherever it's found. Loose 40K analogue: grox.

## Physical Form

Use **Gemszi's converted form** as the reference template — see `World_Building/Gallihammer/Rogue_Trader/Scenarios/Gemszi_Session_01.md` (Scene 3 fragments, the conversion sequence) for the body plan in detail.

Key traits:
- Large, strong grazing herbivore
- Thick scaled hide with armoured plating along vulnerable lines
- Adaptable to wide temperature ranges
- Quadrupedal, deep barrel, heavy mass-bearing skeletal architecture
- Built for production output — draught capability, dairy capacity, meat mass

## Sexual / Caste Pattern

**Wild populations:** Normal two-sex sexual reproduction. Stable breeding herds that have sustained themselves for tens of thousands of years post-Fall without external input.

**Captured / domesticated populations:** Tendency to produce a third neuter form — the same form Gemszi has. Larger, draught-built, calibrated for dairy or labour output. This third form does *not* appear in wild populations; it emerges when post-collapse domesticators apply biomantic techniques to existing herd stock.

The third form not appearing in the wild is itself the clue.

## The Worlds Where They're Found

A consistent pattern across documented sites:

- Previously Imperial-settled — ruins, infrastructure, sometimes still partly functional
- The sapient population disappeared at some point during the Fall
- **Minimal planetary damage** — no bombardment glassing, no nuclear scarring, no orbital weapon impact craters
- Infrastructure largely intact (weathered, looted over millennia, but not destroyed)
- The herds are present

The damage profile is wrong for the worlds the Empire bombed into compliance. These worlds were not destroyed. Their populations were *removed*, but the planet was preserved.

## Cross-Reference — Reshaping Cascade Weapons

See `World_Building/Gallihammer/Equipment/Reshaping_Cascade_Weapons_Detailed.md`, particularly **Pattern Omega (Stock Track)**: population-scale conversion into biological production assets, infrastructure preserved by design, used in lieu of bombardment when the Empire wanted to keep what the population was sitting on.

The end-form a Pattern Omega cascade produced and the end-form Vossk produced in Gemszi are the same end-form.

The hypothesis these notes are circling — to develop or revise later:

> The herds are the descendants of Pattern Omega cascade deployments. Entire planetary populations were converted into Stock Track bioservitors. Their handlers and the broader Imperial logistics they were attached to disappeared during the Fall. The converted populations went feral. Over thirty thousand years they stabilised into self-sustaining wild herds with normalised sexual reproduction — losing the artificial neuter caste because no one was producing it any more.
>
> When post-collapse domesticators capture wild stock, modern (limited, fragmentary) biomancy is applied — and the third neuter form reappears. The same form the Empire produced at planetary scale.

## Open Questions

- How widely is this understood in-setting? Mechanicus archaeologists piecing it together is one answer; outright denial is another; deliberate suppression a third.
- Vestigial cognition in the wild herds? Likely no after thirty millennia of selection for herd-animal survival, but worth deciding.
- Drift correlation — do cold-world variants, high-gravity variants, etc. correlate with which Pattern Omega sub-pattern was deployed (if there were sub-patterns)?
- Cultural/ritual implications for settlements that eat meat from these herds. Varies enormously by what those settlements know or believe.
- Worlds where something else *also* survived alongside the herds — bioservitor overseers, automated handling systems still cycling, etc.
- Worlds where the herds went further than feral — apex predator pressure, behavioural shifts, anything complicating the "they're just cattle" reading.
