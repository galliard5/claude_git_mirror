Si'ken
======

Remade Scout. Formerly kobold. Insectoid hybrid chassis with integrated Mechanicus cybernetic utilities. Valdrekk asset, assigned to the Proving Ground expedition prior to first deployment.

---

## The Original

Si'ken was a kobold. What kind of kobold — warren-born, surface-adapted, which strain, which community — is part of the prologue. The name is kobold. It survived the reshaping, which means either the control architecture didn't target identity anchors or the specialist left it deliberately.

What matters for the sheet: Si'ken had a life before this. A name, a people, a body that was small and scaled and built along lines that thirty thousand years of kobold evolution had refined for their own purposes. That body is gone. The name is what's left — the thread that connects what Si'ken is now to what Si'ken was before someone with enough money and enough indifference decided they wanted something else.

---

## The Reshaping

**Method:** Hybrid. Primarily biological reshaping — the body is organic, grown and grafted and restructured at the cellular level by a specialist with biologis-grade capability. The insectoid architecture is living tissue: chitin-analogue exoskeletal plating, restructured musculature, modified sensory organs, a body plan that bears no resemblance to the kobold original. Layered into this biological framework are Mechanicus-standard cybernetic systems: a neural interface jack (NIJack), mechadendrite utility appendages integrated into the thoracic structure, and the associated processing architecture that makes them functional.

This combination is significant. A biologis who reshapes a subject into an insectoid form is working within one discipline — extreme, but comprehensible. A biologis who also installs Mechanicus cybernetic interfaces during the reshaping is either cross-trained (biologis and cyberwright, rare and valuable) or working alongside a cyberwright partner. Either way, the specialist who made Si'ken had access to Mechanicus technology and the knowledge to integrate it into biological tissue during the reshaping process rather than after it.

This tells us something about the patron: they had Mechanicus connections deep enough to source the components and the expertise. And it tells us something about the purpose: whatever the patron wanted Si'ken for, it required a biological organism that could interface with Mechanicus systems natively.

**Archetype:** Bespoke to the extreme.  The patron's specification was custom — not a standard warform, labour-form, or trophy commission. The insectoid body plan with integrated cybernetic utilities suggests a purpose that required:

- Mobility in environments hostile to baseline organisms (the insectoid form's environmental tolerance)
- Access to spaces too small, too unstable, or too dangerous for standard crew (the body's size and flexibility)
- The ability to interface with Mechanicus systems, record data, and perform technical tasks (the NIJack and mechadendrites)
- Cognizance — a thinking mind directing all of the above

Possible patron purposes (to be resolved in prologue):
- **Infiltration asset** — an organism that can penetrate secured Mechanicus facilities through maintenance spaces, interface with their systems, and extract data without being identified as a person
- **Recovery tool** — a biological probe sent into ruins, wrecks, and hazardous sites to retrieve technology and data that the patron's baseline crew cannot safely access
- **Surveillance instrument** — a cognisant organism installed in the patron's own operation to monitor systems, personnel, and spaces that conventional surveillance cannot reach
- **Research subject** — the integration of Mechanicus cybernetics into biological reshaping was itself the point; Si'ken was a proof of concept, and the patron's interest was in the technique rather than the result

The specific purpose shapes the prologue — what the patron wanted determines what Si'ken's daily existence looked like before escape or acquisition, and what capabilities were built in to serve that purpose.

## The Current Form

Si'ken's body is insectoid — an arthropod-derived body plan that has no relationship to the kobold original. This is not a kobold with chitin plates. It is an insect-form organism with a kobold's brain.

**Scale:** Very small. small enough to fit in a terrarium. The exact dimensions depend on the specific insectoid architecture, but the body plan prioritises compactness — this is a form built to go where larger organisms cannot. The size is an asset for scouting: ducts, collapsed structures, narrow gaps between debris, the spaces inside walls and between floors that larger crew members cannot access.

**Exoskeleton:** Chitin-analogue plating covers the body — segmented, articulated, providing structural support and moderate environmental protection. The plating is organic, grown as part of the reshaping, and maintains itself biologically. Coloration is likely dark — browns, blacks, deep reds — the natural palette of the biological material, not deliberately chosen for aesthetics.

**Limbs:** Multiple — the insectoid body plan provides more limbs than the kobold original, with specialisation by function. Locomotion limbs, manipulation limbs, and the integrated mechadendrite utilities occupy different positions on the body. The mechadendrites emerge from the thoracic segment alongside or between the organic limbs — cybernetic appendages rooted in biological tissue, their interface points where metal meets chitin visible as seams of polymer and surgical bonding.

**Sensory organs:** Restructured. The insectoid form carries sensory organs appropriate to its architecture — compound or multi-faceted visual organs, antennae or analogous structures for chemical and vibration sensing, possibly pressure-sensitive organs along the body for spatial awareness in enclosed spaces. The specifics emerge through play as Si'ken discovers what the new senses can do.

**The NIJack:** Integrated into the head or upper thoracic segment — a Mechanicus neural interface jack wired into Si'ken's brain through the biological tissue of the reshaped body. It functions as standard: communication, personal tools, datasphere access when available, and the foundation for interfacing with compatible Mechanicus systems. The NIJack is the most visible piece of the cybernetic integration — a metal port set into chitin, the technological origin unmistakable against the organic architecture.

**Mechadendrite utilities:** Two utility mechadendrites integrated into the thoracic structure, emerging from hardpoints where the cybernetic mounts were bonded to the exoskeletal frame during reshaping. These are not full enginseer-grade rigs — they are utility appendages, capable of fine manipulation, tool use, and basic technical work. They extend Si'ken's manipulation capability beyond what the organic limbs provide, and their precision is mechanical rather than biological. They were installed for a purpose: the patron needed Si'ken to interact with Mechanicus technology, and the mechadendrites are the interface that makes that possible.

**Voice:** Unknown. The reshaping may have preserved vocal capability, modified it, or removed it. If Si'ken can speak, the voice will not sound kobold. If Si'ken cannot speak, the NIJack provides text-based communication. This is a detail for the prologue to establish.

## How Valdrekk Acquired Si'ken

Si'ken was assigned to the Proving Ground by Forge-Monastery Valdrekk. The monastery acquired Si'ken through channels that were not fully explained to the expedition's crew.

What is known:
- Si'ken arrived at Valdrekk before the Proving Ground's first deployment — during the preparation phase, after the ship was converted but before it received its first targeting data from the Deep Stacks programme.
- Fabricator-Primus Ardenne personally authorised the assignment. The decision was not delegated.
- Magos Biologis Ursa Thenn conducted the initial assessment — medical, capability, cognitive evaluation. Thenn's report to Ardenne is not in the expedition's records.
- Si'ken was presented to the expedition crew as a specialist scout asset. No origin details were provided. Questions about Si'ken's background were met with institutional silence — not hostility, simply the absence of answers.

What is not known (by the crew):
- How Valdrekk obtained Si'ken — purchase, recovery, seizure, trade, or gift
- Who the patron was — or whether the patron is still alive
- Who the specialist was — or whether they had a relationship with Valdrekk
- Whether Si'ken's control architecture is fully inactive or merely dormant
- What Ardenne's actual interest in Si'ken is — the scout function is real, but a Fabricator-Primus does not personally authorise asset assignments for purely operational reasons

What Si'ken knows is part of the prologue. The gap between what Si'ken knows, what Valdrekk knows, and what the crew knows is a persistent narrative thread.

## The Scout Role

Si'ken's assignment to the expedition is as a field scout — first into sites, mapping environments, assessing hazards, reporting conditions before the main team commits.

The insectoid form is genuinely suited to this work:
- **Size and mobility** — Si'ken can access spaces the rest of the team cannot: ventilation systems, collapsed passages, gaps in debris, the interior architecture of structures that have partially failed
- **Environmental tolerance** — the insectoid body's biological resilience (chitin plating, sealed respiratory system, temperature tolerance) allows operation in conditions that would require the rest of the team to suit up or withdraw
- **Sensory capability** — the restructured sensory organs provide environmental awareness that human senses cannot match: chemical detection, vibration sensing, spatial mapping through pressure differentials
- **Cybernetic interface** — the NIJack and mechadendrites allow Si'ken to interact with Mechanicus systems encountered in sites, providing preliminary data-interface capability that other scouts lack
- **Low signature** — small, quiet, and inhuman enough that the automated security systems in pre-collapse facilities may not register Si'ken as a recognised threat category

The team needs Si'ken. This is the foundation of the relationship — not comfort, not acceptance, but practical dependency. Si'ken goes in first and comes back with information that keeps everyone else alive. Everything else — the social dynamics, the theological questions, the crew's individual responses to having a Remade aboard — builds on top of that functional reality.

## Crew Dynamics — Starting Position

Si'ken arrives aboard the Proving Ground before the first deployment. The crew has time to form impressions before the field work begins.

**Hale** — Made the operational decision to accept the assignment after Ardenne's authorisation. Hale's interest is professional: what can Si'ken do that his other scouts cannot? The answer, demonstrated during pre-deployment evaluation, was convincing. Hale treats Si'ken as a specialist asset — valued for capability, assessed by results. Personal warmth is not Hale's default register with anyone, and Si'ken is not an exception.

**Oenthe** — The ship master's response is practical discomfort. Si'ken is a non-standard crew member aboard Oenthe's ship — quarters need modification, life support parameters need adjustment, the ship's routines need to accommodate a body that doesn't fit the spaces designed for baseline crew. Oenthe handles this with professional competence and does not pretend to be comfortable with it. The discomfort is not hostility. It is the friction of a perfectionist forced to adapt a system she considers already optimised.

**Voss** — Curiosity tempered by professional focus. Voss has spent her career studying what the empire built — Si'ken is, in a sense, something that was built. The archaeologist's instinct to examine and understand is visible and, if unchecked, could produce interactions that feel more like assessment than conversation. Voss is aware of this tendency and moderates it imperfectly.

**Vos** — The chief enginseer's interest is technical. Si'ken carries Mechanicus cybernetic systems integrated into biological tissue — this is Vos's professional domain, the interface between machine and organism. Vos will want to inspect the mechadendrites, assess the NIJack integration, understand how the cybernetic components interact with the biological architecture. Whether this attention feels like maintenance or violation depends on how it's approached, and Vos's sensitivity to this distinction is untested.

**Harsk** — The skitarii alpha's response is operational. Si'ken is a unit in Harsk's field security planning — an asset with capabilities and vulnerabilities that need to be accounted for. Harsk will assess Si'ken the way he assesses any new element: what can it do, what can't it do, where does it go in the formation, what happens if it goes down. The assessment is impersonal because Harsk's professional framework is impersonal. Respect comes from demonstrated field performance, and Si'ken has not yet demonstrated anything.

**Cade** — The logik's response is minimal. Cade is difficult to reach socially under the best circumstances, and a non-standard crew member does not change the calculus. Si'ken's NIJack capability is noted — another interface-capable operative is functionally useful — but Cade's engagement will be through data rather than conversation.

**Pael** — The archivist observes. Si'ken's presence aboard is documented. The circumstances of the assignment — Ardenne's personal authorisation, the absence of origin details, the theological implications of a Remade individual serving on a Mechanicus vessel — are noted with the thoroughness that Pael applies to everything. Pael's reports to Ardenne will include assessment of Si'ken's integration with the crew. Whether Pael's personal view of Si'ken is compassionate, clinical, or something else is not visible through the professional surface.

**Thrace** — The chirugen's response is immediate and medical. Si'ken is a patient with non-standard physiology that Thrace has not been trained for. Thrace will insist on a full medical assessment before Si'ken is cleared for duty — and the assessment will be thorough, because Thrace does not clear anyone for duty without understanding what she's working with. The process may be invasive. Thrace will not apologise for this. She will also not be cruel about it, and if Si'ken is injured in the field, Thrace will fight to save them with the same ferocity she brings to any patient, regardless of how well her training prepared her for insectoid biology.

**Notes on starting state:**

The tree is deliberately sparse. Si'ken has been reshaped — the body is new, the senses are new, the interface with the world is fundamentally different from what the kobold knew. Most of the foundation trees begin at zero not because Si'ken is incompetent but because competence in the old body does not automatically translate to competence in this one.

**Perception I** carries over because awareness is cognitive as much as sensory — the instinct to notice, to track, to assess environment. The *method* of perception has changed (new sensory organs, new data), but the *habit* of perception survives the reshaping.

**Survival I** carries over because the drive to survive is deeper than any body plan. Si'ken endured the reshaping. Si'ken endured whatever came after. The survival instinct is proven.

**Essence** initializes at the reshaping — the moment Si'ken's fundamental nature changed. The tree is unnamed until the prologue establishes what the experience felt like from the inside. The first branch will develop through play as Si'ken learns to inhabit the insectoid form.

Additional starting nodes may be added after the prologue establishes Si'ken's pre-reshaping background. A kobold who was a tunnel-runner might bring Edge/Stealth I. A kobold who worked with their hands might bring Craft/Making I. The prologue decides.

## The Prologue

The origin session covers:

1. **Who was Si'ken?** — The kobold before the reshaping. Community, role, the life that was taken. How Si'ken lost autonomy (debt, capture, sale, abandonment — the Remade Origin Framework variables).

2. **The patron.** — Who commissioned the reshaping. What they wanted. Why they wanted it cognisant. Their resources, their Mechanicus connections, their attitude toward the subject.

3. **The specialist.** — Who did the work. Their discipline (biologis with cyberwright cross-training, or a biologis/cyberwright team). Their attitude — reluctant, curious, sympathetic, complicit. The flaw they left.

4. **The reshaping.** — The process. Duration, awareness, pain. The moment the kobold body ended and the insectoid form began. The first experience of the new senses. The first movement of the mechadendrites. The NIJack coming online.

5. **The flaw.** — What the specialist left. May be known to the player and not the character, or unknown to both. Biological, cybernetic, or a hybrid flaw that exploits the junction between the two systems.

6. **The transition to Valdrekk.** — How Si'ken went from the patron's asset to the monastery's. What happened — escape, acquisition, seizure, trade. What Valdrekk knows that Si'ken doesn't. What Si'ken knows that Valdrekk doesn't.

7. **Arrival aboard the Proving Ground.** — First impressions. The crew's reaction. Thrace's medical assessment. The quarters that had to be modified. The first time Si'ken moved through the ship and discovered what the insectoid body could access that the baseline crew could not.

The prologue ends with Si'ken aboard, assessed, assigned, and waiting for the expedition's first targeting data from Magos Loth's Deep Stacks analysis. The campaign begins.
