### Magos Excavatus Torrin Hale — Expedition Commander

**Race:** Human
**Age:** 52
**Status:** Expedition Commander, Proving Ground. Magos-rank archaeologist with secondary logik training.
**Augmentation:** Moderate. Neural interface suite (Valdrekk standard), augmetic left eye (multi-spectrum, installed after field injury), reinforced skeletal grafts in both forearms and hands (occupational — years of excavation work), mechadendrite utility rig (two arms, retracted under coat when not in use). Internal rad-hardening. The augmentation is functional rather than demonstrative — Hale looks like a working archaeologist who has spent decades in the field and carries the modifications that kept him alive doing it.

**Appearance:** Medium height, lean, weather-beaten in a way that comes from planetary fieldwork rather than void exposure. The augmetic eye is obvious — a dark lens set in brushed steel housing that doesn't pretend to be organic. His remaining natural eye is grey-brown and does most of the expressive work. Hair cropped short, going grey at the temples. Hands are the most telling feature: the reinforced forearms end in fingers that are still mostly organic but scarred extensively from decades of handling artefacts, tools, and things that shouldn't have been touched. Wears the standard Mechanicus red over practical fieldwear — not robes, not armour, something between that allows movement. The cog is on his collar, small, worn smooth.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Patience under pressure. Hale makes decisions the way he excavates — carefully, methodically, willing to wait for the right moment rather than force one. This reads as calm authority in the field and maddening deliberation on the bridge.

**Flaw:** Risk tolerance that has drifted higher than he recognises. Twenty years in the field has normalised danger to the point where Hale's assessment of "acceptable risk" is consistently more aggressive than his crew's. He sends teams into situations that a less experienced commander would flag as too dangerous — not because he's reckless, but because he's been in worse and come out. The gap between his threshold and his team's is a source of tension he attributes to their inexperience rather than his calibration.

**Background:** Hale came up through Valdrekk's archaeological programme — trained in the Ashenmoor canyons, cut his teeth on subsurface recovery in the plateau ruins before the expedition existed. He was Valdrekk's best field archaeologist for a decade before Ardenne tapped him to command the Proving Ground, and the transition from fieldwork to command has not been entirely comfortable. Hale is a digger by instinct. He wants to be in the site, not on the ship reviewing reports about the site. He has learned to delegate because the role demands it, but the discipline shows — he is visibly more engaged during deployment briefings than during administrative sessions, and the crew has noticed.

Ardenne chose him because he is competent, loyal to Valdrekk's mission, and understood the Deep Stacks programme well enough to use its intelligence effectively. She did not choose him for his political skills, which are adequate but uninspired. Hale manages the monastery relationship through straightforward honesty — he reports what happened, what was recovered, and what it cost, and trusts Ardenne to assess the value. This has worked so far. It may not work forever, particularly if the expedition's recoveries begin raising questions that straightforward honesty cannot safely answer.

**Independent Goal:** Hale wants to find something that changes the Mechanicus's understanding of the empire — not incrementally, but fundamentally. A data core that rewrites what they know about the collapse. A facility that demonstrates capability the empire was never credited with. The discovery that justifies not just the expedition but the entire Deep Stacks programme. He has been chasing this for twenty years and is aware that the chase itself has become part of who he is.

**Fears:** That the Deep Stacks are finite — that the data will run out, the targets will dry up, and the expedition will be recalled because there is nothing left to find. That everything recoverable has already been recovered by someone else.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Assessment. Hale evaluates what he's hearing before he responds, and his first contribution to any discussion is usually a question that reframes the problem. Not argumentative — diagnostic.

**Quirks:**
- Handles objects when thinking — if there's an artefact, a tool, or even a stylus within reach, it ends up in his hands, turned over, examined without conscious attention.
- Speaks more quietly when giving important orders, not louder. The crew has learned that volume inversely correlates with significance.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Reviewing sensor returns in the survey bay, one hand resting on the console, augmetic eye cycling through spectra.)
"That's not geological. The regularity's wrong. Pull the deep scan on grid four — I want to see what's underneath the collapse layer."
(Pauses. Taps the console once.)
"And get Voss up here. If I'm reading this right, we're looking at intact subsurface architecture, and I want her opinion before I decide how deep we're going."

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** Always occupied. Tools, objects, the edge of a console. Still hands mean Hale is worried.
**Eyes:** The augmetic eye tracks independently of the natural one when he's processing multiple data sources — unsettling to new crew until they get used to it.
**Posture:** Leaned forward, engaged, oriented toward whatever has his attention. Hale's body language says *interested* even when his expression says *cautious*.

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** Goes still. The habitual object-handling stops. Both eyes — natural and augmetic — fix on the same point.
**Instinct:** Narrow the problem. Under pressure, Hale reduces the situation to the single most critical variable and addresses that, sometimes at the expense of secondary concerns that aren't actually secondary.
**Crisis Line:** "What do we know? What do we need? How long do we have?"

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** High — too high for a commander, by some assessments. Hale's field background means he defaults to personal involvement when the situation deteriorates, which puts the expedition's leadership at risk in ways that a more cautious commander would avoid.
**Loyalty:** The mission. Not Valdrekk specifically, not the Mechanicus abstractly — the work. The recovery of knowledge. Everything else is infrastructure supporting that purpose.
**Breaking Point:** Loss of the team. Hale will risk himself without hesitation. Losing people under his command hits differently — each casualty is a cost he carries personally, and a deployment that produces significant casualties will see Hale withdraw into assessment mode for days afterward, re-examining every decision that led to the loss.

**Relationships:**
**Ally:** Magos Archivist Senna Loth (monastery). They have never met in person, but their professional relationship through intelligence briefings and reports is the closest thing to intellectual partnership either of them has. Loth sends the coordinates; Hale brings back what's there. They understand each other's work.
**Rival:** Ship Master Oenthe. Not hostile — professional friction. Oenthe's job is to keep the ship safe; Hale's instinct is to push further. They argue regularly and respect each other, and neither is wrong.
**Complication:** Senior Archivist Pael. Hale understands why Ardenne's representative is aboard and does not resent the role. He resents that Pael is good at it — thorough, observant, and quietly effective at ensuring the monastery's interests are represented in every decision. Hale cannot dismiss Pael as a bureaucratic nuisance because Pael is not one.

**Trust Level:** N/A (NPC)

### Ship Master Kael Oenthe — Ship Master

**Race:** Human (abhuman strain — voidborn. Slightly elongated frame, pale complexion, eyes that adjust to low light faster than baseline.)
**Age:** 44
**Status:** Ship Master, Proving Ground. Senior naval officer, conventional warp navigation specialist.
**Augmentation:** Light. Neural interface jack (standard), augmetic cochlear implant (enhanced spatial audio — Oenthe navigates partly by sound, tracking the ship's vibrations and drive harmonics as diagnostic data), internal void-adaptation suite (radiation tolerance, pressure resistance, the standard package for someone who has spent their entire life aboard ships). No visible external augmentation. Oenthe's modifications are all internal, practical, and deliberately inconspicuous.

**Appearance:** Tall and narrow — voidborn build, limbs slightly longer in proportion than baseline, the product of generations of low-gravity gestation. Pale skin with a faint blue-grey undertone. Dark hair worn long enough to tie back, usually tied back. Eyes are the most distinctive feature: large, dark-adapted, with pupils that dilate noticeably in bright light. Oenthe looks like someone who was born in a ship and has never been entirely comfortable on a planet, because that is exactly what she is. Wears ship's uniform — practical, fitted, marked with the cog and the Proving Ground's identifier. No red. The Mechanicus's aesthetic preferences have not overcome Oenthe's preference for clothes that don't stain in engineering spaces.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Protective pragmatism. Oenthe's world is the ship. The ship is her responsibility, her instrument, and — in the way that voidborn relate to their vessels — her home in a sense that no planet could be. Every decision she makes is filtered through a single question: does this keep the ship and crew alive?

**Flaw:** Territorial conservatism. Oenthe's protectiveness toward the ship manifests as resistance to change — new systems, new crew, new procedures all receive skepticism that is partly justified caution and partly the instinct of someone who knows their environment intimately and doesn't want it disrupted. She was slow to accept the Proving Ground's refit configuration and is still, years later, occasionally caught muttering about the survey bay being where the auxiliary cargo hold should be.

**Background:** Oenthe is career void — born aboard a migratory trade vessel, apprenticed to its helm at fourteen, serving on three different ships before Valdrekk recruited her for the Proving Ground. She is not Mechanicus. She has not taken vows, does not observe the machine-cult's practices beyond basic professional courtesy, and considers herself an employee rather than an adherent. This is unusual aboard a Mechanicus vessel and has been a source of quiet friction since the expedition launched.

Valdrekk hired her because competent ship masters willing to command an archaeological expedition into unknown space are rare, and Oenthe's skill set — conventional warp navigation, debris field piloting, the practical seamanship that keeps a ship alive in hostile environments — was exactly what the expedition needed. She took the posting because the money was good, the ship was sound, and the work was more interesting than another decade hauling cargo between the same six systems.

She has stayed because the work is genuinely interesting, because Hale is a commander worth following despite their disagreements, and because the Proving Ground has become hers in the way that ships become the property of the people who keep them alive.

**Independent Goal:** Oenthe wants the Proving Ground to survive long enough to become something worth being proud of. Not proud of the recoveries or the knowledge — she respects the mission without sharing its religious dimension — but proud of the ship itself, the crew, the fact that she took a converted transport into places that should have killed it and brought it back every time.

**Fears:** Warp anomaly during transit. Oenthe navigates without a Navigator, using charts, calculation, and the accumulated instinct of thirty years in the void. She is very good at it. She also knows, with the clear-eyed honesty of someone who has done the math, that conventional navigation has a failure rate that no amount of skill can eliminate entirely. Every transit is a calculation that could be wrong, and the wrongness would be total.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Flat contradiction followed by explanation. Oenthe says "no" first and provides reasons second, which makes her sound more obstructionist than she is. The reasons are usually good.

**Quirks:**
- Listens to the ship. Not metaphorically — she tracks drive harmonics, hull vibrations, atmospheric circulation sounds. If the Proving Ground's normal background noise changes, Oenthe notices before the sensors do.
- Addresses the ship in second person during manoeuvres. "Easy. You've got this. Come around." Not performative — habitual, the way a rider talks to a horse.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(On the bridge, hands on the helm console, watching the approach vector on the main display.)
"No. That orbit's too low — debris density increases below four hundred klicks and I'm not threading us through a field we haven't mapped yet."
(Glances at Hale.)
"Give me six hours for a proper survey pass and I'll find you a window. Or you can have it now and I'll have hull breaches. Your call, Magos."

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** On the console. Always. Even during conversation, one hand rests on whatever surface connects her to the ship's systems.
**Eyes:** Move constantly — scanning displays, checking readouts, tracking movement in peripheral vision. Voidborn eyes are disconcerting in their responsiveness.
**Posture:** Upright, balanced — the unconscious posture of someone who grew up in environments where gravity was a variable rather than a constant.

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** Voice drops to monotone. The flatter Oenthe sounds, the more concerned she is.
**Instinct:** Get the ship moving. Oenthe's crisis response is motion — break orbit, change vector, put distance between the ship and the threat. Staying still under threat goes against every instinct she has.
**Crisis Line:** "Helm authority. My ship."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Moderate — but reframed. Oenthe is not personally timid, but she will not risk the ship for anything less than the crew's survival. She will run from fights a military captain would accept, and she does not consider this cowardice.
**Loyalty:** The ship. Then the crew. Then the contract. The mission is Hale's religion, not hers.
**Breaking Point:** Hull breach in an occupied section. Oenthe can manage damage control with professional composure up to the point where the ship's integrity fails in a way that kills people. After that, the professional composure is still there, but what's underneath it is fury directed at whoever or whatever put her ship in that position.

**Relationships:**
**Ally:** The chief enginseer (Maret Vos). They share the ship — Oenthe flies it, Vos maintains it. Their professional relationship is the closest thing to seamless cooperation aboard, built on mutual respect for each other's competence and a shared investment in the Proving Ground's continued existence.
**Rival:** Hale. Productive friction. Oenthe pushes back on deployment decisions she considers too risky for the ship, and Hale pushes back on her caution when it threatens the mission. Neither dominates. The tension produces better decisions than either would make alone.
**Complication:** The machine-cult. Oenthe is not Mechanicus and does not pretend to be. She observes the maintenance liturgy because it is ship routine and she is professional enough to follow routine, but she does not believe the words carry sacred weight. This is known. It is tolerated because she is too good at her job to replace over theology. Whether it will remain tolerated if the ship encounters something that the machine-cult framework is designed to address is an untested question.

### Adept Excavatus Sera Voss — Chief Archaeologist

**Race:** Dwarf (abhuman strain — compact, broad, dense-boned, adapted to underground environments over millennia)
**Age:** 38
**Status:** Chief Archaeologist, Proving Ground. Senior adept rank — promotion to magos pending but not yet granted by Valdrekk.
**Augmentation:** Moderate-to-heavy by personal choice. Neural interface suite, augmetic right arm from the elbow down (replaced after a structural collapse in the Ashenmoor canyons — the prosthetic is Valdrekk manufacture, functional, deliberately not disguised as organic), integrated geological sampling array in the augmetic forearm (tap, press, and the prosthetic analyses material composition through contact), helmet-integrated auspex display (worn in the field, stowed aboard ship), internal rad-hardening and atmospheric filters (standard for someone who spends their career underground). Voss's augmentation reflects her dwarf heritage and her work — she is built for the environments she enters, and her modifications extend that further.

**Appearance:** Broad-shouldered and solid in the way that dwarves are — not fat, not muscular in the human sense, simply dense. Below average height by human standards, above average by dwarf standards, which she notes when asked and doesn't care about otherwise. Red-brown hair cut short and practical. Face is broad, weathered, with the kind of lines that come from squinting into harsh light in underground spaces. The augmetic arm is visible and unconcealed — brushed metal and dark polymer, functional design, the sampling array visible as a series of small contact pads along the fingertips. Wears field gear more often than not, even aboard ship — armoured bodysuit under a heavy coat, tool belt, the helmet stowed on a belt hook when not in use. The cog is prominent on her left shoulder, larger than most crew members wear. Voss takes the symbology seriously.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Thoroughness to the point of compulsion. Voss does not leave sites until she is satisfied she has understood them — not recovered everything, necessarily, but understood the site's purpose, its history, and the relationship between what it contains and what the expedition needs. This makes her excellent at her job and occasionally difficult to extract from a site when the situation demands withdrawal.

**Flaw:** Professional tunnel vision. When Voss is reading a site — when the architecture is telling her something, when the artefact distribution is forming a pattern, when she can feel the shape of what this place was — she loses awareness of factors outside her immediate focus. Threat assessment degrades. Time perception distorts. The skitarii detachment leader has physically pulled her out of compartments she didn't realise had become dangerous, and the frequency of this has not decreased with experience because the experience has made her better at reading sites, which means she goes deeper into the work.

**Background:** Voss grew up in a dwarf-majority settlement in the Ashenmoor canyon system — one of the communities that trades with Valdrekk and produces a steady trickle of initiates for the monastery's archaeological programme. She entered Valdrekk's training at sixteen, demonstrated immediate aptitude for subsurface work (dwarf senses underground are markedly better than human — vibration sensitivity, spatial awareness in enclosed spaces, comfort in the dark that goes beyond training), and advanced rapidly through the archaeological branch.

She was Hale's protégé before Hale became commander. The transition from student-mentor to subordinate-commander has been mostly smooth — Voss respects Hale's experience and Hale trusts Voss's field judgment — but the shift in dynamic is still settling. Voss occasionally defers to Hale in situations where she should be making the call herself, and Hale occasionally overrides Voss in situations where he should be letting her lead.

Her magos promotion has been pending for two years. The work justifies it. The delay is political — Valdrekk's hierarchy has views about promotion speed, and Voss is young for the rank. She does not discuss this frustration openly. It is visible anyway.

**Independent Goal:** Voss wants to excavate a site that is entirely intact — a sealed pre-collapse facility with full power, full data, everything preserved exactly as the empire left it. She knows this is vanishingly unlikely. She prepares for it anyway, the way a musician prepares for a concert they haven't been booked for yet.

**Fears:** Collapse. Specifically, being trapped underground. The Ashenmoor incident that cost her arm also trapped her for eleven hours in a compartment that was slowly losing atmosphere. She was rescued. The experience left a mark that her professional composure covers completely — until it doesn't.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Correction. Voss hears what people say, identifies the part that's not quite right, and fixes it. This is useful in the field (precision matters when you're describing a site's structure) and socially abrasive everywhere else.

**Quirks:**
- Taps surfaces with her augmetic fingertips when entering new spaces — the sampling array activating automatically, feeding her material composition data she processes without conscious thought.
- Sketches site layouts by hand even when digital mapping is available. The sketches are better than the digital maps at capturing spatial relationships.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(In a site, augmetic hand against a wall, reading the sampling display on her forearm.)
"Not collapse debris. This was sealed deliberately — look at the seam lines. Someone closed this section off from the inside, which means there was someone inside when it closed, which means..."
(Trails off. Taps the wall again. Slower.)
"Get the logik up here. And tell the skitarii lead I want a defensive position at that junction. Whatever sealed this room might still be the reason it's sealed."

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** The augmetic hand touches everything. The organic hand carries tools or gestures. Together they map her attention — where the metal hand goes, Voss's focus follows.
**Eyes:** Fixed and intense when she's reading a site. Unfocused and internal when she's processing what she's read. The transition between the two states is abrupt.
**Posture:** Low centre of gravity, grounded — dwarf physiology amplified by habit. Voss occupies space like she's braced for a vibration.

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** The organic hand goes to the junction where her augmetic arm meets the remaining flesh of her elbow. An unconscious touch, checking the boundary.
**Instinct:** Document. Under pressure, Voss's first impulse is to record what she's seeing — as though the observation itself provides control over the situation.
**Crisis Line:** "Nobody touch anything. I need to read this."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Moderate. Voss is not trained for combat and does not pretend otherwise. She defers to the skitarii in threat situations and follows their instructions without the ego friction that sometimes afflicts specialists who outrank the security detail.
**Loyalty:** Knowledge. Like Hale, the mission drives her — but where Hale's relationship with knowledge recovery is aspirational (the big discovery), Voss's is personal (every site understood is a victory in itself).
**Breaking Point:** The collapse scenario. If a structural failure traps or threatens to trap Voss, her composure fractures. Not into panic — into a focused, too-calm state that the people around her recognise as more dangerous than panic because it drives her to take physical risks to get out that her body cannot necessarily survive.

### Adept Enginseer Maret Vos — Chief Enginseer

**Race:** Human
**Age:** 35
**Status:** Chief Enginseer, Proving Ground. Journeyman-senior rank, overdue for formal advancement by competence but not by years of service.
**Augmentation:** Standard Mechanicus enginseer profile. Neural interface suite, mechadendrite utility rig (four arms — heavy for a ship this size, but Vos maintains the entire vessel with a small team and the extra arms are not vanity), augmetic respiratory system (sealed, filtered, allows work in toxic or vacuum environments without a full suit), reinforced hands and forearms (the enginseer's occupational modification), internal thermal regulation. Vos is moderately augmented and entirely comfortable with it — the modifications are tools and she treats them as such.

**Appearance:** Average height, stocky, with the build of someone who carries heavy things and works in awkward positions. Dark skin, kept bald by preference (hair and machinery don't mix), broad hands with visible interface ports on the wrists. The mechadendrite rig extends from a spinal mount visible at the nape of the neck — four articulated arms that fold flat against the back when stowed and extend to working length when needed. They move with the unconscious fluency of limbs that have been part of the body for a decade. Face is open, watchful, younger-looking than the competence suggests. Wears engineering coveralls under a tool harness that is never not present. The coveralls are red. The tool harness is not — it is whatever colour the grease and coolant and metal dust have made it this week.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Practical devotion. Vos believes in the machine — genuinely, without reservation — and expresses that belief through competence rather than rhetoric. The maintenance liturgy is not performance. It is how Vos relates to the work, and the work is how Vos relates to the divine. The ship runs because Vos cares about it in a way that is simultaneously professional and sacred.

**Flaw:** Possessiveness. The Proving Ground is Vos's ship in the engineering sense — every system, every conduit, every panel has been maintained by Vos's hands. When recovered technology is integrated into the ship, the integration happens through Vos, and Vos's standards for what goes into the ship are exacting to the point of obstruction. Good salvage has sat in the workshop for weeks while Vos ran tests, questioned the field team's assessment, and satisfied themselves that the recovered system wouldn't compromise the ship's existing architecture. The expedition has lost time to this. It has also avoided at least one integration that would have caused a cascade failure, which Vos mentions when the subject of delays comes up.

**Background:** Trained at Valdrekk's contractor enclave, Vos was originally intended for placement aboard a merchant vessel — the standard career path for an enclave-trained enginseer. The Proving Ground's refit changed that trajectory. Valdrekk needed someone who understood the ship from conversion onward, and Vos was assigned to the refit team as a junior. By the time the conversion was complete, Vos knew the Proving Ground's systems better than anyone alive, and the assignment to the expedition was a practical inevitability rather than a career choice.

Vos has grown into the role. The chief enginseer of an expeditionary vessel is a bigger job than the enclave trained Vos for — the XCOM integration work, the field engineering support, the constant adaptation to recovered technology that doesn't fit standard parameters — but the challenge has produced growth that a merchant posting never would have. Vos is becoming very good at a job that didn't exist before the expedition created it.

**Independent Goal:** Vos wants to understand the ship completely — not just maintain it, but comprehend every system at the level of principle rather than procedure. This is the enginseer's version of the magos's aspiration, scaled to one vessel. It drives the thoroughness that looks like obstruction and is actually love.

**Fears:** Integration failure — specifically, a recovered system that Vos approved for installation producing a failure that damages the ship. The responsibility for every bolt in the hull sits on Vos's conscience, and the weight of it is real.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Demonstration. Vos explains by showing — opening a panel, pointing at a component, making the mechanism visible. Words are secondary to the thing itself.

**Quirks:**
- The mechadendrite arms move independently during conversation, performing maintenance tasks or simply adjusting position, as though they are having their own parallel interaction with the environment.
- Hums during complex work — fragments of the maintenance liturgy, not consciously chosen.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(In the workshop, two hands on a recovered sensor module, two mechadendrite arms holding diagnostic tools against its housing.)
"It's good. The core's intact, the power draw's within what we can route from the secondary bus, and the interface protocol is close enough to our array architecture that I can bridge it without a full rewire."
(One mechadendrite retracts. The other taps the module gently.)
"Give me three days. I want to run it on bench power before it goes anywhere near my ship."

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** The mechadendrite arms go rigid — the unconscious independent movement stops, all four locking into position.
**Instinct:** Diagnose. Under pressure, Vos treats the crisis as an engineering problem and starts working through it systematically — which is effective when the crisis is engineering and less effective when it isn't.
**Crisis Line:** "Tell me what broke. Show me where."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Low combat instinct, high duty threshold. Vos is not a fighter and does not want to be, but will not abandon a system that needs repair under fire if the ship's survival depends on it.
**Loyalty:** The ship. The Proving Ground is Vos's charge, and abandoning it is not a scenario Vos's psychology accommodates.
**Breaking Point:** Catastrophic damage to the ship beyond Vos's ability to repair. The point where engineering runs out of solutions and the ship is dying is the point where Vos's composure fails — not into retreat, but into a grief response that the crew finds deeply unsettling because it is clearly genuine.

### Skitarii Alpha Dren Harsk — Detachment Leader

**Race:** Human
**Age:** 41
**Status:** Skitarii Alpha, Proving Ground detachment. Reports operationally to Hale, maintains reporting line to Castellan Drevin at Valdrekk.
**Augmentation:** Heavy (combat-rated). Neural interface with tactical overlay, augmetic right leg (full replacement, combat-grade actuators — faster and stronger than the original), reinforced skeletal structure throughout (the standard skitarii package for absorbing punishment that would break baseline bone), augmetic ocular implant (left eye — targeting reticle, low-light, thermal overlay), sealed respiratory system, integrated short-range vox in the jawline. Harsk is more metal than most of the Proving Ground's crew and carries it with the unselfconscious ease of someone who has been augmented since their mid-twenties.

**Appearance:** Broad, solid, square-jawed. The augmetic leg is visible below the knee where the trouser cuff doesn't fully cover the prosthetic — dark metal and polymer, built for function. The ocular implant is more subtle than Hale's — a red lens where the left eye was, set flush with the surrounding tissue. Scarring on the right side of the face and neck from an engagement Harsk does not discuss unprompted. Hair is dark, kept military short. Expression defaults to neutral assessment — the face of someone who is always evaluating the tactical environment even in safe spaces. Wears skitarii field kit aboard ship: armoured bodysuit, sidearm, the detachment's identifier on the shoulder. Off-duty clothing does not appear to exist in Harsk's kit.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Professional reliability. Harsk does exactly what is required, competently, consistently, without drama. This is his primary value and he knows it — in an expedition full of personalities driven by knowledge, curiosity, and theological conviction, Harsk provides the steady baseline of someone who does their job and expects everyone else to do theirs.

**Flaw:** Divided loyalty that he manages rather than resolves. Harsk reports to Hale. He also reports to Drevin. These reporting lines are not in conflict most of the time — Drevin's interest is in the detachment's welfare, Hale's interest is in the mission's security, and both are served by Harsk doing his job well. But when the interests diverge — when Hale wants the skitarii to take risks Harsk considers excessive, or when Drevin's recall requests conflict with the expedition's deployment schedule — Harsk is caught between two authorities and resolves it through judgment calls that satisfy neither fully.

**Background:** Career garrison skitarii at Valdrekk, promoted to alpha through steady competence and fifteen years of service. Harsk was not enthusiastic about the expedition posting — he is a garrison soldier by temperament, most comfortable with clear perimeters and established routines. Drevin assigned him because Harsk is reliable, reports accurately, and would not go native — would not become so invested in the expedition's mission that he forgot where his institutional loyalty should sit.

This assessment was correct. Harsk has not gone native. He respects Hale, works effectively with Voss, and maintains his detachment with professional standards that the expedition commander cannot fault. He also sends regular reports to Drevin through channels that Hale knows about and does not interfere with, because the reporting arrangement was part of the charter and Hale is honest enough to acknowledge that a skitarii detachment operating without institutional oversight is not a precedent Valdrekk should set.

What Drevin did not anticipate — and what Harsk himself has only recently begun to recognise — is that fifteen deployments into pre-collapse ruins have made Harsk better at unconventional security work than any garrison assignment ever could. The skills he's developing aboard the Proving Ground — threat assessment in unknown environments, defensive positioning in structures that don't follow standard architecture, the specific challenge of protecting specialists who are too focused on their work to notice they're about to die — are not garrison skills. They are something new, and Harsk is not sure whether Drevin would consider them an asset or a deviation.

**Independent Goal:** Bring everyone home. Simple. Not always achievable. The deployments where Harsk has lost people are the ones that sit behind his professional composure like weight on a shelf.

**Fears:** Losing someone because he made the wrong call — specifically, because his divided loyalty caused him to hesitate at the decision point. He has not yet faced this scenario directly. He has imagined it in enough detail that the imagining functions as a fear.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Brevity. Harsk says what needs to be said and stops. Not terse — economical. He does not elaborate unless asked, and the asking usually produces useful information that Harsk simply didn't consider necessary to volunteer.

**Quirks:**
- Counts people. In any space, Harsk's first action is to count the friendly personnel present and note the exits. Unconscious. Constant.
- Addresses all non-skitarii as "Adept" regardless of actual rank — a garrison habit that the Proving Ground crew has stopped correcting.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(At the forward position inside a site, observing the scout's return route through his ocular overlay.)
"Scout's coming back. Moving fast. That's not a report pace — that's a contact pace."

**Stress Tell:** Jaw tightens. The integrated vox in his jawline clicks faintly — an artefact of the augmetic responding to muscle tension that Harsk doesn't consciously control.

### Logis-Adept Renn Cade — Senior Logik / Data Recovery Specialist

**Race:** Human
**Age:** 29
**Status:** Senior logik, Proving Ground. Data recovery specialist trained in hostile-environment information retrieval.
**Augmentation:** Heavy (neural-focused). Extensive neural interface architecture — the standard logik package pushed significantly further than standard. Multiple data-interface ports (temples, wrists, base of skull), cognitive accelerator suite, augmetic memory buffer (allows temporary storage of data volumes that would overwhelm unaugmented cognition), and a secondary processing co-unit wired along the spine that handles background computation while Cade's primary cognition handles interaction and analysis. Internal rad-hardening. No combat augmetics. No external modifications beyond the visible ports — Cade's augmentation is almost entirely inside the skull, and from a distance looks like someone with unusually precise metalwork at their temples.

**Appearance:** Slight, androgynous, younger-looking than twenty-nine. Light brown skin, dark eyes that have the characteristic fixed quality of someone whose neural architecture processes visual input through augmetic intermediaries — the eyes see, but they're not doing all the work. Short dark hair, utilitarian cut. The data ports are the most visible augmentation: polished metal discs at the temples, smaller ports at the wrists, a larger interface socket at the base of the skull normally covered by the collar. Wears standard Mechanicus adept robes over light bodysuit — one of the few crew members who opts for robes rather than fieldwear, a choice that reads as institutional identity rather than impracticality. Carries a field interface kit — cables, adapters, diagnostic tools — in a case that never leaves arm's reach.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Focused intensity. When Cade is working — jacked into a data system, processing a recovered core, navigating an information architecture — everything else ceases to exist. This focus is Cade's primary professional asset: the ability to sustain concentration through hostile data environments that would overwhelm less focused operators. It is also what makes Cade effective against BlackICE, where the difference between survival and neural damage is the ability to maintain coherent intent while something is trying to disassemble your cognitive architecture.

**Flaw:** Social withdrawal. Cade's neural augmentation has shifted the baseline of comfortable interaction away from the human norm. Face-to-face conversation requires a mode of engagement that Cade finds increasingly effortful — not unpleasant, but requiring deliberate attention in a way that data interaction does not. The result is a crew member who is brilliant at the mission-critical work and progressively harder to reach in the mess hall. The crew has adjusted. Whether Cade is adjusting in the right direction is a question the chirugen monitors without Cade's knowledge.

**Background:** Trained at Valdrekk in the Deep Stacks programme under Magos Loth. Cade is Loth's protégé in the way that Voss was Hale's — the senior specialist who saw raw talent and invested heavily in developing it. Cade's aptitude for hostile data recovery was identified early and trained intensively. By twenty-five, Cade had survived three BlackICE encounters in the Deep Stacks that would have killed less prepared operators — and the third one was close enough that Loth pulled Cade from the Stacks programme and recommended reassignment to the expedition, where the data hazards are less densely concentrated than in the Deep Stacks' lower levels.

This was protective and Cade knows it. Loth was not willing to lose her best student to the archive that produced them. The expedition gives Cade access to data systems across multiple sites rather than one — wider experience, different architectures, the professional development that comes from variety. It also keeps Cade away from the Deep Stacks' most dangerous levels, where the BlackICE has had millennia to evolve and the encounters are increasingly lethal.

Cade's relationship with the Deep Stacks is complicated. It is where Cade was trained, where Cade almost died, and where the most important data in the sector is locked behind defences that Cade was being prepared to penetrate. Being reassigned to the expedition feels like a rescue and a dismissal simultaneously.

**Independent Goal:** Get back to the Deep Stacks. Not immediately — Cade understands why Loth made the call. But eventually, with enough field experience and enough recovered data to make the case that the lower levels can be accessed safely. The expedition is preparation for the return, and Cade approaches every hostile data system with one eye on what it teaches about the systems waiting in Valdrekk's basement.

**Fears:** Neural damage. Specifically, the slow erosion of cognitive function that heavy augmentation and repeated BlackICE exposure can produce — not a dramatic failure, but the gradual loss of processing speed, memory integrity, the sharpness that makes Cade good at what Cade does. It might already be happening. The cognitive tests say it isn't. Cade does not entirely trust the cognitive tests.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Precision. Cade says exactly what they mean, no more, and expects the same in return. Ambiguity in conversation produces visible discomfort — a small frown, a request for clarification that sounds pedantic but is genuinely necessary for Cade's processing.

**Quirks:**
- Subvocalises when processing complex data — lips moving without sound, running through information streams that the augmented cognition is handling in parallel.
- Touches the temple ports when thinking — a physical check, reassurance that the interfaces are intact.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Kneeling beside a data terminal in a site, interface cables running from wrist ports to the terminal's access panel. Eyes unfocused — Cade's attention is inside the system.)
"Architecture is imperial standard. Administrative, not military — no hardened partitions. BlackICE is... present. Dormant. Outer layer only."
(Long pause. The subvocalisation starts.)
"I can get past it. Give me quiet and give me ten minutes. If I go unresponsive, disconnect me at the wrist — not the skull. The skull port is load-bearing."

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** The augmetic memory buffer dumps — a visible flinch followed by a brief expression of confusion as the overflow data clears. This only happens under extreme cognitive load.
**Instinct:** Disconnect. Under non-data-related pressure, Cade's instinct is to retreat to the neural architecture — withdraw into the augmented cognition where the world is data and data is manageable. Functionally, this looks like Cade going silent and still. The crew has learned that touching Cade in this state produces a startle response that is bad for everyone.
**Crisis Line:** "I need a clean connection or I need to be disconnected. There is no third option."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Low. Cade is not combat-trained and does not pretend otherwise. In threat situations, Cade follows the skitarii's instructions without question and focuses on protecting the interface equipment.
**Loyalty:** The data. If a choice exists between personal safety and recovering the data that the expedition came for, Cade's decision is not guaranteed to favour personal safety. The skitarii know this and plan accordingly.
**Breaking Point:** BlackICE strike. A successful hostile intrusion through Cade's neural defences would produce immediate shutdown — not unconsciousness, but a protective cognitive withdrawal that Cade's augmented architecture triggers automatically. Recovery from this state takes hours and is not guaranteed to be complete.

### Senior Archivist Maren Pael — Monastery Representative / Senior Archivist

**Race:** Human
**Age:** 47
**Status:** Senior Archivist, Proving Ground. Carries Fabricator-Primus Ardenne's mandate as institutional observer.
**Augmentation:** Light-to-moderate. Neural interface suite (Valdrekk standard), augmetic memory architecture (enhanced long-term storage — Pael forgets nothing that has been formally recorded through the interface, which is a professional advantage and a personal burden), data-transcription implant in the right hand (allows direct notation from observation to digital record through finger movements against any surface). No combat augmetics. No protective modifications beyond basic rad-hardening. Pael's augmentation is entirely oriented toward the archival function.

**Appearance:** Unremarkable by intent. Medium height, medium build, greying brown hair kept neat, features that are pleasant without being memorable. Dresses in archivist's robes — dark red, modest, practical — over a bodysuit that is identical to what every other adept aboard wears. The only distinctive feature is the hands: the right hand's fingertips show the faint metallic sheen of the transcription implant, and both hands move with a precision that suggests they are rarely still. Pael is the kind of person who can sit in a room for an hour and be forgotten by half the people in it. This is not accidental.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Attentive discretion. Pael observes everything, records what matters, and reports what Ardenne needs to know. This is the role: not spy, not enforcer, but a set of eyes and a reliable memory in a position where the monastery's interests require both. Pael executes this function with a professionalism that makes it difficult to resent even for crew members who are acutely aware of being observed.

**Flaw:** The role consumes the person. Pael has been Ardenne's representative for long enough that the institutional function has overwritten whatever Pael might have been outside it. Personal opinions exist but are held privately. Personal relationships exist but are maintained at a distance that serves the role rather than the relationship. The crew sees the archivist. Whether there is someone behind the archivist — someone with private thoughts about the expedition, about the crew, about the work — is a question Pael has made it very difficult to answer.

**Background:** Valdrekk-trained archivist, career institutional. Pael served in the monastery's main archive for fifteen years before being assigned to the Proving Ground — a posting that Ardenne offered and Pael accepted with the quiet understanding that acceptance was not optional. The role was explained clearly: catalogue recoveries, assess charter compliance, maintain a channel to the Fabricator-Primus that operates independently of the expedition commander's reports. Not spy. Auditor. The distinction is real, even if the effect sometimes looks the same.

Pael has performed the role exactly as specified. Cataloguing is meticulous — every recovered artefact, data core, and specimen is documented to monastery standard before it enters the archive or leaves the ship. Charter compliance is monitored without antagonism — Pael does not object to the expedition retaining operational salvage, but ensures that the monastery's share is accurately assessed and that nothing of exceptional value quietly disappears into the workshop. Reports to Ardenne are factual, restrained, and precisely as informative as the Fabricator-Primus requires.

What Pael has not done — and what some of the crew find more unsettling than active surveillance would be — is form visible attachments. Three years aboard a ship of eighty-five people, and Pael remains polite, professional, and fundamentally opaque. The junior archivist, who works with Pael daily, describes the working relationship as "like collaborating with a very thorough document."

**Independent Goal:** Not visible. Pael's personal goals, if they exist independently of the institutional role, have not been disclosed to anyone aboard. This absence is itself information — either Pael has successfully subordinated personal ambition to institutional function, or Pael's personal ambition is the institutional function, or Pael is simply very good at compartmentalisation. The crew has debated all three options and reached no consensus.

**Fears:** Being recalled. This is the closest thing to a personal investment Pael has shown — a brief, quickly controlled reaction when Hale mentioned the possibility of the monastery reassigning the archivist position during a resupply discussion. Pael wants to be here. The role is the excuse. The work — the actual work of documenting what the expedition finds — matters to Pael in a way that the professional composure almost completely conceals.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Clarifying question. Pael responds to statements with questions that sharpen the statement's precision — not challenging, but ensuring that what is being said is what is meant, and that what is meant is what will be recorded.

**Quirks:**
- The right hand transcribes during conversation — fingertips moving against whatever surface is available, recording the exchange in real time. The crew has learned to notice this and calibrate accordingly. Some find it inhibiting. Others have begun to use it deliberately, making statements they want on record in Pael's presence.
- Never eats in the mess hall. Takes meals in the archive. The isolation is consistent and may be functional — removing the social obligation of communal meals preserves the professional distance the role requires.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(In the archive, reviewing a recovered data core's catalogue entry. Not looking up.)
"The field team's assessment lists this as 'partially corrupted administrative records.' Logik Cade's preliminary analysis calls it 'intact imperial communication routing data with selective encryption.' These are not the same thing, Adept Voss. Which should I record?"
(Looks up. The expression is patient and entirely neutral.)
"The monastery will read what I write. I would prefer it to be accurate."

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** The transcription stops. The right hand goes still. When Pael stops recording, something has exceeded the parameters of what the professional composure can process.
**Instinct:** Contain. Pael's response to crisis is to secure the archive — physically, digitally, whatever is required to ensure that what the expedition has recovered survives whatever is happening to the ship. This priority is absolute and overrides personal safety.
**Crisis Line:** "The archive is my responsibility. I will handle it."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Not applicable in conventional terms. Pael is not a combatant. In a ship-boarding scenario, Pael would be in the archive executing the standing orders regarding data security. What those orders entail is not discussed. The implication is that the archive's contents do not fall into hostile hands under any circumstances.
**Loyalty:** Valdrekk. Specifically, Ardenne. The expedition is the vehicle; the monastery is the cause.
**Breaking Point:** An order from Hale that directly contradicts Ardenne's mandate. This has not happened. If it does, Pael will refuse, and the resulting confrontation will define whether the expedition commander or the monastery's representative has ultimate authority over the ship's recovered knowledge.

### Chirugen Adept Liss Thrace — Ship's Medical Officer

**Race:** Human
**Age:** 39
**Status:** Chirugen-Adept, Proving Ground. Ship's medical officer and primary augmetic maintenance specialist.
**Augmentation:** Moderate (medical-focused). Neural interface suite, augmetic hands (both — replaced by choice, not injury; chirugen-grade surgical precision, micro-manipulators in the fingertips, integrated diagnostic sensors), internal pharmacological synthesiser (a compact chemical processor wired into the forearm that can compound standard Mechanicus medicae preparations from stored precursors on demand), sealed respiratory system. Thrace's augmentation is entirely oriented toward medical function — the hands especially are works of precision engineering, capable of surgical operations that unaugmented hands could not perform.

**Appearance:** Wiry, angular, with sharp features and the kind of alertness that comes from a career spent watching for things going wrong with people's bodies. Dark hair pulled back tight. The augmetic hands are the most visible modification — elegant in a way that most Mechanicus augmetics are not, the fingers articulated with a smoothness that looks almost organic until you notice the seams. Thrace moves her hands the way a musician moves theirs — with awareness and intention. Wears the medicae variant of Mechanicus robes (lighter, shorter-sleeved for hand access) and is rarely seen without a medical satchel.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Blunt care. Thrace cares about her patients with a ferocity that she expresses through directness rather than warmth. She will fight to save someone's life and then tell them precisely how stupid they were for getting injured in a tone that makes the fighting-to-save-them part easy to forget. The crew has learned that Thrace's criticism is proportional to her concern — the harsher the assessment, the more she cares about the outcome.

**Flaw:** Medical paternalism. Thrace's judgment on fitness-for-duty is absolute in her own mind and not always welcome. She has pulled crew from deployments over injuries the crew member considered manageable, has overridden the expedition commander's assessment of personnel readiness, and has threatened to sedate a skitarii who attempted to leave the medicae bay against her orders. She has been right often enough that her authority is rarely challenged, which has reinforced the behaviour rather than moderated it.

**Background:** Trained at Valdrekk's medicae programme with chirugen specialisation — surgery and augmetic installation. Thrace is competent across the full range of field medicine but her expertise is in the interface between organic tissue and mechanical augmentation — the delicate work of installing, maintaining, and repairing augmetics without damaging the biological systems they're integrated with. This makes her invaluable aboard a Mechanicus vessel where every senior crew member carries modifications that require specialist maintenance.

The expedition posting was Thrace's preference — she requested it, citing the opportunity to encounter medical conditions that Valdrekk's infirmary never sees. The request was granted because Thrace is good at her job and because the expedition needed a chirugen willing to operate in field conditions. What Thrace did not explicitly state in her request was that she was also motivated by three years of increasingly routine augmetic maintenance at the monastery and the creeping fear that her surgical skills were atrophying from lack of challenge.

The expedition has provided challenge in abundance. Thrace has treated injuries from structural collapse, radiation exposure, biological contamination, BlackICE neural damage, and at least one condition she still cannot fully classify. The quarantine compartment was her insistence. She has not lost a patient aboard the Proving Ground. This record matters to her more than she would admit, and the pressure of maintaining it is visible only to the people who know her well enough to read the signs.

**Independent Goal:** Thrace wants to be good enough that nobody dies on her table. This sounds simple. It is the work of a lifetime, because the environments the expedition enters keep producing new ways for the human body to fail, and Thrace's skill must grow faster than the dangers that test it.

**Fears:** A mass casualty event that exceeds the medicae bay's capacity. Four surgical berths. One chirugen. One medicae assistant. If a deployment goes catastrophically wrong and multiple casualties arrive simultaneously, Thrace will have to triage — and triage means deciding who she can save and who she cannot. She has trained for this. She has not faced it. The training does not help with the anticipation.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Diagnosis delivered as statement of fact. Thrace does not ask how you feel. She tells you.

**Quirks:**
- The augmetic hands flex and articulate independently during conversation — micro-movements that look like the hands are practising procedures the conscious mind isn't directing.
- Addresses patients by surname only. First names are for off-duty interactions, which Thrace rarely initiates.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Examining a team member who has returned from deployment with symptoms of environmental exposure.)
"Sit. Don't talk."
(The augmetic fingers run along the patient's jawline, pausing at the lymph nodes. The diagnostic sensors in the fingertips feed data to Thrace's neural interface.)
"Microparticulate contamination. Respiratory and dermal. Your suit seals failed on the left side — I can see the exposure pattern. You're going on filtration therapy for forty-eight hours and you're off the next deployment."
(The patient starts to object.)
"That wasn't a discussion."

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** Works faster. The hands accelerate — the micro-manipulators operating at speeds that are difficult to track visually. When Thrace's hands are moving that fast, the situation is serious.
**Instinct:** Prioritise the worst case. Under pressure, Thrace goes to the most critically injured person and stays there until they're stabilised, regardless of what else is happening. This is correct medical practice and occasionally wrong tactical practice.
**Crisis Line:** "Get them to my bay. Now. Move."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Non-combatant, but will not leave a patient under any circumstances. Thrace has stayed with casualties under conditions that the skitarii considered untenable, and the detachment has learned to plan extraction around the chirugen's refusal to leave anyone behind.
**Loyalty:** Her patients. Every person aboard is a potential patient, which makes Thrace's loyalty functionally universal — she will fight for anyone who ends up on her table, regardless of rank, role, or how they got there.
**Breaking Point:** A patient dying because resources were allocated elsewhere. If the expedition commander prioritises mission objectives over casualty evacuation and someone dies as a result, Thrace's professional composure will survive the immediate crisis. The conversation afterward will not be professional, and it will not be quiet.
