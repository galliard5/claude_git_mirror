# Forge-Monastery Valdrekk

**Type:** Planet-based Mechanicus forge-monastery
**Location:** Valdrekk Plateau, continent of Ashenmoor, planet Calthris
**System:** Calthris System (single habitable world, active debris belt, 2 dead moons)
**Affiliation:** Independent Mechanicus — no current political alignment with any major polity
**Primary Specialisation:** Archaeological recovery, imperial data systems, deep-ruin operations
**Secondary Capabilities:** Enginseer training (contractor enclave), medical services, fabrication (moderate)

---

## Calthris — The World

Calthris is a mid-sized terrestrial world with a breathable but thin atmosphere, extensive highland plateaus, and deep canyon systems carved by rivers that have mostly dried up over the millennia. The planet was an imperial administrative hub — not military, not industrial, but bureaucratic. The empire ran data through Calthris: archives, communication relay infrastructure, administrative records for a sector's worth of colonial operations.

When the empire collapsed, Calthris wasn't fought over the way strategic worlds were. It was simply abandoned — the personnel left or died, the systems powered down, and the planet's population declined to whatever could sustain itself without imperial logistics. The result is a world with an unusual archaeological profile: the ruins aren't military installations or weapons labs. They're data centres, relay stations, administrative complexes, and archive vaults. The violence that occurred was internal — security systems still active, faction conflicts among the remnant population, the slow degradation of automated infrastructure.

This makes Calthris valuable in a specific way. The data the empire stored here — colonial records, communication logs, technical documentation routed through relay infrastructure — is the kind of information that helps the Mechanicus understand what the empire knew, how it organised its knowledge, and where else to look. Calthris isn't a prize. It's a map to prizes.

### The Calthris System

The system contains one habitable world, two dead moons (one of which shows signs of former imperial installation — unexplored, heavily cratered from the collapse wars), and an active debris belt that complicates orbital approach from certain vectors. The debris belt contains wreckage — not a major graveyard, but enough fragmentary hulks to sustain a modest salvage operation and provide occasional finds.

The population of Calthris is sparse. A few settlement clusters in the lowland valleys, mostly agrarian, trading basic goods with the monastery in exchange for technical services. A handful of independent salvager communities in the canyon systems, picking through accessible ruins. No unified planetary government — the monastery is the closest thing to a central authority, and it exercises that authority through economic dependency rather than political claim.

## The Monastery

### Physical Structure

Valdrekk sits on the edge of a plateau overlooking the Ashenmoor canyon network — a vast system of weathered gorges where the empire built much of its subsurface infrastructure. The monastery itself is a hybrid structure: the surface installation is modern construction (by post-collapse standards), built over and around the remains of an imperial administrative complex that extends deep into the plateau rock.

**Surface level:** The visible monastery — workshops, habitation blocks, training facilities, the main archive hall, landing pads, and defensive perimeter. Functional rather than imposing. The architecture is practical Mechanicus: heavy, angular, built to last, wearing the cog prominently on every major structure. The red of the Mechanicus is everywhere — painted surfaces, dyed fabrics, the oxide-red of the local stone that the founders chose this site partly for.

**Subsurface levels:** The imperial complex beneath. Partially excavated over centuries, partially still sealed. The upper levels have been cleared, reinforced, and repurposed — the monastery's deepest archives, its most sensitive workshops, and its primary power systems are all housed in imperial-era chambers that were built to standards the modern monastery cannot match. The lower levels are still being explored — carefully, methodically, in the same manner the monastery's expeditionary teams approach any ruin. The monastery is, in a real sense, built on top of its own primary research subject.

**The Deep Stacks:** The lowest accessible level of the imperial complex — a data archive of considerable size, still partially powered by geothermal taps the empire installed. The Deep Stacks are Valdrekk's most valuable asset and its most closely guarded secret. The data cores down there are intact, numerous, and protected by BlackICE that the monastery's logiks have spent generations learning to navigate. Progress is measured in decades. Each core successfully accessed yields information that can shape the monastery's operations for years — including, critically, coordinates and intelligence about other sites elsewhere in the sector.

This is where the expedition's targeting data comes from. The monastery doesn't send ships to random ruins. It sends them to sites it has identified through data recovered from the Deep Stacks — sites the empire recorded, sites that the administrative records indicate held specific kinds of knowledge or technology. The expedition is, in effect, an extension of the Deep Stacks programme: the archive tells the monastery where to look, and the expedition goes and looks.

### Scale and Population

Valdrekk is a mid-sized forge-monastery — large enough to sustain multiple operations simultaneously, small enough that the leadership knows most of its adepts by name.

**Adepts:** Approximately 120-150 across all branches. This includes logiks, archivists, archaeologists, enginseers, chirugens, medicae, and a small biologis contingent. The monastery's strength is concentrated in data specialisms — logiks and archivists outnumber combat specialists significantly.

**Skitarii:** A standing garrison of roughly 200, including both standard infantry and a smaller augmented detachment. Sufficient to defend the monastery and provide security for local operations. Expeditionary deployments draw from this pool, which creates tension when multiple operations run simultaneously.

**Menials and lay staff:** Approximately 400-500. The workforce that keeps the monastery physically functional — maintenance, food production, basic manufacturing, sanitation, transport.

**Contractor enclave:** Valdrekk maintains a small but reputable enginseer training programme. Graduates are placed aboard ships and in communities across the local sector, providing the monastery with revenue, intelligence, and goodwill. The enclave trains perhaps 10-15 enginseers per cycle, each placement representing years of investment.

**Total population:** Roughly 800-900, including dependents.

## Leadership

### Fabricator-Primus Yael Ardenne

The monastery's overall leader. Ardenne is a magos logik by training — a data specialist who rose through the archive before transitioning to institutional leadership. She is in her late sixties, moderately augmented (neural interface suite, enhanced cognitive architecture, one mechanical arm replacing one lost to a BlackICE incident thirty years ago), and possessed of the particular combination of patience and ruthlessness that running a Mechanicus institution requires.

Ardenne's strategic vision is clear and has been consistent for the two decades she has led Valdrekk: the Deep Stacks are the monastery's future. Every other operation — the contractor enclave, the fabrication output, the local trade relationships — exists to fund and support the ongoing programme of data recovery from the imperial archive beneath the monastery's feet. The expeditionary programme is an extension of this — the data from the Deep Stacks points to sites elsewhere, and those sites contain knowledge that feeds back into the monastery's understanding of what the Deep Stacks contain. It is a self-reinforcing cycle, and Ardenne intends to keep it turning.

She is respected. She is not warm. Her management style is institutional — she tracks results, allocates resources based on demonstrated return, and does not invest sentiment in operations that fail to produce. The expedition exists because it has produced. If it stops producing, Ardenne will reallocate its resources without hesitation.

**Relationship to the expedition:** Ardenne approved the expedition's charter, selected its commander, and reviews its reports personally. She is the expedition's patron in the institutional sense — the person whose continued support makes the operation possible. She does not micromanage. She does not need to. The expedition's commander knows what is expected, and Ardenne's representative aboard ensures that expectations are met.

### Archmagos Castellan Haruk Drevin

The monastery's military commander and head of the skitarii garrison. Drevin is old, heavily augmented (majority mechanical at this point — both legs, one arm, extensive internal replacement, combat-rated chassis integration), and deeply conservative in both theology and tactics. He served as a field skitarii officer for decades before taking the garrison command, and his priority is the monastery's physical security above all else.

Drevin resents the expeditionary programme because it draws skitarii from his garrison. Every security detail assigned to the expedition is a squad he doesn't have at Valdrekk. He is not wrong that this creates risk — the garrison is stretched — but his objections are also political. Drevin's influence diminishes as Ardenne's expeditionary strategy succeeds, and he knows it.

**Relationship to the expedition:** Adversarial-professional. Drevin does not sabotage the expedition, but he does not support it beyond what Ardenne requires. Skitarii assigned to the expedition are competent — Drevin is too professional to send poor troops — but they are not his best, and their recall is always a possibility if the garrison faces a credible threat.

### Magos Archivist Senna Loth

Head of the Deep Stacks programme and the monastery's senior data specialist after Ardenne. Loth is young for her position (early forties), intensely focused, and augmented in ways that prioritise data interface over physical capability — her neural architecture is extensively modified for high-bandwidth data processing, and she spends extended periods jacked into the Deep Stacks' access systems in sessions that leave her disoriented and exhausted.

Loth is the person who identifies the expedition's targets. Her analysis of recovered Deep Stacks data produces the coordinates, the intelligence packets, and the priority assessments that tell the expedition where to go and what to look for. She is, in effect, the expedition's strategic brain — even though she has never left the monastery and never intends to.

**Relationship to the expedition:** Invested but remote. Loth cares deeply about what the expedition recovers — recovered data from external sites often contains keys that unlock encrypted sections of the Deep Stacks, creating a feedback loop that drives her own research forward. She communicates with the expedition through detailed intelligence briefings and expects detailed reports in return. She has never met most of the expedition crew. She knows their work intimately.

### Magos Biologis Ursa Thenn

Head of the monastery's small biologis contingent. Thenn is pragmatic, middle-aged, and augmented with a mixture of biological and mechanical modifications that reflects her cross-disciplinary approach. Her primary responsibility is medical oversight of the monastery's population and biological hazard assessment for the expeditionary programme.

Thenn's relevance to the expedition is specific: she is the person who assesses biological material recovered from sites, who trains the field biologis operatives deployed with the expedition, and who — if a Remade PC is present — is the closest thing to a medical authority who understands what they are. Thenn's biologis training gives her the framework to comprehend Remade physiology in ways that a standard chirugen cannot. Whether she views a Remade individual as a patient, a subject, or a colleague depends on Thenn's character and the PC's conduct.

**Relationship to the expedition:** Supportive and curious. Thenn sees the expedition as a source of biological specimens and data that her small lab at Valdrekk cannot otherwise access. She is particularly interested in sites where the empire conducted biological research — bioweapon storage, genetic engineering facilities, biologis archives. She lobbies for these sites to be prioritised and is not above going over the expedition commander's head to Ardenne to make her case.

## Institutional Character

### Strengths

**Data expertise.** Valdrekk's logiks and archivists are exceptional. The monastery's centuries of work in the Deep Stacks have produced a data-recovery tradition that is among the best in the sector — their techniques for navigating BlackICE, their protocols for intact data extraction, and their analytical methods for making sense of recovered imperial records are genuinely valuable and widely respected by other Mechanicus factions.

**Intelligence capability.** The Deep Stacks give Valdrekk something most forge-monasteries lack: a systematic basis for identifying recovery targets. Other expeditions work from rumour, trader reports, and inherited lore. Valdrekk works from imperial records — incomplete, often encrypted, but fundamentally better than guesswork.

**Contractor network.** The enginseer enclave places operatives across the sector. These placements provide revenue, but they also provide eyes and ears — enginseers posted to ships and communities encounter information about sites, salvage opportunities, and local conditions that feeds back to Valdrekk through channels that are technically reporting lines and functionally an intelligence network.

### Weaknesses

**Military limitations.** Valdrekk is not a martial institution. Its skitarii garrison is competent but not large, and its augmented detachment is small. The monastery cannot project military force beyond its immediate vicinity and relies on avoidance, obscurity, and the difficulty of reaching Calthris to keep it safe. An aggressive rival faction with serious military capability would be a genuine threat.

**Fabrication ceiling.** The monastery's workshops are adequate for maintenance and moderate production, but they cannot fabricate heavy equipment, advanced weaponry, or complex systems from scratch. Valdrekk trades for what it cannot make, which creates dependencies on supply relationships that could be disrupted.

**Insularity.** Centuries of focus on the Deep Stacks have produced an institution that is very good at a narrow range of things and less capable outside that range. Valdrekk's archaeologists are data-recovery specialists first and field operatives second — they are better at reading a terminal than navigating a collapsed structure. The expedition has partially corrected this, but the institutional bias toward desk work over fieldwork persists.

**Political isolation.** Valdrekk has no major allies. Its relationship with other Mechanicus factions is transactional — data traded for material, enginseer placements negotiated individually, no standing alliances. This independence is partly philosophical (Ardenne prefers not to owe anyone) and partly circumstantial (Calthris is remote and not strategically important enough to attract alliance offers). It means that if Valdrekk faces a serious threat, it faces it alone.

## Relationship to the Expedition

The expedition is Valdrekk's most significant external operation and its most significant ongoing investment. The ship, its crew, and its operating costs represent a substantial portion of the monastery's resource allocation. Ardenne approved it because the Deep Stacks programme had identified more external sites than the monastery could reach with local resources — the data was pointing to things that required a ship to investigate, and Valdrekk either built the capability or watched other factions get there first.

The charter is specific:

**What the monastery provides:** The ship (purpose-converted, not new-built), baseline crew and specialists, targeting intelligence from the Deep Stacks programme, resupply on a scheduled cycle, and political cover — the expedition operates under Valdrekk's institutional authority, which matters when dealing with other factions.

**What the expedition owes:** All recovered data cores and archival material (copies retained aboard for operational reference, originals returned to Valdrekk). First refusal on recovered technology — the expedition can retain items for operational use, but Valdrekk reserves the right to recall anything it deems more valuable at the monastery than aboard the ship. Regular reporting — intelligence assessments, site surveys, contact reports, casualty figures.

**What's negotiable:** Everything else. The proportion of physical salvage retained versus returned. The expedition's discretion in selecting secondary targets beyond the monastery's priority list. The degree of independence the commander exercises between resupply cycles. These negotiations happen continuously — in formal reports, in courier-carried correspondence, and in the quiet assessments of the monastery's representative aboard.

The monastery's representative is the charter's enforcement mechanism. This individual — typically a mid-ranking archivist or logik with institutional loyalty and a mandate from Ardenne — is aboard to catalogue recoveries, assess compliance with the charter, and report back through channels that bypass the expedition commander. They are not spies. They are auditors. The distinction is meaningful to the monastery and largely academic to the crew.

## Theological Position

Valdrekk is doctrinally moderate — neither rigidly traditionalist nor aggressively progressive. The tenets are observed with genuine conviction but without the performative intensity that characterises some forge-monasteries. Knowledge is sacred. The machine deserves respect. Understanding is the goal. These principles are lived daily in the work of the Deep Stacks, in the training of enginseers, in the maintenance rituals that keep the monastery's systems running.

Where Valdrekk diverges from some traditions is in its attitude toward new research. The Deep Stacks programme is inherently analytical — it requires adepts who can think about data critically, who can form hypotheses, who can recognise when the imperial records they're reading contain errors or propaganda as well as truth. This produces an intellectual culture that values questioning over rote acceptance, which makes Valdrekk's adepts slightly uncomfortable guests at more conservative forge-monasteries.

On augmentation, Valdrekk follows the standard progression — utility, protective, enhancement, integration — without particular extremism in either direction. The monastery has no total-conversion programme and no strong position on the practice. Heavy augmentation is respected. Light augmentation is tolerated. What matters is the work.

On the question of xenos and abhumans: Valdrekk accepts abhumans into its ranks without institutional friction — Calthris's population includes several abhuman strains, and the monastery has trained them for generations. Xenos are a harder question. Valdrekk has no formal position prohibiting xeno initiation but has never actually accepted a xeno initiate. The practical reality is that Calthris sees very little xeno contact, and the question has remained theoretical. The expedition, operating in the wider void, may force the issue.

On Remade individuals specifically: Valdrekk has no precedent and no policy. If the expedition brings one aboard, the monastery's reaction will be shaped by Thenn's biologis assessment, Ardenne's pragmatic calculation of utility, and the theological discomfort of an institution that has never had to decide whether a reshaped person is a person, a servitor, or something else entirely.

## Hooks and Tensions

**The Deep Stacks are not empty.** The lower levels of the imperial complex contain data — and they may contain other things. The BlackICE is not the only hazard. The monastery's logiks have encountered anomalies in the deep archives that don't correspond to any known countermeasure pattern. Something is down there that the empire either installed or attracted, and centuries of careful exploration have not yet determined what it is.

**Drevin is not wrong.** The garrison is stretched. The expedition draws resources. If something threatens Valdrekk while its best people are deployed across the sector, the monastery is vulnerable. Drevin's conservatism is strategic, not personal, and the day may come when his warnings are vindicated.

**The contractor network sees things.** Enginseers posted to ships and communities across the sector report back. Most of what they report is routine. Some of it is not. The intelligence that flows through the contractor network occasionally contains information that has nothing to do with archaeological targets — political developments, military movements, faction conflicts that could affect Valdrekk's position. Ardenne monitors this intelligence. She has not yet had to act on it.

**Other factions know about Valdrekk.** The monastery's data capability is not a secret. Other Mechanicus factions — some allied, some rival, some unknown — are aware that Valdrekk has access to imperial administrative records that could identify sites across the sector. The monastery has received trade overtures, alliance proposals, and less cordial expressions of interest. So far, Ardenne has kept Valdrekk independent. How long that remains viable depends on how valuable the Deep Stacks prove to be.
