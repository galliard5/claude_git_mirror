# The Proving Ground
### Mechanicus Expeditionary Vessel. Valdrekk Charter. Two and a Half Kilometres of Purpose.

## The Name

The Proving Ground is not a ship name chosen for intimidation or mythology. It is a Mechanicus name — institutional, deliberate, referencing what the vessel does. Every site the expedition enters is a proving ground: a test of the team's capability, a test of the data that sent them there, a test of whether the knowledge recovered justifies the cost of recovering it. The name was assigned when the vessel was recommissioned for the expedition, replacing whatever it was called before. Nobody uses the old name. Nobody aboard remembers it. The ship became the Proving Ground when it became Valdrekk's instrument, and the identity stuck because it was accurate.

---

## The Hull

The Proving Ground is a converted medium transport — originally a civilian cargo hauler of uncertain vintage, acquired by Valdrekk through trade, stripped to the frame, and rebuilt for expeditionary work. She sits at approximately two and a half kilometres stem to stern, narrow-beamed for her length, with a hull profile that prioritises manoeuvrability and sensor clearance over cargo volume or combat survivability.

She is not imperial construction. This matters less than it might seem. Imperial hulls are stronger, denser, stranger in their metallurgy — but they are also old, full of systems nobody fully understands, and prone to the specific category of problems that come with technology that has outlived the civilisation that built it. The Proving Ground is modern. Every rivet, every weld, every system aboard was put there by people who understood what they were doing. There are no sealed compartments that predate the crew. There are no sounds in the lower decks that nobody can explain. The ship is exactly what it appears to be, which is — by void standards — remarkably unusual.

The hull is robust without being armoured in any military sense. The original transport was built to survive the void, not combat — thick enough to shrug off microdebris and minor impacts, not thick enough to withstand deliberate weapons fire beyond the lightest grade. Valdrekk's refit added reinforced plating around the bridge, engineering, and shuttle bay — the critical sections — but the overall protection remains transport-grade. The Proving Ground does not survive fights. It avoids them.

## What She Carries

The refit stripped the original cargo configuration and replaced it with the systems an archaeological expedition requires. The result is a vessel that is internally more complex than her modest exterior suggests — not cramped, but dense, every section serving a defined purpose with minimal wasted volume.

### Bridge and Command

The bridge is compact and functional — a Mechanicus workspace, not a throne room. The expedition commander and ship master share the space without ceremony. Sensor displays dominate: the Proving Ground's primary tactical asset is knowing what's around her before it knows she's there. Navigation, communications, and ship status are arrayed for rapid assessment by a small command crew. The bridge seats eight comfortably and can function with three in an emergency.

The expedition commander's planning room adjoins the bridge — a working space with chart surfaces, a hololith projector for site mapping, and secure communication equipment for monastery contact. This is where deployment decisions are made: the commander, the chief archaeologist, and the ship master reviewing sensor data, intelligence packets from Valdrekk, and the scout's preliminary reports before committing the team.

### Sensor Arrays

The Proving Ground's sensors are her best feature and Valdrekk's most significant investment in the refit. The array suite is extensive for a vessel this size — long-range auspex for system survey, medium-range spectral analysis for orbital site assessment, and a close-range penetrating scanner package designed specifically for reading structures through rock, metal, and debris. The penetrating scanners are the monastery's own design, refined through decades of work in the Ashenmoor canyons, and they are genuinely good — capable of mapping subsurface architecture to a useful depth before the team sets foot on the ground.

This is a starting capability. The overview's XCOM progression applies here directly — recovered imperial sensor modules can be integrated into the existing array, extending range, adding spectral bands, or providing capabilities the monastery's engineers never designed for because they didn't know they existed until the expedition found them in a ruin.

### The Survey Bay

A dedicated space for orbital site assessment — the bridge handles flying the ship, the survey bay handles studying what she's found. Workstations for the chief archaeologist, the senior logik, and the biologis (when deployed) allow real-time analysis of sensor returns during approach and orbit. Atmospheric sampling, geological survey, electromagnetic mapping, and biological detection all feed into the survey bay for interpretation before anyone deploys.

The survey bay is also where the expedition builds its site maps — layering sensor data, scout reports, and archival intelligence from Valdrekk into a composite picture that the deployment team carries into the field. These maps are living documents, updated as the team feeds information back from inside the site. The survey bay crew — typically an archivist and a logik — maintain the map in real time during deployment, providing the field team with updated intelligence through their communication links.

### Shuttle Bay

The Proving Ground carries three shuttles in a ventral bay that opens through the hull's underside.

**Shuttle One — the workhorse.** A standard personnel transport, capacity for the full deployment team plus equipment. Armoured to the degree that a shuttle can be, which is to say enough to survive atmospheric entry and light ground fire but not a direct hit from anything serious. This is the team's primary delivery and extraction vehicle.

**Shuttle Two — the hauler.** Configured for cargo rather than personnel. Recoveries that are too large or too delicate for the team to carry come out in Shuttle Two. It can also serve as a supply run vehicle for basecamp operations during major expeditions — ferrying equipment, provisions, and replacement personnel between the ship and the surface.

**Shuttle Three — the tender.** The smallest of the three, configured for close-range void operations: orbital wreck penetration, debris field navigation, satellite recovery. Shuttle Three is what the team uses when the site is in space rather than on the ground. It carries a two-person crew and minimal cargo, trading capacity for the agility needed to navigate the tight spaces inside wrecked stations and ship graveyards.

The shuttle bay is a development target. Additional shuttles, specialist landers (shielded crawlers, submersibles, heavy-lift platforms for major recovery operations), and improved maintenance facilities for the existing vehicles are all potential upgrades as the expedition's capability grows.

### The Workshop

Amidships, occupying a section that was originally the transport's main cargo hold, the workshop is the Proving Ground's industrial heart. This is where equipment is maintained, field gear is prepared, recovered technology is assessed, and — increasingly — salvaged systems are integrated into the ship.

At campaign start, the workshop is functional but limited. It handles maintenance competently: weapon servicing, armour repair, communication equipment calibration, shuttle upkeep, the daily grind of keeping the expedition's kit working. It can fabricate replacement parts for standard Mechanicus equipment from raw stock. It cannot fabricate complex systems, produce advanced equipment from scratch, or work with materials beyond what its current tooling supports.

The workshop is staffed by two full-time enginseers (the ship's chief enginseer and a junior), supported by a team of lay technicians. Damien-level improvisation genius is not available here — the workshop crew are competent professionals who follow procedures, maintain standards, and produce reliable results within their capability. When they encounter something beyond that capability, they document it and flag it for the monastery.

**Development path:** Recovered fabrication templates, salvaged tooling, and imperial manufacturing data from the Deep Stacks programme all feed into the workshop's growth. A recovered plasma-cutting rig means the workshop can work with materials it previously couldn't. An intact Mechanicus fabrication template means it can produce equipment it previously had to requisition. The workshop's ceiling rises with every successful mission that brings back something the enginseers can use.

### The Archive

The Proving Ground's archive is small by monastery standards and enormous by the standards of any ship this size. It occupies a climate-controlled section forward of the workshop — racks of data storage, a handful of physical specimen cases, and workstations where the archivists and logiks process recovered material.

The archive serves two functions. First, it is the expedition's institutional memory — session logs, site surveys, intelligence assessments, cross-references with the monastery's records, everything the team has learned about the sites they've visited and the patterns they're beginning to recognise across sites. Second, it is the staging area for recovered data — material brought back from the field is stored here, catalogued, and prepared either for analysis aboard or for transfer to Valdrekk at the next resupply.

The archive is staffed by two archivists — the senior archivist (who doubles as the monastery's representative aboard) and a junior who does most of the actual cataloguing work. A dedicated logik handles data-core interfacing and recovery processing.

**Security note:** The archive contains copies of targeting intelligence from the Deep Stacks programme — the coordinates and assessments that tell the expedition where to go. This information is among Valdrekk's most closely guarded assets. The archive's data systems are secured to monastery standard, and the senior archivist has standing orders regarding what happens to the archive if the ship is boarded or captured. Those orders are not discussed openly.

### Medicae Bay

Positioned between the crew quarters and the workshop, the medicae bay is equipped for field-expedition medicine — trauma surgery, environmental exposure treatment, augmetic maintenance, and the specific category of injuries that come from entering places humans were not meant to go.

The bay has four surgical berths, a recovery ward with eight beds, a quarantine compartment (sealed, separately ventilated, capable of maintaining negative pressure), and a pharmaceutical station that can compound standard Mechanicus medicae preparations from stored materials. It is staffed by the ship's chirugen — the senior medical officer — and a medicae assistant.

The quarantine compartment was not part of the original refit specification. It was added after the second mission, when the team brought something back that shouldn't have left the site. The chirugen insisted. The expedition commander agreed. It has been used three times since.

**Remade considerations:** If a Remade PC is aboard, the medicae bay may require modification to accommodate non-standard physiology. A biologically reshaped individual may need atmospheric adjustments in the recovery ward. A cybernetically converted individual may need diagnostic interfaces the bay doesn't currently carry. These modifications are part of the ship's development — adapting to the crew it actually has rather than the crew it was designed for.

### Crew Quarters

The Proving Ground carries fewer people than the Fates Cast by an order of magnitude, and the living conditions reflect it. Quarters are not spacious — this is still a ship — but they are individual rather than communal for all adepts and specialist crew. Menials share dormitory-style berths, four to a compartment, which is better than most ships manage.

The senior staff — the expedition commander, the ship master, the chief archaeologist, the chirugen, the senior archivist — have slightly larger private quarters. "Slightly larger" means room for a desk and a shelf in addition to the bunk. Luxury is not a Mechanicus value.

Common spaces include a mess hall that doubles as a briefing room, a small machine-chapel for observances, and a maintenance bay that serves as informal social space for the engineering crew. The ship is small enough that privacy is limited and everyone knows everyone. This is by design — Valdrekk's expedition planners understood that a team that cannot live together in close quarters for months at a stretch will not function in the field. Crew selection accounts for this.

**The machine-chapel** is modest — a small chamber near the engineering deck containing the ship's primary cog-symbol, an altar of sorts where the daily maintenance liturgy is performed, and space for perhaps fifteen to stand during formal observances. The chapel is not ornate. It is serious. The rites performed here are the same ones performed in Valdrekk's own chapel, adapted for shipboard conditions — the Proving Ground carries the monastery's traditions into the void, and the chapel is where those traditions are maintained.

### Engineering

The drive section occupies the aft third of the ship — standard modern warp-capable drives, reliable, maintainable, and adequate for the Proving Ground's operational profile. The ship is not fast by warship standards but she is responsive, and the drives are tuned for the kind of careful manoeuvring that archaeological survey requires: slow orbital approaches, station-keeping in debris fields, the patient work of positioning a ship precisely where her sensors need her to be.

The engineering deck is the chief enginseer's domain. Power distribution, drive maintenance, life support management, and the ongoing work of integrating recovered systems into the ship's infrastructure all happen here. The chief enginseer and the workshop crew share personnel — on a ship this size, the distinction between shipboard engineering and workshop fabrication is staffing rather than structural.

The engineering deck also houses the ship's secondary power systems — backup generators, emergency life support, and the dedicated power feeds for the sensor arrays and archive systems. These are the systems that stay running if everything else fails, because losing sensors in a debris field or losing archive integrity during a data transfer are both outcomes the expedition cannot afford.

### Armament

The Proving Ground carries defensive armament only:

**Point defence arrays** — Four turret positions covering the main approach vectors. Adequate against debris, small craft, and torpedoes at close range. Not adequate against a warship's broadside. The point defence is automated with manual override — the ship doesn't carry dedicated weapons crew, so the system needs to function without human intervention in most scenarios.

**Dorsal lance** — A single light lance battery mounted on the upper hull. This is the Proving Ground's only offensive weapon and it is modest — sufficient to discourage a raider, insufficient to threaten anything larger than a corvette. The lance was part of the original transport's self-defence fit and was retained during the refit because removing it would have been more work than keeping it.

**The philosophy** is simple: the Proving Ground does not fight. If something hostile appears, the ship runs. If it cannot run, the point defence buys time. If time isn't enough, the lance is a last argument. The expedition commander who picks a fight in the Proving Ground is an expedition commander who has run out of better options.

**Development note:** Recovered imperial weapons technology could change this calculus. A salvaged weapons system integrated into the Proving Ground's hardpoints would give the ship capabilities it currently lacks — but would also change the ship's profile from "archaeological vessel not worth engaging" to "archaeological vessel carrying something worth taking." The decision to install recovered weapons is a strategic choice with consequences beyond the immediate improvement in firepower.

### Defensive Systems

**Void shields** — A single-layer void shield generator, transport-grade. Sufficient to absorb a few hits from light weapons, not sufficient to sustain combat against a military vessel. The shield generator is reliable and well-maintained — it is one of the systems the chief enginseer checks personally on a daily cycle.

**Electronic countermeasures** — The sensor suite that makes the Proving Ground good at finding things also makes her reasonably good at not being found. The ECM package is passive — sensor absorption coatings on the hull, emission management protocols, the ability to run silent when the situation demands it. The Proving Ground's best defence is being somewhere nobody is looking, and the ECM supports that strategy.

## Crew Complement

The Proving Ground operates with a total complement of approximately 80-100 personnel:

**Command:** 3 (expedition commander, ship master, chief archaeologist)

**Field team:** 15-20 (scouts, archaeologists, logiks, field enginseer, field medicae, field biologis — not all deployed simultaneously; team composition varies by mission)

**Skitarii detachment:** 20-25 (security for deployments and ship defence; drawn from Valdrekk's garrison, reporting to a detachment leader who takes operational direction from the expedition commander but maintains a separate line back to Castellan Drevin)

**Ship crew:** 15-20 (navigation, helm, engineering, drive technicians, sensor operators, shuttle pilots)

**Support staff:** 10-15 (archivists, workshop technicians, medicae staff, menials)

**Monastery representative:** 1 (the senior archivist, carrying Ardenne's mandate)

This is a small crew for a ship this size, and the volume shows — large sections of the ship are empty or underutilised, former cargo spaces that the refit didn't fill because there wasn't need or budget. These empty sections are potential development space: a compartment that's currently bare hull can become a biologis laboratory, an expanded workshop bay, additional crew quarters, or a specimen containment facility as the expedition's needs and capabilities grow.

## Operating Profile

### Transit

The Proving Ground makes warp transit on established routes where possible and careful short-range jumps where necessary. She does not carry a dedicated Navigator — Valdrekk could not justify the expense for a vessel this size, and Navigators willing to serve on an archaeological expedition are vanishingly rare. Transit is handled by the ship master using conventional warp navigation: established lane charts, short hops with extensive real-space survey between jumps, and the conservative approach that trading vessels have used since warp travel was rediscovered.

This limits range. A ship with a Navigator can cut across open warp, taking direct routes between systems. The Proving Ground follows the lanes — slower, safer, limited to destinations accessible by charted routes. Sites in uncharted systems require a series of careful jumps from the nearest lane exit, each one a calculated risk. The ship master's competence in this area is critical to the expedition's reach.

**Development opportunity:** Acquiring a Navigator — through Valdrekk's connections, through a contract, or through circumstances the expedition did not anticipate — would transform the ship's operational capability. It would also transform the ship's culture. A Navigator aboard a Mechanicus vessel is a theological and practical complication that the machine-cult framework is not naturally designed to accommodate.

### Deployment Cycle

A typical operational cycle runs:

**Transit** — travel to the target system. Duration varies from days to weeks depending on distance and route complexity.

**Survey** — orbital assessment of the target site. The sensor arrays and survey bay work the site from orbit, building the initial picture. Duration: hours to days, depending on site complexity and what the sensors reveal.

**Deployment** — the team goes in. Scouts first, then the main team, then support and security as the site's character becomes clear. Duration: hours for a quick recovery, days to weeks for a standard exploration or major expedition.

**Extraction** — the team comes out with whatever they've recovered. Shuttle operations return personnel and material to the ship. The survey bay crew updates the site map with final data.

**Processing** — the archivists and logiks work through recovered material aboard ship. The workshop crew assesses recovered technology. The chirugen treats injuries. Reports are drafted for Valdrekk.

**Transit out** — return to Valdrekk for resupply and reporting, or transit to the next target if the expedition commander judges the ship's supplies and crew condition sufficient for another deployment.

### Resupply

The Proving Ground returns to Calthris on a roughly quarterly cycle — more frequently if supplies run low or casualties require replacement personnel, less frequently if the expedition is deep in a productive run of sites. Resupply at Valdrekk involves:

- Transferring recovered material to the monastery (data cores, artefacts, specimens — per the charter's terms)
- Receiving new targeting intelligence from Magos Loth's Deep Stacks analysis
- Restocking consumables (food, medical supplies, fabrication raw stock, ammunition)
- Personnel rotation (replacing casualties, rotating skitarii, occasionally receiving new specialists)
- Ship maintenance beyond what the onboard crew can handle
- The expedition commander's report to Fabricator-Primus Ardenne — in person, detailed, assessed

Resupply is also when the monastery's political dynamics touch the expedition most directly. Ardenne's assessment of the expedition's performance, Drevin's complaints about skitarii allocation, Loth's enthusiasm about what the recovered data reveals, Thenn's requests for biological specimens — all of these converge during the days the ship spends docked at Valdrekk's modest orbital platform.

## The Ship's Character

The Proving Ground is not the Fates Cast. She has no mysterious lower decks, no millennia of accumulated strangeness, no sealed compartments that whisper. She is a working vessel, purpose-built, institutionally owned, crewed by professionals who are here because the monastery sent them or because the work called to them. Her character comes not from age or mystery but from the crew who serve aboard her and the work they do.

The machine-cult rituals are observed with Valdrekk's particular flavour of moderate devotion — genuine but not performative. The maintenance liturgy is spoken because the machine deserves respect, and because the rituals remind the crew that what they do matters. The cog is everywhere: on every bulkhead, on every uniform, on the chapel altar, on the tools the enginseers carry. The red of the Mechanicus marks the ship inside and out. This is an institution's vessel, and the institution is present in every surface and practice.

What gives the Proving Ground her own identity — distinct from Valdrekk, distinct from any other Mechanicus vessel — is the work. The crew goes into places that have been sealed for millennia. They bring back knowledge that changes what the monastery understands about the empire, about the collapse, about what was lost. Every successful mission is a small victory against the entropy that has been consuming the empire's legacy for thirty thousand years. The crew knows this. It matters to them — not abstractly, not as institutional rhetoric, but as the daily reality of their work. They are the people who go into the dark and bring back what they find, and the Proving Ground is the ship that carries them there and brings them home.

That is enough history for a ship that is still writing it.

## Development Targets — Campaign Progression

The following are the ship's primary development categories, referencing the XCOM progression outlined in the campaign overview. Each category lists the starting state and examples of what upgrades look like.

### Sensors and Survey
**Start:** Valdrekk penetrating scanners, standard auspex, spectral analysis.
**Early upgrades:** Recovered imperial atmospheric analyser (better pre-deployment bio-hazard detection). Salvaged deep-scan module (penetrating scanners reach further into subsurface structures).
**Late upgrades:** Imperial-grade auspex integration (detection range rivals military vessels). Arcanum-sensitive scanner (detects warp contamination before deployment — if the crew is willing to install something that reads the warp).

### Workshop and Fabrication
**Start:** Maintenance-grade. Standard Mechanicus parts fabrication from raw stock.
**Early upgrades:** Recovered plasma-cutting rig (work with exotic materials). Salvaged fabrication template library (produce equipment previously requisitioned from Valdrekk).
**Late upgrades:** Imperial micro-fabricator (precision manufacturing at tolerances the modern workshop cannot achieve). Full production capability for specialist field equipment — breaching tools, hardened data-recovery rigs, environmental suits tailored to specific site conditions.

### Archive and Analysis
**Start:** Standard data storage, manual cataloguing, single logik workstation for data-core processing.
**Early upgrades:** Recovered cogitation array (faster processing, pattern recognition across multiple data sources). Improved cross-referencing capability (the archive begins connecting findings across sites without archivist intervention).
**Late upgrades:** Imperial-grade analysis engine (the archive becomes genuinely predictive — identifying likely site contents based on pattern matching with the Deep Stacks dataset). This upgrade would make the Proving Ground's archive more capable than some sections of Valdrekk's own, which has political implications.

### Medical and Life Support
**Start:** Competent field medicine. Standard chirugen capability. Basic quarantine.
**Early upgrades:** Recovered imperial diagnostic suite (the chirugen can identify conditions they currently miss). Improved quarantine containment (biological specimens can be held safely aboard for study rather than destroyed in the field).
**Late upgrades:** Gene-therapy capability (the chirugen can treat genetic damage from radiation or biological weapon exposure). Biologis-grade laboratory (full biological analysis aboard — specimens don't need to wait for Valdrekk). Life support modifications for non-standard crew physiology (the ship adapts to its Remade crew member rather than requiring the crew member to adapt to the ship).

### Shuttle Bay and Deployment
**Start:** Three shuttles (personnel, cargo, void tender). Standard capacity.
**Early upgrades:** Additional shuttle (redundancy, or specialist configuration). Improved landing gear for rough-terrain deployment. Shuttle-mounted sensor package (the scout can survey from the air before going in on foot).
**Late upgrades:** Shielded surface crawler (deployable from Shuttle Two's cargo bay — armoured ground transport for irradiated or contested sites). Submersible pod (underwater site access). Heavy-lift platform (recovery of large artefacts or equipment from major sites).

### Armament and Defence
**Start:** Point defence and a single light lance. Transport-grade void shields.
**Early upgrades:** Improved point defence tracking (faster response, better accuracy against small targets). ECM enhancement (harder to detect, harder to lock onto).
**Late upgrades:** Recovered imperial weapons system (a significant capability jump — but changes the ship's threat profile permanently). Upgraded void shields (military-grade, if a compatible unit is recovered — transforms survivability but requires power system modifications the chief enginseer will have opinions about).

### Crew Capacity and Quarters
**Start:** 80-100 crew in existing habitation. Empty hull sections available for expansion.
**Early upgrades:** Additional crew quarters in former cargo space (support a larger team or specialist additions). Dedicated biologis laboratory (if the expedition's work trends biological).
**Late upgrades:** Expanded skitarii barracks (support a larger security detachment for major operations). Arcanum containment chamber (if the expedition encounters warp-adjacent phenomena that require secure study — a controversial addition that the monastery's moderate theology may resist). Navigator quarters and helm integration (if the expedition acquires a Navigator — a transformation of the ship's capability and culture simultaneously).
