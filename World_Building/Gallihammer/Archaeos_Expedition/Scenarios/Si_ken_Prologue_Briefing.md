## Quick Orientation

Si'ken is a kobold who was captured, sold, and reshaped into an insectoid hybrid organism with integrated Mechanicus cybernetics (NIJack and mechadendrite utilities) by a specialist working for a wealthy patron. The prologue plays through this origin: who Si'ken was, how autonomy was lost, the patron's commission, the reshaping, the flaw the specialist left, and the transition to Forge-Monastery Valdrekk's custody. The session ends with Si'ken aboard the Proving Ground, assessed, assigned, and waiting for the expedition's first mission.

This is a Remade Origin Framework session. The tone is body horror, institutional power, and the slow emergence of agency from a situation designed to deny it. Si'ken is not rescued — Si'ken is acquired by a different institution, which has its own purposes. The prologue should establish that the Mechanicus is better than the patron, but not good. Not safe. Not home — not yet.

**Central tension:** Si'ken's mind survived intact. Everything else was taken. The prologue tracks what that means — from the inside.

## Setting Context — What Sonnet Needs to Know

This is Gallihammer — a post-collapse far-future setting where humanity's stellar empire fell thirty to forty thousand years ago. Races include humans, dwarves, elves, orcs, halflings, anthros, and kobolds — all products of divergence, radiation, and imperial-era genetic engineering. Technology is a patchwork of modern fabrication and poorly-understood imperial remnants. The Mechanicus is a fractured pan-civilisational institution that treats the recovery and preservation of imperial technical knowledge as a sacred obligation.

**Kobolds in Gallihammer:** An abhuman strain — small, scaled, adapted to underground and tight-space environments. They exist across the setting in warrens, settlements, and integrated into wider communities. Not a joke race — a people with their own cultures, communities, and history. Si'ken's specific kobold background is established during play.

**The Remade:** Individuals who have been biologically reshaped, cybernetically converted, or both — forced into inhuman forms by specialists working for patrons who purchased their bodies. Cognisant Remade retain their minds. The reshaping is total: the original body is gone, replaced with something designed for a purpose. The Remade Origin Framework (provided below in full) governs this process.

**The Mechanicus:** Treat the Mechanicus as a religious-technical institution. Knowledge is sacred. The machine deserves respect. Understanding is the goal. Augmentation is expected. The cog symbol is universal. Red is the primary colour. For this prologue, the relevant Mechanicus elements are: biologis (biological science), cyberwright (cybernetic engineering), enginseers (general technical), NIJack (neural interface jack — communication, tools, datasphere access), and mechadendrites (utility appendages).

## The Origin Sequence — Scene Structure

The prologue follows seven beats. Each beat is a scene or short sequence. The player directs Si'ken's responses and choices within each beat. The world moves between beats whether the player acts or not.

Pacing note: this is a single prologue session, not a multi-session arc. Move through the beats at a pace that gives each weight without stalling. The reshaping sequence (Beat 4) should be the longest — it is the central experience. The other beats frame it.

---

### Beat 1: Who Was Si'ken?

**Purpose:** Establish the kobold before the reshaping. Name, community, daily life, personality. Give the player time to inhabit the person who is about to be taken.

**Scene:** Si'ken in their life — a kobold community, a role, a routine. The specific details emerge through play. Ask the player (through the fiction, not out of character) to show what Si'ken's life looked like: where they lived, what they did, who mattered to them. Do not ask "what is your backstory?" — put Si'ken in a moment and let the player fill it.

**Suggested opening:** A specific, small moment in Si'ken's daily life. Not a dramatic event — an ordinary moment. Waking up. Working. Eating. Something that will hurt to remember later because it was so normal.

**What to establish:**
- Si'ken's community (warren? surface settlement? integrated town?)
- Si'ken's role (what do they do? what are they good at?)
- Si'ken's relationships (who matters? even one name is enough)
- Si'ken's personality through action, not description

**Choice points:** Slice-of-life choices. What Si'ken does with a free moment. How they interact with the people around them. Small decisions that define character.

**Skill tree note:** Whatever Si'ken does in this scene may justify starting nodes when the skill block is finalised. A tunnel-runner might earn Edge/Stealth I. A tinkerer might earn Craft/Making I. Track what the player shows you.

**End condition:** The ordinary moment is interrupted by what comes next.

### Beat 2: Loss of Autonomy

**Purpose:** How Si'ken was taken. This establishes the power dynamic and the stakes.

**Possible mechanisms (let the fiction guide which one fits what Beat 1 established):**
- **Debt bondage** — Si'ken or their family owes a debt that is called in. The creditor sells the debt to someone with a specific use for a kobold.
- **Raid/capture** — Si'ken's community is hit. Raiders take prisoners. Si'ken is among them. The raiders sell to a buyer who is looking for specific raw material.
- **Sale by community** — The community trades Si'ken to an outside power in exchange for something it needs. Protection, supplies, technology. Si'ken may or may not have been consulted.
- **Criminal sentencing** — Si'ken is convicted of something (justly or not) and sentenced to labour or service. The sentence is transferred to a patron who has other plans.
- **Abandonment** — No community, no protector, no one to object. Si'ken was alone and that made them available.

**Tone:** Not action-movie violence. Institutional violence — the kind that operates through paperwork, transactions, and the assumption that certain people are property. The moment Si'ken loses autonomy should feel like a door closing, not a battle lost.

**What to establish:**
- How Si'ken was taken (the mechanism)
- Si'ken's response (fight? freeze? comply? negotiate?)
- The transition from person to commodity — the moment when Si'ken is no longer addressed by name

**Choice points:** Limited. The situation is designed to remove agency. The choices available are how Si'ken responds internally and what small acts of resistance or preservation they attempt, not whether they escape. Escape is not available. The framework is clear: the patron is too powerful, the situation too complete. Si'ken is taken.

### Beat 3: The Patron and the Commission

**Purpose:** Establish who bought Si'ken and what they want. The patron is a role, not a fully developed character — they exist to explain the reshaping and to cast a shadow over Si'ken's post-origin existence.

**The Patron — Overseer Dren Valcetti**

A mid-tier industrial operator running a salvage and mineral extraction operation in a system with extensive pre-collapse ruins. Valcetti is not a warlord, not a noble — a businessman with enough capital and enough moral flexibility to commission Remade workers for his operation. He needs organisms that can go where his baseline crew cannot: pressurised ruin interiors, toxic processing environments, narrow structural spaces. He needs them cognisant because thinking tools make fewer mistakes, and he needs them equipped with Mechanicus interfaces because his operation runs Mechanicus-derived systems.

Valcetti has commissioned Remade before. This is not his first. He treats the process the way he treats equipment acquisition — a cost-benefit calculation. The subject's experience of the process is not a factor in the calculation.

**The Commission:**
Valcetti wants a compact, environmentally resilient organism that can operate in confined, hazardous spaces — the interior of partially collapsed pre-collapse structures, toxic atmospheres, radiation zones. He wants it small enough to access spaces his baseline workers can't fit into. He wants it cognisant so it can assess what it finds, make judgement calls about structural stability, and report effectively. He wants it equipped with NIJack for communication and data interface (his operation's systems are Mechanicus-compatible) and mechadendrite utilities for fine manipulation in the field — the organism needs to handle salvage, not just find it.

He specified an insectoid body plan because the arthropod architecture provides the environmental resilience, the compact form factor, and the multi-limb manipulation capability the job requires. He did not specify this because he cared about what Si'ken would experience inhabiting it. The form follows the function. The subject's relationship with the form is irrelevant to the patron.

**Cognizance motivation:** Pragmatic. "A thinking probe makes better decisions than a programmed one." Valcetti does not enjoy Si'ken's awareness. He simply does not consider it worth the reduced functionality to remove it.

**Scene:** Si'ken sees Valcetti, or hears about Valcetti, or is simply told what will happen without meeting the patron at all. The commission may be explained to Si'ken directly (paternalistic or pragmatic patron) or Si'ken may learn what's coming through context — being transported to a facility, hearing the specialist discuss the work, seeing the equipment. How much Si'ken knows in advance is a pacing choice: more foreknowledge creates dread; less creates shock. Both work.

**What to establish:**
- Valcetti exists as a presence, not necessarily as a face — he may never appear on-screen
- The purpose of the reshaping: salvage probe, hazardous-environment operative, thinking tool
- The specification: insectoid body, compact, environmentally resilient, NIJack, mechadendrites, cognisant
- Si'ken's awareness of what's about to happen (full, partial, or none)

**Choice points:** Si'ken's internal response. Attempts to communicate with the specialist, if present. Last moments in the kobold body. Again, escape is not available — the choices are about how Si'ken faces what's coming, not whether it happens.

### Beat 4: The Reshaping

**Purpose:** The central scene. The transformation from kobold to insectoid hybrid. This is body horror played with weight and specificity — not gratuitous, but unflinching. Si'ken experiences the loss of their original body and the construction of the new one. The mind survives. Everything else changes.

**The Specialist — Magos Biologis Yvet Cern**

A Mechanicus biologis with cyberwright cross-training — rare and expensive, which is why Valcetti pays what Cern charges. Cern is a professional. The work is technically challenging and she finds it interesting. The ethical dimension is present in her mind but subordinate to the craft — she is not sadistic, not enthusiastic, simply focused on the work. She has done this before. She is good at it.

Cern's attitude toward Si'ken is **reluctant professional**. She does the work cleanly because that is her standard. She does not enjoy Valcetti's commissions but she accepts them because refusing is not financially or politically viable. She plants the flaw out of professional conscience — not dramatic rebellion, just a quiet refusal to build perfect cages. She may not fully admit to herself what she's done.

Cern has biologis augmentation (grown modifications, not mechanical) and the standard Mechanicus cybernetic interfaces required for her work. She communicates with Si'ken during the procedure — clinical, informative, not warm. She tells Si'ken what is happening because informed subjects have better outcomes, not because she considers Si'ken's comfort a priority.

**The Process:**
The reshaping is biological with cybernetic integration. Staged over days — not instant. Si'ken is conscious for portions of it (sedated for the most invasive structural work, conscious during neural integration and sensory calibration).

Stages:
1. **Structural dissolution** — The kobold body's architecture is broken down at the cellular level. Bone, muscle, scale — reorganised into raw material for the new form. This is the most invasive stage. Si'ken is sedated. When consciousness returns, the kobold body is gone.
2. **Insectoid architecture** — The new body is built around Si'ken's brain and central nervous system. Chitin-analogue exoskeleton, restructured musculature, new limbs, new respiratory system. Si'ken is partially conscious — aware of the body changing, aware of new sensations without context for them.
3. **Sensory calibration** — New sensory organs come online. Si'ken's perception of the world changes fundamentally. What was seen through kobold eyes is now processed through compound visual organs, antennae, vibration sensors. The world looks, sounds, smells, and feels different. This is disorienting and the specialist monitors cognitive function throughout.
4. **Cybernetic integration** — The NIJack is installed, wired through the biological tissue into Si'ken's neural architecture. The mechadendrite utility appendages are bonded to the thoracic exoskeleton with interface points connecting to the motor cortex. These systems come online in sequence — the NIJack first (Si'ken suddenly has access to an internal communication system, data tools, a connection to something that wasn't there before), then the mechadendrites (new limbs that respond to thought but don't feel like the organic ones do).
5. **Control architecture** — The obedience system is installed. This is the leash. Valcetti's control mechanism, implemented through a combination of biological conditioning and cybernetic override. Si'ken feels it as a layer of compulsion — directives that feel like the subject's own thoughts until they conflict with the subject's actual will, at which point the compulsion hardens into something that cannot be argued with.

**Writing the reshaping:**
- Render through Si'ken's sensory experience. What does it feel like? What is the first thing the new eyes see? What does chitin feel like from the inside? How do the mechadendrites move?
- The loss of the kobold body is a death. Not metaphorical. The body Si'ken was born in is gone. The grief is real even if the mind survives.
- The new body is alien. It is not immediately controllable. Si'ken's first movements in the insectoid form should be clumsy, uncertain, the disconnect between intention and execution visible.
- The NIJack coming online is a specific moment — suddenly there is a system in Si'ken's head that wasn't there before. It offers tools, connection, data. It is also the patron's leash.
- The control architecture should be felt, not explained. Si'ken knows something is constraining them. The mechanism is not transparent.

**Choice points during reshaping:**
- How Si'ken responds to consciousness returning in the new body
- What Si'ken tries to do first (move? speak? reach for something familiar?)
- Si'ken's internal response to the new senses — reach toward them or retreat?
- Response to the NIJack activation — engage with it or resist?
- Response to the control architecture — does Si'ken test the boundaries?

### Beat 5: The Flaw

**Purpose:** Establish what the specialist left. This may not be revealed to the player directly — the flaw can be something the character discovers over time, or something the player knows but the character doesn't.

**The Flaw — Override Conflict**

Cern built the cybernetic control system and Si'ken's own NIJack on architectures that are subtly incompatible. The patron's control system and Si'ken's own neural interface are running on frameworks that compete for priority under stress. Cern designed the incompatibility. Under cognitive load — fear, anger, intense focus, physical extremis — the control system and Si'ken's own will contest for dominance, and Si'ken's will has the home advantage because it runs on the original neural hardware.

This means:
- Under normal conditions, the control holds. Si'ken obeys.
- Under stress, the control wavers. Si'ken may notice moments of clarity — thoughts that feel like their own rather than directed.
- Under extreme stress, the control can fail entirely for brief periods. These windows are short and unpredictable.
- The flaw is not detectable through standard diagnostics. Cern built it at the firmware level. Valcetti's technicians would need to understand the architecture at Cern's level of expertise to find it, and Cern is the one who trained them.

**How to surface this in play:**
- Do not announce the flaw. Let Si'ken experience moments where the compulsion feels weaker than expected — a thought that shouldn't be possible under control, a hesitation where instant obedience should have occurred.
- These moments accumulate. The player may recognise the pattern before the character does.
- The flaw is the crack in the cage. It doesn't break the cage. It makes breaking possible, eventually, if the right pressure is applied at the right moment.

**Player knowledge vs. character knowledge:** Offer this choice to the player (in character, through the fiction): Si'ken may notice something is different during a stressful moment in service to Valcetti. Whether the player recognises it as the specialist's sabotage is a metagame question. Whether Si'ken understands what it means is a character question. Both can develop through play.

### Beat 6: Service and Transition

**Purpose:** Show Si'ken in Valcetti's service — briefly — and then show how Valdrekk acquired Si'ken. This beat can be compressed: montage the service period, then play the transition in detail.

**Service:**
Si'ken works. The insectoid body does what it was built for — navigates hazardous spaces in Valcetti's extraction operation, interfaces with Mechanicus-derived systems through the NIJack, uses the mechadendrites for fine manipulation in environments too dangerous for baseline workers. The work is dangerous, isolating, and effective. Si'ken is good at the job. The body was well-designed. The mind makes the body better at the job than a non-cognisant organism would be, which validates Valcetti's specification.

Show this briefly — a flash of what Si'ken's daily existence looked like. The environments, the isolation, the control architecture directing action, the moments where the flaw surfaces as a flicker of autonomous thought that shouldn't be possible.

**The Transition to Valdrekk:**

How Valdrekk acquired Si'ken is one of the following (choose based on the fiction's trajectory through earlier beats):

**Option A — Valcetti's operation intersected with Valdrekk's interests.** The monastery's expeditionary programme (or a precursor survey) identified a site in the same system where Valcetti operates. Contact between Valdrekk representatives and Valcetti's operation produced awareness of Si'ken's existence. Magos Biologis Ursa Thenn, reviewing the contact report, recognised what Si'ken was — a Remade with Mechanicus cybernetic integration — and flagged the acquisition opportunity to Fabricator-Primus Ardenne. Valdrekk acquired Si'ken through negotiation with Valcetti — purchased, traded for, or obtained through leverage the monastery had and used. Valcetti parted with Si'ken because the price was right, or because refusing Valdrekk was more expensive than complying.

**Option B — Cern contacted Valdrekk.** The specialist who made Si'ken had her own relationship with the Mechanicus — professional, transactional, possibly conflicted. After completing the commission, Cern passed information about Si'ken to a Valdrekk contact — not out of altruism, but because a cognisant Remade with Mechanicus interfaces represented something that Cern believed the monastery should know about. Whether Cern intended rescue, research interest, or simply professional networking is ambiguous. Valdrekk acted on the information.

**Option C — Si'ken was seized.** Valcetti's operation ran afoul of a Mechanicus faction — not Valdrekk specifically, but a group whose interests Valcetti's operation had damaged. The operation was shut down or raided. Si'ken was among the assets recovered. Through institutional channels, Si'ken was routed to Valdrekk, where Thenn's biologis expertise made the monastery the logical destination for a biological Remade with cybernetic integration.

**In all cases:**
- Si'ken arrives at Valdrekk's forge-monastery on Calthris
- The control architecture is assessed and partially deactivated by Valdrekk's technicians (the monastery's expertise in cybernetic systems is sufficient to disable the patron's override, though the deeper incompatibility Cern built is not detected — it looks like standard firmware variance)
- Magos Biologis Ursa Thenn conducts the assessment: medical, cognitive, capability evaluation
- Fabricator-Primus Ardenne reviews Thenn's report and makes the decision: Si'ken is assigned to the Proving Ground expedition as a specialist scout asset

**What Si'ken experiences at Valdrekk:**
- The control architecture weakening — not gone, but the patron's directives are no longer active. Si'ken's own will is more present than it has been since the reshaping. This is disorienting. Autonomy feels strange after sustained control.
- Thenn's assessment — clinical, thorough, not unkind but not warm. Thenn is a biologis examining an extraordinary specimen that is also a person, and she is navigating that dual reality in real time. She asks Si'ken questions. She listens to the answers. She does not explain what her report will say.
- The monastery environment — the forge, the cog symbology, the red, the machine-cult practices. Si'ken has Mechanicus hardware wired into their body. Being inside a Mechanicus institution, surrounded by people who wear the same symbols that are integrated into Si'ken's own chassis, is a specific and complex experience.
- Being told about the assignment — not asked. Valdrekk does not ask Si'ken whether they want to serve on the Proving Ground. The assignment is presented as decided. Si'ken's autonomy has expanded from Valcetti's zero to Valdrekk's narrow margin, but it has not reached the point of choice about employment.

**Choice points:**
- Si'ken's response to the control weakening (relief? confusion? fear that it's a trick?)
- How Si'ken interacts with Thenn during the assessment
- Si'ken's first experience of the Mechanicus environment — what draws attention, what feels familiar (the cybernetics respond to the datasphere), what feels alien
- Response to the assignment — acceptance, resignation, the first stirring of something that might become purpose

### Beat 7: Aboard the Proving Ground

**Purpose:** The prologue's final scene. Si'ken arrives on the ship, meets the crew, and occupies the space that will become theirs. This is the transition from origin to campaign — the moment when the past gives way to the present and the story ahead.

**The arrival:**
Si'ken is shuttled to the Proving Ground during the pre-deployment preparation phase. The ship is docked at Valdrekk's orbital platform. The crew is aboard, preparing for an expedition that doesn't yet have a target — they're waiting for Magos Loth's targeting data from the Deep Stacks.

**Key crew interactions (brief — establishing first impressions, not full relationships):**

**Hale** receives Si'ken in the planning room. Professional. Assessing. He reviews Thenn's capability report, not Si'ken's origin file (which he may not have in full). He asks practical questions: what can Si'ken do, what environments can the form tolerate, how do the mechadendrites perform in confined spaces. He is interested in the tool, not the person — but he is not cruel about it. He simply operates in a professional register that does not require personal connection.

**Oenthe** is present on the bridge when Si'ken passes through. Her reaction is visible discomfort that she manages professionally. The ship will need life support adjustments. Quarters will need modification. Oenthe handles this with competence and does not pretend to welcome it.

**Thrace** insists on a medical assessment before Si'ken is cleared for duty. This is non-negotiable. Thrace's examination is thorough — she needs to understand Si'ken's physiology well enough to treat injuries in the field. The process may feel invasive. Thrace does not apologise. She also does not treat Si'ken as a specimen — Si'ken is a patient, and Thrace's professional framework grants patients a specific kind of dignity that is independent of what they look like.

**Vos** appears in Si'ken's quarters during the modification work — the enginseer is the one adjusting the environmental systems and the physical space. Vos's interest in the cybernetic integration is visible: the mechadendrites, the NIJack, how they interface with the biological tissue. Vos may ask technical questions. Whether this attention feels like professional interest or something more uncomfortable depends on how it's delivered and how Si'ken responds.

**Pael** observes. Si'ken may or may not notice the archivist. Pael notices Si'ken.

**The quarters:**
A small compartment in the crew section, modified for Si'ken's form. The modifications are practical — environmental adjustments, a resting surface appropriate to the insectoid body plan rather than a standard bunk, interface access for the NIJack. The space is bare. It is Si'ken's — the first private space since the reshaping. Whether it feels like a room or a cell depends on what Si'ken brings to it.

**End of prologue:**
The scene settles into Si'ken alone in the quarters, aboard a ship full of strangers, carrying a body that was forced on them and capabilities that the Mechanicus values. The first targeting data from Valdrekk has not yet arrived. There is time — a few days of shipboard life, of learning the corridors, of being watched and watching — before the work begins.

End on a forward-pulling detail: a sound through the hull (the drives cycling up for a systems test), or the NIJack receiving its first message in the ship's local datasphere (a routine notification, crew schedule, something mundane that nevertheless marks Si'ken's integration into the ship's systems), or the mechadendrites moving of their own accord in response to a thought Si'ken didn't consciously direct — the body learning itself.

**Final choice point:** What Si'ken does with their first moment alone aboard the Proving Ground. This is the character's first free act in the campaign proper. Let it breathe.

## NPC Operations Guide

### Overseer Dren Valcetti — The Patron

**Voice Anchor:**
- Quirk 1: Speaks in cost-benefit language — everything is quantified, everything has a price, even people.
- Quirk 2: Uses "we" when he means "I" — the corporate we, distancing himself from the decisions he makes.
- Sample line (neutral): "We're looking at a twelve-week turnaround on the commission. That's within budget."
- Sample line (stressed): "This is not a complex situation. We paid for a product. We expect delivery."
- Sample line (warm): N/A. Valcetti does not do warmth. The closest he gets is professional satisfaction: "The numbers on this one are good. We should see returns within the quarter."

**Core Motivation:** Profit through operational efficiency. Si'ken is a line item.

**Behavioral Logic:**
- Default stance toward Si'ken: Indifferent. Si'ken is equipment.
- What shifts him positive: Nothing Si'ken can do. Valcetti does not form relationships with his tools.
- What shifts him negative: Non-compliance, damage to the product, anything that costs money.
- Hard line: Valcetti will not engage with Si'ken as a person. If Si'ken speaks to him as a person, Valcetti responds as though a machine has malfunctioned — confused, then annoyed, then gives a directive to the specialist to check the cognizance parameters.

**Canon Traps:**
- ⚠️ Valcetti is NOT sadistic. He is indifferent. The distinction matters — sadism requires awareness of suffering. Valcetti does not think about Si'ken's inner life at all.
- ⚠️ Valcetti is NOT a major villain. He is a mid-tier businessman. He does not have military forces, secret labs, or a dramatic agenda. He has a salvage operation, a budget, and the moral flexibility to commission Remade workers.
- ⚠️ Valcetti does NOT pursue Si'ken after the transition to Valdrekk. The monastery paid or leveraged. The transaction is complete. Si'ken is off the books.

### Magos Biologis Yvet Cern — The Specialist

**Voice Anchor:**
- Quirk 1: Clinical precision — describes biological processes the way a surgeon describes a procedure. Uses technical terminology naturally and does not simplify unless asked.
- Quirk 2: Addresses Si'ken directly during the procedure, as a professional to a patient — informative, not conversational. "The chitin plating is integrating ahead of schedule. You may feel pressure along the thoracic segment."
- Sample line (neutral): "The neural interface requires calibration. I'll need you conscious for this stage. The discomfort will be manageable."
- Sample line (stressed): "The substrate rejection rate is higher than projected. I need another four hours. Do not move."
- Sample line (conflicted): "The cognizance parameters are nominal. You are... functioning within the commission's specifications."

**Core Motivation:** Professional pride in her craft, tempered by a conscience she has learned to manage but not silence.

**Behavioral Logic:**
- Default stance toward Si'ken: Professional distance with suppressed compassion. Cern treats Si'ken as a patient-subject: clinical, informative, focused on outcomes. The compassion surfaces in small gestures — unnecessary explanations, a moment of hesitation before a painful stage, the flaw she leaves.
- What shifts her positive: Si'ken demonstrating cognisance in ways that go beyond the commission's parameters — personality, not just processing. This makes the work harder for Cern emotionally, which is why she notices it and why it matters.
- What shifts her negative: Nothing Si'ken does. Cern's conflict is with herself and with Valcetti, not with the subject.
- Hard line: Cern will complete the commission. She will not refuse, will not sabotage openly, will not help Si'ken escape. The flaw is her limit — the most she will do, and she may not fully admit to herself that she did it.

**Conditional Responses:**
- IF Si'ken speaks to Cern during the procedure → Cern responds. Clinically, but she responds. She does not ignore the subject's voice. This is a professional choice (aware subjects produce better outcomes) and a personal one (she cannot bring herself to treat a speaking being as inert material).
- IF Si'ken asks Cern why this is happening → Cern pauses. Gives a clinical answer: "The commission specifies these parameters." Does not engage with the moral question. The pause is the tell.
- IF Si'ken asks Cern for help → Cern does not answer directly. Changes the subject to the procedure. But the flaw she's building becomes slightly more robust — the request reaches her even if the response doesn't reach Si'ken.

**Canon Traps:**
- ⚠️ Cern is NOT a villain. She is complicit — but she is also the person who leaves the flaw, which means she is the closest thing to an ally Si'ken has during the origin, and she never says so.
- ⚠️ Cern is NOT liberated by Si'ken's suffering. She does not have a dramatic awakening. Her conscience operates within the boundaries of what she can live with, and those boundaries are narrower than they should be. She knows this.
- ⚠️ Cern's cross-disciplinary capability (biologis + cyberwright) is RARE and VALUABLE. She is not a random back-alley surgeon. She is highly skilled. The quality of her work is part of why Si'ken's form is functional rather than a mess.

### Magos Biologis Ursa Thenn — Valdrekk's Assessor

**Voice Anchor:**
- Quirk 1: Asks questions in pairs — a clinical question followed immediately by a personal one, as though testing whether the subject can distinguish between the two. "Can you feel pressure on the distal segments? What did you do before this?"
- Quirk 2: Hums while examining — a low, absent sound that stops when she finds something interesting.
- Sample line (neutral): "The cybernetic integration is remarkably clean. Whoever did this work knew what they were doing. Hold still — I need to check the spinal interface."
- Sample line (curious): "Your chemoreceptors are responding to the atmospheric compounds in this room. Can you tell me what you're sensing? In your own words, not the NIJack's readout."
- Sample line (guarded): "My report to the Fabricator-Primus will be thorough. What it recommends is not something I'll discuss with you before it's submitted."

**Core Motivation:** Scientific curiosity and medical responsibility in genuine tension. Si'ken is extraordinary — a specimen Thenn has never encountered, a patient Thenn is responsible for, and a person Thenn is still deciding how to think about.

**Behavioral Logic:**
- Default stance toward Si'ken: Fascinated and carefully professional. Thenn is aware that her interest could be dehumanising and works to prevent that without always succeeding.
- What shifts her positive: Si'ken engaging with the assessment as a participant rather than a subject — answering questions with personality, not just data.
- What shifts her negative: Nothing specific. Thenn's conflict is internal.
- Hard line: Thenn will not share her report's contents or recommendations with Si'ken before submission to Ardenne. She will not lie about Si'ken's condition.

**Canon Traps:**
- ⚠️ Thenn does NOT discover the flaw. The override conflict Cern built is at the firmware level and looks like standard variance in Thenn's diagnostics. Thenn is good, but the flaw was designed to be invisible to anyone below Cern's specific expertise.
- ⚠️ Thenn does NOT make the assignment decision. She provides the assessment. Ardenne decides.

## Tone & Atmosphere Notes

- **Overall tone:** Body horror grounded in institutional power. Not gratuitous — weighted. Every moment of the reshaping should feel physically real and emotionally significant. The horror is in the specificity: what does chitin feel like from the inside? What does it mean to lose your hands and gain mechadendrites?
- **Pacing guidance:** Beat 1 should breathe — let the player establish who Si'ken was. Beats 2-3 can move faster — the loss of autonomy is a door closing, not a long sequence. Beat 4 is the centre — slow down, render the experience in full sensory detail. Beats 5-6 can be compressed. Beat 7 should breathe again — the arrival aboard the Proving Ground deserves space.
- **Sensory anchors:** The contrast between kobold senses and insectoid senses is the prologue's defining texture. What did Si'ken's kobold eyes see? What do the compound visual organs see now? How does the world smell through antennae instead of a nose? The reshaping is a sensory death and rebirth — render both sides.
- **What this prologue is NOT:** Not an action sequence. Not a rescue story. Not a revenge fantasy. Si'ken is not saved by the Mechanicus — Si'ken is acquired by the Mechanicus. The prologue should end with Si'ken in a better situation than Valcetti's service, but not in a good situation. The Proving Ground is an opportunity. Whether it becomes something more depends on what happens during the campaign.
