# Si'ken — Prologue Session Summary

**Campaign:** Archaeos Expedition
**Session Type:** Origin Prologue (pre-campaign)
**PC:** Si'ken — Remade scout, formerly kobold
**In-Game Period:** Pre-campaign — days immediately preceding Proving Ground assignment
**Played:** 2026-04-13

---

## Where We Started

Si'ken is a kobold, captured in a corsair raid that destroyed his village. Those not killed were taken. Si'ken hasn't seen his family since before the ships came down. He woke in an enclave holding cell — shackled, chain fixed to a floor ring, two other kobolds for company. He was working the wrist. Not making progress.

He'd been there long enough to know the cell's rhythms. Not long enough to stop being someone.

---

## The Specialist

A specialist appeared at the cell grate — lean, sharp-featured, data-monocle seated over one eye. They ran a scanner. Found what they were looking for. Si'ken stared back when they looked at him. That was apparently the deciding factor.

He was separated from the others and taken upstairs. The specialist explained, with the flat honesty that would characterise everything they said: Si'ken was not going to a labour form. A patron had commissioned a specific requirement. The process would be extensive. He would not look the way he looked now. His mind would still be his — that was part of the specification. The patron needed something that could think.

Si'ken asked what he was supposed to get out of it.

*Survival,* the specialist said. *And a body built to do something difficult and specific.*

He gave them his name. They noted it — not in a pad, internally — and used it from that moment on.

Then they put him on the table.

---

## The Reshaping

Si'ken was sedated. He didn't count.

He surfaced to pain first — not all at once, but as information from a nervous system rewired to report from places that shouldn't exist. New surfaces. Wrong joints. Something along the thorax that ached with the deep ache of new growth.

He took stock before he opened his eyes. The way you check a tunnel before you commit your weight to it.

*I'm still here.* The thought was his. The voice it came in was his.

He opened his eyes — primary pair, finding them instinctively — and the specialist was there, tired from long work, watching him. They used his name first. Then they began explaining.

---

## The Inventory — What Si'ken Found

Over the following days Si'ken mapped the new body with methodical patience. In rough order of discovery:

**Morphology:** Insectoid hybrid chassis. Compact, dense. Chitin-analogue exoskeletal plating with iridescent banding. Six legs, abdominal extension where the tail was. Mandibles at the front of the face — involuntary at first, responding to emotion and pain, gradually coming under partial control. The body is significantly smaller than human scale, approximately kobold height, but built differently in every direction.

**Locomotion:** Six legs required relearning. The front pair mapped fastest — closest to the arms-that-were. The brain learned to send intention wide across all six simultaneously rather than committing front-first. Walking became reliable within the first day. Running, climbing, lateral movement, and backwards navigation followed through the calibration period. The body proved faster than expected and significantly quieter in motion than Si'ken anticipated — the legs default to silence without instruction.

**Chitin:** Grips stone and other surfaces via adhesive microstructure — enables vertical climbing on walls and irregular surfaces. Edges along forearm plating are sharp enough to cut fabric cleanly. Dorsal and thoracic plating distributes impact force across a wide surface rather than concentrating it — blunt impacts disperse, the chitin is harder than it looks. A metal rod used to test the distribution dented on contact.

**Visual system:** Primary eyes forward-facing, familiar in function if not in precise character. Secondary eyes positioned upper-lateral — motion detection, peripheral awareness, approximately 320-degree combined visual field. The secondaries don't focus but they register movement and map space at the edges of awareness. Si'ken learned to use them as background rather than foreground input, letting the brain sort the hierarchy without interference.

**Olfactory:** Significantly enhanced. Loud at first — the antiseptic of the white room arriving in layers, each component distinguishable. Develops a threat-assessment function over time: certain biological compounds (fear response, predator markers, specific toxins) register differently from background smell. Si'ken began detecting insect presence by scent before visual confirmation within the first yard session.

**Hunting suite:** Built-in, not consciously accessible. Comprises prey location (olfactory + interface tendril combined), movement suppression (the legs default to silence in hunting configuration without instruction), and strike reflex (the manipulator tendril struck and secured a beetle before the conscious decision fully formed). Si'ken found this concerning in a specific way — he wanted to know when he was deciding and when something else was. Calibration addressed this.

**Mechadendrites:** Two, integrated into thoracic structure during the reshaping itself rather than retrofitted after.
- *Manipulator:* Fine-work, tight-space, precision tool. Haptic resolution approximately four times that of the forearm limbs — reads surface history, material composition, mechanical tolerances. Si'ken used it to trace the crack in the table, identify a fossil in a yard stone, assess insect prey, and open a complex mechanical puzzle in eleven minutes.
- *Interface:* Direct connection to machine systems and Mechanicus architecture. Designed for contact interface. In practice Si'ken discovered it has passive ambient capability — he can sense nearby machine spirits and mechanical systems without physical contact, reading them as a kind of peripheral awareness analogous to the secondary eyes. The specialist noted this as early and significant. It was recorded in the formal assessment as *acceptable variance.*

**NIJack:** Neural interface jack, direct connection to compatible Mechanicus ports. Faster and more complete than the tendril interface — the connected system arrives in awareness like entering a large room. Si'ken learned to maintain a partition during calibration: NIJack running in background while the body navigates and the tendrils work simultaneously. Took three attempts before it stopped feeling like drowning.

**Ambient machine sense:** The interface tendril's passive capability extends to a general environmental awareness of mechanical systems in proximity. By the end of the calibration period this was running constantly, a seventh sense as natural as hearing — the room's machines sorted and familiar, each one distinct, the city's infrastructure arriving as a vast conversation when Si'ken left the enclave. He corrected a climate system that had been running the wrong task for decades because it was bothering him.

## The Specialist — What Was Left Out of the Report

The specialist kept Si'ken's name from the first moment he gave it. Used it consistently. Built the name into the assessment framework as something that mattered.

They told Si'ken about the second subject — aquatic morphology, preserved cognition, stopped speaking voluntarily after the first week. Responded when addressed. Followed instruction. Stopped initiating. The specialist believed this happened because the mind was intact and had nothing to reach toward.

They wrote *acceptable variance* for the ambient machine sensitivity — accurate enough to survive scrutiny, imprecise enough to leave something unreported. When Si'ken asked directly whether they would tell the patron, the specialist said they were required to report full capability assessment. Which was not yes. Which was also not no.

On the last evening before the patron's people arrived, the specialist said: *The name matters. Keeping it matters. A mind intact inside a new body needs something to anchor to.*

They said they would remember Si'ken's name after handoff.

They built well. The gap between *to specification* and *well* is where Si'ken actually lives.

---

## The Patron's People — Assessor Vane

Three people arrived: Assessor Vane (representative of patron/Valdrekk interests) and two security personnel. Vane addressed the specialist first, then redirected to Si'ken directly after Si'ken spoke unprompted.

Si'ken told Vane about the ambient machine sensitivity that the file described as acceptable variance. *I'd rather Valdrekk knows what I am accurately than discover it incrementally.* Vane noted it for appending. Something shifted in their assessment — the small variety of respect.

The terms of the Valdrekk arrangement: specialist asset designation (not labour property), quarters, defined function, non-expendable status under Mechanicus institutional framework. Assignment to Forge-Monastery Valdrekk, operational posting to the Proving Ground, scout and machine-interface function.

Si'ken read the document twice, including with the manipulator tendril. Signed it.

*Things should do what they're for,* he told Vane, when asked what the file didn't contain.

Vane said the patron was interested in knowledge. Recovery. Understanding what was lost.

*Then we want the same thing.*

---

## Where We Ended

Si'ken said goodbye to the yard — one breath of outside air through the door — and walked out of the enclave for the first time since arriving as a prisoner in shackles.

The city hit him fully: the vast ambient machine conversation of infrastructure and systems and mechanical life going about itself in enormous indifferent quantities. He processed it and kept walking.

Into the vehicle. Six hours ground transit. Staging facility overnight. Shuttle to orbital dock. The Proving Ground from there.

He has a name. He has something to reach toward. He knows when he's deciding and when something else is.

He's still here.

## Character State — End of Prologue

**Body:** Fully operational. All six legs reliable including left third. Climbing, running, silent movement, hunting configuration all functional. Chitin properties understood.

**Eyes:** Primary and secondary both active and integrated. ~320-degree combined field.

**Tendrils:** Both functional. Manipulator — precision tool, haptic resolution confirmed. Interface — contact and ambient capability, ambient sensitivity beyond design specification.

**NIJack:** Functional. Partition management under load — achieved by end of calibration.

**Hunting suite:** Active, instinct-level. Si'ken understands it's there and when it engages.

**Ambient machine sense:** Running constantly. City-scale environmental awareness confirmed on departure.

**Skill tree notes:** No formal trees opened yet — prologue is origin, not operation. Body (locomotion, climbing, physical capability), Edge (hunting suite, strike reflex, silent movement), and Essence (machine sense, ambient awareness, the thing the specialist recorded as acceptable variance) all have meaningful development to recognise when formal tree system is applied. First session aboard the Proving Ground is the appropriate moment to establish the starting tree state.

**Unresolved threads:**
- Family — alive or not unknown, location unknown
- The second subject — aquatic form, somewhere, stopped speaking
- The specialist — will remember the name. Built something that exceeded specification and partially concealed it. That relationship is closed but its weight carries
- What the patron specifically needed the form for — Vane described it as site assessment in pre-collapse ruins, knowledge recovery. The deeper specifics remain ahead
- The ambient sensitivity — Valdrekk now knows it exists. What they do with that knowledge is an open question
