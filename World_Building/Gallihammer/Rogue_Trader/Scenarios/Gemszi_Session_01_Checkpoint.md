## Summary

Gemszi, kobold galley support aboard the *Geld Maw*, took a single protein strip from the ship's dry stores during a quiet end-of-shift moment. Two days later the cook Prast logged a discrepancy and reported it up. Gemszi was collected from the galley by two deck crew and brought to Captain Dresk, who sentenced him to conversion without ceremony — the economic calculation made, the ledger notated, the transaction closed before Gemszi finished speaking. He tried to argue — *it was one strip, the bread wasn't me* — and Dresk did not engage. The sentence stood.

He walked to Vossk's conversion bay on his own feet.

The conversion took place across multiple sedation cycles. He was conscious for the hands — the moment of trying to close fingers that were no longer fingers, feeling something wrong and blunt answer instead. He named his Essence tree *Isu* at that moment, after an uncle his parents told him about who escaped from a ship. Not the escape he would have chosen. The one available.

Vossk completed the procedure: full quadruped rebuild, dairy installation, vocal apparatus restructured, manual dexterity gone. He walked himself to the hold. Learned the gait in the corridor. Found a maintenance panel and looked at his own reflection. Walked circuits through the ship's night cycle. Took care of the body's basic requirements. Mapped the stall infrastructure and understood from the wear marks that the *Geld Maw* runs conversions regularly. Three stalls. Modular. Used.

He is intact. The part that matters is untouched. He is resting.

## GM Notes

He named it Isu. Not after what was done to him — after the possibility of elsewhere. Hold this through the farm arc. Pragmatism and the frame are not armour against grief; they are how grief gets carried. Both things stay true.

The reflection was his. He went and looked on his own terms while the ship slept. The eyes in the panel are the thread that reaches the Haldrens eventually. Don't force it. Let it accumulate.

The dairy becomes routine. The horror is in the normalisation. Maren's practicalness will be its own discomfort when recognition comes.

Edric talks without filtering. The intimacy of being understood without consent — even kindly meant — is its own thing.

Do not sentimentalise the Haldrens. The farm still needs a draught animal after recognition. That does not resolve.
