## Summary

The *Geld Maw* entered Erdfall orbit and the hold crew loaded Gemszi onto a shuttle with the cargo. He descended watching the planet's light come through the viewport — green through a break in the clouds, the first real colour that wasn't ship grey. At the landing field, Oskar Haldren completed the purchase without ceremony, handed the lead line to Edric, and walked them north along a dirt track to the farm. Gemszi sampled grass on the way, read Oskar's voice and posture, and had his first extended encounter with open sky — managed, with difficulty, by keeping his attention horizontal. At the farm Maren read the dairy pressure immediately, led him to the outbuilding, and the first cycle was completed. He spent the evening in the barn examining stall mechanisms, eating grain beside Wender, and settling into the straw for the first night. The east field waits tomorrow.

## GM Notes

The accounting scene in the evening — food better, air real, barn large, no half-rations — is the scenario's central discomfort rendered quietly. He is not making it mean more than it means. He is not making it mean less. Both things simultaneously. This is what Isu looks like in practice: the frame is not armour, it is how grief gets carried while you are also noticing that the grain is good.

Maren is ahead of Oskar on this and will continue to be. She works from the body outward — she read the dairy pressure in the yard, she prepared the straw before they arrived, she looked at his face last and held it longest. When recognition comes for Oskar it will be slower and more deliberate. When it comes for Maren it will be a thing she has already been sitting with for some time.

Edric is fifteen and lonely in the specific way of children on frontier worlds with limited stimulation and no peers. He will keep talking to Gemszi. He will keep not finishing the question. Watch for the moment he decides the question is safe to finish.

The east field is not only ploughing. It is the first time the body does what it was built to do at full capacity, with harness and yoke and someone directing it. That scene needs room. Don't rush past it.

The dairy normalising is slow and should be slow. Don't mark it until it's genuinely unremarkable in the narration — when it stops generating interiority and becomes simply part of the day. That shift is the horror. Let it take sessions.
