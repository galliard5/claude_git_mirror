# Gemszi — Session 01: Product

**Campaign:** Gemszi — Livestock Conversion
**Setting:** Aboard the *Geld Maw*, in transit
**In-Game Date:** TBD
**PC:** Gemszi (kobold, galley support)
**Session Arc:** Person → product. Opens in the galley, ends in the hold.

---

## Scene 1: The Galley

**Purpose:** Establish who Gemszi is before everything changes. The player needs to inhabit his smallness, his hunger, his competence within a system that doesn't value him.

**Setting:** The *Geld Maw's* galley — a working ship kitchen. Not filthy but not clean. Grease on surfaces, heat from the cookers, the smell of protein rations being stretched. Late in the shift cycle — most of the crew has eaten. Gemszi is cleaning.

**Sensory anchors:**
- The sound of his claws on the deck plating — light, quick, the footfall of something small moving fast
- The heat from the cookers against scales
- The smell of food he didn't get enough of
- His hands — busy, dexterous, useful. Scrubbing a tray, stacking containers, moving with the efficiency of someone who has learned exactly how much work keeps him invisible

**Key details to surface:**
- Gemszi's position in the hierarchy. He eats last, gets least, occupies the margin. The galley crew — human, larger — don't actively abuse him but don't see him as a person either. He is a function: the thing that cleans.
- His hunger. Not dramatic starvation — the persistent, grinding hunger of someone whose rations are technically adequate and practically short. He's been hungry for weeks. Maybe months. It's background noise that never quite goes silent.
- His hands. Show them working. The dexterity, the precision, the way kobold fingers handle tools and surfaces. This is what he loses. The player needs to feel them before they're gone.

**Beat:** The galley empties. The shift ends. Gemszi is alone with the stores. The food is right there.

**Choice Point:**

> The galley is empty. The shift lock won't cycle for another twenty minutes. The dry stores — protein strips, pressed grain, the hard bread that keeps — are behind a latch that doesn't lock properly because nobody has fixed it because nobody thinks the galley kobold would dare.
>
> Gemszi's stomach has been talking to him for two hours.

1. **Take what he needs.** A partial loaf and some protein strips. Enough to fill the gap. Eat some now, hide some for later.
2. **Take only what he can eat right now.** Nothing to find on him if someone checks. Swallow the evidence.
3. **Take more than he needs.** If he's going to risk it, make it count. Enough to last a few days.
4. **Don't do it.** Walk away. The hunger is survivable. The risk isn't worth it.

**GM notes on the choice:** Options 1–3 all lead to getting caught — the how and when varies, but the outcome is fixed. This is not a choice about whether the story happens. It is a choice about who Gemszi is. Option 1 is pragmatic. Option 2 is cautious. Option 3 is desperate or defiant. Option 4 delays — he goes hungry tonight, but the hunger doesn't go away, and the opportunity comes again. If the player consistently chooses 4, the world pushes: the hunger gets worse, an opportunity becomes harder to refuse, or someone else steals and Gemszi gets blamed anyway. The conversion happens. How Gemszi arrives at it is the player's.

**If he takes the food — getting caught:** This can be immediate (a crewman walks in) or delayed (the shortage is noticed next shift, inventory check traces it to Gemszi's watch). Immediate is more dramatic. Delayed lets the player have a scene of eating, of the brief relief, before the knock comes. Follow the player's energy — if they chose option 2 (eat immediately, no evidence), the delayed discovery is better because it lets them think they got away with it.

## Scene 2: The Sentence

**Purpose:** The moment Gemszi understands what is going to happen to him. This scene is about the gap between the crime and the punishment — the grotesque disproportion that tells him everything about what he is worth on this ship.

**Setting:** Depends on ship culture. Options: dragged to the captain's cabin, brought to a corridor meeting with Dresk and a couple of enforcers, or simply told in the brig after being detained. The setting should feel institutional, not theatrical — this is not a trial. It's a disposition.

**NPC: Captain Dresk**

*Voice:* Flat. Transactional. Short sentences. Doesn't raise his voice because he doesn't need to. Speaks about Gemszi in the third person even when Gemszi is standing in front of him — "the kobold," "the galley asset." Not cruel. Just doing math.

*Speech quirks:*
1. Refers to crew by function, not name — "the kobold," "galley support," "the cargo"
2. Ends statements with a pause that isn't an invitation to respond — the silence is a period, not an opening

*Sample:*
(Doesn't look up from whatever he's working on.)
"Galley support took from stores. Protein and bread. That's a write-down."
(Pause. The pause is not for Gemszi.)
"Conversion value's higher than replacement cost. Prep him for Vossk."

**Key beats:**
- Gemszi may not understand immediately. "Conversion" may be a word he's heard but not connected to himself. The understanding may arrive in stages — hearing the word, seeing the enforcer's reaction (or lack of reaction), being taken not to the brig but *down*, toward the medical bay, toward Vossk's space.
- If Gemszi resists, protests, begs — this is valid player input and should be honoured. Dresk does not engage. The enforcers are not rough unless Gemszi makes them be. The machine processes him. His words land on nothing.
- The last moment of being treated as a person — even a low-status person — is somewhere in this scene. After this, he is material.

**Choice Point:**

> The enforcer's hand is on Gemszi's shoulder. The corridor to the lower decks is ahead. He knows what's down there. Everyone aboard knows what's down there.

1. **Go quiet.** Don't give them anything. Walk.
2. **Beg.** Appeal to Dresk, the enforcer, anyone. Make them hear that this is a person they're doing this to.
3. **Fight.** It won't work. He's eighteen kilos against armoured crew. But the body wants to fight.
4. **Ask a question.** Something specific — what will happen, how long it takes, whether it hurts. Information as a survival reflex.

**GM notes:** None of these change the outcome. All of them define who Gemszi is going into the conversion bay. Option 1 is control. Option 2 is humanity — the attempt to be seen, which fails, which matters because it fails. Option 3 is instinct — brief, physical, ended quickly and adding bruises to what follows. Option 4 is the pragmatism from his character sheet — even now, he's calculating, trying to understand what's coming. Vossk might actually answer a technical question, which is its own kind of horror.

## Scene 3: The Conversion

**Purpose:** The central set-piece. This is not a medical procedural — it's the experience of being unmade and remade from the inside. Key moments, not a step-by-step log. The player should feel specific losses and specific wrongnesses, not a catalogue of procedures.

**Setting:** Vossk's conversion bay. Below the medical suite. The space functions as workshop and operating theatre simultaneously — surgical tools alongside tanks of biomantic compounds, specimen jars, the smell the Biomancer doc describes as *wrong in a way they can't quite name*. The lighting is functional. The table is sized for larger subjects than Gemszi — he is small on it.

**NPC: Vossk (biomancer)**

*Voice:* Clinical. Efficient. Occasionally explains what he's doing — not to Gemszi, to himself, the way a craftsman narrates their work. Uses precise terminology without affect. Does not acknowledge Gemszi's distress as relevant data.

*Speech quirks:*
1. Talks in the passive voice about the subject — "the tissue is responding," "resistance in the thoracic cluster," never "you" or "he"
2. Makes small satisfied sounds — a quiet "mm" or "there" — when something goes right technically, regardless of what the subject is experiencing

*Sample:*
(Adjusts something Gemszi can't see. The smell of compounds, sharp and organic.)
"Skeletal elongation is tracking. Good calcium uptake."
(Pause. A quiet "mm." Something changes in the pressure against Gemszi's spine.)
"Forelimb conversion next. Tissue needs another hour to set before we start on the extremities."

**Structure:** The conversion plays out in fragments — Gemszi is conscious for some, sedated for others, and the boundaries between aren't always clean. The scene should move through key moments rather than continuous time:

### Fragment 1: The Table

Gemszi is restrained. Vossk is preparing compounds. The bay smells wrong. This is the last moment of the old body — intact, whole, his. The compounds go in. Warmth, then heat, then something deeper than heat — the feeling of tissue responding to something that is not natural. Gemszi's hands are still his hands. His body is still his body. Not for much longer.

**Sensory focus:** The restraints against scale. The coldness of the table through the body heat. Vossk's hands — large, precise, indifferent. The compounds entering the bloodstream and the first moment something inside begins to *change*.

### Fragment 2: The Hands

This is the scene. The moment the scenario turns on.

Gemszi surfaces from sedation — partial, foggy, enough to feel. His forelimbs are wrong. The fingers are wrong. He tries to move them and the signals come back different — heavy, blunt, the fine motor control gone. He tries to flex and something that is no longer a hand responds with something that is no longer a grip. The dexterity — the thing that made him useful, the thing that let him work, the thing that kobolds *are* — is being consumed. Replaced by mass, by structure, by weight-bearing architecture that has no use for precision.

He cannot stop it. He is awake enough to feel it happening and cannot stop it.

**Sensory focus:** The feeling of trying to close fingers that are no longer fingers. The weight at the end of his arms — heavy, dense, wrong. The phantom precision — his brain sending commands that no longer have a destination. Vossk's voice somewhere above, narrating a successful restructuring.

**No choice point here.** This is not a moment of agency. This is a moment of experience. The player witnesses. The scene holds.

### Fragment 3: The Mass

Time has passed. Gemszi surfaces again and the body is enormous. Not his — a structure being built around the seed of him. He can feel the growth — bone extending, muscle layering, organs scaling into cavities that didn't exist yesterday. He is heavy in a way that has no reference point. The table is too small now, or he is too large. His centre of gravity is somewhere he has never felt it.

The tail — thicker, rebalanced. His spine — longer, curved differently, configured for horizontal carriage. His skull — heavier, the jaw deep, the teeth wrong.

**Sensory focus:** Weight. The unfamiliar mass of the body pressing into the table. The strangeness of breathing with lungs that are the wrong size. The sound of his own heartbeat — slower, deeper, audible in a way it never was.

### Fragment 4: The Voice

Gemszi tries to speak. He has been trying, perhaps, for a while — the desire to say something, anything, to produce words that assert the existence of the person inside. His mouth opens. His tongue moves. What comes out is a low, animal sound — a grunt, a half-vocalisation, something from the chest and throat of a creature that does not speak. The larynx is rebuilt. The oral cavity is wrong. The fine motor control that speech requires is gone the same way the fingers are gone.

He tries again. The same sound. Different pitch, same emptiness.

**Sensory focus:** The vibration in the chest — deep, resonant, nothing like a kobold's voice. The tongue — thick, heavy, configured for feeding. The moment of trying to form a word and feeling the apparatus fail, not from damage but from design.

### Fragment 5: The Dairy

The last modification. Gemszi is conscious — Vossk needs the endocrine system responsive for calibration. The glandular installation is not painful exactly — the compounds suppress acute pain — but it is *present*. Tissue developing where tissue should not be. Weight accumulating along the lower abdomen. A system being wired into his hormonal cycle that has no precedent in his biology, no reference point, no context.

Vossk checks function. The clinical touch of a practitioner assessing output potential. The body responds because the body was built to respond. Gemszi's mind has no framework for what is happening to him.

**Sensory focus:** The unfamiliarity. Not pain but *wrongness* — the feeling of a body doing something the mind did not ask for and does not understand. Vossk's satisfied "mm."

**GM notes on the conversion:** The fragments don't need to be equal length. The hands scene is the longest — it deserves the most room. The mass and voice fragments can be shorter. The dairy fragment should be handled with weight — not spectacle, not clinical detachment, but the honest rendering of what it feels like from inside. Do not rush it. Do not linger gratuitously. Let the moment land and move on.

## Scene 4: Standing

**Purpose:** Transition from the table to the hold. Gemszi must learn to exist in the new body — the first time he stands, walks, moves as what he now is.

**Setting:** Still in the conversion bay, then moved to the cargo hold. Vossk monitors the initial motor adaptation. The bay is too small for the body now — another detail that marks the scale of the change. The same room that contained a kobold now barely contains what the kobold became.

**Key beats:**
- The first attempt to stand. Four legs. The brain sends bipedal signals and gets quadruped feedback. He stumbles, catches himself on limbs that feel like someone else's. The motor cortex begins remapping — not instantly, not comfortably, but it starts.
- The first steps. Heavy. The sound of his weight on the deck plating — not the light click of kobold claws but a deep, solid impact. The sound of livestock.
- Walking to the hold under his own power. Through corridors he used to clean. Past crewmates who see an animal being moved, not a colleague who disappeared.
- The hold. A stall. A trough. Straw on the deck. The partition is chest-height on the new body — he could look over it if he raised his head. The water is in a basin. The food is in a trough. He is expected to eat from it.

**Choice Point:**

> The stall. Straw, trough, water basin. The hold is dim and smells of cargo — oil, metal, the organic sweetness of feed stores. Vossk left after checking his vitals. The partition clicks shut. It's not locked. It doesn't need to be. Where would he go?
>
> The trough has feed in it — grain and compressed nutrient pellets. His stomach is empty. The body is hungry in a way the old body never was. The new metabolism demands input.

1. **Eat.** He's hungry. The body needs fuel. Lower his head to the trough and eat what's in it, the way the body is built to eat.
2. **Don't eat.** Not yet. Not like this. Stand in the stall and refuse to do the thing the body was designed to do, for as long as that refusal holds.
3. **Explore.** The partition isn't locked. Test the body — move through the hold, learn what the legs do, find the boundaries of the space. Don't eat yet.
4. **Try to make a sound.** Not speech — he knows that's gone. But something. A sound that is his, not the body's. Something that comes from Gemszi and not from the product.

**GM notes:** This is the session's final choice point and it's the most revealing. Option 1 is pragmatism winning — the body has needs and Gemszi serves them, which is the beginning of the accommodation pattern that defines his character arc. Option 2 is defiance, fragile and temporary but *present* — the refusal to perform the function, even briefly, even at cost. Option 3 is curiosity and control-seeking — learning the cage before accepting it. Option 4 is the attempt to assert identity through the only channel left.

Whatever the player chooses, the scene settles into the hold at night. Alone. The hum of the ship's drives. The body breathing — slow, deep, the massive lungs cycling air that smells like cargo. The mind that remembers the galley, the hands, the bread.

---

## Session End

The session ends in the hold. Not on a dramatic beat — on a quiet one. The body is settling. The mind is not.

**Closing image:** Gemszi in the stall. The hold is dark except for the running lights along the deck. The ship is in transit. Somewhere ahead, there is a planet he has never heard of, and a farmer who needs a draught animal, and a life that has no category for what he is. He doesn't know any of this yet. He knows the straw under his body and the weight of what he has become and the silence where his voice used to be.
