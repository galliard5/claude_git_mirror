# Looting Rules — Item Survivability

## Core Principle

Any item exposed to damaging conditions during combat — even if not directly targeted — has a chance of being broken or destroyed when recovered. Survival is not guaranteed simply because the item's owner survived. The chaos of combat, collateral force, heat, impact, and energy transfer all take their toll on equipment.

---

## Damage Categories

### AoE and Heavy Weapons — Highest Risk
Area of effect attacks and heavy weapons present the greatest threat to lootable items. AoE attacks **automatically hit all items** in the affected area regardless of whether any individual item was the intended target. Heavy weapons carry the highest chance of item destruction on direct hits.

### Standard Attacks — Moderate Risk
Most standard attacks — ranged or melee — carry *some* chance of destroying items other than armour, even when the attack is not specifically targeting equipment. Only the most subtle, precise, or well-targeted attacks carry a negligible item damage chance.

### Exceptions — Clean Hits to Unarmoured Locations
A clean shot to an unarmoured location may leave armour intact but does not guarantee other carried items survive the encounter.

---

## Item-Specific Rules

| Item Type | Primary Risk Condition | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Melee weapons | Melee combat | Exposure to impact, parrying, and direct force |
| Armour | Any hit to armoured location | Exceptions for clean shots to unarmoured locations only |
| Ranged weapons | Direct hits, AoE | Energy and heat damage particularly destructive |
| Carried gear | AoE automatic; others by chance | Pouches, packs, and worn items all at risk |
| Electronics/tech | Energy weapons, AoE | Higher fragility — GM may apply increased failure chance |

---

## GM Guidance

**Roll when:** Items are recovered from a defeated enemy who took significant damage, particularly from AoE, heavy weapons, or sustained fire.

**Roll less or skip when:** The enemy was taken down cleanly with minimal collateral — a single precise shot, a subtle takedown, a surrender.

**Severity scales with damage type:**
- Subtle/precise attack: Minimal or no item check
- Standard ranged or melee: Item check, low-moderate failure chance
- Heavy weapons: Item check, elevated failure chance
- AoE: Automatic check on all items, elevated failure chance

The goal is to make combat loot feel earned and uncertain — players who want to recover intact equipment have an incentive to think about *how* they bring down a target, not just *whether* they bring it down.

---

## Notes for Rogue Trader

Pre-collapse imperial equipment warrants special consideration — it is irreplaceable. GMs may apply harsher survivability penalties to ancient gear on the grounds that it cannot be repaired if damaged, or conversely rule that imperial-grade materials are tougher than modern equivalents and apply bonuses to survivability rolls. Either approach is valid; establish the table's preference early.

Ship salvage operations in graveyards follow the same principle at scale — the method of clearing a compartment directly affects what survives in it.
