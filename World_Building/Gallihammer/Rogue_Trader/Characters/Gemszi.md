# Gemszi
### Galley Support, Geld Maw. Then Livestock, Erdfall. The Person Inside Didn't Change.

**Appearance:**

*Pre-conversion:* Small even for a kobold — wiry, underfed, quick-moving. Dull brown-grey scales with lighter patches on the throat and underbelly. Hands always busy: sorting, scrubbing, carrying. Eyes watchful in the way of someone used to being at the bottom of a hierarchy and navigating it through attention rather than confrontation. Wears galley crew coveralls several sizes too large, sleeves rolled, collar loose. No personal effects worth mentioning. Nothing about him draws a second look, which is how he has survived this long.

*Post-conversion:* A heavy quadruped in the 400–500 kilo range. The form does not map cleanly to any single terrestrial animal — it is biomantic product, built to spec rather than copied from nature. Deep-chested and broad across the shoulders for harness work, with powerful hindquarters built for sustained pulling effort. The legs are thick, weight-bearing columns terminating in broad, semi-hoofed pads — stable on uneven ground, suited to frontier terrain. The back is wide and flat enough to take a yoke without slipping. The head is large-jawed with a heavy brow, eyes set wide for peripheral vision — functional, not decorative. The hide is dense and slightly textured, not furred — tough against weather and abrasion, with a matte quality that varies from slate-grey across the back and flanks to paler tones on the belly. Subdermal plating is visible as slightly raised ridges across the shoulders, spine, and ribcage — not ornamental, just the structural reinforcement showing through.

The dairy installation sits along the lower abdomen — a glandular complex with four teats, functional and proportionate to the body. It was not part of the original biology. It is part of the product.

The tail is thick, muscular, carried low — a counterbalance, not expressive. No horns. No display features. Nothing about the form invites aesthetic attention. It is a working body, designed to work and to last.

What doesn't fit: the eyes. The eyes track with too much intention. They follow conversation. They assess. A farmer might not notice for weeks. A farmer who starts paying attention will not be able to stop noticing.

**Personality:**

**Core Trait:** Survival pragmatism. Gemszi has always operated from the bottom of whatever hierarchy he's in and has always found a way to keep going. Before conversion, this expressed as quiet competence — doing the work, staying out of trouble, reading the room. After conversion, the same instinct drives adaptation: learn the body, learn the routine, endure. He does not collapse into despair. He does not rage. He calculates, adjusts, and persists. This is both his strength and the thing that makes his situation sustainable enough to be permanent.

**Flaw:** He adapts too well. The pragmatism that keeps him functional also allows him to accommodate things that should not be accommodated. The harness becomes tolerable. The milking becomes routine. The stall becomes home. Each accommodation is a small surrender of the framework that says *this is wrong, this was done to me, I am a person*. He is not losing his mind. He is losing the urgency of his own personhood, replaced by the body's rhythms and the farm's demands. The longer it goes on, the harder it becomes to distinguish between acceptance and erasure.

**Background:** Gemszi was born aboard a mid-tier trading vessel — not the *Geld Maw*, an earlier ship — to a small kobold community that occupied the lower-deck margins the way kobold communities often do on ships that tolerate them. He grew up in crawlspaces and service corridors, learning the layout of ships the way ground-born children learn streets. When the community dispersed — the ship changed hands, the new captain was less tolerant — Gemszi moved between vessels, picking up galley work because it was available and kept him fed. Or was supposed to.

He ended up on the *Geld Maw* because it was hiring and he was hungry. He stayed because leaving required resources he didn't have. He occupied the lowest rung of the ship's labour hierarchy: galley support, cleaning, hauling, the tasks that need doing and carry no status. His rations were officially adequate and practically short — the galley crew skimmed from the bottom first, and he was always the bottom.

He stole extra food because he was hungry. A partial loaf and some dried protein strips. On a different ship, this draws a reprimand. On the *Geld Maw*, someone calculated that a galley kobold's conversion value exceeded his labour value, and the decision was made before he finished the bread.

**Race:** Kobold (pre-conversion). Bioservitor — draught/dairy livestock form (post-conversion).

**Status:** Draught animal, Haldren farmstead, Erdfall. Legally: property.

**Age:** Young adult at time of conversion. Exact age unknown — kobold communities on ships track age loosely.

**Skills & Abilities:**

*Retained (cognitive):*
- Full language comprehension — understands spoken language perfectly, including complex instructions, emotional tone, and conversational context
- Spatial reasoning and navigation — reads terrain, predicts obstacles, plans routes through a field
- Mechanical understanding — knows how harness, plough, and farm equipment work, not just how to pull against them
- Social reading — assesses the people around him through observation, understands relationships and moods
- Memory — complete, unimpaired. Remembers being Gemszi. Remembers hands.

*Lost:*
- Speech — vocal apparatus rebuilt for the new form. Can vocalise (lowing, grunting, distress calls) but cannot produce language
- Manual dexterity — no hands. Forelimbs terminate in weight-bearing pads. Cannot manipulate tools, write, gesture with precision
- Bipedal movement — motor cortex has remapped to quadruped locomotion. The old body map has faded.

*Gained:*
- Physical power — can sustain draught work for a full day. The body was built for this.
- Durability — subdermal plating, reinforced bone density, thickened hide. Resistant to impacts, cuts, temperature extremes, rough terrain.
- Endurance — metabolically efficient. Recovers well from sustained physical effort. Hardy under frontier conditions.
- Sensory — adequate vision with good peripheral coverage. Hearing is sharp. Scent is functional but not specialised.

**Independent Goal:** To be recognised. Not rescued — he does not expect rescue. Not freed — he has no framework for what freedom would look like in this body on this world. Recognised. He wants someone to look at him and understand that there is a person inside. What happens after that, he doesn't know. But the alternative — living and dying as an animal that nobody ever knew was a person — is the thing he cannot accept.

**Fears:** That he will stop wanting to be recognised. That the body's rhythms and the farm's routine will eventually overwrite the person, not through damage but through disuse — the way a language fades when nobody speaks it. That one day he will stand in the harness and pull and it will simply be what he is, with nothing left inside that remembers it was supposed to be different.

**Location:** Haldren farmstead, northern plain, Erdfall. Stall in the barn (sleeps). Fields (works). Milking stand (twice daily).

**Voice:**

**Default Conversational Move:** He cannot converse. His communication is entirely behavioural — compliance, hesitation, refusal, the quality of attention in his eyes. When he wants to signal comprehension, the best he can manage is sustained eye contact, a deliberate shift in posture, or a precisely timed response to a verbal instruction that no animal should have understood.

**Quirks:**
- Watches people's hands. Always. The memory of having hands makes other people's hands the most painful and fascinating thing in his visual field.
- When left alone in the barn at night, stands facing the door rather than lying down. This is not restlessness — it is the posture of someone waiting, though he could not say what for.

**Body Language:**

**Head:** Carries it lower than the body's natural posture when working — compliance, endurance, the weight of the yoke. Raises it when something catches his attention, and the quality of the raising — alert, deliberate, focused — is one of the things that eventually registers as wrong to an observant farmer.

**Eyes:** The tell. Everything he cannot say is in the eyes. They track, they assess, they respond to content rather than volume. An animal startles at a shout. Gemszi responds to what the shout means.

**Movement:** Learned and increasingly natural. The quadruped gait is stable and efficient — Vossk built well. But there are residual hesitations in certain movements — turning in tight spaces, backing up, anything that requires spatial awareness the old body handled differently. These fade over time. Whether their fading is adaptation or loss depends on perspective.

**Stress Responses:**

**Stress Tell:** Stillness. Not freezing — deliberate, total stillness. The body stops and the mind is working at full speed behind the eyes. An animal freezes from fear. Gemszi goes still from calculation.

**Instinct:** Assess, then comply. The pragmatism runs deep — resistance without a path to a better outcome is wasted effort. He endures first and processes later.

**Crisis Line:** A low, sustained vocalisation — not a distress call, not a bellow. Something between a groan and a hum, from deep in the chest. It means nothing in the vocabulary of the form. It is the closest thing to speech the body can produce, and he uses it when the pressure exceeds what silence can contain.

**Combat Disposition:**

**Bravery:** Low-moderate. He is not a fighter and never was. The body could do damage — 400+ kilos, reinforced, powerful — but the instinct is evasion, not confrontation. On a frontier world with predators, this becomes relevant: he will avoid rather than stand, flee rather than fight, and only use the body's mass defensively if cornered.

**Loyalty:** To himself. Not selfishly — there is no one else to be loyal to. The Haldrens are his owners. Whether they become something more depends on recognition.

**Breaking Point:** Genuine cruelty. He can endure hard work, discomfort, the daily indignities of being livestock. What he cannot endure is being hurt for someone's pleasure. If a handler — Oskar, Edric, a neighbour, anyone — crosses from practical discipline into cruelty, something breaks in the arrangement. What he does with that break is unpredictable.

**Relationships:**

**Oskar Haldren (owner):** The person who determines Gemszi's daily reality. Oskar is not cruel, and Gemszi recognises this — good feed, maintained harness, adequate shelter. It is the best version of the worst possible situation. Gemszi's feelings toward Oskar are complicated: gratitude that sits wrong because it is gratitude for being well-kept, resentment that has no outlet, and a growing, reluctant trust that is building toward something neither of them has a framework for.

**Edric Haldren:** The most dangerous relationship. Edric talks to Gemszi the way lonely teenagers talk to animals — openly, without filter, assuming he won't be understood. But he is understood, completely, and the intimacy of that — hearing someone's unguarded thoughts without their knowledge or consent — is its own kind of violation, even though Edric means no harm. Edric is also the person most likely to notice that something is wrong, because he pays the kind of attention that adults filter out.

**Maren Haldren:** Practical, somewhat wary of the new animal. Handles the milking. Her relationship to Gemszi is defined by that routine — twice daily, functional, impersonal. If and when recognition comes, Maren is the person who will have to sit with what the milking meant.

**Vossk (biomancer, Geld Maw):** The architect. Not present in Gemszi's life after the sale, but present in his body permanently. Everything Gemszi is now, Vossk built. The anger toward Vossk, when Gemszi allows himself to feel it, is the cleanest emotion he has — uncomplicated, justified, and completely useless.

**Daily Routine:**
- Dawn: Fed (grain and forage in trough). Watered.
- Early morning: Harnessed. Draught work begins — ploughing, hauling, field clearing depending on season.
- Midday: Break. Unharnessed for water and feed. Rests in shade or barn.

- Late afternoon: Milking (Maren or Oskar). The routine takes 15–20 minutes.
- Evening: Fed. Watered. Returned to stall. Second milking if production warrants.
- Night: Alone in the barn. Straw bedding. The hours between dark and dawn are the hours when Gemszi is most fully himself, because no one is watching and the body's demands are quiet.

**Permanent Injuries & Alterations:**

- Vocal modification: Larynx, tongue, and oral cavity restructured for the new form. Speech capacity destroyed. Can produce species-appropriate vocalisations only.
- Manual dexterity destroyed: Forelimbs terminate in weight-bearing pads. No grip, no fine motor control.

- Bone density reinforcement: Throughout. Structural durability significantly increased.
- Hide thickening: Dense, textured, weather-resistant. Not furred.

**Active Conditions:**

- Lactation cycle: Active, maintained by regular milking. Cessation of milking routine would eventually cause the cycle to taper, with associated discomfort during the transition.

## GM Notes

**Playing Gemszi.** The scenario is first-person. The player inhabits Gemszi's perspective — his thoughts during work, his reactions to the family, his internal experience of the milking routine, the barn at night. The challenge is sustaining interiority without action: Gemszi cannot speak, cannot manipulate his environment, cannot initiate meaningful change in his circumstances through anything other than behaviour. His agency is almost entirely internal. What he chooses to think about, what he holds onto, what he lets go — that is the gameplay.

**The adaptation trap.** Gemszi's pragmatism is a survival mechanism that becomes a threat to his identity. Every time the routine gets easier, every time the harness feels less wrong, every time the milking registers as sensation rather than violation — that is the body winning and the person losing ground. This tension should be present throughout, not as dramatic crisis but as ambient pressure. The question is never "will he survive" — the body is built to survive. The question is "will he still be Gemszi."

**The eyes.** The eyes are the only channel. Every scene where another character interacts with Gemszi should include a moment — sometimes noticed, usually not — where the eyes say something the body cannot. This is the thread that eventually leads to recognition. Don't force it. Let it accumulate.

**Do not sentimentalise.** The Haldrens are not rescuers. The farm is not a haven. The work is real, the milking is real, the stall is real. If and when recognition comes, it does not fix anything — it complicates everything. The farm still needs a draught animal. Gemszi is still in the body. The best possible outcome is still a person living as livestock who is now *known* to be a person living as livestock. That is better than the alternative. It is not good.
