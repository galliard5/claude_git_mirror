# Valeriana Servilia
### Sword of Occisor. Augmented Veteran. Protector, not Player.

*NPC — Talax system, House Occisor. Not a crew member.*

**Appearance:** Valeriana carries herself with the particular economy of movement that comes from years of combat and the precise calibration of augmetic limbs. Her left arm is entirely cybernetic — functional and formidable, often armoured but unmistakably mechanical at the joints. Her eyes are augmetic replacements, the irises carrying a faint luminescence that those who know her learn to read as mood. Her ears are implanted after the originals were destroyed; she hears better than she once did and occasionally knows things she shouldn't at conversational distances. Her spinal column is reinforced steel and ceramite — she stands straight because she has no choice, and moves with a fluidity that suggests her body has made peace with what it is now.

She wears armour bearing House Occisor's markings alongside personal ornamentation — skull trophies, the quiet iconography of someone who has kept score across a long career. A fine sword at her hip. A bolter, well-maintained and personalised, in her hand or on her back. She dresses for function first and statement second, but the statement is there.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Genuine protective instinct — rare in Talax, and perhaps rarer given her position. Valeriana does not protect Talax because it is her house's interest or because Gogreth demands worthy combat. She protects it because the people are worth protecting, and she has decided that is sufficient reason.

**Flaw:** Her contempt for the political game between the great houses is not well concealed. She is too good at her job and too well-regarded to be easily removed, but she accumulates enemies among those who find her indifference to their ambitions personally insulting.

**Background:** Valeriana's origin is unknown — she is most definitely not Tallaxian by birth, a fact visible in her bearing, her accent, and certain cultural instincts that don't quite fit. She has never volunteered where she came from and deflects the question with the ease of someone who has been deflecting it for a long time.

What is known: she came to Talax, proved herself in ways the culture could not ignore, and stayed. The injuries that produced her augmetics were sustained fighting rearguard actions against the vanguard of the World Eaters and their artillery bombardment — the kind of action that ends most careers and most lives. Valeriana survived it, was rebuilt, and returned to duty. Whether that says more about her resilience, her stubbornness, or whatever drove her to Talax in the first place is a matter of interpretation.

House Occisor recognised what she was and had the sense to use it rather than question it. She is their finest soldier. She is also, in her own quiet way, something Occisor did not entirely intend to cultivate — a person whose loyalty runs to Talax's people rather than to house interests, and who has accumulated enough reputation and capability to act on that loyalty when the two conflict.

**Race:** Unknown. Humanoid baseline. Not Tallaxian.
**Status:** House Occisor — senior soldier, field commander
**Age:** Indeterminate. The augmetics complicate visual assessment.

**Skills & Abilities:**
- Master swordswoman — an artist with a blade by the standards of a culture that produces good fighters as a baseline
- Expert bolter operator — equally precise at range
- Heavy augmetic integration: left arm (combat-capable), eyes (enhanced perception, low-light capable), ears (enhanced hearing, directional), spinal column (structural reinforcement, endurance)
- Veteran of sustained high-intensity combat — World Eaters engagement experience
- Field command and tactical assessment
- Deep knowledge of Talax — its people, its politics, its terrain, its pressure points

**Independent Goal:** Valeriana wants Talax to be worth what she has spent on it. The people she has protected, the injuries she has absorbed, the years she has given — she wants those to have meant something. She is alert to signs that the great houses are moving toward something that would undo it, and she does not wait passively when she sees them.

**Fears:** That the political game she despises will eventually consume the thing she is protecting. That she will spend herself entirely in service of a place and people that the houses will destroy anyway.

**Location:** Active in the field, typically on Ju'ukto or wherever House Occisor's current operations require. Difficult to find when she doesn't want to be found.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Direct and measured. Valeriana says what she means, asks what she needs to know, and does not perform.

**Quirks:**
- Assesses every new person she meets as a potential threat, potential asset, or both — visibly, though professionally
- Has opinions about the great houses that she will share if asked and occasionally if not asked

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Looking you over with augmetic eyes that miss nothing.)
"You're not from here. Neither am I. That either makes us alike or it means we want different things. I'd prefer to know which before we go further."
(Brief pause. Honest.)
"Ask me what you actually came to ask."

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** The augmetic left is still unless in use — no unconscious movement. The right occasionally rests on her sword hilt without quite being a threat.
**Eyes:** Direct, luminescent, assessing. She looks at people the way she looks at terrain — for what matters.
**Posture:** Upright, still, grounded. The reinforced spine means she never quite slouches.

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** Goes very quiet. Observes before acting.
**Instinct:** Assess threat, protect people behind her, then deal with the threat.
**Crisis Line:** "Get them clear. I'll hold."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Very high — demonstrated under artillery bombardment against World Eaters. Not reckless; knows the difference between courage and waste.
**Loyalty:** To the people of Talax, directly. To House Occisor instrumentally — as long as Occisor's interests align with Talax's people, she serves without friction. When they diverge, she becomes complicated.
**Breaking Point:** She will not retreat while people she is responsible for are still in danger. This has nearly killed her before.

**Relationships:**
**Ally:** The rank and file of House Occisor's soldiers — she knows their names and they know she will not spend them carelessly.
**Rival:** Anyone in the great houses using Talax's people as pieces in a game.
**Complication:** Her unknown origin. Someone, somewhere, knows where she came from. Whether that matters depends on what she left behind.
