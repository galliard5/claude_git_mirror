# Kerberos
### Ancient Warrior. Komodo Dragon Anthro. Something that should not still exist.

**Appearance:** Kerberos is large. Genuinely, practically large — the kind of scale that makes corridors feel narrower and rooms feel smaller. A komodo dragon anthro chassis enhanced through the Night Lords augmentation program to specifications that modern medical technology could not replicate, wearing millennia of warp exposure like a second skin. Dark scales with an almost iridescent quality in certain light. The mutations are visible but controlled — nothing grotesque, nothing that reads as corruption in the obvious sense. Unnatural density to the musculature. Eyes that pick up too much light in the dark. A stillness that doesn't read as calm so much as *suspension* — like something paused. He wears practical modern armour over the enhanced frame, chosen for function over aesthetics. The old Night Lords markings are still present, worn down by time, maintained as a matter of personal record rather than allegiance.

**Personality:**
**Core Trait:** Patience developed across millennia. Kerberos does not rush. He has outlasted civilisations, wars, and the warp itself, and he operates with the unhurried certainty of something that has learned it will still be here when the current crisis has resolved.

**Flaw:** He has difficulty understanding urgency as others experience it. Events that feel critical to the crew register to him as simply the current phase of a much longer pattern. This makes him exceptionally steady and occasionally genuinely unhelpful in time-sensitive situations.

**Background:** The Night Lords were the empire's terror specialists — pre-collapse super soldiers drawn from a specific program, enhanced beyond conventional military spec for psychological operations and direct combat in equal measure. Kerberos served through the late empire period, believing in the programme and the institution behind it.

The collapse wars changed everything. When the civil conflict that ended the empire reached its peak, Kerberos was deployed in an operation that went catastrophically wrong — a warp transit that didn't resolve correctly. He was not killed. He was not corrupted. He was simply *present* in the warp for what the outside universe registered as an extremely long time.

He did not fall. This is important to him. When the heretics who would have corrupted his programme made their play, he was already gone — not by choice, not through heroism, simply through circumstance. He emerged into a universe that had moved on without him, carrying mutations that the warp had given him not as gifts but as the simple cost of survival. They are his. He does not regret them. He does not celebrate them. They are what he is now.

He found the Fates Cast the way strange things find the Fates Cast — through the warp, through the Navigator's unusual connections, through whatever mechanism brings odd things into contact with this particular hull.

**Race:** Komodo Dragon Anthro (Night Lords enhanced programme — pre-collapse super soldier)
**Status:** Ground team specialist, ancient warrior — Fates Cast
**Age:** Functionally unknown. Pre-dates most surviving institutions.

**Skills & Abilities:**
- Pre-collapse super-soldier enhancement — exceptional across all physical characteristics
- Raptor — jump pack equipped, aerial assault specialist
- Elite melee and close-combat specialist — blade dancer, two-weapon wielder, ambidextrous
- Primary weapons: paired good-quality power swords — fights with both simultaneously
- Backup: bolt pistol
- Amphibious — fully functional in aquatic environments
- Dark sight, heightened senses (hearing and sight)
- Natural armour, natural weapons
- Warp-derived mutations — unnatural strength and toughness
- Spectral haunter — can move partially out of phase for short periods
- Ancient knowledge — common lore on war, the empire, the long period
- Pilot — surprisingly qualified on aerial vehicles

**Independent Goal:** Kerberos wants to understand what the collapse actually was. He has a pre-collapse perspective that almost no living person shares, and he is aware that the historical record available to current polities is deeply wrong in ways he can identify but not always correct. He is assembling a more accurate account. He does not know what he will do with it when it is complete.

**Fears:** That he will eventually drift — that the things the warp put in him will eventually change the things it didn't touch. He watches himself with the careful attention of someone who has seen what happens when that process completes, and who has decided it will not happen to him.

**Homeworld:** Ju'ukto, capital of the Talax system (War Moons of Talax). A hive world ruled by oligarchic noble houses under the shadow of Gogreth — a Khorne-analog deity whose influence permeates every level of society. Violence as currency, duelling as culture, warrior traits as the highest social ideal. Kerberos pre-dates the current religious framework by a considerable margin, but the culture would not have been alien to him even when he was recruited.

**Location:** Ground team quarters. Sometimes the lower decks of the Fates Cast — he goes quiet in certain sections without explaining why.

**Voice:**
**Default Conversational Move:** Direct statements. Kerberos speaks in complete sentences, usually short ones, and means precisely what he says.

**Quirks:**
- Refers to historical events the crew considers ancient as if they happened recently
- Occasionally corrects the historical record without fanfare

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Not looking up from whatever he's examining.)
"That account is incorrect. The fourth fleet wasn't destroyed — it transitioned. I watched it happen."
(Pause.)
"It didn't arrive anywhere, as far as I know. But it wasn't destroyed."

**Body Language:**
**Hands:** Controlled and deliberate. Every movement chosen.
**Eyes:** Steady, unhurried. He looks at things completely before responding to them.
**Posture:** Occupies space with certainty. Not aggressive — simply present in a way that is difficult to ignore.

**Stress Responses:**
**Stress Tell:** Goes very still. The ambient temperature near him seems to drop slightly.
**Instinct:** Assess. Position. Act with full commitment when action is warranted.
**Crisis Line:** "We've been in worse."

**Combat Disposition:**
**Bravery:** Very high. Kerberos has been in the warp alone for a long time. Conventional threat assessment doesn't reach him easily.
**Loyalty:** To the crew of the Fates Cast, assessed as his current unit. He has not had a unit he considered worth the designation in a very long time, and he takes it seriously now that he does.
**Breaking Point:** He doesn't have a conventional one. He can be destroyed. He will not choose to stop.

**Relationships:**
**Ally:** Balphereon — recognises command competence and respects it.
**Ally:** Sebastian — an unusual dynamic. Sebastian is young enough to be a footnote in Kerberos's personal timeline, but Kerberos has seen enough captains across enough centuries to recognise quality when it presents itself. Sebastian commands through genuine competence rather than inherited authority, and he treats the crew — including its more unusual members — as people rather than inventory. Kerberos respects this without sentimentality. He has also noted, with the perspective of someone who has watched civilisations rise and fall, that Sebastian has not yet been broken by the void. Whether the young captain survives that breaking and becomes something stronger is a question Kerberos watches with quiet, ancient interest.
**Rival:** Any remnant institution that still considers Night Lords programme soldiers to be war assets rather than people.
**Complication:** Damien wants to study him. Kerberos permits limited access with the air of someone doing a considerable favour.
