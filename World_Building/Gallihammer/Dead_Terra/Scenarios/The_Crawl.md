# The Crawl

**Campaign:** Dead Terra — Remade Origin
**Setting:** Dead Terra (post-collapse Earth), Europan Reaches, Frankish Reaches
**Framework:** Remade Origin Framework
**Estimated Duration:** Open-ended (origin sequence ~2-3 sessions, then open world)
**Tone:** Gritty, psychological, espionage thriller with body horror undertones

---

## Overview

Veth Domaric's personal assassin — an anthro operative who has done the quiet, deniable work for years — gets caught on a job. The warlord can't protect him without exposing the connection, so the sentence is death. Publicly. Cleanly. Case closed.

Except Domaric still needs his knife. And his biomancer, Solen Kast, has been pitching a project: an infiltration-form bioservitor, purpose-built for access-based espionage. Something that can move through infrastructure — vents, walls, drainage, the layered ruin-structure under every settlement in the Europan Reaches. Something with a mind sharp enough to gather intelligence and a body designed to go where no one else can.

Domaric offers the condemned man a choice: real death, or this. The execution is faked. The assassin is delivered to Kast's compound on the western outskirts of Laut. The campaign begins in the prep room, the day the procedure starts.

**Hook:** The PC has already made the choice. He's standing in the fourth building of Kast's compound — the one that doesn't appear on the facility's official documentation — knowing what's about to happen to him. There is no going back. The only question is what comes next.

**Core Conflict:** A man remade into a weapon for someone who already burned him once. The leash is chemical — a drug dependency the PC believes is fatal to break. The truth is more complicated. The campaign follows the PC through transformation, deployment into the Graumark Confederacy's strongholds, the slow discovery that the cage has a crack in it, and whatever he does with that knowledge.

**Resolution:** Open-ended. The PC's arc depends on whether he discovers the flaw, whether he escapes, and what he builds (or destroys) after.

## The Political Context

### Why This Campaign Exists

Four years ago, someone sent an assassin into Laut. The target was Veth Domaric. The attempt failed — Domaric's fourth serious assassination attempt and his closest call. The assassin was never publicly identified. Domaric knows who sent them: the Graumark Confederacy, specifically the coalition of three warlords who have been consolidating territory in the eastern Heartland.

The Confederacy controls the approaches to the Rhine. Its three warlords hold complementary assets — one controls the primary Rhine toll point, one controls the road junction connecting the Confederacy to Frankenhalt, and the third holds a ruin site of unclear significance that the other two defer to on questions of politics and strategy in ways that are not publicly explained. Together, they represent a combined military capacity that Domaric cannot match through conventional force.

What Domaric can do — what he has always done — is know what his rivals are planning before they plan it. His intelligence operation is more sophisticated than Laut's apparent scale would suggest, built on a network of paid informants across the Heartland, relationships with several information brokers in Parisc, and an understanding that his territory's survival depends on information advantage.

The arthropod infiltration project is the next step. Conventional spies can be caught, turned, interrogated. What Kast is proposing cannot be recognised, cannot be interrogated under normal circumstances, and cannot be traced back to Laut. Domaric approved it. He considers it an expensive experiment. If it works, it changes what he is capable of. If it fails, he is out the cost of the commission and one operative he was going to execute anyway.

### The Broader Web

The Crawl does not exist in a political vacuum. Domaric's territory sits at a nexus of Europan Reaches tensions that provide both context and potential campaign expansion:

**The Graumark Confederacy** — the primary intelligence target and the reason the project exists. Three warlords, combined territory, four-year cold standoff with Domaric since the assassination attempt. Both sides are gathering intelligence and positioning resources. The Confederacy cannot successfully move against Domaric's drops without significant preparation, and Domaric's operation is designed specifically to detect that preparation.

**The Frankish Drop** — Domaric's most valuable asset. Two parallel descent mechanisms on the Basin wall, capable of moving significant cargo loads between the Europan Reaches and the Great Basin floor. The drops are the economic foundation of his entire territory. The revenue they generate keeps his military loyal and his settlements fed during bad seasons. Several rival factions want the drops. None has taken them yet.

**House Vauclain** — a formally governed territory to the west that treats Domaric's Basin drops as an unfair economic advantage it intends to correct. Currently pursuing Basin wall access through the Wound Council's arbitration process. Domaric has been quietly ensuring the arbitration goes nowhere.

**The Basin Authority** — controls the drop infrastructure below the wall; Domaric controls the access above it. They need each other and neither is comfortable with the dependency. Current arrangement: negotiated tariff split, reviewed annually, always contentious.

**The Wound Council** — the seven-seat governing body of Rimhalt, the neutral diplomatic venue on the crater rim in the eastern Heartland. Domaric uses Rimhalt as intended; the Council uses his drops with appropriate tariff. Cautious mutual respect, but the Council's interest in maintaining regional balance means they would not mourn a reduction in Domaric's power.

**Parisc's Succession Crisis** — House Veln's internal succession dispute in the Heartland's primary trading city is drawing in outside parties. Both Frankenhalt and Romass are backing different factions. This risks turning a commercial dispute into a proxy conflict between the two largest city-cores, which would reshape the political map and create both threats and opportunities for Domaric.

## Origin Sequence (Remade Origin Framework)

### 1. Capture / Acquisition

**Method:** Criminal sentencing under coerced consent.

The PC was Domaric's personal covert operative — assassinations, intelligence gathering, the kind of work that doesn't appear on any official record. He was good at it. He got caught on a job — not through incompetence but through circumstance (the details are a Session Zero variable: betrayed by an informant, target had unexpected security, bad luck, or Domaric set him up deliberately).

Domaric could not intervene without revealing the connection. The PC was sentenced to death by whatever local authority holds jurisdiction — likely a Heartland settlement in the Graumark's sphere of influence, or possibly one of the smaller warlord territories that fill the gaps between major powers. Domaric arranged the execution to be faked — the body that was buried or burned was not the PC. The PC was delivered to Kast's compound instead.

The "choice" was presented as voluntary: transformation or actual death. The PC chose transformation. Whether that constitutes consent is a question the biomancer tradition's three competing positions — the Khet lineage's restrictive stance, the Vann's permissive stance, the Alur's procedural stance — would answer differently. Kast's position, if asked, would be that the distinction is interesting but not his problem.

**The anthro context:** The PC is an anthro — one of the empire's spliced lines, carrying animal characteristics grafted into human stock by imperial bioengineers for purposes that ranged from utilitarian to aesthetic to fetishistic. Thirty thousand years of post-collapse evolution have turned those decorative features functional: the claws that were ornamental in the empire are weapons now; the enhanced senses that were charming in a companion are survival advantages. The PC's specific strain is a Session Zero variable, but whatever it is, it carries the double legacy of imperial design and post-collapse adaptation. The form Kast is about to give him will erase all of it.

**Session Zero Variables:**
- PC's name and anthro subtype (what animal strain? what functional adaptations has the lineage developed?)
- How long the PC worked for Domaric before the arrest
- What the failed job was — who was the target, why it mattered, where it happened
- Whether the PC suspects Domaric burned him deliberately
- The PC's relationship to his anthro heritage — does he carry it proudly, pragmatically, or with the complicated awareness that his ancestors were made to be pretty for someone?

### 2. Commission

**Patron:** Veth Domaric, Warlord of the Frankish Reaches

Fifty-three years old. Physically unremarkable. Survivor of four assassination attempts. Controls a territory centred on Laut — a mid-tier city-core on the Rhône corridor, approximately eighty kilometres from the northern Basin wall — and the two Frankish Drop access points that connect his territory to the Basin floor trade routes and, from there, to the Nordafrik Expanse.

Domaric is pragmatic to a fault. He does not have strong ideological commitments — his politics are almost entirely instrumental. He treats people as assets, opportunities, or threats, and calibrates his behaviour accordingly. This makes him difficult to manipulate through appeals to principle and relatively easy to deal with if you understand what he actually wants: the drops secure, the territory stable, his rivals contained. He is not cruel by preference — cruelty is expensive — but he does not hesitate when he has decided something is necessary.

He has been patient about the Graumark Confederacy for four years. The arthropod project is what his patience has been building toward.

**The commission specifications:**

- **Form:** Arthropod body plan. Multi-limbed, segmented, capable of navigating infrastructure — vents, wall cavities, drainage systems, the layered ruin-structure that underlies every Europan settlement. The Reaches are the most densely rebuilt region on Earth; every major city is a layered core where pre-collapse streets sit buried under empire-era megastructures buried under the wreckage of the collapse wars. The form must be able to move through those layers. Must be able to climb any surface, compress through structural gaps, operate in darkness and confined spaces.

- **Cognizance:** Full. Domaric's motivation is pragmatic — a thinking infiltrator is worth ten that can't. The form must retain complete cognitive function: memory, judgment, tactical assessment, language comprehension. This is the entire point: an operative that can assess what it finds, prioritise intelligence, and make kill/no-kill decisions independently in the field. Domaric does not think of this as cruelty. He thinks of it as problem-solving.

- **Combat capability:** Secondary but present. The form must be able to eliminate a target quietly when required. Not a frontline combatant — an assassin's toolkit built into the body. Venomous delivery, speed, the ability to strike from positions no one expects an attack from.

- **Control:** Chemical dependency. Domaric wants an absolute leash — something that guarantees the asset returns. Kast proposed a metabolic dependency on a compound administered at regular intervals. Domaric was told that withdrawal is fatal within seventy-two hours. The PC was told the same thing.

- **Sensory suite:** Rebuilt entirely. The form doesn't see or hear the way the PC used to. Domaric specified superior intelligence-gathering capability; Kast interpreted this as a complete sensory redesign optimised for the infiltration environment.

### 3. Reshaping

**Specialist:** Solen Kast, Biomancer

Mid-sixties. Eleven years in Domaric's employ, the relationship beginning as a commission for agricultural organism development and evolving into something more comprehensive. Operates from a facility on the western outskirts of Laut that is officially a licensed medical and agricultural research compound. Three buildings publicly used for the stated purposes; a fourth building behind the main complex that is not listed on the facility's official documentation. The fourth building is where Kast does his actual work. The neighbourhood has learned to attribute its distinctive smell to the agricultural research.

Kast shows moderate self-modification accumulated over decades of practice: enhanced sensory acuity, reduced sleep requirement, a subtle restructuring of the hands that makes fine work easier. His modifications follow a coherent logic — practical enhancements oriented toward the craft — but the cumulative effect is visible. He does not look entirely like whatever he started as. His race is difficult to classify. This is typical of long-practicing biomancers; the drift is professional, not random.

The arthropod infiltration project is the most technically ambitious thing Kast has attempted. He has been thinking about it for longer than Domaric knows — refining the concept on paper and in smaller experiments for years before the assassination attempt gave him a commission to build it. He pitched it to Domaric as a solution to a specific problem, but for Kast, it is a design challenge he has been waiting for.

**How Kast works:** Biomancy operates at the intersection of two knowledge streams that rarely survive intact in the same practitioner. The first is the remnant science of imperial bioengineering — procedural, compound-based, technical. The second is arcane ability oriented toward living systems — the capacity to feel the structure of an organism directly, to perceive its state, to push it. Kast uses both. His workspace contains an extensive collection of prepared compounds — each serving specific functions in priming tissue, suppressing rejection, triggering or inhibiting biological processes. His arcane sensitivity allows him to maintain active awareness of the subject throughout the procedure, feeling the change as it happens and steering it with continuous small adjustments. The combination produces transformation that a purely technical practitioner cannot achieve without intact imperial equipment.

**The workspace:** The fourth building is cluttered with the accumulated evidence of long practice — jars that no longer have labels because Kast simply knows what they are, equipment modified past recognition, specimens at various stages of various projects. It smells wrong in a way the PC can't name. The workspace is both a medical facility and an artist's studio. There are specimens — finished work, failed work, experiments in progress. The PC will see what Kast does before Kast does it to him.

**The procedure:**

- **Duration:** Days. This is a complex transformation requiring staged work. Kast will not rush it. The exhaustion cost to the practitioner is proportional to the complexity — maintaining active arcane awareness through a transformation this radical will leave Kast genuinely impaired for a recovery period afterward.

- **Consciousness:** The PC is conscious for prep and early stages. Sedated for the most intensive restructuring. Brought back to consciousness periodically so Kast can assess cognitive function and make adjustments. The moments of awareness during the process are disorienting — the body is wrong and getting wronger, the senses are shifting, nothing maps to what the PC expects.

- **Staged process:** Kast works in phases. Skeletal dissolution first — the arthropod form doesn't use an internal skeleton. Then tissue reshaping, limb formation, sensory organ development, integument (the chitin-analogue). The chemical dependency is introduced during the process, woven into the form's metabolism as it develops.

- **Kast's demeanour:** Professional, absorbed, occasionally conversational in the way someone is conversational while doing something that occupies most of their attention. He talks to the PC during conscious phases — partly to assess cognitive function, partly because he is genuinely interested in the subject's experience. The questions are a craftsman's questions: *How does this feel? Can you sense the difference? What are you perceiving right now?* He is not cruel. He is not kind. He is fascinated. He will answer questions honestly when they're about the procedure. He will deflect questions about the ethics because they're not interesting to him.

- **The two-streams in practice:** The PC, if perceptive during conscious moments, may notice the duality of Kast's method — the precise, procedural application of compounds alongside something else, something less visible. A stillness in Kast's posture that suggests deep concentration of a kind that has nothing to do with his hands. The occasional pause where Kast seems to be listening to something the PC cannot hear. This is the arcane component. Kast is feeling the PC's tissue directly, perceiving the transformation as it happens at a level of resolution that no equipment could provide. If asked about it, Kast will describe it in technical terms that don't quite explain what is actually happening.

### 4. The Flaw

**Type:** Chemical dependency — fabricated lethality.

Kast built the drug dependency exactly as commissioned. The compound is real. The dependency is real. The mild euphoria is real. What is not real is the lethality of withdrawal.

Domaric was told: *Without the compound administered every forty-eight to seventy-two hours, the subject's modified metabolism will cascade into systemic failure. Death within seventy-two hours of the last dose, no exceptions.* The PC was told the same thing during the transformation process, presented as a simple biological fact of the new form.

**The truth:** Withdrawal is severe but survivable. The symptoms are genuine and brutal — metabolic distress, sensory disruption, pain, temporary loss of some of the form's capabilities. It feels like dying. It is not dying. The form's biology will stabilise after a withdrawal period of approximately five to seven days. Full capability returns within two weeks.

**Why Kast did it:** Engineering philosophy, not conscience. A leash that kills the asset on failure is a bad leash — it means any supply disruption, any logistics failure, any accident destroys something Kast spent days of exhausting arcane-intensive work creating. The craftsman builds in tolerances. He builds the dependency the client asked for, and he makes it survivable because that's better design. He doesn't tell the client about the tolerance because the client didn't ask for it and wouldn't appreciate it.

This is consistent with Kast's broader professional character. He cares about the quality of his work more than he cares about Domaric's control requirements. Domaric values Kast's work, pays well, and leaves Kast's methods to Kast. He does not want to know the details of what happens in the fourth building. Kast takes advantage of that distance.

Whether Kast feels anything about the PC's situation is ambiguous. What he definitely feels is that his work should not be destroyed by a missed supply shipment. The biomancer tradition's consensus on identity persistence — that the person who emerges from transformation is the person who went in, changed but continuous — is something Kast knows and has opinions about. Those opinions are not obviously expressed in any direction. He is a craftsman. The craft has produced its best work. Whether the person inside that work is happy about it is an interesting question, but not, in Kast's assessment, his question.

**Discovery:** The PC does not know the truth at campaign start. Discovery of the flaw is a potential campaign arc — it could come through accidental supply interruption (a deployment runs long, a supply shipment along the Rhône corridor is delayed), through information from another biomancer or someone who understands the biology (the Khet Lineages in the Indic Belt maintain the most comprehensive institutional knowledge; an independent practitioner in the Reaches might know enough), or through the PC's own growing awareness that the dependency doesn't behave quite the way he was told it would.

### 5. Escape

**Not yet determined.** The campaign begins pre-transformation. Escape is a future event whose shape depends on play. Possible vectors:

- **Active escape:** The PC discovers the flaw and deliberately breaks the leash by enduring the withdrawal period. The Graumark Gap — thirty kilometres of forested upland with no clear jurisdictional claim between Domaric's territory and the Confederacy — is the most obvious immediate refuge. Neither side acknowledges patrolling it. Both do.

- **Passive failure:** Extended deployment degrades the conditioning. The PC begins to question.

- **Catastrophic event:** Domaric's territory is attacked — by the Graumark Confederacy, by House Vauclain, by an opportunistic third party exploiting the Parisc succession crisis. Supply lines are cut. The PC is stranded without his dose and survives what should have killed him.

- **Discarding:** A mission goes wrong. Domaric considers the asset compromised and writes it off, not knowing the PC survived.

- **The Basin:** The Great Basin floor — five kilometres below the surrounding terrain, salt flats and pre-collapse ruins — is an environment where the PC's form could potentially survive and pursuers could not easily follow. The drop infrastructure is controlled, but the Basin has other ways in for something that can crawl.

## The Form — Design Notes

### Body Plan

Arthropod. Multi-segmented, multi-limbed, no internal skeleton. The form uses an exoskeletal structure (chitin-analogue integument) with enough flexibility at segment joints to compress through structural gaps. Not a direct copy of any natural arthropod — Kast designed from function, not from reference.

The Europan Reaches' layered city-cores are the operating environment. Romass has seven mapped underground layers; Parisc has a functioning sub-city of storage vaults and movement corridors; even mid-tier cities like Laut sit on ruins of structures from previous eras whose lower levels remain incompletely explored. The form is built to move through all of it — the maintained infrastructure and the sealed, forgotten spaces beneath.

**Key physical characteristics:**

- **Size:** Roughly dog-sized at full extension, but capable of compressing significantly. The segmented body can fold, flatten, and reconfigure to fit through spaces much smaller than its full profile suggests.

- **Limbs:** Multiple pairs — enough for rapid, silent movement across any surface including ceilings and vertical faces. Some limbs are specialised: gripping, cutting, manipulating small objects. Fine manipulation is possible but different from hands — the PC will need to relearn how to interact with physical objects. The form's relationship to physical space is fundamentally different from anything the PC's anthro body could do.

- **Integument:** Chitin-analogue. Hard enough for protection, with flexible joints. Coloration is a Session Zero variable — could be dark/matte for concealment, could shift based on environment (chromatophore-adjacent capability Kast may have built in). Provides moderate physical protection but the form is not built to take hits — the chitin guards against incidental contact and the infrastructure environment, not weapons.

- **No recognisable face.** The form has sensory organs but they do not map to eyes-nose-mouth. The PC perceives through antennae, vibration-sensing plates, chemoreceptors. There is nothing to make eye contact with. This is deliberate — Domaric specified a form that would not be recognised as containing a person. The bioservitor tradition's observation that the person persists through transformation applies here: the PC is still himself. He is inside something that has no face.

- **Neuter.** Per the Remade Origin Framework's default — the form has no sex unless there is a specific functional need. There isn't.

### Sensory Suite

The PC's old senses are gone. His anthro senses — whatever enhanced hearing, low-light vision, or olfactory acuity his strain had developed over twenty thousand years of post-collapse evolution — are erased along with everything else. Replaced with:

- **Vibration sensing:** Through substrate contact (floor, wall, any solid surface). Effectively replaces hearing and provides spatial awareness through solid structures. The PC can feel footsteps through walls, sense machinery through floors, detect structural stress in the material he's moving through. In the layered ruin-structure of an Europan city-core, this sense provides information about the building's internal state that no visual system could match — structural integrity, water flow in pipes, the rhythm of human habitation transmitted through stone and metal.

- **Chemoreception:** Air sampling through specialised antennae. Detects biological signatures (people nearby, their approximate number, possibly emotional state via stress hormones), chemical composition (atmosphere quality, toxins, accelerants, the distinctive fungal spore signatures that indicate parasitic colony presence), and environmental information. In the confined spaces of underground infrastructure, chemoreception is the primary early warning system — the PC will taste a parasitic fungal colony's spore front before any visual system could detect it. He will know what someone ate, whether they are afraid, whether the air ahead is safe to breathe.

- **Limited visual capacity:** Not eyes as the PC knew them. Light-sensitive structures that provide motion detection and gross spatial awareness in open environments. Possibly extended into infrared or ultraviolet. Detail is poor compared to the PC's old vision; the form relies on vibration and chemoreception as primary senses. In the dark infrastructure environments where the form will operate, this is not a limitation — vibration and chemical sensing are superior to vision underground.

### Combat Capability

Secondary to infiltration but present. Kast designed an assassin's toolkit built into the body:

- **Venom delivery:** One or more limbs equipped with envenomation capability. Kast designed multiple compounds — paralytic, lethal, and possibly a sedative variant. Delivery is through puncture, silent, fast. The venomous serpents of Dead Terra — widespread across all terrestrial biomes, capable of striking in one hundred to two hundred milliseconds with near-invisible camouflage — are the biological reference point, though the PC's venom is engineered rather than evolved.

- **Speed and ambush:** The form can strike from concealment with speed that exceeds human reaction time. Ceilings, wall cavities, behind structural elements — the attack comes from a direction the target is not watching. The infrastructure itself is the ambush terrain. In a city-core with seven underground layers, the attack can come from below.

- **Cutting/gripping:** Limb structures capable of severing soft targets. Not designed for sustained combat — designed for a single decisive action and immediate withdrawal.

- **Durability:** The chitin provides moderate physical protection but the form is not built to take hits. If a fight goes past the first few seconds, the form is in the wrong fight. The design philosophy is the same as the venomous serpents in the Dead Terra compendium: stealth movement, precision striking, rapid withdrawal.

### The Drug

**Name:** Session Zero variable — whatever Domaric or Kast calls it.

**Administration:** Liquid or injectable compound, provided by Domaric's people at regular intervals (every forty-eight to seventy-two hours). Administered at Kast's compound or through the handler during deployments.

**Effects when taken:** Mild euphoria, metabolic stabilisation, a sense of physical wellbeing. The high is subtle — not incapacitating, not strongly pleasurable, just a quiet rightness. Enough to notice its absence.

**Perceived withdrawal (what the PC believes):** Systemic metabolic failure. Organ shutdown. Death within seventy-two hours.

**Actual withdrawal:** Severe metabolic distress (cramping, tremors, sensory disruption — chemoreception becomes unreliable, vibration sensing grows erratic). Loss of some capabilities (venom production slows, chitin integrity may temporarily weaken). Intense discomfort verging on agony for the first forty-eight to seventy-two hours. Gradual stabilisation over days three to five. Near-full recovery by day seven to ten. Complete capability restoration by day fourteen.

**The experience of withdrawal** should feel like the PC is dying, especially in the early hours. Everything Domaric told him appears to be true. The moment when the dying *stops* — when the body stabilises despite everything the PC was told would happen — is the moment the leash breaks.

## The Infrastructure Ecology

The layered ruin-structure beneath Europan cities is not empty. The PC will share the walls, vents, and drainage systems with things that have been living in the infrastructure for millennia. Kast knew this when he designed the form. The sensory suite is built to detect these threats. Knowing they exist and surviving them are different skills.

### What Lives in the Walls

**Parasitic Fungal Colonies** — The most insidious infrastructure threat. Bioweapon remnants evolved over thirty thousand years, these organisms transmit through airborne spores and are optimised for dense, enclosed environments — exactly the kind of spaces the PC will navigate. Infection progression runs one to seven days incubation, one to six weeks active infection with behavioural modification (the fungus drives hosts toward conditions that optimise spore dispersal), and a terminal fruiting stage. The PC's chemoreception is the primary defence — fungal spore fronts have distinctive chemical signatures that the form can taste in the air before entering a contaminated zone. Whether the PC's chitin-analogue integument provides any resistance to spore contact is unknown at campaign start and is something Kast may or may not have designed for.

**Venomous Serpents** — Widespread across all terrestrial biomes, present in urban ruins where they shelter-seek in structural cavities. Near-invisible camouflage matching substrate. Strike speed of one hundred to two hundred milliseconds. Neurotoxic and hemotoxic variants, both potentially lethal. The PC's vibration sensing should detect serpent movement through substrate contact, but the serpents are adapted to stillness — they may be undetectable until the strike. In the confined spaces of infrastructure navigation, a serpent encounter is a close-quarters engagement with an organism that has been evolving for this exact kind of ambush for thirty thousand years.

**Hyper-Aggressive Stinging Insects** — Nesting in structural cavities throughout urban ruins. Fast, coordinated swarms with territorial aggression. A single sting is rarely fatal; a swarm attack of twenty or more insects frequently is. The PC's chitin may provide some physical protection, but joints and sensory organs are vulnerable. Nesting density is extreme in some sections of underground infrastructure. The PC's chemoreception will detect nest pheromone signatures — the question is whether the PC can navigate around an established colony's territory in confined spaces where alternative routes may not exist.

**Terran Microbial Life** — The omnipresent baseline threat. Every surface, every inhalation. Mutation rates higher than pre-collapse normal. The PC's transformed biology — non-standard, engineered, maintained by biomantic design rather than by thirty thousand years of immune system co-evolution with the local microbial ecology — may be more or less susceptible than baseline human biology. Kast's design presumably accounts for this. Whether his account is thorough enough is a question the infrastructure will answer.

**Formica War Ants** — Bioweapon remnants operating as continent-spanning supercolonies. Pheromone-coordinated distributed intelligence. In urban ruin environments, they expand through structures. The PC's form is arthropod — whether the Formica recognise it as a fellow arthropod, as prey, or as something outside their recognition framework is unknown. An encounter with a recruitment trail in confined infrastructure is potentially unsurvivable regardless of the answer. The form's speed and ability to move vertically are the primary escape options.

**Other Infrastructure Fauna** — The deep layers of Europan city-cores contain organisms that have been evolving in sealed underground ecosystems for millennia. Dwarven communities that inhabit the deep ruins have accumulated working knowledge of what is down there; the PC has no such knowledge and will be learning through encounter. The lower layers of Romass — below the fourth stratum, sealed by Triumvirate decree six hundred years ago following an incident the decree does not describe — contain things that three senior Triumvirate members have declined to learn about. The PC, navigating infrastructure by necessity rather than choice, does not have the luxury of declining.

### The Form's Advantages in This Ecology

The PC is not defenceless. Kast designed the form for this environment, which means:

- The sensory suite provides early warning that no baseline organism has — vibration sensing detects movement through substrate; chemoreception detects spore fronts, pheromone trails, and biological signatures before visual contact
- The chitin provides physical barrier protection against casual contact with contaminated surfaces, minor bites, and environmental hazards
- The form's speed and surface-climbing capability allow rapid withdrawal from threats that a ground-bound organism could not escape
- The compressed-profile capability allows access to refuge spaces too small for most predators

The disadvantage is that the PC is alone, in an ecology he does not understand, wearing a body he is still learning. The form's capabilities are theoretical until tested. The infrastructure's threats are real now.

## Key NPCs

### Veth Domaric — Warlord of the Frankish Reaches

**Role:** Patron. The PC's owner, employer, and the person who arranged his fake execution.

- **Character:** Pragmatic to a fault. Politics are almost entirely instrumental. Treats people as assets, opportunities, or threats. Not cruel by preference — cruelty is expensive — but does not hesitate when he has decided something is necessary. Fifty-three. Physically unremarkable. Survivor of four assassination attempts.

- **Resources:** Controls Laut and the surrounding Frankish Reaches — approximately 120,000 people across six significant settlements. His two Frankish Drop access points on the Basin wall are the most valuable thing he controls, connecting his territory to Basin floor trade routes and Nordafrik commerce. The drop revenue keeps his military loyal.

- **Intelligence apparatus:** More sophisticated than Laut's scale suggests. Paid informants across the Heartland, relationships with information brokers in Parisc, and now the arthropod project. The investment is driven by Domaric's assessment that his territory survives on knowing what rivals plan before they plan it.

- **Relationship to PC:** Pre-existing. The PC has worked for Domaric for years. There may be something resembling mutual respect buried under the power dynamic — or there may not. Domaric burned the PC when it was convenient and offered transformation as a favour. Whether he sees the irony is a Session Zero question.

- **Pursuit tendency:** If the PC escapes, Domaric will pursue — not obsessively, but methodically. The PC knows too much about Domaric's operations. A loose bioservitor with full cognizance and assassination capability is a threat that cannot be ignored. Domaric does not want to know the details of what happens in Kast's compound; he knows enough about what the PC can do to take recovery seriously.

- **Rivals:**
  - *The Graumark Confederacy* (northeast) — source of the assassination attempt, primary intelligence target, the reason the PC exists in this form
  - *House Vauclain* (west) — trade competitor pursuing Basin wall access through Wound Council arbitration
  - *The Basin Authority* — controls drop infrastructure below the wall, complicated mutual dependency

### Solen Kast — Biomancer

**Role:** Specialist. The craftsman who performs the transformation.

- **Character:** Curious craftsman. Technically fascinated by the commission — an infiltration-optimised arthropod with full cognitive retention pushes his capabilities in directions he finds genuinely exciting. The PC is a subject, a project, and — during conscious phases — a source of data about how the experience feels from the inside. Not cruel, not kind. Professional in a way that is both reassuring and unsettling.

- **Practice:** Eleven years with Domaric, beginning with agricultural organism development. Operates the four-building compound on Laut's western outskirts. Three buildings for the licensed medical and agricultural work that justifies the facility's existence; the fourth for actual biomantic work. Mid-sixties, moderate self-modification (enhanced sensory acuity, reduced sleep requirement, restructured hands for fine work). His race is difficult to classify — the accumulated drift of decades of self-modification.

- **The two streams:** Kast works at the intersection of remnant imperial bioengineering procedure and arcane sensitivity to living systems. The compounds are real. The arcane perception is real. The combination allows him to do work that should require a facility that no longer exists. The cost is exhaustion — maintaining active arcane awareness through a complex transformation is physically and cognitively depleting.

- **The flaw:** Built in the survivable withdrawal out of engineering philosophy. May or may not resurface later in the campaign. If he does, his interest in the PC will be professional: how is the form performing? What has the PC discovered about its capabilities? Has the design held up? The biomancer tradition's consensus — that the person persists through transformation, changed but continuous — is something Kast knows. Whether it changes his behaviour is not clear.

- **Relationship to the Khet Lineages:** Kast is an independent practitioner, not Lineage-trained. The Khet Lineages — the most significant biomancer institution on Earth, eight thousand years of continuous existence, three founding lineages with different theoretical orientations — respond to successful independents by attempting to recruit them and to problematic ones by using credentialing leverage to limit their client base. Kast's profile — retained by a warlord, doing military-application transformation work — would put him squarely in the Lineages' complicated zone. Whether they know about him is a campaign variable.

### The Handler

**Role:** Domaric's representative who manages the PC's deployments and administers the drug.

- **Name and character:** Session Zero variable. The handler is the PC's primary sapient contact during active service. The quality of this relationship matters — the handler provides mission briefings, administers the drug, debriefs the PC after deployments. They may have personal feelings about the PC (respect, contempt, discomfort, fascination, indifference) that colour the operational relationship.

- **The drug cycle:** The handler controls the supply. This makes them the immediate face of the leash, regardless of their personal feelings about it. The handler arrives at Kast's compound or at a designated meeting point with the dose. The regularity of this contact — every forty-eight to seventy-two hours — structures the PC's operational life.

- **Operational function:** Carries intelligence from Domaric's apparatus to the PC and intelligence from the PC back. May or may not have the full picture of what the PC discovers. May or may not be trusted by Domaric with the full scope of the operation. The handler's position in Domaric's hierarchy is a campaign variable that affects how much the PC learns about the broader political situation through official channels.

### The Graumark Warlords — Intelligence Targets

Three warlords whose combined territory forms the Graumark Confederacy in the eastern Heartland. They are the primary reason the PC exists in this form.

**The Rhine Toll Lord** — Controls a fortified position on the primary Rhine toll point. The most commercially oriented of the three. His territory is the Confederacy's revenue engine — Rhine traffic pays for everything else. His stronghold is built for defence against conventional assault. Its underground layers are standard Heartland ruin-structure: layered, partially mapped, partially occupied by the things that live in Europan infrastructure. The PC's form is designed to move through infrastructure exactly like this.

**The Road Junction Lord** — Controls the main road junction connecting the Confederacy to Frankenhalt, the Heartland's primary military manufacturing centre. This makes him the Confederacy's logistics link to the best weapons available in the Reaches. His territory is more spread out than the Rhine Toll Lord's — the junction is a geographic feature, not a single stronghold. Intelligence gathering here is about the flow of material and communication, not about what is inside a single building.

**The Ruin Lord** — The third warlord. The most important target and the most difficult to access. Holds a ruin site of unclear significance that the other two defer to on questions of politics and strategy in ways that are not publicly explained. His residence is built into a partially explored imperial-era ruin complex whose underground sections are not mapped.

The Ruin Lord's complex is the ultimate target of the arthropod project. Kast knew what the targets would be when he designed the form. An unmapped imperial-era underground complex — the kind of space that might go deep, that might connect to sealed sections, that might contain things that have been running programs from thirty thousand years ago undisturbed — is exactly the environment the PC's capabilities are specifically suited to operate in. This is not a coincidence.

What the Ruin Lord has found down there, what he knows, and why the other two warlords defer to him are questions Domaric wants answered. They are also questions whose answers may be more significant than Domaric expects.

## Key Locations

### Laut — Home Base

A mid-tier city-core on the Rhône corridor, eighty kilometres from the northern Basin wall. Population approximately 180,000 in the city and immediate surrounds. Not in the same weight class as Romass or Parisc, but important because of its position: the Rhône corridor connects the Heartland to the Basin wall, and Domaric's two drops sit at its terminus. Laut is the last major settlement before the Basin wall. Everything going into the Basin from the western Heartland passes through or near Laut.

The city's cultural identity is organised around the transit economy — services for the constant flow of traders, agents, and travellers. Inns, warehouses, contract services, information brokers. The population knows how to deal with strangers, how to extract value from transient relationships, and how to stay out of business that isn't theirs.

**Domaric's compound:** A substantial complex in the old city centre, built on the ruins of a structure that was significant in a previous era and whose lower levels remain incompletely explored. Contains his personal residence, working facility, and command staff.

**Kast's compound:** Western outskirts. Three public buildings (medical and agricultural research). The fourth building behind the main complex — unlisted, distinctive smell, where the PC's transformation takes place and where the PC returns between deployments. This is the campaign's logistical anchor.

**The infrastructure:** Like every Europan city-core, Laut is a layered site. The current city sits on ruins of previous civilisations. The underground is less dramatic than Romass or Parisc — no sealed districts, no famous lower layers — but it exists. For the PC, Laut's underground is the first environment to learn the form in. Familiar territory seen from inside the walls, literally.

### The Rhône Corridor

The maintained transit route from Laut south to the Frankish Drop on the Basin wall. The most reliable route in Domaric's territory — well-marked, regularly patrolled, equipped with relay stations at day-travel intervals (Beauval, Montresq, and Drivenal, the last settlement before the wall descent begins). Moving goods along it is predictable. Moving personnel without being noticed is difficult. This is intentional.

For the PC, the corridor is the supply line. The drug comes from Kast's compound along the Rhône road to wherever the PC is deployed. If the corridor is disrupted — by the Graumark, by House Vauclain, by a natural event, by something from the Basin — the drug supply is interrupted. This is one possible trigger for the flaw's discovery.

### The Basin Wall Stations

Three fortified positions on the Basin wall above the Frankish Drop:
- **Upper Station** — rim level, customs and cargo management
- **Mid Station** — halfway down, emergency shelter and equipment cache
- **Lower Station** — bottom of the descent, transition to Basin Authority infrastructure

All three are Domaric's installations. The Lower Station shares physical space with the Basin Authority's operation and the boundary between them is occasionally contentious. The drop itself comprises two parallel descent mechanisms capable of moving significant cargo. Heavily fortified above and below.

For the PC, the wall stations represent both operational infrastructure and potential escape route. The Basin floor — salt flat, pre-collapse ruins, extremophile ecosystems, the Basin Authority's extraction stations — is an environment where pursuit would be difficult. The drop is controlled, but five kilometres of Basin wall has other ways down for something that can climb.

### The Graumark Gap

Approximately thirty kilometres of forested upland between Domaric's northeastern border and the Confederacy's southwestern extent. No clear jurisdictional claim. Both sides patrol it. Neither acknowledges doing so. A number of things have happened in the Gap over the past four years that neither side has reported publicly.

For the PC, the Gap is the operational boundary zone — the transition between home territory and target territory during deployments against the Confederacy. It is also the most immediate refuge in an escape scenario: contested ground where neither power has reliable surveillance, covered in forest that a small arthropod form can disappear into. The Gap's ecology includes the standard Europan suite — pack hunters, venomous serpents, megafauna omnivores, stinging insects — but these are surface threats. The PC's form is built for infrastructure, not forest. An escape through the Gap would require adaptation to an environment the form was not designed for.

### The Ruin Lord's Complex

The third Graumark warlord's residence and stronghold. Built into a partially explored imperial-era ruin complex in the eastern Heartland. Underground sections unmapped. The other two Confederacy warlords defer to the Ruin Lord on political and strategic questions for reasons that are not publicly explained.

This is the campaign's primary infiltration target — the place the PC was built to access. Imperial-era ruins in the Europan Reaches are among the most intact pre-collapse structures on the planet, their underground sections potentially going deep into strata that no one currently alive has fully explored. What the Ruin Lord has found in those sections — pre-collapse technology, sealed chambers, active systems, or something else — is the intelligence Domaric wants.

For the PC, this location represents the convergence of capability and purpose: the form is specifically designed for unmapped underground terrain, and the target is unmapped underground terrain. What the PC discovers there — about the Ruin Lord's power, about what the empire built, about the things that may still be operating in the deep levels — may be more consequential than the political intelligence Domaric commissioned him to gather.

### Deployment Targets — The Wider Reaches

Beyond the Graumark, the Europan Reaches offer an expanding set of potential deployment targets as the campaign develops:

- **Romass** — the Eternal Ruin, population 800,000, fourteen archaeological strata, seven mapped underground layers, the sealed district below the fourth stratum. The most extensive underground network of any city in the Reaches. The PC's capabilities would be extraordinary here — and the things in Romass's lower layers would be extraordinary threats.

- **Parisc** — the Crossroads, population 500,000, the Heartland's commercial centre. The underground is less dramatic but more practically useful — functioning storage vaults and movement corridors. The succession crisis within House Veln and the proxy conflict it's generating between Frankenhalt and Romass could make Parisc an intelligence target for Domaric.

- **Frankenhalt** — the Arsenal, population 350,000, the Heartland's military manufacturing centre. The lower levels of the manufacturing complex contain partially functional imperial equipment. The guild personnel who work down there are selected carefully and do not describe their work. This is exactly the kind of secret that an arthropod infiltrator could uncover.

- **Rimhalt** — the neutral ground on the Wound crater rim. The Wound Council's diplomatic venue. The Wound's chemical lake — opaque, not reflecting light normally — and whatever is at its bottom. The Council maintains that the interior is sacred and off-limits. The PC could access it.

## Main Plot Arc

### Prologue: The Prep Room (Campaign Start)

The PC arrives at the fourth building of Kast's compound. He has been "executed" — as far as anyone outside Domaric's inner circle knows, he is dead. He is here because the alternative was being dead for real.

**Key scenes:**

- The PC sees the workspace. The specimens. The tools. Kast. The accumulated evidence of decades of practice — jars without labels, modified equipment, things in various stages of various projects. The smell.

- Kast conducts a pre-procedure assessment — physical examination, questions about the PC's history, explanation of what's about to happen. This is the PC's last opportunity to ask questions with a mouth that can form words. Kast will answer honestly about the procedure. He may not answer honestly about everything.

- Domaric (or the handler) may be present briefly to confirm the commission and explain the terms: the drug, the deployment expectations, the chain of command. Then they leave. Domaric does not want to know the details of what happens in the fourth building. Kast doesn't want them in the room.

- The PC and Kast have a conversation. The biomancer is honest about the procedure — the staging, the duration, the consciousness periods, what the form will be capable of. He describes the drug dependency as a biological fact. He asks the craftsman's questions about the PC's anthro form — the specific characteristics, the sensory profile, the things the PC will lose. The interest is professional. The information is useful for the work. The subtext — that Kast is cataloguing what he is about to destroy — is present whether or not Kast acknowledges it.

**Player choices:**
- What questions does the PC ask?
- How does the PC approach this — resigned, angry, terrified, clinical?
- Does the PC try to negotiate any aspect of the transformation?
- What is the last thing the PC does with his old body?
- What does the PC notice about Kast — the self-modifications, the demeanour, the way the workspace feels?

### Act 1: The Reshaping

The transformation itself. Played through selected conscious moments — the PC is not aware for the entire procedure, but the moments of awareness are the campaign's first test of how the player inhabits the character.

**Key beats:**

- First awareness after initial restructuring — the body is wrong, the senses are changing. The anthro characteristics that defined the PC — the fur, the ears, the eyes, the claws that twenty thousand years of evolution made functional — are dissolving. What's replacing them has no reference point. Kast's voice is the only familiar thing, and even that is beginning to sound different as the auditory system is rebuilt.

- A conscious assessment phase where Kast tests cognitive function — conversation, memory, problem-solving — while the PC is mid-transformation. The biomancer is monitoring two streams simultaneously: the procedural compound work and the arcane sensing of the tissue change. The PC, if perceptive, may notice the duality — the precise hands and the stillness that suggests deep concentration of another kind entirely.

- The introduction of the drug — Kast explains the dependency as a biological fact, administers the first dose, and the PC feels the quiet euphoria for the first time. The sensation is subtle. The absence of the sensation, later, will not be.

- Completion — the PC is aware, fully transformed, inhabiting a body that bears no resemblance to what he had. The anthro form — the imperial legacy, the post-collapse adaptation, the identity built around a specific set of senses and capabilities — is gone. Replaced with something that has no face, no fur, no bilateral symmetry. Learning to move. Learning to perceive. The vibration sensing comes first — the PC feels the building through the surface he's resting on, feels Kast's footsteps, feels the hum of the compound's infrastructure. Then the chemoreception — the PC tastes the air and the air is full of information he has no framework for interpreting. The person persists. Everything else is new.

### Act 2: Early Deployment

The PC is operational. Domaric begins using him. First missions — initially in familiar territory (Laut's own infrastructure, testing capabilities) then escalating to the Graumark Confederacy's sphere.

**Key threads:**

- **Learning the form** — capabilities and limits through use. The infrastructure ecology is the classroom. First encounters with the things that live in the walls — serpents, insects, fungal zones, microbial environments. The sensory suite's theoretical advantages become practical survival skills or don't.

- **The drug cycle** — regular administration, the subtle euphoria, the awareness of dependency. The handler arrives with the dose. The relief is real. The leash is felt.

- **The handler relationship** — who manages the PC between missions, who carries the intelligence back and forth, who holds the supply.

- **Laut from inside** — the PC knows this city. He's operated from here for years. After transformation, he sees it from inside the walls, literally. The transit economy, the strangers, the information brokers — all visible from a perspective no one else has. Intelligence that Domaric didn't commission but that the PC gathers incidentally — things visible from inside the infrastructure that are invisible from the street.

- **The Rhône corridor** — deployment south toward the Basin wall, or northeast toward the Graumark. The corridor's relay stations (Beauval, Montresq, Drivenal) as operational waypoints. The infrastructure at each station as operating environment.

- **First Graumark deployment** — the Gap crossing, the entry into Confederacy territory, the first approach to one of the three warlords' strongholds. Likely the Rhine Toll Lord first — the most commercially oriented, the most accessible, the one whose intelligence value is most immediately useful to Domaric's defensive planning.

- **Infrastructure encounters** — other things living in the spaces the PC navigates. Not all of them are fauna. Escaped bioservitors, feral or cognisant, may exist in the margins of any Europan city-core's underground. The Remade Origin Framework specifies this: others have come through this process, some kept their minds, some didn't, they exist in the infrastructure. The PC may not be the only thinking thing in the walls.

### Act 3: The Crack

The control begins to show its weakness. How this happens depends on play, but possible triggers include:

- **Supply disruption** — a deployment runs long. The Rhône corridor is disrupted (Graumark raid, House Vauclain action, Basin Authority tariff dispute escalating to blockade, something from the Basin itself). The drug supply is delayed. The PC experiences early withdrawal symptoms — metabolic distress, sensory instability — and survives past when he should have died.

- **Biomancer contact** — the PC encounters another practitioner, or someone with relevant knowledge. An independent biomancer operating in the Heartland. A Khet Lineage-trained practitioner who has opinions about the dependency's pharmacology. A dwarven community in the deep ruins whose knowledge of underground biology includes things relevant to the PC's modified metabolism.

- **Self-knowledge** — the PC's own growing understanding of his biology contradicts what he was told. The form's systems are not opaque to the PC — the chemoreception, in particular, provides information about his own metabolic state. Over time, the PC may notice patterns that don't match the story of fatal dependency.

- **Kast resurfaces** — with information, a request, or a warning. The biomancer's ongoing interest in his work's performance provides a legitimate reason for contact. What Kast says — and what he doesn't say — may open the crack or widen it.

- **The Ruin Lord's complex** — the deepest deployment, the most dangerous target. The unmapped imperial-era underground. What the PC finds there may include technology or knowledge relevant to his own condition — the empire that made anthros also made the bioengineering knowledge that Kast's craft descends from. The ruins may contain information about biomantic transformation that neither Kast nor the Khet Lineages currently possess.

**This is the turning point.** The PC discovers — or begins to suspect — that the leash is not what he was told it is. What he does with that knowledge is the rest of the campaign.

### Act 4+: Open World

Per the Remade Origin Framework's post-origin structure. The PC is loose (or choosing to remain), aware of the flaw (or suspecting it), carrying a body built for a purpose he may or may not have rejected.

**Campaign hooks from the expanded setting:**

- **Domaric hunts** — methodically, not obsessively. The PC knows the warlord's operations, his intelligence network, his drops. A loose arthropod with that knowledge and assassination capability is a threat Domaric takes seriously.

- **The Graumark discovers** — if the PC's infiltration was detected, the Confederacy knows Domaric has capabilities they didn't expect. This changes the cold standoff. It may accelerate whatever the Confederacy was planning.

- **The Ruin Lord's secret** — whatever the PC found in the unmapped complex becomes a factor. If the secret is significant enough — pre-collapse technology, sealed systems, something the empire considered worth building into the deep strata — then the PC carries knowledge that multiple factions would kill for or kill to suppress.

- **The Basin** — five kilometres below the surface, the Great Basin floor is an environment where pursuit is difficult. The PC's form can navigate terrain that baseline biology cannot. The Basin Authority's extraction stations, the salt flat ecology, the pre-collapse coastal ruins exposed on the floor — all potential refuge, all carrying their own threats. The Basin interior cults — varied, in several cases not entirely human — are another variable.

- **Other escaped bioservitors** — they exist in the margins. The Remade Origin Framework specifies community dynamics: fully cognisant escapees can organise, partially cognisant ones follow routines, feral failures are unpredictable. The Europan Reaches' dense underground networks are the kind of environment where such a population could persist. The PC may find community — or may find something that was once community and is now something else.

- **The Khet Lineages** — the most significant biomancer institution on Earth, operating from the Indic Belt. If the PC's existence becomes known beyond Domaric's circle, the Lineages would have opinions. An arthropod infiltration form with full cognitive retention, commissioned under coerced consent by a warlord patron — this is exactly the kind of work that the Lineages' internal consent debate is about. The Khet lineage's restrictive position would condemn it. The Vann's permissive position would note the consent. The Alur's procedural position would want to see the paperwork. All three would want to examine the form, for different reasons.

- **Kast's agenda** — the biomancer has his own trajectory. Eleven years with Domaric, but the arthropod project was Kast's concept before it was Domaric's commission. The PC is the proof of concept for something Kast has been developing for years. If the PC escapes — if the design is validated in the field — Kast's career changes. Whether that change aligns with the PC's interests is unclear.

- **Reversal** — is it possible? At what cost? Kast could theoretically reverse the transformation, but the exhaustion cost of a second major procedure would be enormous. Another biomancer would need to understand Kast's specific work. The Khet Lineages have the institutional depth but not the motivation unless the PC offers something in return. And the question beneath the question: after months or years in the form, after learning to perceive through vibration and chemoreception, after discovering capabilities the old body never had — does the PC want reversal? Or has the form become something other than a cage?

- **The system is still running** — other patrons, other specialists, other subjects. Domaric is not the only warlord who retains a biomancer. Kast is not the only practitioner doing military-application work. The infrastructure that made the PC — the intersection of warlord power, biomantic capability, and disposable people — is not unique to Laut. The PC may encounter its products in other forms, in other territories, serving other masters.

- **The Wound** — the chemical lake at the Wound crater's centre, opaque and not reflecting light normally. The Wound Council's position that the interior is off-limits. Whatever is at the bottom. The PC's form could access it. Whether the PC should is a different question.

- **Off-planet contact** — Rogue Traders touch at Atlantic-facing ports in the Europan Reaches, primarily through Iberis. The void polities that approach from the Atlantic are different from those working the Pacific routes. If the PC reaches Iberis, the possibility of leaving Earth entirely — leaving the Reaches, leaving Domaric's sphere, leaving the setting — becomes a campaign-scale question. The Panpacifik Conclave's control of Pacific-facing contact points is another route, but farther and through more contested territory.

## Session Zero Checklist

Per the Remade Origin Framework, establish before play:

1. **Who were you before?** — Name, anthro subtype and strain history (what animal characteristics? how has the lineage changed since the empire?), background before Domaric's service. How long in the warlord's employ. What kind of work. The PC's relationship to his anthro heritage — pride, pragmatism, or the complicated awareness that the empire made his ancestors to be pretty for someone.

2. **Who bought you?** — Domaric is established. What matters is the relationship: was there trust? Respect? Pure transaction? How did the PC feel about working for someone who treats people as assets? Did the PC know about the drops, the intelligence network, the broader political position? How much of the apparatus was visible from the operative's level?

3. **What did they want?** — Established above (arthropod infiltration form). Any additional specifications the player wants to add or that Domaric would have requested. The player should understand the degree of reshaping: this is not subtle modification. The result will be completely inhuman. No face, no fur, no bilateral symmetry. The anthro form — everything it is, everything it means — will be gone.

4. **Who did the work?** — Kast is established. What does the PC notice about him? The self-modifications, the workspace, the professional demeanour, the moments of stillness that suggest the arcane component. Does the PC find Kast reassuring, unsettling, or both? What does the PC think about someone who is clearly fascinated by what they're about to do to him?

5. **What's the flaw?** — Known to the GM. The player may choose to know OOC that the withdrawal isn't lethal, or may choose to discover it in play. Discuss preference. The flaw's nature — engineering philosophy rather than conscience — may affect how the player interprets Kast's character.

6. **The failed job** — What was the mission that got the PC caught? Who was the target? Where did it happen — Graumark territory, a neutral settlement, somewhere else in the Heartland? Why did it fail? Does the PC blame Domaric? Was the PC set up, or was it genuinely bad luck?

7. **The drug** — What does Domaric call it? What does Kast call it? What does the first dose feel like? How does the PC relate to the dependency — as a biological fact, a chain, a comfort, or something more complicated?

8. **The Graumark targets** — How much does the PC know about the Confederacy? Has the PC operated against them before, in his previous life? Does the PC have any existing knowledge of the three warlords, their territories, their strengths?

## Mechanics & Rules Notes

**Transformation sequence:** Run narratively, not mechanically. The focus is on the experience, not the stats. Mechanical character creation (if applicable) happens after transformation is complete — the PC's capabilities are the form's capabilities.

**Sensory translation:** The PC's rebuilt senses provide equivalent or superior information to his old ones, but the *experience* is alien. For gameplay, the PC can still "see" and "hear" — but the player should be encouraged to describe perception in terms of the new senses (vibration through substrate, chemical signatures in the air, pressure changes in confined spaces) rather than defaulting to visual/auditory language.

**The drug cycle:** Track doses. The drug is administered every forty-eight to seventy-two hours. Missed doses should be noted. Early withdrawal symptoms begin at seventy-two-plus hours: mild discomfort, sensory instability (chemoreception becomes noisy, vibration sensing loses precision). The PC (and player) will interpret these as the onset of fatal withdrawal. They are not.

**Infiltration mechanics:** The form's access capability is the primary mechanical feature. The PC can navigate infrastructure that is physically inaccessible to unmodified people. This includes vertical surfaces, confined spaces, and structural interiors. The limitation is *time* — moving through walls is not instant — and *detection* — the PC is quiet but not silent. Vibration-sensitive organisms in the infrastructure may detect movement. Parasitic fungal colonies may be present in confined spaces. The things that live in the deep layers may respond to the PC's passage.

**Infrastructure ecology:** Track environmental threats during infiltration sequences. The PC's sensory suite provides early warning — chemoreception detects fungal spore fronts, vibration sensing detects serpent movement — but the warning is only useful if the PC acts on it. Confined spaces limit evasion options. The ecology is not background flavour; it is an active component of every infiltration.

**The Graumark Gap:** Thirty kilometres of forested upland, contested, no clear jurisdiction. Treat as wilderness with standard Europan fauna threats (pack hunters with fifty to two-hundred-plus square kilometre territories, venomous serpents in ground cover, megafauna omnivores, stinging insect nesting zones). The PC's form is optimised for infrastructure, not wilderness — the capabilities transfer imperfectly. Surface mobility is different from wall-and-vent mobility.
