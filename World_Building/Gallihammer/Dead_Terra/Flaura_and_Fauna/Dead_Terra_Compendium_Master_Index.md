### **REPTILIAN PREDATORS**

#### **Ambush Reptiles (Crocodilian Analogs)** `[HIGH]` `[Native Evolved]`
- **Status:** Present in all water-rich regions
- **File:** `Flaura_and_Fauna/Ambush_Reptiles.md`
- **Threat Level:** High (aquatic environments)
- **Primary Niche:** Semi-aquatic apex predator
- **Size Range:** 3-8 meters length
- **Behavior:** Stillness + explosive burst attack + death-roll in water
- **Bite Force:** Crushing; immediately lethal or incapacitating
- **Sensory:** Vision, lateral line detection, olfaction
- **Predation:** Large mammals at water's edge, ungulates crossing rivers
- **Mortality Risk:** Direct confrontation in water nearly invariably fatal
- **Habitat:** Any water source (rivers, lakes, coastline, swamps)
- **Territorial Behavior:** Large territories; active defense
- **Interaction:** Treat all water sources as potential kill zones; observe from elevation; test depth before crossing
- **Settlement Integration:** Raised water collection, barrier fortification, perimeter management

---

#### **Venomous Serpents** `[HIGH]` `[Native Evolved]`
- **Status:** Widespread across all terrestrial biomes
- **File:** `Flaura_and_Fauna/Venomous_Serpents.md`
- **Threat Level:** High (invisible threat)
- **Primary Niche:** Stealth predator via venom
- **Size Range:** 1-4 meters length
- **Camouflage:** Near-invisibility matching substrate
- **Venom Types:** Neurotoxic, hemotoxic, combination
- **Lethality (neurotoxic):** 40-60% mortality without intervention
- **Lethality (hemotoxic):** 60-80% mortality without intervention
- **Strike Speed:** 100-200 milliseconds
- **Detection Difficulty:** Extreme; often invisible until strike
- **Behavior:** Stealth movement, precision striking, rapid withdrawal
- **Prey:** Rodents, small mammals, insects, other reptiles
- **Settlements:** Invisible presence in sleeping areas; corpse scavenging
- **Interaction:** Systematic checking of sleeping areas, careful movement, knowledge of local species
- **Medical Response:** Antivenom availability critical for survival

### **MAMMALIAN PREDATORS**

- **Status:** Widespread in diverse biomes
- **File:** `Flaura_and_Fauna/Pack_Hunters.md`
- **Threat Level:** Moderate-High (coordinated threat)
- **Primary Niche:** Persistence predator via exhaustion
- **Pack Size:** 4-15 individuals (larger packs documented)
- **Individual Size:** 1.5-2.5m body length; 0.7-1.2m shoulder height

- **Prey Preference:** Medium-large herbivores, juveniles, elderly
- **Territorial Behavior:** 50-200+ sq km territories; boundary defense
- **Human Predation:** Rare unless isolated or prey-scarce
- **Pursuit Endurance:** Extraordinary multi-day capability
- **Pack Intelligence:** Evidence of learning, behavioral adaptation, tactical modification
- **Interaction:** Territory mapping, seasonal awareness, group defense capability
- **Settlement Coexistence:** Livestock defense, professional hunting, perimeter management
- **Tactical Response:** Fire/smoke deterrent, coordinated defense, territorial respect

### **FUNGAL THREATS**

#### **Parasitic Fungal Colonies** `[CRITICAL]` `[Bioweapon Remnant/Evolved]`
- **Status:** Epidemic zones worldwide; variable prevalence
- **File:** `Flaura_and_Fauna/Parasitic_Fungal_Colonies.md`
- **Threat Level:** Extreme (systemic biohazard)
- **Primary Niche:** Parasitic organism; behavioral modifier
- **Transmission:** Airborne spores; inhalation, wound contact, ingestion
- **Host Range:** Multi-species capable; humans, animals, plants

- **File:** `Flaura_and_Fauna/Terran_Microbial_Life.md`
- **Threat Level:** Extreme (systemic, omnipresent)
- **Primary Niche:** Competitive ecosystem; human pathogenesis
- **Composition:** Bacteria, viruses, complex ecology
- **Mutation Rate:** Higher than Earth-normal
- **Cross-Species Infection:** Frequent barrier crossing; rapid adaptation
- **Disease Types:** Respiratory, gastrointestinal, systemic, neurological

- **Seasonal Pattern:** Variable by microbe type; some show seasonal peaks

- **Individual Variation:** Substantial variation in susceptibility and resistance
- **Long-term Survival:** Knowledge and cultural practices essential; acceptance of baseline disease

## ECOSYSTEM INTERACTION MATRIX

### **Predator-Prey Relationships**

```
Formica War Ants
├─ Prey: Small insects, arthropods, corpses
├─ Predators: (None documented; too numerous/coordinated to effectively target)
└─ Competitive: Stinging insects, other arthropod predators

Megafauna Worms
├─ Prey: Medium-large surface animals, pack hunters, humans (isolated)
├─ Predators: Rare; young worms occasionally taken by pack hunters
└─ Competitive: Large reptiles, omnivores (indirect via shared prey)

Ambush Reptiles
├─ Prey: Large mammals, ungulates, humans (in water)
├─ Predators: None (apex aquatic); young potentially taken by omnivores
└─ Competitive: Pack hunters (indirect prey competition)

Pack Hunters
├─ Prey: Medium-large herbivores, small mammals, scavenging opportunities
├─ Predators: Megafauna omnivores, humans, worms (young)
└─ Competitive: Omnivores, humans (shared prey)

Megafauna Omnivores
├─ Prey: Herbivores, pack hunters (young), humans, scavenging
├─ Predators: Humans (organized hunting); rarely each other
└─ Competitive: Pack hunters, humans (shared prey and food sources)

Parasitic Fungi
├─ Prey/Hosts: All living organisms (non-discriminate)
├─ Predators: Certain microbes, some immune systems
└─ Competitive: Formica (scavenge infected corpses)

Venomous Serpents
├─ Prey: Small mammals, insects, reptiles
├─ Predators: Larger reptiles, pack hunters, omnivores
└─ Competitive: Small carnivores, birds

Stinging Insects
├─ Prey: Flying insects, small arthropods
├─ Predators: Birds, some reptiles, humans (nest removal)
└─ Competitive: Spiders, other insectivores

Humans
├─ Prey: Nearly all fauna (selective hunting)
├─ Predators: Pack hunters, omnivores, worms, ambush reptiles (isolated humans)
└─ Competitive: All predators (indirect competition for shared prey)

Microbes
├─ Hosts/Prey: All living organisms
├─ Predators: Immune systems, certain antibiotics (rare)
└─ Competitive: Other microbes (constant competition)
```
