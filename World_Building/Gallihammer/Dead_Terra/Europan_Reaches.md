# The Europan Reaches — Regional Detail

## Overview

The Europan Reaches are the most densely inhabited, most politically fractured, and most fought-over region on Earth. The reasons are geographic: the Reaches contain the highest concentration of intact city-cores anywhere in the world, sit adjacent to the Great Basin with its trade and ruin access, and occupy temperate land that is genuinely productive. People want to be here. People have always wanted to be here. The thirty thousand years since the collapse have been an uninterrupted argument about who gets to stay.

The result is a region that looks, from a distance, like chaos — dozens of warlord territories in constant flux, borders that shift with each generation, alliances that last a decade and enmities that last centuries. Up close it is more structured than it appears. The city-cores are stable anchors that outlast the warlords who claim them. The trade routes are maintained by everyone because everyone needs them. And certain geographic facts — the Basin wall, the Alpine barrier, the river systems — impose a logic on political geography that thirty thousand years of human stubbornness has not fully overridden.

## Sub-Regions

### The Shelf Plains

**What it is:** The former North Sea and southern Baltic seafloor, now exposed terrain stretching north and east from the former British coastline across to the Scandinavian fringe. Flat, mineral-rich, geologically young by local standards — the exposure happened relatively recently and the terrain still shows it. Largely treeless in the central sections. River systems from the surrounding highlands cut across it, some following ancient pre-drainage courses, some new channels carved in the last few thousand years.

**Why it matters:** The Shelf Plains are the primary agricultural zone feeding the northern Europan city-cores. The soil, though young, is extraordinarily fertile — millennia of seafloor sediment accumulation before the drainage. Whoever controls productive sections of the Shelf Plains controls the food supply for substantial populations inland.

**Political character:** No single power dominates. The Plains are divided among a patchwork of agricultural settlements, small warlord territories, and the eastern fringe of Albian cultural influence. Several larger city-states in the adjacent highlands maintain agricultural client communities on the Plains, providing protection in exchange for food production. This works until it doesn't — the Plains are periodically fought over, and the farming communities have learned to build defensible structures regardless of which patron currently claims them.

**Notable features:**
- *The Drowned Roads* — in several places, the exposed seafloor has preserved segments of pre-collapse infrastructure: pipelines, cable runs, the foundations of undersea installations. Most are structurally compromised. A few are intact enough to be entered. What is inside them varies.
- *The Salt Line* — the boundary zone where the exposed seafloor transitions from fertile sediment to mineral-heavy salt crust. Agricultural settlements stop at the Salt Line. Beyond it, nothing grows that most people would want to eat.
- *Former Dogger Bank* — a raised area of the former North Sea floor, now a low plateau above the surrounding Plains. The highest point in the Shelf zone. Several fortified settlements exist here for the visibility and defensibility. Watchtowers on the plateau can see for thirty kilometres in clear conditions.

### The Heartland

**What it is:** The interior of former Western and Central Europe — former France, Germany, the Low Countries, northern Italy. The most densely populated zone in the Reaches. Major river systems: the Rhine, the Danube (upper), the Seine, the Loire. The Alps and their foothills form the southern boundary. The terrain is varied — river valleys, forested uplands, productive plains — and the ruins layer is thicker here than anywhere else on Earth.

**Why it matters:** The Heartland contains the majority of the Reaches' city-cores and the densest concentration of warlord territories. The river systems are the primary trade arteries — goods moving between the Basin wall and the northern shelf plains pass through Heartland-controlled waterways. Whoever taxes river traffic in the Heartland is never poor.

**Political character:** Maximum fragmentation. A map of the Heartland's warlord territories at any given moment looks like a patchwork quilt assembled by someone having an argument. The city-cores are the stable points; everything between them is contested, negotiated, and renegotiated constantly. The Wound Council's Rimhalt sits in the eastern Heartland and serves as the primary diplomatic venue for disputes that don't resolve through simple violence.

**River control:** The Rhine and its tributaries are the most contested geographic features in the Reaches. Several warlords derive most of their revenue from river tolls. The communities along the river banks are accustomed to paying whoever currently claims jurisdiction, which changes often enough that they have developed sophisticated protocols for managing the transition between patrons without interrupting trade.

### The Basin Wall Zone

**What it is:** The southern fringe of the Europan Reaches, where the terrain drops toward the Great Basin. The actual descent varies by location — in some places the wall is a dramatic escarpment hundreds of metres high; in others the terrain slopes more gradually over tens of kilometres. The wall zone is where the drops are, and where the warlords who control them have built their power bases.

**Why it matters:** The drops are the only reliable way into and out of the Great Basin from the Europan side. Basin floor trade — the goods recovered from pre-collapse coastal infrastructure, the salt and mineral products from the floor's chemical landscape, the material that the Basin Authority extracts and routes upward — comes up through the drops. Anything going to Nordafrik goes down through them. The drop-controlling warlords are among the wealthiest in the Reaches regardless of the size of their territory.

**Political character:** Drop-adjacent territories are fiercely defended and frequently coveted. The warlords who hold drops tend to have more resources than their territory size would suggest, which they invest heavily in military capability. The threat of losing a drop to a rival is existential — it removes the economic foundation of the entire territory. This creates a particular kind of warlord: well-resourced, defensive in orientation, and intensely intelligence-focused, because knowing what rivals are planning is the difference between holding the drop and losing it.

**Named drops:**
- *The Frankish Drop* — the largest operational drop in the Reaches, located in the territory controlled by Veth Domaric. Two parallel descent mechanisms capable of moving significant cargo loads. Heavily fortified above and below. The primary transit point for Basin-Heartland trade in the western section.
- *The Romass Drop* — controlled by the city-state of Romass. Older infrastructure, partially functional. Slower throughput than the Frankish Drop but under more stable management. The Romass city administration treats the drop as a civic asset rather than a revenue maximisation opportunity, which makes it popular with traders who prefer predictable rates.
- *The Eastern Descent* — a series of three smaller drops in the eastern Basin wall zone, operated independently by three different warlords who have a fractious co-management agreement. Throughput is lower and reliability is worse than the major drops, but the rates are negotiable and the operators ask fewer questions about cargo.
- *The Wound Drop* — a drop adjacent to the Wound crater, managed technically by the Wound Council but operated day-to-day by a contractor faction. The proximity to the Wound's chemical environment makes it unpopular for goods that can degrade. It is popular for things that need to move quickly and discreetly.

### The Alpine Barrier

**What it is:** The Alps and their immediate foothills — the mountain range that bisects the southern Heartland and separates it from the northern Italian plain (which drops toward the Basin). The mountains themselves are not significantly changed from pre-collapse configuration — high, cold, glaciated in the upper reaches (the glaciers have retreated but not disappeared), and traversable only through specific passes.

**Why it matters:** The passes through the Alps are the primary land routes between the Heartland and the northern Italian plain. Control of a pass is control of the movement between the two zones. The cities on both sides of the major passes derive significant revenue from pass traffic and invest correspondingly in the infrastructure and security of the routes.

**Political character:** The Alpine communities are distinct from both the Heartland and the Italian plain. They have maintained relative independence across most of the post-collapse period by being difficult to reach, difficult to hold, and useful enough to both sides that conquest is less profitable than trade. Several Alpine passes are controlled by communities that have held them for thousands of years and have developed their own cultural traditions around the specific demands of that function.

### The Iberian Fringe

**What it is:** Former Spain and Portugal — the southwestern peninsula. Separated from the Heartland core by the Pyrenees, with its own distinct political character. The western coast faces the Atlantic remnant. The southern coast looks down toward the Great Basin — the former Mediterranean here has drained to create a wide, hot shelf connecting Iberia to the northern Nordafrik coast.

**Why it matters:** Iberis, the major city-core of the Iberian Fringe, is the primary Atlantic-facing port of the Europan Reaches — the main point of contact with trade from across the ocean and, occasionally, with Rogue Trader contacts that approach from the Atlantic rather than the Pacific. This gives the Fringe a different character from the interior Heartland: more outward-looking, more accustomed to dealing with unfamiliar things arriving from outside.

**Political character:** Less fractured than the Heartland. The Pyrenees create a natural separation, and the Fringe's relative isolation from Heartland politics has allowed somewhat larger and more stable territories to develop. Two major powers split influence across most of the peninsula, with the city-state of Iberis itself maintaining independence by being too valuable to both of them to be worth the cost of conquest.

---

### The Scandinavian Reach

**What it is:** Former Scandinavia — now connected to the Heartland across the Shelf Plains where the North Sea once was. The terrain is mountainous in the west (Norway's coast, now significantly different with ocean retreat), forested in the interior, and opens onto the Shelf Plains in the south. The Baltic's drainage has created new terrain eastward — formerly submerged land connecting to the Urshic sphere's western fringe.

**Why it matters:** Timber, mineral wealth from the highland geology, and access to what remains of the northern Atlantic fisheries. Not a primary political power in Heartland terms, but significant as a resource base for the powers that control the Shelf Plains and northern Heartland.

**Political character:** Confederacy-oriented, similar in structure to the Urshic nomadic confederacies but based on settled rather than nomadic communities. The terrain discourages centralisation. Major decisions require negotiation between multiple community leaders. Outside powers that want Scandinavian resources have generally found that tribute arrangements work better than occupation.

## The City-Cores

### Romass — The Eternal Ruin

**Location:** Central Italian plain, approximately 200km from the Basin wall.
**Population:** Approximately 800,000 within the city and its immediate dependent settlements.
**Government:** The Triumvirate — three elected positions representing the military, commercial, and knowledge interests of the city. Elections are held every seven years and are fiercely contested. The Triumvirate's authority is real but bounded by a council of hereditary interests that has existed in various forms for longer than the election system.

Romass is the most famous city in the Europan Reaches and possibly on Earth. It has been continuously inhabited for longer than reliable history extends, and the layers of civilisation beneath the current city are proportionally deep. The name is a corruption of what it has always been called, in various forms, across the entire period of human habitation — it is one of the few place names on Earth that has survived in recognisable form across thirty thousand years of linguistic drift.

The current city occupies the upper layers of a site that goes down at least fourteen distinct archaeological strata, each representing a previous civilisation of significant scale. The deepest accessible layers — accessible meaning that certain underground routes have been maintained, not that the descent is easy or safe — date to the empire era and possibly earlier. The layers below that have not been entered by anyone currently alive.

**What Romass controls:** The Romass Drop on the Basin wall (see above), administered as civic infrastructure. A significant agricultural hinterland that feeds the city. Trade relationships with most of the major powers in the Heartland and the Italian plain. An extensive knowledge archive — the city has been accumulating records for a very long time, and while the oldest records are fragmented and the more recent ones are not entirely reliable, the sheer volume represents one of the largest collections of historical information in the Reaches.

**The underground:** Romass has the most extensive underground network of any city in the Reaches. Seven distinct layers of underground construction have been mapped — tunnel systems built by different civilisations on top of each other, some connecting, some sealed off, some of unknown extent. The city government administers the upper three layers as civic infrastructure: water systems, sewage, storage, movement corridors. The lower four layers are officially closed. In practice, they are accessed regularly by people who know how, and the city government's enforcement of the official closure is selective enough that everyone understands the real policy is tolerance with deniability.

What lives in the lower layers is a subject of ongoing debate. What has come up from the lower layers over the years is documented, in some cases, in the city's knowledge archive. The documents describing the most significant incidents are restricted to senior Triumvirate access. Several senior Triumvirate members have declined to read them after being told what category they fall into.

**Relationship to other powers:** Romass is significant enough that most Heartland warlords prefer to maintain workable relations with it rather than attempt conquest. The city's resources, knowledge base, and the symbolic weight of its name make taking it attractive; the cost of holding it against determined resistance from a population that has been defending this site for thirty thousand years makes the calculation unfavourable. Several warlords have tried anyway. None are currently in power.

### Parisc — The Crossroads

**Location:** Northern Heartland, on the Seine river system.
**Population:** Approximately 500,000.
**Government:** The Merchant Assembly — a rotating body drawn from the major commercial houses that control trade through the city. Technically a democracy of property-holders. In practice, three commercial houses have dominated the Assembly for most of the last eight hundred years, and the current tensions within those three houses are the primary driver of Parisc politics.

Parisc sits at the confluence of several major trade routes: the Seine system connecting it eastward to the Rhine and westward to the Atlantic fringe, overland routes to the south toward the Basin wall, and the primary road network to the northern Shelf Plains. It is the Heartland's primary trading city — not the most powerful, not the most militarily capable, but the most economically central. Goods moving through the Reaches tend to pass through Parisc at some point, which means money moving through the Reaches tends to pass through Parisc at some point.

**Character:** Commercial to the core. Parisc's culture is organised around trade: the ethics of contract, the social protocols of negotiation, the elaborate rituals of commercial relationship that have developed over millennia of market activity. Violence is expensive and bad for business; Parisc resolves disputes through arbitration structures that most of the rest of the Reaches consider unnecessarily complicated and that Parisians consider self-evidently correct.

**The three houses:** House Veln (river trade and currency), House Marceau (overland freight and information), House Aldric (Basin access and Nordafrik connections). Currently in a three-way standoff over succession within House Veln following the death of the previous head without clear heir. The standoff has been ongoing for four years and is beginning to affect the Assembly's ability to make decisions. Outside parties with interests in Parisc's stability are watching carefully.

**The underground:** Parisc's underground is less dramatic than Romass but more practically useful — a functioning network of storage vaults, movement corridors, and market spaces that extend beneath most of the city's commercial district. Built over centuries, well-maintained by the commercial interests that depend on them. No sealed lower levels; what is underground in Parisc is understood and administered. This is unusual for a Heartland city-core.

### Frankenhalt — The Arsenal

**Location:** Eastern Heartland, Rhine tributary junction.
**Population:** Approximately 350,000.
**Government:** The Marshal's Council — a military hierarchy with a civilian advisory body that has genuine influence on domestic policy and no formal role in military decisions. The current Marshal has held the position for nineteen years, which is longer than average and has produced unusual stability.

Frankenhalt is the Heartland's primary military manufacturing centre. The city's pre-collapse ruins include substantial imperial-era industrial infrastructure, portions of which have been restored to partial function over the millennia — not well understood, but producing outputs that the city's craftspeople have learned to work with. Frankenhalt weapons, armour, and vehicles are the best consistently available in the Reaches. They are correspondingly expensive.

**Character:** Hierarchical, disciplined, and proud of it. Frankenhalt's cultural identity is built around its manufacturing capability and military tradition. The population is organised into formal guilds — not commercial guilds but craft guilds, each responsible for specific components of the manufacturing process. The guild structure is old enough to have acquired something like religious significance: the initiation rites for senior guild membership are conducted in the deep sections of the manufacturing complex by personnel who do not discuss what those sections contain.

**What they make:** Standard arms and armour that are visibly better than regional alternatives. Occasionally, pieces of equipment that use partially functional imperial components and are significantly better than anything else available. These latter items are rare, expensive, and the subject of intense interest from multiple parties including the Wound Council, Veth Domaric, and several factions that do not make their interest publicly known.

**The manufacturing complex:** The lower levels of Frankenhalt's primary industrial district go deep. The upper levels are fully operational and well-understood. The lower levels are where the partially functional imperial equipment is, and the guild personnel who work down there are selected carefully and do not describe their work to outsiders. There have been accidents. The details of the accidents are documented internally and not shared.

### Laut — The Warlord's Seat

**Location:** Western Heartland, on the Rhône corridor, approximately 80km from the northern Basin wall.
**Population:** Approximately 180,000 in the city and immediate surrounds; approximately 120,000 more across the territory.
**Government:** Veth Domaric, warlord. Direct administration through appointed officers. No formal council — Domaric governs by personal decision supported by a staff of advisors whose advice he takes selectively.

Laut is a mid-tier city-core — significant by regional standards, not in the same weight class as Romass or Parisc. Its importance derives from its location: the Rhône corridor connects the Heartland to the Basin wall, and Domaric's two drops sit at the terminus of that corridor. Laut is the last major settlement before the Basin wall. Everything going into the Basin from the western Heartland passes through or near Laut.

**Character:** Functional, purposeful, and not particularly interested in being more than it needs to be. Laut's cultural identity is organised around the transit economy — the city provides services to the constant flow of traders, agents, and travellers moving between the Heartland and the Basin. Inns, warehouses, contract services, information brokers, and the various enterprises that grow around them. The city's population knows how to deal with strangers, how to extract value from transient relationships, and how to stay out of business that isn't theirs.

**Domaric's apparatus:** Laut contains the operational infrastructure of Domaric's territory: his personal residence and working facility (a substantial compound in the old city centre, built on the ruins of a structure that was significant in a previous era and whose lower levels remain incompletely explored), his command staff, his intelligence operation, and Solen Kast's medical compound on the city's western outskirts.

The intelligence operation is more sophisticated than Laut's apparent scale would suggest. Domaric has invested heavily in information: a network of paid informants across the Heartland, relationships with several information brokers in Parisc, and — most recently — the arthropod infiltration asset operating from Kast's facility. The investment is driven by Domaric's clear-eyed assessment that his territory's survival depends on knowing what rivals are planning before they plan it.

**The Rhône corridor:** The road from Laut south to the Basin wall drops has been maintained continuously for most of the post-collapse period. It is the most reliable route in Domaric's territory — well-marked, regularly patrolled, and equipped with relay stations at day-travel intervals. Moving goods along it is predictable. Moving personnel along it without being noticed is difficult. This is intentional.

**Domaric's rivals:** Three significant powers border or threaten Laut's territory:
- *The Graumark Confederacy* (northeast) — a loose alliance of three warlords who have cooperated long enough to become structurally integrated. The source of the assassination attempt four years ago. Domaric knows this. The Confederacy knows he knows. Neither has made a direct move in three years, which both parties interpret as evidence that the other is planning something.
- *House Vauclain* (west) — a formally governed territory that presents itself as more civilised than warlord operations and treats Domaric's Basin drops as an unfair economic advantage it intends to correct. Currently pursuing Basin wall access through the Wound Council's arbitration process. Domaric has been quietly ensuring the arbitration goes nowhere.
- *The Basin Authority* — technically not a rival, but the relationship is complicated. The Authority controls the drop infrastructure below the wall; Domaric controls the access above it. They need each other and neither is comfortable with the dependency. Current arrangement: negotiated tariff split, reviewed annually, always contentious.

### Iberis — The Atlantic Gate

**Location:** Southwestern Iberian Peninsula, former Atlantic coastal position (now several kilometres inland with ocean retreat).
**Population:** Approximately 600,000.
**Government:** The Consulate — a dual executive (two Consuls, elected for three-year terms on staggered cycles) supported by a Senate of regional representatives. Older and more stable than most Heartland governance structures.

Iberis is the westernmost major city-core in the Reaches and the primary point of contact between the Europan sphere and the Atlantic. Ships from across the ocean arrive here. Rogue Trader contacts that approach from the Atlantic rather than the Pacific route through Iberis. The city has been managing the intersection between its own culture and whatever arrives from outside for long enough that it has developed both institutional structures for the management and a cultural sophistication about unexpected arrivals that other city-cores lack.

**Character:** Outward-facing. Iberis is curious about what arrives from the Atlantic in a way that most Heartland cities are not curious about anything. This is partly commercial — what arrives may be valuable — and partly cultural, the accumulated effect of thousands of years of dealing with the new and strange. Iberis is one of the few places in the Reaches where someone arriving with unusual physical characteristics, unusual knowledge, or an unusual background is more likely to be met with interested questions than immediate suspicion. This makes it a destination for certain kinds of people.

**The harbour:** Iberis's harbour is no longer coastal — the ocean has retreated — but the city has maintained its port infrastructure by extending it seaward as the coastline moved. The current harbour is at the end of a maintained causeway, several kilometres from the old city. The causeway is a feat of post-collapse engineering that has been extended and repaired so many times that its original construction is now essentially invisible under the accumulated modifications.

**Off-planet contact:** The Rogue Trader contacts that come through Iberis are less frequent than those in the Panpacifik but more diverse — the Atlantic approach brings vessels from different void polities than those that work the Pacific routes. Iberis maintains a dedicated contact office that has been in continuous operation for approximately two thousand years, staffed by personnel with specialised training in void-polity protocol. Their records are among the most interesting documents in the Europan Reaches, and the Consulate keeps them very carefully.

### Rimhalt — The Neutral Ground

**Location:** Eastern Heartland, on the rim of the Wound crater.
**Population:** Approximately 40,000 permanent population; significantly more during major diplomatic events.
**Government:** The Wound Council (see Factions document).

Rimhalt is not a city-core in the archaeological sense — it does not sit on the ruins of a long-inhabited pre-collapse settlement. It was built specifically for its current function, approximately three thousand years ago, by the coalition that became the Wound Council. Everything in Rimhalt is designed around its role as a neutral diplomatic venue.

**Physical character:** The city is arranged on the crater rim in a deliberate design — the residential and commercial districts on the outer slope, the diplomatic facilities on the inner rim overlooking the crater. The view from the inner rim is striking and deliberately so: the chemical lake at the Wound's centre, the glassy blast-zone terrain, the sheer scale of the impact site. Diplomats negotiating in Rimhalt are perpetually reminded that civilisations end.

**The Wound itself:** The crater interior is not accessible in the conventional sense. The chemical lake at the centre is toxic. The blast zone terrain is unstable in places. The inner wall of the crater descends several hundred metres. The Council maintains the position that the interior is a sacred site not to be disturbed. Whether this is genuine religious conviction or convenient policy to discourage investigation of whatever is at the lake's bottom is a question several parties have wanted to investigate more closely than the Council permits.

**What Rimhalt offers:** Neutral ground for negotiations that cannot happen on either party's territory. Secure lodging for delegations in transit. A functioning arbitration system with procedural legitimacy accumulated over three thousand years. Access to the Council's records — a diplomatic archive of most of the significant agreements made in the Reaches over that period. And the drop on the Wound's Basin wall side, for parties that want to conduct Basin business while in the area.

## The Political Map

### Current Major Powers (by territory size)

**Dominant:**
- Romass city-state and hinterland (Italian plain, central)
- Parisc and the Seine commercial zone
- Frankenhalt and the Rhine industrial corridor

**Significant:**
- Veth Domaric's territory (Frankish Reaches, Basin wall access)
- The Graumark Confederacy (eastern Heartland, three-warlord coalition)
- House Vauclain's territory (western Heartland, formally governed)
- The Iberian dual-power arrangement (two powers splitting the peninsula, Iberis independent between them)
- The Wound Council's sphere (Rimhalt and immediate surrounds)

**Regional:**
- Multiple smaller warlord territories filling the gaps between the above, varying in stability from well-established to actively contested

### Current Tensions

**Domaric vs. the Graumark Confederacy:** The four-year cold standoff following the assassination attempt. Both sides are gathering intelligence and positioning resources. The Confederacy has three warlords' worth of military capacity; Domaric has the drop revenue and a more sophisticated intelligence operation. The intelligence advantage matters more than it might appear — the Confederacy cannot successfully move against Domaric's drops without significant preparation, and Domaric's operation is designed specifically to detect that preparation.

**Parisc's succession crisis:** House Veln's internal dispute is drawing in outside parties. Both Frankenhalt and Romass have interests in Parisc's stability; both are quietly backing different factions within House Veln. This risks turning a commercial succession dispute into a proxy conflict between the two largest city-cores in the Reaches.

**The Basin Authority's rate dispute:** The Authority has raised transit tariffs at the Romass Drop by fifteen percent, citing infrastructure maintenance costs. Romass disputes both the necessity and the scale of the increase. The dispute has been in the Wound Council's arbitration process for eight months. The Basin Authority is applying the increase anyway pending resolution. Several Heartland traders are routing around the Romass Drop through the Eastern Descent as a result.

**The shelf plains consolidation:** A warlord in the northern Shelf Plains has absorbed three neighbouring territories over the past twelve years through a combination of military action, strategic marriage, and the kind of economic pressure that is technically not coercion. The result is the largest single territory on the Shelf Plains in several centuries. The Heartland powers bordering the Shelf are paying attention. The question of whether this consolidation represents a threat worth responding to, and if so how, has not yet produced consensus.

## Key Locations — Named Sites

**The Rhône Road:** The maintained transit route from Laut to the Frankish Drop. Best-maintained road in Domaric's territory. Day-relay stations at Beauval, Montresq, and Drivenal (the last settlement before the wall descent begins).

**The Wall Stations:** Three fortified positions on the Basin wall above the Frankish Drop — Upper Station (rim level, customs and cargo management), Mid Station (halfway down, emergency shelter and equipment cache), Lower Station (bottom of the descent, transition to the Basin floor Authority infrastructure). All three are Domaric's installations; the Lower Station shares physical space with the Authority's operation and the boundary between them is occasionally contentious.

**Kast's Compound:** Solen Kast's medical facility on the western outskirts of Laut. Official designation: a licensed medical and agricultural research facility. Three buildings publicly used for the stated purposes; a fourth building behind the main complex that is not listed on the facility's official documentation. The fourth building is where Kast does his actual work. It has a distinctive smell that the neighbourhood has learned to attribute to the agricultural research.

**The Graumark Gap:** The contested territory between Domaric's northeastern border and the Confederacy's southwestern extent. Approximately thirty kilometres of forested upland with no clear jurisdictional claim. Both sides patrol it. Neither acknowledges doing so. A number of things have happened in the Gap over the past four years that neither side has reported publicly.

**The Wound's Shore:** The accessible edge of the Wound's chemical lake — the lowest point of the crater interior that has been reached by documented expeditions. The ground here is encrusted with chemical deposits in colours that have no pre-collapse reference point. The lake itself is opaque and does not reflect light normally. The Wound Council's official position is that the Shore is off-limits to visitors. The Council's less official position is that parties with sufficient reason and appropriate discretion can be accommodated.

**The Sealed District, Romass:** The section of Romass's underground network below the fourth stratum, sealed by formal Triumvirate decree approximately six hundred years ago following an incident that the decree does not describe in detail. The physical seals are intact. Several of the access points have been reinforced within living memory, suggesting that the sealing is an ongoing maintenance concern rather than a historical decision that has since become irrelevant.

## Trade and Movement

### Primary Routes

**The Rhine Corridor:** The major north-south artery of the eastern Heartland. Rhine and its tributaries carry goods from the Shelf Plains south to Frankenhalt and the Alpine passes. The most commercially active waterway in the Reaches. Several independent tolling operations maintain infrastructure along its length with Council arbitration as the dispute resolution mechanism.

**The Western Road:** Overland route from Parisc west to the Iberian Fringe. Less commercially active than the Rhine but strategically important — the only reliable land connection to Iberis that does not go through other major powers' territory. House Marceau of Parisc maintains most of the road infrastructure as a commercial asset.

**The Basin Transit:** The overland routes from the major Heartland cities to the Basin wall drops. Converge at the wall and diverge into specific drop approaches. The approaches to the Frankish Drop and the Romass Drop are the busiest. The Eastern Descent approaches are less maintained and involve navigating the jurisdictions of three independent warlords, which adds time and negotiation to every transit.

**The Alpine Passes:** Several maintained routes through the Alpine barrier. The major passes are administered by Alpine communities that collect passage fees from both directions. The fees are not cheap and the communities are not apologetic about this.

### What Moves

**Northward (Basin to Heartland):** Salt and mineral products from the Basin floor. Pre-collapse recovered material from the coastal ruins (varied — everything from intact equipment to raw processed metal to things with no clear identification). Nordafrik goods that have come up through the drops.

**Southward (Heartland to Basin/Nordafrik):** Manufactured goods from Frankenhalt. Agricultural products from the Shelf Plains and Heartland valleys. Information, which is often the most valuable thing moving in either direction.

**East-West:** Rhine corridor goods moving between the Shelf Plains and the Alpine approaches. Iberian Atlantic trade goods moving inland. Frankenhalt weapons moving in all directions to whoever can afford them.

## Running the Europan Reaches

### Tone

The Reaches are the most "civilised" part of Dead Terra without being safe. The city-cores have urban cultures, administrative structures, commercial norms, and a patina of order. Underneath the patina: the deep ruin layers with their unknown contents, the bioweapon-organisms that have found the underground networks congenial, the cults operating in the Basin interior, and the constant low-level political violence of a region where dozens of warlords are always adjusting their positions relative to each other.

The danger in the Reaches is not usually the open wasteland danger of the Urshic Steppe. It is the danger of complex situations — of things that look one way and are another, of the moment when the city's surface civility runs out and the reality beneath it becomes visible.

### The Crawl Context

Laut and Domaric's territory are the primary home ground for *The Crawl* campaign. The city provides urban infiltration opportunities for the PC's capabilities. The Rhône corridor provides the transit infrastructure for deployment to the Graumark Confederacy's territory. The Gap is the operational boundary zone. Kast's compound is the logistical anchor.

The Graumark Confederacy's three warlords are the primary intelligence targets in the campaign's early deployment phase. Their combined territory covers the eastern Heartland approaches to the Rhine. Each holds a different asset: the first holds a fortified position on the primary Rhine toll point, the second controls the main road junction connecting the Confederacy to Frankenhalt, the third holds a ruin site of unknown significance that the other two defer to on questions of politics and strategy in a way that is not publicly explained.

The third warlord is the most important target and the most difficult to access. His residence is built into a partially explored imperial-era ruin complex whose underground sections are not mapped. The PC's capabilities are specifically suited to operating in unmapped underground terrain. This is not a coincidence — Kast knew what the targets would be when he designed the form.
