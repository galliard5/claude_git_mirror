# Imperial Relic Weapons — Specific Examples

## A Note on What These Are

The weapons described in this document are not exotic. That is the first thing to understand about them.

They were not experimental. They were not prototype systems commissioned late in the empire's history when things were already going wrong. They were standard. They were issued. They were carried by soldiers who complained about the weight of the ammunition and the calibration schedules and the maintenance requirements, in the same way that soldiers have always complained about equipment.

The empire built weapons that could do these things and then distributed them. Soldiers carried them. Tank crews loaded them, fired them, and recorded their use in maintenance logs alongside notes about tread wear and filter replacement. They were, in the empire's context, unremarkable.

Thirty thousand years later, the post-collapse world has partial access to these weapons. It cannot replicate them. It cannot fully explain the principles they operate on. It maintains the ones it has through ritual and careful engineering, and it regards them with a reverence that the people who originally carried them would find baffling. A Mechanicus magos who has spent decades reconstructing the operating principle of a single Kasaba howitzer round, who holds the reconstruction as the achievement of their career, would not have been remarkable to an imperial logistics officer who signed off on a resupply request for two hundred of them.

That gap is the horror. Not the weapons. What the weapons imply about what they were fighting.

## Mag-Acc Sidearms

**Classification:** Personal ranged — secondary carry  
**Imperial tier:** Relic (lower end — the principle is reconstructible in theory; the precision is not)  
**Availability:** Rare but not vanishingly so; several intact examples in active use across Mechanicus and void-faring communities; limited local reproduction attempts with mixed results

The magnetic accelerator sidearm — mag-acc, in every tradition that has preserved them — is a coilgun pistol. A sequential series of electromagnetic coils accelerates a ferromagnetic projectile down the barrel through precisely timed pulsed magnetic fields, each coil handing the round to the next at exactly the moment required to sustain acceleration without deceleration. No propellant charge. No combustion. No pressure vessel. No significant noise signature. No muzzle flash.

The round exits the barrel at velocities that chemical-propellant sidearms cannot approach, delivering kinetic energy to target that is proportional to mass times velocity squared and which, at mag-acc velocities, makes the mass of the round less important than would be intuitive. A mag-acc sidearm fires a small projectile at high enough velocity that the kinetic delivery at impact compares favourably to larger-calibre chemical-propellant weapons of equivalent size. The round penetrates before it transfers energy, which is a different damage mechanism from a round that is large enough to transfer energy on contact with soft tissue.

This is why they were sidearms.

This is the thing that stops practitioners who examine working examples from assuming they've found the principle wrong. The calibre is correct, the coil dimensions are correct, the operating physics are correct — and the result, a weapon that could plausibly be carried as a secondary arm by any soldier as a matter of standard issue, delivers kinetic impact that by rights should require a weapon of significantly greater size and mass. That it doesn't is a consequence of the velocity achievable by imperial coil-sequencing technology, which modern engineering can describe at the surface level and cannot replicate in the combination of miniaturisation and precision the sidearms achieve.

**The ammunition question:** Mag-acc ammunition is dense ferromagnetic material machined to tight tolerances. The tight tolerances are not incidental — coil-acceleration requires consistent geometry in the projectile for the magnetic coupling to function correctly. An out-of-tolerance round misstimes its interaction with the coil sequence, loses energy to deceleration between coils, and exits with considerably less velocity than a correctly-dimensioned projectile. Local production of mag-acc ammunition is therefore a machining quality problem as much as a materials one, and the machining quality required is at the top end of what industrial-tier manufacturing can achieve.

Communities that maintain working mag-acc sidearms also maintain strict ammunition quality standards. The rituals around ammunition inspection — measuring, weight-checking, surface examination for deformation or dimensional deviation — are among the better-preserved maintenance traditions, because the consequences of skipping them are immediate and visible. A batch of out-of-tolerance rounds is a batch of reduced-performance weapons, and every practitioner who has learned this has learned it by firing defective ammunition and comparing the result.

**What they feel like to carry and use:** The absence of noise is described consistently by practitioners who have fired them alongside chemical-propellant equivalents as initially disorienting. The weapon fires and the target responds before the operator's nervous system finishes processing the lack of expected report. Experienced operators report adapting to this quickly. Less experienced ones report shooting past their targets on the first engagement because they were waiting for noise that didn't come.

The absence of muzzle flash matters operationally. A weapon that does not announce its operator's position with a bright pulse of light and a sonic report every time it fires has advantages in conditions where concealment matters. Imperial soldiers who carried mag-acc sidearms as secondary arms presumably had those conditions in mind. What their primary arms were, in those conditions, is a question that the recovered examples do not answer.

## FLOOFL Grenades (Strange Matter–Boosted FOOF Incendiaries)

**Classification:** Infantry munition — hand-thrown or launcher-fired  
**Imperial tier:** Relic (upper range) — the base oxidiser is reconstructible in principle; weaponisation at usable scale is not; the strange matter component is not  
**Availability:** Extremely rare as complete original munitions; partial reconstruction attempts of the base compound exist; functioning originals treated as strategic assets and handled accordingly

The formal designation of this munition type has not survived in any complete document. What has survived is the name that soldiers gave it, which is an approximation of the sound it makes when it detonates — a deep, pressurised, extended ignition that practitioners who have heard it describe as a sustained exhalation rather than a sharp report. The name is FLOOFL. The acronym, when Mechanicus physik teams reconstructed it from fragmentary documentation, expands to Fluorine-Oxy-Oxy-Fluorine Liquid: a formal chemical designation for dioxygen difluoride, stabilised into a deliverable liquid state, loaded into an infantry munition.

It has been FLOOFL for as long as anyone has a record of it being called anything. The soldiers who named it were doing what soldiers do.

**What dioxygen difluoride is:** FOOF — the shorthand that Mechanicus physik teams use internally, the shorthand that the original imperial chemists would have used — is a compound in a specific category that chemistry defines by what it does rather than what it is. It is an oxidiser of extreme capability. Normal oxidisers react with things that can burn. FOOF reacts with things that cannot.

Stone burns. Metal burns. Ceramics burn. Materials that are specifically engineered to resist combustion — that are used in industrial and military applications precisely because they do not ignite — ignite. FOOF does not recognise the category of fireproof. It reacts with the molecular structure of whatever it contacts and releases the energy of that reaction as heat. The reaction is fast. The heat is significant. The materials involved are, in the ordinary sense of the word, not flammable, but the ordinary sense of the word was defined before something like FOOF was introduced as a reference point.

The reaction products include hydrogen fluoride — generated wherever FOOF contacts any hydrogen-bearing material, including biological tissue — which is separately and additionally harmful in ways that the incendiary effect does not already address. A FLOOFL detonation does not simply burn the target area. It burns it with an oxidiser that attacks the chemical identity of every material present, and the smoke from the burn is corrosive to biological tissue in its own right.

This is the base incendiary component. It is not the notable part.

**The practical problem and how the empire solved it:** FOOF is extraordinarily difficult to handle. It reacts with containment materials. It degrades rapidly. Stabilising it into a form that can be stored in a munition, survive field conditions, and deliver reliably on detonation rather than while being carried requires manufacturing precision and material science capability that the empire had and the post-collapse world largely does not. Mechanicus physik teams that have attempted to reconstruct a weaponisable FOOF formulation have produced compounds that burn with unusual aggression and that have also destroyed several workshops whose containment was not adequate.

The word "adequate" is doing significant work in that sentence.

Imperial munitions containing FOOF are double-sealed in materials that — somehow, through a technique the Mechanicus has not fully reconstructed — remain stable in contact with the compound until the detonation sequence initiates. The outer casing is something that FOOF does not immediately attack. Understanding what that material is and how it is produced is a separate Mechanicus research programme that has been running for decades with limited success.

**The strange matter boost:** Strange matter is a theoretical-to-confirmed state of matter consisting of roughly equal numbers of up, down, and strange quarks — a form of quark matter that, under specific conditions, is more thermodynamically stable than conventional nuclear matter. A strangelet — a small piece of strange matter — has, in theoretical models that have never been comfortable to examine too closely, the property of converting ordinary nuclear matter it contacts into strange matter on contact. The conversion is energetically favourable. The conversion produces energy. The conversion, if not contained or arrested, continues.

What the FLOOFL's strange matter booster does is couple the FOOF oxidation reaction energetically to the nuclear structure of the material being oxidised, rather than to its chemical bonds alone. FOOF attacks chemical bonds with extraordinary aggression. The strange matter component ensures that the oxidation event is drawing energy from deeper than the chemical level — from the nuclear matter of the target material itself. The result is an incendiary reaction that is more energetic than the volume of FOOF involved would produce through oxidation chemistry alone, burning faster, hotter, and reaching materials that even FOOF's extraordinary reactivity would not otherwise access on the timescale of a single detonation.

In field terms: a FLOOFL detonation covers the radius its size suggests. Within that radius, everything burns. Not metaphorically everything — everything. The ceramics in armour burn. The metals in structural supports burn. The reactive armour systems that are specifically designed to defeat energy-based attack contribute their own energy to the reaction when the FLOOFL reaches them. The fire-suppression systems in vehicles activate and then the FLOOFL reacts with the suppression agent. The biological tissue inside any protection that the FLOOFL also attacks burns simultaneously with the protection.

The area, after a FLOOFL detonation, is not on fire. It has been on fire and is now the residue of everything that was in it having undergone energetic oxidation. The distinction is relevant to anyone doing post-event assessment who would otherwise expect something to have survived.

**What they cannot do:** The strange matter booster does not propagate the strangelet conversion reaction uncontrolled through the environment. The FOOF oxidation provides the energy and temperature conditions for the FLOOFL's local effect; once the FOOF is exhausted, the conditions required for strangelet propagation are not maintained. FLOOFL detonations do not produce expanding spheres of converted matter. They produce contained incendiary events of exceptional violence.

This has been confirmed by examination of post-detonation sites in the deep ruins. The physical extent of the burn is consistent with a munition of the FLOOFL's approximate size. The character of the burn is consistent with an oxidiser that did not recognise the fireproof properties of the materials present. The sites look like a contained event in which everything burned. They do not look like the end of everything.

The Mechanicus documentation on the non-propagation of the strangelet reaction is among the most emphatic in any materials they have produced on imperial weaponry. Whether this emphasis reflects genuine comfort with the conclusion or a need to have it clearly stated for the benefit of practitioners who are less comfortable with it, the institution does not specify.

**The maintenance question and the handling problem:** Original FLOOFL munitions cannot be maintained in any meaningful sense. The strange matter component degrades over time in ways that are not fully understood. The FOOF stabilisation also degrades, and an intact FLOOFL munition in which the stabilisation has partially failed is a FOOF incendiary that is reacting with its own casing. This is not a failure mode that allows for gradual detection and managed response.

Working examples discovered in sealed imperial caches are handled according to assessment rituals that amount to: confirm stabilisation integrity, do not disturb the casing, decide immediately whether to use or continue storage. There is no middle option of partial maintenance.

Attempted reconstruction of the full munition has not succeeded. Attempted reconstruction of the FOOF compound alone has produced results that Mechanicus documentation describes as "effective at inducing combustion in materials rated non-combustible" and "extremely difficult to handle without incident." These partial reconstructions exist. They are not widely distributed. The workshops that produce them are treated as high-risk installations by the factions that host them, because the failure mode of a FOOF production facility is the FOOF reacting with the facility.

## Kasaba Howitzers

**Classification:** Vehicle-mounted primary armament — ground forces  
**Imperial tier:** Relic (upper end) / Imperial relic  
**Availability:** Rare; confirmed working examples number in low dozens across the setting; associated battle-shield systems are prerequisites for safe operation and their availability limits deployment further

The Kasaba howitzer is a nuclear weapon used as a tank gun.

This sentence is correct. It is not missing context that would make it less straightforward. The empire mounted nuclear weapons in tank turrets, engineered the tanks to survive firing them, and issued these tanks to ground forces as a standard heavy armour configuration.

**Operating principle:** The Casaba-Howitzer concept derives from shaped nuclear charge research — specifically, the observation that a nuclear detonation need not distribute its energy omnidirectionally if the detonation geometry is engineered to concentrate it. A standard nuclear device releases energy spherically. A shaped nuclear charge, using a dense liner material in a specific geometric configuration, directs the X-ray and thermal output of the detonation along a preferred axis, ablating and accelerating the liner material into a focused jet of plasma.

The Kasaba howitzer scales this principle into a tank-mounted weapon system. The round contains the nuclear device and the shaped charge geometry. When the round is fired and armed, the detonation occurs at or near the barrel exit, and the resulting plasma jet — moving at approximately one thousand kilometres per second in a narrow cone — is what strikes the target. The jet is not a nuclear explosion in the conventional sense. It is the focused product of a nuclear detonation: a dense, extremely fast, extremely hot column of plasma, delivering kinetic and thermal energy at a point rather than across a radius.

The effective range in atmosphere is measured in hundreds of metres, not kilometres. Plasma at one thousand kilometres per second is fast; plasma dispersing into atmospheric gas is also expanding and cooling, and the coherent jet becomes a less coherent event as the range increases. In void or near-vacuum conditions, the effective range is significantly greater. The Kasaba howitzer's primary role in ground operations is the defeat of hardened positions, heavy armour, and anything that relies on material density for protection — the jet does not need to defeat density, because it arrives faster than density-based mechanisms can respond and carries enough thermal energy to be destructive even where the physical penetration is incomplete.

**The tank survives.** This is the element that practitioners examining the system consistently find most difficult to process. The nuclear detonation occurs in the immediate vicinity of the firing platform. The tank crew is not killed. The tank is not destroyed. This requires explanation that goes beyond the geometry of the shaped charge, because even a perfectly directed detonation produces significant secondary effects — X-ray flash, thermal pulse, electromagnetic pulse — extending beyond the primary jet.

The explanation is the battle-shield system.

**Battle-shields:** Tank-mounted energy shielding systems are a prerequisite for Kasaba howitzer operation, not an optional enhancement. Without the shield, firing the weapon destroys the tank. The shield absorbs or deflects the secondary effects of the detonation that the shaped charge geometry cannot direct away from the firing platform. It also provides the platform with protection against incoming fire at a level that unshielded armour cannot approach, which is separately valuable and is the reason shield-equipped tanks sometimes remain in service even when their Kasaba armament cannot be maintained.

How the battle-shield generates and maintains its defensive field is the question that Mechanicus physik teams have expended the most effort on and achieved the least consensus about. The components involved are identifiable and their function is describable at a surface level: a power system, field emitters, a regulation system that maintains field coherence and responds to incoming energy. The underlying physics of the field itself — what it is doing to the energy it intercepts, how it maintains coherence under detonation-level thermal load, why it does not simply absorb the detonation and release the energy internally — is not explained by any currently successful model. The maintenance rituals for battle-shields are among the most elaborate preserved procedures in the Mechanicus's vehicle tradition, and among the most rigorously observed, because the consequences of a maintenance failure during Kasaba operation are self-evident.

**What firing one is like:** Accounts from practitioners who have witnessed a Kasaba howitzer firing consistently describe a perceptual sequence: the round leaving the barrel, then a brief pause shorter than the human nervous system processes as a pause, then a simultaneous event that observers in different positions describe differently depending on what their senses registered first. At close range: the detonation as light, as concussive pressure in the atmosphere, as a wave of heat that the observer's skin registers before their eyes have finished processing the flash. At medium range: a column of plasma where the target was. At extended range in vacuum: nothing visible in transit — the jet is fast enough that it is not a streak but an arrival — and then the target responding to having been struck.

Tank crews who operate Kasaba howitzers consistently describe the moment after firing as a brief period of confirmation. The tank is still there. The shields held. The round connected. Everything is fine. The matter-of-factness with which experienced crews describe this process — the practiced calm of checking the shield readout after firing a nuclear weapon at close range — is its own kind of information about what kind of institution the empire was.

## C+ Cannons

**Classification:** Naval primary armament — void forces  
**Imperial tier:** Imperial relic  
**Availability:** Working examples extremely rare; associated guidance systems (see below) often failed independently of the gun assembly and are rarer still; found primarily aboard the most intact surviving imperial vessels

The C+ cannon fires a projectile at approximately 0.99 times the speed of light. This is the opening statement of what a C+ cannon does and is, taken alone, a description of a weapon in the extreme tier of what any manufacturing civilisation can achieve. It is not the notable part of a C+ cannon.

**The round:** The standard C+ cannon slug is train-car sized — a massive iron-over-lead projectile with a xenon-filled core. The mass is intentional; at relativistic velocities, kinetic energy scales as mass times velocity squared times the relativistic factor, and a very massive round at .99c delivers a kinetic impact that compares to the yield of tactical nuclear weapons purely from momentum transfer. The xenon core serves the hyperspace interface function described below.

The manufacture of a round that can be accelerated to .99c and survive the acceleration process requires materials and construction precision that no post-collapse manufacturing can achieve. C+ cannon ammunition, where it exists, is imperial-era production. When it is expended, it is gone. Communities in possession of C+ cannons and imperial ammunition treat the rounds as strategic assets. Known stockpiles are counted individually.

**The hyperspace interface:** This is the notable part.

Each C+ cannon round contains a miniature hyperspace engine — a small device capable of transitioning the round into the warp-adjacent bands of lower hyperspace and back to realspace. The round is accelerated to .99c by the gun, then the miniature engine activates and transitions the round into hyperspace. Inside hyperspace, the round continues to move — at effective velocities that are superluminal relative to realspace — and is guided by a small guidance system toward the target. The guidance system flickers the round back to realspace briefly, acquires updated target data, and returns to hyperspace to adjust trajectory. This sequence repeats until the round is positioned to exit hyperspace at or near the target.

The practical consequence: a C+ cannon round fired from a ship cannot be intercepted by conventional point defence, because it is not in realspace long enough to be tracked and engaged. It arrives from hyperspace in the target's vicinity without having crossed the intervening realspace distance in any way that conventional sensors would register as an incoming projectile. There is no transit time to detect. There is no visible approach. There is the target, and then the round emerging from hyperspace adjacent to it, and then the kinetic impact of a train-car sized object moving at velocities that no material structure survives.

**The C++ variant:** The standard C+ cannon fires one round that makes a single hyperspace transit with corrections. The C++ cannon fires rounds programmed for multiple sequential transits. Each transit allows the guidance system to acquire additional and refined target data, correcting trajectory errors accumulated in previous legs. The successive corrections converge toward the target's actual position rather than its predicted position at time of firing.

At sufficient transit count, the final jump can place the round inside the target rather than adjacent to it. The round exits hyperspace having partially materialised within the target's physical structure, and the detonation of a train-car sized relativistic slug inside a void ship's hull is not an event from which that ship recovers.

Whether a given engagement involves this outcome depends on the number of transits the C++ round is programmed for, the quality of the guidance system's target data, and the target's own profile — a ship that presents less physical volume to emerge into is harder to achieve internal materialisation against than a very large, slow-moving structure. The C++ is not reliable in the sense that every round terminates inside its target. It is reliable in the sense that the probability of this outcome increases with programmed transit count, and that a ship in the path of a full C++ salvo from a functional imperial vessel is engaged by a weapon system that conventional point defence cannot address and that delivers its effect faster than any realspace defensive response can be initiated.

**The guidance system question:** The guidance system inside a C+ round is a small, specialised information-processing architecture. It is not sapient. The empire's prohibition on machine sapience applied to every system it produced, and the C+ guidance architecture was explicitly designed within that constraint — it is a sophisticated but bounded reactive system, not an AI. Practitioners who have examined damaged examples describe it as the most capable non-sapient information-processing system they have encountered in any recovered imperial technology, which suggests that the empire's prohibition on machine sapience did not prevent it from developing information-processing capability that approaches the boundary from below.

The guidance systems degrade independently of the gun mechanisms and the round casings. Temperature cycling, residual magnetic effects from the rounds they were never loaded into, and the general entropy of thirty thousand years produce guidance system failure even in intact-seeming rounds. A C+ cannon with viable ammunition that has guidance system failures is a C+ cannon that fires rounds which do not complete their hyperspace transit reliably — which means the round may re-enter realspace at the wrong point, continue in hyperspace indefinitely, or fail to transition at all and impact the target at .99c in the conventional manner, which is also lethal but significantly less elegant and considerably more detectable.

**Maintenance:** The C+ gun mechanism itself is maintainable through Mechanicus traditions. The round stockpiles cannot be replenished. The guidance systems inside the rounds require assessment before each engagement to confirm viability. Practitioners who have established the ritual for guidance system assessment — a process that involves specific interface protocols with the round's internal systems — describe it as the most technically demanding maintenance operation in the ship-weapons tradition, because the assessment system is checking a system that operates on principles the practitioner is not fully reconstructing.

## C++ Cannon Note

All of the above applies. The additional technical element of the C++ is the multi-transit programming — each leg of the transit sequence is individually calculated before firing, and the final leg's calculation requires accurate predictive modelling of where the target will be at the conclusion of the transit chain. The calculation load for a full C++ salvo is described in fragmentary documentation as requiring the dedicated operation of fire-control systems that have no equivalent in modern manufacturing.

Imperial vessels that carried C++ cannons also carried the fire-control computing architecture to calculate their fire solutions. These computing systems are present in surviving examples as non-sapient, extremely capable, somewhat baffling information-processing systems. In the most intact surviving examples, the fire-control system is still capable of performing the C++ calculation. In less intact examples, the computing capability has degraded below what the calculation requires, and the vessel can still fire C+ rounds but not C++ rounds with full transit programming — the rounds are fired with fewer transits, which reduces the probability of internal materialisation but not the probability of target interception.

The doctrinal note in recovered fragmentary documentation, on the use of C++ fire at short engagement ranges, is terse and suggests experience: at range below a certain threshold, the transit chain has insufficient distance to complete before the target is passed. The round exits hyperspace behind the target. The guidance system, having arrived in the wrong position, cannot correct. This is noted as a known failure mode to be managed by fire-control rather than a fundamental limitation. That it required noting suggests it had happened.

## Space String Collapse Weapons

**Classification:** Strategic — Naval or fixed installation  
**Imperial tier:** Imperial relic (upper extreme)  
**Availability:** Functioning examples unconfirmed; physical evidence of their use persists in the form of regional gravitational anomalies and what certain Mechanicus arcanum teams describe as "topological scarring" in spacetime structure at specific sites

Cosmic strings are theoretical-to-confirmed topological defects formed in the early universe — one-dimensional structures of compressed false vacuum energy, relics of phase transitions in the first moments after the universe's formation. They are extraordinarily dense. A string of GUT-scale formation carries mass-energy on the order of ten to the twenty-second kilograms per metre of length. They are under immense internal tension. They do not, under normal conditions, simply release this stored energy, because the conditions required to destabilise them are not encountered naturally in the universe as it currently exists.

Space string collapse weapons create those conditions artificially.

**What the weapon does:** The operating principle, as reconstructed from Mechanicus arcanum research on the physical anomalies left by their use, is the forced destabilisation of a pre-existing cosmic string segment. The destabilisation event releases the energy stored in the collapsed segment — the mass-energy equivalent of the string's length and density — over a very short period. For a string segment of even modest length, this release is in the range of events that rewrite regional spacetime rather than merely destroy objects within it.

The physical evidence of a string collapse event is not debris. It is distortion. Regions where a string collapse weapon was used — identified by Mechanicus arcanum teams through spacetime geometry analysis, gravitational surveys, and the characteristic pattern of electromagnetic anomalies that persist long after the event — show curvature deformations that no other known process produces. The curvature is not an impacted area. It is a fundamentally different local spacetime geometry at the site, as if the rules governing that volume of space were slightly different from the rules governing adjacent volumes. Whether this constitutes damage to spacetime or an alteration of it is a question that arcanum practitioners argue about without resolution.

**The targeting question:** Cosmic strings, where they exist, are not randomly distributed. They are ancient structures whose current positions are deterministic results of the universe's expansion since their formation. A weapon that requires a pre-existing string in the target region is therefore not a general-purpose weapon — it is a weapon that can only be used at locations where a string is present and accessible. The empire that built string collapse weapons presumably knew where the strings were, had the means to locate and confirm them, and factored their positions into operational planning.

What this means for post-collapse practitioners is that even the most intact surviving string collapse weapon is limited to use at positions where a string can be confirmed. Finding those positions without the navigational and astronomical database the empire maintained is its own problem, separate from the question of whether the weapon itself functions.

**Physical characteristics of the weapon platform:** The string collapse weapons that can be inferred from the physical evidence were not handheld. The aperture required to reach a string and initiate the destabilisation event — through whatever mechanism the empire developed — requires a physical system of significant scale. Mechanicus arcanum analysis of the anomaly sites suggests an emitter system of no less than several kilometres in active surface, which implies the weapon was either a fixed installation or mounted on something capable of carrying installations of that scale through the void. The list of candidates for the latter is short and is the same list as the candidates for the vessels that might be carrying functional C++ cannons.

**Use during the collapse:** The dead zones of Earth — the regions where the ground is wrong, where the physics is slightly off, where certain biological processes do not occur correctly and certain measurements return results that contradict the expected values — are not all the product of bioweapons and nuclear ordnance. Some of them are older than the recorded history of the collapse wars. Some of them do not have the physical signature of any weapon in any classified catalogue that any Mechanicus faction has assembled. Some of them have gravitational profiles that are not explained by the geology.

Whether string collapse events caused some of these zones, or whether the weapons were used in the collapse wars at all, is not confirmed. That the weapons existed is confirmed by their physical legacy elsewhere in the system. That humanity built them is confirmed. That humanity used them somewhere is implied by the existence of weapons at all. What they were used against, and why, is part of the empire's history that did not survive into the fragment record in any complete form.

The arcanum teams that study the dead zones do not discuss their findings widely. Several have stopped filing progress reports without explanation. The institution treats this as normal for long-term arcanum research, which produces a higher proportion of practitioners who stop filing reports than other branches. Whether this is because the research is difficult and solitary and produces people who become too absorbed to maintain institutional correspondence, or because of something specific to what the dead zones contain, is not something the institution has formally addressed.

## Reshaping Cascade Weapons (Biological Catalyst Systems)

**Classification:** Strategic biological weapon — area denial through biological restructuring  
**Imperial tier:** Imperial relic (upper extreme)  
**Availability:** Confirmed examples extremely rare; five distinct pattern-types documented; operational status unknown; detailed analysis relocated to separate documentation due to content complexity

The reshaping cascade weapon represents perhaps the most disturbing example of imperial bioengineering capability. Recovered examples are handheld devices containing what Mechanicus biomantic teams describe as "impossible catalyst compounds" — biological agents that restructure organic matter at the cellular level through mechanisms that contemporary science cannot fully explain.

What is known comes from fragmentary documentation and post-deployment site analysis. The weapons appear to have been issued to imperial forces as standard equipment, which raises questions about what the empire routinely faced that required such measures. The devices function through cascading biological processes that propagate through organic populations over a period of weeks, culminating in complete physiological restructuring of affected organisms.

**The archaeological evidence:** Five distinct weapon variants have been identified, each apparently calibrated for different operational outcomes. The biological agents involved operate through principles that blend conventional bioengineering with what Mechanicus researchers describe as "arcane-sensitive organic chemistry" — techniques that involve warp-touched materials in ways that post-collapse science cannot reproduce.

Surviving examples are mechanically intact but biologically inert. The catalyst compounds have long since degraded beyond any possibility of function. Attempts to synthesise replacement compounds have uniformly failed, often catastrophically. Several Mechanicus workshops that attempted to reconstruct the biological agents are no longer operational for reasons that those factions prefer not to discuss in detail.

**What this implies:** The reshaping cascade weapons were not experimental. Logistics maintained stockpiles. Field commanders had deployment authority. The fragmentary records suggest they were used routinely in what imperial doctrine termed "infrastructure preservation operations" — circumstances where other weapons would have damaged valuable assets that the empire wished to preserve.

The existence of these weapons as standard equipment raises the same questions as the other imperial relics: not what they could do, but what the empire faced that made them necessary. If mag-acc pistols were backup weapons, if Kasaba howitzers were standard tank armament, and if reshaping cascade weapons were routine deployment options, then what was the primary threat that required an empire to carry tools for fundamentally altering the biological identity of entire regions?

**The current question:** That no post-collapse faction has successfully recreated these weapons is simultaneously reassuring and unsettling. Reassuring, because the techniques involved died with the empire. Unsettling, because their existence suggests that the empire had operational experience that required biological restructuring on a scale that no current military doctrine addresses. What does it mean about the empire's operational environment that they built weapons designed not to destroy populations, but to transform them into something else entirely?

The Mechanicus does not speculate publicly about the implications. The weapons exist. They were standard. They were used. What they were used against, and why biological conversion was preferable to annihilation, remains a question that the archaelogical record does not answer.

## What These Weapons Together Imply

A Kasaba howitzer fired nuclear weapons as primary tank armament. C+ cannons fired relativistic rounds through hyperspace as standard naval guns. Soldiers carried FLOOFL grenades with strange matter boost as infantry munitions. Mag-acc sidearms were secondary carry — backup weapons, pulled when the primary was unavailable. Reshaping cascade weapons converted entire planetary populations into purpose-built worker castes as a routine alternative to conquest.

What was the primary weapon? What were the tanks' Kasaba howitzers supplementing? What were the soldiers' primary arms, if the secondary was a magnetic accelerator sidearm at relativistic velocity? What were the C+ cannons supporting, if they were the standard and not the exceptional naval armament? What kind of opposition required routine population conversion rather than simple military occupation?

The Mechanicus does not pursue these questions in any coordinated way. The questions are too large. The answer, if assembled from the fragments, suggests a scale of violence that the post-collapse world is not equipped to categorise, let alone replicate. The empire fought wars with these weapons, against something, and the something was either comparable to the empire in scale and capability — which means it no longer exists in recognisable form — or it was not, and the empire applied weapons of this category to threats that did not require them.

Either possibility is uncomfortable for different reasons.

The comfortable interpretation is that the empire was simply extravagant. That Kasaba howitzers were overkill against the threats they typically faced, and the empire built them because it could, and issued them because logistics preferred standardisation, and used them because soldiers use what they're given. That mag-acc sidearms were luxury equipment, and the empire's manufacturing capacity was sufficient that luxury became standard. That the C+ cannon was a prestige system that contributed to deterrence more than to actual combat effectiveness. That reshaping cascade weapons were deployed for efficiency rather than necessity.

This interpretation requires explaining the string collapse weapons. String collapse weapons are not prestige. They are not deterrence in any sense that leaves the deterred party capable of being deterred. They are an answer to a question that is very difficult to ask without confronting what kind of question it is.

The empire built weapons that could reshape spacetime. It built them. It tested them somewhere. It presumably used them, or had them ready to use. Against something, or as a threat against something, or as a hedge against something that might require them.

What that something was is a gap in the record that no amount of ruin archaeology has yet filled.
