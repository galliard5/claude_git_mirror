# Reshaping Trap Tables

**Type:** Setting-agnostic mechanical framework
**Relationship:** Mechanical companion to the Reshaping Programme Framework and Remade Origin Framework — the *environmental* version of institutional or personal reshaping
**Usable in:** Gallihammer (abandoned imperial facilities, bioweapon sites, rogue Selenar forges), Aethelmark (cursed dungeons, alchemical accident zones, enchanted ruins), any setting where a space can reshape whoever passes through it
**Designed for:** External dice rolling — the tables can be run without Claude in the loop

---

## Concept

Some places reshape people. The biomancer is gone, the institution is gone, the consent framework is gone — what remains is architecture that still performs the work. Each trap delivers a fragment of transformation: a priming compound, an alchemical catalyst, a cursed sigil's discharge, a bioactive spore. One trap is barely noticeable. Cumulative exposure is not.

This document provides the dice tables to run that cumulatively. The system tracks successful hits, escalates results through three tiers, locks the character into a branch at hit 4, commits them to a sub-form at hit 9, and completes the transformation at hit 15.

---

## Roll Procedure (Per Trap)

1. **Dud check** — roll d10. On 1–4, the trap misfires and nothing happens. On 5–10, proceed.
2. **Effect roll** — roll 2d8 on the current tier's table.
3. **Tier check** — if the result is a trait, tick the hit counter by 1. If the result is a body-plan modifier, roll d20 on the body-plan table; do *not* tick the hit counter.
4. **Milestone check** — if the hit counter reaches 4, 9, or 15, resolve the relevant milestone before the next trap.

---

## Tier Progression

| Hit count | Table rolled |
|---|---|
| 1–3 | Tier 1 — universal priming |
| **Hit 4** | **Branch lock milestone.** Dungeon type determines the branch: labour, warform, utility, recon, or stock. |
| 4–8 | Tier 2 — branch-specific generalist |
| **Hit 9** | **Sub-lock milestone.** Roll d6 on the branch's sub-lock table (or GM chooses based on accumulated traits). |
| 9–14 | Tier 3 — sub-form specialist |
| **Hit 15** | **Terminal milestone.** Roll d6 on the sub-form's silhouette table. The form completes. |

**Note on sub-lock override:** At hit 9, the GM may override the d6 roll if the character's accumulated traits clearly favour one direction. A character full of spinal reinforcement and shoulder remodel reads as hauler; one full of toxin glands and tremor-sense reads as excavator; one full of anchor points and reduced mobility reads as integrated.

## Dud Roll (Every Trap)

| d10 | result |
|---|---|
| 1–4 | **Dud.** Trap triggers but payload fails — compounds inert, field misfires, sigil discharged harmlessly. No effect. |
| 5–10 | **Hit.** Proceed to the tier effect table. |

## Body-Plan Modifier Table (d20, Universal)

Rolled whenever a tier roll lands in a body-plan slot. Does *not* count as a tier hit — these are topological shifts that happen alongside the cumulative tier progression.

**Reroll rules:**

- **Mandatory reroll** — results impossible on the current body. "Lose lower limbs" on a character already sessile; "extra arm pair" when there is physically nowhere left to put one; "gill slits" on a form whose respiratory system has been rebuilt as something incompatible. GM call — if it can't exist, it doesn't get rolled.
- **Optional reroll (player or GM choice)** — results that *contradict* or *reverse* a previous body-plan change. Lost legs, now rolled "extra leg pair." Lost arms, now rolled "extra arm pair." Either party may invoke the reroll. If both accept the contradictory result, the GM can rule the body grows the feature anyway — producing something confused and asymmetric, a partial regrowth alongside the original absence, a body visibly pulled in two directions. This is the misfire outcome, and it's thematically correct. Let it stand sometimes.
- **Second roll of the same result** — not a reroll. Escalates the feature toward full functionality.

| d20 | modifier |
|---|---|
| 1 | Extra arm pair — buds beneath shoulders or at mid-torso. Weak and partial; fully functional on second roll. |
| 2 | Lose dominant arm pair — arms reabsorb or restructure. Manipulation shifts to feet, tendrils, or emerging apparatus. |
| 3 | Extra leg pair — hindquarters extend, centauroid plan develops. Completes into quadruped or hexapod on second roll. |
| 4 | Lose lower limbs — legs fuse and restructure. Locomotion becomes serpentine, inchworm, or sessile. |
| 5 | Tail — coccygeal extension grows into a functional tail. Prehensile, balancing, or striking per branch flavour. |
| 6 | Wings bud — scapular or thoracic appendages emerge. Non-functional at first; glide-capable on second roll. |
| 7 | Dorsal ridge — spine of sensory or structural tissue along the back. Rigid, flexible, or sensory depending on branch. |
| 8 | Digitigrade conversion — ankle and foot restructure to digitigrade stance. Gait and standing posture change. |
| 9 | Prehensile tongue — tongue extends and musculates into a functional manipulator. Speech adapts. |
| 10 | Accessory eyes — additional visual organs emerge above, beside, or beneath existing eyes. Peripheral awareness expands. |
| 11 | Gill slits — respiratory slits open along neck or flanks. Dual-mode breathing; low-oxygen and aquatic tolerance. |
| 12 | Secondary feeding apparatus — additional mouth-parts: mandibular, proboscis, or beak per sub-form. |
| 13 | Thoracic restructure — ribcage reorganises. Opens, armours, or reconfigures to house additional organs. |
| 14 | Pelvic reshape — hip girdle restructures to support extra limbs, gestational load, or altered locomotion. |
| 15 | Sensory tendrils — fine filaments from face, scalp, or limbs. Vibration, chemical, or electromagnetic sensitivity. |
| 16 | Cranial restructure — skull reorganises. Muzzle extension, cranial expansion, or shifted eye placement. |
| 17 | Spinal elongation — vertebral count increases, torso lengthens. Reach, flexibility, and silhouette all change. |
| 18 | Anchor points — bone or keratin hardpoints at shoulders, spine, hips. For equipment mounting or fixed-install coupling. |
| 19 | Thoracic bisection — torso develops a midline division. Organs duplicate or relocate; fully segments on second roll. |
| 20 | Secondary head bud — second cranial structure begins developing. Subordinate; fully formed on second roll. |

## Tier 1 — Universal Priming (2d8)

Hits 1–3. Small, generic changes. Any branch is still compatible from here.

|---|---|

| 11 | Regenerative priming — minor wounds close overnight. Scarring reduces; old scars fade. |
| 12 | Pigment drift — hair, skin, or iris begins to shift colour. Irreversible; will continue with further hits. |
| 13 | Peripheral vision expansion — orbital bones shift, field of view widens. |
| 14 | Nictitating membrane — translucent secondary eyelid. Functions in dust, glare, or underwater. |
| 15 | Bone marrow shift — red marrow production increases. Bleeding clots faster; skin takes on a ruddier cast. |
| 16 | Keratin growth — nails thicken and harden, grow faster. Functional scraping or digging. Trimming becomes a chore. |

---

## Branch Lock (Hit 4)

At the fourth successful hit, the character's accumulated priming coordinates. The dungeon type determines the branch:

| Dungeon type (example) | Branch |
|---|---|
| Mine, quarry, foundry, hauling-yard, construction ruin | **Labour** |
| Armoury, garrison, kill-box, hunting preserve, combat proving-ground | **Warform** |
| Ship interior, engine-space, conduit warren, maintenance complex | **Utility** |
| Watchtower, signal-relay, sensor array, reconnaissance outpost | **Recon** |
| Breeding facility, biological factory, stockyard, flesh-rendering plant | **Stock** |

From hit 4 onward, all tier rolls go to the branch-specific table.

## Labour Sub-Lock Milestone (d6, Hit 9)

The body commits. Roll d6, or GM chooses based on accumulated traits.

| d6 | sub-lock |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Hauler** — the body commits. Spine reorganises to support larger loads; hips broaden; the head settles lower on the shoulders. The character's silhouette is now unmistakably that of something built to carry. |
| 3–4 | **Excavator** — the body commits. Chest deepens; arms shorten and thicken; eyes sink into protective sockets and the nose and ears flatten against the skull. The character's silhouette is unmistakably that of something built to move through tight spaces under the ground. |
| 5–6 | **Integrated** — the body commits. Hardpoints emerge at spine, shoulders, and hips; lower limbs thicken and shorten, trending toward a base rather than legs; fine motor capability concentrates in the upper body. The character's silhouette is unmistakably that of something built to be installed. |

Each follows the same structure as the Labour branch: Tier 2 generalist (12 trait entries with body-plan slots 2–4), sub-lock milestone (d6), three Tier 3 sub-form tables (10 trait entries each with body-plan slots 2–6), and three terminal silhouette tables (d6 each).

## Design Notes

**Why 15 hits to terminal.** With a 50% dud rate, reaching terminal takes roughly 30 trap encounters — a long campaign arc rather than a single dungeon. A party that wants to stop short can; the transformation *continues* passively for hits already seeded, but the dungeon itself is not dragging them further.

**Why body-plan mods don't count as hits.** Body-plan modifications are topological — they change the shape of the character's form rather than accumulating toward a specialisation. Not counting them as hits prevents a run of body-plan rolls from pushing a character into terminal before their form has actually coordinated. In exchange, body-plan mods become relatively *more* likely as the character progresses through tiers — the tail of the 2d8 curve widens at each step (tier 1: 1 slot; tier 2: 3 slots; tier 3: 5 slots).

**Why duds exist.** Partly pacing — a 50% hit rate lets trap-dense areas feel dense without ruining characters. Partly flavour: the premise of the framework is that whatever made this place is dead and the mechanisms are old. Half of them should fail.

**Why sub-lock is rollable but overridable.** The dungeon *is* the agent in this framework. Most of the character's transformation is being done to them by dice. But accumulated traits create a narrative weight that a pure random roll can ignore — overriding the sub-lock roll based on what the character has already become lets the GM honour the story the dice have been telling.

**Campaign note — the partial.** A character who accumulates hits and leaves the dungeon before terminal is a *partial*. The changes don't reverse on their own. The character is locked into their branch if they passed hit 4, locked into their sub-form if they passed hit 9, and the accumulated traits continue to settle and refine for weeks after exposure ends. Partials are the most common outcome of trap-architecture dungeons, and they are the framework's natural bridge to the Remade origin: someone who went into a dungeon one shape and came out another, with no one to ask for reversal.
