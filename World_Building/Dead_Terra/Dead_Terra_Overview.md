# Dead Terra — World Overview

## The World

Earth. Thirty to forty thousand years from now. The planet is still habitable — barely, in places — but it wears its history like scar tissue. Continents are blanketed in layered ruins: pre-collapse cities buried under empire-era megastructures buried under the wreckage of the wars that ended everything. The sky is wrong in places. Certain regions glow at night. Others grow nothing. Others grow things that shouldn't exist.

This is not a world that ended cleanly. It festered.

---

## The Collapse

Humanity built a stellar empire spanning dozens of systems across thousands of years. Earth was its cradle — ancient, overcrowded, symbolic. When the empire finally fractured — through overextension, internal war, resource exhaustion, or something else entirely (accounts differ, and most accounts are mythology now) — the factions that survived came home.

They came home to fight over it.

The wars that followed were not quick. They lasted generations. Orbital strikes. Engineered plagues. Wars fought with weapons nobody fully understood anymore. By the time the shooting slowed, Earth was a ruin, and the people left on it had mostly forgotten what they were fighting over.

---

## The Knowledge Problem

The empire's accumulated knowledge did not survive intact. Libraries burned. Data cores corrupted or were hoarded. The gap between what humanity once knew and what it knows now is staggering — and most people don't even know the gap exists.

Technology still functions. Ships still fly, in the hands of the powerful. Weapons still fire. Medical machines still operate, tended by people who learned the rituals of their use without understanding the principles. The difference between a technician and a priest is largely a matter of vocabulary.

Factions that control working imperial technology — or ruins of it — control territory. Knowledge of how things *actually work* is the most valuable commodity on the planet.

---

## The Horrors of the Old Wars

The empire's wars did not end cleanly. Many of the weapons they deployed were never recalled, never deactivated, never contained. Thirty thousand years later, some of them are still out there.

**Bioweapons** — engineered organisms released during the collapse wars — haunt the deep ruins, the rad-wastes, and the dark places between settlements. Most have mutated far beyond their original design parameters. Some hunt. Some spread. Some do things that have no clinical description anymore because the clinicians who would have written one are long dead. Communities build walls not just against other humans but against things that were once human, or were designed to look like it.

The worst of them are patient. They don't attack settlements — they infiltrate them. Slowly. Quietly. By the time anyone notices, the process is usually too far along to stop.

**Dead zones** exist across the planet — regions where some weapon or experiment from the collapse era is still active, still working, still doing whatever it was built to do. Some dead zones are avoided by instinct. Others are sought out by the desperate, the stupid, and the devout — because the ruins inside them sometimes contain the most intact pre-collapse technology anywhere on Earth.

## The Faiths

History became mythology somewhere around the tenth millennium. The stellar empire is now effectively a pantheon — different factions worship different aspects of it, different emperors elevated to godhood, different technologies treated as divine gifts or divine punishments.

Common religious threads across most cultures:
- **The Ascension** — the founding of the empire, treated as a creation myth
- **The Fall** — the collapse, variously blamed on hubris, betrayal, divine punishment, or enemy action
- **The Return** — a promised restoration, interpreted differently by everyone

Cults are everywhere. Many are benign — communities built around preserving old knowledge under religious framing. Others are dangerous. And some are something else entirely.

**Arcane Power is real.** Nobody agrees on what it is. Some say it's a latent human ability unlocked by millennia of mutation and radiation. Some say certain ruins leak something — energy, radiation, signal — that rewires the human mind. Some say the empire knew about it, studied it, weaponised it, and buried the results. Whatever the source, certain individuals and certain cults demonstrably do things that should not be possible. They call it different things — the Voice, the Old Fire, the Inheritance, the Wound. It manifests differently across different traditions. And it draws attention, because power always does.

The most dangerous cults are the ones that have it. Not because arcane power is inherently destructive — though it can be — but because the kind of belief system that successfully cultivates it tends to be the kind that has stopped treating other people as people.

Some cults appear to be in contact with something. What that something is remains an open question. The cults that know aren't telling.

## The Races

Thirty to forty thousand years of divergence, radiation, empire-era genetic engineering, and deliberate modification have fractured humanity into distinct strains. These are not separate species — interbreeding is possible at the margins — but the differences are real and old enough that most people treat them as simply *how things are*.

- **Humans** — the baseline, the most numerous, the most politically fractured
- **Elves** — a long-lived strain, likely the product of empire-era longevity programs; sharp senses, slow to reproduce, strong cultural memory going back further than most. Some elven communities remember things they shouldn't.
- **Dwarves** — stocky, resilient, adapted to underground environments; many dwarven communities live in the deep ruins and know them better than anyone — including what lives down there
- **Orcs** — descended from empire-era soldier-caste modifications; powerful, often marginalised, with a warrior culture built around the only legacy they were given
- **Halflings** — small, agile, remarkably adaptable; often found in the cracks between larger powers
- **Anthros** — a broad category covering humanity's spliced lines: empire-era biotech that grafted animal characteristics into human stock for specific roles. Dozens of distinct strains exist. Some are integrated into mainstream society. Others run wild in the wastes.
- **Other strains** — the gaps between categories are blurry. Mutations accumulate. New variants emerge. Not all of them are friendly. Not all of them are sane. Not all of them started as human.

## The Current Age

There is no unified power on Earth. There are warlords, city-states, tribal confederacies, religious orders, and scavenger bands. There are factions with orbital assets and factions that have never seen the sky unobstructed. There are communities that have rebuilt something worth calling civilisation, and stretches of wasteland between them where nothing holds.

The age has no name yet. That's usually a sign someone is about to give it one.

What everyone agrees on, quietly, is that something is changing. The bioweapons are moving differently than they used to. The cults are growing. The dead zones are shifting. Whether that means things are getting better or catastrophically worse depends entirely on who you ask — and whether they're still entirely human when they answer.
