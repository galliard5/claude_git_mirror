## Notes

The split between the two forms is stark enough that they almost play as different characters sharing a body. The Normal form is the detective — high Reason (40) and Psyche (40) make him one of the sharpest investigators in the room, and his Talents (Detective/Espionage, Criminology, Law) back this up mechanically. The Werewolf form is a wrecking ball who cannot think.

**Resist Domination** as a Talent is notable in a Marvel setting where mind control is common. A werewolf who's already fighting his own Berserker would have strong reasons to develop mental discipline.

**Popularity of Shift 0** for a police detective hero means either nobody knows he's the werewolf (plausible — he transforms in secret) or that the public knows and hates/fears him (also plausible for a monster). The Criminal contact suggests he has underworld sources, which fits detective work.

The 30'/100' leap distance in human/werewolf forms gives him cross-building mobility in werewolf form without flight, which is the monster's primary tactical advantage over normal movement.

Resources: Good (8) — cop salary, not rich. He funds his hero activities out of a public servant paycheck.

The enhanced senses note ("2 karma, 1 sp") at the bottom of the powers section shows the sheet was actively being modified during play — someone spent karma mid-campaign to buy that power.
