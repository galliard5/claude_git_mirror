## Relationship to Icarus

Icasus shares the same species template as Icarus (uplift dolphin, Machine Telepathy psionic, Electrokinesis, Dreamer + Imaginative quirks, TK 1) and was created about four months after him — Kai clearly liked the concept and tried it again. But this is not a revision. Different name, different gender, different birthday, dramatically different size (SM 0 vs SM+1), different TL (8 vs 10), different point total, and a completely different skill set and setting flavor. Where Icarus is a TL10 space pirate captain running heavy weapons through a drone body, Icasus is a TL8 social operator who pilots lightsails, tends bar, reads tarot, and fights with a force sword.

See `Icarus.md` for the earlier dolphin character.

## Hooks & Notes

The Low Ceiling 10' Flight limitation is the most characterful mechanical detail — she can fly, but only barely. In a TL8 setting built around ships, docks, and stations, a 10-foot ceiling covers most interior spaces. She skims along corridors and decks rather than soaring. It's exactly the kind of limitation that makes sense for a dolphin operating in built environments rather than open water or space.

The social skill suite is extensive for a 79-point character — bartending, connoisseurship (literature and wine), public speaking, politics, carousing, body language. She's been in rooms where things are decided, listening and talking. Given that dolphins Cannot Speak (Disturbing Voice to air-breathers), all of this social investment implies she communicates through alternative means: the telepathy spells, a text interface, a translation system, or simply through Charisma 1 and body language that reads across species lines.

The Force construct/ally spell with reach 20 is her solution to No Manipulators in a melee context. She creates a force construct that punches at range — an extension of herself that can hold things, operate controls, and fight. Combined with Force Sword and Mind Blade, she has a coherent close-combat approach built entirely around not having hands.

The Fortune-Telling (Tarot) skill appears on David Schalow as well. Whether this is coincidence, a shared cultural detail in Kai's setting, or Galliard's recurring personal interest in the skill is unrecorded.

Lightsail Piloting TL8 situates her in early interplanetary space — solar sails, slow burns, the kind of travel that takes weeks and requires patience. A sociable, psionic bartender who reads tarot and pilots a lightsail is a very different character from Icarus the armored pirate captain. The "rebooted" concept went somewhere genuinely distinct.
