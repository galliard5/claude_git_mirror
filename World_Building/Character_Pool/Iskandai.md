# Iskandai

**System:** Pathfinder 1e (Hero Lab .por format)
**Player:** Galli(ard)
**Alias:** Garlic Eater | **Epithet:** Garlic-slayer
**Files:** Iskandai_Worg.pdf (CR 16, 2022), Garlic_Eater.pdf (CR 60, 2023), Garlic_Eater_DRAGON.pdf + Iskandai_Dragon.pdf (CR 61, 2023)

## Identity
- **True Race:** Taninim (advanced, brutish) — draconic exemplar class (*In the Company of Dragons*, 3PP)
- **Alignment:** Lawful Neutral | **Deity:** Apsu (draconic god of good dragons — notable for a LN character)
- **Size:** Tiny as listed (1 ft, 20 lb) — this is the taninim's starting size; by 20th level Dracomorphosis scales to **Colossal**
- **Type:** Outsider (Augmented Dragon)
- **Portrait:** Blue-silver winged worg (visible on the worg sheet — this is how the campaign knows him)

## The Two Forms

Iskandai's true form is a dragon. His shapeshifted form is a winged worg — which is how he operated earlier in the campaign and how most people know him. The worg form is not a disguise exactly; it reflects a different mechanical presentation of the same entity, with the Worg racial template and monk levels expressing differently depending on which "mode" is active.

**Why "Garlic Eater":** Campaign nickname of unknown origin. "Garlic-slayer" appears as a formal epithet on the earlier worg sheet. Some early incident is implied.

## Campaign Progression Note
The three sheets represent Iskandai at different campaign stages:
1. **CR 16 worg sheet (2022):** Mid-campaign, operating primarily as a winged worg monk. Deity Apsu already established, Chosen template active, massive Intimidate (+50) and Perception (+46). The worg *is* the identity most NPCs know.
2. **CR 60 dragon sheet — worg mode on (2023):** Full draconic exemplar build. The worg mode remains viable and useful — faster, stealthier, different attack profile.
3. **CR 61 dragon sheet — worg mode off + Hunter 1 (2023):** Added Hunter for an animal companion. True dragon form fully expressed at session snapshot. The Hunter dip is the final campaign addition visible in the record.

## Hooks & Notes
The alignment tension is interesting: Apsu is the draconic god of lawful good dragons, and Iskandai is Lawful Neutral with two draconic compulsions — one requiring a Will save to commit overtly good or evil deeds (Balanced/sovereign), and one requiring a save to flee any encounter (Brave/bronze). A LN dragon who literally struggles to flee a fight and must actively resist both compassion and cruelty. The deity choice adds a further layer: worshipping a good god while being structurally neutral.

The "Garlic Eater" alias suggests the worg form was the social face of this character for most of the campaign. Whatever incident earned the "Garlic-slayer" epithet is unrecorded.

By max level the true form is Colossal — a dragon so large it can crush creatures three or more size categories smaller underfoot, and whose tail sweep clears a 30-foot half-circle automatically. The worg form provides tactical flexibility that a Colossal dragon doesn't easily have: stealth, speed, and a more approachable profile.

The Hunter 1 dip was a late addition. An animal companion for a Colossal dragon who already has telepathy 300 ft. and plane shift at will raises interesting questions about what, exactly, Iskandai chose as a companion.
