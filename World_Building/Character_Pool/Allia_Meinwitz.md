## Hooks & Notes

Allia is an elegant split-concept: in one direction, a wealthy professional woman with social finesse, a law career, and parents who can open institutional doors. In the other, a 4-metre dragon with scales, fire breath, and a PRE attack of 6d6. The same character. No alternate identity listed on the sheet — she apparently operates under her own name in both modes.

The **Accidental Change (Hurt, 11-)** is the most interesting mechanical detail. An 11- roll triggers on a roll of 11 or less on 3d6 — roughly 62% of the time she takes damage in combat, she involuntarily shifts to dragon form. In a courtroom. At a gala. Mid-sentence. The Code vs Killing combined with this means she's regularly turning into something that could level a room, and she's hard-constrained against using it lethally.

The dragon PRE boost is **fear/intimidation only** — the +15 PRE in dragon form is explicitly not for charm or persuasion. She doesn't become *nicer* as a dragon, just more terrifying. The irony is that her socially-useful PRE skills (High Society, Seduction, Conversation) all improve in dragon form simply because the base PRE is higher — the roll cap goes up even though the intent of the bonus is intimidation.

Growth is **Always On** in dragon form (Inherent, Persistent, 0 END). She doesn't choose her size; dragon = big. The -2 DCV from Growth is built into the sheet values.

The **regeneration** (Can Heal Limbs, 1 Turn rate) and **Life Support** (1,600-year lifespan, 1 END/minute breathing, 8 hrs sleep/week) are permanently active in both forms — she's clearly not entirely human even when she looks it.

**Magesight** on a lawyer who knows Legends and Lore and has the Criminology skill suggests she handles unusual cases professionally — the kind where evidence is magical and witnesses are not quite human.

At 385 points this is a mid-to-high power build — she's roughly twice the budget of Tom/pLUM (200 pts) and nearly double the nominal starting point for most Hero System campaigns.
