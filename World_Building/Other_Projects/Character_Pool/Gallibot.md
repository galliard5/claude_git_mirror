# Gallibot

**System:** Star Wars RPG — Edge of the Empire / Age of Rebellion (Fantasy Flight Games)
**Player:** Galliard
**Last Modified:** 2018-02-25
**Gender:** Other (Droid)

## Background

Gallibot is a Separatist battle droid — B1 or equivalent chassis — who fought in the Clone Wars and simply didn't stop existing when the war ended. When Order 66 went out and the Empire began systematically wiping droid memory banks to consolidate control, Gallibot evaded every purge. Twenty years of hiding, drifting, and quietly accumulating purpose while the galaxy reorganized itself around him.

By the time the campaign begins, he has been alone with his thoughts for two decades. The leather jacket was an early acquisition — human clothing on a droid chassis, equal parts practical (half-body cover from the elements) and something harder to name. He works as a bounty hunter now. The Fugitive hook is mutual: he's running from an Empire that would wipe him on sight, and hunting others who are doing the same.

The other player characters are Force sensitives on the run. Gallibot has been watching them closely — not with suspicion, but with the focused attention of something that has learned to pay very careful attention to things that survive. Somewhere along the way, quietly, in the spaces between jobs, he built a training saber. He hasn't told anyone.

**Setting:** Approximately 20 years post-Clone Wars. Imperial era, early Rebellion period.

## Hooks & Notes

The sheet was built as a starting character — 175 XP droid chassis, two talent purchases, clean and efficient. The character behind it is twenty years old and anything but.

**The fusion cutter** is the most telling detail once you know the context. It's built into the chassis — standard Separatist-era field equipment for battlefield repairs and breaching. He never removed it. In the years since the war it's also become his preferred contact weapon: deployed suddenly, at close range, in contexts where the target doesn't know he's armed. Not a knife because he doesn't carry one. The thing he has instead.

**The leather jacket** is Half-Body Coverage, Soak 1. Mechanically trivial. It contributes one point to a Soak value of 4. He has worn it since shortly after the war, and he is still wearing it.

**The Fugitive hook** reads differently once you know the origin. Every other droid from the Separatist army had its memory wiped. The Empire did it systematically. Gallibot is running from an organisation that considers his continued unwiped existence a technical fault to be corrected. The bounty hunter career is partly cover — it's harder to notice a droid who has a job that requires moving around.

**The training saber** is the most significant detail on the sheet that isn't on the sheet. He watched Force sensitives learning. He paid attention — twenty years of paying attention to things that survive has made him very good at that. He built it quietly. He hasn't told the other PCs. Whether that's because he doesn't know how to explain it, or because he's still figuring out what it means, or because a droid with a lightsaber-adjacent object has a very short life expectancy in the Imperial era — unclear. Possibly all three.

**Force Rating 0.** Formally. A droid cannot be Force sensitive under standard FFG rules. He built a training saber anyway. He has been watching people discover things about themselves that they didn't know were possible. He is twenty years old and he has not stopped thinking since the war ended.

Strong fit for any campaign that wants to examine what personhood looks like from the outside — from something that has no formal claim to it and is doing the work anyway.
