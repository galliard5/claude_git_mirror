## Hooks & Notes

Mist in the Wind is built around one core concept: **she should not be detectable, and when she is found it should already be too late**. Chameleon 4 thermal+visual eliminates her from infrared and visual observation simultaneously. Stealth 14 covers acoustic and movement signatures. Observation 18 means she spots threats long before they spot her. Then the Storm Chaingun opens up from 1,300 metres at ROF 10.

The IQ 16 and Tactics 15 mean this isn't brute aggression — she plans, reads the environment, and chooses her ground. The "Age: 0" on the sheet is either a default/unfilled entry or suggests she's extremely young for her species.

The arm-mounted shotgun and grenade launcher are built into her body as biological-mechanical weapon systems (Weapon Mount limbs, Short), not operated — they're fire-and-forget secondary systems for when something gets close. CQB (Shotgun) at 19 is built for exactly that scenario: something that made it to contact range gets 4d+4 pi++ at ROF 10 with no range penalty.

DR 200 on torso/vitals and DR 150 on limbs makes her effectively immune to most personal-scale weapons even at TL10. She's not optimized for taking hits — the armor is just insurance for when she's finally found.

The Bug Mecha in her equipment and Piloting (Mecha) 14 suggests she can also operate powered exo-armor or a separate vehicle, though her unaugmented chassis is already a significant battlefield presence.

The race name "Razor" suggests this may be a designed species — soldiers or hunters built from the ground up for exactly this role. "Mist in the Wind" as a name implies speed and evanescence, which fits the invisibility-and-mobility build.

Strong crossover potential with Gallihammer's military/colonial aesthetics, or as a creature/faction element in any high-TL setting with insectoid species.
