## Character Arc

**This is the same Gia Schalow as `Gia_Schalow.md` (Legion War Stories), at a later point in her life.**

The Legion War Stories campaign was her first war. After it ended, she left active service and retired into local civilian law enforcement — presumably within the Legion's administrative sphere ("EA police" in her Law skill). She worked as a cop for some years, acquiring the forensics, criminology, surveillance, and hand-to-hand investigation training that civilian law enforcement demands.

Then a second war started. She was called back up.

The 400pt sheet represents her at recall — carrying everything she built in LWS plus a career's worth of law enforcement experience layered on top. The age listed as 22 is a sheet artifact (it was never updated from the original template); she's canonically older in the second campaign.

See `Gia_Schalow.md` (LWS, 100pts) for her first war baseline. See `David_Schalow.md` and `Eve_Schallow.md` for other Schalow family members.

## Hooks & Notes

The 16-point investment in Explosives (Demolition) — IQ+4 — is the signature of this build. A skill of 16 means she succeeds on a 16 or less on 3d6, which is roughly 98% of the time under normal conditions. At TL11, this means she can design, place, and calibrate complex explosive systems with near-certainty. This is well past military competence and into engineer-level mastery.

The Dependency (medicine, daily) is the most significant new element versus the LWS version. At TL11, daily medication could mean anything from post-injury treatment to gene therapy maintenance to something acquired in a mission. The fact that the substance is "Common" means it's available but not trivially so in a military context. Missing a day costs HP, which in a field environment is genuinely dangerous. She plans around this.

The Telesending (hypercomm) at 30pts is a substantial investment. This is psionic communication channeled through the Legion's hypercomm network — she can send thoughts, not just speak. Combined with her surveillance and investigation skills, this suggests she's been used for covert intelligence operations where traditional communication was too risky.

The Law (EA police) skill and Forensics are not on the LWS sheet at all. She knows the legal framework she operates in and how to read a crime scene. Whether "EA police" means she worked alongside them, was one, or is simply trained to understand their procedures is unrecorded.

Basic Move 11 on a SM-1 mouse is exceptional. She's fast even by human standards, let alone relative to her size. The cloak, compact frame, and stealth of 16 together suggest someone who moves through spaces undetected, not just quickly.

The hyperdense vibro knife with (10) armor penetration means she has a close-combat option that cuts through almost anything. This isn't a utility knife — it's a precision blade for when things get close and armor shouldn't stop her.
