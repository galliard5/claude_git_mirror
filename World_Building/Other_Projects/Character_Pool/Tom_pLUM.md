## Hooks & Notes
Tom is a Hero System take on the "accidental transformation" archetype with a specific and unusual spin: the transformation is permanent, social, and deeply humiliating. Being a pony isn't a disguise or a power — it's what he is now, all the time, and his family threw him out for it.

The No Hands complication (25 pts, Very Frequently / Greatly Impairing) is the most expensive disadvantage on the sheet, reflecting how central and constant it is. The Telekinesis with Fine Manipulation is the direct mechanical answer — his TK is his hands. The Concentration limitation means using it in active combat is risky (half DCV), which creates genuine tactical tension: he needs his TK to do almost anything, but using it carefully makes him vulnerable.

The Embarrassed psychological complication at only 5 pts (Uncommon / Moderate) understates what's clearly a significant emotional reality — he's a teenager in a pony body who got kicked out of his home. The mechanical undercosting relative to the narrative weight is telling.

Running 25m vs the human base of 12m: he's notably fast for his size, as a pony would be. The high CON (20) and INT (20) reflect that whatever hit him didn't dull his mind and left him physically robust. PRE 15 is surprising for a character with an Embarrassed complication — either he's working through it, or his pony presence is genuinely imposing in ways he hasn't reconciled with.

The Killing Attack HTH is the oddest power — armor-piercing natural weapon, perceivable. Probably a horn or a sharp hoof. The decreased STUN multiplier suggests it's more about bypassing protection than knocking people out.

Strong fit for a street-level or transitional superhero campaign. The "disowned teenager transformed into a magical pony" setup has obvious pathos and clear character growth trajectory. The magic theming is loose enough to port into most fantasy-adjacent settings.
