## Scythe Claws
Two additional weapon-mount arms terminating in dedicated striking claws. Not manipulators — they're built exclusively for attack.

- **Extra Arms ×2 (Weapon Mount, -80%)** — the claw arms exist only as weapon platforms
- **Striker, Impaling (Claw):** thr+1 imp base
- **Striking ST 9 (No Fine Manipulators, One Attack Only: Claw)** — effective ST 15 on claw strikes only
- **Claw damage (effective):** 1d+1 imp at ST 15 → **1d+2 imp**

A ST 6 creature with ST 15 on claw strikes is a significant melee threat in a narrow context. The claws cannot do anything else.

## Hooks & Notes

Navigation (Hyperspace) at 16 is the identity of this character — on a 100-point build that's a mastery-level investment in one skill, and it's the least obvious choice for a small creature with scythe claws and climbing silk. Riktiki is a specialist: the ship cannot plot a hyperspace jump without them, and they know it.

The ultrasound communication with no normal speech means they're inaudible to everyone around them without translation hardware. In a mixed-species crew, this shapes every interaction — they need a translator, or everyone's reading display text. The Third Person speech quirk adds another layer: even through a translator, they say "Riktiki thinks the jump window opens in 3.2 hours."

The Cynophobia is the most specific and incongruous fear on the sheet — a highly specialized space navigator with scythe-claw arms who is specifically afraid of dogs, rolling CR 12 (fails on 11-) to avoid flinching when one appears. At TL9 in a sci-fi setting, dog-adjacent creatures or robots are entirely plausible threats. The Disowned social stigma suggests their own community rejected them, which combined with the fear of dogs may or may not be coincidence.

The scythe claws at ST 15 effective damage are a hard left turn from everything else on the sheet. They exist, they're built, they're built well. Riktiki is not defenceless at close range.
