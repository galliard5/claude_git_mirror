## Hooks & Notes
David is lighter and less physically imposing than Gia (ST 9 vs her ST 10, DX 11 vs her DX 11–14 depending on version), but his skill distribution is different in character. Where Gia is a demolitions specialist who reads situations through explosives, David's standout investment is **Camouflage at IQ+4** — one of the best skills on the sheet — combined with Stealth and Observation at Per+2. He hides, watches, and calls in what he sees.

The **Honor Guard** designation is a Legion role that comes with boosted Will and HP as free bonuses — it's not just a title but a functional status with mechanical weight. He serves the Legion directly and is called up frequently (Duty FR:12).

**Fortune-Telling (Tarot)** is the most distinctive personal detail: a soldier who reads cards. Whether this is a genuine conviction, a useful social habit for building rapport with civilians, or just something he does in camp is unrecorded. It's a 2-point skill investment on a 100-point character — he chose it deliberately.

The Guns (Light Anti-Armor Weapon) skill is a meaningful addition at his level: at 100 points it costs opportunity, which means the ability to put a man-portable anti-armor round accurately on target was worth buying. He's expected to encounter armored threats in the field.

No equipment listed on the sheet, which is unusual. Either the sheet was left incomplete at creation or his gear is campaign-managed.
