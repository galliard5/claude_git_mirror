# RAEL
**Nexagen Asset Designation:** IA-7-F3
**Cell:** Seven
**Status:** Active — Week 6 post-transformation

---

## PRE-TRANSFORMATION

**Birth name:** Rael Kovacs
**Background:** Eight years in private security, last four with Auris Security Solutions in a close-protection and threat assessment role. Competent, methodical, better at reading rooms than filing reports. Left Auris under circumstances that are summarised in her Nexagen intake file as "voluntary departure following contract dispute" and not elaborated further.

The Nexagen recruiter found her three months after she left Auris. The pitch was straightforward: the programme wanted people with field experience and threat assessment skills. The augmentation package was worth more than she'd earned in her last four years combined. The identity dissolution was presented as a feature — a clean break from whatever the contract dispute had been.

She read the contract. Most of it. She signed on page 47 and didn't reach page 94.

**What she knew going in:** That the transformation was permanent. That she would lose her hands. That she would gain augmentations worth considerably more than she could have accessed through any legitimate channel. That her legal identity would be suspended for the contract term.

**What she didn't know:** That the suppression suite would be handler-administered. That the sex outcome was variable. That page 94 existed.

---

## THE TRANSFORMATION

**Donor animal:** Female leopard. Wild-caught, held at a Nexagen-contracted facility for thirty days prior to the procedure.

**Sex outcome:** Female — female donor, no variance possible. Rael was male prior to transformation. The intake briefing covered the sex outcome possibility in the context of male donors. It did not make clear that a female donor made the outcome certain. Whether this was an oversight or a presentation choice is not something the intake coordinator addressed when she raised it afterward.

**Physical result:** A leopard in full. Pale gold coat, black rosette patterning, heavier through the shoulders than the donor — Nexagen's process tends to produce forms at the upper end of the donor's size range, a side effect of the augmentation integration. One hundred and thirty pounds. Low to the ground. Built for short explosive bursts and absolute stillness in between.

**Augmentation loadout (Cell Seven standard):**
- Vocaliser — mid-range synthetic, clear at conversational volume, carries well at distance. Sounds like her. This surprised her.
- Neural HUD — standard Integrated Assets configuration. Mission data, biometrics, comms feed. Green overlay on the upper right of her vision, all the time, including when there's nothing displayed. She's still getting used to that.
- Subdermal tracking — standard. Always on. She knows this intellectually. She has not stopped noticing it.
- Optical enhancement — low-light amplification, thermal overlay on command. Standard feline enhancement — the donor's night vision was already exceptional. Nexagen built on it.
- Scent analysis suite — chemical compound identification, trace detection. The implant interfaces with the olfactory processing the leopard body already does natively. The result is that she smells things and simultaneously receives a classification tag in the HUD. She finds this disorienting. It hasn't stopped being useful.

## CURRENT STATE — WEEK 6

**The form:** She is still learning it. Six weeks is not long. The movement is becoming instinctive in ways that occasionally alarm her — she crossed a Mid-Deck rooftop two weeks ago and didn't think about the drop until she was already on the other side. The body knows things her mind hasn't caught up with. This is useful and unnerving in roughly equal measure.

She does not think about her hands often. She thinks about specific tasks — opening things, picking things up, writing — and then doesn't think about hands directly, just the obstacle. This is probably how it's going to work.

**The suppression:** Handler-administered, every 48 hours. She does not know what happens past 48 hours. She has not wanted to find out. She has noticed that she's sharper in the 12 hours before a dose than in the 12 hours after, and has not mentioned this to her handler.

**The HUD:** She's stopped flinching at the comms feed updates. This feels like a loss she can't quite name.

**The name:** She kept it. The designation is IA-7-F3 on paperwork. In the cell she's Rael. Her handler uses the name. She hasn't decided yet whether that makes things better or worse.

## PERSONALITY

Direct. Methodical. Comfortable with silence and with violence, in that order of preference. Eight years in private security built specific habits: assess the room before entering it, know where the exits are, assume the person smiling at you has done the math already.

She is not given to sentiment but she is precise about it — when she feels something she knows what it is, even if she doesn't address it. Six weeks into a leopard's body and a corporate asset classification and she has not addressed very much.

She has a dry sense of humour that surfaces in low-stakes situations and disappears entirely in high ones. Under pressure she becomes very quiet and very efficient. People who haven't worked with her before sometimes mistake the quiet for uncertainty. This is a mistake people tend not to repeat.

**Adjusting to:** The form. The contract. The cell. The specific texture of being formally not a person while functionally being treated like one by most of the people around her, which is better than the alternative and still strange.

**Not adjusting to:** The tracking. The handler-controlled suppression schedule. The fact that page 94 exists.
