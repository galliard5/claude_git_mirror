# CELL SEVEN
**Division:** Nexagen Integrated Assets
**Status:** Active
**Base of operations:** Fourth District, Mid-Deck — repurposed HVAC maintenance facility, sub-level two

---

## HANDLER

**Petra Solís**
*Title:* Cell Seven Handler, Integrated Assets Mid-Tier Operations

Forty-one. Compact. Hair kept short for practicality rather than style. Has been with Integrated Assets for seven years, which makes her one of the longer-serving handlers in the division. The turnover rate for handlers is not something Nexagen publishes.

She came to the role from corporate security — Korrigan Dynamics, six years in their internal enforcement division, the part that handled labour disputes and whistleblower situations. She knows how institutions work and she knows how they fail, and she has made a professional decision to function within that knowledge rather than against it.

She is not unkind. She is not warm. She manages the cell with the same methodical competence she brings to the logistics paperwork and the suppression schedules — everything gets done correctly, nothing gets more investment than it requires. She genuinely believes that consistency is a form of care. She is not entirely wrong about this.

She has never told a Unit something she knew to be false. She has, on several occasions, not told a Unit something true. She has made her peace with the distinction.

**Speech quirks:** Addresses people by surname as a default — *Kovacs, Dresh, Arun.* Speaks in complete sentences, no contractions, slight pause before she answers anything she hasn't already decided. The pause is short enough that most people don't register it.

**What she knows that the cell doesn't:** The current client contract with Korrigan Dynamics has a second phase that was not in the briefing. She is waiting for confirmation from division before she presents it. She is also aware of the Auris situation — she knows Auris has a source inside Integrated Assets and she has her own suspicions about who it is. She has not raised these suspicions formally because formal suspicions require documentation and documentation has consequences she hasn't fully mapped.

## UNITS

**RAEL — IA-7-F3** *(player character)*
Female leopard. Week 6 post-transformation. Former Auris security. See character file.

---

**DRESH — IA-7-M1**
*Designation in cell:* Dresh
*Form:* Male wolf — Eurasian grey, large even by programme standards, thick through the neck and chest. The augmentation integration sits visibly on him: reinforced skeletal framework changed his gait slightly, gives him a mechanical quality at slow speeds that disappears when he moves fast.
*Background:* Former city enforcement, Mid-Deck district four — the same district the cell operates out of. He knows the streets here the way you know a place you've worked for six years: exits, rhythms, who runs what block, which checkpoints are staffed by people who care and which ones aren't. This is operationally useful and occasionally complicated — he still knows people here, and some of them still know him, and a former city enforcement officer is recognisable in ways a wolf technically isn't but practically is.
*Contract status:* Year three of five. He has stopped counting down and started counting the things he knows that he didn't know at year one.
*Role in cell:* He's become the de facto operational anchor — not because anyone assigned it but because he knows the territory and Solís trusts his read on the ground. He is aware this makes him the person new Units default to, and he is still figuring out how he feels about that.
*Relationship to Rael:* Cautious warmth. He remembers week six. He is not going to tell her everything he's learned — some of it she needs to find herself — but he is not going to let her walk into things he could have flagged either.
*Speech quirks:* Talks like someone who spent six years writing incident reports — clear, sequential, the relevant detail first. Occasional dry observation delivered in the same register as the factual content, which makes it hard to tell if he's joking until it's already landed.

**ARUN — IA-7-F2**
*Designation in cell:* Arun
*Form:* Female caracal. Smaller than Rael, considerably faster, tawny with the black ear tufts the breed is known for. The optical augmentation package is more extensive than the cell standard — long-range enhancement, spectral analysis, a secondary facial recognition overlay that runs continuous passive identification on anyone in her line of sight. The HUD is busier for her than for the others. She has adapted to this by developing an ability to hold a conversation while processing information most people would need to stop moving for.
*Background:* Not military, not security. She was a data analyst for a mid-tier financial firm — the kind of work that sits at a screen and builds models and occasionally brushes against information that firms prefer to remain internal. The programme offered her a way out of a situation that had become, in her words during the intake interview, "structurally untenable." She has not elaborated on this to the cell. Solís has her intake file. Rael and Dresh do not.
*Contract status:* Year two of five. She signed with clearer eyes than most — she read the whole contract, including page 94, before she signed page 47. She has questions she hasn't asked yet, but they're different questions than Rael's.
*Role in cell:* Intelligence and pattern analysis. She is better at reading data than people but she is also better at reading people than she presents — she simply finds it less interesting to perform the social indicators that would reveal this. She is very good at being underestimated by people who meet a small cat and draw their conclusions before she opens her mouth.
*Relationship to Rael:* Neutral-curious. She's watched six-week Units before and she finds them interesting in a clinical way. This is not as cold as it sounds — she is genuinely interested in how people adapt, and she's keeping mental notes. Whether she'll ever do anything with the notes is another question.
*Speech quirks:* Asks clarifying questions when she already knows the answer — she wants to know how you explain a thing, not just what it is. Finishes her own sentences in the middle and picks up a different thread. If she's being direct it usually means she's already processed all the versions where being indirect was more efficient and discarded them.

## BASE OF OPERATIONS

Sub-level two of a city utilities maintenance facility in the Fourth District. Officially the space is a decommissioned HVAC control room leased to a Nexagen shell company called Meridian Infrastructure Consulting. The cover is thin but functional — Nexagen maintains several similar facilities across Caldera, and the paperwork is clean enough that a casual inspection would not pursue it.

The space is functional rather than comfortable. Ceiling height accommodates the wolf form without difficulty. The sleeping arrangements are adapted — no beds, pressure-relief matting and climate regulation. A food prep station that Solís operates, since none of the Units can. A workstation she uses for briefings, with three secondary screens angled to be readable from floor level.

There is a roof access point that Rael has used twice. Solís is aware of this and has not raised it. This is either trust or data collection. Possibly both.

The Fourth District outside is known territory for Dresh and unfamiliar to the others. Mid-Deck infrastructure — elevated walkways above, the Low Streets below, the standard compression of a district that was planned for one population density and is running at two-thirds higher. The cell operates in both layers depending on the contract.

## CELL DYNAMICS

Three Units, one handler, six weeks since the cell's last composition change (Rael's addition, replacing a Unit whose contract concluded — Solís has not elaborated on the circumstances of that conclusion).

The cell is functional. It is not a family. Dresh and Arun have two years of working proximity and the specific fluency that produces — they don't need to explain their shorthand and they don't bother. Rael is six weeks into learning the language. This is the normal state for a new addition. It passes.

The thing that doesn't pass is what you learn in the learning. Rael is watching a three-year Unit and a two-year Unit navigate a life she's just started. What she takes from that observation will shape what she becomes.
