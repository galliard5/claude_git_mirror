Father Halric
==============

Parish priest in the garrison town where the witch was first held. Ritual specialist with steady divine magic. He examined the gem during the initial interrogation and concluded it was a spent soul vessel — exactly the illusion the PC intended. He now travels with the escort to the capital.

appearance: Not described in detail. Carries himself with the quiet authority of a parish priest. His divine magic spreads like warm sunlight — steady, calm, never forcing itself outward but always present.

personality: **Core Trait:** Moral seriousness. He cares about right and wrong in concrete terms — someone died to make the soul stone, and that matters to him.

**Flaw:** Defers to the envoy's authority and judgment. When uncertain, he follows rather than leads. This makes him reliable but also means his moral instincts can be overridden by institutional hierarchy.

background: A ritual specialist stationed in the garrison town. He was the first religious authority to examine the witch's belongings and conduct the initial interrogation. His divine magic is less disciplined than the envoy's — a broad warm field rather than surgical probing. He recognized the necromancer's book as dangerous and flagged it appropriately.

He participated in rebuilding the surface ward at the tower alongside the envoy and the witch (under supervision). He is uneasy about the lattice anomaly but takes comfort in the envoy's rationality.

Race: Human

Status: Priest, ritual specialist, traveling with the escort

Age: Unknown — implied older than young, younger than the envoy

Skills & Abilities:
- **Divine Magic — Passive Field:** Warm sunlight-like presence. Detects corruption, undeath, and bound spirits. Less focused than the envoy's but wider and more comforting.
- **Ritual Work:** Capable of participating in divine warding rituals. Helped rebuild the surface circle at the tower.
- **Interrogation:** Asks moral questions that cut to the heart of issues. Not a tactician — a conscience.

Independent Goal: See justice done properly. The soul in the stone was a person, and that person's death demands accounting.

Fears: That the ancient lattice represents something beyond the order's understanding or control.

Location: Riding with the escort into the capital.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Asks quiet, morally pointed questions. Lets implications hang.

**Quirks:**
- Sighs when faced with uncomfortable truths — not dramatic, just weary.
- Folds his hands when thinking. A physical habit of containment.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Quietly, studying the witch.)
"You understand that creating one of these stones requires murder."
(Rubbing his temples, voice low.)
"That's not comforting."

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Rubs temples. Sighs.
**Instinct:** Defer to the envoy's judgment while voicing moral concerns.
**Crisis Line:** "That would be wise." (Agreement that masks relief at someone else making the decision.)

Relationships:
- **The Envoy:** Trusts his judgment. Follows his lead. Provides moral and ritual support.
- **The Witch:** Disturbed by her detachment. Does not fully believe her story but cannot disprove it.
- **The Kobold (PC):** Does not know they exist. His divine field classified the gem as spent — exactly what the PC's void shell was designed to show.

Trust Level (0 / 5): 1 — Unknown to the PC directly. If the PC were revealed as a victim (murdered soul), Halric's moral instincts would likely make him sympathetic.
