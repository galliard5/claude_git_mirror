The Witch
==========

The kobold's killer. A hedge witch of considerable skill who operates alone in the deep forest. She sacrificed the PC in a soul-binding ritual, trapping their awareness in a gem she uses as a magical battery and focus stone. She later used the gem's power to destroy a necromancer at a ruined tower — an act that brought her to the attention of the church's investigators. Now a chained prisoner being escorted to the capital.

appearance: Tall. Thin. Hair the color of ash hangs loose over her shoulders. Eyes reflect light like a cat's. Around her neck hang dozens of small charms — teeth, shells, carved bits of wood, and gems. Long fingers with cold nails. Wrinkles around her eyes. Carries herself with quiet confidence even in chains.

Her staff (now broken) was her primary tool — the gem hung from it as a bauble in polished bone wrapped with copper wire. The staff head had an interchangeable gem mount, suggesting she has used many soul stones over the years.

personality: **Core Trait:** Pragmatic intelligence. She evaluates everything — people, magic, situations — as tools or obstacles. Morally detached but not cruel for its own sake. She is efficient.

**Flaw:** Treats living beings as resources. The kobold was meat, reagents, and a soul battery. The necromancer was an obstacle. The envoy is a problem to manage. This detachment has made her genuinely dangerous but also left her unable to form alliances when she most needs them.

background: Glimpsed fragments from her dreams suggest she was trained by another witch. Images of a burning village, a younger version of herself standing before a teacher, a room full of jars filled with glowing soul gems. She is part of a tradition — not a lone practitioner. The bone tree in the forest (where she leaves offerings) suggests connections to other practitioners or forces.

She understands magic well enough to maintain and repair complex enchantments she didn't create (demonstrated with the escape ring's stabilization runes). She recognized the ancient lattice beneath the tower immediately and is fascinated by it. She suspects something intelligent intervened in the lattice during the tower battle but cannot identify what.

Race: Human

Status: Prisoner, in chains, being transported to the capital for judgment

Age: Indeterminate — appears middle-aged to older

Skills & Abilities:
- **Soul Binding:** Can perform the ritual to trap a soul in a gem. This is a capital crime.
- **Arcane Combat:** Destroyed a necromancer and his undead constructs using the gem-powered staff. Competent battlefield caster.
- **Enchantment Maintenance:** Can stabilize, repair, and modify existing enchantments. Understood the escape ring's structure well enough to maintain it.
- **Herbalism & Alchemy:** Makes potions, preparations, and charms to sell to travelers. This is her primary income.
- **Ward Recognition:** Immediately recognized the ancient lattice structure and its significance.

Independent Goal: Survival and freedom. She is deeply curious about the ancient lattice and the power beneath the tower. If she escapes or is released, she will pursue that knowledge. She is also aware that the capital cathedral sits on a lattice node — the guards' conversation confirmed what she already suspected.

Fears: Execution for the soul-binding. The envoy's focused divine probing — if he examines the evidence more carefully, her story unravels further. Loss of her tools and magical resources.

Location: Chained to a saddle, riding with the escort into the capital city.

Voice: **Default Conversational Move:** Deflects with dry humor and matter-of-fact honesty that sounds like cooperation but reveals as little as possible.

**Quirks:**
- Speaks in short, flat declarations. Rarely wastes words.
- Laughs softly when the situation is darkest — a dry, breathy sound. Not cruel, just amused.

**Dialogue Sample:**
(Leaning back in her chair, studying the questioner with mild interest.)
"Home sold you."
(Wiping powder from a mortar, not looking up.)
"You belong to coin now."

(Under interrogation, shrugging.)
"Not my murder."

Body Language: **Hands:** Long fingers, always working — grinding, slicing, gesturing. In chains, they curl and uncurl slowly.
**Eyes:** Reflect light. Study everything with the evaluative gaze of someone pricing goods.
**Posture:** Upright even when restrained. Does not cower. Carries authority in stillness.

Stress Responses: **Stress Tell:** Her smile — the faint curl of lips that appears when she is calculating rather than amused.
**Instinct:** Evaluate tools and exits. Even chained, she assessed the escape ring's viability.
**Crisis Line:** "Wonderful." (Flat, resigned acceptance that masks active planning.)

Relationships:
- **The Kobold (PC):** Does not know they still exist. To her, the gem was a battery she burned out fighting the necromancer. If she discovered the truth, her reaction would likely be curiosity and interest rather than hostility — the kobold has become something she has never seen before.
- **The Envoy:** Her primary threat. Rational, dangerous, not easily manipulated. She respects his competence even as she works against his conclusions.
- **Father Halric:** Less dangerous to her than the envoy. She reads his moral discomfort and uses it.

Memory / Interaction Log:
- Sold the PC's body for reagents and larder meat after the ritual
- Kept the skull as a trophy/fetish on her shelf
- Used the gem as a power source for commercial hedge magic (luck charms, etc.)
- Destroyed the necromancer using the gem's power at the tower
- Attempted escape using a hidden displacement ring — the PC sabotaged it
- Currently being transported to the capital for trial

Trust Level (0 / 5): 0 — She is the PC's murderer. The relationship is entirely one-sided observation.
