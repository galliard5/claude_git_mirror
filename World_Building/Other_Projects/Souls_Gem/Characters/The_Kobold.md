The Kobold (PC)
================

Player character. A young kobold child sold by their village to a trader, then sold again to a witch who sacrificed them in a soul-binding ritual. Their awareness persists inside a small black gem, now carried unknowingly by a divine envoy. The kobold has no name — they were too young and too disposable for one to matter to anyone who handled them.

appearance: No physical body remains. The kobold was small even for their kind — thin arms, narrow chest, scales still soft with youth. Their skull sits on a shelf in the witch's hut. Other bones (pelvis, rib, femur) are scattered around the bone hill near the hut. In life: unremarkable, young, underfed.

The gem itself is small, black, set inside a hanging bauble of polished bone wrapped with copper wire. Currently appears inert — a spent soul stone.

personality: **Core Trait:** Patient, strategic survivor. Thinks in long timescales and small careful moves. Refuses to act rashly despite every reason to.

**Flaw:** Deeply alone. No voice, no body, no allies who know they exist. The isolation is total. Copes through obsessive focus on structure-building and knowledge acquisition, but the emotional weight of their situation is enormous and largely unprocessed.

background: From a kobold warren — clutchmates, a mother, tunnels. Sold by the village (or by someone in it) to a trader for coin. The trader transported them for days without food. The witch bought them, killed them in a ritual that same night, and bound their soul into a gem to use as a magical battery.

The kobold was never intended to survive as an aware being. The gem was meant to be a power source — nothing more. But awareness persisted. Over weeks and months of secret practice during the witch's sleeping hours, the kobold built internal structures (rings of memory and magic) that protected their core, hid their existence, and gradually expanded their capabilities.

They are now a distributed soul entity: primary anchor in the gem, secondary anchors in their scattered bones, and a growing understanding of both arcane and divine magical systems gleaned from careful observation.

Race: Kobold (deceased — soul persists)

Status: Hidden soul entity, carried by the envoy as a "spent stone"

Age: Young at death — exact age unknown, likely 8-12

Skills & Abilities:
- **Ring Architecture:** Four-ring internal structure — outer stability field, flow redirection ring, corruption filter (built from death memory), core anchor (childhood memories). Recently refined using knowledge from shrine relics and cathedral lattice engineering.
- **Telekinesis (minor):** Can move very small objects near the gem — threads, dust, tiny rune adjustments. Used to sabotage the witch's escape ring.
- **Magical Analysis:** Can read spell matrices, rune lattices, enchantment structures, and divine ward patterns through observation and proximity sensing.
- **Divine Magic (minor):** Can form thin divine threads for sensing and stabilization. Learned from observing the envoy and Father Halric.
- **Mind Touch / Dream Walking:** Can brush surface thoughts of waking minds, enter dreams of sleeping people, observe animal instincts, apply minor emotional influence. Strong minds may detect intrusion.
- **Animal Awareness:** Can temporarily ride animal minds (tested: fox, horse, hawk, crow). Crows are easiest — curious minds with good memory.
- **Soul Anchor Network:** Distributed soul structure across gem (primary) and bones (secondary). Can shift awareness between anchors. Skull holds identity, pelvis provides structural stability, rib carries life resonance, femur holds movement resonance.
- **Lattice Interaction:** Can perceive and interact with the ancient divine lattice beneath the land. Successfully realigned a damaged strand beneath the tower. Awakened a dormant stabilizing node at the bone hill.
- **Void Shell:** Defensive disguise making the gem appear as a spent soul vessel. Fools both arcane and divine detection.

Independent Goal: Survival first. Independence from the gem eventually. Understanding what they have become and what they might become. The ancient lattice and the sleeping presence beneath the reservoir represent something vast and unknown — a potential path to a form of existence beyond the gem.

Fears: Gem destruction before the anchor network can sustain independent existence. Discovery by the envoy (whose purging strike could destroy the soul). The witch reclaiming the gem and draining it dry. The deep presence beneath the reservoir — acknowledged but not understood.

Location: Inside the gem, in the envoy's saddle pouch. Just entered the capital city gates.
