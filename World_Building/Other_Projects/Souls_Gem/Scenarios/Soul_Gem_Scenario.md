# Soul Gem Campaign

**Setting:** Unnamed kingdom — medieval fantasy with institutional divine magic, arcane practitioners, and an ancient underground lattice system predating current civilization
**Status:** Ongoing — active play
**Tone:** Slow-burn survival horror from the inside. The PC has no body, no voice, no allies. Every gain is incremental. Every risk could end them.

---

## Overview

A young kobold child is sold by their village to a trader, then sold again to a hedge witch who sacrifices them in a soul-binding ritual. Their awareness persists inside a small black gem — trapped, silent, and used as a magical battery. Over weeks and months of secret practice, the kobold builds internal defenses, develops capabilities, and begins to understand the magical systems around them.

When the witch travels north to confront a necromancer at a ruined tower, the resulting battle burns out the gem's outer layers — but the kobold's hidden core survives. The witch is captured by a divine order investigator (the envoy), and the gem is taken as evidence. Everyone believes the soul inside is gone.

The kobold now rides silently in the envoy's saddle pouch, studying divine magic, reading minds, riding animals, and discovering an ancient lattice system buried beneath the land. They have awakened a dormant lattice node and are approaching the kingdom's capital — the most complex and dangerous environment they have ever faced.

**Hook:** The PC begins as a powerless victim and must build every capability from nothing. The central tension is between growing power and the absolute necessity of remaining hidden.

**Core Conflict:** Survival, identity, and the question of what the PC is becoming. They are no longer a living kobold. They are not a ghost, not undead, not a traditional magical entity. They are something new — a distributed soul awareness that is slowly integrating with an ancient magical infrastructure no one fully understands.

## Active Threads

1. **Arrival at the Capital** — The escort is entering the city. The witch will be delivered for judgment. The gem and the necromancer's book will be examined by the order.
2. **The Cathedral Lattice Node** — A major anchor in the ancient system, heavily reinforced by centuries of church construction. The PC has scouted it from a crow. The engineers beneath the cathedral may be the first people who could detect the PC's awakened hill node.
3. **The Witch's Trial** — She murdered someone to create the soul stone. The envoy has evidence (the memory echo from the gem). But she also destroyed the necromancer. "Two truths can exist at once."
4. **The Lattice Anomaly** — The envoy knows something changed the lattice before they rebuilt the surface ward. He does not know what. This mystery will drive the order's investigation.
5. **The Deep Presence** — Something vast and ancient sleeps beneath the reservoir, below the lattice. It has acknowledged the lattice changes but has not acted. Its nature is unknown.
6. **The Bone Hill Node** — The PC's awakened lattice node continues redistributing pressure. If the cathedral engineers study lattice flows carefully, they might notice the new node.
7. **Independence from the Gem** — The PC's long-term goal. The bone anchor network may eventually support independent existence if the gem is destroyed or lost.
8. **The Order's Relic Vaults** — May contain artifacts and knowledge related to the ancient lattice system.

## World Lore

**The Ancient Lattice:** A network of divine architecture built into the bedrock across the kingdom, predating the current civilization and the church. Its purpose is to distribute and contain the pressure of vast magical reservoirs buried deep underground. The lattice uses stabilizing nodes (surface anchor points — old standing stones, ruins, temples) connected by underground channels. The church built the cathedral on top of one of the major nodes, and engineers have been working beneath it for years. The lattice was not created by the current order — whoever built it had greater resources.

**Soul Binding:** A forbidden practice. The witch's tradition involves trapping a soul in a gem to use as a magical battery and focus stone. The interchangeable mount on the witch's staff suggests she has used many such stones. Creating one requires murder — a capital crime. The soul is not intended to remain aware; the kobold's persistence is anomalous.

**Divine Magic:** Institutional and trained. Three observed layers: passive detection field (warm, broad), focused probing thread (surgical, reads magical structures), purging strike (heavy, destructive, held in reserve). Divine magic detects undeath, corruption, trapped souls, and sacred resonance. The PC's void shell disguises the gem as a fourth category: spent soul vessel.

## Campaign Notes

- No in-game calendar has been established. Time is tracked loosely (days of travel, nights of practice).
- No names have been given to the kingdom, the capital city, the garrison town, or the shrine town. The PC and all major NPCs are unnamed.
- The PC's progression follows the Emergent Skill Tree System principles — capabilities develop through use and narrative gates, not pre-set advancement.
- The Essence archetype would initialize here: the PC's soul-binding and gem existence is the transformation event. Branches might include Ring Architecture, Lattice Attunement, Mind Walking, and Anchor Network.
- The tone is intimate and slow. The PC experiences the world through pressure, vibration, magical resonance, and borrowed senses. Every scene is filtered through the gem's perspective.
