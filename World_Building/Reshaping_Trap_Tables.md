# Reshaping Trap Tables

## Overview

A table system for dungeons, ruins, or contaminated sites where the environment reshapes the intruder. Each trap is a dose: priming compounds, bioactive agents, exposure to fields or atmospheres, alchemical spills, arcane residue. One trap is a mild change. Accumulated exposure produces progressively more coherent transformation until the character is locked into a new form.

The tables are setting-agnostic. The fiction around them varies:

- **Gallihammer** — abandoned imperial reshaping facility, bioweapon spill site, archaeotech crèche still running after ten thousand years
- **Aethelmark** — corrupted wizard's tower, alchemist's laboratory where the wards have failed, a ruin built on top of something older that is still leaking through
- **Any setting** — freak alchemical accident, planar contamination, cursed artefact, any environmental reshaping event that stacks over time

Intended for external play — dice at the table, GM tracking hit count between sessions.

---

## How It Works

### Per Trap — The Roll Flow

1. **Dud check (d10).** 1–4: dud, trap fires but nothing takes. 5–10: hit, proceed.
2. **Effect roll (2d8).** Roll on the table for the character's current tier. Low rolls (2 through a tier-dependent threshold) redirect to the body-plan modifier table.
3. **Tick the hit count.** Duds do not count as hits. Body-plan modifiers do not count as hits. Only trait-table results from the tier tables count.

### Tier Progression

| Hit count | Tier | Body-plan slots |
|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | Tier 1 — universal priming | roll of 2 only (1 slot) |
| 4–8 | Tier 2 — branch generalist | rolls 2–4 (3 slots) |
| 9–14 | Tier 3 — sub-form specialist | rolls 2–6 (5 slots) |

- **Hit 4 — Branch Lock.** Character's body commits to one branch (Labour / Warform / Utility / Recon / Stock). Default: determined by the dungeon/site type. Alternatives: GM call, or 1d5 if fully random. Roll 3d20 on the body-plan modifier table — player or GM picks the result that best fits the emerging branch (standard reroll rules still apply). Does not count as a hit.
- **Hit 9 — Sub-Form Lock (Milestone 1).** The branch commits to one of three sub-forms. Determined by dungeon type, GM call, or 1d3. Roll 2d20 on the body-plan modifier table — player or GM picks the result that best fits the emerging sub-form (standard reroll rules still apply). Then roll on the sub-form's lock-in table for narrative flavour.
- **Hit 15 — Terminal (Milestone 2).** Final body-plan shift. Character is now functionally locked into the completed form. Roll for silhouette variant.

### Body-Plan Modifier — When Rolled

Redirects from the main tier table when the roll lands in a body-plan slot. Rolled on **d20**. Does not count as a hit.

**Reroll rules:**
- **Mandatory reroll** — results impossible on the current body. Losing lower limbs when the character is already sessile, growing extra arm pairs when there's nowhere left to put them, gill slits on a form whose respiratory structure has been rebuilt as incompatible. GM call; if it cannot exist, it does not get rolled.
- **Optional reroll (player or GM choice)** — results that contradict or reverse a previous body-plan change. Lost legs and now rolled "extra leg pair." Lost arms and now rolled "extra arm pair." A thoracic restructure that would undo a previous thoracic restructure. Either party can invoke the reroll; if both accept it, the GM can rule that the body goes ahead and grows the contradictory feature — resulting in something confused and asymmetric, a partial regrowth alongside the original absence, a body pulled in two directions. Let it stand sometimes. The misfire outcome is thematically valid.
- **Second roll of the same body-plan result** — not a reroll. Escalates the feature toward full functionality (see individual entries).

### Dud Rate — Setting Variant

The base 40% dud rate (1–4 on d10) is tuned for Gallihammer's "imperial stuff is old and broken" flavour. For settings where the reshaping source is fresh or actively maintained — a functioning wizard's laboratory, a recently triggered spill, an artefact working as intended — drop the dud range to 1–2 (20%). For settings where the source is extremely degraded, raise it to 1–6 (60%).

## Labour Branch

Default for dungeons whose original purpose involved **sustained physical work in hostile conditions** — mines, heavy industry, deep-ruin salvage sites, construction facilities, any place where the imperial (or wizardly, or alchemical) need was for bodies that could work where nothing else could.

**Hit 4 — Branch Lock:** when the character reaches hit 4 in a Labour-type dungeon, roll 3d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the three results best fits Labour. Standard reroll rules apply. Does not count as a hit. The character then enters Tier 2.

### Tier 2 — Labour Generalist (2d8, hits 4–8)

Body begins adapting coherently to sustained work. Second rolls of the same result escalate the feature.

| 2d8 | Change |
|---|---|
| **2–4** | **Body-plan modifier** — roll d20 on the body-plan table. |
| 5 | Compressive spine — vertebrae develop the ability to compress and decompress under load. Character loses and regains 3–4 inches of height across a working day. |
| 6 | Toxin-purge glands — secondary glands develop beneath the skin. Body expels industrial contaminants as a dark, oily sweat. Character stops being poisoned by things that used to poison them. The sweat stains cloth and smells wrong. |
| 7 | Grip-plate palms — palm and finger pads develop keratinised ridges. Grip on rough, wet, or slick surfaces substantially improves. |
| 8 | Additional digit or opposable thumb-spur — develops on each hand. Grip strength roughly doubles; hand proportions shift. |
| 9 | Vertebral reinforcement — bone spurs and denser plates along the spine. Carry capacity doubles; spine becomes visible as a ridge through the skin. |
| 10 | Shoulder remodel — shoulder girdle restructures for sustained overhead work. Range of motion and sustained force improve dramatically; shoulders visibly broaden and thicken. |
| 11 | Forearm extension — forearms lengthen and thicken. Reach and leverage substantially improved; proportions visibly shift. |
| 12 | Load-bearing calluses — hardened plates form at knees, elbows, shoulders, and spine. Kneeling or leaning on surfaces becomes comfortable; standing upright on hard ground for hours is now default. |
| 13 | Respiratory filter organ — develops behind the sinuses. Particulate atmosphere no longer damages lungs; character can work in dust, smoke, or chemical haze for extended periods. Clean air feels thin. |
| 14 | Stance shift — hindlimbs thicken, feet broaden, centre of gravity lowers. Posture becomes slightly stooped but immensely stable. Running gait changes. |
| 15 | Pain dampening — nerve thresholds raised. Injury registers late and dull. Minor wounds go unnoticed until they are found by sight or smell. |
| 16 | Pedipalp bud — a third manipulative limb begins developing at the sternum or lower abdomen. At first a small appendage; fully functional on second roll. |

### Hit 9 — Labour Branch Milestone (Sub-Form Lock)

The body reaches coordination inflection. Accumulated changes resolve into a committed silhouette. The character has a specific job the architecture is preparing them for.

**Sub-form assignment:** determined by dungeon type (a mine produces excavators, a quarry-hauler route produces haulers, a pumping station or reactor hall produces integrated), or by GM call, or by 1d3 (1 Hauler, 2 Excavator, 3 Integrated).

**Automatic body-plan shift:** roll 2d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the two results best fits the emerging sub-form. Standard reroll rules still apply (mandatory for impossible results, optional for contradictions). This body-plan change occurs automatically at the milestone; it does not count as a hit.

Then roll on the appropriate sub-form lock-in table below for how the commitment manifests narratively.

#### Hauler Lock (d6)

| d6 | The moment |
|---|---|
| 1 | Character wakes from an exhausted sleep to find their shoulders and hips have thickened overnight. The new proportions feel right. Old clothing does not fit. |
| 2 | During heavy exertion, the body discovers a gear it did not previously have. Endurance extends. Stopping becomes harder than continuing. |
| 3 | A trap that should have crippled the character is absorbed. They walk out of it carrying the load they were carrying when it fired. |
| 4 | Legs lock into place under weight for the first time without conscious effort. Character stands holding a burden for hours before realising they have not moved. |
| 5 | The character's sense of their own body's mass recalibrates. They begin placing their feet differently, leaning differently, filling space in a way their old instincts no longer match. |
| 6 | A fellow survivor — themselves reshaped — looks at the character and addresses them as something. A role-name, a category, a term in whatever language the facility used. The character understands what they have just been called. |

#### Excavator Lock (d6)

| d6 | The moment |
|---|---|
| 1 | Character finds themselves pressing a palm against stone to feel what is on the other side. It works. They realise they have been doing this for days without noticing. |
| 2 | Hands cramp into a digging posture during sleep. On waking, the posture feels more natural than an open palm. |
| 3 | A narrow passage that should not admit the character's shoulders admits them anyway. The ribs shifted. Nothing consciously chose to shift them. |
| 4 | Taste of dust, stone, and mineral becomes distinct. The character realises they can name the rock they are inside by licking it. They find themselves doing so. |
| 5 | Full daylight, encountered unexpectedly, is painful. The character retreats into shadow without deciding to. |
| 6 | The character stops thinking of tunnels as obstacles and begins thinking of them as terrain. Open rooms start feeling exposed. |

#### Integrated Lock (d6)

| d6 | The moment |
|---|---|
| 1 | Character sits down to rest against a wall and discovers, on trying to stand, that something from their back has briefly rooted into the substrate. It withdraws. The withdrawal hurts. |
| 2 | The infrastructure of the site begins registering as physical sensation — pressure changes in distant chambers, load on distant supports, flow through distant conduits. The character is aware of the whole place. |
| 3 | A coupling port opens on the character's chest or flank without warning. It weeps a clear fluid. It is functional. |
| 4 | The character attempts to leave a chamber they have been resting in and finds leaving difficult. Not physically — psychologically. Staying feels correct. |
| 5 | Their voice, in the resonant architecture of the site, carries differently than it should. Walls carry it further than air. The character begins using walls on purpose. |
| 6 | The character eats normally and finds it unsatisfying. They notice a nutrient feed line nearby. They consider it. The consideration is new and unfamiliar and not entirely unwelcome. |

### Tier 3 — Integrated Sub-Form (2d8, hits 9–14)

Specialised for fixed-install infrastructure work. The body becomes part of the facility. Immobility compensation, long-term stability, sensory displacement into surroundings. The darkest of the labour sub-forms — bioservitor territory where the worker *is* the equipment.

| 2d8 | Change |
|---|---|
| **2–6** | **Body-plan modifier** — roll d20 on the body-plan table. |
| 7 | Anchor growth — bone spurs and keratinous tendrils begin extending from the character's back, feet, or base of spine. When left stationary, these extend into floor or wall substrate. Can be withdrawn with effort and pain. |
| 8 | Pressure-feeding — digestive system restructures to accept nutrient slurry via port or tendril rather than mouth. Character can take sustenance from any infrastructure offering compatible feed; normal eating becomes difficult. |
| 9 | Immobility musculature — muscles restructure for tonic contraction. Character can hold fixed positions indefinitely with minimal fatigue; motion requires conscious deliberate effort and eventually feels wasteful. |
| 10 | Distributed sensory nerves — nerve endings extend beyond the body's physical boundary through any substrate the character is anchored to. Floor vibrations, wall stresses, structural loads register as physical sensation. |
| 11 | Reduced peripheral circulation — blood flow prioritises core organs. Limbs go cold and pale when stationary; fine motor control in extremities diminishes permanently. |
| 12 | Coupling apertures — ports open on the back, chest, or flanks for mechanical or biological coupling to external systems. Can receive power, data, or fluid through these connections; they function as hardpoints even when unused. |
| 13 | Vocalisation shift — voice deepens, slows, becomes resonant. Character's voice carries through solid material better than through air; speaking normally feels effortful. Hums and low tones become the natural register. |
| 14 | Sleep displacement — the character no longer sleeps in the standard sense. Cognition cycles through lowered-attention states while remaining aware of surroundings. Dreams occur but are indistinguishable from reverie. |
| 15 | Structural integration — portions of the character's skin and underlying tissue develop the same material properties as the infrastructure they are anchored to. Patches of skin become stone-like, metallic, or fibrous depending on the surrounding substrate. |
| 16 | Position-binding instinct — character develops a powerful aversion to leaving their anchored position once set. Not a physical limitation — a psychological one. Staying feels correct. Leaving feels wrong in a way that is difficult to override. |

## Warform Branch

Default for dungeons whose original purpose was **combat or violence** — fortifications with security systems still running, guard-thing crèches, old battlefields with contaminated ground, the armouries of warlords long dead. The architecture produces bodies that fight.

**Hit 4 — Branch Lock:** when the character reaches hit 4 in a Warform-type dungeon, roll 3d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the three results best fits Warform. Standard reroll rules apply. Does not count as a hit. The character then enters Tier 2.

### Tier 2 — Warform Generalist (2d8, hits 4–8)

Foundational martial shape. Not yet committed to siege, warrior, or infiltrator. Second rolls of the same result escalate the feature.

| 2d8 | Change |
|---|---|
| **2–4** | **Body-plan modifier** — roll d20 on the body-plan table. |
| 5 | Venom gland bud — develops at a soft-tissue site (gums, glands behind the ears, fingertips). Low-dose neurotoxic or paralytic secretion; slow onset but persistent. |
| 6 | Resonant chamber — throat and chest cavity develop chambers for vocal weaponry. Character can produce deep calls, stunning bellows, or subsonic pulses. |
| 7 | Aggression gating — neurochemistry shifts so threat response sharpens focus instead of scattering it. Under fight conditions, character becomes more capable, not less. |
| 8 | Natural weaponry — claws, spurs, tusks, or fangs develop. Functional as unarmed attacks; a real addition to baseline combat capability. |
| 9 | Subdermal plating — keratinous or bony plates develop beneath the skin across torso and forearms. Visible as raised patterning; absorbs blunt impact and partially deflects edged strikes. |
| 10 | Combat reflexes — nerve pathways restructure. Reaction speed improves measurably; the body responds before conscious thought catches up. |
| 11 | Strike musculature — shoulders, arms, and hips restructure for impact delivery. Baseline strike force significantly increases; character finds it awkward to move gently. |
| 12 | Targeting vision — visual cortex and eye structure adapt for motion tracking. Moving targets highlight; character has difficulty not watching movement. |
| 13 | Pain gating — pain signals can be suppressed below conscious awareness during combat. Wounds register afterward, often worse than expected, because the character kept using the damaged limb. |
| 14 | Wound tolerance — organs migrate slightly, redundant tissue develops around vital structures. Injuries that would kill a baseline become survivable. |
| 15 | Adrenal surge — dedicated gland develops for combat metabolism. Character can trigger a brief window of heightened reaction, strength, and endurance; recovery is flat exhaustion. |
| 16 | Skeletal reinforcement — bones thicken and develop internal cross-bracing. Character weighs more; blunt trauma resistance improves substantially. |

### Hit 9 — Warform Branch Milestone (Sub-Form Lock)

The body commits to a combat specialty. Siege, warrior, or infiltrator — each a different answer to the question *what kind of violence*.

**Sub-form assignment:** determined by dungeon type (a breach-force crèche produces siege, a guard-thing warren produces warriors, an assassin-cult facility produces infiltrators), or by GM call, or by 1d3 (1 Siege, 2 Warrior, 3 Infiltrator).

**Automatic body-plan shift:** roll 2d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the two results best fits the emerging sub-form. Standard reroll rules still apply. Does not count as a hit.

Then roll on the appropriate sub-form lock-in table below.

#### Siege Lock (d6)

| d6 | The moment |
|---|---|
| 1 | Character wakes to find their hands have thickened into pounding-grade striking surfaces. Fine manipulation becomes difficult; breaking things is easier than closing a latch. |
| 2 | In a fight, the character takes a hit that should have stopped them. Seconds later, they notice they are still fighting. The hit registered as information, not as damage. |
| 3 | The character's gait broadens and slows. They no longer move quickly. When they move, they do not stop for obstacles; they move *through* them. |
| 4 | An interior warmth develops behind the sternum — a second heart taking over load. Combat stamina extends. At rest, the doubled pulse is audible at arm's length. |
| 5 | The character realises, mid-combat, that they have stopped dodging. Stepping into blows is now easier than stepping around them. |
| 6 | Skin across shoulders, chest, and thighs feels tight, then hardens. Over a day, visible plating emerges and interlocks. The character's silhouette widens. |

#### Warrior Lock (d6)

| d6 | The moment |
|---|---|
| 1 | A weapon placed in the character's hand fits in a way no weapon previously has. They find themselves doing precise things with it before their training catches up. |
| 2 | The character realises, mid-sparring, that they are tracking the opponent's intent through micro-cues the opponent has not consciously made yet. It feels like listening. |
| 3 | A wound that would have slowed the character a week ago barely registers. They keep fighting through it without deciding to. |
| 4 | Muscular structure refines — not larger, denser. Movements become faster, quieter, more efficient. Gestures the character intended as broad read as precise instead. |
| 5 | The character stops noticing fear during violence. Not suppression — it simply does not arrive where it used to. |
| 6 | Killing, when it happens, is accompanied by a new neurochemical response. Character notices. Character does not immediately know how to feel about what they have noticed. |

#### Infiltrator Lock (d6)

| d6 | The moment |
|---|---|
| 1 | The character walks into a room and realises they have been unheard. People present did not register their arrival until addressed. |
| 2 | Skin tone begins shifting in low light — pigmentation responds to ambient surroundings. Not full chameleon; enough to blur the character's outline. |
| 3 | The character's resting breath becomes inaudible to anyone within arm's reach. They notice they stopped making small noise without intending to. |
| 4 | In a narrow space, the character's bone structure shifts for a moment to let them through. The memory of compressing is visceral. |
| 5 | A blade placed in the character's hand fits differently now — it does not extend from the hand, it becomes part of it. Throws, cuts, and concealment all feel natural for the first time. |
| 6 | The character realises they have been watching a conversation from cover for minutes. The accumulated details — heart rates, scent of fear, weight shifts — are enough to know how each person will move when the violence starts. |

## Utility Branch

Default for dungeons whose original purpose was **maintaining complex systems** — workshops, fabrication halls, reactor rooms, life-support cores, anywhere the architecture produced bodies that kept other things running. The utility worker is a skilled technician by design, built around the specific kinds of work their facility requires.

Utility differs from the Labour-Integrated sub-form in a key respect: utility bodies *service* infrastructure; integrated-labour bodies *become* infrastructure. A utility worker goes home at end of shift (in principle). An integrated-labour worker has no home separate from the machine.

**Hit 4 — Branch Lock:** when the character reaches hit 4 in a Utility-type dungeon, roll 3d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the three results best fits Utility. Standard reroll rules apply. Does not count as a hit. The character then enters Tier 2.

## Recon Branch

Default for dungeons whose original purpose was **surveillance, pathfinding, or early warning** — observation posts, tracker kennels, perimeter-watch installations, sensor arrays with biological components, any place built to know what was coming and where it was going. The architecture produces bodies that see, hear, and smell things other bodies cannot, and move in ways that leave little trace.

Recon is distinct from Warform-Infiltrator in purpose. Infiltrators are weapons that hide; recon forms are eyes that hide. A warform-infiltrator kills and leaves. A recon form watches and reports. The tools overlap — stealth, sensory refinement, small profile — but the intent is different, and the terminal silhouettes diverge sharply.

**Hit 4 — Branch Lock:** when the character reaches hit 4 in a Recon-type dungeon, roll 3d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the three results best fits Recon. Standard reroll rules apply. Does not count as a hit. The character then enters Tier 2.

## Stock Branch

Default for dungeons whose original purpose was **biological production** — clone-vats, medical-supply facilities, tissue-culture farms, alchemical ingredient factories, livestock programmes, any place built to produce *from* bodies rather than *with* them. The architecture does not care what you can do. It cares what you can grow, secrete, gestate, or yield.

Stock is the darkest of the five branches. The other four produce workers — bodies shaped around a task they perform with agency. Stock produces bodies shaped around what they output or what they are made of. The subject's will is irrelevant to the function. The function continues whether the subject participates or not.

**Hit 4 — Branch Lock:** when the character reaches hit 4 in a Stock-type dungeon, roll 3d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the three results best fits Stock. Standard reroll rules apply. Does not count as a hit. The character then enters Tier 2.

### Tier 2 — Stock Generalist (2d8, hits 4–8)

Foundational production shape. Body softens, glandular systems develop, combat-relevant musculature atrophies. The architecture is preparing the body to *yield*. Second rolls of the same result escalate the feature.

| 2d8 | Change |
|---|---|
| **2–4** | **Body-plan modifier** — roll d20 on the body-plan table. |
| 5 | Fat deposition — subcutaneous fat increases across the torso, thighs, and upper arms. Body composition shifts toward reserve storage; muscle definition fades. |
| 6 | Glandular proliferation — existing glands enlarge; new secretory tissue develops at chest, flanks, or abdomen. The body is preparing to produce something it does not yet produce. |
| 7 | Skin softening — keratinisation reduces. Skin becomes thinner, more pliable, more sensitive. Abrasion resistance drops; tactile sensitivity increases. |
| 8 | Docility shift — neurochemistry adjusts. Stress response dampens; aggression threshold rises substantially. Character becomes calmer, more compliant, less reactive to threat. The shift is noticeable from inside. |
| 9 | Metabolic surplus — appetite increases sharply; body begins converting food to stored reserves with unusual efficiency. Character gains mass even on moderate intake. |
| 10 | Muscle atrophy — combat-relevant musculature (shoulders, arms, core) reduces. Functional strength for daily activity remains; peak output drops substantially. |
| 11 | Pheromone production — skin glands begin producing chemical signals. Effect on others varies (calming, attracting attention, signalling biological state); the character may not be aware they are producing them. |
| 12 | Healing acceleration — wound closure and tissue regeneration speed up substantially. Cuts close in hours; bruises fade overnight. The body prioritises keeping itself intact and productive. |
| 13 | Digestive expansion — stomach and intestinal capacity increase. Character can process larger volumes of food and extract more nutrition; can eat things a baseline would find indigestible. |
| 14 | Thermal regulation — body temperature stabilises within a narrow band regardless of environment. Character stops shivering and sweating; internal conditions are kept optimal for biological production. |
| 15 | Passive secretion — skin or specialised pores begin producing a clear, faintly-scented substance. May be nutritive, lubricant, or medicinal depending on the facility's purpose. Production is involuntary. |
| 16 | Sedentary comfort — the body restructures for extended stationary periods. Sitting and lying become the default comfortable postures; standing and walking for extended periods produce fatigue faster than they used to. |

### Hit 9 — Stock Branch Milestone (Sub-Form Lock)

The body commits to a production specialty. Breeder, producer, or meat — each a different answer to *what the body yields*.

**Sub-form assignment:** determined by dungeon type (a clone-vat facility or gestation chamber produces breeders, a medical-supply or secretion-harvest facility produces producers, a tissue-culture farm or biomass facility produces meat), or by GM call, or by the tables below.

**Breeder sub-form options:** The breeder sub-form has two variants. The GM should determine before the campaign which variant is available, or offer the player a choice at lock-in.

- **Breeder (Incubator)** — biological manufacturing platform. The body gestates engineered tissue, clone-stock, cell-lines, or non-self organisms. Reproductive biology is a factory floor, not a sexual function.
- **Breeder (Reproductive)** — functional breeding stock. The body is optimised for conception, gestation, and birth. The facility's purpose was to produce offspring — engineered, hybridised, or simply volume-produced.

**Assignment roll options:**
- **Without reproductive variant (d3):** 1 Breeder (Incubator), 2 Producer, 3 Meat
- **With reproductive variant (d4):** 1 Breeder (Incubator), 2 Breeder (Reproductive), 3 Producer, 4 Meat

**Automatic body-plan shift:** roll 2d20 on the body-plan modifier table. Player or GM selects whichever of the two results best fits the emerging sub-form. Standard reroll rules apply. Does not count as a hit.

## Post-Terminal Notes

A character who reaches hit 15 is **locked**. Further trap hits on the same character should either:
- Be ignored (the form is complete; further priming has nowhere to go)
- Add minor cosmetic drift via tier 1 rolls (ruling: body-plan slot results are ignored at this stage — the silhouette is fixed)
- Trigger a "failure cascade" if the GM wants to escalate — accumulated over-exposure begins destabilising the completed form, and the character declines physically over time

Reversal from a terminal form requires biomantic intervention (in-setting) or equivalent — a competent practitioner, time, and usually a facility. It is not easy. See `Gallihammer\Reshaping_Programme_Framework.md` for the reversal framework, which applies here with the caveat that the original "practitioner" who did the reshaping is the dungeon itself, and is not available for consultation.
