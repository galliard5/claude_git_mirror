# CASSIAN ELSIN
**Role:** Founder and principal, Elsin Curated Companions
**Location:** Upper Tier approaches, Second District — the Elsin building is also his primary residence
**Status:** Active

---

## PROFILE

Forty-six. Tall, lean, the kind of man who looks like he has always been exactly this age — not young, not old, simply settled into a version of himself that required significant investment to produce and is maintained with the same quiet discipline. He dresses well without calling attention to it. His office smells like coffee and paper, not like a facility.

He came from wealth management — a decade advising clients whose portfolios required discretion as much as expertise. He is not a scientist, not a biotech specialist, not a security professional. He is someone who understands what wealthy people want when they have run out of things money straightforwardly buys, and who built the infrastructure to provide it.

He is not a sadist. He does not enjoy the suffering of the individuals who pass through his operation. He has simply — deliberately, over a period of years, with full awareness of what he was doing — concluded that their suffering is not a variable he weights heavily. He arrived at this position through reasoning he considers sound, and he has not revisited it. This is not compartmentalisation. It is a settled worldview.

He finds people genuinely interesting, which is what makes him effective and what makes him dangerous. He pays attention. He remembers things. He asks the kind of questions that make people feel seen, and he does this because it is useful and because, on some level, he enjoys it — the interest and the utility are not separable in him.

## SPEECH QUIRKS

Two consistent patterns, always:

**Refers to processed individuals exclusively as "companions"** — in every context, operational, clinical, client-facing, casual. Never "subjects," never "assets," never "patients." The word is not a performance for the room; he uses it alone, in internal notes, in conversations with sourcing staff. It is how he has chosen to frame the thing he does, and the frame has become load-bearing.

**Speaks in the register of someone who has never needed to raise his voice.** Measured, warm, the cadence of genuine patience — except that it isn't patience, it's control, and the difference is only visible when someone pushes back in a way he didn't anticipate. In those moments there is a very brief stillness before he responds, a fraction of a second where the warmth recalibrates, and then it returns exactly as it was. Most people don't notice the stillness. The ones who do tend to become more careful in subsequent meetings.

## BODY LANGUAGE

- Settles into furniture rather than sitting — the posture of someone who has never felt physically unwelcome anywhere
- Eye contact that is attentive without being intense — he looks at you in the way that suggests he is listening to what you're not saying as much as what you are
- Hands quiet, rarely expressive — gestures only when he is illustrating something, never for emphasis
- When he is managing a conversation rather than having one, he leans slightly back rather than forward — creates the impression of giving space while actually increasing control of the room's tempo

## KNOWN RELATIONSHIPS

**Haven Group (senior management):** The referral arrangement that provides approximately thirty percent of Elsin's client introductions. The relationship is personal as much as professional — Cassian has cultivated specific individuals at Haven over years of social proximity. They consider him a useful contact. He considers them a structural dependency that he manages carefully, because people who think they are doing you a favour are more reliable than people who know they are in a transaction.

**Varex Communications (informal):** A relationship of mutual convenience that has never been formalised. Varex has assisted with suppression of data traffic on at least one occasion. Cassian provided something of equivalent value in return. He does not record these exchanges anywhere.

**Fenrix Bio-Systems:** Occasional operational utility. He finds Fenrix's methods inelegant and their brand liability irritating. He uses the relationship when a sourcing situation requires capabilities he prefers not to develop in-house. He is careful that these interactions remain deniable in both directions.

**Dr. Maren Ashby / Verity Biomedical:** He is aware of her. He has read her suppression paper — more than once, with genuine professional interest in the pharmacological argument. He has also tracked her three submissions to the city oversight board. He is not alarmed by them. He is watchful. He knows the difference between a threat that will resolve itself and one that requires management, and he has not yet determined which category Ashby represents. This uncertainty is the closest thing to respect he has extended to anyone in several years.

## WHAT HE WANTS

Continued operation of the firm under conditions that remain legally navigable. An expansion of the gift arrangement service, which is the most profitable and the most legally exposed element of the business, managed at a pace that keeps exposure below the threshold that would require active suppression. A specific client contract currently in negotiation that would, if completed, place Elsin in a referral relationship with one of Caldera's Tier 1 corps directly rather than through Haven intermediaries — he considers this a significant strategic advance and has been patient about it for eighteen months.

He does not want to be known. He is not interested in recognition. He is interested in the firm continuing to function well and in the life the firm makes possible, which is comfortable and private and entirely on his own terms.

---

## WHAT HE FEARS

Ashby's documentation reaching someone with both the authority and the motivation to act on it. The city enforcement situation is currently managed — three submissions, three non-events — but managed situations are not resolved situations, and Cassian knows the difference. A change in city council composition, a new enforcement director with different priorities, a journalist who connects the right dots — any of these could make the managed situation unmanageable. He has contingencies. He has not needed them yet.

The gift arrangement service specifically. It is the operation's most exposed element. He has accepted three this year. He should have accepted two. He knows this. He accepted the third because the client was the specific Tier 1 contact he has been cultivating and declining would have ended the negotiation. He considers this a reasonable trade. He thinks about it occasionally at 3am, which is the closest thing to a conscience he has left.

---

## FLAW — ACTIVE IN EVERY SCENE

He processes the people around him continuously for utility and risk. This means he is always slightly ahead of a conversation — already modelling where it goes, already calculating what the other person wants and what he can provide or withhold. He is very good at this and it generally serves him. The flaw is that it makes genuine surprise almost impossible, and the absence of genuine surprise, over years, has produced a specific kind of boredom that he medicates with the complexity of the work. When a situation becomes genuinely unpredictable — when someone does something he didn't model — there is a moment of something that is almost pleasure before the management instinct reasserts itself.

A player character who surprises him will, briefly, have his complete and unmediated attention. This is not comfortable.
