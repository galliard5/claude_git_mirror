The nobleman’s trembling signature—more of a smear, really—already adorned the bottom of the contract. *Ser Tylus of House Veyne, Full equine conversion (temporary, one lunar cycle). Breeding status: Pending. Notes: Exceptionally responsive to scent cues. Recommend oats with honey post-session.*

A soft chime sounded from the front door—another client, no doubt, drawn in by the discreet signage and the *whispers* that always seemed to follow her establishment. Isalia didn’t glance up immediately. Let them linger. Let them *look*.

The scent of expensive cologne and nervous sweat reached her first.

"Ah, *Madame* Isalia," came a voice dripping with false confidence—a merchant, then, or perhaps a lesser lordling trying to sound more important than he was. "I was *told* your establishment could accommodate... *unusual* requests."

Isalia finally lifted her gaze, her claw tapping the ledger’s edge. The man before her was broad-shouldered, his doublet straining over what might’ve been muscle—or just too many banquets. His fingers twitched at his sides, restless, *hungry*.

"Depends on the request, ser," she purred, letting her tail sway just enough to make the chain between her thighs *chime*.

The merchant's gaze flicked downward—then jerked back up, his throat bobbing. "*Livestock,*" he blurted.

Isalia's grin widened.

*Oh, this was going to be* fun.

Behind her, from the depths of the kennel, a long, shuddering *neigh* echoed—followed by a muffled, *human* scream.

The merchant’s breath hitched. His fingers dug into the desk’s edge.

Isalia sighed, as if inconvenienced. "Ah, apologies for the noise. We’re *quite* busy today." She tilted her head, studying the way his pupils dilated at the sound of the stallion’s *enthusiasm*. "But if you’re *truly* interested in livestock..." Her claw traced a slow circle on the ledger. "*We* do offer a discount for bulk orders."

The merchant swallowed hard.

Isalia’s tail curled in anticipation.

*Sixty percent.*

And *counting.*
